Gastroenteritis, Klebsiella oxytoca

Clinical Setting

  • Antibiotic-associated diarrhea/hemorrhagic colitis.
  • Human commensal and opportunistic pathogen that can cause a variety of other infections such as urinary tract infection, bacteremia, pneumonia, intraabdominal infection

Etiologies

  • Klebsiella oxytoca complex

Primary Regimens

  • Responds to stopping antibiotic.
  • Stopping NSAIDs may help (N Engl J Med 355:2418, 2006).

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Consider in patients with apparent antibiotic-associated colitis but no evidence of Clostridioides difficile.
  • See Clin Microbiol Rev. 2022;35:e0000621.
  • Case report: successful treatment with fecal microbiota transplant (Clin Infect Dis 2023; 77:1700).
