Vilobelimab
Tradename: Gohibic

Usage and Dosing

  • Vilobelimab is a chimeric monoclonal IgG4-kappa antibody that binds to complement factor 5a (C5a) and blocks its interaction with the C5a receptor.
  • EUA issued by US FDA on 4 April 2023 for the treatment of COVID-19 in hospitalized adults when initiated within 48 hours of receiving invasive mechanical ventilation or ECMO. Fact sheet here.
  • Clinical data supporting EUA are based on PANAMO, a Phase 3, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled multicenter trial evaluating vilobelimab in adults requiring requiring invasive mechanical ventilation or ECMO. Kaplan-Meier estimated 28-day mortality rate in the vilobelimab group was 31.7% vs 41.6% in the placebo group, resulting in a hazard ratio of 0.67 (95% CI [0.48, 0.96], p<0.05). Results similar at day 60.
  • Product availability: 200 mg/20 mL single-dose vials. Must be diluted before use.
  • Pregnancy risk and lactation safety: see Table.

Adult Dose

  • 800 mg IV within 48 hours of intubation (day 1), followed by administration on Days 2, 4, 8, 15 and 22 as long as the patient is hospitalized (even if discharged from ICU).
  • Prepare doses in NS (total volume 250 mL) and infuse over 30-60 minutes.
  • Diluted vilobelimab is stable for 4 hours at room temperature, 24 hours if refrigerated.

Adverse Effects

  • Hypersensitivity reactions
  • Serious infections due to bacterial, fungal, and viral pathogens. The most commonly reported nonfatal serious infections with vilobelimab in the PANAMO trial were pneumonia, sepsis, and septic shock.

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 No human data, no evidence of toxicity in animals
Use during Lactation No data. Follow clinical practice guidelines to avoid infant exposure to COVID-19.
