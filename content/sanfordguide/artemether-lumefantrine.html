Artemether-Lumefantrine
Tradename: Co-artemether, Coartem

Usage and Dosing

  • Effective against all species of malaria and 1st line in WHO guidelines.
    • In US approved only for P. falciparum but used in practice for all malaria species according to CDC Treatment guidelines.
  • For uncomplicated malaria only.
  • Not for use in malaria chemoprophylaxis.
  • Not widely distributed in the US. Call 1 855-COARTEM to obtain.
  • Antimalarials should not be given concomitantly, unless there is no other treatment option, due to limited safety data.
    • Mefloquine: If used immediately before treatment, monitor for decreased efficacy of co-artemether tablets and encourage food consumption.
  • Artemisinin resistance is present in the Greater Mekong subregion of Asia Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2021; AAC0112121 and in Eritrea, Rwanda and Uganda.
    • Artemisinin resistance alone rarely leads to treatment failure but resistance to both the artemisinin derivative and the partner ACT drug (Mekong region only) does.
    • Do not use ACT for malaria from the Mekong region

Adult Dose

  • 4 tablets initially, then 4 tablets after 8 hours, then 4 tablets q12h for 2 days (24 tablets total).
  • Take with food for optimal drug absorption.

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days)<2 mon, <5 kg: not recommended

25 to <35 kg: 3 tabs now, then 3 tabs after 8 hr, then 3 tabs q12h x2 days (18 tabs total).
≥35 kg: adult dosing (24 tabs total).

Other Adjustment

  • Obesity: Limited pharmacokinetic data suggest no dosage adjustment required in adults (Int J Antimicrob Agents 2022;59:106482). Two small clinical studies showing occasional failures in non-immune adults >65 kg (Am J Trop Med Hyg 2008, 78:241 and Clin Infect Dis 2017, 64:199)
  • See Obesity Dosing Adjustments for details.

Adverse Effects

  • Adverse effects experienced by >30% of adults: headache, anorexia, dizziness, arthralgia and myalgia.
  • Post-artemisinin delayed hemolysis (PADH) characterized by a decline in hemoglobin levels occurring more than 1 week after treatment initiation occurs after oral therapy with artemisinin derivatives but is mild and clinically insignificant due to low parasitemia in patients treated with oral therapy. Emerg Infect Dis. 2016, 22:1381. Monitor hematocrit for 1 month after treatment.
  • QT interval prolongation.
    • See list of concomitant drugs that may result in prolongation of QTc interval.

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Can be used in all trimesters per WHO and CDC evidence review and policy 4
Use during Lactation No data on breastmilk excretion in humans but artemether and lumefantrine are transferred into rat milk indicating human milk presence.

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • For uncomplicated malaria the oral ACT Artemether/lumefantrine is recommended as first choice by the US CDC for all species of malaria unless assuredly chloroquine sensitive.
    • P. falciparum is rarely chloroquine sensitive and other species uncommonly chloroquine resistant.
  • Consolidated WHO guidelines GRADE give a strong recommendation to preferentially treat adults and children with all of uncomplicated P. vivax, P. ovale, P. malariae or P. knowlesi malaria with either an oral ACT (including all pregnant women including in the first trimester)
    • However, chloroquine remains an option unless there is known chloroquine resistance in which case an ACT should be used.

  • Algorithm for diagnosis and treatment of malaria in the US  https://www.cdc.gov/malaria/hcp/clinical-guidance/diagnosis-treatment.html
