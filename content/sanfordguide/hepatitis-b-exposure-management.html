HBV Exposure Management

Clinical Setting

  • Exposure to Hepatitis B virus (HBV): occupational and non-occupational.
  • See CDC Guidelines (https://www.cdc.gov/hepatitis-b/hcp/infection-control/index.html)
  • General steps in management:
    • Wash clean wounds/flush mucous membranes immediately (use of caustic agents or squeezing the wound is discouraged; data lacking regarding antiseptics).
    • Assess risk by doing the following:
      • Characterize exposure
      • Determine/evaluate source of exposure by medical history, risk behavior, and testing for Hepatitis B/C, HIV
      • Evaluate and test exposed individual for Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C and HIV.

Comments

  • Persons previously infected with Hepatitis B are immune to reinfection and do not require post-exposure prophylaxis.
  • Follow-up to assess vaccine response or address completion of vaccine series.
  • For known vaccine series responder (titer ≥10 million-International units/mL), monitoring of levels or booster doses not currently recommended. Known non-responder (<10 million-International units/mL) to 1º series HB vaccine and exposed to either HBs Ag+ source or suspected high-risk source—treat with HBIG & re-initiate vaccine series (preferably with Hepaslav-B product) or give 2 doses HBIG 1 month apart. For non-responders after a 2nd vaccine series, 2 doses HBIG 1 month apart is preferred approach to new exposure.
  • If known high risk source, treat as if source were HBs AG-positive.
  • Non-occupational exposure
    • The same guidelines for management of occupational exposures can also be used for nonoccupational exposures.
    • For a previously vaccinated person (i.e., written documentation of being vaccinated) and no documentation of postvaccination titers with a discrete exposure to a HBs Ag-positive source, it also is acceptable to administer a booster dose of Hepatitis B vaccine without checking titers.
    • No treatment is required for a vaccinated person exposed to a source of unknown HBs AG status.
    • Post-exposure prophylaxis is recommended for persons with discrete nonoccupational exposure to blood or body fluids. Exposures include percutaneous (e.g., bite, needlestick or mucous membrane exposure to HBs AG-positive blood or sterile body fluids), sexual or needle-sharing contact of an HBs AG-positive person, or a victim of sexual assault or sexual abuse by a perpetrator who is HBs Ag-positive. See also HIV post-exposure prophylaxis and antibacterial prophylaxis post-sexual assault.
    • If immunoprophylaxis is indicated, it should be initiated ideally within 24 hours of exposure. Post-exposure prophylaxis is unlikely to be effective if administered more than 7 days after a parenteral exposure or 14 days after a sexual exposure.
    • The hepatitis B vaccine series should be completed regardless.
