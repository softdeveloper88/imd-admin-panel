Erythema Nodosum

Clinical Setting

  • Erythema nodosum is a delayed hypersensitivity reaction to antigens, typically from infectious pathogens.
  • Manifests as red/violet tender subcutaneous nodules, most often in skin of pretibial region.
  • Biopsy demonstrates inflammation of subcutaneous fat.
  • Common associated infections and conditions:
    • Sarcoidosis
    • Streptococcal pharyngitis (most common infectious cause)
    • Tuberculosis
    • Leprosy
    • Coccidioidomycosis
    • Hodgkin's lymphoma
    • Whipple's disease
    • Histoplasmosis
    • Blastomycosis
    • Bacterial gastroenteritis
    • Inflammatory bowel disease
    • Bechet's disease
    • Oral contraceptives
    • Inflammatory bowel disease
    • Covid-19 vaccination
    • Many other rheumatologic and infectious processes, plus idiopathic

Etiologies

  • Many, see above

Primary Regimens

  • Self-limited process usually requiring no therapy that resolves once triggering condition is addressed, but a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory agent (NSAID) may be prescribed

Alternative Regimens 

  • None

Comments
