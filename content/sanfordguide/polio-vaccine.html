Polio, Vaccine

Indications for Vaccination

Routine

  • Primary series for all US infants and children.
  • US born adults who received any childhood vaccines almost certainly were vaccinated for polio.
    • However, all adults aged ≥18 years who are known or suspected to be unvaccinated or incompletely vaccinated against polio should complete a primary polio vaccination series with IPV.
  • Fully vaccinated US adults at increased risk for poliovirus exposure (eg local US outbreak) may receive a single lifetime booster dose of IPV.
  • Persons aged < 18 yrs arriving in developed countries who do not have adequate documentation of vaccination with 3 doses of poliovirus vaccines with any combination of IPV (always trivalent) or trivalent OPV.
    • Important: tOPV was replaced in all endemic countries by bivalent OPV in 2016.
  • Complete primary vaccination of unvaccinated (or inadequately vaccinated) household members or caregivers of international adoptees from polio risk countries or persons who may have received OPV.
  • Adults who have received a primary series of tOPV or IPV in any combination and who are at an increased risk for poliovirus exposure may receive another dose of IPV.
  • Definition of increased risk: Adults living or working in a community where poliovirus is circulating (eg local outbreak) and all laboratory workers and at risk health care workers with contact with persons or specimens with possible polio infection.

Travel

  • Travel (especially aid, refugee, and health care workers) to risk countries (per CDC)
  • Travel to Saudi Arabia for Hajj/Umra pilgrimage. 
  • Travelers who have not received a complete primary series or if vaccinated have not received 1 additional dose during adult yrs.
  • Persons who are residents of or travelers with stays of more than 4 weeks in a country with an exit recommendation (due to an outbreak of WPV or cVDPV in that country)
  • Some polio-free countries may have an entry requirements for polio vaccination for travelers residing in or arriving from polio-endemic or polio-infected countries.  
    • 2023 WHO country-by-country polio vaccine entry and exit requirements
  • A few countries have an exit recommendation and long-stay (>4 wks) travelers and residents may need proof of a recent annual dose whenever they leave the country

Persons Born Outside the US

  • Children born in Bangladesh, Cuba, Ecuador, India, Nepal, Sri Lanka likely received 2 fractional doses (1/5 full dose) IPV given i.d
    • 2 fractional doses (fIPV) should be considered valid and counted as 1 full IM dose towards the U.S. vaccination schedule.
    • 1 dose of fIPV should not be considered valid or counted towards the U.S. vaccination schedule.
    • IPV doses administered in these countries and recorded as "IPV" should be assumed to be fractional IPV doses.
  • For persons >18 yrs verbal reports of previous polio vaccination can be accepted unless obvious doubt exists.

Efficacy, Duration of Protection

  • VE against paralytic polio – 36%–89% for 1 dose and 89%–98% for 2 doses.
  • After completion of a 3-dose primary series of IPV, seroconversion is 99% to 100%; ≥ 90% seroconversion rate after 2 doses in wealthy countries.
  • Immunity against paralytic disease may be present even in absence of detectable antibodies
  • Immunity to some serotypes wanes after 10 yrs. necessitating an adult booster in at-risk travelers
  • IPV prevents clinical disease but does not prevent multiplication and excretion of poliovirus in the GI tract after oral infection.
  • OPV vaccinees can carry the virus out of an endemic country and into another.
    • One dose of trivalent IPV significantly reduces excretion of poliovirus in persons previously given at least 1 dose of trivalent OPV (in developing countries) at some point in their lives hence
    • OPV series are now supplemented with at least 1 dose of IPV in developing countries

Choice, Interchangeability

  • All brands of monovalent IPV available in different countries are interchangeable.
  • Combination pediatric primary vaccines containing IPV have varying schedules and complex rules for interchanging products.
  • Polio vaccine doses given outside the US are valid if documented that the dose was given at >6 wks of age and was either IPV or trivalent OPV (tOPV).
    • Since 2016 all OPV doses worldwide have been bivalent and non-countable for US purposes.
    • If only trivalent OPV was administered, and all 3 doses were given at age < 4 yrs., give 1 dose of IPV.
    • If incomplete documentation of trivalent OPV, completely revaccinate with IPV according to the US IPV schedule.

Toxicities

Contraindications

  • Anaphylactic reaction to a previous dose or a vaccine constituent.
    • IPV contains trace amounts of streptomycin, polymyxin B, and neomycin.
      • previous reactions to topical antibiotic preparations do not count

Precautions

(Definition: vaccination should generally be deferred but might be indicated if the benefit of protection from the vaccine outweighs the risk for an adverse reaction)

  • Moderate or severe acute illness with or without fever

Adverse Effects

  • OPV: has not been used in the US or highly developed non-endemic countries for over 20 yrs. due to a 3.8 per million doses risk of reversion of the vaccine strain to fully virulent polio virus especially in immunocompromised persons.
  • IPV: Minor local reactions (pain, erythema, induration) may occur at the injection site. No serious adverse events occur.

Drug Interactions

  • IPV and IPV-containing vaccines can be administered simultaneously with (or at any time before or after) other vaccines or with any antibody-containing preparation.
  • Conflicting data on OPV reducing immune response to rotavirus vaccines.

Special Populations

Pregnancy, Breastfeeding

  • No evidence exists of risk to the fetus.
    • WHO recommends use of OPV in pregnancy.
  • Receipt of IPV is not a contraindication to breastfeeding.

Immunocompromised / HIV

  • IPV might confer some protection to immunocompromised persons but no safety issues exist
  • bOPV should be given if HIV infected asymptomatic with >200 CD4.

Serologic Testing

  • Not indicated or useful under any circumstances 
  • If in doubt about whether an individual is immune, vaccinate as recommended for age and vaccination history.

Comments

  • All doses of polio vaccine administered in the US since 2000 were with IPV not live oral polio vaccine.
  • Small outbreak of vaccine-derived poliovirus type 2 in unvaccinated persons in New York State in July 2022. See CDC report.
  • IPV is a component of multivalent pediatric combination vaccines including Pediarix, Kinrix, Quadracel, Pentacel, Vaxelis which have varying schedules.  See CDC Child Immunization Schedule (Birth to 18 yrs.).
  • Outside the US trivalent IPV is produced by many major manufacturers.
  • OPV that is bivalent (types 1 and 3) or monovalent (types 1, 2, or 3) is used in most non-industrialized countries according to the specific needs of control programs in each individual country
  • Polio-endemic countries and countries at high risk for importation of polio WHO recommends 3 doses of bOPV and 2 doses of IPV as the vaccination schedule.
    • bOPV at 6, 10, 14 wks of age, then IPV at a minimum of 14 wks of age and IPV dose 2  >4 mos later.
    • An extra non-countable bOPV birth dose is advised in certain countries.
  • Persons with a previous diagnosis of poliomyelitis still need full vaccination;  immunity to 1 serotype does not cover other serotypes.
  • CDC ACIP recommendations may be broader (off-label use) or narrower compared to FDA Package Insert information often accessed by vaccine administration personnel at point of care.
