Lice, Pubic

Clinical Setting

  • Sexually transmitted lice infestation involving pubic area. Can spread to eyelashes (Pediculosis ciliaris).
  • Also known as crab louse.
  • Common to have another sexually transmitted disease (STD); test at time of diagnosis.
  • Diagnosis: Visualization of louse or nits (eggs) attached to pubic (or other) hair.

Etiologies

  • Phthirus pubis (crab louse)
  • Pediculosis pubis
  • Pediculosis ciliaris (eyelash)

Primary Regimens

  • Permethrin 1% cream, or Pyrethrins with piperonyl butoxide, to pubic and perianal skin, thighs, trunk and axillae.  Wash off after 10 minutes.  Evaluate for retreatment after 7 days.
    • If patient unlikely to return, advise empiric retreatment
  • Whether topical or oral therapy, remove visible nits (eggs) with fine-toothed nit comb or tweezers.
  • Pediculosis ciliaris (eyelash infestation):
    • Apply petrolatum or occlusive ophthalmic ointment to lid margins bid.
    • After 8-10 days, mechanically remove lice and nits.
    • If normal removal fails, Ivermectin 200 µg/kg po one week apart.

Alternative Regimens

  • Malathion 0.5% lotion to pubic and perianal skin, thighs, trunk and axillae.  Wait 8-12 hours before washing off. Malodorous. (not FDA approved for treatment of pubic lice)
  • Ivermectin 250 µg/kg po x 1 dose then repeat in 2 weeks (oral form not FDA approved for treatment of lice).
    • Do not use Ivermectin during pregnancy or for children weighing <15 kg.
    • Topical ivermectin also efficacious
    • Ivermectin 0.5% lotion (Trade name: Sklice)
      • Kills adults; causes death of newly hatched lice within 48 hrs
      • Convenient: Apply to affected areas 10 minutes and then rinse
      • Reported cure rate of 74% at 15 days for head lice (N Engl J Med 367:1687, 2012; Med Lett 54:61, 2012)
      • Over the counter formulation approved in 2023 (Med Lett Drugs Ther 65:103 2023)

Comments

  • Treat sexual contacts within the previous 30 days.
  • Do not use Lindane; toxicities include seizures and aplastic anemia.
  • Wash bedding and clothing in hot water and dry at ≥ 130ºF.
  • Items that cannot be washed should be dry cleaned or sealed in plastic bags for 2 weeks.
  • See 2021 CDC STD Guidelines: MMWR Recomm Rep 70:1
