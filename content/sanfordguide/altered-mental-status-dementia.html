DDx: Encephalopathy, Dementia, HIV

Clinical Syndrome

  • HIV positive patient with CD4 count < 200 cells/mm3 and signs of encephalopathy and/or dementia, declining mental acuity or altered mental status.
  • Presents without fever or focal neurologic deficits.

DIfferential Diagnosis

AIDS Dementia Complex

  • Clinical Presentation:
    • Slowly progressive over weeks or months
    • CSF is usually normal
    • MRI shows cerebral atrophy plus T2 spilled milk fluffed in white matter and basal ganglia
  • Diagnosis:
    • Clinical
  • Therapy:
    • ART

Progressive Multifocal Encephalopathy

  • Etiology: JC Polyoma virus
  • Clinical Presentation:
    • Dementia is rare
    • Only occurs late in illness
  • Diagnosis:
    • PCR for JC virus in CSF, typical MRI
  • Therapy:
    • None
  • Prevention:
    • ART
  • Reference: Treatment of Opportunistic Infections in HIV-Infected Adults and Adolescents (clinicalinfo.hiv.gov/guidelines)

CMV Encephalitis

  • Clinical Presentation:
    • Delirium or confusion in 90%
    • CSF non-specific
    • MRI with contrast shows meningeal and periventricular enhancement
    • Maybe associated polyradiclomyelopathy with Guillain-Barre like findings
    • Look for possible associated retinitis, colitis, and/or pneumonitis
  • Diagnosis:
    • PCR for CMV DNA (90% sensitivity, 90% specificity)
  • Therapy:
    • Treatment options for CMV encephalitis
  • Reference: Treatment of Opportunistic Infections in HIV-Infected Adults and Adolescents (clinicalinfo.hiv.gov/guidelines)

Neurosyphilis

  • Clinical Presentation:
    • General paresis
    • Dementia
  • Diagnosis:
    • Positive serum VDRL( or RPR)  and FTA/ABS (90%); CSF VDRL positive in 10-89%; FDA positive in > 95%
    • CSF lymphocyte pleocytosis
  • Therapy:
    • Treatment options for Neurosyphilis

Herpes simplex Encephalitis

  • Clinical Presentation:
    • Acute onset of confusion and fever
    • Taste and olfactory hallucinations
    • Brain CT or MRI with contrast shows asymmetric inflammation in frontal temporal region
  • Diagnosis:
    • PCR of CSF for H. simplex virus
  • Therapy:
    • Treatment options for HSV Encephalitis
