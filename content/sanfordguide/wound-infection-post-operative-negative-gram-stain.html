Wound Infection, Post-operative, Negative Gram Stain

Clinical Setting

  • Divided into Non-GI tract and GI tract surgery.  Empiric therapy, negative Gram stain, with culture results pending
  • Non-GI Tract, Non-GU Tract surgery, e.g., "Clean" surgery.
    • Post-operative wound infection, negative Gram stain, culture result pending.
    • Surgery not involving the gastrointestinal tract or female genito-urinary tract.
    • Treatment regimen determined by severity of infection: e.g., may be mild in afebrile patient or severe in febrile patient.
  • GI Tract or GU Tract surgery, e.g., potential contamination by bowel or vaginal flora.
    • Post-operative wound infection, negative Gram stain, culture result pending.
    • Surgery involving the gastrointestinal tract (including oropharyngeal and esophageal) or female genito-urinary tract.
    • Patient is febrile, with neutrophilia.

Etiologies

  • Non-GI Tract, Non-GU Tract surgery: skin flora
    • Staph. aureus
    • Streptococcus sp. (Group A, B, C, G)
  • GI Tract or GU Tract surgery: skin flora, GI and vaginal flora
    • Staph. aureus (MSSA, MRSA)
    • Coliform species: e.g., E. coli
    • Bacteroides species: e.g., B. fragilis
    • Other anaerobic bacteria

Primary Regimens

  • If skin incision, usually remove sutures to drain wound, obtain culture and sensitivity, and pack wound (see Comment)
  • Non-GI Tract, Non-GU Tract Surgery:
    • Mild infection (without sepsis; afebrile), if antimicrobial needed:
      • TMP-SMX-double strength 1-2 tabs po bid
      • Clindamycin, 300-450 mg po tid (See Commen)
    • Severe infection (sepsis; febrile patient):
      • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL
  • GI Tract or GU Tract Surgery:
    • Mild infection:
      • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po bid
      • If Staph. aureus suspected: add TMP-SMX double strength 1-2 tabs po bid
    • Severe infection:
      • [Piperacillin-tazobactam or (P Ceph 3 + Metronidazole) or (Doripenem or Ertapenem or Imipenem or Meropenem)] + [ Vancomycin (as above) or Daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q24h]

Alternative Regimens

  • Non-GI Tract, Non-GU Tract Surgery:
    • Mild infection (without sepsis; afebrile patient):
      • Clindamycin 300–450 mg po tid
    • Severe infection (sepsis; febrile patient):
      • (Daptomycin 6 mg per kg IV q24h or Telavancin 10 mg/kg IV q24h or Delafloxacin 300mg IV q 12h) + [(Ciprofloxacin or Levofloxacin) + Metronidazole]
  • GI Tract or GU Tract Surgery: Can substitute Linezolid for Vancomycin

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • In randomized trial of TMP-SMX and Clindamycin both regimens efficacious, but fewer recurrences with Clindamycin: Clin Infect Dis 62:1505, 2016

Comments

  • See IDSA Guidelines (Clin Infect Dis 59:147, 2014) for assessment and management of infected surgical wounds. In the absence of systemic response, wounds with < 5 cm erythema and no induration or necrosis may be treated with opening and dressing changes only.
  • Omadacycline 100mg IV/300mg po daily following loading doses FDA approved for ABSSSI and may be considered as an option.
  • See also, Wound Infection, Positive Gram Stain.
