HIV Infection in Children, Overview

Overview

  • HIV infection in children:
    • Child age <18 months with positive virologic assays (HIV RNA or DNA PCR) on 2 separate determinations from one or more: HIV DNA PCR, HIV RNA PCR;  p24 antigen is no longer recommended.
    • Child age ≥18 months born to HIV+ mother or infected by blood products, sexual contact who is HIV antibody + by ELISA & Western blot or + PCR (2 separate samples).
  • Perinatally Exposed: A child who does not meet criteria above but:
    • Is HIV seropositive and age <18 months
    • Unknown antibody status but born to HIV+ mother
  • HHS Guidelines recommend an assay that detects HIV non-B subtype viruses or Group O infections (e.g., an HIV RNA NAT or a dual-target total DNA/RNA test) is recommended for use in infants and children who were born to mothers with known or suspected non-B subtype virus or Group O infections
  • HIV-uninfected (definitive): Child with 2 or more HIV PCR assays which are negative: 1 after 1 month and 1 after 4 months of age, or HIV antibody negative after 6 months (2 separate specimens).
  • Diagnostic testing in children with non-perinatal exposure only or children with perinatal exposure aged >24 months relies primarily on the use of HIV antibody (or antigen/antibody) tests. When acute HIV infection is suspected, additional testing with an HIV NAT may be necessary.

Timing of Testing

  • Low risk (born to mothers on ART during pregnancy with sustained viral suppression and good adherence)
    • NAT testing test at  14-21 days, and again age  1-2 months. Final test at 4-6 months of age
  • High risk (born to mothers without prenatal care, without antepartum ART, intrapartum ART only, initiated ART in late 2nd or 3rd trimester, acute HIV during pregnancy, detectable viral load (>50 copies/mL) close to delivery, or lacked sustained viral suppression)
    • NAT testing at birth, 14-21 days, and again age  1-2 months, and 2-6 weeks after stopping antiviral prophylaxis. Test again at 4-6 months of age
  • HIV exposed infants who are being breast fed
    • NAT testing at birth, 14-21 days, 1-2 months, and 4-6 months. 
    • If breast feeding continues beyond 6 months test every 2-3 months during breastfeeding (HHS recommends q 3 months for infants and q 2 months for mothers but this can be burdensome so consider testing both q 2 months)
    • After breastfeeding stops, test 4-6 weeks, 3 months and 6 months after stopping
  • See Recommendations for the Use of Antiretroviral Drugs During Pregnancy and Interventions to Reduce Perinatal HIV Transmission
