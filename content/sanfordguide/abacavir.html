Abacavir
Tradename: Ziagen

Usage and Dosing

  • Abacavir (ABC) is indicated as part of a nucleoside backbone of an antiretroviral regimen, usually combined with Lamivudine (3TC) or Emtricitabine (FTC).
  • Abacavir hypersensitivity reaction: fever, rash, N/V, malaise, diarrhea, abdominal pain, respiratory symptoms (severe reactions may be ↑ with 600 mg dose).
    • Hypersensitivity reactions are related to presence of a specific HLA-B allele (5701). Test HLA-B*5701 before use.
    • If a hypersensitivity reaction occurs and the drug is stopped, DO NOT RE-CHALLENGE! This is associated with fatal outcomes.
  • Studies raise concerns re ABC/3TC regimens in patients with VL ≥ 100,000; activity not non-inferior to competitor agent (TDF/FTC) in ACTG study 5202. However, when used in combination with Dolutegravir and Lamivudine, ABC works well in those with VL > 100,000 c/ml.
  • Report suggests possible ↑ risk of cardiac event in patients with other cardiac risk factors (D:A:D study group Lancet 371;1417, 2008; updated CROI 2009, LB abstr 44).
    • 2024 IAS-USA recommendation: Patients with, or at high risk for, cardiovascular disease who are receiving an abacavir-containing regimen should switch to a non-abacavir-containing regimen if an active regimen is available (JAMA 2024 Dec 1. doi: 10.1001/jama.2024.24543 [online ahead of print].
  • Combination formulations
    • Epzicom (Abacavir/Lamivudine)
    • Trizivir (Abacavir/Lamivudine/Zidovudine)
    • Triumeq (Dolutegravir/Abacavir/Lamivudine)
  • Abacavir 300 mg tablets are available in a generic formulation.
  • Abacavir oral solution does not require refrigeration.
  • Avoid the combination of Abacavir + Tenofovir (this is a less effective nucleoside backbone).

    Adverse Effects

    • Most Significant:
      • Black Box warning-Hypersensitivity reaction (HR) in 3-8%; symptoms of malaise, fever, GI upset/abdominal pain, rash, lethargy & respiratory symptoms most commonly reported; myalgia, arthralgia, edema, paresthesia less common. Discontinue immediately if HR suspected.
      • RECHALLENGE CONTRAINDICATED; may be life-threatening. Severe HR may be more common with once-daily dosing. HLA-B*5701 allele predicts increased risk of HR in Caucasian population; excluding patients with B*5701 markedly decreased HR incidence (NEJM 358:568, 2008; Clin Infect Dis 46:1111, 2008).
      • DHHS guidelines recommend testing for B*5701 and use of Abacavir-containing regimens; use Abacavir only if HLA-B*5701 negative; vigilance essential in all groups.
      • Possible increased risk of MI under study (www.fda.gov/CDER; J Infect Dis 201:318, 2010).
    • Most Common:
      • Headache 7–13%, nausea 7–19%, diarrhea 7%, malaise 7-12%.
    • See ARV Drugs, Overview for class-wide adverse effects.
