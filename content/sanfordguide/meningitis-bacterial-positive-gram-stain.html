Meningitis, Bacterial, Positive Gram Stain

Clinical Setting

  • Bacterial meningitis, acute: organism seen on CSF Gram stain
  • Empiric therapy

Etiologies

  • Appearance of common bacteria on Gram stain
    • Gram-positive diplococci: Streptococcus pneumoniae
    • Gram-negative diplococci: Neisseria meningitidis
    • Gram-positive bacilli or coccobacilli: Listeria monocytogenes
    • Gram-negative bacilli: Haemophilus influenzae, enteric Gram-negatives, Pseudomonas aeruginosa

Primary Regimens

  • Gram-positive diplococci: Strep. pneumoniae (see Comments)
    • (Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q4-6h (where available)) + Vancomycin 500–750 mg IV q6h
  • Gram-negative diplococci: N. meningitidis
    • (Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q4-6h (where available) or Penicillin G 4 million units IV q4h 
  • Gram-positive bacilli or coccobacilli: L. monocytogenes
    • Ampicillin 2 gm IV q4h
  • Gram-negative bacilli:
    • H. influenzae
      • Ceftriaxone or Cefotaxime as above
    • Suspected or possible other Gram-negatives (e.g., Enterobacterales, P. aeruginosa)
      • Ceftazidime 2 gm IV q8h or Cefepime 2 gm IV q8h
    • For specific therapy (ID and susceptibility known), see Meningitis, Gram Negative Bacilli

Alternative Regimens

  • Gram-positive diplococci: Strep. pneumoniae (see comments).
    • Meropenem 2 gm IV q8h or Moxifloxacin 400 mg IV q24h + Vancomycin 500–750 mg IV q6h
  • Gram-negative diplococci: N. meningitidis
    • Ampicillin 2 gm q4h or Moxifloxacin 400 mg IV q24h
  • Gram-positive bacilli or coccobacilli: L. monocytogenes
    • TMP-SMX 5 mg/kg q6–8h
  • Gram-negative bacilli: H. influenzae, Enteric Gram-negatives, P. aeruginosa
    • Meropenem 2 gm IV q8h

Comments

  • Reference: JAMA. 2022; 328:2147.
  • Dexamethasone should be started before or at the same time as the first dose of antibiotic (but may be effective if given within 4 hours of the first dose) in those with suspected Strep. pneumoniae or suspected H. influenzae on Gram strain.
    • Dose: 0.15 mg/kg q6h IV x 4 days; it need not be given or continued for meningitis due to other organisms.
