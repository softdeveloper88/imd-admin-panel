Rheumatic Fever, Prophylaxis

Clinical Setting

  • Secondary prophylaxis is indicated for previous documented rheumatic fever or those with rheumatic heart disease, specifically mitral stenosis.
  • For primary prophylaxis see Acute Rheumatic Fever.

Etiologies

  • Post-infectious complication of group A streptococcal pharyngitis

Primary Regimens

  • Benzathine Penicillin G  (oral agents preferred in non-endemic regions, e.g., US and western Europe)
    • Weight <27 kg: 600,000 units IM q3-4 weeks
    • Weight >27 kg: 1.2 million units IM q3-4 weeks
  • For duration, see Antimicrobial Stewardship

Alternative Regimens

  • Penicillin V 250 mg po bid (preferred oral regimen)
  • Azithromycin (preferred regimen for penicillin allergy)
    • Weight <27 kg: 5 mg/kg (up to 250 mg) po once daily 
    • Weight >27 kg: 250 mg po once daily
  • Erythromycin 10 mg/kg twice daily (up to 250 mg twice daily)
  • For duration, see Antimicrobial Stewardship

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of prophylaxis varies with the pattern of the rheumatic fever (see J Am Coll Cardiol 63 :e57, 2014).
    • If no rheumatic carditis: continue prophylaxis 5 years after the acute rheumatic fever or until age 21, whichever is longer.
    • Carditis without residual heart disease: continue prophylaxis for 10 years after the acute rheumatic fever or until age 21, whichever is longer.
    • Carditis with residual valvular disease: continue prophylaxis for 10 years since last episode or until age 40, whichever is longer.
