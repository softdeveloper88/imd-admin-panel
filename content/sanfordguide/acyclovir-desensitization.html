Acyclovir Desensitization

Overview

  • Oral desensitization procedure for acyclovir hypersensitivity.
  • Reference: J Oncol Pharm Pract 2021;27:1033.

Method

  • First, add 3 mL of commercially available oral suspension (200 mg/5 mL) to 27 mL sterile water. Shake well. Final concentration is 4 mg/mL. This diluted suspension will be used for steps 5-8.
  • Next, add 3 mL of the 4 mg/mL diluted suspension (just prepared) to 27 mL sterile water. Shake well. Final concentration is 0.4 mg/mL. This diluted suspension will be used for steps 1-4.
  • For steps 9-12, use commercially available oral suspension. Shake well.
  • Perform the desensitization. After each drug administration, observe for 15 minutes for an allergic reaction (e.g., urticaria, angioedema, shortness of breath, hemodynamic instability).
  • Stop after 12, 13, or 14 steps if target dose is 200 mg, 400 mg, or 800 mg, respectively.
