Wound Infection, Post-trauma

Clinical Setting

  • Uncomplicated, mild or moderate wound infection (usually outpatient management in afebrile patient).
    • Debridement of wound may be indicated:
    • Obtain culture and sensitivity, check Gram stain.
  • Give tetanus prophylaxis and vaccine if indicated.
  • See also, Wounds, Bites and Burns: Overview.
  • Complicated, severe (usually hospitalized patient who is often febrile):
    • Debridement as needed
    • Collect specimen for culture and sensitivity
    • Tetanus toxoid if indicated

Etiologies

  • Polymicrobic (Microbial flora dependent on nature of the trauma):
    • Staphylococcus aureus (MSSA, MRSA)
    • Streptococcus sp. (aerobic and anaerobic)
    • Enterobacterales
    • Clostridium perfringens
    • Clostridium tetani
    • Pseudomonas sp. (water exposure)
    • Aeromonas sp.
    • Acinetobacter species; esp. problem in soldiers infected in Afghanistan, Iraq

Primary Regimens

  • Uncomplicated, mild or moderate, afebrile patient:
    • Regimen focuses on Staph. aureus, Streptococcus sp.
      • Surgical debridement may be indicated.
    • TMP-SMX double strength 1-2 tabs po bid
    • Clindamycin 300–450 mg po tid (If Gram-negative bacilli are suspected: add Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po bid or a fluoroquinolone).
  • Complicated, severe, febrile patient: (Piperacillin-tazobactam or Imipenem-cilastatin or Meropenem or Ertapenem) + Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative target is trough of 15-20 μg/mL

Alternative Regimens

  • Uncomplicated, mild or moderate:
    • Minocycline 100 mg po bid or Linezolid 600 mg po bid
    • Omadacycline
      • 200 mg IV (over 60 min) loading dose and then 100 mg (over 30 min) q24 h
      • 100 mg IV over 30 min bid on day one and then 100 mg iv over 30 min q24h
      • 450 mg po q24h on days 1 and 2 and then 300 mg po q24h                                              
      • Do not use an older tetracycline for reason of resistance and/or clinical  failures.
  • Complicated, severe:
      • (Vancomycin (as above) or Daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q 24h or Linezolid 600 mg IV/po q12h or Telavancin 10 mg/kg IV q24h) + either Ciprofloxacin or Levofloxacin IV

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • In randomized trial TMP-SMX and Clindamycin both regimens efficacious, but fewer recurrences with Clindamycin: Clin Infect Dis 62:1505, 2016
  • If S. aureus are Erythromycin-resistant in vitro, may have inducible resistance to Clindamycin; make sure lab checks if using the latter.

Comments

  • Acinetobacter sp. infection seen in traumatic wounds in soldiers (Iraq, Afghanistan, elsewhere in Middle East): Clin Infect Dis 47:444, 2008.
