Meningitis, Chronic

Clinical Setting

  • Chronic meningitis is defined as showing symptoms plus CSF pleocytosis for 4 weeks or more.
  • There is a long list of possible etiologies: bacteria, parasites, fungi, viruses, neoplasms, vasculitis, and other miscellaneous etiologies.

Diagnosis

  • Critical to establish an etiologic diagnosis.
  • Next-generation sequencing technologies may be of use in establishing diagnosis.

Etiologies

  • M. tuberculosis
  • Fungal:  Cryptococcosis, histoplasmosis, coccidioidomycosis, blastomycosis
  • Brucella spp.
  • Leptospirosis
  • Neurocysticercosis
  • Leptospira spp. (see N Engl J Med 370:2408, 2014).
  • Neoplasms
  • Lyme disease (more common in Europe than US)
  • Syphilis
  • Whipple's disease
  • Autoimmune disease: Sarcoidosis, Granulomatosis with polyangitis, IG4 disease, Rheumatoid arthritis, others

Primary Regimens

  • Treatment depends on the etiology. See specific infection.
  • There is no urgent need for empiric therapy unless tuberculosis is suspected, then treatment should be expeditious.

Alternative Regimens

  • See specific infection

Comments

  • Review article: N Engl J Med 2021; 385: 930
  • TB unlikely if the duration of symptoms is several months or years: consider fungal etiology, non-infectious etiology (e.g., autoimmune process, anti-NMDA receptor antibodies).
