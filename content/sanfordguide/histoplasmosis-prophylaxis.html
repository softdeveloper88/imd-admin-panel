Histoplasmosis, Prophylaxis

Clinical Setting

  • Consideration for prophylaxis for histoplasmosis infection can be considered in the following:
    • HIV infected patients with CD4+ count <150 cells/µL who are at high risk because of occupational exposure or live in a community with a hyperendemic rate of histoplasmosis (>10 cases/100 patient-years, MMWR 58(RR-4):1, 2009).
    • Solid organ transplant recipients, patients scheduled to receive TNF-inhibitors, and those with hematological malignancy scheduled to undergo intensive chemotherapy who have recovered from active histoplasmosis infection (with or without treatment) during the past 2 years.
  • Serologic screening for histoplasmosis is not routinely recommended for patients in endemic areas prior to solid organ transplant, stem cell transplant, or use of TNF-inhibitors due to the low reactivation rate (Transpl Infect Dis 4:148, 2002; Clin Infect Dis 49:710, 2009).
  • Solid organ donor identified to have histoplasmosis at the time of donation, prophylaxis for organ recipient (Am J Transplant 12,:2414, 2012)

Etiologies

  • Histoplasma capsulatum

Primary Regimens

  • Itraconazole 200 mg po daily

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Secondary prophylaxis (i.e., suppressive therapy) is indicated in HIV-infected patients with CD4 count < 150 cells/mm3 and should be considered in other patients in whom immunosuppression cannot be reversed or who have relapsed infection following completion of standard therapy.
