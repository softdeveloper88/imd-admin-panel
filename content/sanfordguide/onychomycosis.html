Onychomycosis

Clinical Setting

  • Fungal infection of the fingernail or toenail.
  • Main patterns of onychomycosis include distolateral subungual, proximal subungual (associated with immunosuppressive condition, HIV), superficial and total dystrophic.
  • Diagnosis confirmed by microscopic evaluation and culture of nail clippings.

Etiologies

  • Dermatophytes (Trichophyton rubrum and T. mentagrophytes) account for most of cases
  • Rarely candida and molds can cause disease

Primary Regimens

  • Fingernail, adult:
    • Terbinafine 250 mg po q24h x 6 weeks (79% effective)
    • Itraconazole (~ 60-70% effective)
      • 200 mg po q24h x 3 months OR
      • 200 mg po bid x 1 week/month x 2 months
  • Fingernail, pediatric:
    • 10-20 kg: Terbinafine 62.5 mg po q24h x6 weeks
    • 20-40 kg: Terbinafine 125 mg po q24h x6 weeks
    • >40 kg: adult Terbinafine dose
  • Toenail, adult:
    • Terbinafine 250 mg po q24h x 12 weeks (66-76% effective)
    • Itraconazole (~ 60% effective)
      • 200 mg po q24h x 3 months OR
      • 200 mg bid x 1 wk/mo x 3–4 months
  • Toenail, pediatric:
    • 10-20 kg: Terbinafine 62.5 mg po q24h x12 weeks
    • 20-40 kg: Terbinafine 125 mg po q24h x12 weeks
    • >40 kg: adult Terbinafine dose

Alternative Regimens

  • Fingernail:
    • Fluconazole 150–300 mg po q wk x 3–6 months (~50% effective)
  • Toenail:
    • Fluconazole 150–300 mg po q wk x 6–12 months (~50% effective)
    • Efinaconazole (Jublia) 10% solution applied once daily for 48 weeks for the topical treatment of toenail onychomycosis due to T. rubrum and T. mentagrophytes.  Complete or almost complete cure, 23-26% (package insert).
    • Tavaborole (Kerydin) 5% solution applied once daily for 48 weeks. Complete or almost complete cure rates of 15-18%. Ref.: Med Lett Drugs Ther 57:35, 2015.

Comments

  • Terbinafine was associated with a higher cure rate for toenail fungal infections than azoles in a meta-analysis (JAMA 319: 397, 2018).  Terbinafine: serious but rare cases of hepatic failure have been reported; avoid in those with chronic or active liver disease.
  • The role of nail removal in treatment is unclear and may add benefit in cases due to drug-resistant fungi.
  • FDA has approved laser therapy that is temporarily effective. Reported expense of each treatment: $300 - $1000 (Med Lett Drugs Ther 55:15, 2013).
