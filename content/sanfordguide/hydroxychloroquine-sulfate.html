Hydroxychloroquine sulfate
Tradename: Plaquenil

Usage and Dosing

  • Approved for chloroquine-sensitive uncomplicated malaria (P. ovale, P. malariae, P. knowlesi, P. falciparum) (P. vivax, except Papua, Indonesia)
  • Note regarding COVID-19:
    • Chloroquine or Hydroxychloroquine ± Azithromycin: Not recommended due to lack of data supporting efficacy and risk of serious cardiac arrhythmia. See FDA Drug Safety Communication (04/24/2020)
    • FDA Emergency Use Authorization (EUA) revoked on June 15, 2020.

Adult Dose

  • Chloroquine-sensitive malaria:
    • 800 mg salt (620 mg base) po, followed by 400 mg salt (310 mg base) po at 6, 24, and 48 hours. Total: 2000 mg salt (1,550 mg salt)

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days) Chloroquine-sensitive malaria:
10 mg base/kg po, then 5 mg base/kg at 6, 24, and 48 hrs

Mild impairment (Child-Pugh Class A) Use with caution
Moderate impairment (Child-Pugh Class B) Use with caution
Severe impairment (Child-Pugh Class C) Use with caution

Other Adjustment

  • No adjustment for obesity

Adverse Effects

  • Contraindications
    • If retinal or visual field changes of any etiology
    • Hypersensitivity to 4-aminoquinolone compounds
    • Prolonged QT interval at baseline
    • Increased risk for arrythmia
  •  Warnings
    • Cardiac effects, including QTc prolongation
    • Severe hypoglycemia
    • Hemolysis in G6PD deficiency, pancytopenia, aplastic anemia, neutropenia
    • Hepatic impairment: use with caution
    • Renal impairment: increased toxicity reactions
    • Convulsions
    • Worsening of psoriasis, porphyria
    • Retinopathy
  • Common AEs
    • Dizziness, headache, loss of appetite, nausea/vomiting

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • Antimalarial
