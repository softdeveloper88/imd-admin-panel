Tinea Corporis, Cruris, Pedis

Clinical Setting

  • Superficial fungal skin infections:
    • Tinea Corporis
    • Tinea Cruris (Jock Itch)
    • Tinea Pedis (Athlete's Foot)
  • Unlike Tinea capitis (ringworm), these infections above can often be cured with topical therapy alone. Systemic therapy can be reserved for severe or refractory infection, recurrent infection, or in immunocompromised patients.

Etiologies

  • Trichophyton rubrum
  • T. mentagrophytes
  • Epidermophyton floccosum

Primary Regimens

  • Terbinafine 1% cream (Lamisil AT) for T. corporis, T. cruris, and interdigital T. pedis apply twice daily for 1 week; for plantar T. pedis apply twice daily for 2 weeks.
  • Butenafine 1% (Lotrimin Ultra) for T. corporis and T. cruris apply once daily for 2 weeks; for T. pedis apply once daily for 4 weeks or twice daily for 2 weeks.

Alternative Regimens

  • Topical
    • Naftifine Hydrochloride 1-2% cream or gel apply once daily for 2 weeks
    • Imidazoles (clotrimazole, ketoconazole, econazole, etc.) apply twice daily for 2-4 weeks
  • Systemic
    • Terbinafine 250 mg po q24h x 2 weeks
    • Ketoconazole 200 mg po q24h x 4 weeks
    • Fluconazole 150 mg po 1x/wk x 2–4 weeks
    • Griseofulvin (microsize formulation):
      • Adults: 500 mg po q24h x 4–6 weeks
      • Pediatric: 10–20 mg/kg/day x 2-4 weeks (for T. corporis), 4-8 weeks (for T. pedis)

Comments

  • Serious but rare cases of hepatic failure have been reported in patients receiving systemic Terbinafine and it should not be used in those with chronic or active liver disease.
  • Review of superficial fungal infections: Pediatr Rev 33:e22, 2012.
