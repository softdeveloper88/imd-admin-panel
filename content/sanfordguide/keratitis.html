Keratitis, Viral

Clinical Setting

  • Viral infection of the cornea.
  • Fluorescein staining shows dendritic epithelial lesions in Herpes simplex (HSV) viral infection.
  • Adenovirus becoming recognized as a complication of adenovirus conjunctivitis (Cur Opin Ophthalmology 29: 365-72, 2018)
  • Distinguish from other causes of infectious keratitis (Ophthalmology 124: 1678, 2017)

Etiologies

  • Herpes simplex (Types 1, 2)
  • Varicella-zoster ophthalmicus
  • Adenovirus

Primary Regimens

  • H. simplex: Trifluridine eye drops, one drop every 1-2 hours up to 9 drops/day until re-epithelialization, then one drop q4h up to 5x/day, for a total course up to 21 days
  • Varicella-zoster: Famciclovir 500 mg po tid or Valacyclovir 1 gm po tid x 10 days
  • Adenovirus:  Symptomatic Rx. No antiviral agent proven to be effective. Topical 1% povidone-iodine in combination with 0.1% dexamethasone shown to be effective (always consult ophthalmology to follow anytime steroid drops are used in the eye)

Alternative Regimens

  • H. simplex:
    • Ganciclovir 0.15% gel, 1 drop in affected eye 5x/day until corneal ulcer heals, and then 1 drop 3x/day for 7 days
    • Acyclovir 3% ointment 5x/day is a first-line treatment outside the US
    • Acyclovir 400 mg 5x/day or Valacyclovir 1000 mg bid
  • Varicella-zoster: Acyclovir 800 mg po 5x/day for 10 days

Comments

  • H. simplex (see Clin Exp Ophthalmol 2016 Dec;44(9):824-837). 
    • Approx. 30% rate of recurrence within 2 years.
    • Secondary prophylaxis with acyclovir 400 mg po bid x 1 year reduces recurrences (Arch Opthal 130:108, 2012; N Engl J Med 339:300, 1998). 500 mg valacyclovir also effective.
    • Consider adding oral anti-HSV treatment in severely affected or immunocompromised patients, such as Acyclovir 400 mg po 5s/day.
  • Varicella-zoster:
    • Clinical diagnosis most common: dendritic figures with fluorescein staining in patient with varicella-zoster of ophthalmic branch of trigeminal nerve.
  • Adenovirus
    • Cidofovir drops associated with lacrimal canalicular obstruction (rarely) when used > 1 week
    • Resistance is a concern
    • ddC is being evaluated topically
  • Treatment should be carried out under the supervision of an ophthalmologist
