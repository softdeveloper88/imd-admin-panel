ART: Effect on Specific OIs

Effect of ART on Specific Opportunistic Infections

  • Common clinical presentation and presentation after antiretroviral therapy (ART).
  • See also Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome (IRIS).

Candidiasis (oral)

  • Oral candidiasis
  • Common Presentation: White plaques on oral & pharyngeal mucosa (thrush).
  • After ART: Clinical resolution of oral candidiasis without antifungal therapy. This is independent of immune reconstitution & may be a direct effect lowering plasma HIV RNA, which impairs immune system function
  • Treatment: Oropharyngeal Candidiasis.

Castleman's disease

  • Castleman's disease
  • Common Presentation: Fever, lymphadenopathy.
  • After ART: Clinical recovery with resolution of lymphadenopathy (J Inf 40:90, 2000). However, late relapse (fatal) after initial response described in 5 patients despite immune reconstitution (Clin Infect Dis 35:880, 2002).

Cryptococcus neoformans

  • Cryptococcus neoformans
  • Common Presentation: Meningitis usually indolent, cerebrospinal fluid leukocytosis uncommon.
  • After ART: Overt meningitis, marked cerebrospinal fluid leukocytosis. IRIS seen in 13% of patients with cryptococcal meningitis and was associated with elevated baseline serum cryptococcal antigen (Clin Infect Dis 49:931, 2009).
  • Delay ART for ~2 weeks after starting anti-fungal therapy 
  • Treatment: Cryptococcal meningitis.

Cryptosporidiosis, microsporidiosis

  • Cryptosporidiosis, microsporidiosis
  • Common Presentation: Diarrhea.
  • After ART: Clinical, microbiological resolution associated with significant reduction in viral load (Lancet 351:256, 1998).

Cytomegalovirus

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV)
  • Common Presentation: Retinitis, vitreitis, uveitis (uncommon).
  • After ART: Atypical (non-retinitis) manifestations of CMV, including pneumonitis, pseudotumoral colitis, adenitis & symptoms of viremia (Lancet 351:228, 1998). Immune recovery uveitis (JAMA 282:1633, 1999; Eur J Clin Micro Inf Dis 22:114, 2003).

Eosinophilic folliculitis

  • Eosinophilic folliculitis
  • Common Presentation: Inflammatory reaction involving new hair follicles—especially on face and trunk.
  • After ART: Inflammatory reaction to Demodex folliculorum mites. May respond rapidly to Ivermectin (AIDS 18:701, 2004; Dermatology 205:394, 2002).

Hepatitis B (chronic)

  • Chronic hepatitis B (HBV)
  • Common Presentation: Asymptomatic or nonspecific symptoms.
  • After ART: Acute flare of clinical hepatitis 5–12 weeks after beginning ART. Usually resolves without change in therapy. There is a description of resolution of e-antigen in a patient with a 5-yr history of e-antigen positive hepatitis B (Lancet 349:996, 1997).  Flares of transaminase enzymes when abruptly stopping ARV Rx (esp those with anti-HBV activity; i.e., tenofovir, emtricitabine, lamivudine).

Hepatitis C (chronic)

  • Chronic hepatitis C (HCV)
  • Common Presentation: Asymptomatic.
  • After ART: Acute hepatitis, cirrhosis or HCV-associated disorder such as cryoglobulinemia within 1–9 months after initiation of ART (JID 181:2033, 2000). HCV seropositivity may be associated with smaller CD4 recovery on ART (Lancet 356:1800, 2000). Both ↑ & ↓ in HCV RNA levels have been described following ART & overall outcomes not yet known (Clin Infect Dis 35:873, 2002).

Herpes simplex (ano-genital)

  • Herpes simplex virus (HSV): ano-genital infections
  • Common Presentation: Painful, ulcerated lesions.
  • After ART: Recrudescence of recurrent episodes of ano-genital lesions (Clin Infect Dis 42:418, 2006). Note reconstitution of HSV-specific T cell immunity in HIV-infected patients receiving ART (J Infect Dis 195:410, 2007).

Herpes zoster

  • Herpes zoster virus
  • Common Presentation: May be severe accompanied by complications.
  • After ART: Mild presentation, uncomplicated. May see increased incidence of zoster after initiation of ART.

HIV-1 associated nephropathy

  • HIV-1-associated nephropathy
  • Common Presentation: Impaired renal function with high-grade proteinuria.
  • After ART: Reversal of pathology & recovery of function (Lancet 352:783, 1998).

HIV-associated non-Hodgkin’s lymphoma

  • HIV-associated non-Hodgkin's lymphoma
  • Common Presentation: Typical Stage I-IV lymphoma.
  • After ART: Improved clinical outcome & survival (Clin Infect Dis 37:1556, 2003).

Human papillomavirus (HPV) infection

  • Human papilloma virus (HPV)
  • Common Presentation: Genital warts, squamous intraepithelial lesions (SILs) of cervix, cervical cancer.
  • After ART: Effective and adherent ART associated with reduced burden of HPV infection and SILs (J Infect Dis 201:681, 2010).

Kaposi's Sarcoma

  • Kaposi's sarcoma
  • Common Presentation: Skin lesions, disseminated disease, oral lesions.
  • After ART: Regression of lesions coincident with significant reduction in viral load (AIDS Pt Care 11:161, 1997;  Lancet 357:1411, 2001; J AIDS 31:384, 2002; J Antimicrob Chemother 51:1095, 2003). Laryngeal obstruction from mucosal edema is rare IRIS complication of ART. Decreased incidence in US after introduction of ART (JAMA 305:1450, 2011).

Lymphoepithelial parotid cysts

  • Lymphoepithelial parotid cysts
  • Common Presentation: Parotid cysts.
  • After ART: Resolution on ART (Ann Intern Med 128:455, 1998).

Molluscum contagiosum

  • Mollusum contagiosum
  • Common Presentation: Disseminated skin lesions.
  • After ART: Resolution of severe disease coincident with 10-fold increase in CD4 cells (Clin Infect Dis 24:1023, 1997).

Mycobacterium avium complex (MAC)

  • Mycobacterium avium complex (MAC)
  • Common Presentation: Disseminated disease, weight loss, diarrhea, mycobacteremia.
  • After ART: Focal lymphadenitis, granulomatous masses, endobronchial proliferative lesions, abdominal lymphadenopathy & pain (see above for more details), clearance of bacteremia without antimycobacterial therapy (Clin Infect Dis 26:758, 1998), development of cavitation in pulmonary nodules (Clin Infect Dis 27:1542, 1998). Immune reconstitution lymphadenitis may occur despite Azithromycin prophylaxis (Clin Infect Dis 34:371, 2002; Clin Infect Dis 42:418, 2006). Intraabdominal disease results in greater morbidity than peripheral lymphadenitis (Clin Infect Dis 41:1483, 2005).

Mycobacterium tuberculosis

  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis
  • Common Presentation: Subclinical disease.
  • After ART: Can develop "unmasked TB-IRIS syndrome" after initiation of ART (J Infect Dis 199:437, 2009). May also occur in patients with drug-resistant infections (Clin Infect Dis 48:667, 2009).  ARV should be started within first 2 weeks of TB Rx in those with advanced HIV; Patients with less advanced disease (e.g. CD4 > 200 can wait until 2 - 4 weeks of therapy.
  • Common Presentation: Pulmonary infiltrates.
  • After ART: Fever, lymphadenopathy, worsening pulmonary infiltrates. Note: incidence of tuberculosis decreased with ART (Clin Infect Dis 45:1518, 2007).

Oral warts

  • Oral warts
  • Common Presentation: Relatively rare oral lesions.
  • After ART: Reported marked increase in oral warts which are progressive & recur after removal (Lancet 357:1411, 2001; Clin Infect Dis 34:641, 2002). Recent prospective study did not confirm increase in oral warts, but did show increase in HPV DNA after ART initiation (AIDS 30: 1573, 2016).

Parvovirus B-19 infection (chronic)

  • Chronic parvovirus B-19
  • Common Presentation: Anemia; AIDS wasting syndrome; encephalitis.
  • After ART: Anecdotal case reports of response in patients with each syndrome receiving ART (Clin Infect Dis 36:1191, 2003). Case report of development of parvovirus B-19 encephalitis in 1 patient on ART (Clin Infect Dis 36:1191, 2003).

Progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML)

  • Progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML)
  • Common Presentation: Neurologic deficits, MRI demonstration of hypodensities without contrast enhancement.
  • After ART: Neurologic deficits; neurologic deficits with enhancing lesions on MRI, frequently with peripheral enhancement (AIDS 13:1426, 1999); long-term, see remission of neurologic symptoms & improvement of radiographic findings (Lancet 349:850, 1997) & increased survival in approx. 50% of cases (Clin Infect Dis 36:1047, 2003; J Infect Dis 180:621, 1999). With flare suggestive of IRIS, can use corticosteroids while continuing ART. Fatal, paradoxical worsening of PML (non-responsive to steroids) has also been described in patients shortly after initiation of ART (Clin Infect Dis 35:1250, 2002), very likely IRIS.

Sarcoidosis

  • Sarcoidosis
  • Common Presentation: Cutaneous lesions; diffuse pulmonary involvement; adenopathy.
  • After ART: Flares of preexisting or new onset sarcoidosis described in patients on ART (Radiology 218:242, 2001).

Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)

  • Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE)
  • Common Presentation: Incidence of SLE decreased in immunosuppressed AIDS patients.
  • After ART: Anecdotal reports of new onset of SLE & flares of preexisting SLE following ART (J Rheum 27:2711, 2000).

Tegumentary leishmaniasis

  • Tegumentary leishmaniasis
  • Common Presentation: No lesions or few erythematous papules.
  • After ART: Worsening of prior lesions or development of disseminated lesions (J Infect Dis 192:1819, 2005).

Visceral leishmaniasis

  • Visceral leishmaniasis
  • Common Presentation: Fever, hepatosplenomegaly.
  • After ART: Long-term remission (J Inf 40:94, 2000); development of post-kala-azar dermal leishmaniasis (J Inf 40:199, 2000). Decreased incidence of VL seen in France after ART in 1996 (J Infect Dis 186:1366, 2002).
