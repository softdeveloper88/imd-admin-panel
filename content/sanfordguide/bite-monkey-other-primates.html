Bite: Monkey, Other Primates

Clinical Setting

  • Viral infection most concerning; fatal human cases of myelitis and hemorrhagic encephalitis have been reported following bites, scratches, or eye inoculation of saliva from monkeys. Herpes B virus found in macaque monkeys, including rhesus macaques, pig-tailed macaques, and cynomolgus monkeys.
  • Initial symptoms include fever, headache, myalgias and diffuse adenopathy, incubation period of 2–14 days (Emerg Infect Dis 9:246, 2003).
  • Bacterial infections also occur.
  • First aid is critical following a monkey bite. Aggressive cleansing of bite wound.  Wounds to the head, neck or torso are at greatest risk for rapid spread to CNS; therefore post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) is indicated.
    • Note: PEP should be offered in every case of bite exposure because risk is always greater than zero.

Etiologies

  • Viral: Herpes simiae also referred to as Herpes B, Monkey B Virus, herpesvirus B
  • Bacteria:
    • Haemophilus sp.
    • Fusobacterium nucleatum
    • Peptostreptococcus micros, now Peptoniphilus micros
    • Actinomyces sp.
    • Oral streptococci
    • Black-pigmented anaerobic rods
    • Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans
    • Wolinella sp.
    • Campylobacter sp.
    • Capnocytophaga sp.
    • Eikenella corrodens
    • Spirochetes

Primary Regimens

  • Viral (Herpes B):
    • Post-exposure prophylaxis
      • Valacyclovir 1 gm po q8h x 14 days
      • Acyclovir 800 mg po 5 x/day x 14 days
    • Treatment
      • No CNS symptoms: Acyclovir 12.5–15 mg/kg IV q8h or Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV q12h
      • CNS symptoms present: Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV q12h
  • Bacterial
    • Early wound, no sign of infection: Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po bid x 5 days
    • Later (3-24 hours), signs of infection:
      • Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 gm IV q6h or Cefoxitin 2 gm IV q8h
      • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h
    • For serious infections, until MRSA is excluded, add Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) or Daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q24h

Alternative Regimens

  • A carbapenem can be used in place of Ampicillin-sulbactam, Cefoxitin, or Piperacillin-tazobactam if parenteral therapy is required
  • If patient is Penicillin allergic: Clindamycin + (Ciprofloxacin or Levofloxacin or Moxifloxacin).
  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 1000 mg-200 mg IV q8h (where available)

Comments

  • Administer tetanus toxoid or Tdap (preferred) if > 10 years since last dose
  • Rabies in monkeys rare to non-existent but general caution requires PEP after monkey bites in travelers (common occurrence)
    • See Rabies, Vaccines and PEP for detailed evaluation procedure and recommendations
  • In vitro Acyclovir and Ganciclovir less active than other nucleosides (Penciclovir or 5-ethyldeoxyuridine may be more active; clinical data needed)(Antimicrob Agents Chemother 51:2028, 2007).
  • References on treatment and prevention, Clin Infect Dis 20:421, 1995, Clin Infect Dis 35:1191, 2002.
  • Bacteria similar to human bites, see Clinical Infectious Diseases 2014;59(2):e10–5, Clin Micro Rev, Apr. 2011, p. 231–246.
