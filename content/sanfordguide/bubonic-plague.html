Bubonic Plague

Clinical Setting

  • Febrile, regional, painful lymphadenitis (Buboes). Regional lymph nodes become enlarged and tender; inguinal lymph nodes are most common.
  • Result of bite of infected rodent flea.
  • If untreated can progress to bacteremia, with and without shock, and seeding of lungs and rarely meninges.
  • Reviews: Clin Infect Dis 2020; 70(suppl 1): S1-S60

Diagnosis

  • Fastest is either antigen detection or PCR. Typical appearance on stain of aspirate from Bubo (suppurative node). With proper staining has bipolar appearance like a closed clothes pin.

Etiologies

  • Yersinia pestis (Small gram-negative bacillus)

Primary Regimens

  • See Plague Pneumonia

Alternative Regimens

  •  See Plague Pneumonia

Comment

  • Treatment option.
    • Adult: Doxycycline 200 mg IV/po for one dose and then starting 12 hrs later: 100 mg IV/po bid x 7-10 days.
    • Children:
      • Weight <45 kg: Doxycycline 2.2 mg/kg po q12h x 7-10 days
      • Weight ≥45 kg: Doxycycline 100 mg po q12h x 7-10 days
      • No evidence of tooth staining in children.   
  • Comprehensive reviews of antibacterial therapy options:  MMWR. Recommendations and Reports,  July 16, 2021 / 70(3);1–27.
