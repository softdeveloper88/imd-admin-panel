Tuberculosis, Exposure, Initial TST Negative

Clinical Setting

  • Post-exposure management of household members and close contacts to an infectious case of M. tuberculosis.
  • Initial tuberculin skin test (TST) or interferon-gamma release assay (IGRA) is negative (note TST > 5 mm is considered a positive in this setting).
  • Treatment is essential for neonates and indicated for children age <5 years even if TST is negative (TST preferred for children <5 years of age)
  • No treatment is indicated for children age >5 years to adult (unless initial TST ≥ 5mm) except in those with high risk of progression of disease (e.g., HIV, immunocompromised host); await results of second test.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis

Primary Regimens

  • Neonates:
    • Isoniazid (INH) 10 mg/kg/day x 8-10 weeks
    • Repeat  TST in 8-10 weeks
      • If TST negative and chest x-ray (CXR) normal & infant age at exposure > 6 months, stop INH
      • If TST positive OR age < 6 mos, treat with INH for total of 9 months
      • If follow-up CXR abnormal, treat for active tuberculosis
  • Child age < 5 years:
    • Isoniazid 10-15 mg/kg/day x 8-10 weeks
      • Repeat TST at 8-10 weeks
      • If < 5 mm, discontinue Isoniazid
      • If > 5 mm, treat for a total of 9 months
  • Child age > 5 years or Adult:
      • If initial TST is > 5 mm or IGRA positive, treat for latent tuberculosis
      • If initial TST is < 5 mm or IGRA negative, repeat TST or IGRA at 8-10 weeks
        • If repeat TST > 5 mm or IGRA positive, treat for latent tuberculosis
        • If repeat TST < 5 mm of IGRA negative, no prophylaxis needed
  • See latent tuberculosis for alternative regimens to Isoniazid for those with a positive TST or IGRA who were exposed to a suspected or known case infected with an isoniazid-resistant or MDR (resistance to both isoniazid and rifampin) strain.

Alternative Regimens

  • See latent tuberculosis for alternative regimens to Isoniazid.

Comments

  • Neonates: If the mother is being treated, separation of infant from mother is not indicated. 
  • Five-year risk of developing active tuberculosis in case contacts who acquire latent tuberculosis infection and are not treated with preventative therapy may be less than previously thought, 2.4% (95% confidence interval, 1.2-4.7%) (Am J Respir Crit Care Med 190:1044, 2014).
