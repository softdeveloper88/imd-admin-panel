Aspergillosis, Allergic Bronchopulmonary

Clinical Setting

  • Allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis (ABPA) is characterized by: wheezing, pulmonary infiltrates, bronchiectasis and fibrosis.
  • Airway colonization is associated with increased blood eosinophils, increased serum IgE and increased specific serum antibodies.

Etiologies

  • Aspergillus fumigatus (most common) and other Aspergillus sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Corticosteroids + Itraconazole oral solution 2.5 mg/kg po q12h or 200 mg capsules po q12h

Alternative Regimens

  • Corticosteroids + Voriconazole 200 mg po q12h

Comments

  • Review:  Thorax 70:270, 2015. 
  • Itraconazole given for 16 weeks has been demonstrated to be steroid sparing: decreases number of exacerbations requiring corticosteroids with improved immunological markers, lung function, and exercise tolerance.
  • Emerging data may support the use of different biologic agents such as those that traget anti-IgE, anti-IL-4, and anti-IL-5.  Inhaled corticosteroids may have a role in maintenance treatment as well.
  • For further information see Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 63:433, 2016; Am J Respir Crit Care Med 183:96, 2011; 
    Eur Respir J 4;63, 2024.
