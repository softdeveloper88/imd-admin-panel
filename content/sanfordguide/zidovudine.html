Zidovudine
Tradename: Retrovir

Usage and Dosing

  • Zidovudine (AZT, ZDV) is indicated as part of a nucleoside backbone in the treatment of HIV. It is usually combined with Lamivudine (3TC).
  • Zidovudine adverse effects include bone marrow suppression, anemia, GI intolerance, headache, insomnia, malaise, myopathy.
  • Combination formulations:
    • Combivir (Lamivudine/Zidovudine)
    • Trizivir (Abacavir/Lamivudine/Zidovudine)

Adult Dose

  • 300 mg po q12h

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Black Box warning—hematologic toxicity, myopathy.
    • Mitochondral toxicity manifest as liver and muscle injury; lactic acidosis
    • Anemia (<8 gm, 1%), granulocytopenia (<750, 1.8%). Anemia may respond to epoetin alfa if endogenous serum erythropoietin levels are ≤500 milliUnits/mL. Possible ↑ toxicity if used with Ribavirin.
  • Most Common:
    • Nausea 50%, anorexia 20%, vomiting 17%, headache 62%.
    • Also reported: asthenia, insomnia, myalgias, nail pigmentation. Macrocytosis expected with all dosage regimens.
