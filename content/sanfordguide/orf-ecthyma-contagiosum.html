Orf, Ecthyma contagiosum

Clinical Setting

  • Also known as contagious pustular dermatitis virus, infectious labial dermatitis, thistle disease, and scabby mouth 
    • Ecthyma refers to a deeply ulcerated form of viral impetigo
  • Localized cutaneous nodules at site of viral inoculation, typically fingertips, hands, forearms, or face.
  • Initial lesion is a firm, red to blue papule that evolves to a nodule, which ulcerates and is covered by a crust.
  • Acquired by animal exposure, typically goats and sheep.  Other animals (e.g., dogs, cats, horses, camels and cattle) can be infected.

Etiologies

  • Orf Virus (ORFV; Parapoxvirus)

Primary Regimens

  • Self-limited disease, no treatment necessary
  • Immunocompromised patients may require topical therapy (below).  Liquid nitrogen can be effective

Alternative Regimens

  • Anecdotal reports of treatment with topical cidofovir or topical imiquimod
