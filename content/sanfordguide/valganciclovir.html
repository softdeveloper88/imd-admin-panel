Valganciclovir
Tradename: Valcyte

Usage and Dosing

  • Valganciclovir is a prodrug of Ganciclovir with excellent bioavailability, equivalent to that of IV Ganciclovir and much better than oral Ganciclovir.
  • Black box warning: hematologic toxicity, impairment of fertility, fetal toxicity, mutagenesis, carcinogenesis.
  • Tablets should not be crushed or broken. Avoid direct contact with crushed or broken tablets, the powder for oral solution, and the reconstituted oral solution.
  • Oral solution must be prepared by the pharmacist prior to dispensing.
  • Allergy: Protocol for Valganciclovir desensitization.
  • See also, CMV prevention in solid organ transplant patients and stem cell transplant patients.

Adverse Effects

  • Hematologic (neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, anemia)
  • Gastrointestinal (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)
  • Fatigue, insomnia
  • Headache
  • Tremor
  • Pyrexia
  • Acute renal failure. Use caution in patients receiving potential nephrotoxic drugs).
  • Possible temporary or permanent inhibition of spermatogenesis in males, suppression of fertility in females
  • Mutagenic and carcinogenic (based on animal data)

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV)
  • Herpes viruses (though rarely used for these viruses)

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 No human data. Embryo-fetal toxicity in animals.
Use during Lactation Safety not established, avoid use
