Pinworm

Clinical Setting

  • Intestinal roundworm, usually peri-anal pruritus in children at night.
  • Adults may migrate and cause appendicitis, terminal ileum obstruction, and in females adults that emerge at night onto peri-anal skin may migrate to endometrium or fallopian tubes and die causing inflammation or obstruction.
  • Common in children.
    • Self oral re-infection from eggs trapped under fingernails during anal itching.
  • If more than one infection in the household, treat all household members concomitantly as eggs contaminate environment.
  • Adult worms (just visible to the naked eye) are also diagnostic, when found in the perianal area, or during anorectal or vaginal examinations.

Diagnosis

  • Microscopy: Identification of eggs. 
    • Before morning defecation and washing, press transparent cellulose tape on the perianal skin and then examine the tape placed on a microscope slide.
    • Alternatively, anal swabs or “Swube tubes” (a paddle coated with adhesive material) can be used for collection.

Etiologies

  • Enterobius vermicularis

Primary Regimens

  • Adult
    • Mebendazole 100 mg po x 1, repeat in 2 weeks
    • Pyrantel pamoate 11 mg/kg base (to maximum dose of 1 gm) po x 1 dose; repeat in 2 weeks (OTC in US)
    • Albendazole 400 mg po x 1 dose, repeat in 2 weeks
  • Pediatrics:
    • WHO allows Albendazole, Mebendazole use in children 1 year of age and older.
      • Albendazole 400 mg po once if >2 yrs of age. For 1-2 yrs 200 mg po once.  Repeat in 2 wks.
      • Mebendazole 100 mg po once if >1 yr of age. Repeat in 2 wks.
    • CDC states children aged > 2 years only. Albendazole 400 mg po once, but acknowledges WHO guidelines.
      • Mebendazole 100 mg po x 1, repeat in 2 weeks for age >2 yrs
      • No minimum age for pyrantel pamoate
  • Pregnancy:
    • If the pinworm infection is compromising the pregnancy (i.e., weight loss, sleeplessness) then albendazole treatment can be considered, but it should be withheld until the 3rd trimester
    • WHO allows use of Albendazole in 2nd and 3rd trimesters.Remains FDA pregnancy risk category C.
      • Large systematic analyses show no harm in 1st trimester albendazole https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31071321/
    • Pyrantel pamoate safe in pregnancy, highly effective but no effect against trichiuris or hookworm.
    • Breastfeeding should not be withheld during albendazole or mebendazole therapy.
      • Only about 2% – 10% of an oral dose is absorbed

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Vermox brand Mebendazole 500 mg tablets is FDA-approved but for donation only and not sold in the U.S.
  • Albendazole 200 mg tablets available from Expert Compounding Pharmacy (see Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources).  
  • Generic Albendazole (Albenza) and marketed by Amneal very costly and is difficult for retail pharmacies to access.
  • Breastfeeding should not be withheld during Mebendazole treatment, less data on Albendazole.
