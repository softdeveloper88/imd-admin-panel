Syphilis, Early Latent

Clinical Setting

  • Latent syphilis infection less than one year duration.
  • Remember: chancre is painless

Etiologies

  • Treponema pallidum

Primary Regimens

  • Benzathine Penicillin G (Bicillin L-A) 2.4 million units IM x 1 dose)

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x 14 days or Tetracycline 500 mg po qid x 14 days
  • Ceftriaxone 1 gm IM/IV q24h x 10 -14 days though exact duration and dose unclear
  • European 2020 Syphilis Guidelines recommend Procaine penicillin 600 000 units IM daily for 10–14 days, if benzathine not available. 
  • Follow-up is mandatory.
  • Pregnancy: all should be treated with Penicillin; If penicillin-allergic, the patient should be desensitized. See Methods for Penicillin Desensitization.

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Azithromycin non-inferior to penicillin (J Infect Dis 201:1729, 2010). However, due to emerging resistant T. pallidum (New Engl J Med 351:154, 2004; Antimicrob Agents Chemother 54:583, 2010) no longer recommended in latest CDC 2021 guidelines.

Comments

  • Penicillin allergy: Method for Penicillin desensitization.
  • Test all patients with syphilis for HIV and all HIV patients for syphilis.
  • Treatment the same for HIV patients with syphilis as non-HIV patients with syphilis.
  • Perform quantitative nontreptonemal test (VDRL or RPR) at 0, 3, 6, 12 and 24 months after treatment: expect 2 tubes decrease titer (4-fold) at 12 months.
  • Current 2021 CDC recommendations no longer recommend to examine CSF if titers fail to fall 4-fold, rather to follow titers and neurological exam. 
  • Syphilis references: JAMA 312: 1905, 2014; Clin Infect Dis 2015 61:S818.; Lancet 2017 389:1550 and N Engl J Med. 2020;382:845
  • 2021 CDC STD guidelines MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021; European 2020 Syphilis Guidelines 
  • Review of evidence for CDC guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 74:S127 2022
