Nipah Virus, Hendra Virus

Clinical Setting

  • Zoonotic infection.
  • Nipah virus (NiV) causes severe illness characterized by encephalitis or respiratory disease, with high death rates.
    • Fruit bats (flying fox) are the natural reservoir: transmission is by direct contact with infected pigs, food contaminated by bat urine or close contact with infected persons (Curr Opin Virol 2017 Oct 13;28:7-11).
    • Outbreaks reported in Malaysia, India, Philippines. Bangladesh.
  • Hendra virus (HeV), closely related to NiV, causes a severe flu-like illness in humans and fatal systemic disease in horses.
    • Fruit bats (flying fox) are the natural reservoir.
    • Cases have been confined to Australia, principally Queensland.
    • Transmission is by direct contact with secretions of infected horses. 
  • Diagnosis: ELISA or PCR.

Etiologies

  • Nipah virus (Paramyxoviridae family, Henipaviruses)
  • Hendra virus (Paramyxoviridae family, Henipaviruses)

Primary Regimens

  • Supportive care
  • No known effective antiviral therapy.  Remdesivir effective in African green monkey Nipah virus challenge model (Sci Transl Med. 2019; 11(494):eaau9242) and active against Paramyxoviridae family viruses, including Hendra, in vitro (Scientific Reports 2017; 7:43395)
  • Human-to-human transmission reported with Nipah virus: contact and droplet precautions recommended.
  • No human-to-human transmission reported with Hendra virus but contact and droplet precautions are recommended .

Alternative Regimens

  • Ribavirin active in vitro, clincal usefulness unknown.

Comments

  • Incubation period ranges from 4-45 days.
  • Encephalitis and seizures occur in severe cases progressing to coma within 24-48 hours.
  • References: StatPearls; 2022 Jan. 2022 Apr 28 and Pathogens. 2022;11:1419.
