Sinusitis, Rhinosinusitis, Overview

Overview

  • Infection of the paranasal sinuses
    • Often concomitant inflammation of mucosa of nasal passage and sinuses, hence, the term rhinosinusitis. is preferred
    • Applies to all the cranial sinuses: sphenoid, frontal, maxillary and ethmoid
    • Infection may be viral (most common), bacterial, fungal or a combination
      • May be primary and independent of concomitant factors
      • May be secondary to concomitant illness (immunosuppression) or mechanical obstruction of sinus ostia
