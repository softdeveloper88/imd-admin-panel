Rilpivirine
Tradename: Edurant, Juluca, Complera, Cabenuva

Usage and Dosing

  • Rilpivirine (RPV) is a non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NNRTI) used in antiretroviral therapy (ART) regimens for HIV.
  • It is best used in patients with viral load <100,000.
  • Option for some patients who have NNRTI resistance mutations, e.g., K103N at baseline. Expert consultation is advised.
  • Product availability: 25 mg film-coated tabs, 2.5 mg dispersible tabs for pediatric use
    • Dispersible tabs must be dispersed in 5 mL water and ingested immediately. Not to be chewed or swallowed whole.
    • The two products differ in bioavailability and are thus not interchangeable on a mg-per-mg basis.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • Also available in these co-formulations:
    • Complera (RPV/FTC/TDF)
    • Juluca (RPV/DTG)
    • Odefsey (RPV/FTC/TAF)
    • Cabenuva (DTG / DTG; injectable)

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • At supra-therapeutic doses, Rilpivirine can increase QTc interval; use with caution with other drugs known to increase QTc.
    • May cause depressive disorder, including suicide attempts or suicidal ideation.
  • Most Common:
    • Headache (3%), rash (3%; led to discontinuation in 0.1%), insomnia (3%), depressive disorders (4%). Psychiatric disorders led to discontinuation in 1%.
    • Increased liver enzymes observed.
