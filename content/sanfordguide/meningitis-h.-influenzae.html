Meningitis, Haemophilus influenzae

Clinical Setting

  • Specific therapy for meningitis caused by Haemophilus influenzae.
  • Usually due to encapsulated (typable) H. influenzae A, B, or F, occasionally non-typable H. influenzae
  • Positive culture of CSF with in vitro susceptibility results available.
  • Consider PCR for Gram-stain-negative, culture-negative cases of clinically suspected bacterial meningitis.
  • A parameningeal focus (sinusitis, otitis) of infection is often present in adults.
  • See Comments for Chemopropylaxis

Etiologies

  • Haemophilus influenzae

Primary Regimens

  • Dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg q6h should be administered prior to or concurrent with the first dose of antibiotic and continued for 4 days in microbiologically confirmed cases.
  • Child: Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IV q12h (max 4 gm/day)
  • Adult: Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h

Alternative Regimens

  • If β-lactamase-negative and ampicillin-susceptible, can use Ampicillin
    • Child: Ampicillin 300 mg/kg/day divided into 6 doses, to maximum of 12 gm/day
    • Adult: Ampicillin 2 gm IV q4h
  • Serious penicillin allergy:
    • Meropenem 2 gm IV q8h (adults)
    • Aztreonam
      • Adult: 2 gm IV q6h
      • Child: 90-120 mg/kg/day, divided q6-8h (max 8 gm/day)

Comments

  • Uncommon since introduction of H. influenzae type B conjugate vaccine
  • Chloramphenicol less effective than most alternative agents (J Antimicrob Chemother 70:979, 2015): a fluoroquinolone may be another option, although clinical data are limited (JAMA. 2022; 328:2147).
  • Chemoprophylaxis for H. influenzae type B (HiB): (see also Red Book) Some would prophylax H. inlfuenzae type A (HiA)
    • Household Contacts: Rifampin chemoprophylaxis  20 mg/kg po (maximum dose 600 mg) once per day x 4 days for individuals age ≥1 month and 10 mg/kg once per day x 4 days for age < 1 mo is recommended for index patients (unless treated with cefotaxime or ceftriaxone) and all household contacts in households with members age < 4 years who are not fully vaccinated or members age < 18 years who are immunocompromised, regardless of their vaccination status.
    • Child Care Contacts:  Rifampin chemoprophylaxis is recommended in child care settings when two or more cases of invasive Hib disease have occurred within 60 days and unimmunized or underimmunized children attend the facility; when prophylaxis is indicated, it should be prescribed for all attendees, regardless of age or vaccine status, and for child care providers.
