Adenovirus

Clinical Setting

  • Common cause of disease in stem cell transplant and solid organ transplant patients.
  • Wide range of clinical syndromes including pneumonitis, colitis, hemorrhagic cystitis, and disseminated disease
      • Clinical syndromes include: fever, elevated liver enzymes, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia, diarrhea, pneumonia, hemorrhagic cystitis, and rarely, meningoencephalitis.
  • Monitor for viremia (1 - 2 times / week) in those with Allo-HSCT with haploidentical donor or unrelated cord blood graft; Severe GVHD; Severe lymphopenia; and / or therapy with alemtuzaumab (Campath). Monitoring should continue until immune reconstitution. Those with at least 1 risk factor and viremia should be treated with Cidofovir. 
  • Outbreaks in institutional settings have been reported. CDC/HICPAC guidelines recommend contact and droplet precautions during hospitalization for the duration of illness to prevent nosocomial transmission; extended duration of precautions may be required for immunocompromised patients owing to prolonged shedding.  

Etiologies

  • Adenovirus

Primary Regimens

  • In severe cases of pneumonia or post hematopoietic stem cell transplant (HSCT)
    • Cidofovir 5 mg/kg/week x 2 weeks, then q2 weeks + Probenecid 1.25 gm/M2 given 3 hours before Cidofovir and 3 and 9 hrs after each infusion OR
    • Cidofovir 1 mg/kg IV 3x/week

Alternative Regimens

  • Few options, see Comments

Prevention

  • See Adenovirus, Vaccine for indications, available products, dosing, and vaccine characteristics for pre-exposure prevention.
    • Protects against Adenovirus 4 and 7 only and not against strains seen in transplant and immunocompromised patients.

Comments

  • The initial and most important components of therapy is decrease in immunosuppression (if possible) and supportive care.
  • Brincidofovir (CMX001 Chimerix) has shown virologic activity and good safety in phase 2 study of HSCT patients with adenovirus viremia (Blood 129:2033, 2017).
    • However, clinical trials discontinued as of May 2019
    • Expanded access remains available for selected patients: compassionateuserequest@chimerix.com
  • Ribavirin has had mixed activity; not recommended.
  • Vidarabine and Ganciclovir have in vitro activity against adenovirus; but little to no clinical data.
  • Adenovirus Specific T-Cells (Cytovir) infusions under development; use should be reserved for academic centers as part of a clinical trial. (Cytotherapy 16.4: S22, 2014).
  • For adenovirus hemorrhagic cystitis
    • Intravesical Cidofovir (5 mg/kg in 100 mL saline instilled into bladder) 
  • Guidelines available (Clin Transplantation e13527, 2019; Current Res in Translational Med, 2023)
