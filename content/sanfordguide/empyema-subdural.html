Empyema, Subdural

Clinical Setting

  • Fever, altered level of consciousness, focal neurologic deficits.
  • In adults, 60-90% of subdural empyemas are an extension of sinusitis or otitis media infection.

Etiologies

  • Viridans group streptococci
  • Staph. aureus
  • Streptococcus pneumoniae
  • Anaerobic bacteria
  • Aerobic gram-negative bacilli
  • Mixed infections common

Primary Regimens

  • Consider surgical emergency: drain and culture the empyema
  • Empiric therapy: same as for brain abscess.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Adjust antimicrobial regimen based on results of culture and sensitivity; if cultures are no growth, continue broad coverage
  • Duration of therapy not established but a 4-6 week course of therapy is typically recommended.
