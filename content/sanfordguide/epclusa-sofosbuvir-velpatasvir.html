Epclusa: Sofosbuvir + Velpatasvir
Tradename: Epclusa

Usage and Dosing

  • Epclusa (Sofosbuvir + Velpatasvir) is a fixed dose combination of NS5B RNA polymerase inhibitor (Sofosbuvir) and NS5A inhibitor (Velpatasvir) indicated for treatment of chronic Hepatitis C (HCV) genotypes 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 or 6 infection in adults and pediatric patients ≥3 years of age without cirrhosis or with compensated cirrhosis, or with decompensated cirrhosis in combination with Ribavirin. (summary in Medicine 101:e31183, 2022)
  • Duration of treatment is 12 weeks.
  • Test for evidence of current or prior Hepatitis B (HBV) infection: measure HBsAg and anti-HBC before initiating HCV treatment. If HBV co-infection, monitor patient for HBV reactivation during and post-HCV therapy.
  • Product availability (listed as Sofosbuvir/Velpatasvir):
    • Tablets: 400 mg/100 mg, 200 mg/50 mg
    • Packets of oral pellets: 200 mg/50 mg, 150 mg/37.5 mg
  • Preparation and use of oral pellets:
    • Do not chew (to avoid bitter aftertaste).
    • Taken directly in the mouth or with food. In patients ≤6 years of age, sprinkle on one or more spoonfuls of non-acidic soft food (e.g., pudding, chocolate syrup, ice cream) at room temperature or below. Pellets should be swallowed within 15 minutes of mixing with food.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).

Adult Dose

  • Dose: One tablet (Sofosbuvir 400 mg/Velpatasvir 100 mg) po once daily, with or without food.

      Adverse Effects

      • Most common:
        • Fatigue, headache
        • If Ribavirin added: fatigue, anemia, nausea, headache, insomnia, and diarrhea
      • Black Box Warning: Cases of HBV reactivation, occasionally fulminant, during or after DAA therapy have been reported in HBV/HCV coninfected patients who were not already on HBV suppressive therapy.
