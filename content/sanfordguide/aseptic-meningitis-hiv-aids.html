Aseptic Meningitis, Acute HIV Infection

Clinical Setting

  • Acute/early HIV. Aseptic meningitis.
  • Fever, headache, meningismus, nausea, vomiting, photophobia, macular rash
  • Focal neurologic signs and altered mental status typically are not present, but can occur
  • Short duration of symptoms, less than a week, in most cases
  • CSF with normal glucose, mild elevation of protein normal, lymphocytic pleocytosis- usually with a few hundred cells

Etiologies

  • HIV

Primary Regimens

  • Same regimens as for treatment of chronic HIV such as
    • Descovy (Tenofovir alafenamide fumarate (TAF) 25 mg + Emtricitabine 200 mg) 1 tablet once daily + (Dolutegravir 50 mg once daily or Raltegravir 400 mg bid) (see Comments) OR
    • Biktarvy (Tenofovir alafenamide fumarate (TAF) 25 mg + Emtricitabine 200 mg + Bictegravir 50 mg) 1 tablet once daily (see Comments)

Alternative Regimens

  • Descovy (TAF 25 mg + Emtricitabine 200 mg) 1 tablet once daily + (Darunavir 800 mg once daily with Ritonavir 100 mg once daily)

Comments

  • Regardless of the regimen selected, check for drug-drug-interactions.
  • Pregnancy considerations
    • Dolutegravir not recommended as initial therapy during the first trimester of pregnancy or in women of childbearing potential who are planning to become pregnant or who are sexually active and not using effective contraception. 
  • Laboratory findings may include negative HIV serology (although seroconversion may be detected as early as 2 weeks in later generation tests) or documented seroconversion and high HIV viral load.
  • Early (immediate) therapy is ideal.  Check viral resistance when starting antiretroviral therapy, but do not wait for result prior to initiation of ARV treatment (Rx can be adjusted once resistance test results return).
