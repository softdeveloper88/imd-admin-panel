Conjunctivitis, Trachoma

Clinical Setting

  • Chronic bacterial keratoconjunctivitis
  • Most common infectious cause of blindness world-wide
  • Linked to poverty

Etiologies

  • Chlamydia trachomatis

Primary Regimens

  • Child: Azithromycin 20 mg/kg po single dose, 78% effective
  • Adult: Azithromycin 1 gm po single dose

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x minimum of 21 days
  • Tetracycline 250 mg po qid x 14 days

Comments

  • Single dose Azithromycin is the regimen of choice
  • WHO SAFE strategy to prevent blindness
    • Surgery to treat trachomatous trichiasis, the blinding stage of the disease;
    • Antibiotics to clear infection, particularly mass drug administration of the antibiotic azithromycin, which is donated by the manufacturer to national programs through the International Trachoma Initiative;
    • Facial cleanliness; and
    • Environmental improvement, particularly better access to water and sanitation.
  • Mass treatment of entire communities recommended in areas of high prevalence (> 10% in children ages 1 to 9 years)
  • Review: StatPearls Publishing; 2022 May 29.
