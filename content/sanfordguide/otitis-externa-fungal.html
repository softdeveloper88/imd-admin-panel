Otitis Externa, Fungal, Otomycosis

Clinical Setting

  • Otomycosis. Fungal infection of the ear canal. May develop de novo (particularly in tropical climates) or secondary following treatment with topical antibacterials.

Etiologies

  • Candida sp.
  • Aspergillus sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Clotrimazole 1% solution to affected ear 3-4 times daily until resolution

Alternative Regimens

  • If topical therapy not effective, consider culture-directed oral therapy
