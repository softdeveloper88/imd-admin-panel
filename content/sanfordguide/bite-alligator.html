Bite: Alligator, Crocodile

Clinical Setting

  • Bite wound from American alligator (Alligator mississippiensis).
  • Crocodile bites largely similar
  • See also Aeromonas sp.

Etiologies

  • Aeromonas hydrophila
  • Pantoea agglomerans (formerly Enterobacter agglomerans)
  • Vibrio spp.
  • Citrobacter spp.
  • Clostridium perfringens and other anaerobes

Primary Regimens

  • Severe wound:
    • Surgical debridement.
    • (Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV or 750 mg po bid or Levofloxacin 750 mg IV once daily) + Metronidazole 500 mg IV/po q8h OR Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h

Alternative Regimens

  • Severe wound:
    • Surgical debridement.
    • (TMP-SMX 8-10 mg/kg /day IV divided q6h or q8h or Cefepime 2 gm IV q8h) + Metronidazole 500 mg IV or po q8h

Comments

  • Administer tetanus toxoid or Tdap (preferred) if > 10 years since last dose
  • Oral flora of American alligator isolated: Aeromonas hydrophila and other gram-negative bacteria; anaerobes including Clostridium sp. (Southern Med J 82:262, 1989).
  • Crocodile bites in Malawi, microbiology and management (Malawi Med Journal 21:29 2009)
  • See Clinical Infectious Diseases 2014;59(2):e10–5
