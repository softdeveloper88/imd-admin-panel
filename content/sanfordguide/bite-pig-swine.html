Bite: Pig, Swine

Clinical Setting

  • Bite wound from pig or other swine.

Etiologies

  • Polymicrobic:
    • Gram positive cocci
    • Gram negative bacilli
    • Anaerobes
    • Pasteurella sp.
    • Actinobacillus suis

Primary Regimens

  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po bid

Alternative Regimens

  • Parenteral Cephalosporin (3rd Gen) or Ampicillin-sulbactam or Imipenem-cilastatin

Comments

  • Administer tetanus toxoid or Tdap (preferred) if > 10 years since last dose
  • Information on swine bites is limited but infection is common and serious, Lancet 348:888, 1996.
  • Pigs may be colonized with MRSA (Clin Microbiol Rev 24: 231, 2011).
  • Potential pathogens include Streptococcus suis leading to meningitis (N Engl J Med 354: 1325, 2006; Rinsho Shinkeigaku 53:732, 2013)
