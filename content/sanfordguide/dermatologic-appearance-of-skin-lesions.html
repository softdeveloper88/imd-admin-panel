DDx: Skin Lesions, HIV patient

Overview

  • Skin lesions are commonly seen in HIV patients.
  • Listed below are selected skin lesions with links to treatment/management pages.

Differential Diagnosis

Macules

  • Light-exposed skin
    • Photosensitivity reaction, drug-related, e.g., Doxycycline, Fluoroquinolones, Sulfanomides
    • Porphyria cutanea tarda (liver disease; especially chronic viral hepatitis with cirrhosis)
  • Non-light-exposed skin
    • Drug reaction (e.g., TMP-SMX)
    • Secondary syphilis

Papules

  • Non-umbilicated, e.g., scabies, sporotrichosis
  • Umbilicated
    • Molluscum contagiosum
    • Cutaneous histoplasmosis, cryptococcus
    • Talaromyces (Penicillium) marneffei

Nodules

  • Bacillary angiomatosis
  • Disseminated Mycobacterium avium-intracellulare complex (MAC)
  • Sporotrichosis
  • Furuncles

Folliculitis

  • Staphylococcus aureus
  • Pseudomonas aeruginosa (Hot Tub)
  • Folliculitis barbae
  • Eosinophilic

Vesicles

  • Herpes simplex
  • Varicella zoster
  • M pox
  • Porphyria cutanea tarda
  • Stevens Johnson (includes mucous membranes)

Bullae, Blisters

  • Staphylococcus aureus impetigo
  • Staphylococcal scalded skin syndrome
  • Pemphigus
  • Stevens Johnson (includes mucous membranes)
