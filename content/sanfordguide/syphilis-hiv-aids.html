Syphilis, HIV/AIDS

Clinical Setting

  • Evaluation and treatments of individuals with HIV infection and syphilis are the same as individuals without HIV infection (Clin Infect Dis 2015 61:S818).
  • See 2021 CDC guidelines for STI MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021 or 2020 European Guidelines 
  • Review of evidence for CDC guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 74:S127 2022
  • Lumbar puncture (LP) on all HIV-positive patients with late syphilis and serum RPR ≥1:32 is no longer recommended
    • Inquire carefully about changes in vision or hearing or other neurological symptoms that would prompt an LP to detect neurosyphilis.

Etiologies

  • Treponema pallidum

Primary Regimens

  • Treatment same as for HIV negative patient with closer follow-up. See specific setting or Syphilis, Overview.
  • Penicillin is the mainstay of treatment.
  • For primary or secondary syphilis: Penicillin G benzathine, 2.4 million units in a single IM dose
  • Treat early neurosyphilis in HIV patients for 10-14 days regardless of CD4 count, MMWR 56:625, 2007.
  • Late latent syphilis (>1y or indeterminate) requires longer treatment.

Alternative Regimens

  • For primary and secondary syphilis:
    • Doxycycline, 100 mg orally twice a day x 14 days (first alternative)
    • Ceftriaxone, 1–2 gm IM/IV daily x 10–14 days (second alternative)
    • European 2020 Syphilis Guidelines (but not CDC guidelines) recommend Procaine penicillin 600 000 units IM daily for 10–14 days if Benzathine not available. 
  • In some countries, benzathine Penicillin G is not available and shortages are increasingly common.
    • Consider high dose Amoxicillin 3 gm/day with probenecid (750 mg/day) po given tid in divided doses x 2 weeks (early syphilis) x 4 weeks (late latent or unknown duration) based on Japanese experience in HIV-infected individuals (efficacy 95.5%, 273/286 patients; Clin Infect Dis 61:177, 2015).
    • Another alternative is Amoxicillin 1.5 g/day is better tolerated than above Amoxicillin/Probenicid combination with similar efficacy (94.9%) Sex Transm Infect 98:173 2022

Comments

  • Reference: Clin Infect Dis 44:S130, 2007; Lancet 2017 389:1550; N Engl J Med. 2020;382:845
  • Individuals with HIV infection may have a slower decline in titers after treatment and there are anecdotal reports of prolonged elevated titers or false negative serologies.
  • Consider CSF examination and retreatment for those whose nontreptonemal titers do not decrease 4-fold within 12-24 mo, especially if original titers >1:32. If CSF normal, can retreat with 2.4 million units benzathine penicillin G administered IM weekly x 3 
  • Close follow-up is critical in HIV-infected individuals with syphilis.
