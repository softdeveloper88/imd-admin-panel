Symtuza
Tradename: Symtuza

Usage and Dosing

  • Symtuza (DRV + Cobi + FTC + TAF) is a 4-drug combination formulation for treatment of HIV-1 in treatment naive adults or those who are virologically suppressed (HIV RNA < 50 copies/mL) on another ARV regimen for at least 6 months (and no resistance to DRV or TAF).
  • Test for Hepatitis B (HBV) co-infection before starting Symtuza: risk of severe acute exacerbation of HBV (resulting from FTC or TAF components).
  • Not recommended for use in pregnancy or during lactation.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see below).

Adult Dose

  • One tablet once daily with food
  • Tablet: 800 mg DRV + 150 mg Cobi + 200 mg FTC + 10 mg TAF

Adverse Effects

  • Contraindications: See Major Drug Interactions
  • Warnings/Precautions:
    • Drug induced hepatitis: monitor liver function
    • Severe skin reactions: Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis, drug rash with eosinophilia, acture generalized exanthematous pustulosis
    • IRIS
    • Sulfonamides: monitor if known allergy
    • Discontinue if: Lactic acidosis, hepatotoxicity
    • New onset/exacerbation: Diabetes mellitus, hyperglycemia, fat accumulation/redistribution
    • Hemophilia: risk of increased bleeding events
  • Adverse effects
    • Most common:
      • Diarrhea
      • Rash
      • Nausea
      • Fatigue
      • Abdominal discomfort
      • Flatulence
