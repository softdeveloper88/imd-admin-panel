Keratitis, Fungal

Clinical Setting

  • Empiric therapy is not recommended for fungal keratitis.
  • Important to try to identify organism from corneal scrapings; examine by Gram and Calcofluor white stains, and culture in Sabouraud's medium.
  • Treatments described below are topical.

Etiologies

  • Aspergillus sp.
  • Fusarium sp.
  • Candida sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Natamycin (5%) eye drops: one drop q1-2 hrs for 3-4 days; then one drop q3h to q4h for 14 to 21 days or until resolution of keratitis.
  • Gradual dose reduction at 4-7 day intervals may be beneficial.

Alternative Regimens

  • Amphotericin B (0.05-0.15%) eye drops, beginning as frequently as one drop every hour, and then with a slow taper based on clinical response.
  • See Comments

Comments

  • Numerous other treatment options (1% topical Itraconazole for 6 weeks, oral Itraconazole 100 mg bid for 3 weeks, topical Voriconazole 1% hourly for 2 week, topical Miconazole 1% 5x/day, topical Silver Sulphadiazine 0.5-1% 5x/day) appear to have similar efficacy (Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2:004241, 2012).
  • Review of fungal keratitis: Clin Ophthalmol. 18:85; 2024
