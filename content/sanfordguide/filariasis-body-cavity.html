Filariasis, Body Cavity

Clinical Setting

  • Mansonella perstans (Dipetalonemiasis), a body cavity filariasis, is asymptomatic in most patients, but can cause a variety of symptoms including angioedema, pruritus, fever and headache.
  • Mansonella streptocerca is asymptomatic in most patients but causes chronic pruritic hypopigmented lesions that may be confused with leprosy or onchocerciasis.
  • Mansonella ozzardi infection is usually asymptomatic, but patients may complain of joint pain, pruritus, and/or lymphadenopathy. May have allergic reaction from dying organisms.
    • Note: most treatment regimens are ineffective.
  • All transmitted by arthropod vectors
  • Review of large clinical experience: Clin Infect Dis 2021 Aug 31.

Diagnosis

  • Mansonella perstans and M. ozzardi microfilaria are seen on stained blood film. Neither species exhibits periodicity.
  • Mansonella streptocerca microfilariae are seen in skin snips.
  • Filaria serology may be positive but does not distinguish between the 8 different filarial species infecting humans.

Etiologies

  • Mansonella perstans
  • Mansonella ozzardi
  • Mansonella streptocerca
  • Mansonella "DEUX" (Gabon only uncertain clinical significance)

Primary Regimens

  • Mansonella perstans:
    • Doxycycline 200 mg po once daily or 100 mg po bid x 6 weeks
    • May not be effective outside Mali, Ghana, and Cameroon (Wolbachia endosymbiont present),
      • Definitely not effective in Uganda and Gabon and Wolbachia presence not studied elsewhere.
    • Albendazole 200 mg po bid with or without DEC can be tried for failures in other areas but all anti-helmintics have poor efficacy
  • Mansonella ozzardi:
    • Ivermectin 200 μg/kg x 1 dose. Repeat if symptoms persist. Limited data (Am J Trop Med Hyg 90:1170, 2014) and Am J Trop Med Hyg 98:786, 2018.
      • The safety of ivermectin in children <15kg has not been authorized by most national authorities.
      • WHO allows children who are at least 90 cm tall (equivalent to ~15 kg or ~2 yrs of age) to be treated with Ivermectin.
        • Systematic review (n=1,000 subjects) strongly suggest safety <15 kg (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 2021 Mar 17;15(3):e0009144)
      • Wolbachia present in this species but no clinical data on doxycycline.
  • Mansonella streptocerca:
    • Ivermectin 150 μg/kg x 1 dose reduces microfilaremia but no effect on clinical manifestations such as pruritus.
    • Diethylcarbamazine 6 mg/kg x 12 days kills microfilaria in adults but causes transient exacerbation of clinical symptoms and may cause vision loss and hypotension if co-infected with O. volvulus.
    • No clinical data on Doxycycline or on Wolbachia presence

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Efficacy of Doxycycline in M. perstans due to inhibition of endosymbiont Wolbachia. Ivermectin has no activity. Ref: Am J Trop Med Hyg 101:84, 2019. Randomized Doxycycline trial: N Engl J Med 361:1448, 2009.
  • Some M. perstans strains do not contain Wolbachia; doxycycline non-responsive M. perstans should be treated with 21 days of Diethylcarbamazine in combination with 21 days of Mebendazole but response low. 
  • Anthelmintics (Ivermectin, Albendazole, Mebendazole) do not clear microfilaremia in M. perstans.
