Pyrazinamide

Usage and Dosing

  • Pyrazinamide (PZA) is a first-line anti-tuberculosis drug.
  • A "sterilizing" agent: bactericidal for non-replicating, persistent TB, allowing for shorter courses of therapy.
  • Baseline and follow-up evaluations:
    • Baseline:  AST, ALT, bilirubin, alkaline phosphatase
    • Follow-up: repeat liver function tests monthly if there were abnormalities at baseline, symptoms consistent with hepatotoxicity develop, or for patients
      who chronically consume alcohol, take other potentially hepatotoxic medications, or have viral hepatitis or history of liver disease, human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) infection, or prior drug-induced liver injury..
    • Measure serum uric acid if symptomatic gout attack occurs.

Adult Dose

  • Usual dose: 20-25 mg/kg po q24h (once daily maximum dose 2000 mg/day).
  • Dosing in patients weighing 40-90 kg is based on lean body weight (see calculator). Ref: Clin Infect Dis 2016;63:e147.

      • Obesity: see Obesity Dosing Adjustments table.

      Adverse Effects

      • Arthralgia.
      • Hyperuricemia (with or without symptoms).
      • Hepatitis (not over 2% if recommended dose not exceeded).
      • Gastric irritation
      • Photosensitivity (rare, confirmed by rechallenge).
