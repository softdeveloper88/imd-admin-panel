Encephalitis, Encephalopathy

Clinical Setting

  • Encephalitis, encephalopathy caused by a viral etiology.
  • Fever, altered level of consciousness (drowsiness, confusion, stupor, coma), and focal neurologic deficits.
  • Clinical features of cat scratch (Bartonella) encephalopathy: children under 10 with tender regional lymphadenopathy, and IgG antibody titer > 1:250.
  • CSF pleocytosis, predominantly lymphocytes.
  • International consensus statement on diagnostic tests (Clin Infect Dis 57:1114, 2013).
  • Review of etiologies, treatment, and prognosis (Neurology 84:359, 2015).
  • Review of acute viral encephalitis (N Engl J Med 2018; 379: 557)
  • Review of encephalitis induced by Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors (JAMA Neuro 78:864, 2021)

Etiologies

  • Etiology determined in half of cases. Of cases with a known cause, 20-50% attributed to viruses.
    • H. simplex (HSV)(50-70 %), varicella zoster (VZV)(Clin Infect Dis 72:1192, 2021), enteroviruses, arboviruses, West Nile, rabies, cat scratch disease, parvo B19, SARS-CoV-2
    • Other (Clin Infect Dis 49:1838, 2009): tuberculosis (15%), malaria, listeria (10%), others, including, R. rickettsii, anaplasma, ehrlichia or mycoplasma
      • Powassan  is a tick borne virus increasingly recognized in Massachusetts and northern New England (N Engl J Med 2019; 380:380)
  • Autoimmune antibody-mediated encephalitis, e.g., anti-N-methyl-D-aspartate (Anti-NMDA) receptor most often recognized but many others recognized (New Engl J Med 2018;378:840)(see Comment)

Diagnosis

  • Nucleic Acid Amplification Tests are used to diagnose most viral encephalitis disorders (CID Mar 2024)

Primary Regimens

  • Presumed viral etiology: Acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV (infuse over 1 hr) q8h while awaiting results of CSF PCR for H. simplex and Varicella zoster.
  • Suspected R. rickettsii, anaplasma, ehrlichia or mycoplasma: Doxycycline 100 mg IV q12h
  • Modify treatment based on pathogen identification

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • HSV and VZV most common viral etiologies in a prospective study from England (Lancet Infect Dis 10:835, 2010).
  • In persons < 30 years old autoimmune encephalitis with anti-N-methyl-D-aspartate receptor (NMDAR) antibodies was the most common cause of encephalitis (41%) among specimens referred to the California Encephalitis Project between 2007 and 2011 (vs. Enterovirus 38%, HSV-19%, VZV or WNV 6% each) (Clin Infect Dis 54:899, 2012).
    • Now recognition of many other neuronal cell surface antibodies with a similar clinical syndrome (N Engl J Med 2018;378:840)
    • Features included seizures, hallucinations/psychosis, autonomic instability, movement disorders, language dysfunction. Possible that the anti-NMDAR can be a non-specific response to tissue injury; anti-NMDAR found in 30% of patients with proven H. simplex encephalitis: Ann Neurol 72:902, 2012.
    • Need to demonstrate antibody to cell surface neuronal antigens. Antibody panels available in commercial  (e.g., Mayo) and research labs.
    • Review emphasizes early diagnosis and treatment with steroids , immunoglobulins, and/or plasmapheresis associated with good neurologic outcomes: Crit Care Med 2018;46:1514.
