Streptococcus iniae

Clinical Setting

  • Streptococcus iniae causes infection of skin and subcutaneous tissue and often leads to bloodstream infection.
  • Most of the patients have a history of handling live or killed fish. Exposure to the fish tilapia is common (N Engl J Med 337:589, 1997).
  • Streptococcus iniae is a beta-hemolytic streptococcus that does not react with standard Lancefield antibody panels.
  • Confused in lab with viridans strep. as the beta hemolysis is not always easily apparent.

Classification

  • Streptococcus iniae

Primary Regimens

  • Sensitive to virtually all beta-lactam antibiotics except Aztreonam
  • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV once daily reasonable regimen

Alternative Regimens

  • In penicillin allergic patient, could use Azithromycin 500 mg IV/po once daily
  • Also, generally susceptible to Vancomycin, Clindamycin and Fluoroquinolones

Comments

  • Characterization of 7 clinical isolates (J Clin Microbiol 43:933, 2005)
