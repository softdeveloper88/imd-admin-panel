Tradename: Rebetol, Copegus, Virazole
Drug Shortage Information

Usage and Dosing

  • Ribavirin is used in combination with an Interferon alfa formulation as first-line therapy for Hepatitis C virus (HCV) infections. Used in the treatment of several Hemorrhagic Fever syndromes (e.g., Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever virus, Lassa Fever). Ribavirin aerosol therapy no longer recommended for  severe RSV pulmonary infections except in HSCT patients (see RSV page).
  • Significant adverse effects:
    • Do not use in pregnancy, Ribavirin is teratogenic/embryocidal. Drug may persist for 6 months, avoid pregnancy for at least 6 months after end of treatment of women or their partners.
    • Black Box warnings: Ribavirin monotherapy of HCV is ineffective; hemolytic anemia may precipitate cardiac events.  Only approved for patients with CrClr >50 mL/min.
    • Also should not be used in patients with severe heart disease or some hemoglobinopathies. Acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) reported (Chest 124:406, 2003).v

Adult Dose

  • See specific indications for dosing. There is no usual adult dose.
  • For Hepatitis C, Ribavirin dosing depends on: Interferon alfa formulation used, weight, HCV genotype, and is modified (or discontinued) based on side effects (especially degree of hemolysis, with different criteria in those with/without cardiac disease).
    • For example, the initial Rebetol dose with Intron A (interferon alfa-2b) is weight based: 400 mg am and 600 mg pm for ≤75 kg, and 600 mg am and 600 mg pm for weight >75 kg, but with Peg-intron approved dose is 400 mg am and 400 mg pm with meals.
    • Doses and duration of Copegus with Pegylated-interferon alfa-2a are less in patients with genotype 2 or 3 (800 mg/day divided into 2 doses, for 24 weeks) than with genotypes 1 or 4 (1000 mg/day divided into 2 doses for weight <75 kg and 1200 mg/day divided into 2 doses for ≥75 kg for 48 weeks); in HIV/HCV co-infected patients, dose is 800 mg/day regardless of genotype. (See individual labels for details, including initial dosing and criteria for dose modification in those with/without cardiac disease.)

Adverse Effects

  • Hemolytic anemia (may require dose reduction or discontinuance), dental/periodontal disorders, and all adverse effects of concomitant interferon alfa used.
  • Postmarketing: retinal detachment, hearing impairment, hypersensitivity reactions.
