Dengue Virus

Clinical Setting

  • Vector-borne disease of the tropics / subtropics: Dengue virus (DENV) and Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF)
  • Distinguishing symptoms: fever, retro-orbital pain, rash, platelet count <240,000 cells/mm3, absence of sore throat, and absence of cough.
  • Think Dengue in traveler to tropics or subtropics (incubation period usually 4-7 days) with fever, bleeding, thrombocytopenia, or hemoconcentration with shock.
  • Overview: N Engl J Med 366:1423, 2012. Review of epidemiology, risk factors, symptoms (Rev Med Virol, 28:e2005, 2018)
  • See Prevention for vaccine developments.

Diagnosis

  • Preferred test dependent on time since symptom onset
    • NS1 serum antigen positive from D1 to D6 and may be positive until D18
    • PCR positive from D1 to D5
    • IgM from D5 (in 80%) and 99% positive by D10.  Persists for 90 days
    • IgG from D7 if primary infection, from D1 if subsequent infection
    • NS1 preferred as most patients present D2 to D5 after symptom onset
    • Combination IgG, IgM, and NS1 antigen panel is preferred test (Quest Diagnostics) if resources allow. Frequently asked questions here.
    • CDC DENV 1-4 Real Time PCR Assay for identification of dengue serotypes 1-4 in serum or plasma. See CDC diagnostic page for more information.
  • Review or diagnostics: Clin Infect Dis 62:214, 2016.

Primary Regimens

  • No data on antiviral therapy.
  • Fluid replacement with careful hemodynamic monitoring is critical, especially during defervescence phase ("Critical Phase") between day 3 and 6 of illness. This is when capillary leak syndrome occurs and 'dengue shock' can ensue suddenly.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Prevention 

  • Vaccines
    • Qdenga (TAK-003; Takeda) application for FDA approval was withdrawn in July 2023 and future resubmission is uncertain.
      • Qdenga, which can be given to dengue-naive persons, is approved and available in 30 countries including Europe (EMA) (>4 yrs) and in United Kingdom, Brazil, Argentina, Indonesia, and Thailand.
        • 2 doses space by 3 months
        • No data on boosters 

          • No clinically relevant impact of prior JE or YF vaccination on TAK-003 performance (J Infect Dis. 2024 Apr 29).

      • Aggregated trial data: VE 84% vs hospitalizations; 61% vs symptomatic dengue at 4.5 yrs (Clin Infect Dis 75:107, 2022).
        • Predominant effect is against DENV-2; any efficacy against DENV-3 and DENV-4 as yet unclear from published data.
        • See WHO SAGE Wkly Epidemiol Rec. 2024 May 3;99:203.
      • Consider for introduction in natives of high burden, high transmission settings
        • For 6 - 16 yrs of age at 1-2 yrs prior to the age-specific peak incidence of dengue-related hospitalizations.
      • WHO Recommendations for travelers.
        • Highest benefit is during an ongoing to DENV2 or DENV1 epidemic at the destination.
        • Previously infected (any serotype) seropositive travelers MAY benefit from Qdenga vaccination.
          • Pre-vaccination serostatus screening is not required, but may be considered to inform the assessment of risks and benefits.
        • The benefits of vaccination are lower for seronegative travelers 
          • Any protection of seronegative persons against DENV3 and DENV4 is uncertain and the circulating serotypes at any destination may vary over time. 
        • Administration:
          • Protection starts 14 days after the first dose.
          • Do not reduce the interval between doses. 
          • A second dose that is delayed should be administered at the first available opportunity.
            • Good immunogenicity and safety data (not efficacy ) for a 0, 1 yr regimen (Lancet 2020, 395:1434).  
      • UK JCVI (ACIP equivalent) recommends to consider for seropositive but not seronegative travelers.
    • Dengvaxia (Sanofi Pasteur), is FDA approved for use in dengue endemic areas of US (e.g., Puerto Rico, Guam, USVI), only for persons 9-16 yrs of age but requires lab proof of prior infection and is not commercially available to travelers.
      • Little or no effect against DENV-2 
      • Has yet to be implemented in public health programs in Puerto Rico
      • In practice, is rarely used anymore in endemic areas due to need for pre-screening for dengue antibody
    • TV005 and TV003 (Butantan/NIAID in development), single dose live, attenuated tetravalent vaccine provides balanced sero-response to all 4 serotypes at 3 yrs in Bangladesh  (Lancet Infect Dis. 2023 Sep 27:S1473-3099(23)00520-0).
      • Phase 3 multicenter study in Brazil of a single dose of live-attenuated recombinant Butantan-DV (N Engl J Med 2024;390:397).
        • 80% overall 2-yr vaccine efficacy (VE) in subjects ages 2-60 against any symptomatic infection of any serotype
        • 73.6% in subjects with no evidence of previous dengue exposure (naïve)
        • 89% among dengue experienced subjects
        • 90% overall VE vs DENV-1 with a small drop to 86% in naïve subjects.
        • 70% VE against DENV-2 but only 57% in naïve subjects
      • No VE data on DENV-3 and DENV-4 infection as no cases in any study patients
  • No analysis of potentially confounding ZIKAV serostatus is presented.
  • A large-scale Zika outbreak in Brazil may have driven lower incidence rates of dengue, and as well accounted for the unexpected complete absence of severe dengue or hospitalization in vaccinees or placebo recipients in the 2016-19 timeframe of this trial.
  • Other studies have shown that A person with monotypic DENV immunity who has been infected with ZIKV converts to the immune status of a person who has been infected with two DENV serotypes. 

Comments

  • Rupatadine shortened symptoms and modestly reduced severity of illness in small clinical trial (Scientific Reports,8:3857, 2018)
  • Treatment with Colloids effective: 6% hydroxyethyl starch preferred in 1 study (N Engl J Med 353:9, 2005). Review in Semin Ped Infect Dis 16:60-5, 2005.
  • For more information: CDC Dengue Information; WHO Dengue Guidelines.
