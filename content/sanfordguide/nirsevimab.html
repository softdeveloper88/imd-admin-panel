Nirsevimab-alip
Tradename: Beyfortus

Usage and Dosing

  • Nirsevimab-alip is a monoclonal antibody that provides passive immunization against respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) by targeting the prefusion conformation of the fusion (F) glycoprotein.
  • Indicated for the prevention of RSV lower respiratory tract disease in neonates and infants born during or entering their first RSV season, and in children up to 24 months of age who remain vulnerable to severe RSV disease through their second RSV season.
  • Based on clinical data, the duration of protection offered by a single dose of nirsevimab-alip extends through 5 months.
  • May be given concomitantly with childhood vaccines in separate syringes and at different injection sites.
  • Palivizumab should not be administered to infants who have already received nirsevimab-alip in the same season. There are no data regarding substitution of nirsevimab-alip for palivizumab once prophylaxis treatment is initiated with palivizumab for the RSV season. Nirsevimab-alip may be administered prior to or during the second RSV season to children up to 24 months of age who remain vulnerable to severe RSV disease, and who received palivizumab in their first RSV season.
  • Product availability: Prefilled syringes, 50 mg/0.5 mL and 100 mg/mL.

Adult Dose

  • Safety and efficacy have not been established in children older than 24 months of age.

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days) Neonates, infants born during or entering their first RSV season:
Wt <5 kg: 50 mg IM x1
Wt ≥5 kg: 100 mg IM x1

Children up to 24 mon of age who remain vulnerable to severe disease through their second RSV season:
200 mg IM x1 (given as two 100 mg injections).

For children undergoing cardiac surgery with cardiopulmonary bypass, an additional dose is recommended when the child is stable after surgery:
-First RSV season: if surgery is within 90 days after receiving nirsevimab-alip, base additional dose on body weight. If >90 days have elapsed, the additional dose should be 50 mg.
-Second RSV season: if surgery is within 90 days after receiving nirsevimab-alip, the additional dose should be 200 mg. If >90 days have elapsed, the additional dose should be 100 mg.

Adverse Effects

  • Rash
  • Injection site reaction
  • Serious hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylaxis, have been observed with other human IgG monoclonal antibodies. If signs and symptoms of such a reaction occur, initiate appropriate medications and supportive therapy.
  • As with any IM injection, administer with caution to infants and children with thrombocytopenia, any coagulation disorder, or those receiving anticoagulation therapy.

Major Drug Interactions

  • Nirsevimab-alip does not interfere with RT-PCR or rapid antigen detection RSV diagnostic assays that employ commercially available antibodies targeting antigenic site I, II, or IV on the RSV F protein.
  • For immunological assay results which are negative when clinical observations are consistent with RSV infection, it is recommended to confirm using an RT-PCR-based assay.
