Gastroenteritis, Staph. aureus

Clinical Setting

  • Rare, mostly case reports.
  • Signs and symptoms of gastroenteritis; not to be confused with staphylococcal food poisoning, which is due to ingestion of preformed toxin
  • History of previous antibiotic therapy
  • Heavy growth of Staphylococcus aureus on stool culture
  • Negative work-up for Clostridioides difficile or other causes of gastroenteritis

Etiologies

  • Staphylococcus aureus

Primary Regimens

  • Vancomycin 1 gm IV q12h + 125 mg po q6h x10 days  (oral Vancomycin probably more critical)

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • The role of Staphylococcus aureus as a cause of colitis or gastroenteritis has been controversial with most case reports from Japan describing antibiotic associated enterocolitis due to MRSA (BMC Infect Dis 4:247 2014.)
  • A particularly convincing and well documented case of MRSA colitis can be found in Emerg Infect Dis  23:865, 2017.
