Spirillum minus

Clinical Setting

  • Spirillum minus and Streptococcus moniliformis are the two etiologies of rat bite fever. The bacteria are present in the oral, pharyngeal, and nasal flora of rats.
  • Spirillum minus causes rat bite fever primarily in Asia whereas Strep. moniliformis primarily results from rat bites in the USA.
  • Human infection usually results from a bite or scratch. Cases are reported wherein the exposure was handling a rat or ingestion of food or water that was contaminated with rodent feces.
  • Spirillum infection can present as fever, with or without ulceration at site of bite with regional lymphadenopathy, with onset 7-21 days after exposure; untreated, fever can recur.
  • Diagnosis usually requires demonstration of organism in smears of blood or material from wound.
  • Care for the bite wound to include cleansing and tetanus vaccine; rodents do not carry rabies.

Classification

  • Spirillum minus is a short, thick spirochete whereas Streptococcus moniliformis is a pleomorphic, branching Gram-negative bacillus.

Primary Regimens

  • Aqueous Penicillin G 1 million units IV q4-6h x 10-14 days
  • With improvement, could de-escalate to Amoxicillin 500 mg po tid

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg IV/po bid x 10-14 days
  • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h x 10-14 days

Comments

  • Reference: CDC Clinical care of rat bite fever.
