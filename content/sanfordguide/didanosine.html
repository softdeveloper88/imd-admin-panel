Didanosine
Tradename: Videx, Videx EC

Usage and Dosing

  • Didanosine (ddI) is not recommended by DHHS as initial therapy because of adverse reactions. Indicated as an alternative nucleoside as part of a 3-drug regimen, usually paired with another nucleoside agent (e.g., 3TC or FTC) and a NNRTI, a boosted PI, or a INSTI (Strand-transfer Integrase Inhibitor). Combinations of ddI + TDF generally not as effective (see below).
  • Pancreatitis, peripheral neuropathy, lactic acidosis & hepatic steatosis (rare but life threatening, especially combined with Stavudine in pregnancy). Retinal, optic nerve changes.
  • The combination Didanosine (ddI) + Tenofovir (TDF) is generally avoided, but if used, reduce dose of Didanosine-EC from 400 mg to 250 mg EC q24h (or from 250 mg EC to 200 mg EC for adults <60 kg). Monitor for ↑ toxicity & possible ↓ in efficacy of this combination; may result in ↓ CD4. Possibly associated with noncirrhotic portal hypertension.
  • Available generic formulations: 125 mg, 200 mg, 250 mg, and 400 mg delayed-release capsules.
  • See ARV Drugs, Overview for class-wide adverse effects.

Adult Dose

Didanosine Usual Dose
  • Wt ≥60 kg: Usually 400 mg enteric-coated (EC) po q24h 0.5 hr before or 2 hr after meal. Do not crush.
  • Wt <60 kg: 250 mg EC po q24h.

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Pancreatitis 1–9%. Black Box warning—Cases of fatal & nonfatal pancreatitis have occurred in patients receiving ddI, especially when used in combination with d4T or d4T + hydroxyurea.
    • Fatal lactic acidosis in pregnancy with ddI + d4T. Peripheral neuropathy in 20%, 12% required dose reduction. ↑ toxicity if used with Ribavirin. Rarely, retinal changes. Possible increased risk of MI under study ( J Infect Dis 201:318, 2010). Non-cirrhotic portal hypertension with ascites, varices, splenomegaly reported to the FDA in post-marketing surveillance.
  • Most Common:
    • Diarrhea 28%, nausea 6%, rash 9%, headache 7%, fever 12%, hyperuricemia 2%.
  • See ARV Drugs, Overview for class-wide adverse effects.
