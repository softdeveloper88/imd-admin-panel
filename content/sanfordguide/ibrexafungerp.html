Usage and Dosing

  • Ibrexafungerp is a triterpenoid antifungal indicated in adult and post-menarchal pediatric females for:
    • Treatment of vulvovaginal candidiasis (VVC).
    • Reduction in the incidence of recurrent vulvovaginal candidiasis (RVVC).
  • Works by inhibiting the biosynthesis of β-(1,3)-D-glucan, a key component of the fungal cell wall. This mechanism of action is like that of an echinocandin. However, ibrexafungerp and echinocandins do not share the same binding site on the target enzyme (β-(1,3)-D-glucan synthase), so limited cross-resistance is expected.
  • Pregnancy risk and lactation safety:
    • Contraindicated in pregnancy. May cause fetal harm, based on animal studies. Before beginning treatment, verify pregnancy status in females of reproductive potential. Reassessment of pregnancy status prior to each dose is recommended when ibrexafungerp is used monthly for 6 months for reduction in the incidence of RVVC.
    • For treatment of VVC, effective contraception should be used during treatment and for four days after second dose.
    • For reduction in the incidence of RVVC, effective contraception should be used throughout the 6-month treatment period, and for 4 days after the last dose.
    • See Table.
  • Ibrexafungerp is a CYP3A4 substrate. Moderate to strong CYP3A4 inducers may decrease its concentration. Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors may increase its concentration, but no dosage adjustment is warranted with weak to moderate CYP3A4 inhibitors. See chart below (not all-inclusive).
  • Ibrexafungerp inhibits certain CYP enzymes and transporters. Given the short treatment duration, the effect of ibrexafungerp on substrates of these enzymes and transporters is not considered clinically significant.

Adult Dose

  • Treatment of VVC:
    • 300 mg (two 150 mg tablets) po q12h x2 doses. Take with or without food.
  • Reduction in the incidence of recurrent VVC:
    • 300 mg (two 150 mg tablets) po q12h x2, repeated monthly x6 months. Take with or without food.
