Endocarditis, Gram-negative bacilli

Clinical Setting

  • Specific therapy for endocarditis with blood cultures positive for aerobic gram-negative bacilli.
  • Signs and symptoms of endocarditis. Patient often has a prosthetic valve.

Etiologies

  • Enterobacteriaceae
  • P. aeruginosa

Primary Regimens

  • (Cefepime 2 gm IV q8h or Meropenem 1 gm IV q8h) + Tobramycin 1.5-2 mg/kg IV q8h
  • Could substitute a fluoroquinolone for the aminoglycoside, see Comment

Alternative Regimens

  • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h + Tobramycin 1.5-2 mg/kg IV q8h

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Choice of agents based on in vitro susceptibilities: select definitive regimen to obtain optimal activity.
  • Infectious Diseases consultation recommended; antibiotic choice dictated by susceptibility data.
  • Optimal regimens and duration of therapy not well characterized;  6 weeks of therapy is reasonable.

Comments

  • Fluoroquinolone an option instead of aminoglycoside, but few data; Ann Intern Med. 2007;147:829–835.
  • A rare, often fatal disease with much uncertainty as to its management.
  • Medical therapy alone has a high failure rate, so early valve replacement should be considered.
  • References: Circulation. 2015;132:1435–1486 ; Infect Dis (Lond). 2023; 55:599.
