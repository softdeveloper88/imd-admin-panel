Cytomegalovirus in Transplant Recipients

Clinical Setting

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection is one of the most important viral infections in both solid organ transplant (SOT) recipients and hematopoietic stem cell transplant (HCT) recipients.  Traditionally in SOT recipients, disease onset was early post-transplant (first 100 days) but with the use of effective prophylaxis, disease is now often "late-onset" and occurs following the discontinuation of prophylaxis (reviewed in Transplantation 102:900, 2018). In HCT recipients disease tends to occur following engraftment up to 100 days post-transplant but can occur later depending on degree of immunosuppression.
  • Risk factors:
    • SOT: Seropositive donor (D+) and seronegative recipient (R-); Lymphocyte depleting antibody therapy (thymoglobulin, ATG, OKT-3, alemtuzumab)
    • HCT: Seronegative donor (D-) and seropositive recipient (R+); T-cell depleted or cord blood transplants; Graft versus host diseases (GVHD)
  • Clinical disease:
    • "CMV syndrome" (fever, leukopenia, and thrombocytopenia w/o other end-organ disease)
    • Gastrointestinal disease (colitis, esophagitis, enteritis)
    • Hepatitis
    • Pneumonitis
    • CNS disease (meningoencephalitis, myelitis)
    • Other (cystitis, retinitis, nephritis, etc.)

Diagnosis

  • Diagnostic strategies:
    • PCR (most common test, used on serum, CSF, tissue)
    • pp65 antigen (less commonly used, not recommended in neutropenic patients)
    • Culture (from tissue, lacks specificity)
    • Histopathology (gold standard to confirm end-organ disease)

Etiologies

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV)

Primary Regimens

  • Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV q12h OR
  • Valganciclovir 900 mg po q12h (Am J Transplant 7:2106, 2007)

Alternative Regimens

  • Maribavir 400 mg po q12
  • Foscarnet 90 mg/kg IV q12h
  • Cidofovir 5 mg/kg IV once weekly

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • What is the recommended duration of therapy? Treatment duration should be individualized. Continue treatment until (1) CMV PCR or antigenemia has become undetectable, (2) clinical evidence of disease has resolved, and (3) at least 2-3 weeks of treatment (Clin Transplant 33:e13512, 2019, NCCN Guidelines for Prevention and Treatment of Cancer-Related Infections, 2021).

Comments

  • When is IV Ganciclovir preferred over po Valganciclovir as first-line treatment? IV Ganciclovir may be preferred in patients with life-threatening disease, very high viral loads, and when there is concern for inadequate gastrointestinal absorption.
  • When to give secondary prophylaxis (maintenance therapy)? In SOT recipients, secondary prophylaxis (Valganciclovir 900 mg daily) is not recommended routinely but may be considered in patients recently treated with high-dose immunosuppression such as lymphocyte depleting antibodies, those with severe CMV disease, or those with >1 episode of CMV disease (see CMV Prevention SOT). In HST recipients, secondary prophylaxis should be considered in similar cases balancing the risk of recurrent infection with drug toxicity (see CMV Prevention HST).  Patient at high risk for relapse may do so again after stopping secondary prophylaxis (Clin Infect Dis, 65:2000, 2017)
  • When to worry about Ganciclovir resistance? Consider only if patient has received prolonged antiviral therapy and the viral load does not fall after 2 weeks of appropriate therapy. Other risk factors include high levels of immunosuppression and lung transplant. CMV genotype testing to the UL97 and UL54 genes is available and can confirm resistance. CMV UL97 mutants are susceptible to Foscarnet and Cidofovir (and high dose Ganciclovir with certain mutations). CMV UL54 mutations may result in resistance to Ganciclovir, Foscarnet, and Cidofovir and alternative treatment may be warranted (Clin Infect Dis 2019;68:1420).
  • When to use Maribavir?  Maribavir has been approved for the treatment of CMV disease resistant or refractory to treatment with ganciclovir, valganciclovir, foscarnet or cidofivir.  In a phase 3 randomized clinical trial, maribavir resulted in viral clearance in 56% of cases vs. only 24% in those assigned to other treatments (Clin Infect Dis 2021).  Maribavir is not active against HSV or VZV and if prophylaxis or treatment is needed against these viruses a second agent would be needed.
  • Is there a role for IVIg or CMV hyperimmune globulin (Cytogam)? These agents can be considered as adjunctive therapy in cases of life-threatening disease. In HCT recipients studies of both IVIg (Ann Intern Med 109:777, 1988) and CMV hyperimmune globulin (Ann Intern Med 109:783, 1988) added to Ganciclovir suggested benefit compared to historical controls. More recent studies have not supported the benefit of this treatment (Clin Infect Dis 61:31,2015)
