Arthritis, Septic, Staph. aureus

Clinical Setting

  • Documented septic arthritis, synovial fluid WBC > 25,000 (>50,000 more specific).
  • Acromioclavular or sacroiliac joint septic arthritis often due to Staph. aureus.
  • Gram-positive cocci in clusters on Gram stain and/or isolation of Staph. aureus in synovial fluid or blood culture.
  • Risk factors include rheumatoid arthritis, diabetes, injection drug use, skin infection.
  • For joint prosthesis see recommendations for Prosthetic Joint Infection.

Etiologies

  • Staphylococcus aureus

Primary Regimens

  • In addition to antimicrobial therapy drainage or debridement should always be performed by needle aspiration (repeated as needed), arthroscopy, or arthrotomy.
  • Specific antimicrobial therapy for initial therapy (see comments regarding duration): 
    • MRSA
      • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) x 2-3 weeks
    • MSSA
      • Nafcillin 1.5-2 gm IV q4h or Oxacillin 1.5-2 gm IV q4h x 2-3 weeks or Flucloxacillin 2 gm IV q6h x 2-3 weeks
      • Cefazolin 2 gm IV q8h x 2-3 weeks 

Alternative Regimens

  • MRSA:
    • Daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q24h
    • Linezolid 600 mg q12h po/IV
  • MSSA, patient with  type 1 hypersensitivity to beta-lactams or with significant adverse reaction
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) x 2-3 weeks 
    • Daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q24h
    • Linezolid 600 mg q12h po/IV 

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Possible oral regimens provided in vitro susceptibility is confirmed, include Clindamycin 600 mg po q8h OR ([TMP-SMX 4-5 mg/kg po (Trimethoprim component) bid OR Levofloxacin 750 mg po once daily] + Rifampin 600 mg po q24h), OR Linezolid 600 mg po q12h OR Cephalexin 500 mg po q6h

Comments

  • Consider treatment for 4 weeks in patients with septic arthritis complicated by bacteremia.
  • Reference: Curr Rheumatol Rep 15:332, 2013.
  • Retrospective study of Clindamycin and Cephalexin in children found either to be efficacious (Clin Microbiol Infect 18:582, 2012).
