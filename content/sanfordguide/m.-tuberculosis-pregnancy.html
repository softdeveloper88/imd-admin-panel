Tuberculosis, Pregnancy

Clinical Setting

  • Special considerations for treatment of M. tuberculosis during pregnancy (and post-partum).

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis

Primary Regimens

  • Expert consultation recommended for treatment of tuberculosis in pregnancy.
  • (Isoniazid + Rifampin + Ethambutol + Pyridoxine 50 mg qd) x 9 months
    • Pyrazinamide is recommended by WHO as part of the standard 4-drug regimen for treatment of tuberculosis in pregnancy.
      • ATS guidelines recommend  that clinicians evaluate the risks and benefits of prescribing Pyrazinamide on a case-by-case basis, allowing the patient to make an informed
        and educated decision (See ATS guidelines,  Clin Infect Dis 2016 Oct 1;63(7):e147-e195, for discussion of pros and cons).
      • Including Pyrazinamide in the treatment regimen is likely to be beneficial for pregnant women with tuberculosis and HIV, extrapulmonary or severe tuberculosis.
    • For dosing see First-line TB Drug Dosing Table.

Alternative Regimens

  • Expert consultation recommended for alternative regimens to the above because of intolerance or treatment of infection with drug-resistant organisms.

Comments

  • Because of potential ototoxicity to fetus throughout gestation (16%), Streptomycin, Amikacin, Capreomycin, Kanamycin, fluoroquinolones are contraindicated in pregnancy.
  • Breast-feeding need not be discouraged in patients on first-line TB drugs.
  • Reference: BMC Infect Dis 14:617, 2014.
