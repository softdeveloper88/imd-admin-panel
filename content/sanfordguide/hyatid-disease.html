Echinococcus

Clinical Setting

  • Slowly expanding cyst(s) over years to decades, large single to multiple hepatic and/or pulmonary cysts.
    • Hepatic is the most common.
    • Brain, cardiac, splenic, renal locations uncommonly occur.
    • Metastatic spread into body cavities with spontaneous or iatrogenic cyst rupture.
  • Asymptomatic until other structures, including biliary tree, bronchus, or pulmonary blood vessel compromised.
  • Complicated disease occurs after rupture with resultant infection or anaphylaxis, fistula development with adjacent structures (e.g., in the biliary tract, intestine, and bronchus)
  • Pulmonary involvement can produce chest pain, cough, and hemoptysis
  • Alveolar hydatid disease (E. multilocularis only) resembles malignancies in being locally invasive.
  • E. granulosus transmitted by ingestion of material contaminated with dog feces

Etiologies

  • Echinococcus granulosus (Hydatid disease)
    • several genotypes/strains now named including E. canadensis; poor consensus on nomenclature
  • Echinococcus multilocularis (alveolar cyst disease)
    • Mostly in Europe; rare but reported and may be emerging in US (Vermont; Alaska) and Canada: Clin Infect Dis, 2021; 72:1124 and N Engl J Med 2019, 381:384
  • Echinococcus vogeli & E. oligarthrus (polycystic disease)

Diagnosis

  • Imaging often diagnostic.  Ultrasound key part of classification scheme.
  • Sensitivity of serology varies widely according to location of cyst.  
    • Liver cyst 60-90% but cysts in the lungs, brain, and spleen <50%
    • False positives due to cross-reaction with other helminths; Western blot increases specificity.  
  • Needle aspiration of liver (not lung) feasible in expert hands to avoid anaphylaxis.

Primary Regimens

  • Hydatid disease (Clin Microbiol Rev 32:e00075, 2019; Curr Opin Infect Dis 31:383).  Both publications include image-based (ultrasound based) staging scheme followed by appropriate specific approach below.
  • Liver cysts
    • <5 cm CE1, CE3a cysts and possibly <5 cm CE2, CE3b cysts
      • Consider trial of albendazole 400 mg po bid for 3-6 months and assess response; 30-50% response rate. 
      • Albendazole softens cysts and may cause anaphylaxis due to slow leak of allergenic contents; may require treatment with long course of corticosteroids
    • Uncomplicated 5-10 cm CE1 and CE3a cysts (no internal daughter cysts):
      • Percutaneous aspiration-injection-reaspiration (PAIR) + Albendazole.
        • Anaphylaxis rate is 1.6% with PAIR.
        • Before & after drainage: Albendazole ≥60 kg, 400 mg po bid or <60 kg, 15 mg/kg/day divided bid, for a total of at least 30 days.  
        • Inject contrast after aspiration and before instilling scolocidal agent to ensure no biliary communication
    • CE2 and CE3b cysts have many compartments each one requiring individual puncture;  
      • Relapse common after PAIR which aims to destroy the germinal layer but doesn't not evacuate the cyst outside membranes.
      • An alternative approach consists of evacuating the entire cyst with a large-bore catheter.
        • This is generally done for management of these cysts that are difficult to drain or tend to relapse after PAIR,  (which may contain daughter cysts).
      • Use adjunctive Albendazole with both above approaches.
      • At present surgery likely preferable for these cysts.
    • CE2 and CE3b 5-10 cm and all viable cysts large than 10 cm.
      • Surgical resection (experienced surgeon only)
      • cover with Albendazole starting 1 week pre-operatively and for 4 weeks post-operatively
    • Complicated, multi vesicular, or infected cysts almost always require surgical intervention.
      • Large hepatic cysts >7.5 cm likely to have biliary communication and require surgery. 
    • Laparascopic and large bore laparoscopic approaches being further developed in endemic areas.
    • Watch and wait advised for asymptomatic or non-compromising cysts
    • No therapy for dead cysts (CE4 and CE5)
  • Pulmonary cysts
    • Surgical resection.
      • Avoid pre-operative Albendazole. May give Albendazole 400 mg po bid x at least 28 days post-operatively.
      • Ruptured or complicated cysts may require longer therapy. 
      • Never use PAIR for pulmonary or any extrahepatic cysts.
  • Disseminated disease:  Albendazole 400 mg po bid indefinitely until clinical response.  Periodic surgical debulking if feasible.
  • Alveolar cyst disease
    • Albendazole efficacy not clearly demonstrated, can try in dosages used for hydatid disease for inoperable cases. Wide surgical resection only reliable therapy; technique evolving (Am J Med 118:195, 2005; Acta Tropica 114:1, 2010).

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • No controlled trials at all; wide differences in opinion amongst experts.  Treatment indications for medical or surgical intervention vary with cyst characteristics on imaging, including cyst type, location, size, and complications. 
  • Post cyst treatment do CT/MRI q3 months until stable findings, monitor for at least 5 yrs.
  • Meta-analysis: BMC Infect Dis.18:306, 2018.
  • No clear guidelines for uncommon locations. 
    • Combination of Albendazole (especially for small cysts) and surgery per clinical situation.
  • Many cysts especially those with calcification (CE5) are not viable and require no intervention. Experienced radiologic consultation is important to assess cyst viability according to internationally accepted criteria.
  • Monitor liver function, CBC monthly with chronic Albendazole therapy.  May reinitiate after drug holiday in those with increased LFTs.
  • Albendazole/Praziquantel combination therapy for spontaneous or intraoperative cyst ruptures Acta Tropica 114:1, 2010.
  • Albendazole/Praziquantel combination therapy currently in clinical trials for treatment of intact cysts.  Very limited data from earlier studies to support combination therapy in non-operative candidates (BMC Infect Dis  2018, 18:306).
  • Hydatid and alveolar disease key references: Clin Microbiol Rev 32:e00075, 2019. 
  • Albendazole 200 mg capsules available from Expert Compounding Pharmacy (see Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources). 

    • Albendazole (Albenza) marketed by Amneal is very costly and is difficult for retail pharmacies to access.
