HBV Prevention, Transplant

Clinical Setting

  • Hepatitis B virus (HBV) can reactivate in patients receiving immunosuppressive therapy, even those who have previously cleared HBsAg and have HBsAb. Reactivation following immunosuppressive therapy including solid organ transplantation can result in significant morbidity and mortality. Recurrence of HBV infection following liver transplant in infected recipients can be prevented. Please see HBV Reactivation for discussion on prevention of reactivation for patients with hematological malignancy, stem cell transplant, and other types of immunosuppression.
  • Recommended screening tests: HBsAg, Anti-HBs, and Anti-HBc. Latest screening guidelines: Ann Intern Med 161:58, 2014.

Etiologies

  • Hepatitis B virus (HBV)

Primary Regimens

  • Hepatic transplant recipients (HBV infected)
    • Recipient HBsAg+: HBIg (see below) + Entecavir 0.5 mg daily
    • Recipient HBsAg-/HBcAb+/HBsAb+/-: Monitor q1-3 mo HBV DNA
  • Hepatic transplant recipients (HBV uninfected)
    • Donor HBsAg+: Transplant generally contraindicated
    • Donor HBsAg-/HBcAb+/HBsAb+/-: Entecavir 0.5 mg daily if recipient HBsAb-; Consider monitoring for loss of HBsAb, in HBsAb+ recipients
  • Non-hepatic transplant recipients (HBV infected)
    • Recipient HBsAg+: HBIG (see below) + Entecavir 0.5 mg daily
    • Recipient HBsAg-/HBcAb+/HBsAb+/-: Monitor q1-3 mo HBV DNA, LFTs, HBsAg
  • Non-hepatic transplant recipients (HBV uninfected)
    • Donor HBsAg+: Transplant generally contraindicated
    • Donor HBsAg-/HBcAb+/HBsAb+/-: Entecavir 0.5 mg daily if recipient HBsAb-; Consider monitoring for loss of HBsAb, in HBsAb+ recipients

Alternative Regimens

  • Tenofovir 300 mg po daily (particularly if on Lamivudine pre-transplant)
  • Emtricitabine/tenofovir 200/300mg daily (if on pre-transplant)

Comments

  • HBIg should be used in combination with antiviral therapy in HBV infected recipients with HBV DNA > 20,000 or HBeAg+; known drug-resistant HBV, high-risk for HCC recurrence, HDV or HIV coinfection. Consider antiviral monotherapy (without HBIg) if undetectable or very low HBV DNA pre-transplant. Use of IV vs. IM HBIg and duration should be determined on a case by case basis (Therap Adv Gastroenterol 6:309, 2013).
  • Vaccination for HBV should be considered in all recipients HBsAg-, HBcAb+, HBsAb-.
  • References: Therap Adv Gastroenterol 6:309, 2013; Clin Transplant 33: e13514, 2019; JAMA 312:2505, 2014; JAMA 312:2521, 2014.
