Delamanid
Tradename: Deltyba

Usage and Dosing

  • Delamanid is an anti-tuberculosis drug approved in Europe.
  • Use only in combination with other active agents for treatment of pulmonary tuberculosis due to MDR (multiple drug-resistant) strains.
  • Inhibits the synthesis of the mycobacterial cell wall components methoxymycolic acid and ketomycolic acid. Primarily metabolized in plasma by albumin and to a lesser extent by CYP3A4. Major metabolite is DM-6705.
  • Child-friendly 25 mg dispersible tablets were approved by EMA in 2021 but are not widely available. 50 mg tablets dispersed in water and sugar-syrup are palatable and have similar bioavailability to tablets swallowed whole (Br J Clin Pharmacol 2023 Jan 24 doi: 10.1111/bcp.15672 [ahead of print]).
  • Use in pregnancy and lactation:
    • No human data, toxic in animals.
    • See Table.

Adverse Effects

  • QTc prolongation which increases slowly  in the first 6-10 weeks of treatment and remains stable thereafter; closely correlated with the major metabolite DM-6705.
    • Check EKG at baseline and monthly thereafter
    • Contraindicated for use in patients with QTc > 500 ms
    • Contraindicated for use in patients with serum albumin < 2.8 g/dL.
  • Concomitant use of fluoroquinolones may further increase QTc prolongation and close monitoring is indicated.
    • Co-administration of delamanid and Moxifloxacin has not been studied and is not recommended.
  • Some of the other more common side effects include anemia, reticulocytosis, diarrhea, nausea, vomiting, arthralgias and myalgias.

Comments

  • Treatment with delamanid should not be initiated in patients with the  risk factors for QTc prolongation (e.g., congenital prolongation of QTc, concomitant macrolide therapy, treatment with certain antiarrhythmics, cisapride and other agents known to prolong QTc) unless the possible benefit of delamanid is considered to outweigh the potential risks. Such patients should receive frequent monitoring of ECG throughout the full delamanid treatment period.
  • Efficacy unknown in patients with extrapulmonary tuberculosis.
  • Update on resistance: Clin Infect Dis. 2020;71:3252
