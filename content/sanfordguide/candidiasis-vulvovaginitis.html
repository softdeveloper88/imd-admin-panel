Candidiasis, Vulvovaginitis

Clinical Setting

  • Vaginal or vulvar irritation, burning, or pruritus.
  • Cheesy, white discharge with white plaques on vaginal mucosa.
  • Swelling or erythema of the perineum.

Diagnosis

  • Can be confirmed with wet preparation (saline, 10% KOH) or culture.

Etiologies

  • Candida albicans most common but other Candida sp. can cause disease.

Primary Regimens

  • Uncomplicated:
    • Topical azole therapy:
      • Butoconazole 2% cream (5 gm) q24h at bedtime x 3 days or 2% cream SR 5 gm x 1
      • Clotrimazole 100 mg vaginal tabs (2 at bedtime x 3 days) or 1% cream (5 gm) at bedtime x 7 days (14 days may ↑ cure rate) or 100 mg vaginal tab x 7 days or 500 mg vaginal tab x 1
      • Miconazole 200 mg vaginal suppository (1 at bedtime x 3 days) or 100 mg vaginal suppository q24h x 7 days or 2% cream (5 gm) at bedtime x 7 days
      • Terconazole 80 mg vaginal tab (1 at bedtime x 3 days) or 0.4% cream (5 gm) at bedtime x 7 days or 0.8% cream 5 gm intravaginal q24h x 3 days
      • Tioconazole 6.5% vaginal ointment x 1 dose
    • Fluconazole 150 mg po x 1 dose
    • Ibrexafungerp 300 mg (2 tablets 150 mg each) po, with or without food, q12h x 2 doses for one day
  • Complicated (immunocompromised, recurrence, severe disease, or non-C. albicans species):
    • Topical azole therapy as above for ≥ 7 days
    • Fluconazole 150 mg po q72h x 2-3 doses
  • Recurrent vulvovaginal candidiasis:
    • Oteseconazole: two recommended regimens: oteseconazole only, and otesconazole/fluconazole (fluconazole is not supplied in the carton)
      • Oteseconazole-only:
        • Day 1: Oteseconazole 600 mg po (single dose)
        • Day 2: Oteseconazole 450 mg po (single dose)
        • Starting Day 14: Oteseconazole 150 mg po once weekly for 11 weeks
      • Otesconazole/fluconazole:
        • Day 1, 4, 7: Fluconazole 150 mg po
        • Day 14 through 20: Oteseconazole 150 mg po once daily for 7 days
        • Starting Day 28: Oteseconazole 150 mg po once weekly for 11 weeks

Alternative Regimens

  • Treatment for Candida glabrata infection:
    • Topical Flucytosine cream 17% alone or in combination with Amphotericin B cream (3%) intravaginal daily x 14 days
    • Topical boric acid, gelatin capsule 600 mg intravaginal daily x 14 days

Comments

  • Recurrent vulvovaginal candidiasis: consider induction therapy with 10–14 days of topical or oral azole, followed by Fluconazole 150 mg once weekly for 6 months.
  • Single dose therapy (Fluconazole 150 mg po) during the first-trimester of pregnancy may be associated with miscarriage (FDA Drug Safety Communication)
