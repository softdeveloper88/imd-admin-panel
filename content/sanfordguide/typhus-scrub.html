Typhus, Scrub

Clinical Setting

  • Mite-borne rickettsial infection.
  • Patient from Asian rim of the Pacific with fever, headache and myalgia; exposure to chiggers (mites).
    • Manifests in India as an acute encephalitis (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 2018;12:e0006346)
    • Macular rash in 50%
    • Majority have low platelet counts
  • Review: Curr Opin Infect Dis 2020;33:365.

Etiologies

  • Orientia tsutsugamushi
    • Formerly Ricketsiae tsutsugamushi

Diagnosis

  • Confirm diagnosis with serology and increasingly by PCR

Primary Regimens

  • For severe infection in adults, e.g., ARDS, hepatitis, hypotension/shock, renal failure (usually hospitalized):
    • Combination IV therapy:
      • Doxycycline 200  mg IV bid on day 1 and then 100 mg IV bid x 6 days + Azithromycin 500 mg IV bid on day 1 and then 500 mg IV daily x 6 days
      • Based on prospective randomized controlled trial in non-pregnant adults: New Engl J Med. 2023; 388:792
  • For less severe infections:
    • Doxycycline 100 mg po/IV bid x 7 days (See Comments)

Alternative Regimens

  • Chloramphenicol 500 mg po/IV qid x 7 days
  • Pregnancy: Azithromycin 500 mg po/IV x 1 dose (Clin Infect Dis 39:1329, 2004).
  • If suspected Doxycycline resistance:
    • Rifampin 600 mg po once daily x 5 days (See Comments)
    • Azithromycin 500 mg po once daily x 5 days (arbitrary regimen)

Comments

  • Pediatric use of doxycycline
    • Use of tetracyclines in pediatric patients has historically been limited because of reports of permanent tooth discoloration in children age <8 years caused by incorporation of the drugs and their colored degradation products in enamel.
      • Doxycycline binds less readily to calcium compared with other members of the tetracycline class, and recent comparative data suggest that doxycycline is not likely to cause visible teeth staining or enamel hypoplasia in children <8 years of age.
    • Doxycycline can safely be administered for up to 21 days regardless of age (AAP Red Book 2018, Section 4; J Pediatr 166:1246, 2015)
  • In open label randomized controlled trial (Clin Infect Dis 2018, 67:600) equivalent safety and efficacy with:
    • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x 5 days and
    • Rifampin 600 mg po once daily x 5 days
  • No evidence of resistance to doxycycline: Clin Infect Dis 2020;70:2444..
