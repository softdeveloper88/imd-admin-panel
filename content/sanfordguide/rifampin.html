Rifampin
Tradename: Rifadin, Rimactane, Rifocin, Rifamate, Rifater

Usage and Dosing

  • Rifampin is used clinically in the management of infections due to mycobacteria, Staph. aureus, Neisseria sp., Legionella sp. and other organisms.
  • Rifampin is often used in combination therapy both to achieve additive or synergistic antibacterial efficacy and to reduce the risk of development of resistance.
  • Rifampin is one of the Rifamycin class of antibiotics (Rifampin, Rifabutin, Rifapentine, Rifaximin). All drugs in the class have significant and important drug-drug interactions.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see list below, not all-inclusive).

Adult Dose

Active M. tuberculosisRifampin 10 mg/kg/day po once daily (max 600 mg/day) (IV form is also available from Ben Venue Laboratories)
 Rifamate Combination (300 mg Rifampin / 150 mg Isoniazid). Dose as 2 tabs once daily
 Rifater Combination (50 mg Isoniazid / 120 mg Rifampin / 300 mg Pyrazinamide). Dose for wt >55 kg: 6 tabs once daily
Non-tuberculous mycobacterial infections, e.g., M. kansasiiRifampin is used as part of multi-drug therapy: 10 mg/kg/day po once daily
M. leprae (Leprosy)Multibacillary (Lepromatous): (Rifampin 600 mg + Clofazimine 50 mg + Dapsone 100 mg) po once daily
 Paucibacillary (Tuberculoid): (Rifampin 600 mg + Dapsone 100 mg) po once daily
 Single lesion: (Rifampin 600 mg + Ofloxacin 400 mg + Minocycline 100 mg) po x one dose (Lancet 353:655, 1999)
Staph. aureus (including attempts to eradicate nasal carriage)Rifampin 600 mg po once daily (always in combination with other drugs). For risk benefit of adding Rifampin to standard therapy for the treatment of Staph. aureus native valve endocarditis: AAC 52:2463, 2008
H. influenzae meningitis prophylaxisRifampin 600 mg po once daily x 4 days
N. meningitidis meningitis prophylaxis600 mg po q12h x 2 days
  • Used as part of combination therapy for the treatment of brucellosis.

    Adverse Effects

    Adverse Effects

    • Potentially hepatotoxic. Use with caution in patients with impaired liver function or in patients with history of, or ongoing, excessive use of alcohol.
    • If elevated bilirubin and/or substantive increase in liver-associated enzymes occurs, discontinue Rifampin therapy.
    • Many potential important drug-drug interactions.
    • Flu-like syndrome described with intermittent dosing regimens or intermittent non-compliance.
    • Red-orange discoloration of urine, feces, saliva, sweat, tears and CSF. Can permanently stain contact lenses.
    • Thrombocytopenia and vasculitis can occur (Ann Pharmacother 42:727, 2008). Documentation of drug-induced immune thrombocytopenia (J Thrombosis & Haemostasis 11:169, 2012)
    • Can cause interstitial nephritis.

    Pregnancy Risk

    FDA Risk Category 1 Humans: No well-controlled studies, does not appear to be teratogenic.
    Animals: Teratogenic in rodents, embryotoxic in rabbits.
    Use during Lactation Probably safe, monitor infant for toxicity. Breast milk may be stained a yellow, orange, red, or brown color.
