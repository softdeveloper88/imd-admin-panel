Gastroenteritis, Yersinia enterocolitica

Clinical Setting

  • Specific therapy for Y. enterocolitica gastroenteritis
  • Common clinical presentations include diarrhea, abdominal pain, nausea & vomiting 
  • Fever in 68%, bloody stools in 26%

Diagnosis

  • Laboratory diagnosis difficult: requires “cold enrichment” and/or Yersinia selective agar.
  • Diagnostic yield higher with gastrointestinal multiplex PCR panels Open Forum Infect Dis 6:ofz116 2019

Etiologies

  • Yersinia enterocolitica

Primary Regimens

  • No treatment recommended except for sepsis or severe disease.
  • If severe, combine Ceftriaxone 2 g IV q24h + [(Tobramycin or Gentamicin) 5 mg/kg/day once IV q24h].

Alternative Regimens

  • TMP-SMX
  • Fluoroquinolones
  • Doxycycline

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy unclear:  2 weeks in less severe disease, 3 weeks for more severe disease.

Comments

  • Mesenteric adenitis pain can mimic acute appendicitis or terminal ileitis.
  • Reactive arthritis and erythema nodosum reported after infection.
  • Iron overload states and Deferoxamine chelator therapy may predispose a person to invasive Yersinia disease.
  • Review: BMC Infect Dis 13:236, 2013.
