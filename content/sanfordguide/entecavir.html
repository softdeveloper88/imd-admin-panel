Entecavir
Tradename: Baraclude

Usage and Dosing

  • Entecavir is indicated for the treatment of Hepatitis B infections. Warning: Must screen for HIV prior to use. Entecavir used as mono-therapy for HBV in a patient with HIV co-infection can lead to HIV-cross resistance to Lamivudine (3TC) and Emtricitabine (FTC).
  • Entecavir is a nucleoside analog active against Hepatitis B (HBV) including Lamivudine-resistant mutants.
  • Potential for lactic acidosis and exacerbation of hepatitis B at discontinuation (Black Box warning).
  • Allergy: see protocol for Entecavir desensitization.

Adverse Effects

  • Minimal adverse effects reported: headache, fatigue, dizziness, nausea reported in 22% of patients.
  • Alopecia, anaphylactoid reactions reported.

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Human data insufficient.
Animals: No developmental toxicities observed at systemic exposures 25x higher in rats and 200x higher in rabbits (relative to exposure at human dosage).
Use during Lactation Safety not established, avoid use if possible
