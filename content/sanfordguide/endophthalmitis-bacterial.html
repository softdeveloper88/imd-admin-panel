Endophthalmitis, Hematogenous or Endogenous

Clinical Setting

  • Vision loss (90%), eye pain (~50%), fever (~40%).
  • Haziness of vitreous humor, hypopyon.
  • Various associated settings: endocarditis, meningitis, primary bacteremia, secondary bacteremia (i.e., Klebsiella liver abscess), intravenous drug use.
  • Candidemia (See Candida endophthalmitis).
  • Prior to therapy, culture needle aspirate of both vitreous and aqueous humor.
  • Consultation with / referral to ophthalmologist imperative

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus pneumoniae or other streptococci
  • Neisseria meningitidis
  • Staphylococcus aureus
  • Klebsiella pnemoniae or other gram-negative organisms
  • Candida sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Empiric intravitreal antibiotics (e.g., Vancomycin 1 mg x 1 in 0.1 mL+ Ceftazidime 2.25 mg in 0.1 mL x 1) + systemic antibiotics: (Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q4h (where available outside the US) or Ceftazidime 2 gm IV q8h) + Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) pending cultures.
  • Adjust antibiotics after isolation of pathogen and determination of susceptibility: repeat intravitreal injection indicated at 48h if no improvement or worsening of intraocular inflammation.

Alternative Regimens

  • Intravitreal Amphotericin B 10 µg in 0.1 mL + Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg IV daily or Voriconazole 100 µg in 0.1 mL PLUS
    • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg IV q12h x2 doses, then 4 mg/kg IV q12h for suspected fungal endophthalmitis
  • See Endophthalmitis Candida for treatment of that infection

Comments

  • Urgent consultation with ophthalmologist imperative.
  • Blood, vitreous, and aqueous cultures for bacteria and fungi should be performed.
  • Intravitreal administration of antimicrobials is essential.
