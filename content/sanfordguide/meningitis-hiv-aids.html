Meningitis, HIV/AIDS

Clinical Setting

  • Meningitis in the HIV-positive or AIDS patient.
  • Presents as headache, fever, lethargy with or without nuchal rigidity.
  • Acute presentation favors bacterial etiology. Subacute or chronic clinical course suggests cryptococcal or tuberculous meningitis.
  • Meningitis can occur in Acute HIV seroconversion syndrome.  (Antiretroviral Rx is indicated) 
  • Broad differential diagnosis, see Meningitis, Overview.

Etiologies

  • Neisseria meningitidis
  • Streptococcal pneumoniae
  • Listeria monocytogenes
  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis
  • Cryptococcus
  • Coccidioidomycosis
  • Histoplasmosis
  • Talaromycosis
  • Neurosyphilis
  • Aseptic Meningitis
    • HIV (acute seroconversion)
    • Enterovirus
    • HSV / Mollaret's
