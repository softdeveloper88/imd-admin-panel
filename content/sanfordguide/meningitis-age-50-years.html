Meningitis, Age > 50 years

Clinical Setting

  • Empiric therapy for bacterial meningitis, pending results of Gram stain and culture.
  • Age > 50 years or co-morbidities.

Diagnosis

  • PCR performed on CSF may establish diagnosis in culture-negative cases.

Etiology

  • Neisseria meningitidis (~50%)
  • Strep. pneumoniae (~40%)
  • Listeria monocytogenes (~5%)
  • Other 

Primary Regimens

  • (Ampicillin 2 gm IV q4h) + (Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q6h (where available outside the US)) + Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h + Dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg IV q6h x 2–4 days (1st dose 15–20 min. prior to or concomitant with 1st dose of antibiotic)

Alternative Regimens

  • For patient with severe penicillin allergy precluding empirical use of any beta-lactam:
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h + Aztreonam 2 gm IV q6h + TMP-SMX 5 mg/kg q6–8h.
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h + (Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q8h) + TMP-SMX 5 mg/kg q6–8h.

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Tailor regimen based on results of culture and susceptibility testing.
  • N. meningitidis: Susceptibility testing recommended before switching from empiric ceftriaxone or cefotaxime to penicillin or ampicillin: reported U.S. increase in incidence of penicillin and/or ciprofloxacin resistance (MMWR 69:735, June 19, 2020).

Comments

  • Prior antimicrobial therapy reduces recovery of organisms on culture from ~85% to ~65% of cases
  • Discontinue dexamethasone if pathogen isolated is other than S. pneumoniae (see Cochrane Database Syst Rev.2015 Sep 12;9:CD00440 for meta-analysis on benefits of corticosteroids).
  • If Vancomycin is used, target trough serum concentrations of 15-20 μg/mL.
  • References:  JAMA. 2022; 328:2147 and Neurol Clin. 2022; 40:77.
