Pneumonia, Streptococcus pneumoniae

Clinical Setting

  • Specific therapy for S. pneumoniae.
  • Blood or sputum culture positive for S. pneumoniae or positive urinary antigen or positive nucleic acid amplification test (NAAT). 

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus pneumoniae

Primary Regimens

  • For penicillin-susceptible organism (MIC ≤2 μg/mL):
    • IV regimens:
      • Penicillin G 2 million U IV q4h
      • Ceftriaxone 2gm IV q24h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q8h (where available)
    • Oral regimens:
      • Amoxicillin 1 gm po tid
      • Penicillin-allergic patient:
        • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid (document susceptibility especially if the strain is intermediate or resistant to penicillin)
  • For penicillin-resistant or intermediate organism (MIC >2 μg/mL)
    • Ceftriaxone 2gm IV q24h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q8h (where available)
    • Ceftaroline 600 mg IV q12h
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg q8-12h (may be preferred if penicillin MIC >4 μg/mL)

Alternative Therapy

  • Penicillin susceptible, intermediate, or resistant strains (high level):
    • Levofloxacin 750 mg po/IV q24h or Moxifloxacin 400 mg po/IV
    • Linezolid 600 mg po/IV q12h
    • Clindamycin 600 mg IV q8h (document susceptibility if strain is intermediate or resistant to penicillin)

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Treat until patient is afebrile, 3-5 days (minimum of 5 days therapy). Because of increasing resistance, confirm susceptibility if a macrolide is used.
  • Increasing resistance to beta-lactams and macrolides, particularly to the latter in Europe (Curr Opin Pulm Med 19:209, 2013).
  • For current CDC recommendations for pneumococcal vaccines click here. 
  • For further details on vaccination see: Pneumococcal Vaccines, Adult

Comments

  • Use of adjunctive corticosteroids limited to severe CAP. See Comments, Community-acquired Pneumonia for definition of severe CAP and dosing.
  • An observational study (Clin Infect Dis 2022; 75:2219) found that in patients with pneumonia and pneumococcal bacteremia the addition for as few as two days of azithromycin to a beta-lactam reduced the odds for mortality by 45%. 
  • Pneumococcus is identified in 10-15% of the patients with pneumonia. As the etiologic prevalence of Strep. pneumoniae has deceased over the years the frequency of respiratory viruses (25%) has increased (Clin Infect Dis 2017; 65:1736).
