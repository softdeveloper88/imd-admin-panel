Atovaquone-Proguanil
Tradename: Malarone

Usage and Dosing

  • Atovaquone-Proguanil (Malarone) is used clinically in the treatment and prophylaxis of malaria.
  • Drug content per tablet:
    • Adult-strength: Atovaquone 250 mg, Proguanil 100 mg
    • Pediatric-strength: Atovaquone 62.5 mg, Proguanil 25 mg
  • Does not kill hypnozoites of P. vivax or P. ovale so late relapses may occur even in those taking appropriate prophylaxis who do not have a primary episode of clinical malaria.
  • Atovaquone is a selective inhibitor of parasite mitochondrial electron transport.
  • Proguanil hydrochloride primarily exerts its effect by means of the metabolite cycloguanil, a dihydrofolate reductase inhibitor, formed via CYP2C19.
  • Review for treatment of P. falciparum Cochrane Database Syst Rev 15;1(1):CD004529, 2021.
  • Abbreviated post-exposure prophylaxis regimens not supported by any authority (Travel Med Infect Dis 21:3, 2018).

Adult Dose

Malaria, prevention, adults:One adult-strength tablet per day, starting 1-2 days prior to, during, and 7 days post-exposure in a malaria risk area. Take with food.
Malaria, treatment alternative for uncomplicated Plasmodium falciparum:Four adult-strength tablets once daily x3 days. Take with food.

Adverse Effects

  • Adverse effects in treatment trials:
    • Adults: abdominal pain 17%, nausea/vomiting 12%, headache 10%, dizziness 5%. Treatment stopped in 1%. Asymptomatic mild increase in ALT/AST.
    • Children: cough, headache, anorexia, vomiting, abdominal pain.
