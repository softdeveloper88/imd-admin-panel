Retinitis, Cytomegalovirus

Clinical Setting

  • CMV retinitis is the most common cause of blindness in AIDS patients with CD4 counts of <50/mm3.
  • Differential diagnosis:
    • HIV retinopathy
    • Herpes simplex retinitis
    • Varicella zoster retinitis (rare, hard to diagnose)
  • Watch out for IRIS (immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome) associated with initiation of ARV therapy
  • Corticosteroid therapy reduced inflammatory reaction of immune recovery vitreitis without reactivation of CMV retinitis, either periocular corticosteroids or short course of systemic steroid.

Etiologies

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV)

Primary Regimens

  • Immediate sight-threatening lesions:  (Intravitreal injections of Ganciclovir (2 mg/ injection) or Foscarnet (2.4 mg/ injection) for 1 - 4 doses over a period of 7 -10 days) + Valganciclovir 900 mg po bid x 14 - 21 days; then 900 mg po once daily.  (Note: Ganciclovir intra-ocular implants are no longer manufactured).
  • Peripheral lesions: Valganciclovir 900 mg po q12h x 14–21 days, then 900 mg po q24h for maintenance therapy
  • Post-treatment suppression (CD4 count<100): Valganciclovir 900 mg po q24h. Discontinue once CD4 count >100 for 6 months.

Alternative Regimens

  • Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV q12h x 14–21 days, then Valganciclovir 900 mg po q24h
  • Foscarnet 60 mg/kg IV q8h or 90 mg/kg IV q12h x 14–21 days, then 90–120 mg/kg IV q24h OR
  • Cidofovir 5 mg/kg IV x 2 weeks, then 5 mg/kg every other week (each dose should be administered with IV saline hydration and oral probenecid) OR
  • For relapses only: repeated intravitreal injections with Fomivirsen

Comments

  • Oral Valganciclovir equal to IV Ganciclovir in induction of remission: (N Engl J Med 346:1119, 2002).
  • Because of unique mode of action, Fomivirsen may have a role if isolates become resistant to other therapies.
  • Retinal detachments 50–60% within 1 year of diagnosis of retinitis (Ophthal 111:2232, 2004).
  • Ganciclovir/Foscarnet/Valganciclovir:
    • Equal efficacy of IV Ganciclovir and Foscarnet.
    • Ganciclovir avoids the nephrotoxicity of Foscarnet.
    • Foscarnet avoids bone marrow suppression of Ganciclovir; but Foscarnet bone marrow toxicity may be similar to Ganciclovir.
    • Oral Valganciclovir should replace both.
