Bite: Prairie Dog

Clinical Setting

  • Bite wound from Prairie dog.

Etiologies

  • Monkey pox virus

Primary Regimens

  • No therapy recommended

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • See also, Mpox.
  • Administer tetanus toxoid or Tdap (preferred) if > 10 years since last dose.
  • A pediatric case of tularemia has been reported following a prairie dog bite (Clin Microbiol Rev 24: 231, 2011).
  • Rabies virus very rarely identified in this species (J Infect Dis 126:565, 1972). During 2010, there was no instance among > 6,000 rabid animals in the US (J Amer Vet Med Assn 239:773, 2011).
  • Tularemia has been reported after prairie dog bite (Clin Microbiol Rev 24: 231, 2011)
  • Bubonic plague has been reported after bite of Gunnison's prairie dog (Zoonoses Public Health 65: e254, 2018)
