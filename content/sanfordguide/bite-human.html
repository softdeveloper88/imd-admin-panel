Bite: Human

Clinical Setting

  • Cleaning, irrigation and debridement are most important.
  • For clenched fist or hand injuries, x-rays should be obtained.
  • For bites inflicted by hospitalized patients, consider aerobic Gram-negative bacilli.

Etiologies

  • Streptococci
  • Staphylococcus aureus
  • Eikenella corrodens
  • Fusobacterium sp.
  • Peptostreptococcus sp. (Peptoniphilus sp.)
  • Prevotella sp.
  • Porphyromonas sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Early (wound not yet infected):
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po bid x 5 days
  • Later (signs of infection, usually 3-24 hours):
    • Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 gm IV q6h or Cefoxitin 2 gm IV q8h OR
    • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h
    • For serious infections, until MRSA is excluded, add Vancomycin 1 gm IV q12h (or 1.5 gm q12h if wt >100 kg) or Daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q24h

Alternative Regimens

  • A carbapenem can be used in place of Ampicillin-sulbactam, Cefoxitin, or Piperacillin-tazobactam if parenteral therapy is required
  • If patient is Penicillin allergic: Clindamycin + (Ciprofloxacin or Levofloxacin or Moxifloxacin).
  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 1000 mg-200 mg IV q8h (non-US)

Comments

  • For a review of bacteriology of human bite wounds, see Clin Infect Dis 37:1481, 2003; treatment see https://www.idsociety.org/practice-guideline/skin-and-soft-tissue-infections/.
  • Eikenella spp.
    • Susceptible to: Fluoroquinolones and Beta-Lactam / Beta-lactamase inhibitor combinations: e.g., Ampicillin-sulbactam.
    • Resistant to: Clindamycin, Nafcillin/Oxacillin, Metronidazole, Cephalexin/Cefazolin, TMP/SMX, Aminoglycosides and Erythromycin.
    • Recommend treatment with Amoxicillin-clavulanate, Ampicillin-sulbactam, or Ertapenem; if allergic/intolerant to B-lactam, treat with Fluoroquinolone monotherapy 
  • Clenched fist (and other hand) bite wounds pose risk for deep infections (e.g., bone,  joint, tendon sheath) and require careful evaluation.
    • X-ray to evaluate for fracture or foreign body.
    • Potential risk of transmitting blood-borne pathogens if injury contaminated with another's blood.
  • Review tetanus status
