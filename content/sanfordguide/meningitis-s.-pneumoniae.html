Meningitis, Streptococcus pneumoniae

Clinical Setting

  • Specific therapy for bacterial meningitis caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae in adults.
  • Fever, headache, altered mental status, meningeal signs, CSF pleocytosis, positive CSF and/or blood cultures.
  • Positive Gram-stain in 50%.
  • Treatment recommendations assume the following:
    • All regimens include Dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg IV q6h; first dose is given 15-20 minutes prior to first antibiotic dose, and then continued for 4 days
    • If Penicillin (or third-generation cephalosporin) MIC ≥ 1 μg/mL, CSF exam should be repeated after 24-48 hours to document response

Diagnosis

  • Real-time PCR is highly accurate in diagnosing meninigitis caused by S. pneumoniae, including culture-negative cases (BMC Infect Dis 13:26, 2013).

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus pneumoniae

Primary Regimens

  • Initial empiric regimen pending susceptibility test results
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8h (15-20 μg/mL trough target) + (Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q4–6h)(where available outside the US) 
  • Penicillin susceptibility known
    • Penicillin G MIC < 0.1 μg/mL:
      • Penicillin G 4 million units IV q4h or Ampicillin 2 gm IV q4h
    • Penicillin G MIC > 0.1 μg/mL and Ceftriaxone MIC < 0.5 μg/mL:
      • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q4–6h (where available outside the US) 
    • Penicillin G MIC ≥ 0.1 μg/mL and Ceftriaxone MIC > 0.5 
      • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8h (15-20 μg/mL trough target) + (Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q4–6h)(where available outside the US) 
        • If Ceftriaxone MIC > 2 μg/mL, add Rifampin 600 mg po/IV to above

Alternative Regimens

  • Initial empiric regimen, serious cephalosporin allergy, pending susceptibility test results
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8h (15-20 μg/mL trough target) +  Moxifloxacin 400 mg IV q24h
  • Penicillin susceptibility known
    • Penicillin G MIC < 0.1 μg/mL:
      • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h 
    • Penicillin G MIC > 0.1 and Ceftriaxone MIC < 0.5 μg/mL:
      • Cefepime 2 gm IV q8h or Meropenem 2 gm IV q8h
      • Moxifloxacin 400 mg IV q24h
    • Penicillin G MIC ≥ 0.1 μg/mL and  Ceftriaxone MIC > 0.5 μg/mL:
      • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8h (15-20 μg/mL trough target) + Moxifloxacin 400 mg IV q24h
      • Linezolid 600 mg q12h IV

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy 10-14 days
  • Confirm susceptibility to antimicrobial agent actually used, e.g., penicillin resistant isolates may be relatively resistant to other β-lactams.

Comments

  • European Society of Microbiology and Infectious Diseases (ESCMID) treatment guidelines: Clin Microbiol Infect 2016 May;22 Suppl 3:S37-62.
  • In large cohort study of over 1300 patients, adjunctive dexamethasone significantly decreased the odds of death or unfavorable outcome by half (Lancet Infect Dis 16:339, 2016). Similar  confirmatory results in over 1700 adult patients in the Netherlands (Clin Infect Dis 2022; 74: 657).
