Doravirine
Tradename: Pifeltro

Usage and Dosing

  • Doravirine (DOR) is a non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NNRTI) for treatment, in combination with other antiretroviral agents, of HIV-1 infection in treatment-naive adults.
  • It is formulated as a free-standing single drug product (Pifeltro) or as a component of Delstrigo, a 3-drug fixed dose combination of: DOR + TDF + 3TC.
  • Primarily metabolized by CYP3A4. Doravirine is contraindicated when co-administered with strong CYP3A inducers (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • Pregnancy risk, lactation safety:
    • No human data available
    • See Table.

Adult Dose

  • 100 mg po q24h
  • Take with or without food
  • If co-administered with Rifabutin: 100 mg po q12h

Adverse Effects

  • Contraindications:
    • Co-administration with drugs that are strong CYP3A inducers significantly decreases Doravirine plasma concentrations
  • Warnings:
    • Monitor for IRIS
  • Adverse effects reported:
    • Nausea
    • Dizziness
    • Headache
    • Fatigue
    • Diarrhea
    • Abdominal pain
    • Abnormal dreams

Comments

  • Of note, doravirine retains activity against viruses containing many common NNRTI mutations, including:
    • K103N (without Y188L)
    • V106A (except as below)
    • E138L (except as below)
    • Y181C (without Y188L)
    • G190A (without Y188L or V106A)
    • F227L (without V106A)
    • M230L (without E138L)
  • Resistance to doravirine conferred when RT mutations at the following positions exist:  
    • Y188L, either alone or in combination with other common NNRTI resistance;
    • V106A + either G190A or F227L
    • E138L + either Y181C or M230L
