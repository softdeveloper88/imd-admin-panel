Otitis Externa, Swimmer's Ear

Clinical Setting

  • Swim teams, frequent swimming pool use, mild ear ache.
  • Others: Ear phone use (phone head sets, cell phones, music players), contact dermatitis, psoriasis. All macerate skin of external auditory canal.
  • Treatment summary: JAMA 2018; 320:1375

Etiologies

  • Most common bacterial pathogens:
    • Pseudomonas species.
    • Staphylococcus aureus

Primary Regimens

  • In Cochrane meta-analysis, antiseptics, acidifying agents, glucocorticoids and topical antibiotics have similar outcomes (Cochrane Sys Review Database 2010;1:CD004740). Topical antibiotic therapy favored with cure rates of 65-90%
  • Gentle cleaning plus:
  • Mild disease: acetic acid + propylene glycol + hydrocortisone (Voscol). 5 drops in ear(s) 3-4 times per day until resolved.
  • For moderate to severe disease: antibiotic eardrops (all are expensive):
    • Ciprofloxacin + hydrocortisone (CIPRO-HC otic): 3 drops in ear(s) bid x 7 days
    • Ciprofloxacin + dexamethasone (Ciprodex):  4 drops in affeced ear bid x 7 days
    • Ofloxacin (generic): 5 drops in affected ear once /day X 7 days 
    • Ciprofloxacin 6% otic suspension (Otiprio): single 0.2 mL administration to the external ear canal of each affected ear
    • NOTE: Use of fluoroquinolone ear drops increased the risk of perforation of tympanic membrane with a hazard ratio over 2 (Clin Infect.Dis. 2020; 70: 1103)

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Treatment includes gentle cleaning. Recurrences prevented (or decreased) by drying with alcohol drops (1/3 white vinegar, 2/3 rubbing alcohol) after swimming, then antibiotic drops or 2% acetic acid solution.

Comments

  • No evidence that addition of an oral antibiotic to topical therapy will enhance efficacy of topical therapy in uncomplicated acute external otitis.
  • Systemic therapy needed in diabetics with malignant external otitis and severely immunocompromised patients
  • Ointments should not be used in ear.
  • Do not use neomycin drops if tympanic membrane punctured.
  • Guideline: Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 150:504, 2014.
  • Otiprio (ciprofloxacin 6% otic suspension) FDA-approved for the treatment of acute otitis externa due to P. aeruginosa and S. aureus in patients 6 months of age and older. The product was previously approved for bilateral otitis media with effusion in patients (age 6 months and older) undergoing tympanostomy tube placement.
  • Review of necrotizing otitis externa: J Laryngol Otol 2024; 138:120.
