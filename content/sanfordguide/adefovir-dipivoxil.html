Adefovir dipivoxil
Tradename: Hepsera

Usage and Dosing

  • Adefovir is indicated for the treatment of Hepatitis B infection. WARNING: must check for HIV infection before using Adefovir; do not use in HIV infected patients owing to concern of creating resistant virus to Tenofovir.
  • Adefovir dipivoxil is a prodrug of adefovir. It is an acyclic nucleotide analog with activity against hepatitis B (HBV) at 0.2–2.5 mM (IC50).
  • Active against Hepatitis B YMDD mutant Lamivudine-resistant strains and in vitro vs. Entecavir-resistant strains. To minimize resistance, package insert recommends using in combination with Lamivudine for Lamivudine-resistant virus; consider alternative therapy if viral load remains >1,000 copies/mL with treatment.
  • No food interactions. Generally few side effects, but Black Box warning regarding lactic acidosis/hepatic steatosis with nucleoside analogs and severe exacerbation of hepatitis B on discontinuing therapy; monitoring required after discontinuation.

Adverse Effects

  • Pre-existing or other risks for renal impairment.
  • Hepatitis may exacerbate when treatment discontinued; up to 25% of patients developed ALT increase of 10 x normal within 12 weeks; usually responds to retreatment or self-limited, but hepatic decompensation has occurred.

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Human data insufficient.
Animals: No developmental toxicities observed at systemic exposures 23x higher in rats and 40x higher in rabbits (relative to exposure at human dosage).
Use during Lactation Safety not established, avoid use if possible
