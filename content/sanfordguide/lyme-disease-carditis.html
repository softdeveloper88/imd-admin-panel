Lyme Disease, Carditis

Clinical Setting

  • Carditis is one of the late complications of Lyme Disease.  ECG only performed in those who have symptoms or suspected Lyme Carditis
  • Occurs within 2 months of onset of infections. Manifests as first degree or high degree A-V conduction block. PR interval > 300 msec indication for inpatient workup
  • Usually self-limited; for Bradycardia use temporary pacemaker;  permanent pacemaker not necessary.
  • Other symptoms include: dyspnea, edema, palpitations, lightheadedness, chest pain, and/or syncope.
  • No evidence for chronic cardiomyopathy in US.
  • However, rare unexpected sudden deaths have occurred (MMWR 62:993, 2013).
  • For details on diagnostic methods see: Lyme Disease, Diagnosis and Emerging Infect Dis 22:1169, 2016.

Etiologies

  • Borrelia burgdorferi

Primary Regimens

  • Hospitalize, monitor, and use parenteral drug (e.g., ceftriaxone) for symptomatic patients. After resolution of heart block, can finish course with oral drug. Total antimicrobial duration is 14- 21 days
  • First degree A-V block:
    • Adult: Doxycycline 100 mg po or IV bid x 14-21 days (max 100 mg bid) or Amoxicillin 500 mg po tid x 14-21 days 
    • Pediatric : Doxycycline 4.4 mg/kg/day po divided bid x 14-21 days or Amoxicillin 50 mg/kg/day po divided tid
  • High (2nd or 3rd) degree A-V block:
    • Adult: Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h x 14-21 days.
    • Pediatric (Age ≥ 8 yrs): Ceftriaxone 50-75 mg/kg/d IV x 14-21 days

Alternative Regimens

  • First degree A-V block:
    • Amoxicillin 500 mg po tid x 14-21 days (primary in pregnancy)
  • High (2nd or 3rd) degree A-V block:
    • Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q4h (where available outside the US) or Penicillin G 3 million units IV q4h for 14-21 days

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Pediatrics: Doxycycline can be safely administered for up to 21 days regardless of age (AAP Red Book 2018, Section 4; J Pediatr 166:1246, 2015).

Comments

  • No need for permanent pacemaker; may need temporary pacemaker. A-V block generally disappears within several days of starting antimicrobial therapy. In review, 39% of patients required temporary pacemaker: Clin Infect Dis 59: 996, 2014.
  • 2020 Clinical practice guidelines (Neurology 96:262, 2021)
  • Review in JAMA 315:1767 & 2461, 2016.
  • Earlier Review: N Engl J Med 370:1724, 2014.
