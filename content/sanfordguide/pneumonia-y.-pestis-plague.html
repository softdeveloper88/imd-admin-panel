Plague Pneumonia, Septicemic Plague

Clinical Setting

  • Treatment of plague pneumonia or septicemic plague.
  • Yersinia pestis is a potential bioterrorism agent.
  • Comprehensive reviews of antibacterial therapy options:  MMWR July 16, 2021 / 70(3);1–27.

Etiologies

  • Yersinia pestis

Primary Regimens

  • Contact and droplet respiratory isolation
  • Adult:
    • Treatment, any of the following regimens administered for 10-14 days:
      • Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q8h or 750 mg po q12h
      • Levofloxacin 750 mg po/IV once daily 
      • Moxifloxacin 400 mg po/IV once daily 
      • Gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV q24h 
      • Streptomycin 1 gm IV/IM q12h 
    • Pre- or Post-exposure prophylaxis, any of the following regimens administered for 7 days
      • Ciprofloxacin 500-750 mg po q12h 
      • Levofloxacin 750 mg po once daily 
      • Moxifloxacin 400 mg po once daily 
      • Doxycycline 100 mg po q12h
  • Child:
    • Treatment, any of the following regimens administered for 10-14 days:
      • Ciprofloxacin 10 mg/kg IV q8-12 h (max 400 mg/dose ) or 15 mg/kg po q8-12h (max 500 mg/dose q8h, 750 mg/dose q12h) 
      • Levofloxacin
        • Wt <50 kg: 8 mg/kg po/IV q12h (max 250 mg/dose) 
        • Wt >50 kg: 500-750 mg IV/po once daily
      • Gentamicin 4.5-7.5 mg/kg IV/IM q24h 
      • Streptomycin 15 mg/kg IV/IM q12h (max 1 gm/dose) 
    • Pre- or Post-exposure prophylaxis, any of the following regimens administered for 7 days:
      • Ciprofloxacin 15 mg/kg po q12h (max 750 mg/dose) 
      • Levofloxacin
        • Wt <50 kg: 8 mg/kg po q12h (max 250 mg/dose) 
        • Wt >50 kg: 500-750 mg po once daily 
      • Doxycycline
        • Wt <45 kg: 2.2 mg/kg po q12h
        • Wt ≥45 kg: 100 mg po q12h
  • Pregnancy:
    • Treatment, the following combination regimen administered for 10-14 days:
      • Gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV q24h + (Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q8h or 500 mg po q8h OR Levofloxacin 750 mg po/IV once daily)
    • Pre- or Post-exposure prophylaxis, either of the following regimens administered for 7 days:
      • Ciprofloxacin 500 mg po q8h or 750 mg po q12h
      • Levofloxacin 750 mg po q 24h
  • Dual therapy with two distinct classes of antimicrobials (e.g., Ciprofloxacin + Gentamicin) should be used for initial treatment of patients with severe pneumonic or septicemic plague and patients infected after intentional release of Yersinia pestis.

Alternative Regimens

  •  See MMWR July 16, 2021 / 70(3);1–27 for a more complete list of alternative regimens
  • Adult:
    • Doxycycline 200 mg po/IV x 1 dose, then 100 mg po/IV q12h
    • Chloramphenicol 12.5-25 mg/kg IV q6h
    • Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole 5 mg/kg (trimethoprim component) po/IV q8h
    • Treatment, either of the following regimens administered for 10-14 days:
    • Pre- or Post-exposure prophylaxis, either of the following for 7 days:
      • Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole 5 mg/kg (TMP component) po q12h
      • Tetracycline 500 mg po q6h
  • Child:
    • Doxycycline
      • Weight <45 kg: 4.4 mg/kg po/IV loading dose then 2.2 mg/kg po/IV q12h x 10 -14 days
      • ≥Weight 45 kg: 100 mg po bid x 10 days
    • Moxifloxacin
      • Ages < 2 yrs, 2-5 yrs, 6-11 yrs: 6, 5, 4 mg/kg, respectively, po q12h
      • Ages 12- 17 yrs
        • Wt < 45 kg : 4 mg/kg po q12h (max 200 mg/dose)
        • Wt > 45 kg: 400 mg po once daily
    • Chloramphenicol 12.5-25 mg/kg IV q6h
    • Treatment, either one of the following administered for 10-14 days
    • Pre- or Post-exposure prophylaxis, either of the following administered for 7 days:
      •  Moxifloxacin as above
      • Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole 5 mg/kg (trimethoprim component) po q12h
  • Pregnancy:
    • Treatment, any of the following regimens administered for 10-14 days
      • Moxifloxacin 400 mg po/IV once daily 
      • Doxycycline 200 mg IV x 1 dose, then 100 mg IV q12h
      • Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole 5 mg/kg t(trimethoprim component) po/IV q8h 
    • Pre- Post-exposure prophylaxis, any of the following regimens administered for 10-14 days:
      • Moxifloxacin 400 mg po/IV once daily  
      • Doxycycline 100 mg po q12h
      • Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole 5 mg/kg (trimethoprim component) po/q12h

Comments

  • The IV route of administration is recommended for initial therapy of pneumonia or septicemia and until the patient stabilizes
  • Isolate patients during the first 48 hours of treatment.
  • Notify clinical microbiology of possible plague to avoid occupational exposure.
