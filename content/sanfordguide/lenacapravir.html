Lenacapavir
Tradename: Sunlenca

Usage and Dosing

  • Lenacapavir is indicated for the treatment of HIV-1 infection in heavily treatment-experienced adults with multidrug-resistant HIV-1 infection failing their current antiretroviral regimen due to resistance, intolerance, or safety considerations. It is used in combination with other ARV.
  • First-in-class capsid inhibitor. Lenacapavir binds directly to HIV capsid in a pocket between two adjacent capsid monomers. It interferes with multiple early- to late-stage processes of the viral life cycle: capsid disassembly and nuclear transport, virus production, and capsid assembly.
  • First every 6 month treatment for HIV-1 infection.  Lead in required (see below).  See package insert for detailed instructions for SQ injection (cartoons in figures 1 and 2 are helpful).
  • Clinical Trial (CAPELLA) enrolled 36 participants with multi-class HIV-1 drug resistance treated with every 6 month SQ injection of lenacapavir; 88% (21/24) experienced at least a 0.5 log decrease in HIV RNA after 14 days of add-on Rx vs 17% (2 /12) placebo recipients.  
  • Studies ongoing for use of lenacapavir as PrEP.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions. Lenacapavir is an inhibitor of CYP3A, PGP, and BCRP, and a substrate of PGP, UGT1A1, and CYP3A. See Major Drug Interactions chart below (not all-inclusive).

Adult Dose

  • Initiation using one of two recommended regimens. Tablets may be taken with or without food.
    • Option 1
      • Day 1: 927 mg by sc injection, 600 mg po
      • Day 2: 600 mg po
    • Option 2
      • Day 1: 600 mg po
      • Day 2: 600 mg po
      • Day 8: 300 mg po
      • Day 15: 927 mg by sc injection
  • Maintenance
    • 927 mg by sc injection, six months (26 weeks) after the last injection, then repeated every six months (±2 weeks).

Adverse Effects

  • Injection site reactions
  • Nausea
  • Immune reconstitution syndrome (IRIS)

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Insufficient human data. No evidence of toxicity in animals.
Use during Lactation If on ART + suppressed VL throughout pregnancy, breastfeeding is a reasonable option after counseling about infant feeding options, risks and benefits
