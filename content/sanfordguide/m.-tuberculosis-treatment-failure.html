Mycobacterium tuberculosis, Treatment Failure

Clinical Setting

  • Treatment failure or relapse following course of therapy for M. tuberculosis.
    • Treatment failure is defined persistently positive sputum culture after 4–6 months of therapy.
  • Usually due to poor compliance with drug regimens, resistant organisms, although sub-therapeutic drug levels also contribute (Int J Tuberc Lung Dis 17:221, 2013).

Etiologies

  • M. tuberculosis

Primary Regimens

  • Expert consultation strongly advised.
  • Check susceptibility of the most recent isolate against first-line and second-line agents agents before selecting a directly observed therapy (DOT) regimen
  • Never add a single agent to a failing or failed regimen
    • If possible await results of susceptibility tests
      • If empirical therapy required, use at least 3 new agents to which the patient has not been previously exposed
      • Preferred second-line agents include an injectable, a fluoroquinolone (levofloxacin or moxifloxacin), cycloserine, and linezolid (dosed 600 mg once daily)
  • Choice of definitive re-treatment regimen is based on isolate susceptibility: see Susceptible, Mono-resistant, MDR-TB or XDR-TB.

Alternative Regimens

  • See specific topics, as above

Comments

  • Expert consultation available from Curry International Tuberculosis Center (Toll-free number: 877-390-6682; E-mail: CurryTBcenter@ucsf.edu)
  • Non-compliance is common, therefore institute DOT if not is use already.
  • Consider therapeutic drug monitoring (see Drugs 74:839, 2014 for recommended sampling times and target concentrations).
  • In HIV-positive patients, reinfection is a possible explanation for failure.
