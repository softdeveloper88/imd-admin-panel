Bite: Lizard, Iguana

Clinical Setting

  • Bite from iguana
  • Often involves upper extremities or face

Etiologies

  • Microbiology of iguana oral flora poorly characterized
  • Salmonella sp. has been transmitted by handling iguanas and lizards
  • Infections reported due to Staphylococcus aureus and Serratia marcescens
    • Case report of bullous cellulitis due to S. marcescens.

Primary Regimens

  • Optimal regimens not defined. Would choose agent that covers possible skin flora of victim (Staph and Strep) and well as Gram-negative bacteria, including Salmonella sp. from lizard:
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po twice daily + Ciprofloxacin 750 mg po twice daily

Alternative Regimens

  • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h or Imipenem or Meropenem or Cefepime
  • Anti-staphylococcal agent + agent likely to be active against Serratia and other Enterobacteriaceae such as Gentamicin.
  • Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole possible alternative to a fluoroquinolone

Comments

  • Administer tetanus toxoid or Tdap (preferred) if > 10 years since last dose
  • Review of animal bite wounds (Clin Microbiol Rev 24: 231, 2011)
