Mycobacterium terrae complex

Clinical Setting

  • Chronic tenosynovitis of the hand is most common presentation; also skin, soft tissue, and other bone and joint disease.
    • History of trauma to hand, puncture wound, or exposure risk (e.g., gardening or farming) may be present.
  • Pulmonary infection (uncommon).

Etiologies

  • M. terrae
  • M. sinense
  • M. nonchromogenicum
  • M. hiberniae
  • M. arupense
  • M. kumamotonense
  • Others

Primary Regimens

  • Optimal antimicrobial regimen not known: 2-3 drug combination of Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid + Ethambutol 15 mg/kg po once daily + Rifampin 600 mg po once daily is reasonable

Alternative Regimens

  • Susceptibilities vary widely with species and isolates may be susceptible in vitro to Rifabutin, sulfonamides, fluoroquinolones, Linezolid, aminoglycosides (Microbiol Spectr 2016 Nov;4(6)).

Comments

  • Rare cause of mycobacterial infection, often a contaminant.
  • Duration of therapy not established but 12-18 months has been used (Clin Infect Dis 30:444, 2000).
  • Surgical debridement for devitalized tissue.
