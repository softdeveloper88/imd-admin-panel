Reactive Arthritis

Clinical Setting

  • Occurs weeks after infection (see Etiologies) and presents as urethritis, conjunctivitis, arthritis, and sometimes eye findings, and rash.
  • Arthritis: asymmetrical oligoarthritis of ankles, knees, feet, sacroiliitis.
  • Rash: palms and soles – keratoderma blennorrhagia; circinate balanitis of glans penis.
  • Eye findings: conjunctivitis; iritis and uveitis less commonly.
  • HLA-B27 positive predisposes to Reiter’s.

Etiologies

  • May follow infections caused by
    • C. trachomatis
    • C. jejuni
    • Y. enterocolitica, Y. pseudotuberculosis
    • Shigella sp.
    • Salmonella sp.
    • C. difficile

Primary Regimens

  • Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAID)
  • No antimicrobial therapy

Alternative Regimens

  • Corticosteroids, intra-articular or systemic, for those not responding to NSAIDs 
  • Sulfasalazine or methotrexate for refractory or chronic disease (> 6 month duration)
