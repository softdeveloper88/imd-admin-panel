Pretomanid

Usage and Dosing

  • Pretomanid is approved as part of a three-drug combination regimen with bedaquiline and linezolid for the treatment of adults with pulmonary MDR-TB or XDR-TB.
  • FDA approval of 3-drug regimen based on a an 90% success rate observed in phase 3 open label, non-comparative trial (N Engl J Med. 2020; 382:893-902) of 109 patients (56 HIV-infected) with MDR or XDR TB conducted in 3 centers in South Africa.  This regimen is preferred over the short course regimen listed under alternative regimens as it is entirely oral, employs fewer agents and therefore is likely to be better tolerated, and is effective against XDR TB. Similarly it is preferred over the WHO 4- to 5-drug regimen because fewer drugs are required for efficacy and the duration of therapy is shorter, 6 months compared to 18-24 months. Adverse events include elevations in hepatic enzymes; myelossuppression (48%, attributable to Linezolid); peripheral (81%) and optic neuropathy (both attributable to linezolid); QTc prolongation (frequent monitoring required and discontinue the combination regimen if significant ventricular arrhythmia develops or for QTcF (QT interval correction by Fridericia's formula) prolongation of greater than 500 ms on repeat ECGs); lactic acidosis (attributable to Linezolid). 
  • Levofloxacin, Moxifloxacin, Bedaquiline, Delamanid and Pretomanid cause QTc prolongation and caution is required when these are used in combination

Adverse Effects

  • Adverse effects reported with the combination of pretomanid, bedaquiline, and linezolid:
    • Gastrointestinal (nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea)
    • Hepatotoxicity
    • Increased transaminases
    • Lactic acidosis
    • Myelosuppression
    • Peripheral, optic neuropathy
    • QT-interval prolongation
    • Rash
  • Testicular atrophy, impaired fertility in male rats (effects on human male fertility not known)
