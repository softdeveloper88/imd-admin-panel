Tinea Versicolor

Clinical Setting

  • Tinea versicolor (Pityriasis versicolor)
  • Fine, scaly rash with patches of discolored skin with sharp borders commonly found on back, underarms, upper arms, chest, and neck.
  • Skin may appear lighter than surrounding healthy skin; in African Americans, either hypo- or hyper-pigmentation
  • Rule out erythrasma (Coral red fluorescence with Wood's lamp).

Diagnosis

  • Microscopic exam of epidermal scales with 10% KOH. Skin lesions fluoresce yellow with Wood's lamp. Can culture on mycologic media supplemented with lipid (usually olive oil)

Etiologies

  • Malassezia furfur

Primary Regimens

  • Limited disease:
    • Ketoconazole 2% shampoo daily for 3 days; can use 2 to 3 times/wk for maintenance/prevention
    • Selenium sulfide 2.5% shampoo: daily application while bathing for 1 to 2 weeks, can use 2 to 3 times/wk for maintenance/prevention
  • Extensive disease:
    • Fluconazole 300 mg po once weekly x 2 weeks (total of 2 doses)

Alternative Regimens

  • Itraconazole 400 mg po x 1 or 200 mg daily x 7 days
