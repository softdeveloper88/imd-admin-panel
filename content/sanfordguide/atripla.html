Atripla
Tradename: Atripla: Efavirenz + Emtricitabine + Tenofovir

Usage and Dosing

  • Atripla is indicated as a single pill, fixed-dose combination regimen for patients with HIV infection.
  • Exacerbation of Hepatitis B reported in patients discontinuing component drugs (FTC); some may need anti-HBV therapy (preferred anti-Hep B therapy).
  • Atripla remains Pregnancy category D. The FDA cautions against use in pregnancy or in women who may become pregnant because of the potential risk of fetal harm due to Efavirenz (neural tube defects). However, multiple expert groups consider Efavirenz safe to use during pregnancy (WHO Guidelines) or to be continued once pregnancy identified, if patient is already on Efavirenz (HHS Perinatal Guidelines, clinicalinfo.HIV.gov).
  • See individual components for drug-specific data:
    • Efavirenz (EFV)
    • Emtricitabine (FTC)
    • Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF)
  • For class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.
  • Available in a generic formulation.

Adult Dose

  • 1 tablet po q24h. On empty stomach, preferably at bedtime.
  • Components: EFV 600 mg + FTC 200 mg + TDF 300 mg
