Ventricular Assist Device Infection

Clinical Setting

  • Empiric therapy.
  • Obtain cultures of the following: blood, wounds, drive line, device pocket and, maybe, the pump.

Etiologies

  • Staph. aureus
  • Staph. epidermidis
  • Aerobic Gram-negative bacilli
  • Candida sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) + (Cefepime 2 gm IV q12h) + Fluconazole 800 mg IV q24h.
  • Can substitute as follows:
    • Daptomycin 10 mg/kg/day for Vancomycin
    • (Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q12h or Levofloxacin 750 mg IV q24h) for Cefepime
    • Voriconazole, Caspofungin, Micafungin or Anidulafungin for Fluconazole

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • For definitive therapy, modify regimen based on results of culture and susceptibility tests.

Comments

  • It is often not feasible to remove the device, so suppression is typically required after initial treatment course.
  • Higher than FDA-approved dose for Daptomycin indicated because of potential for emergence of resistance.
  • References: Clin Infect Dis 57:1438, 2013, Clin Infect Dis 64: 222, 2017.
