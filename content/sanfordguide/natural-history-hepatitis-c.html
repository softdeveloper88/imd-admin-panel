HCV Natural History

Acute HCV

  • Usually asymptomatic (elevated transaminases).
  • If symptomatic, usually abates in days to weeks; rarely associated with hepatic failure.
  • Typically leads to chronic infection in 75-85% of cases.
  • Factors associated with natural clearance include:
    • HLA-DRB1*1101 and DQB1*0301 haplotypes.
    • Favorable IL-28B genetic status (CC rs12979860).
    • Low HCV viral load.
  • Treatment of acute HCV infection is recommended to reduce transmission to others; results in high sustained virologic response (SVR, defined below) rates.  

Chronic HCV

Timeline

  • Timeline: 20 to 50 years from acute infection.
  • 75-85% of persons with acute infection progress to chronic HCV.
  • 30% to 40% of those develop cirrhosis over 30 years.
  • Of those who develop cirrhosis:
    • 20% decompensate
    • 1-4%/year develop hepatocellular carcinoma

Factors Associated With Faster Progression

  • Older age at time of infection
  • Male gender
  • Alcohol use
  • HIV Co-infection
  • HBV Co-infection
  • Acquisition or re-infection post-transplant

Survival

  • With compensated cirrhosis: 5 year survival = 91% (10 year = 79%)
  • With decompensated cirrhosis: 5 year survival = 50%

Comments

  • All data summarized in HCV Guidance (www.hcvguidelines.org)
  • Summary of progression among those with advanced disease/scarring (Hepatology 54:396, 2011).
