Capillariasis

Clinical Setting

  • Profound intractable abdominal pain, watery diarrhea, malabsorption, weight loss and cachexia.
  • Larvae produced by this intestinal nematode re-invade mucosa directly and cause auto-infection.
  • Apparent reservoir in fish-eating birds but complete life-cycle and transmission (likely fish) is still unknown.
  • Most cases reported in the Philippines and Thailand, and may be emerging in Egypt, the middle east and Colombia
    • Sporadic cases worldwide.
  • C. hepatica human infection causing hepatitis, very rare on all continents with liver invasion and auto-infection.

Etiologies

  • Capillaria philippinensis
  • Capillaria hepatica

Primary Regimens

  • Albendazole 200 mg po bid x 10 days (Am J Trop Med Hyg 86:126, 2012)

Alternative Regimens

  • Mebendazole 200 mg po bid x 20 days

Comments

  • Albendazole preferred because of action on all life-cycle stages including larvae and shorter course.
  • Large case series Am J Trop Med Hyg 98:27, 2018
  • Detailed parasitological description in: Am J Trop Med Hyg 86:126, 2012.
  • Recent proposal to rename Paracapillaria philippinensis.
  • Vermox brand mebendazole 500 mg tablets is FDA-approved but for donation only and not sold in the U.S.
  • Albendazole 200 mg tablets and Mebendazole 100 mg capsules available from Expert Compounding Pharmacy (see Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources).
  • Branded albendazole (Albenza) marketed by Amneal is very costly and is difficult for retail pharmacies to access.
