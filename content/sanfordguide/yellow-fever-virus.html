Yellow Fever Virus

Clinical Setting

  • Yellow Fever is endemic in sub-Saharan Africa and Amazonian South America (most widespread in Brazil) as a result of increased urbanization of susceptible population. 
  • An estimated 110,000 cases of severe disease and 51,000 deaths in 2018.
  • Mass vaccination efforts in endemic regions reduced mortality by 47% (eLife, 2021).
  • Early phases clinically similar to other flaviviruses (dengue, Zika), chikungunya as well as leptospirosis, HAV, HBV.
  • Diagnosis:  serum IgM or PCR.

Etiologies

  • Flavivirus (Yellow Fever Virus)

Primary Regimens

  • No antiviral therapy.
  • Liver transplantation in severe cases controversial.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Prevention

  • See Yellow Fever, Vaccine for indications, available products, dosing, and vaccine characteristics for pre-exposure prevention.
    • Live virus with many precautions and contraindications
      • Safe and effective for well-controlled persons with HIV
      • Some countries have mandatory vaccination as an Entry Requirement

Comments

  • Efforts underway to identify candidate therapies (Expert Opinion Drug Discovery, 2022)
