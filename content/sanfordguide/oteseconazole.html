Oteseconazole
Tradename: Vivjoa

Usage and Dosing

  • Oteseconazole is indicated to reduce the incidence of recurrent vulvovaginal candidiasis (RVVC) in females with a history of RVVC who are not of reproductive potential.
  • Mechanism of action: inhibits fungal 14-alpha-demethylase (CYP51), an enzyme in the synthetic pathway from squalene to ergosterol.
  • Reduced affinity for human CYP enzymes compared to other azoles.
  • Contraindicated in females of reproductive potential, and in pregnant and lactating women. Animal studies suggest the potential for fetal harm.
  • Oteseconazole should be taken with food. Capsules should be swallowed whole (not chewed, crushed, dissolved, or opened).

Adult Dose

  • Two recommended regimens: oteseconazole only, and otesconazole/fluconazole. The fluconazole is not supplied in the carton.
  • Oteseconazole-only:
    • Day 1: Oteseconazole 600 mg po (single dose)
    • Day 2: Oteseconazole 450 mg po (single dose)
    • Starting Day 14: Oteseconazole 150 mg po once weekly for 11 weeks.
  • Otesconazole/fluconazole:
    • Day 1, 4, 7: Fluconazole 150 mg po
    • Day 14 through 20: Oteseconazole 150 mg po once daily for 7 days
    • Starting Day 28: Oteseconazole 150 mg po once weekly for 11 weeks.
