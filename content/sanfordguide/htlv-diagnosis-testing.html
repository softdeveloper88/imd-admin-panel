HTLV Diagnosis & Testing

Human T-Lymphotropic Virus, HTLV-1

  • HTLV-1 is a retrovirus found worldwide. It causes illness in only 5% of infected persons. Two are associated with HTLV-1:  Adult T-cell leukemia/lymphoma (N Engl J Med 367:552, 2012) and HTLV-1-associated myelopathy (HAM), also known as tropical spastic paraparesis (TSP; reviewed inhttps://doi.org/10.1093/tropej/fmx039). Mogamulizumab (anti-CCR4) antibody being evaluated for refractory T-cell cutaneous lymphoma. (Blood, 2018 )
  • Pathogenesis reviewed (Nature Reviews Cancer 7: 270, 2007)
  • Laboratory diagnosis is by blood and CSF (Reviewed in Clin Infect Dis. IDSA Microbiology Lab Testing Update 2024):
    • Anti-HTLV-1 antibodies are detected by ELISA antibody testing; Western Blot is used for confirmation (Focus Diagnostics or Quest Diagnostics). HTLV DNA can be detected by PCR in circulating CD4 cells.
    • Of interest, the nucleus of infected cells have a lobulated or "flower-like" appearance.
    • Recent influenza vaccine can cause false-positive antibody elevation.
  • HTLV-1 infection increases CD4 counts.
  • Blood donors are screened for HTLV-1 antibody, but not for HTLV-2 antibodies
  • Multi-plex Quantitative PCR for diagnosis / differentiation of HTLV-1 vs HTLV-2 (Front Microbiol 13:831594, 2022)

Human T-Lymphotropic Virus, HTLV-2

  • Ar present, no disease has been associated with HTLV-2 virus.
  • Blood donors are not screened for HTLV-2.
  • Based on antibody screening, HTLV-2 is endemic in Native Americans and injection drug users (IDU).
