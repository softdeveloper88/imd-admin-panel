Mycobacterium celatum

Clinical Setting

  • Pulmonary infection mimicking tuberculosis.
  • Isolated from pulmonary lesions and blood in AIDS patients, rarely causes disease in immunocompetent patients
  • May be confused with Mycobacterium xenopi and M. avium-intracellulare complex (MAC).
  • Susceptibilities are similar to MAC, but M. celatum is highly resistant to Rifampin (Clin Infect Dis 24:140, 1997; J Infect 38:157, 1999).

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium celatum

Primary Regimens

  • Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid + Ethambutol 15 mg/kg po/day + Ciprofloxacin 750 mg po bid ± Rifabutin 300 mg po once daily for 12-18 months

Alternative Regimens

  • May be susceptible in vitro to Clofazimine and Amikacin. 

Comments

  • Reported as a cause of false-positive amplified Mycobacterium tuberculosis direct test (Diagn Microbiol Infect Dis 49:19, 2004).
  • Most reported cases received 3 or 4 drugs (Emerg Infect Dis 9:399, 2003), but optimal regimen is not defined.
  • Pulmonary resection of localized disease may be curative (Yonsie Med J 51:980, 2010)
