Mycobacterium ulcerans, Buruli Ulcer

Clinical Setting

  • Buruli ulcer
  • Fastidious organism that requires special growth conditions.
  • Organism not found in the U.S.; endemic in tropical rain forests of Africa, Southeast Asia, South and Central America, Australia.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium ulcerans

Primary Regimens

  • (Rifampin 600 mg po q24h + Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid or 15 mg/kg extended release once daily) x 8-12 week

Alternative Regimens

  • Clarithromycin 500 mg bid + (Moxifloxacin 400 mg po q24h or Ciprofloxacin 500 mg po bid) x 8-12 weeks
  • Rifampin 600 mg po q24h + (Moxifloxacin 400 mg po q24h or  Ciprofloxacin 500 mg po bid) x 8-12 weeks

Comments

  • General reference: Expert Rev Clin Pharmacol. 2020;13:391-401.
  • Oral rifampin-based regimens appear to be as effective as the Streptomycin combination and are better tolerated and less toxic (Lancet. 2020; 395: 1259–1267).
  • The longer duration of therapy for deeper lesions extending to bone or joint
  • Antibiotic therapy alone may be effective for smaller lesions (< 10 cm in diameter) with surgery reserved for those intolerant of or declining antibiotics, debridement of necrotic tissue, or repair of large defects.
  • See Cochrane Database Syst Rev. 2018 (8): CD012118.
