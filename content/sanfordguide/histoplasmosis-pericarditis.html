Histoplasmosis, Pericarditis

Clinical Setting

  • Pericarditis associated with histoplasmosis in most cases is not due to direct infection but instead due to inflammation of adjacent mediastinal lymph nodes.

Etiologies

  • Histoplasma capsulatum

Primary Regimens

  • Mild symptoms: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) therapy
  • NSAID non-responders or hemodynamic compromise:
    • Prednisone 1 mg/kg po daily tapered over 1-2 weeks + Itraconazole 200 mg po 3 times daily x 3 days and then twice daily x 6-12 weeks
    • Pericardial fluid removal for hemodynamic compromise

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • IDSA treatment guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 45:807, 2007.
