Mycobacterium scrofulaceum

Clinical Setting

  • Environmental organism found in water, soil, dust.
  • Uncommon cause of non-tuberculous mycobacterial lymphadenitis.
    • More common in children than in adults.
    • Surgical excision is primary therapy.
  • Rare cause of pulmonary infection; may cause disseminated disease in immunocompromised patients.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium scrofulaceum

Primary Regimens

  • Chemotherapy generally not needed for isolated lymphadenitis, surgical excision preferred
  • Regimens are not defined, but should include a macrolide and one or two other agents with in vitro activity, such as the follow:
    • Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid + Ethambutol 15 mg/kg po once daily + Rifampin 600 mg po once daily 

Alternative Regimens

  • Rifabutin, Linezolid, and Moxifloxacin may also be active in vitro

Comments

  • Localized disease is best managed surgically: if antimicrobial therapy is used, treat for 12 months.
  • See: Infect Dis (Lond). 2019; 51:602-609
