Ethionamide
Tradename: Trecator-SC

Usage and Dosing

  • Ethionamide is a second-line anti-tuberculous agent.
  • Resistance < 1%.
  • Cross-resistance with INH (Int J Tuber Lung Dis 17:129, 2013): must confirm susceptibility if the strain is INH resistant, particular if resistance is low-level (e.g., INH MIC > 0.1 μg/mL and < 1 μg/mL).

Adult Dose

  • 250-500 mg po bid

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days) 15-20 mg/kg/day (divided q12h)
Max/Day 1 gm

Renal Adjustment

  • Body weight and Creatinine Clearance calculations
  • CrCl = Creatinine clearance (mL/min)
  • CAPD = Continuous Ambulatory Peritoneal Dialysis
  • CRRT = Continuous Renal Replacement Therapy
  • AD = after hemodialysis
  • SLED = sustained low efficiency dialysis

Hepatic Adjustment

NOTE: Use with caution in underlying liver disease.

Mild impairment (Child-Pugh Class A) No dosage adjustment
Moderate impairment (Child-Pugh Class B) No dosage adjustment
Severe impairment (Child-Pugh Class C) No dosage adjustment

Adverse Effects

  • Gastrointestinal irritation (up to 50% on large dose); goiter; peripheral neuropathy (rare); convulsions (rare); changes in affect (rare); difficulty in diabetes control; rashes; hepatitis; purpura; stomatitis; gynecomastia; menstrual irregularity.
  • Give drug with meals or antacids; 50–100 mg pyridoxine per day concomitantly; AST monthly.
  • Possibly teratogenic.

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • M. tuberculosis

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 C
Use during Lactation Probably safe with monitoring, but data limited; avoid if possible

  • May potentiate the adverse effects of concomitantly administered antituberculous drugs
