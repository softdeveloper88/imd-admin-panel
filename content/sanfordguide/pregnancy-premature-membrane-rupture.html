Pregnancy, Premature Membrane Rupture

Clinical Setting

  • Pre-term, premature rupture of membranes (≤34 weeks gestation) in Group B Strep negative women.
  • May require additional antibiotics for therapy of specific existing infections.

Etiologies

  • Vaginal microorganisms
  • Screen for Group B streptococci
  • Prophylactic regimen to include activity vs:
    • Chlamydia trachomatis
    • Ureaplasma hemolyticus
    • Streptococcus pneumoniae
    • Other vaginal aerobic/anaerobic flora

Primary Regimens

  • No antibiotic allergy
    • Azithromycin 1gm po x 1 + Ampicillin 2 gm IV q6h x 48h, then
    • Amoxicillin  250  mg po q8h x 5 days

Alternative Regimens

  • If mild (skin rash) penicillin allergy:
    • Azithromycin 1gm po x 1 + Cefazolin 1gm IV q8h x 48 h, then Cephalexin 500 mg po q 6h x 5 days
  • If IgE mediated allergy to penicillin (anaphylaxis):
    • Azithromycin 1gm po x 1 + Clindamycin 900 mg IV q 8h x 48h + Gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV q 24h x two doses, then Clindamycin 300 mg po q 8h x 5 days
  • If  IgE mediated penicillin allergy plus clindamycin resistant gp B streptococci:
    • Azithromycin 1gm po x 1 + Vancomycin 20mg/kg IV q 8h (max 2g dose) x 48h for GBS resistant to Clindamycin

Comments

  • Antibiotic prophylaxis reduced infant respiratory distress syndrome (50.6% to 40.8%, p = 0.03), necrotizing enterocolitis (5.8% to 2.3%, p = 0.03) and prolonged pregnancy (2.9 to 6.1 days, p <0.001) vs placebo.
  • Decreases infant morbidity (JAMA 278:989, 1997).
  • See ACOG Committee Opinion: Obstet Gynecol. 2020;135(2):e51 and Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews 2013, Issue 12. Art. No.: CD001058.
  • For those who are Group B strep positive, ensure that this entity is treated per guidelines( add link to group B strept page).
  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate is not recommended due to an observation of increased rates of necrotizing enterocolitis (Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews 2013, Issue 12. Art. No.: CD001058).
