Histoplasmosis, CNS

Clinical Setting

  • Presents as chronic meningitis and/or parenchymal brain lesions.
  • Disseminated disease or immunocompromised state often present.
  • Diagnosis may be elusive due to insensitivity of any single test; use of multiple tests (e.g., culture, serological and antigen testing, biopsy, PCR) may be required to establish the diagnosis.

Etiologies

  • Histoplasma capsulatum

Primary Regimens

  • Liposomal Amphotericin B, 5 mg/kg IV daily x 4-6 weeks, then Itraconazole 200 mg bid or tid x at least 12 months and until resolution of CSF abnormalities. Longer duration of therapy may be required in immunocompromised hosts.

Alternative Regimens

  • Amphotericin B 0.7-1 mg/kg IV daily
  • Fluconazole 600-800 mg once daily
  • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg po bid x 2 doses, then 4 mg/kg po bid (Arch Neurology 65: 666, 2008; J Antimicrob Chemother 57:1235, 2006)
  • Posaconazole Delayed-release tabs: 300 mg bid x2 doses, then 300 mg once daily

Comments

  • Use of Itraconazole solution enables higher serum levels than capsules and is the preferred preparation. Serum levels of Itraconazole should be determined after 2 weeks to ensure adequate drug exposure (goal serum level >1.0 μg/mL).
  • Repeat LP at intervals to monitor response to oral therapy.
