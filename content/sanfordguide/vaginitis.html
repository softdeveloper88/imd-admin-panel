Bacterial Vaginosis

Clinical Setting

  • Causes vaginitis and cervicitis (Amsel's criteria = 3 of 4 of below; Nugent's criteria = vaginal gram stain)
    • Copious, malodorous, thin, milky vaginal discharge,
    • pH >4.5.
    • Whiff Test (fishy odor when10% Potassium Hydroxide added to vaginal discharge; ).
    • "Clue" Cell (epithelial cell coated with bacteria).
  • May be transmitted sexually but not a STD; no need to treat sexual partners unless balanitis is present.

Etiologies

  • Etiology is unclear, but associated with:
    • Gardnerella vaginalis
    • Mobiluncus sp.
    • Mycoplasma hominis
    • Prevotella sp.
    • Atopobium vaginae
  • Lactobacillus sp. is protective
  • NAAT tests based upon presence of above bacteria. 

Primary Regimens

  • Metronidazole 0.5 gm po bid x 7 days (DO NOT USE 2 gm po x one dose) 
  • Metronidazole vaginal gel (1 applicator intravaginally) once daily x 5 days
    • 1 applicator contains 5 gm of gel with 37.5 mg Metronidazole
    • Lactobacillus: In randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial, the  vaginal probiotic Lactin-V (Lactobacillus crispatus) reduced recurrence, week 12, from 45% in the placebo group to 30% in the Lactin-V patients, p 0.01. Lactin-V study regimen starting at the end of the metronidazole. Daily vaginal instillation daily x 4 days and then twice a week for 10 weeks. Ref: N Engl J Med. 2020;382:1906.
  • Clindamycin 2% vaginal cream 5 gm intravaginally at bedtime x 7 days
  • Pregnancy Note:
    • Associated with premature labor; treat all symptomatic cases during pregnancy. No need to screen asymptomatic patients unless history of preterm delivery
    • Metronidazole considered safe to use during pregnancy. No evidence of preterm birth or congenital abnormalities (Current Drug Safety 2015;10:170; Pharmacotherapy 2015;35:1152)

Alternative Regimens

  • Tinidazole (2 gm po once daily x 2 days or 1 gm po once daily x 5 days)
  • Clindamycin 300 mg bid po x 7 days
  • Clindamycin ovules 100 mg intravaginally at bedtime x 3 days
  • Secnidazole (Solosec) oral granules: one dose of 2 gm packet of granules with yogurt, applesauce or pudding over 30 minutes
    • Clinical response in 68% of patients treated with secnidazole vs 18% of placebo patients
  • For recurrent refractory infection can consider:
    • Metronidazole 0.5 gm po bid x 7 days, and then
    • Boric acid gelatin cap, 600 mg, intravaginal hs x 21 days, and then
    • Metronidazole vaginal gel, 1 applicator, 2 x/week for 16 weeks
    • Anecdotal: cure rate of 88-92%: Sex Trans Dis 36:732, 2009

Comments

  • See 2021 CDC STD Guidelines: MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021
  • Metronidazole and tinidazole do not induce acetaldehyde dehydrogenase and data to support disulfiram reactions with alcohol consumption are not strong Antimicrob Agents Chemother 64:e02167 2020
  • 2021 CDC STI guidelines no longer recommend avoidance of alcohol when receiving metronidazole for treatment of Trichomonas or bacterial vaginosis Clin Inf Dis 74:S152 2022
  • Pathogenesis is complex and incompletely understood: J Infect Dis 2016;214 (supplement S1): S1-S5
  • Reported 50% increase in cure rate if patient abstains from sex or uses condoms, Clin Infect Dis 44:213, 2007 & Clin Infect Dis 44:220, 2007.
  • Metronidazole extended release tabs 750 mg po q24h x 7 days are available, but no published data.
  • Pregnancy:
    • Metronidazole or Clindamycin oral 7-day regimens, see Canadian Ob/Gyn practice guidelines in J Obstet Gynecol Can 30:702, 2008.
    • US preventive services task force recommends NOT screening for bacterial vaginosis in pregnancy (JAMA 2020; 323:1286)
