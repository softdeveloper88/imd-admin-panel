Pneumocystis Pneumonia, PJP, Prophylaxis

Clinical Setting

  • Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia (PJP) is a potentially life-threatening fungal pneumonia in immunocompromised patients, especially HIV/AIDS.
  • Patients at risk include:
    • HIV/AIDS patients with CD4 counts <200 cells/μL.
    • Solid organ and hematopoietic stem cell transplant recipients. In case-control study, post-transplant PJP  associated with active CMV, allograft rejection, and termination of prophylaxis: Clin infect Dis 2019;68:1320
    • Cancer chemotherapy patients, especially those on high-dose steroid therapy (≥ 20 mg of daily Prednisone, or the equivalent, for over a month).
    • Selected rheumatologic diseases that require ≥ 20 mg Prednisone (or the equivalent) on a daily basis.
  • Indications for primary prophylaxis/chronic suppression:
    • HIV/AIDS: (US National Guidelines)
      • CD4 count 100–200 cells/mm3, if plasma HIV RNA level above detection limits
      • CD4 count <100 cells/mm3, regardless of plasma HIV RNA level 
    • Other populations:
      • Any patient taking equivalent of ≥ 20 mg Prednisone/day for > 1 month
      • Patients receiving Alemtuzumab (monoclonal antibody for CLL), Temozolomide (alkylating agent for astrocytoma therapy)
      • Hematopoietic and solid organ transplant recipients during immunosuppression
      • Patients given Fludarabine (purine analog used to treat hematologic malignancy)
      • Patients with Wegener's granulomatosis treated with Prednisone + cyclophosphamide
  • Indications for discontinuing primary PJP prophylaxis in patients with HIV/AIDS:
    • CD4 count increased from <200 cells/mm3 to ≥200 cells/mm3 for ≥3 months in response to ART 
    • Can consider when CD4 count is 100–200 cells/mm3 and HIV RNA remains below limit of detection of the assay used for ≥3 to 6 months 
  • Indications for discontinuing secondary prophylaxis in patients with HIV/AIDS:
    • CD4 count increased from <200 cells/mm3 to ≥200 cells/mm3 for ≥3 months as a result of ART or can consider if CD4 count is 100–200 cells/mm3 and HIV RNA remains below limits of detection of assay used for 3 to 6 months
    • For people with HIV in whom PCP occurs at a CD4 count >200 cells/mm3 while not on ART, discontinuation of prophylaxis can be considered once HIV RNA levels are suppressed to below limits of detection of the assay used for ≥3 to 6 months, although there are no data to support recommendations in this setting 
    • If an episode of PCP occurs at a CD4 count >200 cells/mm3 while a patient is on ART, it would be prudent to continue PCP prophylaxis for life, especially with plasma HIV RNA below the level of detection, regardless of how high the CD4 cell count rises as a consequence of ART 

  • Indication for restarting primary PJP prophylaxis in patients with HIV/AIDS:

    • CD4 count <100 cells/mm3 regardless of HIV RNA
    • CD4 count 100–200 cells/mm3 and HIV RNA consistently above detection limit of the assay used 

Etiologies

  • Pneumocystis jirovecii

Primary Regimens

  • TMP-SMX-DS 1 tab po q24h
  • TMP-SMX-SS 1 tab po q24h

Alternative Regimens

  • The following regimens can be used for people who are seropositive or seronegative for Toxoplasma gondii:
    • TMP-SMX 1 DS tablet PO three times weekly 
    • Dapsone 50 mg PO daily with pyrimethamine 50 mg plus leucovorin 25 mg PO weekly
    • Dapsone 200 mg plus pyrimethamine 75 mg plus leucovorin 25 mg PO weekly
    • Atovaquone 1,500 mg PO daily with food
  • The following regimens should only be used in people who are seronegative for Toxoplasma gondii:
    • Dapsone 100 mg PO daily or dapsone 50 mg PO twice daily
    • Aerosolized pentamidine 300 mg via Respirgard II nebulizer every month 
    • Intravenous pentamidine 300 mg every 28 days 

Comments

  • Patients with reactions to TMP-SMX: Dapsone is often tolerated for patients with minor reactions to TMP-SMX.  In patients with a history of severe reactions to TMP-SMX, many experts recommend avoiding dapsone.
