Moxidectin

Usage and Dosing

  • Moxidectin is an antihelmintic drug for single dose treatment of onchocerciasis (Onchocerca volvulus) in persons age ≥12 years.
    • Structurally related to ivermectin with same mechanism of action
      • Longer half-life with longer duration of effect on suppressing microfilaria in the skin.
  • Kills skin microfilariae but not adult O. volvulus parasites and the safety and efficacy of repeat treatment has not been studied.
  • FDA approved in 2018 but commercial distribution not planned at present; pivotal trial: Lancet 2018;392(10154):1207.
  • Phase 2 and 3 data show strong effect against Strongyloides stercoralis: Lancet Infect Dis 21(8):1151, 2021.
    • Non-inferior to ivermectin for strongyloidiasis: Lancet Infect Dis 2024 Feb;24(2):196.
    • Fixed dose compared to weight-based for Ivermectin
    • No data on hyperinfection potential indication alone or in combination
  • In advanced development for combination use with DEC, Albendazole, and/or Ivermectin for lymphatic filariasis: PLoS Negl Trop Dis. 2023 Sep 18;17(9):e0011633. 

Adult Dose

  • 8 mg (four 2 mg tabs) po as a single dose

Adverse Effects

  • Warnings:
    • Risk of cutaneous, ophthalmological and/or systemic adverse reactions of varying severity (Mazzotti Reaction)
    • Symptomatic orthostatic hypotension with higher frequency than ivermectin
    • Encephalopathy in Loa loa co-infected patients
    • Edema and worsening of onchodermatitis
  • Common AEs (>10%):
    • Eosinophilia, pruritus, musculoskeletal pain, headache, lymphopenia, tachycardia, rash, abdominal pain, hypotension, pyrexia, leukocytosis, influenza-like illness, neutropenia, cough, lymph node pain, dizziness, diarrhea, hyponatremia and peripheral swelling
