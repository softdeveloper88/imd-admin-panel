Hookworm

Clinical Setting

  • Anemia (severe in chronic infections).
  • Rash at cutaneous site of initial larval penetration; eosinophilic infiltrates during initial migratory phase.
  • Hookworm eggs of the two species are indistinguishable, so speciation is unnecessary.  Trichostrongylus sp eggs may appear identical.
  • Pregnancy: treatment with albendazole reduces neonatal mortality (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 2021 Apr 29;15(4):e0009282). See Comments
  • See Cutaneous larva migrans for non-human hookworm infection.

Etiologies

  • Necator americanus
  • Ancylostoma duodenale

Primary Regimens

  • Albendazole 400 mg po daily x 3 days in persons >6 yrs
    • Pediatric:
      • WHO allows Albendazole use in children 1 year of age and older and this is acknowledged as safe in CDC materials.
      • CDC recommends children aged > 2 years: 400 mg po once only;  1-2 yrs of age 200 mg po once only
      • Do not use albendazole in children <1 yr of age
    • Pregnancy / lactation:
      • WHO allows routine use of single-dose Albendazole in 2nd and 3rd trimesters. Remains FDA pregnancy risk category C.
      • Large systematic analyses show no harm in 1st trimester Albendazole (Intl J Parasitol 2019 Jun;49(7):541)
      • Hookworm anemia has severe adverse effects on pregnancy outcomes; treatment is important and albendazole may have better efficacy that other drugs during pregnancy.
      • It is not known whether albendazole is excreted in human milk. Albendazole should be used with caution in breastfeeding women. WHO allows single-dose albendazole in lactating women

Alternative Regimens

  • Mebendazole 100 mg po bid x 3 days.
    • 500 mg po single dose used in public health programs; less effective than 6 dose course (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 2022 Oct 4;16(10):e0010810)
  • Albendazole 400 mg single dose used in public health programs; much less effective than multi-day.
  • Pyrantel pamoate 11 mg/kg (to maximum dose of 1 gm) po daily x 3 days.
    • Pyrantel pamoate safe in pregnancy,  but no effect against trichiuris or hookworm.

Comments

  • Albendazole markedly superior to Mebendazole for hookworm (JAMA 2008 Apr 23;299(16):1937). 
  • Multi-day dosing recommendation in resource-rich countries due to high risk of co-infection with T. trichiura
  • Single dose therapy with Albendazole (400 mg) or Mebendazole as used in public health programs has lower cure rates and 3-day albendazole superior to 3-day mebendazole, PLoS One 6:e25003, 2011; BMJ. 2017;358:j4307; E Clin Med 2018,1:7.
    • In mass treatment settings consider Albendazole 800 mg single-dose for adults (Clin Infect Dis 2021 Jul 15;73(2):e494-e502)
  • Vermox brand Mebendazole 500 mg tablets is FDA-approved but for donation only and not sold in the U.S.
  • Albendazole 200 mg tablets available from Expert Compounding Pharmacy (see Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources)
  • Generic Albendazole (Albenza) marketed by Amneal is very costly and is difficult for retail pharmacies to access
  • Eosinophilia may be absent but eggs in stool (N Engl J Med 351:799, 2004).
  • Emodepside is promising experimental drug with excellent hookworm effect in Tanzania (N Engl J Med 2023;388:1863).
    • less well tolerated than albendazole at dose used in trials to date
