Malakoplakia

Clinical Setting

  • Malakoplakia or chronic pyelonephritis in immunosuppressed patients, macrophage dysfunction.
  • Rare granulomatous inflammatory reaction presenting as a plaque or a nodule within the genitourinary tract or, rarely in other organs or skin.
  • Presence of Michaelis-Gutmann bodies, which are foamy histiocytes with basophilic inclusions.

Etiologies

  • E. coli
  • Other Gram-negatives

Primary Regimens

  • Surgical excision + Bethanechol chloride + (Ciprofloxacin or TMP-SMX)

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • If possible reduce or eliminate immunosuppression.
  • References: Nat Rev Nephrol 5:418, 2009; Transpl Infect Dis 14:E137, 2012; StatPearls Publishing; 2022 Jan. 2022 Oct 31.
