Cabenuva
Tradename: Cabenuva

Usage and Dosing

  • Cabotegravir (CAB) injectable + rilpivirine (RPV) injectable (Cabenuva) is a two-drug co-packaged product indicated to replace the current antiretroviral regimen in adult patients with HIV-1 infection who are virologically suppressed (RNA <50 copies/mL) on a stable regimen with no history of treatment failure and no known or suspected resistance to either component.
  • Lead-in dosing with oral cabotegravir (30 mg q24h) and oral rilpivirine (25 mg q24h) for one month before initiation of injectable dosing to assess tolerance is optional. Alternatively, patients can proceed directly to IM CAB/RPV on the last day of their current ARV regimen.
  • Cabotegravir and rilpivirine injections are extended-release suspensions for IM administration that do not require further dilution or reconstitution. Concentrations: CAB 600 mg/3 mL, RPV 900 mg/3 mL.
  • Administer injections at separate gluteal sites (on opposite sides or 2 cm apart) during the same visit. The ventrogluteal site is recommended.
  • Monitor patients for approximately ten minutes for post-injection reactions. A 23-gauge, 1½ inch IM needle is recommended (provided in the packaging). Longer needles (2 inch) should be used in patients with BMI >30 kg/m2.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).

Adult Dose

  • Monthly schedule
    • Initiation injection (one)
      • Begin on the last day of oral lead-in or the current oral ARV regimen.
      • Dose: CAB 600 mg IM + RPV 900 mg IM.
    • Continuation injections
      • Begin one month after the initiation injection.
      • Dose: CAB 400 mg IM + RPV 600 mg IM every month, with allowance for a ±7 day window.
  • Every-2-month schedule
    • Initiation injections (two)
      • Begin on the last day of oral lead-in or the current oral ARV regimen.
      • Dose: CAB 600 mg IM + RPV 900 mg IM. Repeat in one month.
    • Continuation injections
      • Begin two months after the second initiation injection.
      • Dose: CAB 600 mg IM + RPV 900 mg IM every two months, with allowance for a ±7 day window.
  • Switching from monthly to every-2-month schedule: administer CAB 600 mg + RPV 900 mg one month after the last monthly continuation injection, then every 2 months thereafter.
  • Switching from every-2-month to monthly schedule: administer CAB 400 mg + RPV 600 mg two months after the last every-2-month continuation injection, then monthly thereafter.
  • Planned missed injections (monthly dosing schedule):
    • If the patient plans to miss a scheduled injection visit by >7 days, administer oral therapy (CAB 30 mg q24h, RPV 25 mg q24h), or any other fully suppressive oral ARV regimen, for up to two months to replace missed injection visits.
    • Begin oral therapy approximately one month after the last dual injections, and continue until injection dosing is restarted.
      • If ≤2 months have elapsed since the last dual injections, resume monthly injections (CAB 400 mg, RPV 600 mg) ASAP.
      • If >2 months have elapsed since the last last dual injections, re-initiate the patient and then resume the monthly injection schedule.
  • Unplanned missed injections (monthly dosing schedule):
    • If a monthly dual injection is missed or delayed by >7 days and oral therapy has not been taken in the interim, assess patient to determine if resumption of injections is appropriate. If so, proceed as determined by elapsed time since last dual injections (see above).
  • Planned missed injections (every-2-month dosing schedule):
    • If the patient plans to miss a scheduled injection visit by >7 days, administer oral therapy (CAB 30 mg q24h, RPV 25 mg q24h), or any other fully suppressive oral ARV regimen, for up to two months to replace one missed injection visit.
    • Begin oral therapy approximately two months after the last dual injections, continue until injection dosing is restarted.
    • If the second initiation dual injections are to be missed:
      • If ≤2 months have elapsed since the first initiation injections, administer CAB 600 mg + RPV 900 mg ASAP, then follow every-2-month dosing schedule.
      • If >2 months have elapsed since the first initiation injections, re-initiate the patient and then follow the every-2-month dosing schedule.
    • If a continuation dual injection is to be missed:
      • If ≤3 months have elapsed since the last dual injections, administer CAB 600 mg + RPV 900 mg ASAP, then follow every-2-month dosing schedule.
      • If >3 months have elapsed since the last dual injections, re-initiate the patient and then follow the every-2-month dosing schedule.
  • Unplanned missed injections (every-2-month dosing schedule):
    • If an every-two months dual injection is missed or delayed by >7 days and oral therapy has not been taken in the interim, assess patient to determine if resumption of injections is appropriate. If so, proceed as determined by elapsed time since last dual injections (see above).

Adverse Effects

  • Hypersensitivity (signs and symptoms include rash, fever, malaise, fatigue, myalgia, arthralgia, blisters, mucosal involvement, conjunctivitis, facial edema, hepatitis, eosinophilia, angioedema, dyspnea).
  • Post-rilpivirine injection reactions (<1% of patients): dyspnea, agitation, cramps, flushing, sweating, oral numbness, changes in BP. May have been associated with inadvertent (partial) IV administration.
  • Hepatotoxicity (monitor transaminases).
  • Depressive disorders
  • QT prolongation (associated with rilpivirine).
  • CPK elevation following intramuscular administration.
  • Pain at injection site is common, but typically short-lived resolving days after injection.
  • Altered ACTH stimulation test of uncertain clinical significance.
