Bronchiolitis

Clinical Setting

  • Wheezy bronchitis, expiratory wheezing in infants and young children (Age < 5 years).
  • Respiratory Syncytial Virus (RSV) is the most important etiology.
  • Other viruses can cause similar presentation
  • Rapid diagnosis of RSV can be made with antigen detection methods. DFA and multiplex PCR can diagnose all usual viral etiologies.
  • Antibiotics should not be used for bronchiolitis unless there is clear evidence of a secondary bacterial infection.

Etiologies

  • Respiratory Syncytial Virus
  • Influenza
  • Human metapneumovirus
  • Parainfluenza
  • Adenovirus
  • Rhinovirus
  • Coronavirus
  • SARS-CoV-2

Primary Regimens

  • Antibiotics are not useful, mainstay of therapy is hydration and oxygen. Suction helpful to clear airway (see Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2011 Jun 15;(6):CD005189).
  • Recent studies suggest benefit to 3% nebulized hypertonic saline - may be considered.
  • Trial of inhaled bronchodilators is generally not recommended or useful but can be considered to evaluate clinical response.
  • Routine use of steroids not recommended based on absence of efficacy by AAP.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Antibiotics are not indicated
  • Adherence to a bronchiolitis clinical pathway decreases antibiotic use, costs and length of stay (Pediatrics. 2017 Mar;139(3). pii: e20163432)

Comments

  • For treatment and prevention of RSV specifically, see Respiratory Syncytial Virus.
