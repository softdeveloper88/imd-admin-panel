Prostatitis, Bacterial, Acute

Clinical Setting

  • Etiologies and treatment of acute prostatitis vary based on patient's age.
  • Acute prostatitis typically presents with fever, chills, malaise, myalgia, dysuria, pelvic/perineal pain, and cloudy urine (review Clin Infect Dis 50:1641, 2010).
  • 2015 Guidelines: BJU Int 2015, 116:509.
  • Complications include: bacteremia, epididymitis, prostatic abscess, extension or metastasis to other locations (e.g., joints), and conversion to chronic prostatitis.

Etiologies

  • Age < 35 years 
    • N. gonorrheae
    • C. trachomatis
  • Age ≥35 years
    • Enterobacteriaceae
    • Coliforms
    • Enterococcus sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Uncomplicated, with risk of STD (Age < 35 years):
    • Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM x 1 dose or Cefixime 400 mg po x 1 dose; then Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x 10 days 
  • Uncomplicated with low risk of STD:
    • Levofloxacin 500-750 mg IV/po once daily or Ciprofloxacin (500-750 mg po or 400 mg IV) twice daily. Treat for 10-14 days (minimum)
    • TMP-SMX 1 DS tablet (160 mg TMP component) po bid x 10–14 days (minimum)
  • Some authorities recommend 4 to 6 weeks of therapy, though this is typically used in Chronic Prostatitis 
  • Use of adjunctive uroselective alpha-adrenergicantagonists (e.g. tamsulosin, alfuzosin and silodosin) should be considered to reduce symptomatology (e.g., poor urinary stream, hesitancy)

Alternative Regimens

  • Azithromycin 1 gm q week x 4 weeks for treatment of proven Chlamydial prostatitis
  • If resistant enterobacteriaceae, use Ertapenem 1 gm IV qd for at least 2 weeks (4 weeks may be required).
  • If resistant pseudomonas, use Imipenem 500 mg IV q6h OR Meropenem 500 mg IV q8h x 4 weeks.

Comments

  • Fluoroquinolones are no longer recommended for gonococcal infections, because of significant rates of resistance.
  • Levofloxacin: usual suggested dose is 500 mg; some prefer 750 mg dose because of higher drug levels and reports of treatment failures at the lower dose
  • Use antimicrobials with ability to penetrate the prostate, such as fluoroquinolones (non-gonococcal) or TMP/SMX (high degree of lipid solubility, low degree of ionization, high dissociation constant, low protein binding, and small molecular size).
  • In AIDS patients, prostate may be focus of Cryptococcus neoformans.
  • Treat as an acute urinary infection for 14 days (not single dose regimen; may need 4-6 weeks of therapy).
  • If uncertain about C. trachomatis and/or N. gonorrhoeae do urine test.
