Lamivudine
Tradename: Epivir, Epivir-HBV, 3TC

Usage and Dosing

  • Lamivudine (3TC) is indicated as part of a nucleoside backbone for a 3-drug regimen for HIV. Usually paired with dolutegravir or tenofovir DF often as part of a fixed dose combination. Can be used with other nucleoside agents but should not be used with Emtricitabine (FTC).
  • Lamivudine is used for both Hepatitis B and HIV infections, but should not be used as monotherapy for either of these infections owing to rapid development of resistance 
  •  Usually well-tolerated. Risk of exacerbation of Hep B after stopping 3TC. Monitor at least several months after stopping 3TC in Hep B patients; some may need anti-HBV therapy.
  • Generic formulations: 100 mg, 150 mg, 300 mg tablets
  • Black Box warnings: caution, dose is lower than HIV dose, so must exclude co-infection with HIV before using this formulation.

Adverse Effects

  • Headache
  • Lactic acidosis/hepatic steatosis; severe exacerbation of liver disease can occur on discontinuance.
  • YMDD-mutants resistant to Lamivudine may emerge on treatment.

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • Hepatitis B virus (HBV)
  • HIV
