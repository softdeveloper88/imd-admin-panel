Ibalizumab-uiyk
Tradename: Trogarzo

Usage and Dosing

  • Ibalizumab-uiyk is a genetically engineered monoclonal antibody that prevents HIV fusion and entry into the CD4 cell.
  • Its primary indication is for patients harboring multi-class resistant HIV for whom fewer than 2 fully active antiretroviral agents are available.
  • Owing to its unique mechanism of action, ibalizumab-uiyk is active against most viruses that harbor NRTI, NNRTI, PI, and INSTI resistance mutations.
  • The requirement for IV dosing and the cost of the medication (~ $4,500 / dose, WAC) limit its use.

Adult Dose

  • 2000 mg IV x1 (loading dose), then 800 mg IV every 2 weeks.
  • The loading dose is administered as a diluted IV infusion (over 30 minutes). Subsequent maintenance doses may be administered as a diluted IV infusion (over 15 minutes), or undiluted IV push.
  • Prepare infusion in 250 mL bag of normal saline. Do not use other dilutents. Once diluted, administer immediately. If not administered immediately, use within 4 hours if stored at room temperature or within 24 hours if refrigerated (2-8°C).
  • Observe patient for 1 hour after completion of first infusion. If no infusion-related adverse effects are observed, the post-infusion observation time can be reduced to 15 minutes.

Adverse Effects

  • Hypersensitivity reactions, including infusion-related reactions and anaphylactic reactions:
    • Symptoms: dyspnea, angioedema, wheezing, chest pain or tightness, cough, hot flush, nausea, vomiting.
  • Diarrhea.
  • Dizziness.
  • Rash.
  • Immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome (IRIS).
  • Based on animal data, may cause reversible immunosuppression in infants exposed to ibalizumab-uiyk in utero. Seek expert guidance. The safety of administering live or live-attenuated vaccines in exposed infants is not known.
