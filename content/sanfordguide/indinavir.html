Indinavir
Tradename: Crixivan

Usage and Dosing

  • Indinavir (IDV) is indicated for use as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually combined with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents (e.g., TDF-FTC or ABC-3TC). Must be used with Ritonavir-boosting.  Recommended in treatment experienced patients where resistance testing indicates activity of Indinavir. Not recommended as part of an initial regimen for naive patients owing to drug interaction and adverse event profile.
  • With Indinavir, important to maintain hydration to prevent kidney stones.
  • Nephrolithiasis, nausea, inconsequential ↑ of indirect bilirubin (jaundice in Gilbert syndrome), ↑ AST/ALT, headache, asthenia, blurred vision, metallic taste, hemolysis. ↑ urine WBC (>100/hpf) has been associated with nephritis/medullary calcification, cortical atrophy.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • Also see ARV Drug-Drug Interactions, and for class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.

Adult Dose

Adult Dose

800 mg Indinavir + 100 mg Ritonavir po q12h (Preferred)

 

Two 400 mg caps (800 mg) po q8h, without food or with light meal (not recommended to use without Ritonavir boosting owing to high dose frequency). 
Can take with enteric-coated Videx.

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Kidney stones. Due to Indinavir crystals in collecting system. Nephrolithiasis in 12% of adults, higher in pediatrics. Minimize risk with good hydration (at least 48 oz. water/day) (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 42:332, 1998).
    • Tubulointerstitial nephritis/renal cortical atrophy reported in association with asymptomatic ↑ urine WBC.
    • Severe hepatitis and hepatic failure reported.
    • Hemolytic anemia reported.
  • Most Common:
    • ↑ in indirect bilirubin 10–15% (≥2.5 mg/dL), with overt jaundice especially likely in those with Gilbert syndrome (J Infect Dis 192: 1381, 2005).  Not harmful; patients complain of aesthetics.
    • Nausea 12%, vomiting 4%, diarrhea 5%.
    • Paronychia of big toe reported (Clin Infect Dis 32:140, 2001).
