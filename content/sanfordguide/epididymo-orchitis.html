Epididymo-orchitis

Clinical Setting

  • Etiologies and treatment differentiated based on patient's age.
  • Midstream pyuria, dysuria; scrotal pain, erythema, and edema; thickened epididymis, swollen testis; typically unilateral
  • Also, men who have sex with men (MSM), the insertive partner in anal intercourse.
  • Rule out testicular torsion (ultrasonograthy useful to distinguish torsion from epididymitis/orchitis).
  • Differential diagnosis, if symptoms don't improve within 72 hours after treatment: tumor, abscess, infarction, testicular cancer, TB, and fungal epididymitis.
  • Reviews: Clin Infect Dis 61:S770, 2015; Eur J Clin Microbiol Infect Dis 37:1001, 2018.

Etiologies

  • Age ≤ 35 years
    • N. gonorrhoeae
    • C. trachomatis
    • Enterobacterales (occasional)
  • Age > 35 years
    • Enterobacterales
    • Less common causes:
      • Mycobacterium tuberculosis, Brucella spp., B. pseudomallei, mumps virus, BCG, fungal (histoplasmosis, coccidioides), Bechet's

Primary Regimens

  • All: Bed rest, scrotal elevation, and analgesics.
  • Age ≤ 35 years: Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM x 1 + Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x 10 days (1 g Ceftriaxone if >150 kg weight)
  • Age > 35 years: Levofloxacin 500-750 mg IV/po once daily x 10-14 days (see Comments)
  • In MSM, there is risk of both STI and enteric organisms so treat with Ceftriaxone + (Levofloxacin 750 mg qd x10-14 days or Ofloxacin 300 mg po bid x 10-14 days)
    • Note: Levo and Oflox are the only FQs with known reliable activity against Chlamydia.

Alternative Regimens

  • Age ≤ 35 years: Levofloxacin 500 mg po once daily x 10 days
  • Age > 35 years:
    • Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 gm IV q6h or Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 po bid
    • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h
    • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h
    • TMP-SMX 1 DS po bid x 10-14 days if STI unlikely and local resistance <20% for E. coli

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Increasing incidence of fluoroquinolone-resistant enteric organisms a potential concern.
  • If enterobacteriaceae are suspected, choice of agent should be guided by local antibiotic susceptibility patterns.

Comments

  • Do urine NAAT (nucleic acid amplification test) to ensure absence of N. gonorrhoeae with concomitant risk of fluoroquinolone-resistant gonorrhoeae or of chlamydia if using agents without reliable activity in patients age < 35 years.
  • If STI etiology documented or suspected, sexual partners within the past 60 days should be evaluated.
  • See 2021 CDC STD guidelines MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021
  • Chronic epidyidmitis is symptoms >6 weeks and is associated with granulomatous disease including tuberculosis. 
  • Review article of differential diagnosis of scrotal pain and masses: Am Fam Physician 89:723, 2014.
  • Complete resolution of discomfort may take weeks after antibiotic treatment is completed, but swelling and tenderness that persist should be evaluated.
  • Levofloxacin: usual recommended dose is 500 mg; consider 750 mg dose because of higher drug levels and reports of treatment failures at the lower dose (similar to prostatitis).
