Gonorrhea: Gonococcal Pharyngitis

Clinical Setting

  • Patients with gonorrhea at another site (e.g., urethra, cervix, or rectum) should have a pharyngeal NAAT for the gonococcus.
  • Usually asymptomatic
  • Pharyngeal Nucleic acid amplification tests (NAAT) can be 'falsely positive' owing to the presence of other Neisseria sp. in the oropharynx, but the CDC still considers NAAT the test of choice.
  • Culture of GC most definitive; though NAAT can be used as a screening test:
    • Urine NAAT should NOT be used for suspected gonococcus in pharynx (or rectum); specific testing at these sites is required.

Diagnosis

  • Pharyngeal Nucleic acid amplification tests (NAAT) can be 'falsely positive' owing to the presence of other Neisseria sp. in the oropharynx, but the CDC still considers NAAT the test of choice.
  • Culture of GC most definitive; though NAAT can be used as a screening test:
    • Urine NAAT should NOT be used for suspected gonococcus in pharynx (or rectum); specific testing at these sites is required.

Etiologies

  • Neisseria gonorrheae

Primary Regimens

  • Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM x 1 dose 
  • European Guidelines: Ceftriaxone 1 gm IM + Azithromycin 2 gm po (see Comments)
  • Due to difficulty in eradication pharyngeal gonococci, a repeat "test of cure" pharyngeal NAAT is recommended 14 days after therapy.

Alternative Regimens

  • Pharyngeal infection is very difficult to treat. If allergy to Ceftriaxone, investigate reaction thoroughly.
  • If serious allergic reaction (e.g. Stevens Johnson) would consult Infectious Diseases to determine best regimen.
  • If alternative regimen used, must perform test of cure.

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Emerging antimicrobial resistance is an ongoing problem with resistance to ceftriaxone and high level resistance to azithromycin reported Nat Rev Urol 14:139, 2017.
  • Due to resistance, do not use a fluoroquinolone.
  • Oral cephalosporins are no longer recommended for treatment of N. gonorrhoeae due to high level resistance (MMWR 61:590, 2012).

Comments

  • CDC 2020 revised gonorrhea guidelines (MMWR 69:1911, 2020) and CDC 2021 STD guidelines (MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021) no longer recommend routine dual treatment of gonorrhea and C. trachomatis. Rather treatment should be guided by NAAT.
  • European gonorrhea guidelines (IUSTI 2020) recommend dual treatment and higher doses Ceftriaxone 1gm + Azithromycin 2 gm with test of cure for all gonorrhea cases.
  • In the pharynx, spectinomycin, cefixime, cefpodoxime and cefuroxime are not effective.
  • For extensively resistant organisms ertapenem 1g IM x3 days has been used in a limited number of cases (IUSTI 2020).

  • Review of evidence for CDC guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 74:S95 2022 (Gonorrhea) and Clin Infect Dis 74:S112 2022 (Chlamydia)
