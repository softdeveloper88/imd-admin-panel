Fosamprenavir
Tradename: Lexiva

Usage and Dosing

  • Fosamprenavir is a prodrug of Amprenavir.
  • Fosamprenavir (FPV) is indicated for use as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually combined with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents (e.g., TDF-FTC, TAF-FTC or ABC-3TC). Can be used without Ritonavir boosting, but Ritonavir-boosting preferred in most situations.  No longer listed as an alternative boosted PI for first-line or second-line therapy by current Guidelines. 
  • Contains sulfa moiety. Potential for serious drug interactions (see label).
  • Rash, including Stevens-Johnson syndrome.
  • Fosamprenavir once daily regimens: (1) not recommended for PI experienced patients, (2) additional Ritonavir needed if given with Efavirenz (see label).
  • Boosted twice daily regimen is recommended for PI-experienced patients.
  • Potential for PI cross-resistance with Darunavir (similar chemical structure).
  • Pediatrics:
    • Unboosted FPV is FDA-approved for ARV-naive children aged 2-5 yrs, but not recommended by the DHHS Pediatric ARV Guideline Panel because of low exposures.
    • Boosted FPV is FDA-approved for ARV-naive infants ≥4 wks and treatment-experienced infants ≥6 months, but the Panel does not recommend use in infants aged <6 months because of low exposures. If used in infants as young as 4 wks, it should only be administered to infants born at 38 weeks gestation or greater.
    • Once-daily dosing is not recommended for any pediatric patient.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • See ARV Drug-Drug Interactions and for class-wide adverse effects, ARV Drugs Overview.

Adult Dose

Usual Dose

1400 mg (two 700 mg tabs) po bid (not recommended)
OR with Ritonavir: [1400 mg Fosamprenavir (2 tabs) + Ritonavir 200 mg] po q24h 
OR [1400 mg Fosamprenavir (2 tabs) + Ritonavir 100 mg] po q24h (preferred dose for treatment naive patients)
OR [700 mg Fosamprenavir (1 tab) + Ritonavir 100 mg] po bid (preferred dose for treatment experienced patients)

NOTE: No ritonavir boosting in hepatic impairment.

Mild impairment (Child-Pugh Class A) 700 mg q12h

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Rarely Stevens-Johnson syndrome, hemolytic anemia. Pro-drug of Amprenavir. Contains sulfa moiety. Angioedema and nephrolithiasis reported in post-marketing experience. Potential increased risk of MI (see FDA label).
  • Most Common:
    • Skin rash ~ 20% (moderate or worse in 3–8%), nausea, headache, diarrhea
