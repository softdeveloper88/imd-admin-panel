Mycobacterium marinum

Clinical Setting

  • "Fish-tank" granuloma.
  • Skin infection characterized by single papulonodular, verrucose, or ulcerated granulomatous lesions, usually on the hand or lower arm.
  • History of contact with aquarium, fish, other water sources typically is present.
  • Skin biopsy to establish diagnosis; incubate cultures at 28ºC to 30ºC to optimize growth.
  • Surgical excision recommended if possible.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium marinum

Primary Regimens

  • Two active agents 
    • Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid + Ethambutol 25 mg/kg po q24h
    • Rifampin 600 mg po q24h + Ethambutol 25 mg/kg po q24h

Alternative Regimens

  • For deep tissue involvement consider using a three drug combination therapy, e.g.,  Rifampin 600 mg po q24h + Ethambutol 25 mg/kg po q24h + Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid, at least initially
  • Rifabutin 300 mg may be substituted for Rifampin.

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy not well established but treat for 1-2 months after resolution of skin lesions in immunocompetent patients and for a total duration of at least 6 months in immunocompromised patients
  • Resistant to Isoniazid, and Pyrazinamide.
  • Also susceptible in vitro to Linezolid and Moxifloxacin, although in vivo efficacy not established
