Maraviroc
Tradename: Selzentry

Usage and Dosing

  • Maraviroc (MVC) is indicated for use as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually combined with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents (e.g., TDF-FTC, TAF-FTC, or ABC-3TC). Considered an alternative regimen for naive patients; used in treatment experienced patients when the tropism test indicates susceptibility to maraviroc (R5 tropic virus) .
  • Black Box Warning: Hepatotoxicity, may be preceded by rash, ↑eosinophils or IgE. NB: no hepatotoxicity was noted in MVC trials.
  • Stevens Johnson syndrome reported.
  • Data lacking in hepatic/renal insufficiency; ↑ concern with either could ↑risk of ↓BP.
  • Currently for treatment-experienced patients with multi-resistant strains; recently approved for treatment naive patients (alternative regimen by HHS Guideline).
  • Document CCR5-tropic virus before use, as treatment failures associated with appearance of CXCR-4 or mixed-tropic virus.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive)

Adult Dose

  • 300 mg bid if coadministered with non-interacting drugs (including NRTIs, enfuvirtide, nevirapine, and raltegravir)
  • 150 mg bid if coadministered with potent CYP3A inhibitors (with or without a potent CYP3A inducer), including all PIs
  • 600 mg bid if coadministered with potent CYP3A inducers (without a potent CYP3A inhibitor), including efavirenz and etravirine

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Black box warning: Hepatotoxicity. May be preceded by allergic features. Black box inserted owing to concern about potential CCR5 class effect; possible case reported on label.  No increased incidence of hepatic disease in clinical trials or in post-marketing follow up so far.
    • Use with caution in patients with Hep B or Hep C. Cardiac ischemia/infarction in 1.3%. May cause ↓BP, syncope.
    • Significant interactions with CYP3A inducers/inhibitors. Long-term risk of malignancy unknown. Stevens-Johnson syndrome reported post-marketing. Generally favorable safety profile during trial of ART-naïve individuals (J Inf Dis 201:803, 2010).
  • Most Common:
    • With ARV background: cough 13%, fever 12%, rash 10%, abdominal pain 8%. Also, dizziness, myalgia, arthralgias. ↑ risk of URI, HSV infection.
