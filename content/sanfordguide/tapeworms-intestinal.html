Tapeworms, Intestinal

Clinical Setting

  • Asymptomatic or mild non-specific GI symptoms
  • Presenting symptom usually passage of proglottids per rectum with taeniasis
  • B12 malabsorption with Diphyllobothrium
  • Dibothriocephalus spp tapeworms infecting humans have a scolex bearing bothria (grooves), instead of suckers as in the Taenia spp
  • See also Cysticercosis (larval T. solium infection); no larval disease with other human Taenia species

Etiologies

  • Taenia saginata (beef)
  • Taenia solium (pork)
  • Taenia suihominis  (also called Taenia asiatica) (pork) (Korea, China, Taiwan, Indonesia, Thailand)
  • Dibothriocephalus latus (Diphyllobothrium latum) (fish). Widespread
  • Adenocephalus pacificus (Diphyllobothrium pacificum) (fish). Pacific coast South America.
  • Dibothriocephalus nihonkaiense (fish). East Asia
  • Other Dibothriocephalus spp.(Diphyllobothrium sp.) (fish)
  • Dipylidium caninum (dog)
  • Hymenolepis diminuta (rat)
  • Hymenolepis nana (Rodentia) (humans)

Primary Regimens

  • Fish, dog, beef, pork tapeworm infecting humans:
    • Praziquantel 5-10 mg/kg po x 1 dose (adult or child same)
    • Praziquantel is not approved for treatment of children less than 4 years old, but this drug has been used successfully to treat cases of D. caninum infection in children as young as 6 months.
    • Systematic review indicates higher T. solium cure rates with 10 mg/kg  (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 14:e0007873, 2020)
  • Rat (H. diminuta), human tapeworm (H.nana):
    • Praziquantel 25 mg/kg po x 1 dose (adult or child same).
  • Attention to personal hygiene after therapy for T. solium as eggs from proglottids can be ingested and result in cysticercosis.
  • After treatment, all stools should be collected for 3 days to search for tapeworm proglottids for species identification and the scolex as an indication of cure as a residual scolex will regenerate the tapeworm. Stools should be re-examined for cure 1 and 3 months after treatment.

Alternative Regimens

  • Fish, beef, pork, dog tapeworm:
    • Niclosamide 2 gm po once for adults or 50 mg/kg orally once for children
  • Human (H. nana), rat tapeworm:  
    • Niclosamide not available in the US.
      • Adult: Niclosamide 2 gm in a single dose daily x 7 days
      • Child:
        • Wt 11-34 kg: Niclosamide 1 gm in a single dose on day 1 then 500 mg/day po x 6 days
        • Wt > 34 kg: Niclosamide 1.5 gm in a single dose on day 1 then 1 gm/day po x 6 days.
    • Nitazoxanide
      • Adult: Nitazoxanide 500 mg po twice daily x 3 days
      • Child:
        • Age 12-47 months: Nitazoxanide 100 mg po twice daily x 3 days
        • Age 4-11 years: Nitazoxanide 200 mg po twice daily x 3 days

Comments

  • Comparative study of Nitazoxamide, Aalbendazole and Praziquantel (Trans R Soc Trop Med Hyg. 2002, 96:193)
  • WHO encourages use of Praziquantel in any stage of pregnancy.  FDA category B.
  • Albendazole, 400mg daily for three days, is a poor option for intestinal taeniasis with data restricted to very small numbers of cases of T. solium or T. saginata.
  • If patient has neurocysticercosis concomitant with intestinal T. solium seizures or increased intracranial pressure may occur with treatment.  Avoid Albendazole for this reason.
