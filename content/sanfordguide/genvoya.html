Genvoya
Tradename: Elvitagravir + Cobicistat + Emtricitabine + Tenofovir alafenamide fumarate

Usage and Dosing

  • Genvoya is a once daily, fixed dose 4 drug combination regimen for patients with HIV-1 infection.
  • Each tablet contains Elvitegravir 150 mg + Cobicistat 150 mg + Emtricitabine 200 mg + Tenofovir alafenamide 10 mg
  • TAF is a phosphonoamidate prodrug of Tenofovir with ARV efficacy similar to TDF at less than one-tenth the dose.
  • See individual components for drug-specific data:
    • Elvitegravir (EVG)
    • Cobicistat (Cobi)
    • Emtricitabine (FTC)
    • Tenofovir alfenamide fumarate (TAF)
  • For class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • Not recommended for use in pregnancy due to substantially lowered exposures of elvitegravir and cobicistat. Treatment should not be initiated in pregnant women, and an alternative regimen should be used in women who become pregnant on Genvoya therapy; continuation with frequent viral load monitoring can be considered (HHS guidelines, Dec 2019).

Adult Dose

  • 1 tab po q24h with food
  • Tablet: EVG 150 mg + Cobi 150 mg + FTC 200 mg + TAF 10 mg
