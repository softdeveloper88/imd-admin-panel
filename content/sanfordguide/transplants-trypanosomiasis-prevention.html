Transplants, Trypanosomiasis Prevention

Clinical Setting

  • Reactivation of Trypanosoma cruzi infection in a patient receiving a heart transplant for chagasic cardiomyopathy.
  • Reactivation of T. cruzi infection in a latently infected patient receiving a non-cardiac solid organ transplant (Clin Infect Dis 29:561,1999).
  • Primary T. cruzi infection following receipt of a T. cruzi infected organ to an uninfected recipient (donor-derived infection) (Am J Transplant 13:2418, 2013).
  • Primary T. cruzi infection can occur through transfusion of blood products around the time of solid organ or hematopoietic stem cell transplants (blood products in the US are screened).

Etiologies

  • Trypanosoma cruzi

Primary Regimens

  • Adult (Age ≥12 years): Benznidazole 5-7 mg/kg/day po divided into 2 doses (q12h) x 60 days 

    • Due to adverse effects use no more than 5 mg/kg and some experienced clinicians treat adults with 300 mg per day for 60 days, regardless of body weight or give 300 mg per day but prolong treatment to complete the total dose corresponding to 5 mg/kg per day for 60 days

    • Benznidazole availability: Exeltis, Inc. Tel: +1 877-303-7181;  www.benznidazoletablets.com
      • Available drugs: Benznidazole (commercial FDA 12.5 & 100 mg approved tabs for age 2-12 yrs, CDC advocates off-label in adults)
      • Fast-access program by contacting Exeltis (Foundation Care) supplies at no charge for under-insured patients
  • Pediatric (Age <12 years): Benznidazole 7.5 mg/kg/day po in 2 doses (q12h) x 60 days
    • Pediatric tablet formulation (LAFEPE and ELEA) used in South America not widely available

Alternative Regimens

  • Nifurtimox 8-10 mg/kg/day divided 3x/day for 90 days

Comments

  • Prophylactic therapy in asymptomatic patients is not recommended and preemptive monitoring with PCR and peripheral smear are preferred to determine the need for treatment. Testing can be coordinated through the CDC.
  • Recommendations on management of T. cruzi infection in transplant recipients: Am J Transplant 11:672, 2011; Transplant Proc 42:3354, 2010; Am J Transplant 13(S4):280, 2013. Clin Microbiol Rev, 2024.
  • See also American Trypanosomiasis, Chagas Disease.
