Usage and Dosing

  • Gentamicin, approved in 1963, is an aminoglycoside that is active against a wide range of gram-negative bacteria, and usually provides synergistic killing vs. certain gram-positive cocci.
  • Naturally produced by Micromonospora purpurea, a soil actinomycete.
  • No activity against anaerobes, poor activity in a low pH environment.
  • Not considered a reliable choice for P. aeruginosa.

    Adverse Effects

    • Gentamicin, like all aminoglycosides, is associated with three major groups of adverse effects:
      • Nephrotoxicity (acute tubular necrosis)
      • Ototoxicity (cochlear and vestibular). Review: Med J Aust 2012;196:701.
      • Enhancement of neuromuscular blockade (rare)
    • See Aminoglycosides, Overview for details.

    Pregnancy Risk

    FDA Risk Category 1 Associated with fetal harm, especially 1st TM use. Avoid in pregnancy, although short-term use may be acceptable if benefit outweighs risk. Topical use probably safe.
    Use during Lactation Probably safe, monitor infant for GI toxicity
