Gangrene, Muscle

Clinical Setting

  • Gas gangrene, myonecrosis; usually contaminated traumatic wound infection.
  • Can also occur spontaneously without trauma.
  • Rarely a marker for occult gastrointestinal malignancy.
  • Surgical debridement is the primary therapy.

Etiologies

  • C. perfringens
  • Other histotoxic Clostridium sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Surgical debridement + (Clindamycin 900 mg IV q8h) + (Penicillin G 24 million units/day divided q4–6h IV)
  • Hyperbaric oxygen is adjunctive and efficacy debated. Probably worthwhile, if easily available, but always after surgical debridement and antibiotic therapy.

Alternative Regimens

  • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h or Erythromycin 1 gm q6h IV (not by bolus)

Comments

  • Clindamycin is given to decrease toxin production.
  • IDSA guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 59: 147, 2014
  • Potential alternatives (in vitro data): tetracycline, erythromycin, chloramphenicol, and metronidazole
