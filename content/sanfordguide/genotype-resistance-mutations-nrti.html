Genotype Resistance Mutations: NRTIs

NRTI Genotype Resistance Mutations

What You Need To Know

  • For nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors (NRTIs), you need to know which drugs are analogs of the same nucleoside.
  • Resistance to one thymidine analog forecasts resistance to all thymidine analogs as exemplified by the thymidine analog mutation (TAM) resistance pattern.

Interpreting Results Reports

  • Gene resistance mutations are reported as follows:
    • Gene (or codon) number is given plus the identified amino acid change.
    • Codon number is preceded by letter code indicating the amino acid encoded in wild-type virus.
    • For example: M46I means codon (gene 46), Isoleucine (resistance codon) has replaced Methionine (wild type virus codon).

Selected NRTI Genotype Resistance Mutations

  • The table contains selected genotype mutations that result in NRTI resistance.
  • The major mutations that forecast resistance are:
    • M184V → Lamivudine, emtricitabine & partial abacavir resistance
    • K65R → All NRTIs except zidovudine
    • Q151M → All NRTIs except possibly tenofovir
  • Numerous nucleoside (or nucleotide) analog RTI mutations (e.g., M47L, L210W, T215Y) may increase susceptibility to NNRTIs in NNRTI-treatment-naïve patients.
  • For complete resistance mutations data, see http://hivdb.stanford.edu and www.iasusa.org.
