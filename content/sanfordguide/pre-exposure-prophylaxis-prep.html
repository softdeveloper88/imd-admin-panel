Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP)

Treatment as Prevention

  • Antiretroviral therapy (ART) is effective in preventing HIV infection (referred to as TasP).
    • Study compared rates of transmission among discordant heterosexual couples in Africa. HIV+ partners randomized to receive early ART. (CD4 > 350 cells/μL) vs. deferred ART (CD4 < 250 cells/μL). There were 27 transmissions in the deferred group (n=882) vs. 1 in the immediate Rx group (n=880); a 96% reduction in transmission from early ART.
    • First evidence of high level reduction in transmission from ART alone in a randomized trial (N Engl J Med 365(6):493, 2011).
    • Partners Study:  among MSM and heterosexual couples having > 77,000 condomless sex acts, there were no cases of transmission of HIV from seropositive partner (with suppressed HIV RNA) to their seronegative partner (JAMA 316:171, 2016;  Lancet 393: 2428 - 38, 2019)
    • Taken together, these data have led to the public policy of "U = U":  Undetectable = Untransmissable

Prophylactic ART (PrEP)

  • Prophylactic Pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) is effective as a preventative measure among those at high risk of HIV acquisition, such as men who have sex with men (MSM).

  •  PrEP is considered a key component in a comprehensive HIV prevention agenda globally, especially when used to prevent transmission among MSM.  Estimated life-time risk of acquisition of HIV in MSM is 1:6; for black MSM, the risk is 1:2.  See 2023 PrEP Guidelines.

  • Pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) for men who have sex with men (MSM):
    • Truvada (Emtricitabine + TDF) one tablet po once daily OR
    • Descovy (Emtricitabine + TAF) one tablet po once daily
      • Once daily Truvada (Emtricitabine + Tenofovir) resulted in a 44% decrease in incidence of HIV infection in one clinical trial (N Engl J Med 363:2587, 2010). However, among those with detectable plasma drug levels, the protection was > 90%.  Truvada is less expensive since going off 'patent protection', which occurred in the US in 2020 (Truvada is already generic in Canada and Europe).  Estimated monthly cost of generic TDF-FTC is less than $50 / month.
      • Descovy (emtricitabine 200 mg + TAF 25 mg) is the second drug to be approved by the US FDA for HIV-1 PrEP. It is indicated in at-risk adults and adolescents weighing at least 35 kg to reduce the risk of HIV-1 infection from sexual acquisition, excluding individuals at risk from receptive vaginal sex.
    • For men who have infrequent sexual exposures, internment or "On Demand" PrEP is an option.  (JAMA, 2023).  On-demand (2-1-1) oral dosing is recommended for cisgender men of any sexual orientation (insufficient data on cis- or trans-gender women).  TDF/FTC 2-1-1 dosing is initiated with a double dose, 2 to 24 hours before planned sexual activity, followed by  single additional doses, 24 and 48 hours after the first dose.  If additional sexual activity occurs within 7 days of the initial planned activity, daily single dosing should be continued until 2 daily doses after the last sexual activity.
    • Apretude (Cabotegravir), is approved as an effective PrEP regimen that was shown to be superior to oral Truvada (TDF-FTC).  Given as one injection every 2 months (after an oral lead-in), HPTN 083 showed it to be superior to one pill once daily (Landovitz RJ et al, NEJM, 2021):  Incident HIV infection occurred in 52 participants: 13 in the cabotegravir group (incidence, 0.41 per 100 person-years) and 39 in the TDF–FTC group (incidence, 1.22 per 100 person-years) (hazard ratio, 0.34; 95% confidence interval, 0.18 to 0.62).
    • Individuals must be screened for HIV-1 infection immediately prior to starting PrEP, and then at least once every three months. The recommended dosage is one tablet orally once daily, with or without food.  The evidence for Descovy safety benefit over Truvada is scant.  Most renal and bone toxicity associated with Truvada occurs when the drug is used with boosting agents, ritonavir or cobicistat (J Viral Eradication, 2018)
