Bite: Seal

Clinical Setting

  • Bite wound from seals or other injury related to handling seal material.
  • Infection can take weeks to appear after bite, Lancet 364:448, 2004.
  • Lesion typically begins as a papule at site of injury and progresses to localized swelling with intense pain. Tenosynovitis or joint involvement may occur (J Infect 45:71, 2002).

Etiologies

  • Mycoplasmas of marine mammals (phylogenetically related to Mycoplasma hominis)

Primary Regimens

  • Tetracycline 500 mg po tid x 2 weeks and 500 mg po qid x 4 - 6 weeks have been reported to be effective

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x 2 weeks

Comments

  • Administer tetanus toxoid or Tdap (preferred) if > 10 years since last dose
  • Isolation of mycoplasma from seal finger: Clin Infect Dis 62:491, 2016;  Clin Infect Dis 27:1168, 1998.
  • Distinguish from seal pox, due to seal pox virus (parapoxvirus), that starts as a papule and progresses to a gray bullous lesion that may resemble orf (Brit J Dermatol 152:791, 2005).
  • Other reported bacteria include Bisgaardia hudsonensis of the family Pasteurellaceae; response to Amoxicillin-clavulanate (J Infect 63: 86, 2011) and Streptococcus halichoeri; resistant to Erythromycin and Tetracycline (J Clin Micro 54: 739, 2016).
  • Review of 11 cases: Wild & Environ Med 26: 497, 2015.
