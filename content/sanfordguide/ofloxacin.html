Ofloxacin
Tradename: Floxin, Oculfox
Drug Shortage Information
Prep Started At Notes
0.3% ophthalmic solution

Usage and Dosing

  • Ofloxacin, FDA approved in 1990, is a fluoroquinolone with broad activity against respiratory and enteric pathogens.
  • Ofloxacin is a racemic mixture of R-ofloxacin and L-ofloxacin (levofloxacin).
  • For most clinical indications, levofloxacin is used rather than ofloxacin.
  • CDC no longer recommends fluoroquinolones (including ofloxacin) for gonococcal infections due to high levels of resistance (MMWR 2007;56:332).
  • See Fluoroquinolones, Overview for additional class-wide information.

Adult Dose

  • 200-400 mg po q12h
  • Avoid dairy products ±2 hours of oral administration.
  • Concomitant metal cations: see Major Drug Interactions below.

      Pregnancy Risk

      FDA Risk Category 1 Avoid in pregnancy due to possible renal, cardiac, and CNS toxicity in the fetus. Consider use if no alternatives exist, and if benefit outweighs risk.
      Use during Lactation Avoid breastfeeding for 4-6 hrs after a dose, monitor infant for GI toxicity
