Valacyclovir
Tradename: Valtrex

Usage and Dosing

  • Valacyclovir is an ester of the prodrug Acyclovir that is well-absorbed. Used in the treatment of Herpes virus infections: Herpes simplex (HSV) and Varicella zoster (VZV), but not cytomegalovirus (CMV).
  • Bioavailability is 3-5 times greater than Acyclovir.
  • Extemporaneous oral suspension formulation:
    • 25 mg/mL or 50 mg/mL, using 500 mg tabs, USP Suspension Structured Vehicle, and cherry flavor.
    • Refrigerate prepared suspension, discard after 28 days.
    • Acyclovir AUC, Cmax similar to tablets (Clin Infect Dis 2010;50:221).

Adverse Effects

  • Similar to Acyclovir. Thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura/hemolytic uremic syndrome reported in patients with advanced HIV disease and transplant recipients participating in clinical trials at doses of 8 gm/day.
  • Neurotoxicity: manifest as hallucinations, death delusions, and involuntary movements. Result of high serum levels as a failure of dose reduction in patients with renal insufficiency: Am J Med 128: 692, 2015.
