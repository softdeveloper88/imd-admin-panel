Pasteurella multocida

Clinical Setting

  • Management of cat and dog bites
    • Pasteurella is normal flora of the mouth of cats and dogs.
    • Soft tissue infection, or even bacteremia, occurs in humans as a result of the bite or sometimes the lick of the cat or dog.
      • Prophylactic (anticipatory) antibiotic therapy indicated   due to high frequency of infection
  • Wound culture, wound cleansing and  debridement are needed.
  • See Cat bites and Dog bites.
  • Can occur in the absence of animal contact (Front Cell Infect Microbiol. 2023; 13: 1267941).
  • Evaluate for need of tetanus toxoid immunization and rabies prophylaxis.

Classification

  • Gram-negative coccobacilli

Primary Regimens

Empiric Therapy

  • Desired spectrum: Predictable activity against 1) Pasteurella species isolated from the mouth of the animal, 2) Staph. aureus, and 3)  Streptococcal species isolated from the patient's skin:
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg tab, 1 po bid x 7-10 days (not defined, but seems reasonable)

Specific/Directed Therapy

  • Culture positive for Pasteurella species (only) and no allergy problems:
    • Penicillin VK 500 mg po qid before meals and hs or
    • Amoxicillin 500 mg po tid before meals

Alternative Regimens

  • IgE-mediated severe penicillin allergy; positive cultures for Pasteurella species; negative for Staph. aureus:
    • Cefuroxime 500 mg po q12h
    • Cefpodoxime 200 mg po q12h
    • Levofloxacin 750 mg po once daily
    • Moxifloxacin 400 mg po once daily
    • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid
    • TMP-SMX-DS tab 1 po bid

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Pasteurella species are susceptible to some, but not all, beta-lactams.
  • If penicillin allergic, do not use cephalexin, clindamycin, or an erythromycin due to frequent in vitro resistance.

Comments

  • For parenteral use: Ceftriaxone, Penicillin G, Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin or Moxifloxacin.
  • High level resistance to Erythromycin, Clindamycin, and Vancomycin.
  • Only moderate susceptibility to Cephalexin, Cefazolin, Azithromycin, Nafcillin, and aminoglycosides.
