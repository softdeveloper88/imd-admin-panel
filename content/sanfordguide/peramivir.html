Peramivir
Tradename: Rapivab, PeramiFlu, Rapiacta

Usage and Dosing

  • Peramivir (Rapivab) is a FDA-approved parenteral neuraminidase inhibitor; in Japan, named Rapiacta; in South Korea, named PeramiFlu.
  • FDA approved (600 mg single IV dose) for the treatment of acute uncomplicated influenza in patients age ≥6 months who have been symptomatic for no more than 2 days.
  • The pharmacokinetics of this dose are not appropriate for hospitalized patients with severe influenza where viral replication is more prolonged and viral loads are higher. Clinical trials in hospitalized patients have used daily dosing ranging from 200-600 mg daily. These trials did not show clear superiority to oseltamivir (Clin Infect Dis 2014:59:e172, Antiviral Ther 2013:18:651). There are no controlled studies to guide duration of therapy. 
  • For use in patients in whom alternative therapy is failing, considered undependable or in whom oral therapy is not feasible.

      Adverse Effects

      • Hypersensitivity reactions (anaphylaxis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, erythema multiforme).
      • Neuropsychiatric events (e.g. delirium, abnormal behavior). Causal connection to peramivir unestablished.
      • In clinical trials the most common AEs were: diarrhea, nausea, insomnia, and hypertension. Decreased WBC reported. One patient had ↑ QTc.

      Major Drug Interactions

      • None reported
      • Avoid live attenuated influenza vaccine within 2 weeks before or 48 hours after administration of Peramivir.

      Comments

      • Strains with H275Y substitution associated with Oseltamivir resistance are likely to be resistant to Peramivir.
      • Reference: Medical Letter 57:17, 2015.
