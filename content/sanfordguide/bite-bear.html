Bite: Bear

Clinical Setting

  • Bite wound from bear

Etiologies

  • S. aureus
  • Coagulase-negative Staphylococcus
  • Viridans streptococci
  • Enterobacteriacaeae
  • Aeromonas spp.
  • B. cereus
  • Neisseria spp.
  • E. durans
  • See Clin Microbiol Rev 24: 231, 2011

Primary Regimens

  • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h

Alternative Regimens

  • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL + [Cefepime 2 gm IV or Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q8-12h] + Metronidazole 500 mg q8h
  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875 mg po bid + Ciprofloxacin 500-750 mg po bid

Comments

  • Injuries often result in hospital level care.
  • Infection with Mycobacterium fortuitum reported (J Clin Micro 43:1009, 2005).
  • Rabies reported in bears (MMWR 48:761, 1999).
    • Bites caused by bears, uncommonly require initiation of PEP.
    • Consultation with local public health is advised.
    • See Rabies, Vaccines and PEP for detailed evaluation procedure and recommendations
