Herpes simplex, Oral lesions: mouth, lips

Clinical Setting

  • Mucocutaneous Herpes simplex virus (HSV) infection, oral labial HSV infection.
  • Gingivostomatitis / stomatitis: Visicular infection of the mouth (usually anterior) and lips.
  • Fever blisters in normal host. Painful, more severe and often recurrent in immunocompromised host.
  • Start treatment with prodrome symptoms (tingling/burning) before lesions appear.

Etiologies

  • Herpes simplex virus (HSV) (Types 1, 2)

Primary Regimens

  • Oral regimens
    • Valacyclovir 2 gm po q12h x 1 day (see Comment for dosing reference)
    • Acyclovir 400 mg po tid x 5 days
    • Famciclovir 500 mg po bid x 7 days (FDA-approved for HIV patients only)

Alternative Regimens

  • At first sign of prodrome: Famciclovir 1500 mg one dose or 750 mg q12h x 1 day (J Amer Acad Dermatol 55:47, 2006)
  • Topical regimens (less preferred):
    • Penciclovir 1% cream q2h during day x 4 days
    • Acyclovir 5% cream 6x/day (q3h) x 7 days (approved for HIV patients only)

Comments

  • Valacyclovir more effective overall (Meta-analysis in J Oral Path Med 46:561, 2017)  
  • Symptoms reduction
    • Oral regimens: Valacyclovir (1 day), Famciclovir (2 days), Acyclovir (½ day)
    • Topical regimens: Penciclovir (1 day); Acyclovir (½ day)
