HIV, Antiretroviral Therapy (ART), Adult

Overview

  • HIV treatment regimens for adults.
  • Antiretroviral therapy (ART) in treatment-naïve adult patients.
  • Guidelines: clinicalinfo.HIV.gov, IAS-USA in JAMA 2024, WHO (2021 Consolidated Guidelines)
  • Review: HIV Infection-Screening, Diagnosis, and Treatment. New Engl J Med. 2021; 384:2131
    .

When to Start ART

  • All patients with HIV regardless of CD4 count 
  • Only exceptions are:
    • Patient is not ready to start (for personal reasons or lack of commitment to take medications)
    • Patient is an “Elite Controller”, i.e., HIV RNA undetectable for extended period without ART. Controversy exists about treating such patients, though many experts suggest ART owing to inflammation resulting from ongoing de novo HIV replication.

    • Many clinics are adopting a 'treat now' policy whereby the ARV regimen is started on the first encounter with the clinic.  In such instances, resistance tests are obtained and the regimen(s) adjusted as indicated once the resistance test data return. 

What Regimen to Start

  • Design a regimen consisting of:
    • Dual-nucleoside/-nucleotide reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NRTI component) PLUS an Strand Transfer Integrase Inhibitor ((InSTI; either Bictegravir or Dolutegravir)
    • A combination of Dolutegravir + Lamivudine (fixed dose combination) can be used in selected patients
    • Alternative regimens include Dual-nucleoside / nucleotide RTI plus either a non-nucleoside RT inhibitor, a protease inhibitor, or raltegravir (InSTI).  
  • Selection of components is influenced by many factors, including:
    • Results of viral resistance testing
    • Pregnancy: See Section below
    • HIV status (e.g., Nevirapine CONTRAINDICATED in women with CD4 >250 cells/μL and men with CD4 >400 cells/μL)
    • Potential drug interactions or adverse drug effects; special focus on tolerability (even low grade side effects can profoundly affect adherence)
    • Co-morbidities (e.g., lipid effects of PIs, liver or renal disease, cardiovascular disease risk, chemical dependency, psychiatric disease)
    • Convenience of dosing. Co-formulations increase convenience, but sometimes prescribing the two constituents individually is preferred, as when dose-adjustments are needed for renal disease.
    • Abacavir (ABC) is no longer recommended; if using this drug, HLA-B5701 testing is required prior to using
    • Co-receptor tropism assay if considering Maraviroc (MVC)

    • Rilpivirine with 2 nucleosides should be used only in patients with a baseline HIV RNA level < 100,000 c/mL.  However, Rilpivirine CAN be used in combination with Dolutegravir in those with > 100,000 c/mL

    • Higher rates of renal dysfunction occur when TDF is combined with boosted PIs; TAF is the preferred drug in this setting.  Conversely, TDF does not  have nearly as much renal toxicity when paired with non-boosted PI drugs. 

Recommended Regimens

Once daily therapy (all component drugs in a single tablet)
  • Biktarvy (Bictegravir/Tenofovir AF/Emtricitabine) 1 tablet once daily OR
  • Dovato (Dolutegravir / Lamivudine) 1 tablet once daily (selected patients: VL < 500K, no lamivudine resistance, not HBV co-infected)
Once daily therapy

(component drugs divided into separate tablets)

  • Descovy (Tenofovir-AF/Emtricitabine) + Dolutegravir 1 tablet each once daily OR
  • Truvada (Tenofovir-DF/Emtricitabine) + Dolutegravir 1 tablet each once daily OR

Alternative Regimens

Single-tablet regimens

  • Atripla (Tenofovir/Emtricitabine/Efavirenz) 1 tablet once daily qhs OR
  • Complera/Eviplera (Emtricitabine/Tenofovir-DF/Rilpivirine) 1 tablet once daily with food (avoid when VL > 100,000 cells/mL) OR
  • Genvoya (Elvitegravir/Cobicistat/Emtricitabine/Tenofovir alafenamide [TAF]) 1 tablet once daily with food OR
  • Stribild (Elvitegravir/Cobicistat/Emtricitabine/Tenofovir) 1 tablet once daily OR
  • Odefsey (Emtricitabine/Tenofovir AF/Rilpivirine) 1 tablet once daily with food (avoid when VL > 100,000 cells/mL) 
  • Juluca (Dolutegravir/ Rilpivirine) 1 tablet once daily with food (Two drug fixed dose combination used to replace initial standard 3-drug regimen after 6 months of successful suppression < 50c/ml with no evidence of virologic failure and baseline wild-type virus with no resistance mutations)
  • Delstrigo (Doravirine /Lamivudine/Tenofovir DF) 1 tablet once daily 
  • Symtuza (Darunavir/Cobicistat/Emtricitabine/Tenofovir AF) 1 tablet once daily with food 
Injectable regimen
  • Cabenuva (cabotegravir / rilpivirine ) 1 month orally administered lead in of the two drugs, then initiate injections of Cabenuva (600 mg of cabotegravir and 900 mg of rilpivirine) on the last day of oral lead-in. Cabenuva injection should be administered at Month 2, Month 3, and every 2 months thereafter.

Toxicities

  • Warning: Epzicom/ Kivexa (Abacavir/3TC) containing regimens (no longer recommended): Use only in patients who are HLA-B5701 negative. Use Abacavir with caution in those with HIV RNA > 100,000 c/mL at baseline (this does not apply when Dolutegravir is the anchor drug of the regimen)

Other Drugs - Selected Populations

  • Juluca (Dolutegravir / Rilpivirine) can be used to simplify 3-drug regimen to 2-drugs when initial regimen successful (< 50c/ml for > 6 months), no baseline pre-Rx resistance mutations, and no virologic failure.
  • Etravirine and Rilpivirine are options for some patients who have NNRTI resistance mutations, e.g., K103N, at baseline. Expert consultation is recommended
  • Boosted PIs can be administered once or twice daily
  • Either Ritonavir and Cobicistat can be used as PI-boosting agents
  • Boosted InSTI regimens are considered alternative regimens
  • Non-boosted PIs are no longer recommended

Pregnancy Considerations

  • Timing of initiation of therapy and drug choice must be individualized, though as a rule ARV Rx should be initiated as early as possible during pregnancy
  • Viral resistance testing should be performed
  • Long-term effects of agents is unknown
  • Dolutegravir and TAF are now recommended for pregnant women owing safety reports and comparative trials
  • Emerging data on bictergravir; bictegravir can be used in pregnancy.
  • Efavirenz is an approved drug for use in pregnancy
  • Certain drugs are hazardous or contraindicated in pregnancy (Didanosine, Stavudine). 
  • See HIV in Women and Pregnancy for further discussion.
