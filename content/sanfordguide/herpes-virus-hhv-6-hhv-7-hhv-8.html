Herpes virus, HHV-6, HHV-7, HHV-8

Clinical Setting

  • HHV-6:
    • Implicated as cause of roseola (exanthem subitum) and other febrile diseases of childhood (N Engl J Med 352:768, 2005).
    • Fever and rash documented in transplant patients (N Engl J Med 2018; 278:659). Reactivation in 47% of 110 U.S. hematopoietic stem cell transplant patients; associated with delayed monocytes & platelet engraftment (Clin Infect Dis 40:932, 2005).
    • Recognized in association with meningoencephalitis in immunocompetent adults.
    • Diagnosis : HHV-6 detected by positive PCR in CSF in patient with compatiable clinical syndrome
      • Of 60 positive  CSF Film Array PCRs, 15 patiients positive for HHV-6. Only 1 of 15 with compatible clinical illness (Clin Infect Dis 2018;67:1125)
    • Possible association with drug reaction with eosinophilia and systemic symptoms (DRESS) syndrome (Arch Dermatol 145:67, 2009)
  • HHV-7:
    • Ubiquitous virus (>90% of the population is infected by age 3 years).
    • No relationship to human disease.
    • Infects CD4 lymphocytes via CD4 receptor; transmitted via saliva.
  • HHV-8:
    • Agent of Kaposi’s sarcoma, Castleman’s disease and body cavity lymphoma.
    • Associated with diabetes in sub-Saharan Africa (JAMA 299:2770, 2008).

Etiologies

  • Human Herpes Virus (HHV) 6, 7, and 8

Primary Regimens

  • Antiviral therapy is not recommended, but see Comments.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • HHV-6
    • Reduced viral copies in response to valaciclovir, valganciclovir, and artesunate in patients with myalgic encephalomyelitis/chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS); artesunate was more effective than the other two agents (Microbial and Immunology 66:193, 2022)
    • Foscarnet therapy improved thrombotic microangiopathy (Am J Hematol 76:156, 2004); case report(s) of foscarnet use in post-hematopoietic stem cell transplant HHV-6 reactivation (Transplant and Cellular Therapy 29 (6): 397, 2023).
    • Cidofovir is second line therapy (Bone Marrow Transplant 42:227, 2008)
  • HHV-7
    • For HIV-positive patients, antiretroviral therapy (ART) may help
  • HHV-8
    • Localized lesions: radiotherapy, laser surgery or intralesional chemotherapy.
    • Systemic:
      • Chemotherapy
      • Castleman’s disease responded to Ganciclovir (Blood 103:1632, 2004) and Valganciclovir
