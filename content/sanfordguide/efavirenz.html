Efavirenz
Tradename: Sustiva

Usage and Dosing

  • Efavirenz (EFV) is indicated for use as a NNRTI agent as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually paired with 2 nucleos(t)ide (e.g., Emtricitabine and Tenofovir) agents. Considered a preferred regimen. Atripla is a single tablet, once daily co-formulation of Emtricitabine + Tenofovir + Efavirenz.
  • Efavirenz rash severe enough to discontinue use of drug in 1.7%.
  • High frequency of diverse CNS AEs: somnolence, dreams, confusion, agitation. Serious psychiatric symptoms.
  • Certain CYP2B6 polymorphisms may predict exceptionally high plasma levels with standard doses (Clin Infect Dis 45:1230, 2007). This haplotype is more common in Blacks.
  • False-positive cannabinoid screen.
  • Pregnancy Use: Efavirenz is recommended as an alternative ARV medication during pregnancy (HHS Perinatal Guidelines, clinicalinfo.HIV.gov).
  • Very long tissue T½. If treatment to be discontinued, may want to stop Efavirenz 1 week before stopping companion drugs. Otherwise, risk of developing Efavirenz resistance, as after 1–2 days only Efavirenz in blood &/or tissue. Also, adequate contraception for 12 weeks after discontinuance. Some authorities bridge this gap by adding a PI to the NRTI backbone if feasible after Efavirenz is discontinued (Clin Infect Dis 42:401, 2006).
  • For children, capsules may be administered by sprinkling the contents into an age-appropriate food. Tablets cannot be crushed, chewed, or broken.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive). See also: ARV Drug-Drug Interactions.

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Caution: CNS effects may impair driving and other hazardous activities. Serious neuropsychiatric symptoms reported, including severe depression (2.4%) & suicidal ideation (0.7%).
    • Elevation in liver enzymes. Fulminant hepatic failure has been reported (see FDA label). Hepatic failure has been reported.
    • Teratogenicity reported in primates; may cause fetal harm (neural tube defects), however incidence is low. WHO now recommends use in pregnancy; HHS Guidelines now OK with continuing EFV if mother is already on drug when pregnancy identified.
    • Contraindicated with certain drugs metabolized by CYP3A4. Slow metabolism in those homozygous for the CYP-2B6 G516T allele can result in exaggerated toxicity and intolerance. This allele much more common in Blacks and women (Clin Infect Dis 42:408, 2006). Has been associated with severe vitamin D deficiency, AIDS 24:1923, 2010.
  • Most Common:
    • CNS side effects 52%; symptoms include dizziness, insomnia, somnolence, impaired concentration, psychiatric symptoms, & abnormal dreams; symptoms are worse after 1st or 2nd dose & improve over 2–4 weeks; discontinuation rate 2.6%. Rash 26% (vs. 17% in comparators); often improves with oral antihistamines; discontinuation rate 1.7%.
    • Can cause false-positive urine test results for cannabinoid with CEDIA DAU multi-level THC assay.
    • Metabolite can cause false-positive urine screening test for benzodiazepines (Clin Infect Dis 48:1787, 2009).
  • Photosensitivity (three case reports).
  • QTc interval prolongation has been observed.
  • Early experimental data suggests potential for neurotoxic effect of the 8-hydroxy metabolite of Efavirenz (J Pharm Experiment Ther 343:696, 2012).
