Prezcobix
Tradename: Darunavir + Cobicistat

Usage and Dosing

  • Prezcobix is a combination formulation of Darunavir + Cobicistat indicated for treatment of HIV-1.
  • See individual components for drug-specific data:
    • Darunavir (DRV)
    • Cobicistat
  • For class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.
  • Not recommended in combination with other protease inhibitors or Elvitegravir owing to possible decrease in plasma concentration of ARVs, loss of therapeutic effect and development of resistance.
  • Not recommended for use in pregnant women due to substantially lower exposures of Darunavir and Cobicistat. Treatment should not be initiated in pregnant women, and an alternative regimen should be used in women who become pregnant during Prezcobix therapy.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see Table below, not all-inclusive).

Adult Dose

  • 1 tab once daily with food (tablets: DRV 800 mg + Cobi 150 mg)

      Adverse Effects

      • See individual components
      • Most common: diarrhea, nausea, rash, headache, abdominal pain, vomiting
      • Hepatotoxicity
      • Severe skin reactions
      • Decreased creatinine clearance
      • Renal impairment (when used with Tenofovir)
      • Sulfa allergy
      • Hyperglycemia
      • Fat redistribution
      • IRIS
      • Hemophilia
