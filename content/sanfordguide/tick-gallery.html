Tick-borne Illness, Overview

Clinical Features

  • Overview of tick-borne illnesses commonly seen in the U.S.
  • Comparison of clinical features of 3 infections transmitted by Ixodes species ticks (in the U.S.)(images below)
    • Concomitant infections with two, or even three, of the 3 pathogens can occur: e.g. Lyme + Babesia or Lyme + Anaplasmosis (HGA)
    • Ixodes sp. ticks are also the vector for a cause of relapsing fever (Borrelia miyamoto) and a tick borne encephalitis (Powassan disease). Relapsing fever is also caused by other species of ticks, e.g., soft ticks.
    • In Europe and Asia, Ixodes ticks are the vector for other diseases, including tick-borne encephalitis (TBE).
      • See Tick-Borne Encephalitis, Vaccines for indications, available products, dosing, and vaccine characteristics for pre-exposure prevention.
      • Emerging: Neoehrlichia mikurensis.
