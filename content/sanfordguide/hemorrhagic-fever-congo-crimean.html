Hemorrhagic Fever, Crimean-Congo

Clinical Setting

  • Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever is a tickborne viral infection (Hyalomma tick).
    • Can also be transmitted to humans via direct contact with blood or tissues of viremic animals (mostly livestock)
  • Epidemiology reviewed in PLoS Neglected Tropical Diseases (2020) 
  • Natural history:
    • Infection and incubation (1 - 9 days)
    • Pre-hemorrhagic phase (1 - 7 days) 
    • Hemorrhagic phase (2 - > 3 days)
    • Convalescence (serveral days)
  • Symptoms include nausea, vomiting, fever, headache, myalgias, dizziness, neck pain / backache, sore eyes, photophobia, and stupor (1/3).
    • Abrupt onset of symptoms
  • Signs: conjunctival injection, hyperemia of face / neck/ chest, hepatomegaly, jaundice, petechiae (1/3).
  • Lab: Decreased platelets, decreased WBC, increased ALT, AST, LDH, CPK (100%).

Diagnosis

  • Quantitative reverse transcription competitive PCR (qRT-PCR); Viremia > 109 copies/ mL associated with mortality
  • Serological assay kits for IgM and IgG antibodies (false negatives are common)

Etiologies

  • Crimean-Congo hemorrhagic fever virus

Primary Regimens

  • Ribavirin, 30 mg/kg po initial dose, then 15 mg/kg po q6h x 4 days, then 7.5 mg/kg q8h po x 6 days (WHO recommendation)
  • See Comments

Alternative Regimens

  • IVIG associated with decreased inflammation
  • High Dose Corticosteroids have been effective
  • Immune modulators to reduce cytokine storm may be effective in severe disease

Comments

  • In case series of 281 patients, Ribavirin reduced case-fatality rate; dexamethasone considered beneficial in patients with severe disease: Clin Infect Dis 57:1270, 2013.
  • 3/3 healthcare workers in Pakistan had complete recovery (Lancet 346:472, 1995) and 61/69 (89%) with confirmed disease treated with Ribavirin survived in Iran (Clin Infect Dis 36:1613, 2003).
  • Shorter time of hospitalization among Ribavirin treated patients (7.7 vs. 10.3 days), but no difference in mortality or transfusion needs in one study done in Turkey (J Infection 52:207, 2006).
  • Early use (< 2 days) of Ribavirin markedly reduced need to transfer patients with CCHF to referral center (minimized symptoms) among 342 patients in Turkey (Vector Borne and Zoonotic Dis 14: 300, 2014).
  • Monoclonal Antibodies (mAb) under development for treatment; DNA vaccines in development
  • Ribavirin therapy reviewed, BMC Infect Dis 10:207, 2010.
  • CCHF reviewed, Nature Rev Micro 21: 463, 2023.
  • General references: Clin Infect Dis 39:284, 2004; Microbial Pathogenesis 169: 2022;
