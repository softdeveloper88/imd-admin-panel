Lice, Body

Clinical Setting

  • Homelessness. Poor personal hygiene. Sharing a bed.
  • Lice live in clothing, especially the seams. Leave clothing for blood meal.
  • Lice can serve as vector for trench fever, typhus, or relapsing fever.
  • Main host complaint is itching.
  • Diagnosis is by visual identification of the louse in clothing.

Etiologies

  • Pediculus humanus humanus (also known as Pediculus humanus corporis)

Primary Regimens

  • 5% Permethrin cream to entire body skin. Leave in place 6-8 hours. Only one application.
    • Treat or destroy all clothing. Either heat wash (149º F) or dry clean.  Iron seams. 

Alternative Regimens

  • Ivermectin 12 mg po on days 0, 7 and 14 reported successful in an outbreak in a homeless shelter (J Infect Dis 193:474, 2006) though not sustained.
  • Topical ivermectin is available over the counter (2023).
  • Alternative regimens used for head lice  could be attempted, but less literature. 

Comments

  • Reference: JAMA 2024; 332:1189 ahead of print.
  • Avoid lindane: toxic
  • Can use topical steroids after permethrin treatment for more rapid resolution of symptoms if severe pruritus or skin irritation.
  • Lice can persist in clothing for weeks.
