Meningitis, Post basilar skull fracture

Clinical Setting

  • Empiric therapy for trauma-associated meningitis, post basilar skull fracture.
  • Typically caused by oropharyngeal flora.
  • Basilar skull fracture can result in chronic CSF leak and recurrent episodes of meningitis (Clin Infect Dis 2020; 70: 2256).

Etiologies

  • Strep. pneumoniae (most common)
  • H. influenzae
  • S. pyogenes
  • N. meningitidis

Primary Regimens

  • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) + (Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h or Cefotaxime 2 gm q4-6h (where available)) + Dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg IV q6h x 2-4 days (first dose before or concomitant with 1st dose of antibiotic)

Alternative Regimens

  • Meropenem 2 gm IV q8h + Vancomycin (as in primary regimen) + Dexamethasone (as in primary regimen)
  • If severe penicillin/cephalosporin allergy: [Chloramphenicol 12.5 mg/kg IV q6h (max daily dose of 4 gm) or Aztreonam 2 gm IV q6h] + Vancomycin + Dexamethasone as in the primary regimen.

Comments

  • Basilar skull fracture complicated by CSF leak or meningitis in approximately 2% and 0.5% of cases (Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 149:931, 2013). 
  • Prophylactic antibiotics not recommended  (Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2015 Apr 28;(4):CD004884).
  • Modify regimen based on culture and sensitivity results.
  • High incidence of pneumococcal meningitis likely results from  communication of subarachnoid space with flora of upper airway, especially the nasopharynx. 
  • Need to close the leak if technically feasible.
