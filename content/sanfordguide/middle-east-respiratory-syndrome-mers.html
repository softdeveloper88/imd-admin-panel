Middle East Respiratory Syndrome, MERS

Clinical Setting

  • Middle East Respiratory Syndrome, MERS-CoV (MERS coronavirus) is a novel coronavirus first identified in a resident of the Middle East in 2012. All patients have had a direct or indirect link to the Middle East.
  • As of Jan 2017, the WHO received reports of 1879 cases in humans from 27 countries with a mortality rate of 35% (N Engl J Med 2017; 376: 584)
  • All patients have presented with respiratory disease. Secondary complications include acute renal failure, multi-organ failure, acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS) and consumptive coagulopathy. Median age is 56 years and 72% of cases are males.
  • Human-to-human transmission has occurred in healthcare settings, among close family contacts and in the workplace.
  • MERS-CoV has been isolated from dromedary camels (Lancet Infect Dis 14:140, 2014).
  • See: www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/mers/index.html.

Etiologies

  • Coronavirus MERS-CoV

Primary Regimens

  • None

Alternative Regimens

  •  Interferon-alpha 2b + Ribavirin (doses for humans for this disorder not determined; likely use doses employed for hepatitis C) (See Comment)

  • Remdesivir has in vitro activity against MERS-CoV; no clinical studies.

Comments

  • Suspect infection in those with fever/pneumonia within 14 days after travel from countries in the Arabian peninsula; or if they had close contact to a known or suspected case of MERS-CoV.
  • MERS-CoV should be considered in any cluster of patients with severe respiratory illness of unknown etiology.
  • NIAID study evaluated Interferon-alpha 2b + Ribavirin in three rhesus monkeys. Animals who were exposed to MERS-CoV virus and were treated 8 hours later, showed only low level viremia and did not develop respiratory symptoms or pneumonitis, compared to 3 control monkeys not treated who developed high level viremia and became very ill (Nature Medicine 19: 1313-17, 2013). However, none of 5 critically ill patients treated with this regimen survived: Int J Infect Dis 20:42, 2014.
  • In retrospective observational study, combination of ribavirin and recombinant interferon alpha or interferon beta was not associated with a reduction in 90-day mortality or faster clearance of MERS-CoV: Clin Infect Dis 2020; 70: 1837.
