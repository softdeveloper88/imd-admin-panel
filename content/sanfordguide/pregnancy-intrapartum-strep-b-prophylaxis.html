Pregnancy, Intrapartum Strep B Prophylaxis

Clinical Setting

  • To minimize risk of neonatal Group B streptococcus infections in infants born to mothers colonized with this organism.
  • Pregnancy—intrapartum antimicrobial prophylaxis procedures:
    • Treat during labor if swab culture positive.
    • Screen all pregnant women with vaginal and rectal swab for Group B Strep (GBS) at 36–37 weeks gestation (unless other indications for prophylaxis exist: GBS bacteriuria during this pregnancy or previously delivered infant with invasive GBS disease; even then, cultures may be useful for susceptibility testing). Use transport medium; GBS survive at room temperature up to 96 hrs.
    • Treat during labor if previously delivered infant with invasive GBS infection, or if any GBS bacteriuria during this pregnancy (for definitions, see MMWR 59(RR-10):1, 2010).
    • Treat if GBS status unknown but if any of the following are present:
      • Delivery at <37 weeks gestation [see MMWR 59(RR-10):1, 2010 algorithms for preterm labor and preterm premature rupture of membranes]
      • Duration of ruptured membranes ≥18 hrs
      • Intrapartum temp. ≥100.4ºF (≥38.0ºC)
      • Note: If amnionitis is suspected, broad-spectrum antibiotic coverage should include an agent active against Group B streptococci
    • Treatment is not indicated if negative vaginal and rectal cultures at 35-37 weeks gestation (unless there was GBS bacteriuria during this pregnancy or the mother previously delivered an infant with invasive GBS disease).
    • Treatment is not indicated if C-section performed before onset of labor with intact membranes (use standard surgical prophylaxis).
  • Neonate: Careful observation of signs and symptoms or laboratory evaluation, or both, are needed (see detailed algorithm in MMWR 59(RR-10):1, 2010).

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus agalactiae (Group B streptococcus)

Primary Regimens

  • Penicillin G 5 million units IV (loading dose) then 2.5 million to 3 million units IV q4h
  • Treat until delivery

Alternative Regimens

  • Ampicillin 2 gm IV loading dose, then 1 gm IV q4h until delivery
  • Penicillin allergic:
    • Cefazolin 2 gm IV initial dose, then 1 gm q8h until delivery
    • GBS susceptible to both Erythromycin and Clindamycin, or if Erythromycin-resistant but proven to be Clindamycin-susceptible confirmed by D-zone (or equivalent) test showing lack of inducible resistance:
      • Clindamycin 900 mg IV q8h until delivery
        • Caution: as of 2020, in vitro resistance over 45% per CDC acute bacterial core surveillance data.
      • If susceptibility of organism is unknown, or if the organism is resistant to Clindamycin, or if inducible Clindamycin-resistance has not been excluded, or if the patient is allergic to Clindamycin, use:
        • Vancomycin 20 mg/kg intravenously every 8 hours, with a maximum of 2 g per single dose.
    • Penicillin allergy testing, if available, is safe during pregnancy and can be beneficial for all women who report a penicillin allergy, particularly those that are suggestive of being IgE mediated, or of unknown severity, or both.
