Erythema Multiforme

Clinical Setting

  • Immune-mediated characteristic skin lesions.
  • Most commonly stimulated by viral infection (90%).
  • Medications are the precipitant in 10% of cases (NSAIDs, Sulfonomides, Anti-seizure drugs, others).

Etiologies

  • H. simplex (most common)
  • Epstein Barr Virus (EBV)
  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV)
  • Adenovirus
  • HIV
  • M. pneumoniae 
  • Medications
  • Many others

Primary Regimens

  • Treat underlying cause of EM; eliminate offending drug(s)
  • Symptomatic therapy
  • In cases of severe EM, macrolide antimicrobial therapy (e.g., azithromycin) is recommended until mycoplasma pneumonia infection can be ruled out

Alternative Regimens

  • Severe disease (Stevens Johnson Syndrome); see comments
