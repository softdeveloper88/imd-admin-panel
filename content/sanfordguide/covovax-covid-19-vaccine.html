Novavax COVID-19 Vaccine

Introduction

  • FDA approval for Novavax (May 2025) restricts use of the vaccine to person >65 yrs and for persons age 12-65 who have at least one medical condition that puts them at high risk from Covid.
    • Guidance on this page will be updated once CDC publishes an update on indications, timing, and coordination with mRNA vaccine doses

  • CDC recommends that everyone >6 mos. of age, whether or not they have ever previously been vaccinated with a COVID-19 vaccine, receive an updated 2024-2025 vaccine this fall.  

    • Antigenic composition: 

      • KP.2 for Moderna and Pfizer mRNA vaccine. Can be used in any person >6 mos 

      • JN.1 for Novavax recombinant protein vaccine. Option for certain persons >12 yrs. 

    • Summary of guidelines for people >12 yrs who are not moderately or severely immunocompromised

      • Initial vaccination

        • 2 doses of 2024–­2025 Novavax

      • Received previous doses of a COVID-19 vaccine

        • 1 dose of 2024–25 Novavax

      • No additional doses of 2024–­25 vaccine for people who are not moderately or severely immunocompromised are recommended at this time.

  • Dosing, including additional discretionary doses for the moderately and severely immunocompromised remains complex (see table below).
  • There is no preferential recommendation for the use of any one COVID-19 vaccine over another when more than one recommended and age-appropriate vaccine is available.
  • Previous doses of Janssen/J&J are not relevant to current guidelines

Vaccine

  • Type: recombinant protein subunit vaccine, adjuvanted.
    • CDC acronym: 1vCOV-aPS
  • 2024–25 Novavax COVID-19 Vaccine.

Indications for Vaccination (US)

  • All US persons age >6 mos should be up-to-date on vaccination regardless of
    • a history of symptomatic or asymptomatic SARS-CoV-2 infection,
    • presence of long COVID, or
    • a history of a SARS-Cov2 breakthrough infection. 
  • In short, for normal hosts:
    • Every normal host aged >12 yrs is
      • Up-to-date after receipt of 1 updated COVID-19 vaccine.
        • Exception: unvaccinated persons need 2 doses of updated Novavax vaccine to be up-to-date
  • CDC schedule for immunocompromised persons (see Immunocompromised, HIV).
  • Defer vaccination until recovery from acute episode and discontinuation of isolation.
    • After SARS-CoV-2 infection may consider delaying next dose by 3 mos from symptom onset or positive test.

      Efficacy, Duration of Protection

      • Time since most recent COVID-19 vaccine dose is likely more important than cumulative number of doses.
      • With continued viral evolution, after the primary series and the critical first booster, each booster dose stands on its own in inducing high VE against hospitalization for 3-6 mos.
      • No human efficacy data is available for the 2023-4 vaccine only boost in Nab titers against current strains.
      • Observational data from recent bivalent vaccines indicate approximate overall effectiveness against hospitalization of ~50% in close proximity to the date vaccine receipt.

      Choice, Interchangeability

      • There is no preferential recommendation for the use of any one COVID-19 vaccine over another when more than one recommended and age-appropriate vaccine is available.
      • Reproducible data indicate that the Moderna vaccine has both a modest VE and durability advantage over the Pfizer vaccine (higher mRNA dose in monovalent doses).

      Toxicities

      Contraindications

      • Severe allergic reaction (e.g., anaphylaxis) after a previous dose or to a component of a Novavax COVID-19 vaccine.
      • History of a known diagnosed allergy to a vaccine component as listed in the Package Insert.

      Precautions

      • Diagnosed non-severe allergy (e.g., urticaria beyond the injection site) to a component of Novavax COVID-19 vaccine or non-severe, immediate (onset less than 4 hours) allergic reaction after administration of a previous dose of a Novavax COVID-19 vaccine
      • Myocarditis or pericarditis within 3 wks after a dose of any COVID-19 vaccine
      • Defer vaccination until resolution of a moderate or severe acute illness, with or without fever
      • People with an allergy-related precaution to one COVID-19 vaccine type may receive the alternative COVID-19 vaccine type in the usual vaccination setting under the supervision of a health care provider experienced in the management of severe allergic reactions.  
      • An observation period of 30 minutes post-vaccination should be considered.
      • Multisystem inflammatory syndrome in children (MIS-C) or multisystem inflammatory syndrome in adults (MIS-A)
      • Not Contraindications
        • Food (including egg and gelatin), pet, insect, venom, environmental, latex, oral medications; any other history of anaphylaxis not related to vaccine or injectables.
        • History of GBS in proximity to another vaccine.

      Adverse Effects

      • Pain at the injection site, sometimes severe, occurs frequently
      • Fatigue, headache, and myalgia common but are mild to moderate, occurring within 1–3 days of vaccination, and resolving within 1–3 days of onset.
      • 8-week interval between the first and second primary series doses of Moderna, Novavax, and Pfizer COVID-19 vaccines may be optimal for some people to reduce myocarditis.
      • Most symptoms were mild to moderate in severity, had onset 1-3 days after vaccination, and resolved within 1–3 days.
      • Symptoms were more frequent in people ages 12–64 years compared to people ages 65 years and older
        • Symptoms are more frequent after dose 2 than dose 1 of the initial vaccination series.
      • Among people >18 yrs who received the Novavax booster dose, symptoms were more frequently reported after the booster dose than dose 2 of the primary series.

      Drug Interactions

      • Influenza (including HD or adjuvanted) and COVID-19 vaccines should be given at the same visit, if eligible.
      • COVID-19 vaccines may be administered without regard to timing of other vaccines.
      • Dose 2 administered no more than 4 days before the minimum (21 or 28 days) interval is valid; however, do not repeat an inadvertent dose administered earlier.
      • No minimum interval between COVID-19 vaccination and mPox vaccine.
      • Consider waiting 4 wks after mPox vaccination before COVID-19 vaccination to minimize myocarditis.
      • Simultaneous administration of COVID vaccine and nirsevimab (a long-acting monoclonal antibody for certain infants and young children for prevention of RSV) is recommended.

      Special Populations

      Pregnancy

      • Vaccinate according to standard recommendations if pregnant, breastfeeding, attempting or contemplating conception
        • Pregnant and recently pregnant persons at increased risk for severe illness and the fetus is at increased risk
      • Benefits outweighs risks of vaccination
      • Antibodies are transferred to the newborn
      • No contraindications to breastfeeding

      Other considerations:

      • Persons with HIV not at higher risk of severe disease after completing vaccination.
      • Severe disease risk higher if CD4<350 even with vaccination; ensure up to date. 
      • Self-attestation to moderately or severely immunocompromised status is acceptable.
      • Revaccinate HCT or CAR-T-cell therapy recipients with an mRNA vaccine >3 mos after HCT or CAR-T-cell therapy.
      • Consider revaccination if 1 or more doses of vaccine received during short-course treatment with B-cell-depleting therapies (e.g., rituximab, ocrelizumab) beginning at about 6 mos after completion of therapy.
      • Administration of vaccines should not be delayed in patients on ongoing immunosuppressives.
      • COVID-19 vaccines should be administered at least 2 wks before initiation or resumption of immunosuppressive therapies.
      • Administer vaccine doses approximately 4 wks before the next scheduled therapy for patients on ongoing B-cell-depleting therapies.
      • Development of moderate or severe immunocompromise after vaccination: People who were vaccinated for COVID-19 and subsequently become moderately or severely immunocompromised should follow the above COVID-19 vaccination schedule according to their age and prior COVID-19 vaccination history.
      • Administration of pemivibart should be deferred for at least 2 wks after a dose of COVID-19 vaccine.

      Serologic Testing

      • Antibody testing is not recommended to assess for immunity following COVID-19 vaccination or to assess the need for vaccination in an unvaccinated person.
      • No correlates of protection are available and assays vary widely.
      • +ve IgG only minimally reassuring, -ve IgG unhelpful in patients whose ability to mount a B-cell response is uncertain.
      • If antibody testing is done, vaccination should be completed as recommended regardless of the antibody test result.
      • Prior receipt of a COVID-19 vaccine will not affect the results of SARS-CoV-2 viral tests (nucleic acid amplification or antigen tests).

      Comments

      • Consider 15 minute observation period post-administration especially for adolescents.
      • Consider 30 minute observation period post-administration if:
      • Allergy related contraindication to another COVID vaccine
      • Non-severe allergic reaction (within 4 hrs) to any previous COVID vaccine
      • Anaphylaxis after non-COVID-19 vaccines or injectable therapies
