Delstrigo
Tradename: Delstrigo

Usage and Dosing

  • Delstrigo is a 3-drug combination formulation of an NNRTI and 2 NRTIs for treatment of HIV-1 in treatment-naive adults:
    • Doravirine (DOR), 100 mg
    • Lamivudine (3TC), 300 mg
    • Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF), 300 mg
  • Testing and monitoring
    • Test for Hepatitis B (HBV) infection before starting Delstrigo.
    • Prior to and during treatment assess the following:
      • Serum creatinine and estimated CrCl
      • Urine glucose and urine protein
      • Serum phosphorus (if chronic kidney disease)
  • Contraindicated when co-administered with strong CYP3A4 inducers (see chart below, not all-inclusive)

Adult Dose

  • One tablet po q24h
  • Take with or without food

Other Adjustment

  • If co-administered with Rifabutin:
    • One tablet (Delstrigo) once daily, then 1 tablet DOR (100mg) 12 hours after the dose of Delstrigo

Adverse Effects

  • Contraindications:
    • Do not co-administer with strong CYP3A inducers (significant decrease in Doravirine plasma concentration)
    • Do not use if known 3TC hypersensitivity reaction
  • Warnings:
    • Risk of new onset or worsening renal impairment (see testing and monitoring above)
    • Avoid concomitant nephrotoxic drugs
    • Bone loss and mineralization defects (monitor BMD if at risk for fractures, osteoporosis or bone loss)
    • Monitor for IRIS
    • Not recommended during lactation
  • Common AEs (≥5%):
    • Dizziness
    • Nausea
    • Abnormal dreams

Antimicrobial Spectrum
  • HIV-1 susceptible strains

Comments

  • Of note, doravirine retains activity against viruses containing many common NNRTI mutations, including:
    • K103N (without Y188L)
    • V106A (except as below)
    • E138L (except as below)
    • Y181C (without Y188L)
    • G190A (without Y188L or V106A)
    • F227L (without V106A)
    • M230L (without E138L)
  • Resistance to doravirine conferred when RT mutations at the following positions exist:  
    • Y188L, either alone or in combination with other common NNRTI resistance;
    • V106A + either G190A or F227L
    • E138L + either Y181C or M230L
  • TDF is resistant to viral variants with K65R mutations
  • 3TC is resistant to viral variants with M184V or M184I mutations
