Meropenem Desensitization

Overview

  • Protocol for intravenous meropenem desensitization.
  • Observe patient for urticaria, angioedema, shortness of breath, or hemodynamic instability.
  • Adapted from reference: Ann Pharmacother 2003;37:1424.

Method

  • This 11-step protocol results in the cumulative administration of approximately 1 gm meropenem over 4-5 hours.
  • Prepare each dose in 50 mL minibags (or syringes) of normal saline (NS).
  • Each minibag is infused over 20 minutes. Flush IV line completely between doses.
  • NS is the preferred vehicle for stability reasons.
  • To begin, prepare a 50 mg/mL stock solution by adding 20 mL of sterile water to a 1 gm vial.
    • Use this stock solution to prepare minibags for steps 6-11.
    • Minibags for steps 1-5 are prepared using aliquots from previously prepared minibags, as indicated in the chart.
    • When a large volume of stock solution (>2 mL) is added to a minibag (steps 9-11), remove a corresponding volume of NS from the minibag beforehand to maintain accuracy. Also, be sure to account for minibag overfill by the manufacturer.
