Pneumonia, Chlamydophila

Clinical Setting

  • Most infections are asymptomatic
  • Cause of community-acquired pneumonia (CAP) with fever, non-productive cough, ranging from mild, self-limited disease to severe pneumonia.
    • May be second most common atypical pneumonia agent after mycoplasma
    • Also causes pharyngitis, sinusitis, and bronchitis.
    • Appears to cause 1-6% of cases of CAP in which a pathogen is identified (See BMC Infect Dis 15:64, 2015  and N Engl J Med 373:415, 2015).

Etiologies

  • Chlamydophila pneumoniae (formerly Chlamydia pneumoniae)

Diagnosis

  • Diagnosis is clinical as well-standardized and reliable tests are largely unavailable.

Primary Regimens

  • Azithromycin 500 mg po/IV on day 1, then 250 mg po daily for 4 days
  • Levofloxacin 750 mg po/IV x 5-7 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po/IV bid x 5-7 days
  • Clarithromycin 500 mg bid x 7 days

Comments

  • Review article: StatPearls;2023. Jan 2.
  • Empiric macrolide therapy improves early clinical response rates, but may not impact overall outcome (Int J Antimicrob Agents 2017; 50:247-251). 
  • A number of diseases have been associated with C. pneumoniae infection including asthma, acute exacerbation of chronic bronchitis, atherosclerosis, coronary artery disease, and multiple sclerosis.
