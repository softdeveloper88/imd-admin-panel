Dovato
Tradename: Dovato

Usage and Dosing

  • Dovato is a once-daily, single-tablet regimen which is a two-drug fixed dose combination for treatment of HIV-1 in treatment-naive persons with no known resistance to either DTG or 3TC.
    • Dolutegravir (DTG) 50 mg
    • Lamivudine (3TC) 300 mg
  • Specifically, Dovato should NOT be used in persons with a baseline M184V or M184I mutation to 3TC at baseline or evidence of INSTI resistance at baseline.
  • Dovato was shown to be non-inferior to a three drug regimen of DTG + 2 other nucleoside drugs among > 1,400 treatment-naive ARV subjects in the Gemini 1 and 2 trials.  Primary endpoint was achievement of HIV RNA < 50 cells/mL.  Adverse events were similar to those observed when using DTG or 3TC in other regimens.
  • Dovato should NOT be used in persons who are Hepatitis B co-infected.  Only tenofovir-based regimens should be used in this setting.  If tenofovir cannot be used, then entecavir should be added to Dovato treatment.
  • Use in pregnancy (HHS guidelines, Dec 2019):
    • Dolutegravir: considered a preferred drug, irrespective of trimester.
    • Lamivudine: no evidence of human teratogenicity.
    • Two-drug ARV regimens are not recommended for use in pregnancy.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see Table below, not all inclusive).
