Pneumocystis pneumonia (PJP), Child

Clinical Setting

  • In infants with perinatally acquired HIV, Pneumocystis pneumonia (PJP) occurs most frequently at 3–6 months, often acute in onset with poor prognosis. HIV+ infants <1 yr of age are at risk even with CD4 ≥1500. Identify infants born to HIV+ mothers promptly (screen mothers during pregnancy), obtain PCR.
  • Begin PJP prophylaxis at 4-6 weeks in infants born to HIV-infected mothers who are HIV positive or who remain indeterminate.
  • Stop prophylaxis in children found to be presumptively HIV-negative (e.g., 2 negative PCRs; one obtained after 14 days and one after 1 month of age).
  • Continued PJP prophylaxis in HIV-infected children (age >1 yr) depends on immunologic stage. Recommended for all children Immunologic Category 3. In resource poor settings, WHO recommends for all HIV-exposed children age <2 yr, WHO Clinical Stage 2, 3 or 4, or CD4% < 25%.

Etiologies

  • Pneumocystis jirovecii (formerly Pneumocystis carinii - PCP)

Primary Regimens

  • Child Primary Prophylaxis
    • Infant age 1-12 months (HIV-infected or HIV-indeterminate)
      • TMP-SMX 150/750 mg/M2/day po in 2 divided doses 3x/wk on consecutive days
    • Child age 1-5 years (HIV-infected, CD4 < 500 or CD4 < 15%
      • TMP-SMX 150/750 mg/M2/day po single dose 3x/wk on consecutive days
    • Child age 6-12 years (HIV-infected, CD4 < 200 or CD4 < 15%)
      • TMP-SMX 150/750 mg/M2/day po q24h in 2 divided doses 3x/wk on alternate days

Alternative Regimens

  • Child Primary Prophylaxis, if TMP-SMX not tolerated
    • Aerosolized Pentamidine 300 mg once monthly via Respirgard II nebulizer (Age ≥ 5 years)
    • Dapsone 2 mg/kg (max 100 mg) po q24h (Age ≥ 1 month)
    • Atovaquone 30 mg/kg po q24h (Age 1-3 months)
    • Atovaquone 30 mg/kg po q24h (Age 4-24 months)
    • Pentamidine 4 mg/kg IV every 2–4 wks (if other options are unavailable)
