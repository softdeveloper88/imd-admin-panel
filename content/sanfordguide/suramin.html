Suramin
Tradename: Germanin

Usage and Dosing

  • Fexinidazole (oral drug) is now drug of choice for most cases of East and West African trypansomiasis

  • Suramin needed for Stage 1 disease in children aged < 6 years or body weight < 20 kg

    • can use while waiting for fexinidazole to become available
  • Available from CDC Drug Service or from WHO-designated centers in other countries (See Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources) 
  • Does not cross blood-brain barrier; no effect on CNS infection.
  • 1 gm dry powder for reconstitution; add 10 mL of sterile water for injection, USP, to yield a 10% (100 mg/mL) solution.
  • Do not use if test dose not tolerated.
  • Possible modes of action: Antimicrob Agents Chemother 64:e01168, 2020.

Adult Dose

    • 5 mg/kg IV (test dose), then 20 mg/kg up to 1 gm (2 mg/kg test dose in children) on days 1, 3, 7, 14 and 21

Adverse Effects

  • Side effects are frequent but usually are mild and reversible. They can include arthritis, nephrotoxicity (discontinue if casts appear or if increasing albuminuria), hepatotoxicity, fever, malaise, rash/pruritus, local edema, ocular reactions, neurologic complications (e.g., headache and paresthesia), and relative adrenal insufficiency.
  • Severe exfoliative dermatitis, agranulocytosis, hemolytic anemia, and life-threatening idiosyncratic (anaphylactic-type) reactions have been reported uncommonly. A test dose is given (see above) because of the possibility of a hypersensitivity reaction.
