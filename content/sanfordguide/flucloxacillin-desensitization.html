Flucloxacillin Desensitization

Overview

  • Protocols for intravenous or oral desensitization for flucloxacillin hypersensitivity.
  • Oral desensitization is preferred for safety reasons.
  • Observe patient during protocol for urticaria, angioedema, shortness of breath, or hemodynamic instability.
  • Flucloxacillin tolerance is limited to the period of continued drug exposure.
  • Reference: Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2018;62:e01371-18.

Oral Method (preferred)

  • Administer doses at 15 minute intervals.
  • Following completion of all 14 steps, approximately 800 mg of flucloxacillin has been administered. Observe patient for 30 minutes, and if no reaction occurs, administer 500-1000 mg flucloxacilin orally.

Intravenous Method

  • Administer doses at 15 minute intervals.
  • Following completion of all 18 steps, approximately 2560 mg of flucloxacillin has been administered. Observe patient for 30 minutes, and if no reaction occurs, administer 2 gm IV.
  • If the target dose is 1 gm, omit step 18. If no reaction occurs after 30 minutes, administer 1 gm IV.
