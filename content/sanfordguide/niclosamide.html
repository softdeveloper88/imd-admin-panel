Niclosamide
Tradename: Niclosamide

Usage and Dosing

  • Treatment option for tapeworms (Praziquantel is primary choice).
  • Not available in the US.
  • Formulated as 500 mg tablets that are chewed.
  • Non absorbed.

Adult Dose

Fish, dog, beef and pork tapeworm

2 gm po x 1 dose

Hymenolepis diminuta (rat) or
Hymenolepis nana (human) tape worm		2 gm po once daily x 7 days

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days)Fish, dog, beef, pork tapeworm:
50 mg/kg single dose (max 2 gm)
Rat (H. diminuta) or human (H. nana) tapeworm:
Wt 11-34 kg: 1 gm on day 1, then 500 mg q24h x6 days
Wt >34 kg: 1.5 gm on day 1, then 1 gm q24h x6 days

Adverse Effects

  • Anorexia and/or nausea and/or vomiting in roughly 3% of patients.

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Category B. Limited data in pregnancy. Not systemically absorbed. Use if benefit outweighs risk.
Use during Lactation Breast milk excretion unknown, but not systemically absorbed. Classified by WHO as compatible with breastfeeding.

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • Taenia solium
  • Taenia saginata
  • Diphyllobothrium sp.
  • Hymenolepis diminuta (rat)
  • Hymenolepis nana (human)

Pharmacology

Note: Niclosamide may be taken on an empty stomach (1 hour before or 2 hours after a meal). However, taking after a light meal (e.g., breakfast) helps to lessen GI upset.
