Surgical Prophylaxis, Head, Neck

Clinical Setting

  • Administer dose 30-60 minutes prior to incision
  • Antimicrobial prophylaxis in head and neck surgery appears efficacious only for procedures involving oral/pharyngeal mucosa (e.g., laryngeal or pharyngeal tumor) but even with prophylaxis, wound infection rate can be high.
  • No prophylaxis for uncontaminated head and neck surgery unless prosthetic device is to be implanted. 
  • Tympanostomy tubes do not require prophylaxis.
  • 2013 Clinical Practice Guidelines: Am J Health Syst Pharm 70:195, 2013.
  • See also, Prevention of Surgical Infections, Overview.

Etiologies

  • Normal flora of the mouth and oropharynx
  • Mixture of aerobic and anaerobic bacteria
  • Nasal flora can include Staph. aureus

Primary Regimens

  • Cefazolin 2 gm IV as single dose for clean procedures with prosthesis (repeat dose for procedures longer than 4 hours)
  • (Cefazolin 2 gm IV or Cefuroxime 1.5 gm IV) as a single dose (repeat dose for procedures longer than 4 hours) + Metronidazole 500 mg IV as a single dose for clean-contaminated procedures
  • Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 gm IV as a single dose for clean-contaminated procedures; repeat dose for procedures longer than 2 hours

Alternative Regimens

  • Clindamycin 900 mg IV x 1 dose (repeat dose for procedures longer than 6 hours) ± (Gentamicin or Tobramycin 5 mg/kg IV x 1 dose)
  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 1000 mg-200 mg IV x 1 dose (non-US, repeat dose for procedures longer than 2 hours)

Comments

  • No benefit from prophylaxis in tonsillectomy and certain other procedures (Am J Health Syst Pharm 70:195, 2013).
  • Gentamicin or Tobramycin can be added to Clindamycin for clean-contaminated procedures where there is risk of infection with gram-negative organisms.
