Candidiasis, Overview

Overview

  • Candida (albicans and non-albicans species) are a common cause of nosocomial bloodstream infections.
  • Candida species are part of the normal human GI flora and broad spectrum antibiotic therapy, GI surgery, and neutropenia are all risk factors for severe candidal infection
  • Candida species differ in their susceptibility to different antifungal agents which influences decisions on empiric and directed antifungal therapy for different species
  • Candida auris has recently emerged as a potentially muti-drug resistant species of Candida known to cause severe disease in the health-care setting. Cases have been identified in the US but larger outbreaks seen internationally (Clin Infect Dis 64:134, 2017). Be aware that isolates can be misidentified as Candida haemuloni or Saccharomyces cerevisiae and require molecular testing for definitive identification.  CDC Site on Candida auris. Also, published review: Clin Infect Dis 2018;66:306
  • See IDSA treatment guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 62:409, 2016.
