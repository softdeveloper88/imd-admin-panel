Atovaquone
Tradename: Mepron

Usage and Dosing

  • Atovaquone is a a hydroxy-1,4-naphthoquinone, an analog of ubiquinone.
  • Clinical uses:
    • Treatment of babesiosis
    • Treatment and prophylaxis of Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia (PJP)
    • Treatment and prophylaxis of toxoplasmosis

Adult Dose

  • FDA-approved: 1500 mg po daily (divided q12h-q24h).
  • Higher doses are used in certain situations.
  • Refer to disease pages for dosing details:
    • Babesiosis
    • Pneumocystis pneumonia (PJP), Adult
    • Toxoplasmosis, HIV/AIDS
    • Toxoplasmosis, Immunocompetent
    • Toxoplasmosis, Immunocompromised (AIDS, Post-transplantation)

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days) Age ≤12 yrs: safety and efficacy not established
Suggested dosing:
Babesiosis: 20 mg/kg q12h (w/azithro)
PJP treatment, mild-moderate disease:
Age 1-3 mon: 30-40 mg/kg q24h
Age 4-24 mon: 45 mg/kg q24h
Age >2 yrs to 12 yrs: 30-40 mg/kg q24h
Age ≥13 yrs: 750 mg q12h
PJP primary prophylaxis:
Age 1-3 mon: 30-40 mg/kg q24h
Age 4-24 mon: 45 mg/kg q24h
Age >2 yrs to 12 yrs: 30-40 mg/kg q24h
Age ≥13 yrs: 1500 mg q24h
Max/Day 1500 mg

Adverse Effects

  • Rash
  • GI (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)
  • Headache
  • Fever
  • Insomnia

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • Toxoplasma
  • Plasmodium species (malaria)
