Viekira: Paritaprevir/ritonavir + Ombitasvir + Dasabuvir
Tradename: Viekira Pak, Viekira XR

Usage and Dosing

  • NOTE: THIS PRODUCT WAS DISCONTINUED IN 2019 AND IS NO LONGER AVAILABLE.
  • Viekira Pak is a combination formulation of Ombitasvir, Paritaprevir, Ritonavir, and Dasabuvir and is indicated (with or without Ribavirin) for treatment of adults with genotype 1 chronic HCV, including patients with compensated cirrhosis( although caution should be used in treating those with advanced liver disease owing to fatal decompensation noted in some patients within the first 1 - 4 weeks of treatment; see Warning below)  The product consists of fixed-dose tablets of Ombitasvir 12.5 mg, Paritaprevir 75 mg, and Ritonavir 50 mg co-packaged with tablets of Dasabuvir 250 mg. The Ritonavir serves as a boosting agent for Paritaprevir.
  • Viekira XR is an extended-release formulation of Ombitasvir 8.33 mg + Paritaprevir 50 mg + Ritonavir 33.33 mg + Dasabuvir 200 mg. The recommended dose is three tablets orally once daily with food (with or without Ribavirin). Taking Viekira XR under fasting conditions may result in reduced virologic response and development of resistance.
  • Technivie is a combination formulation of Ombitasvir, Paritaprevir, and Ritonavir and is indicated (with Ribavirin) for treatment of adults with genotype 4 chronic HCV (Ombitasvir/Paritaprevir/Ritonavir) tablets every morning.
  • Test for evidence of current or prior Hepatitis B (HBV) infection: measure HBsAG and anti-HBC before initiating HCV treatment. If HBV co-infection, monitor patient for HBV reactivation during and post-HCV therapy.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions and contraindications (see Table below, not all-inclusive)

Adverse Effects

  • WARNING: Hepatic decompensation and hepatic failure, including liver transplantation or fatal outcomes, have been reported mostly in patients with advanced cirrhosis.
  • Black Box Warning: Cases of HBV reactivation, occasionally fulminant, during or after DAA therapy have been reported in HBV/HCV coninfected patients who were not already on HBV suppressive therapy
  • If administered with Ribavirin, contraindications to Ribavirin also apply.
  • Do not co-administer with drugs that are highly dependent on CYP3A for clearance; strong inducers of CYP3A and CYP2C8; and strong inhibitors of CYP2C8.
  • Do not use if known hypersensitivity to Ritonavir (e.g., toxic epidermal necrolysis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome).
  • With Ribavirin: fatigue, nausea, pruritus, other skin reactions, insomnia and asthenia.
  • Without Ribavirin: nausea, pruritus and insomnia.
