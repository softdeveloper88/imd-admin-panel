Recommendations for HIV Testing

HIV Testing Guidelines, Overview

  • All sexually active persons should be tested at least once for HIV, regardless of age.  Those with continuing risk should be tested  at least annually if at high risk of acquiring HIV and any time a person is screened or tested for a Sexually Transmitted Infection
  • See CDC HIV testing guidance. 
  • Universal HIV testing and linkage to care is a cornerstone of the U.S. CDC program: Ending the HIV Epidemic (2019).
  • The CDC suggested that signed consent for HIV testing be eliminated and included in the general consent for medical care. Pre-test counseling was not recommended unless patients were from high-risk groups. Patients should be told verbally that they will be tested as part of their medical care and given the opportunity to opt out of testing. This discussion should include an explanation about HIV infection, how it is transmitted, and implications about a positive test. If patient declines, it should be recorded on his/her chart.
  • The implications of a positive test, both screening test and confirmatory test, can be profound and have the potential for physical and psychological harm, particularly for women. Counseling and referral services for partner notification should be made available.
  • While the new recommendations indicate a change in direction, they may conflict with established state laws. Clinicians need to be aware of local regulations regarding consent and counseling. See CDC compilation of State laws.

Post-Testing Counseling

  • Rapid HIV antibody tests, which can provide results within 20 minutes has improved the efficiency of point of care testing. However, a confirmatory test such as an HIV RNA (viral load) test is still required. It is critically important to offer counseling to those who test positive. Issues to be discussed should include:
    • Emphasize that HIV infection can now be managed successfully as a complicated
      disease like diabetes
    • Stigma and the fear of disclosure of HIV status
    • Need to inform previous/current sexual partner(s)
    • Testing of children and partners at potential risk
    • Information about antiretroviral treatment and initiation of ARV therapy as soon as feasible
    • Communication that once on treatment and HIV RNA is undetectable, transmission to others via sexual activity is ~ zero
    • Strict adherence to safe-sex practices (especially consistent use of condoms) to help prevent other STIs
    • Avoidance of drugs that may cause disinhibition (amphetamines, etc.)
