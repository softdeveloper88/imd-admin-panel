Evotaz
Tradename: Atazanavir + Cobicistat

Usage and Dosing

  • Evotaz is a combination formulation of Atazanavir + Cobicistat indicated for
  • See individual components for drug-specific data:
    • Atazanavir (ATV)
    • Cobicistat (Cobi)
  • For class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.
  • Not recommended for use with regimens containing ritonavir, other protease inhibitors or elvitegravir.
  • Not recommended for use in pregnancy due to substantially lowered exposures of atazanavir and cobicistat. Treatment should not be initiated in pregnant women, and an alternative regimen should be used in women who become pregnant on Evotaz therapy; continuation with frequent viral load monitoring can be considered.
  • Contraindicated if previous hypersensitivity reaction to either component, e.g., Stevens-Johnson syndrome, erythema multiforme, toxic skin eruptions.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see Table below, not all-inclusive).

Adult Dose

  • 1 tablet once daily with food.
  • Tablets: ATV 300 mg + Cobi 150 mg.

      Adverse Effects

      • See individual components
      • Most common: jaundice, ocular icterus, nausea
      • PR interval prolongation
      • Rash
      • Renal failure, Fanconi syndrome (in combination with Tenofovir)
      • Nephrolithiasis, cholelithiasis
      • Hepatotoxicity
      • Hyperbilirubinemia
      • IRIS
      • Hemophilia
      • Hyperglycemia
      • Fat redistribution
