Laryngitis, Acute

Clinical Setting

  • Hoarseness with, or without, other signs of inflammation in the upper airway: e.g., sore throat, cough, nasal congestion.
  • Acute vocal strain (e.g., screaming) can cause acute laryngitis
  • Chronic laryngitis has a much larger differential diagnosis: inflammation from irritants (smoking), chronic aspiration associated with gastroesophageal reflux disease, vocal cord polyps, laryngeal cancer are all examples

Etiologies

  • Viral pathogens are the etiology in approximately 90% of the cases of acute laryngitis

Primary Regimens

  • Antimicrobial therapy is not indicated

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Two placebo-controlled studies and a Cochrane review (Cochrane Database Syst Rev. (5)CD004783,2015 May 23) support no treatment recommendation.
