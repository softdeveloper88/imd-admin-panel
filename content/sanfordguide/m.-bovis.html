Mycobacterium bovis

Clinical Setting

  • The M. tuberculosis complex includes M. bovis.
  • All isolates are resistant to Pyrazinamide (PZA).
  • Extrapulmonary disease and gastrointestinal disease more common than pulmonary disease and isolation is not required unless there is pulmonary involvement.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium bovis

Primary Regimens

  • Isoniazid + Rifampin + Ethambutol for 2 months then Isoniazid + Rifampin for an additional 7 months.
    • For dosing see First-line TB Drug Dosing Table.

Alternative Regimens

  • Regimens without PZA used to treat M. tuberculosis infection are likely also to be effective for M. bovis.

Comments

  • Monoresistance to PZA is a marker of M. bovis infection.
