Thyroiditis, Suppurative

Clinical Setting

  • Rare, potentially life threatening bacterial infection of the thyroid gland
  • Can present at any age
  • Causes include pyriform sinus fistulae, hematogenous, direct extension, ruptured or perforated esophagus, diagnostic procedures
  • Presentation includes anterior neck pain, swelling and fever

Etiologies

  • Many potential bacterial etiologies, depending in part on cause of infection (Thyroid 3:247 2010)
    • Gram positive aerobes ~39%
    • Gram negative aerobes ~ 25%
    • Anaerobes ~12% (often polymicrobial)
    • Others including Pneumocystis jirovecii (AIDS), Nocardia, M. tuberculosis

Primary Regimens

  • Piperacillin/tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h

Alternative Regimens

  • Meropenem 1 gm IV q8h
  • Vancomycin, Cefepime, Metronidazole

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Therapy can be narrowed based on blood cultures if positive

Comments

  • Blood cultures and gram stain and culture of thyroid or abscess tissue should be used to guide therapy
  • Imaging of neck and chest is critical
  • Evaluate thyroid function and thyroglobulin antibodies - endocrinology consultation useful
  • Fine needle or radiologically-guided aspirate to drain abscess and obtain specimens
  • Surgery may be necessary if poor response and to address pyriform sinus fistulae
  • References: Children: J Paediatr Child Health. 2012 Mar;48(3):E116-8; General: Thyroid 3:247 2010
