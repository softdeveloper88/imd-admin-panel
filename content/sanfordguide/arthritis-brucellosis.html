Brucella sp., Arthritis

Clinical Setting

  • Subacute or chronic presentation, often preceded by a flu-like illness. Most cases in the U.S. are imported.
  • History of animal exposure (e.g., veterinarian, farmer, butcher or others with contact to goats, cattle, pigs) or exposure to unpasteurized milk or milk products.
  • Sacroiliac joint involved in ~60% of cases, spondylodiscitis in ~40% (Intern Med 50:421, 2011).
  • See Brucella sp. for full disease spectrum

Diagnosis

  • Joint fluid findings often non-specific and synovial fluid cultures sterile;
  • Culture:
    • Slow growing coccobacilli. Can culture from blood, bone marrow, or other body fluids
    •  Sensitivity of culture varies with severity of illness and lab methods employed (15-70%)
    • Inform lab personnel of possible brucella so as to protect technologists from exposure
  • Serology
    • Can detect elevated or rising titers of serum antibodies using a variety of techniques, e.g., serum agglutination, ELISA
    •  Be sure to screen with serial dilutions of serum as prozone phenomena can occur.
  • Molecular tests (PCR) are emerging ; Sensitive and can detect organism within 10 days of onset

Etiologies

  • Brucella abortus (cattle)
  • Brucella melitensis (goats)
  • Brucella suis (pigs)
  • Brucella canis (dogs)

Primary Regimens

  • Adult and child age ≥ 8 years:
    • (Doxycycline 100 mg po bid + Rifampin 600-900 mg po q24h) x 3 months + Gentamicin 5 mg/kg once daily x first 7 days
    • (Ciprofloxacin 750 mg po bid + Rifampin 600-900 mg po once daily) x minimum of 3 months
  • Child age < 8 years: TMP-SMX 5 mg/kg (TMP component) po q12h x 6 weeks + Gentamicin 5 mg/kg q24h IV/IM q8h x 2 weeks
  • Pregnancy: TMP-SMX 1 DS tab (160 mg TMP component) po qid + Rifampin 600–900 mg po q24h) x 6 weeks

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy for osteomyelitis or spondylitis is 3 months.

Comments

  • Brucellosis is a reportable disease in many states and is listed as an agent of possible bioterrorism by CDC.
