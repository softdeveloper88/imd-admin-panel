Bartholin's Gland Abscess

Clinical Setting

  • Pain and swelling of labia major, perivaginally, typically unilateral
  • Erythema, + fluctulance, purulent drainage
  • Secondary to obstruction of Bartholin gland duct

Etiologies

  • E. coli
  • Staphylococcus aureus
  • Streptococcal species
  • Sexually transmitted pathogens less common

Primary Regimens

  • Word catheter or marsupialization preferred to needle aspiration or simple incision and drainage, which are not recommended due to high rates of recurrence
    • Success rate of about 50% with needle aspiration in one non-randomized prospective study (Eur J Obstet Gynecol Reprod Biol. 2022; 276:92)
  • Antimicrobial therapy not recommended unless concomitant infection.
    • If concomitant UTI, extensive cellulitis, sexually transmitted infection, or recurrent infection:
      • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po bid x 5-7 days
      • TMP-SMX 160 mg/800 mg po bid x 5-7 days
      • See gonococcal infection and chlamydial infection for abscess associated with sexually transmitted infection (Am J Emerg Med. 2021; 44:323).

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  •  Review articles: American Family Physician. 2019; 99:760 and Prim Care. 2021; 48:569.
