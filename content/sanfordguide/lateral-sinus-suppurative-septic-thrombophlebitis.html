Lateral Sinus Suppurative (Septic) Thrombophlebitis

Clinical Setting

  • Lateral sinus suppurative (septic) thrombophlebitis is most often a complication of chronic otitis media and/or mastoiditis.
  • Treatment:
    • ENT consultation: Consider radical mastoidectomy
    • Obtain cultures
    • Antibiotic therapy
    • Note: use of anticoagulants is controversial.
  • Prognosis: favorable

Diagnosis

  • CT or MRI venograms

Etiologies

  • Polymicrobial (often); reflects microbiology of chronic otitis media
  • Aerobes: Proteus sp., Escherichia coli, others; rarely Pseudomonas aeruginosa
  • Anaerobes: e.g, Bacteroides fragilis
  • Staphylococcus aureus

Primary Regimens

  • Cefepime 2 gm IV q8h + Metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h + Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL

Alternative Regimens

  • Meropenem 2 gm IV q8h (high dose to penetrate CSF) + Linezolid 600 mg IV q12h

Comments

  • Use of anticoagulants controversial
  • Usually favorable prognosis
  • References: J Emerg Med 2015;48: e9-e13; Pediatric Infect Dis. 2022;41:297
