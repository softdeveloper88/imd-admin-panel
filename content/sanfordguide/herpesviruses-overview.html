Herpesviruses, Overview

Overview

  • Human Herpes Viruses (HHV) are a family of DNA viruses that are highly prevalent and capable of causing a wide range of diseases. There are nine known human herpesviruses that can cause disease in humans, including:  HSV-1, HSV-2, HSV-6, HSV-7, HSV-8, EBV, CMV, VZV, and herpes simiae.
