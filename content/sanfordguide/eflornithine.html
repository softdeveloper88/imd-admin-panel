Eflornithine
Tradename: Ornidyl

Usage and Dosing

  • Antitrypanosomal agent approved (US) for trypanosomiasis, but not commercially marketed. Sanofi-Aventis product (Ornidyl). May be obtained from CDC Drug Service.
  • Fexinidazole now preferred by WHO and CDC where available with several clinical exceptions. See Trypanosomiasis, West African
  • NECT may have lower fatality and fewer relapses but oral fexinidazole much higher adherence (Cochrane Database System Rev 2021 Dec 9;12(12):CD015374)
  • Large clinical series using NECT (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 2021 Nov 8;15(11):e0009903).
  • Available in US from CDC Drug Service. See Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources

Adult Dose

  • Eflornithine 400 mg/kg/day IV divided q12h x 7 days + Nifurtimox 15 mg/kg/day po, divided q8h x 10 days (abbreviated NECT)

Adverse Effects

  • Diarrhea, vomiting, abdominal pain
  • Bone marrow suppression, thrombocytopenia, anemia, leukopenia
  • Seizures, headache
  • Alopecia
  • Jaundice
  • Hearing impairment
  • Fever
