Aspergillosis, Chronic Cavitary Pulmonary

Clinical Setting

  • Chronic pulmonary disease often with systemic symptoms.
  • Multiple cavities, with or without aspergilloma.
  • Positive aspergillus antibodies in serum, positive culture of bronchopulmonary specimen, compatible CT-scan.

Etiologies

  • Aspergillus sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg po/IV q12h x 1 day, then 4 mg/kg po/IV q12h

Alternative Regimens

  • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily
  • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs)
    • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily
    • Suspension 400 mg po bid
  • Itraconazole oral solution 2.5 mg/kg po q12h or 200 mg capsules po q12h

Comments

  • Review:  Thorax 70:270, 2015. 
  • Antifungal therapy is standard of care.
  • Duration of therapy may be prolonged, 6-12 months. For response rates with Voriconazole see Eur J Clin Microbiol Infect Dis 31:3231, 2012.
  • Surgical resection associated with high morbidity and mortality.
  • For further information see IDSA Guidelines: (Clin Infect Dis 63:433, 2016)
