Parenteral Penicillin Desensitization

Overview

  • Oral desensitization route is preferred over the parenteral (Pen G) route. 1/3 of patients will develop a transient reaction, usually mild, during desensitization or treatment.
  • For oral method, see Oral Desensitization with Pen-VK.
  • May be administered either subcutaneously, IM or IV.
  • Perform in ICU setting. Discontinue all β-blockers. Have IV line, epinephrine, ECG, and spirometer available.
  • Note regarding Penicillin skin testing for evaluation of penicillin allergy:
    • Testing with major determinant (Benzylpenicilloyl Polylysine) and minor determinants has negative predictive value of 97-99%. Risk of systemic reaction to skin testing <1%. See Ann Allergy, Asthma & Immunol 106:1, 2011.
    • See also, Penicillin (Beta Lactam) Allergy Overview.
  • References: Clin Infect Dis 35:26, 2002; Am J Med 121:572, 2008; Curr Clin Top Infect Dis 13:131, 1993.
  • See also discussion in 2015 CDC STD Guidelines:  MMWR 64(RR-3):1, 2015.
