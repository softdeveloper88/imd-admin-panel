Paromomycin
Tradename: Humatin, Aminosidine

Usage and Dosing

  • Oral aminoglycoside
  • FDA approved only for intestinal amebiasis; not effective against extraintestinal infection.
  • Not absorbed except in rare patient with concomitant inflammatory bowel disease.
  • Topical preparation ointment indicated for leishmania treatment only when low potential for mucosal spread.
  • Aminosidine is a parenteral form of paromomycin not widely available and used in protocols for visceral leishmaniasis in combination with other agents.

Adult Dose

E. histolytica*25-35 mg/kg/day po in 3 divided doses x 7 days
Dientamoeba fragilis infection
Same as above
Alternative: Giardia duodenalis10 mg/kg po tid x 5-10 days

* Asymptomatic cyst passer or to eliminate lumenal cysts after treatment of invasive disease caused by trophozoites

Adverse Effects

  • If absorbed due to concomitant inflammatory bowel disease can result in ototoxicity, nephrotoxicity.
  • Doses >3 gm daily are associated with nausea, abdominal cramps, diarrhea.
  • Intestinal bacterial overgrowth due to neomycin like effect
  • Contraindicated in intestinal obstruction

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Limited data. Non-absorbed, fetal risk low.
Use during Lactation No data, but unlikely to be present in breast milk. Widely used for giardiasis in pregnancy.
