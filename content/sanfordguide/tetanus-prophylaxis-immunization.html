Tetanus Prophylaxis, Immunization

Clinical Setting

  • Tetanus post-exposure prophylaxis.
  • Determine wound classification.
  • Wound classification guides immunization recommendation.
  • For treatment of primary tetanus infection, see Tetanus, Treatment.

Tetanus Immunization history

  • For immunization details, see Tetanus, Vaccines.
  • Patient's history of tetanus immunization determines recommendation.
    • Unknown or <3 previous doses:
      • For dirty, tetanus-prone wound:
        • Give Tetanus and diphtheria (Td) toxoids adsorbed (adult)
        • Give Tetanus immune globulin (TIG)(human)
      • For clean, not tetanus-prone wound:
        • Give Tetanus and diphtheria (Td) toxoids adsorbed (adult), but not TIG.
    • 3 or more previous doses:
      • For dirty, tetanus-prone wound:
        • Give Td only if >5 years since last booster, no TIG.
      • For clean, not tetanus-prone wound:
        • Give Td only if >10 years since last booster, no TIG.

Comments

  • For children age <7 years, give DTaP (or DT if pertussus is contraindicated) instead of Td.
  • For persons age ≥7 years, Td is preferred to tetanus toxoid alone.
  • If ≥ 10 years old, can use Tdap instead of Td (MMWR 64: 243, 2015).
  • Incidence of tetanus higher in those 65 or older, who also have higher mortality (MMWR 64: 243, 2015)
  • References: MMWR 39:37, 1990; MMWR 67:2, 2018.
