Isavuconazonium sulfate
Tradename: Cresemba

Usage and Dosing

  • Isavuconazonium sulfate (Cresemba) is the prodrug of the azole antifungal Isavuconazole, an agent for treatment of invasive aspergillosis and invasive mucormycosis in adults.
    • Deemed non-inferior to Voriconazole in the treatment of invasive aspergillosis. All cause mortality at day 42 was 19% for Isavuconazole and 20% for Voriconazole (Lancet 387:760, 2016).
    • Mucormycosis approval based on 37 patients compared to historic controls. All cause mortality was 38% (Lancet Infect Dis 16:828, 2016).
  • Administered as the prodrug Isavuconazonium sulfate (186 mg of Isavuconazonium sulfate = 100 mg Isavuconazole).
  • Available in bioequivalent oral and IV formulations.
    • Intravenous administration: Dilute reconstituted solution in 250 mL D5W or NS. Infuse over 1 hr using in-line filter (0.2 to 1.2 micron). Complete infusion within six hours of dilution.
    • Nasogastric tube: Use freshly reconstituted injection. Provides systemic exposure similar to oral capsules. Administer within one hour of reconstitution.
    • Oral administration: Capsules must be swallowed whole. Do not chew, crush, dissolve, or open capsules.
  • Do not use a pneumatic transport system for the IV solution. Avoid unnecessary shaking or vibration.
  • Therapeutic Drug Monitoring is not routinely recommended because there is not a clear exposure-response relationship and low variability of plasma levels (Med Mycol 57: S168, 2019).
  • Contraindications:
    • Numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive)
    • Do not use in patients with shortened QTc interval (isavuconazole shortens the QT interval in a concentration-related manner).

      Adverse Effects

      • Most common: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, headache, elevated liver chemistry tests, hypokalemia, constipation, dyspnea, cough, peripheral edema, and back pain.
      • Hepatic: increased ALT, AST.

      Pregnancy Risk

      FDA Risk Category 1 No human data; fetal harm in animals.
      Use during Lactation Avoid use

      Antimicrobial Spectrum

      • Cryptococcus spp.
      • Trichosporon spp.
      • Aspergillus flavus
      • Aspergillus fumigatus
      • Aspergillus niger
      • Mucorales such as Rhizopus oryzae
