Skin Abscess, Boils, Furuncles

Clinical Setting

  • Purulent skin lesions including boils, furuncles, carbuncles and abscesses. Acute bacterial skin and skin structure infection (ABSSSI).
  • Incision and drainage is the mainstay of therapy; needle aspiration is inadequate.
  • Review of management: N Engl J Med 370:1039, 2014. 
  • See also, Recurrent Furunculosis (decolonization).

Diagnosis

  • If diagnostic uncertainty and/or to assess adequacy of drainage, ultrasonography can help.

Etiologies

  • Staphylococcus aureus
    • Methicillin sensitive (MSSA)
    • Methicillin resistant (MRSA)

Primary Regimens

  • Oral therapy, outpatient, empiric therapy (see Comments)
    • Low suspicion, low prevalence of MRSA
      • Incision and drainage PLUS
        • Dicloxacillin 500 mg po tid-qid OR Cephalexin 500 mg po tid-qid
    • Moderate or high suspicion, moderate or high prevalence of MRSA
      • Incision and drainage PLUS
        • TMP-SMX 160/800 (1 DS tab; 2 DS tabs in obese patient, BMI > 40) po bid 
        • Clindamycin 300-450 mg (higher dose in obese patient, BMI > 40) po tid 
    • If no response after 2-3 days, look for undrained abscess
  • Inpatients:
    • Incision and drainage: culture abscess and blood
      • Empirical antibiotic therapy (in absence of specific culture and sensitivity data select an agent with activity against MRSA)
        • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL)
      • Definitive therapy once culture results are available
        • MSSA
          • Nafcillin or Oxacillin 1 gm q4h IV
          • Cefazolin 1 gm IV q8h
        • MRSA
          • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL)

Alternative Regimens

  • For the patient with moderately severe or severe infection for whom a parenteral agent is preferred and who is a candidate for out-patient therapy: Dalbavancin 1.5 gm IV infused over 30 min single dose
  • Oral agents
    • MSSA
      • Dicloxacillin 500 mg po tid-qid OR Cephalexin 500 mg po tid-qid
      • Flucloxacillin 250-500 mg po tid 
      • TMP-SMX 1-2 DS bid
      • Clindamycin 300-450 mg tid
    • MRSA
      • Linezolid 600 mg po q12h 
      • Doxycycline 100 mg po q12h or Minocycline 100 mg po q12h 
  • Parental agents
    • MSSA
      • Flucloxacillin 1 gm IV q4-6h
    • MRSA
      • Daptomycin 4 mg/kg IV q24h
      • Linezolid 600 mg IV q12h
      • Ceftaroline 600 mg IV q12h

 

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Recent studies indicate that shorter course of therapy, 5-7 days are as effective as longer 10-14 courses.
  • Agents active against MRSA are also active against MSSA, but a MSSA-specific beta-lactam antibiotic is preferred over these alternatives in the absence of serious allergy or toxicity that precludes use of the beta-lactam.

Comments

  • Two high quality, randomized, double-blind placebo-controlled trials have shown benefit of treating even small abscesses with active drug (TMP-SMX or Clindamycin) with higher cure rates, fewer recurrences, and good tolerability (N Engl J Med 2016;  374:823-32 and N Engl J Med 2017;  376:2545-2555). 
  • Obesity: Lower dosage range of TMP-SMX (1 DS instead of 2 DS) and Clindamycin (300 mg instead of 450 mg) found to be associated with treatment failure in obese patients (BMI > 40) (J Infect 65:128, 2012) .
  • Other options: Fusidic acid 250-500 mg po q8-12h + Rifampin 300 mg po bid. Fusidic acid is available in Europe, but not in the US.
  • Avoid a fluoroquinolone unless the isolate is known to be susceptible in vitro.
