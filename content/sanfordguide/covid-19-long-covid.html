COVID-19, Long COVID

Clinical Setting

  • Long-COVID, or Post-Acute COVID Syndrome, is an infection-associated chronic condition that presents with a wide array of possible symptoms affecting many organ systems, most commonly gut, lung, heart, and brain.
  • By definition, symptoms of Long-COVID occur at least 4 weeks following a documented bout of acute SARS-CoV-2 infection, typically after resolution of symptoms of acute COVID-19.  Long COVID can follow an asymptomatic course of acute COVID-19
  • Common symptoms include (either occurring in isolation or overlapping):
    • Bloating, constipation, or diarrhea
    • Difficulty concentrating; memory difficulties
    • Recurring or persistent headaches 
    • Fatigue
    • Lightheadedness
    • Tachycardia; Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome (POTS)
    • Alterations in taste and/or smell
    • Dyspnea and / or persistent cough
    • Sleep disturbances
  • Symptoms may wax and wane.
  • Diagnosis is clinical; no specific test determines the presence of Long-COVID
  • Occurs more often in women, individuals of Hispanic ancestry, those with hospitalization for acute COVID-19, and those with underlying other co-morbidities
  • COVID-19 vaccines are associated with reduced incidence of Long-COVID

Etiologies

  • Unknown; Follows an acute bout of COVID-19

Primary Regimens

  • Symptomatic treatment for the most prevalent or bothersome symptom
    • Antiviral Rx (e.g., nirmatrelvir-ritonavir, other anti-SARS-CoV-2 medications are not proven to be effective)

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

    • Long-COVID is a real disorder, affecting 5 - 8% of those who have had acute COVID-19
    • Multiple studies ongoing to assess treatment for the most common symptom complex(es) of Long-COVID (RECOVER Trial)
