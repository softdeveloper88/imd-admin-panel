Surgical Prophylaxis, Neurosurgery

Clinical Setting

  • Antimicrobial prophylaxis for clean craniotomy, CSF shunt and intrathecal pump implantation procedures.
  • 2017 Infectious Diseases Society of America's Clinical Practice Guidelines for Healthcare-Associated Ventriculitis and Meningitis Clinical Infectious Diseases, Volume 64, Issue 6, 15 March 2017, Pages e34–e65, https://doi.org/10.1093/cid/ciw861
  • 2013 Clinical Practice Guidelines: Am J Health Syst Pharm 70:195, 2013.
  • See Prevention of Surgical Infections, Overview.

Etiologies

  • Skin flora: to include possible Staph. aureus, coagulase-negative staphylococci; also C. acnes and gram-negative cocci
  • Propionibacterium acnes (Cutibacterium acnes), can infect shunts and other intracranial hardware
  • Occasionally aerobic gram-negative bacilli

Primary Regimens

  • Cefazolin 2 gm IV as a single dose (3 gm if weight ≥ 120 kg)

Alternative Regimens

  • Vancomycin 1 gm IV as a single dose (l.5 gm if weight > 90 kg)
  • Clindamycin 900 mg IV as a single dose

Comments

  • Randomized study in a hospital with high prevalence of infection due to methicillin-resistant Staph. aureus (MRSA) showed Vancomycin was more effective than Cefazolin in preventing CSF shunt infections (J Hosp Infect 69:337, 2008).
  • The benefits of continuing prophylaxis for external ventricular drains is undefined and actual practices are variable (Am J Health Syst Pharm 70:195, 2013).
