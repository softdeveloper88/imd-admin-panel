DDx: Cardiomyopathy, HIV patient

Clinical Syndrome

  • Reduction in ejection fraction of both right and left ventricles.
  • Reduced ejection fraction common in AIDS patients but only symptomatic in 1-3 %, usually when CD4 count < 100/μL.
  • Diagnosis: by exclusion. Endomyocardial biopsy rarely useful.

Differential Diagnosis

  • Most commonly idiopathic.  Many potential causes that are not mutually exclusive: HIV virus-induced damage, other infections (CMV, coxsackie virus, adenovirus, toxoplasmosis and even Chagas Disease), autoimmune myocarditis, accelerated atherosclerosis,  and drug toxicity (alcohol, doxorubicin).
  • Consider pericarditis and pulmonary hypertension.

Treatment

  • Antiretroviral therapy (ART)
  • Diuretics, ACE inhibitors, and beta-blockers
  • If evidence of disseminated CMV infection: Valganciclovir 900 mg po q12h x 3 weeks
  • References: Prog Cardiovasc Dis. 2020;63:134; Am J Cardiol 2023;186:50
