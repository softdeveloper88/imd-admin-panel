Keratitis, Acanthamoeba

Clinical Setting

  • Protozoal keratitis is an uncommon corneal infection usually associated with trauma or soft contact lens use.
  • Usually uniocular with circular stromal infiltrate of the iris; corneal ulceration may occur.
  • Large case series (Am J Ophthamol Case Rep 2022 Jan 27;25:101372)

Diagnosis

  • Microscopy of corneal scrapings; direct visualization with a confocal microscope is sometimes possible.
  • PCR or antibody probe of corneal scrapings may be available in research laboratories.

Etiologies

  • Acanthamoeba castellani
  • Acanthamoeba polyphaga
  • Other Acanthamoeba sp.
  • Other free-living amoeba species including Hartmannella and Vahlkampfiid amoebae are causative (Cornea 36:785, 2017)

Primary Regimens

  • Topical 0.02%-0.2% biguanide chlorhexidine and/or  0.02%-0.06% Polyhexamethylene biguanide (PHMB)
    • for both PHMB and chlorhexidine 0.02% usual starting dose.
      • titrate to response working with ophthalmologist. 
      • 2023 RCT demonstrated PHMB 0.08% monotherapy with a cure rate of >86%, with a similar efficacy as combination therapy with PHMB 0.02% and propamidine 0.1%.  Ophthalmology. 2023 Oct 5:S0161-6420(23)00710-8.
      •  Propamidine (0.1%) or hexamidine (0.1%) harder to find and less used.
  • Apply drops hourly day and night for 48 hrs then qhourly during the daytime for 1 wk then taper over 3 - 4 weeks.
  • Debridement may also be warranted but unproven. Am J Ophthalmol 145:130, 2008. 

Alternative Regimens

  • Limited data, see Comments

Comments

  • Treatment for up to 12 months sometimes required
  • Other treatment alternatives , but limited data
    • Small RCT (n=23) showed efficacy of Voriconazole 1% solution (Eye (Lond) 2020 Jul 27)
    • Uncontrolled reports of amebic eradication with oral Miltefosine but post-miltefosine inflammatory response leads to corneal thinning and possible perforation (Ocul Immunol Inflamm 2021 Nov 17;29(7-8):1576)
  • Surgical approaches for advanced disease unproven.
    • corneal transplant may be successful
  • Protozoa in contact lens solution (BMC Ophthalmol 2016, 16:191)
  • Soft contact lens reference (Clin Infect Dis 35:434, 2002)
  • Early use of topical steroids associated with poorer outcome but remains controversial
  • For a review, see Biology (Basel)  2023, 12:1489.
