What's in the digital Sanford Guide?

  • Are you a new user of the Sanford Guide? New to the digital edition? Long-time print edition user? Just interested in the scope of content and where to find it?
    • The digital Sanford Guide (web and apps) is a topic-based resource that focuses on treatment of infectious diseases.
    • If you are familiar with the tabular organization of The Sanford Guide to Antimicrobial Therapy (print edition), this will help you find your way through the topic-based digital content.
    • Rest assured that all of the information found in the print tables is included in the digital edition. In fact, the digital Sanford Guide both extends and expands on the print edition coverage and is updated continuosly.
  • Print vs. digital organization
    • Each row in the major therapy tables (print Tables 1, 11, 12, 13, 14) is a digital edition topic
    • In print, drug information is grouped in various tables.
    • In the digital edition, all information for a drug is combined on a single page for that drug: Usage & dosing (adult, pediatric) dose adjustments (renal impairment, hepatic impairment), adverse effects, spectra of activity, pharmacology, drug interactions.
    • We have not abandoned tables in the digital Sanford Guide by any means: duration of therapy, pharmacology, pediatric dosing, renal impairment dosing and others are all found under the Tables & Tools menu.
    • The Activity Spectra includes links to drug and pathogen pages
    • In the digital edition, interactive features, e.g., calculators replace printed formulas.

Navigation

  • Navigating the digital edition content is fast and easy with several alternatives:
    • Full text search
    • Drop-down menus
    • Overview or "signpost" pages
    • Links on pages
  • Full text search
    • Enter one of more terms in the Search Site field (below the Sanford Guide logo)
    • As you type, search results begin to populate in a drop-down list. Select from the list to go to that topic
  • Drop-down menus
    • The main categories are: Syndromes, Pathogens, Anti-infectives, Hepatitis, HIV/AIDS, Prevention, Tables & Tools
    • Hover over (or tap on a mobile device) a main category to view sub-categories that allow you to drill down to a topic
  • Overview pages
    • Larger topics, for example, endocarditis, include an Overview or "signpost" page containing information relevant across the broad topic as well as links to each specific topic.
  • Links on pages
    • Drugs on syndrome and pathogen pages are linked to drug pages
    • References are linked to PubMed
    • Related topics are linked

Organization

  • Syndromes
    • Think of Syndromes as the equivalent of print Table 1
    • Syndromes covers antibacterial therapy, both empiric and specific
    • On each syndrome page: drugs are linked to the page containing all information for that drug; references are linked to PubMed; and related topics are linked
  • Pathogens
    • Bacterial organisms, think print Table 2, are organized alphabetically. Like Syndrome pages, drugs, references and related topics are linked
    • Fungal infections (Table 11)
    • Mycobacterial infections (Table 12)
    • Parasitic infections (Table 13)
    • Viral infections (Table 14)
  • Anti-infectives
    • Antibacterial agents are grouped by category, with Overview pages for larger groups or class-wide information
    • Antifungal, antimycobacterial, antiparasitic, antiretroviral and antiviral agents are similarly organized
    • Each drug page covers:
      • Usage and dosing in general
      • Dosing specifics
        • Adult
        • Pediatric
        • Renal impairment
        • Hepatic impairment
      • Adverse effects
      • Activity
      • Pharmacology
      • Drug interactions
  • Hepatitis
    • The Hepatitis menu includes Hepatitis A through E, with expanded coverage of HBV and HCV, plus prevention and drug page links
  • HIV/AIDS
    • Think of HIV/AIDS as the equivalent of The Sanford Guide to HIV/AIDS Therapy
  • Prevention
    • This is where to find prophylaxis (dental, Group B strep, surgical, post-exposure, rabies, tetanus, transplants, etc.) that is covered in print Tables 15 and 20.
    • Links to the current CDC immunization recommendations are found here
  • Tables & Tools
    • A wealth of information lives under Tables & Tools, including
      • Activity Spectra (Table 4)
      • Adverse effects & allergy: beta-lactam allergy, desensitization, photo-sensitivity, QTc interval prolongation
      • Interactive calculators
      • Comparative tables optimized for large and small screens
        • Obesity dosing
        • Pediatric dosing (Table 16)
        • Renal impairment dosing (Table 17)
        • Pregnancy risk and lactation safety
        • Pharmacology (Table 9): pharmacologic features, pharmacodynamics, enzyme- & transporter-mediated interactions
      • Duration of therapy (Table 3)
      • Novel dosing strategies
        • Continuous or prolonged infusion
        • Inhalation
        • Intraperitoneal
