Osteomyelitis, Candida

Clinical Setting

  • Predisposing factors include hematogenous dissemination (67%), direct inoculation (25%), and contiguous infection (9%) in one large series of 207 cases (Clin Infect Dis 55:1338, 2012).  Many cases are not associated with immunosuppression.

Etiologies

  • Candida albicans and less frequently non-albicans Candida sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Fluconazole 400 mg (6 mg/kg) IV/po daily OR
  • Caspofungin 70 mg loading dose, then 50 mg/day IV or Micafungin 100 mg/day IV or Anidulafungin 200 mg IV loading dose, then 100 mg/day IV x at least 2 weeks, followed by Fluconazole as above

Alternative Regimens

  • Lipid-based Amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg daily x at least 2 weeks, followed by Fluconazole as above

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of treatment: total of 6-12 months.

Comments

  • Surgical debridement is often necessary; hardware should be removed whenever possible since hardware retention may lead to treatment failure in up to 45% of cases (Clin Infect Dis 55:1338, 2012).
  • IDSA treatment guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 62:409, 2016.
