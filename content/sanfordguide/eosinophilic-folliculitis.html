Eosinophilic Folliculitis, HIV

Clinical Syndrome

  • A pruritic papular skin eruption of unknown etiology. Papular folliculitis or small pustules on face, scalp, neck and upper chest.
  • Common in patients with advanced HIV (low CD4 counts).
  • Rare in non-HIV patients; may be associated with bone marrow transplantation.
  • Rare manifestation of adverse reaction to medications.
  • Diagnosis: skin biopsy + perifollicular infiltrates of lymphocytes and eosinophils. Elevated serum IgE and eosinophilia in 25-50%.

Etiologies, Differential Diagnosis

  • Etiology unknown. Not likely bacteria or fungus. Some theorize demodex mites. Possibly an autoimmune reaction to sebum or sebum-producing cells.
  • Differential diagnosis:
    • Acne
    • Bacterial folliculitis, e.g., Staph. aureus, P. aeruginosa
    • Corticosteroid folliculitis
    • Pustular psoriasis
    • Atopic dermatitis
    • Scabies

Treatment

  • Antiretroviral therapy (ART)
  • Topical corticosteriods to control pruritis
  • Antihistamines may help
  • UVB phototherapy effective but relapse reported
  • Anecdotal reports of benefit from Metronidazole 250 mg po tid x 3-4 wks
  • May respond to single dose ivermectin (Clin Exp Dermatol 34: e981, 2009)
