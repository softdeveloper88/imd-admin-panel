BK Virus

Clinical Setting

  • BK virus induced nephropathy in renal transplant recipients
  • BK virus hemorrhagic cystitis in hematopoietic stem cell transplant recipients
  • Asymptomatic viremia/viruia in immunocompromised patients is common and may not be clinically significant

Diagnosis

  • Definitive diagnosis: renal biopsy with histopathologic changes (viral changes and tubular injury) plus positive immunohistochemistry for BK.  Identification of BK viremia plus nephropathy is also suggestive of the diagnosis. BK viuria is less specific of a finding.

Etiologies

  • BK virus  (polyomavirus)

Primary Regimens

  • Reduce immunosuppression if possible
  • No antiviral or immunotherapies have demonstrated clear benefit for prevention or treatment

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Updated American Society of Transplantation Guidelines on management of BK following renal transplant: Clin Transplant 33; e13528, 2019
  • In cases of refractory BK nephropathy and hemorrhagic cystitis, cidofivir has been used for treatment (IV and intravesicular) although efficacy has not been clearly been demonstrated (Am J Transplant 5:1997, 2005)
  • Report of cidofovir as potentially effective for BK hemorrhagic cystitis (Clin Infect Dis 49:233, 2009).
  • Adoptive immunotransfer of BKV specific T-cell being explored to treat hemorrhagic cystitis (Euro J Haematology 98:632, 2017). 
  • Levofloxacin (and likely other fluoroquinolones) are ineffective and preventing or treating infection (JAMA 312; 2019, 2014)
