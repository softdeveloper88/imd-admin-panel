Dapsone
Tradename: Aczone

Usage and Dosing

  • Sulfonamide-based drug (sulfone) used in patients with, or for prevention of Pneumocystis infection.
  • Used as part of combination therapy for leprosy.
  • Alternative for toxoplasmosis prophylaxis (with Pyrimethamine and folinic acid)
  • Most widely used for many non-infectious dermatologic conditions.
  • Major concerns are hemolytic anemia, with & without, G6PD deficiency and methemoglobinemia (N Engl J Med 364:957, 2011).
  • Dapsone hypersensitivity syndrome occurs in 0.5-3.6% of exposed patients with mortality of 9.9%. Pathogenesis unclear.
    • Syndrome: fever, rash, eosinophilia, lymphadenopathy, hepatitis, pneumonitis (multiorgan dysfunction). Stop the drug and give steroids: Clin Exp Dermatol 28:496, 2003.
    • Genetic marker found that is strongly associated with the syndrome: HLA-B*13.01 allele.  Ref: N Engl J Med 369:1620, 2013.
    • Found in 2-20% of Chinese, 1.5% of Japanese, and 2-4% of Southeast Asians; absent in Europeans and Africans.
  • Caution: Numerous drug interactions (see Major Drug Interactions chart below, not all-inclusive).

Adult Dose

  • Pneumocystis pneumonia (PJP)
    • Prophylaxis alternative; not a preferred drug:
      • (Dapsone 200 mg plus pyrimethamine 75 mg plus leucovorin 25 mg) po weekly
    • Treatment for not acutely ill patients: (Dapsone 100 mg po q24h + Trimethoprim 5 mg/kg po tid) x 21 days
  • Leprosy: combination drug regimens vary with specific type of leprosy, see Mycobacterium leprae.
  • Brown recluse spider bite: Dapsone often used despite marginal supportive data.

Adverse Effects

  • Causes dose-dependent hemolysis of no consequence at usual doses.
  • Hemolysis greatly enhanced in G6PD-deficient patients, test before use.
  • GI irritation: anorexia, nausea, vomiting
  • Photosensitivity (confirmed by rechallenge)
  • Dapsone metabolite is common cause of methemoglobinemia. Metabolite converts heme iron from normal +2 to non-oxygen carrying +3. Result is hemolysis and progressive symptoms of hypoxia. Process accentuated if patient is G6PD deficient. Normal blood levels of methemoglobin 1%; cyanosis occurs at 10%; headache, fatigue, tachycardia, dizziness at 30-40%; acidosis and coma at 60%; death at 70-80%. Treatment: methylene blue 1-2 mg/kg IV over 5 minutes–1 dose only.
  • Acute poisoning with ingestion of more than 1.5 gm: hemolysis, methemoglobinemia, jaundice, and coma.
