Post-Liver Transplant Biloma

Clinical Setting

  • Bilomas are infected intrahepatic or parahepatic bilious collections. Post-transplant, they occur as a complication of biliary tree injury (ischemia) in the setting of hepatic artery thrombosis or biliary obstruction.
  • Clinical symptoms include fever, abdominal pain, elevated liver function tests.

Etiologies

  • Enterococcus sp. (commonly VRE)
  • Candida sp.
  • Gram-negative bacilli (including P. aeruginosa)
  • Anaerobes

Primary Regimens

  • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h + (Linezolid 600 mg IV/po bid + Fluconazole 400 mg IV q24h)

Alternative Regimens

  • Levofloxacin 750 mg IV q24h + Daptomycin 10 mg/kg IV once daily + Micafungin 100 mg IV q24h

Comments

  • Definitive therapy should be based on culture results
  • Duration therapy should be based on resolution of infection based on repeat imaging
  • Patients with hepatic artery thrombosis often have recurrent bilomas and may ultimately require retransplantation.
  • Review article:  Clin Transplant 33; e13595, 2019
