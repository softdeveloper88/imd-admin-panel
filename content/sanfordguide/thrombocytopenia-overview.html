DDx: Thrombocytopenia, HIV Patient

Clinical Syndrome

  • Thrombocytopenia in HIV patient. Platelet count less than 150,000 per microliter of blood; severe thrombocytopenia defined as < 50,000 platelets per microliter
  • Can develop at any stage of HIV infection; may evolve during immune reconstitution.
  • Result of antiplatelet antibodies.
  • Risk of serious hemorrhage if platelet count falls below 20,000/μL.
  • Severe thrombocytopenia defines as < 50,000 platelets per microliter

Differential Diagnosis

  • In HIV/AIDS patients:
    • Reduction in platelet life span. Mechanism is antiplatelet antibodies in serum and on platelet surface.
    • Increased splenic uptake: coated platelets removed by RES macrophages.
    • Decrease in platelet production. HIV can infect megakaryocytes; associated with increased apoptosis.
    • Primary HIV-associated thrombocytopenia (PHAT):
  • Secondary thrombocytopenia:
    • Amphotericin
    • Dapsone
    • 5-Flucytosine
    • Ganciclovir
    • Ketoconazole
    • Pentamidine
    • Pyrimethamine
    • Pyrazinamide
    • Rifabutin
    • Rifampin
    • TMP-SMX
    • Valganciclovir
    • Zidovudine
    • Differential diagnosis includes thrombotic thrombocytopenic purpura, disseminiated intravascular coagulation, parvovirus, malignancy, opportunistic infections, co-morbid disease (e.g., hepatic cirrhosis) and drug toxicity.

Treatment

  • Consultation with a hematologist
  • Treatment of choice is antiretroviral therapy (ART); see  J Acquir Immune Defic Syndr 67:493, 2014.
  • For more rapid control: IVIG 1 gm/kg IV with repeat dosing if platelet count remains less thatn 50,000/μL. Duration of response is 3-4 weeks.
  • Alternative to IVIG: Anti-Rh (D) Immune globulin (Anti-Rh D immunoglobulin), intravenous (human) (WinRho). Only for Rh-positive patients who are NOT splenectomized. Can cause mild hemolysis. Works in some patients who do not respond to IVIG. Dose: 50-75 μg/kg IV. May repeat every 3 weeks if needed.
  • Less attractive alternatives: Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day for 7-14 days, thentaper; danazol 400-800 mg po once daily; alpha-interferon 3 million units sc 3 times weekly.
  • Avoid splenectomy if possible.
