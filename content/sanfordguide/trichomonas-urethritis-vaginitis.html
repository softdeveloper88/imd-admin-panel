Trichomonas: Vaginitis, Urethritis

Clinical Setting

  • Copious foamy vaginal discharge, pH >4.5, purulent, malodorous.
  • In women: burning, dysuria, pruritus, dyspareunia; post-coital bleeding.
  • In men: symptoms of urethritis, epididymitis or prostatitis (low prevalence in MSM) [Clin Infect Dis 73:1119, 2021].
  • Most infected people are asymptomatic.
  • Associated with poor birth outcomes
  • Evaluate and treat sexual partners.
  • Trend is to test appropriate specimen by NAAT for 3 pathogens: Trichomonas, Chlamydia and Gonorrhea. Using NAAT, prevalence of Trichomonas in a high risk population was 27% in women and 9.8% in men: Clin Infect Dis 59:834, 2014.
  • CDC 2021 STD Treatment Guidelines: MMWR Recomm Rep 70:1, 2021
  • Review of evidence for CDC guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 74:S152 2022

Etiologies

  • Trichomonas vaginalis

Diagnosis

  • Nucleic acid amplification test (NAAT) widely available, replacing wet mount microscopy (3-5 times less sensitive for Trichomonas). 
  • Culture also available in some centers. 
  • Samples from anatomic females include clinician-collected endocervical swabs, clinician-collected vaginal swabs, female urine specimens, and liquid Pap smear specimens. 
  • Reliability/FDA approval of tests for male urine or urethral samples depends upon test. 
  • Antigen tests also available, may be less sensitive than NAAT.
  • Culture is the preferred diagnostic test in treatment failures as can assess resistance. 
  • Kit available from CDC to perform metronidazole drug-resistance testing (https://www.cdc.gov/laboratory/specimen-submission/detail.html?CDCTestCode=CDC-10239) or contact CDC +1 404-718-4141.

Primary Regimens

  • Metronidazole 500 mg po bid x 7 days (for women) or 2 gm x 1 dose (for men)
    • If HIV Positive:
      • Metronidazole x 7 days
      • Screen all HIV+ women once a year
    • Treat all sexual partners exposed during the last 60 days: Metronidazole 2 gm as single dose, can give expedited partner treatment if partner not readily available
    • Failures usually due to non-adherence to medication, partner therapy, or intercourse restrictions
    • Use of Metronidazole during pregnancy deemed safe (Pharmacotherapy 2015;35:1052). 
    • RCT supports 7 day regimen over single dose in all women (Lancet Infect Dis 18:1251, 2018). 
    • Optimal regimen in men unclear, so current recommendations are for single dose therapy. 
    • For discussion of treating Trichomonas, including issues in pregnancy, see CDC 2021 STD Guidelines, MMWR Recomm Rep 70:1, 2021

Alternative Regimens

  • Tinidazole 2 gm po x 1 dose
  • Treatment failure:
    • 1st failure: Re-treat with Metronidazole 500 mg po bid x 7 days
    • If 2nd failure: Metronidazole or Tinidazole 2 gm po q24h x 7 days
    • If 3rd failure: Tinidazole 2 gm po q24h x 7 days
  • Continued failure: suggest ID consultation and/or contact U.S. CDC: (+1) 770-488-4115
  • Secnidazole in women effective in RCT [Clin Inf Dis 73:e1282, 2021]; can also be used to treat partners. 2 gm packet (granules) once orally on applesauce, yogurt or pudding.
  • For Drug Resistant organisms:
    • Metronidazole or Tinidazole 2 gm po q24h x 7 days
    • If high dose fails, consider:
      • High-dose oral tinidazole 2 g daily plus intravaginal tinidazole 500 mg 2 times/day for 14 days.
      • High-dose oral tinidazole (1 g 3 times/day) plus intravaginal paromomycin (4 g of 6.25% intravaginal paromomycin cream nightly) for 14 days.

Comments

  • Testing for other STIs, including HIV, syphilis, gonorrhea, and chlamydia, should be performed for persons with T. vaginalis.
  • Symptomatic infection: No intercourse for 1 week after both partners have completed treatment and are asymptomatic
  • Asymptomatic infection:  No intercourse for 1 week after both partners have completed treatment.
  • Single dose Tinidazole is better tolerated than Metronidazole.
  • All currently approved medications for treatment are 5-nitroimidazoles. In cases of hypersensitivity, metronidazole desensitization [Sex Trans Dis 48:e111, 2021] should be performed.
  • Avoid metronidazole gel, poor efficacy.
  • Due to high rate of reinfection in women, retesting recommended within 3 months per CDC guidelines MMWR Recomm Rep 70:1, 2021, with many experts suggesting test of cure (TOC) particularly for those with a prior history of T. vaginalis or women given single dose metronadizole treatment [Sex Trans Dis 2021 doi: 10.1097/OLQ.0000000000001574]. Data for retesting are less clear for men.
  • No need to screen asymptomatic women for Trichomonas if HIV negative; Screen all HIV+ women annually
  • Metronidazole and tinidazole do not induce acetaldehyde dehydrogenase and data to support disulfiram reactions with alcohol consumption are not strong Antimicrob Agents Chemother 64:e02167 2020
  • 2021 CDC STI guidelines no longer recommend avoidance of alcohol when receiving metronidazole or tinidazole for treatment of Trichomonas or bacterial vaginosis Clin Inf Dis 74:S152 2022
  • For alternative options in refractory cases, see Clin Infect Dis 53(S3):S160, 2011, MMWR Recomm Rep 70:1, 2021
  • Review of Trichomonas in men: Clin Infect Dis 73:1119, 2021
  • RCT of Secnidazole in women [Clin Inf Dis 73:e1282, 2021]: cure rate of 92.2% for treated patients with trichomoniasis (n=64) as compared to placebo (p<0.001) (n=67)
  • Partners can also be treated with Secnidazole. 
  • Pregnancy:
    • There is no data indicating that Metronidazole is teratogenic or mutagenic in any trimester (MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021, Current Drug Safety 2015;10:170).
    • Avoid Tinidizole and Secnidazole as not enough data.
    • Breastfeeding should be deferred for 72 hours after a single 2-g oral dose of Tinidazole
