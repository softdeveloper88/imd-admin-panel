Candidiasis, Esophageal

Clinical Setting

  • Encountered primarily in AIDS patients.
  • Dysphagia or odynophagia plus oropharyngeal candidiasis is predictive of esophageal candidiasis.
  • A diagnostic trial of antifungal therapy is appropriate before performing an endoscopic examination.

Etiologies

  • Candida albicans most common but other Candida sp. can cause disease.

Primary Regimens

  • Fluconazole 200-400 (3-6 mg/kg) mg po/IV daily x 14-21 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Caspofungin 50 mg/day IV or Micafungin 150 mg/day IV or Anidulafungin 100 mg IV on day 1, then 50 mg/day IV
  • Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg/day x 14-21 days
  • Amphotericin B 0.5 mg/kg/day x 14-21 days
  • Itraconazole oral solution 200 mg/day x 14-21 days
  • Voriconazole 200 mg q12h x 14-21 days
  • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs)
    • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily OR
    • Suspension 200 mg qid, then 400 mg po bid after stabilization of disease OR
    • Posaconazole IV 300 mg IV over 90 minutes bid x 1 day, then 300 mg IV daily
  • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily

Comments

  • For Fluconazole-refractory disease, Itraconazole (80% will respond), Posaconazole, Voriconazole, echinocandins, or an Amphotericin B product can be used.
  • Echinocandins associated with higher relapse rate than Fluconazole.
  • Antiretroviral therapy (ART) for underlying HIV is recommended.
  • For AIDS patients with recurrent esophageal candidiasis, suppressive Fluconazole 200 mg 3 x weekly was effective (Clin Infect Dis 41:1473, 2005); discontinue once CD4 > 200/mm3.
  • See Clin Infect Dis 62:409, 2016 for current guidelines.
