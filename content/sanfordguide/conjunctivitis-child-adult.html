Conjunctivitis, Inclusion

Clinical Setting

  • Conjunctivitis in children or adults, may be unilateral or bilateral.
  • Prevalent in resource-poor settings (Indian J Ophthalmol 2017; 65:97-102).
  • Indolent clinical course with conjunctivitis unresponsive to topical therapy.
  • High prevalence of oculogenital disease in adults.

Etiologies

  • Chlamydia trachomatis

Diagnosis

  • Diagnosis by nucleic acid amplification test (NAAT).
  • Also test urine by NAAT for both Chlamydia and gonorrhea.

Primary Regimens

  • Azithromycin 1 gm once 

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg bid po x 7 days

Comments

  • Reference: JAMA 310:1721, 2013.
  • Azithromycin (preferred) and Doxycycline yield similar cure rates (Am J Ophthalmol 135:447, 2003); repeat dosing may be necessary (Eye 24:985, 2010).
  • Treat sexual partner.
