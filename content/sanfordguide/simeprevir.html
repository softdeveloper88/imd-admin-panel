Simeprevir
Tradename: Olysio

Usage and Dosing

  • Protease inhibitor for treatment of Genotype 1 Hepatitis C infection in combination with other direct acting agents (DAAs)
  • Need to screen patients with genotype 1a for the Q80K polymorphism; if present consider alternative therapy.
  • Contraindicated in pregnant women and in men whose female partners are pregnant. Simeprevir is pregnancy risk category C; concern is the combination with ribavirin which is pregnancy risk category X.
  • Test for evidence of current or prior Hepatitis B (HBV) infection: measure HBsAG and anti-HBC before initiating HCV treatment. If HBV co-infection, monitor patient for HBV reactivation during and post-HCV therapy.
  • No longer marketed in US; may be available in other areas (Nov 2020)

Adult Dose

  • Usual Dose: one 150 mg capsule po once daily with food administered with other DAA (typically Sofosbuvir, with or without Ribavirin)

Adverse Effects

  • Most common (in combination with interferon and ribavirin): rash, pruritus, nausea
  • Black Box Warning: Cases of HBV reactivation, occasionally fulminant, during or after DAA therapy have been reported in HBV/HCV coninfected patients who were not already on HBV suppressive therapy
