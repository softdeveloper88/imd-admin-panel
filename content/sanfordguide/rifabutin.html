Rifabutin
Tradename: Mycobutin

Usage and Dosing

  • Rifabutin is a rifamycin anti-tuberculous agent used primarily to treat M. avium infection and as a replacement for Rifampin in treatment of tuberculosis
    • Drug interaction profile better than with Rifampin and often used instead
    • Drug interactions are, however, also numerous with Rifabutin
    • Also has activity against:
      • M. bovis
      • M. kansasii
      • M. marinum
      • M. ulcerans
  • As adjunctive therapy for resistant Acinetobacter: Pharmacology, dosing, side effects (Open Forum Infect Dis 2020, doi:10.1093/ofid/ofaa460)
  • Comprehensive review: J Antimicrob Chemother 71:1759, 2016.
  • See also Rifampin.

Adult Dose

  • 300 mg/day

Hepatic Adjustment

Mild impairment (Child-Pugh Class A) No dosage adjustment
Moderate impairment (Child-Pugh Class B) No dosage adjustment
Severe impairment (Child-Pugh Class C) Consider 150 mg daily

Adverse Effects

  • Polymyalgia, polyarthralgia, granulocytopenia, thrombocytopenia
  • Anterior uveitis rare (< 0.01%) when given alone at 300 mg dose; increases to 8-38% if  concomitant antifungal azole, clarithromycin or protease inhibitors (J Antimicrob Chemother 71:1759, 2016)
  • Reddish urine, orange skin (pseudojaundice).
