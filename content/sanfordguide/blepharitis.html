Blepharitis

Clinical Setting

  • Bacterial infection associated with inflammation of the eyelid or eyelash follicles.
  • Clinical diagnosis with flaking and crusting involving the lashes.
  • Complication of seborrhoeic dermatitis (Practitioner 258:23, 2014).

Etiologies

  • Etiologies are unclear, but may include:
    • Staphylococcus aureus
    • Staphylococcus epidermidis
    • Seborrhea
    • Rosacea
    • Dry eye

Primary Regimens

  • Eyelid hygiene and lid margin care with baby shampoo and warm compresses q24h
  • Artificial tears if associated dry eye.

Alternative Regimens

  •  Topical antibiotics (e.g., topical ophthalmic bacitracin ointment once daily to eyelid margin) may provide symptomatic relief but otherwise of uncertain benefit (Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2017 Feb 7;2:CD011965); consider use of topical antibiotics if there is no improvement with above measures.

Comments

  • Reference on medical management: Curr Opin Ophthalmol 26:289, 2015. 
  • Cochrane review of effectiveness of various interventions: Cochrane Database Syst Rev. 2012 May 16;5:CD005556.
