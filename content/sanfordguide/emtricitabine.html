Emtricitabine
Tradename: Emtriva

Usage and Dosing

  • Emtricitabine (FTC) is indicated as part of a nucleoside backbone for a 3-drug regimen. Usually paired with Tenofovir as part of a fixed dose combination. Can be used with other nucleoside agents (e.g., Abacavir, Zidovudine); however, should not be used with Lamivudine (3TC).
  • Differs only slightly in structure from Lamivudine (5-fluoro substitution).
  • Emtricitabine is well tolerated; headache, nausea, vomiting & diarrhea result occasionally, skin rash rarely. Skin hyperpigmentation.
  • Black Box Warning: Exacerbation of hepatitis B reported in patients after stopping FTC. Monitor at least several months after stopping FTC in Hep B patients; some may need anti-Hepatitis B therapy.
  • Do not use as monotherapy for Hep B owing to development of resistance.
  • For class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.
  • Combination formulations:
    • Truvada (Emtricitabine +Tenofovir disoproxil)
    • Atripla (Emtricitabine + Tenofovir disoproxil + Efavirenz)
    • Complera/Eviplera (Emtricitabine + Tenofovir + Rilpivirine)
    • Stribild (Emtricitabine + Tenofovir + Elvitegravir + Cobicistat)
    • Genvoya (Emtricitabine + Elvitegravir + Cobicistat + Tenofovir alfenamide)
    • Bictarvy (Emtricitabine + Bictegravir + Tenofovir alafenamide)

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Potential for lactic acidosis (as with other NRTIs). Also Black Box warning (see Usage & Dosing).
    • Potential for immune reconstitution syndrome (IRIS).
  • Most Common:
    • Well tolerated. Headache, diarrhea, nausea, rash, skin hyperpigmentation.
  • See ARV Drugs, Overview for class-wide adverse effects.
