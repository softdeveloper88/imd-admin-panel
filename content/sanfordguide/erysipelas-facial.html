Cellulitis, Erysipelas: Facial

Clinical Setting

  • Sudden onset of rapidly spreading red edematous tender plaque-like skin on the face in an otherwise healthy host.
  • Staph. aureus erysipelas of the face can mimic streptococcal erysipelas of an extremity.

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus sp. (Groups A, B, C, G)
  • Staph. aureus

Primary Regimens

  • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative target is trough of 15-20 μg/mL

Alternative Regimens

  • Daptomycin 4 mg/kg IV q24h
  • Linezolid 600 mg po/IV q12h

Comments

  • If erysipelas-like on the face, must treat as if MRSA is present.
  • Usual duration of treatment is 7-10 days, longer if patient is bacteremic.
  • Very rarely, Streptococcus pneumoniae can cause a similar picture (would be covered by treatments recommended above); in children, Haemophilus influenzae can cause an erysipelas-like facial cellulitis (requires gram-negative coverage).
  • For full differential diagnosis of a red face (Clin Dermatol. 1993; 11:307).
