Dilated cardiomyopathy

Pathophysiology
  • Myocardial contractile dysfunction → ventricular dilation
  • Heart failure & myocardial fibrosis can eventually develop*
  • May be preceded by myocarditis (myocardial inflammation)

Notable causes
  • Infection (eg, viral, Chagas disease)
  • Toxins (eg, alcohol, cocaine)
  • Medications (eg, anthracyclines, anti-HER2 agents)
  • Inherited (eg, classic familial, Duchenne muscular dystrophy)
  • Rheumatologic/infiltrative (eg, sarcoidosis, amyloidosis)
  • Nutritional deficiency (eg, thiamine, selenium)
  • Other (eg, idiopathic, peripartum, tachycardia induced)

*Cardiomyopathy may be reversible if disease process is halted before substantial fibrosis develops.
