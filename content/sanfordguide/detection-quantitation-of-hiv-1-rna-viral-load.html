Detection & Quantitation of HIV-1 RNA, Viral Load

Viral Load Testing

  • HIV-1 RNA quantitation is useful as:
    • A way of monitoring the effectiveness of anti-retroviral therapy
    • A way to  establish diagnosis of primary (acute) HIV infection.
  • There are 3 FDA-approved commonly used real-time HIV-1 RNA PCR (RT-PCR) assays to quantify HIV RNA copies/mL of plasma.
    • All use between 0.5 and 1.0 ml of  plasma (not serum) and are >98% sensitive. 
    • EDTA is the recommended anticoagulant.  Heparin is an unacceptable anticoagulant. Separate plasma within 6 hours; store at -20∘C.
  • It is important to use the same assay when following HIV RNA levels over time. If the assay method changes, a new baseline must be established.

Interpretation of Results

  • Need change of ≥50% (3-fold or 0.5 log10 copies/mL) to be significant.

Factors That Increase Viral Load

  • Progressive uncontrolled HIV infection due to non-adherence with ART or ineffective regimen.
  • Active non-HIV infection, e.g., tuberculosis (5-160 fold ↑), pneumococcal pneumonia (3-5 fold increase) and other acute illnesses.
  • Immunization, e.g., influenza, pneumococcal (return to baseline in 1-2 months).

Falsely Low Viral Loads

  • Falsely low viral loads may indicate:
    • Non-B subtype of HIV-1 not detected by assay used.
    • HIV-2 infection.
