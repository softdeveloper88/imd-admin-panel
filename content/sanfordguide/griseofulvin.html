Griseofulvin
Tradename: Fulvicin, Grifulvin, Gristacin

Usage and Dosing

  • Griseofulvin is used for treatment of tinea capitis, cruris, corporis, pedis, and unguium (onychomycosis).
  • Increases blood and urine porphyrins, should not be used in patients with porphyria.
  • Avoid in pregnancy.

Adult Dose

  • 500-1000 mg (microsize formulation) in a single daily dose, or divided bid.
  • Use higher dose for tinea pedis and onychomycosis.

Hepatic Adjustment

Griseofulvin is contraindicated in hepatic failure

Mild impairment (Child-Pugh Class A) Use not recommended
Moderate impairment (Child-Pugh Class B) Use not recommended
Severe impairment (Child-Pugh Class C) Use not recommended

Adverse Effects

  • Photosensitivity, urticaria.
  • GI upset.
  • Fatigue.
  • Leukopenia (rare).
  • Increases blood and urine porphyrins, should not be used in patients with porphyria.
  • Minor disulfiram-like reactions.
  • Exacerbation of systemic lupus erythematosus.
