Flucytosine
Tradename: Ancobon

Usage and Dosing

  • Flucytosine has activity against Candida sp., Cryptococcus sp., and dematiaceous molds, but is primarily used in combination therapy for the treatment of cryptococcal meningitis.
  • Use with caution in patients with renal impairment or receiving concomitant nephrotoxic agents (e.g., Amphotericin B).
  • Monitor renal function and CBC.
  • Measure serum concentrations in patients with renal impairment. Goal is peak serum concentration of 25-100 μg/mL.
  • Expensive

Adult Dose

  • 50-100 mg/kg/day po in 4 divided doses

Adverse Effects

  • Overall 30%.
  • Gastrointestinal (6%): diarrhea, anorexia, nausea, vomiting.
  • Hematologic (22%): leukopenia and/or thrombocytopenia, when serum level >100 μg/mL (esp. in azotemic patients). Aplastic anemia (rare, just 2 or 3 cases).
  • Hepatotoxicity (asymptomatic ↑SGOT, reversible)
  • Photosensitivity
  • Skin rash (7%)
  • False increase in serum creatinine on EKTACHEM analyzer.
