Pneumonia, Haemophilus influenzae

Clinical Setting

  • Common cause of community-acquired bacterial pneumonia (CABP).
  • Isolation of H. influenzae from blood culture, pleural fluid, or as the predominate organism from sputum.
  • Adults account for most infections in the US; most strains non-typeable.
  • Strains are often β-lactamase positive.

Etiologies

  • Haemophilus influenzae

Primary Regimens

  • Empiric therapy should cover for β-lactamase positive strains until susceptibility is confirmed.
  • Confirmed β-lactamase positive:
    • Ceftriaxone 1 gm q24h IV
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po bid
  • Confirmed β-lactamase negative:
    • Ampicillin 2 gm IV q6h
    • Amoxicillin 1 gm po q8h
  • See Comments for duration of therapy

Alternative Regimens

  • TMP-SMX 5-10 mg/kg of TMP component q12h IV/po
  • Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q12h or 750 mg po bid
  • Levofloxacin 750 mg po/IV q24h
  • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid
  • Azithromycin 500 mg x1 on day 1, then 250 mg once daily x 4 days
  • See Antimicrobial Stewardship for duration of therapy

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy: 5-7 days  (N Engl J Med 370:543, 2014)
  • Patient can be switched to po therapy when improving clinically and able to take oral medications.
  • Antibiotics can be safely discontinued after 5 days in patients who are afebrile for 48 hours or more and have no more than one of the following (JAMA Intern Med 2016;176:1257):
    • Systolic BP < 90 mm Hg
    • Heart rate > 100/min
    • Respiratory rate > 24/min
    • Arterial oxygen saturation < 90% or room-air PaO2 less than 60 mm Hg
