Cryptococcosis, Meningitis, Non-HIV

Clinical Setting

  • Chronic meningitis with headache, visual changes, cranial nerve or focal neurological deficits in immunocompetent patient.
  • CSF lymphocytic pleocytosis, high protein, and/or low glucose.
  • Positive cryptococcal antigen in CSF or serum or positive culture.
  • Increasing use of PCR for diagnosis
    • In CSF and in patients with low numbers of cryptococci, PCR may be negative but antigen may remain positive
    • False negative detection of Crypto with multiplex PCR meningitis panel
    • References: Diagnostic Micro and Infect Dis 2016; 84:268; Medical Mycology 2020; 58:408; Clin Infect Dis 2019; 68:2094

Etiologies

  • Cryptococcus neoformans
  • Cryptococcus gattii (less common)

Primary Regimens

  • Induction phase: (Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-4 mg/kg IV q24h or Amphotericin B lipid complex 5 mg/kg IV q24h) + Flucytosine 25 mg/kg po q6h
    • Duration: Minimum 2 weeks for transplant recipients and minimum 4 weeks in non-immunocompromised patients. Treat until patient is afebrile and cultures are negative.
    • Need to monitor intracranial pressure; If over 25 cm, need to remove CSF.  See Comments.
  • Consolidation phase: Fluconazole 400-800 mg po daily for 8 weeks
  • Maintenance phase:  Fluconazole 200 mg po daily for 6-12 months

Alternative Regimens

  • Induction (duration is regimen specific):
    • (Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-4 mg/kg IV q24h or Amphotericin B lipid complex 5 mg/kg IV q24h or Amphotericin B 0.7-1 mg/kg IV q24h) + Fluconazole 800-1200 mg/day IV/po x 2 weeks (consolidation therapy should be with Fluconazole 800 mg po q24h)
    • Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-4 mg/kg IV q24h or Amphotericin B lipid complex 5 mg/kg IV q24h or Amphotericin B 0.7-1 mg/kg IV q24h x 4-6 weeks
    • Fluconazole 800-1200 mg (higher dose preferred)/day IV/po + Flucytosine 25 mg/kg po q6h x 4–6 weeks
    • Fluconazole 1200-2000 mg po daily x 10-12 weeks
    • Regardless of regimen, remove CSF for spinal fluid pressure over 25 cm. See comment.

Comments

  • IDSA Guidelines (2010): Clin Infect Dis 50:291, 2010.
  • Consider extending induction phase to 6 weeks in patients with neurological deficits.
  • Insufficient evidence to recommend an oral Fluconazole-based regimen for induction phase.
  • If CSF opening pressure >25 cm H2O, repeat LP to drain fluid to control pressure.
  • Duration of therapy in solid organ transplant (SOT) recipients is 6-12 months. IRIS occurs in 5-12% of SOT recipients and occurs often 4-6 weeks after diagnosis (Clin Transplant 33:e13543, 2019)
  • Outbreaks of C. gattii meningitis have been reported in the U.S. Pacific Northwest (Emerg Infect Dis 13:42, 2007):
    • Severity of disease and prognosis appear to be worse than with C. neoformans with high rates of increased intracranial pressure, hydrocephalus, and neurological sequelae (Clin Infect Dis 55:789, 2012).
    • Initial therapy with Amphotericin B + Flucytosine recommended.
    • C. gattii less susceptible to Fluconazole than C. neoformans (Clin Microbiol Infect 14:727, 2008).
  • Outcomes in both AIDS and non-AIDS cryptococcal meningitis improved with Amphotericin B + Flucytosine induction therapy for 14 days in those with neurological abnormalities or high organism burden (PLoS ONE 3:e2870, 2008).
