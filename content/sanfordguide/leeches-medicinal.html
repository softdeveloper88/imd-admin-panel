Wound, Leeches, Medicinal

Clinical Setting

  • Complication of medicinal use of leeches to debride wounds.
  • Wound infection from Aeromonas hydrophila found in the intestinal tract of leeches.

Etiologies

  • Aeromonas hydrophila

Primary Regimens

  • Ciprofloxacin (750 mg po or 400 mg IV) bid OR
  • Parenteral cephalosporin (3rd Gen)
    • Choice of alternative agents based on susceptibility testing
    • Aeromonas sp. universally resistant to ampicillin, penicillin and cefazolin

Alternative Regimens

  • TMP-SMX DS 1 tab po bid

Comments

  • Some use prophylactic antibiotics when leeches are used medicinally, but not universally accepted as necessary. Some base this on periodic microbiologic sampling of leech tank fluid (Eur J Clin Microbiol Infect Dis 35:1001, 2016)
  • Cellulitis due to Ciprofloxacin-resistant Aeromonas hydrophila from medicinal leech use has been described, J Clin Microbiol 51:1324, 2013.
  • Other organisms such as Vibrio spp. have been encountered, Clin Infect Dis 22:709, 1996.
  • Other reference, Clin Infect Dis 35:E1, 2002.
