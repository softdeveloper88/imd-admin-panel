Osteomyelitis, Hemoglobinopathy

Clinical Setting

  • Sickle cell disease or thalassemia.
  • Gram-negative pathogen isolated (see Comments).
  • Recommendations for primary and alternative regimens are for susceptible gram-negative pathogens
  • See also: Osteomyelitis, Overview

Etiologies

  • Salmonella sp.
  • Other gram-negative bacilli
  • Staph. aureus and other gram-positive cocci

Primary Regimens

  • Empiric therapy:
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) + (Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q12h OR Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h)

Alternative Regimens

  • Oral step-down therapy for susceptible gram-negatives:
    • Ciprofloxacin 750 mg po bid
    • Levofloxacin 750 mg po q24h

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Adjust and de-escalate based on results of culture and susceptibility testing.
  • Duration of therapy not well established for this specific entity, but treat for 6 to 8 weeks of therapy as in other cases of osteomyelitis.

Comments

  • Thalassemia: transfusion and iron chelation risk factors.
  • Evidence-based recommendations are lacking  (Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2016 Nov 14;11:CD007175). 
  • Salmonella isolates may exhibit decreased susceptibility to fluoroquinolones (Clin Infect Dis 50:241, 2010).
  • Staph. aureus also a common pathogen (J Natl Med Assoc 96:224, 2004) and should be covered empirically pending culture results.
