Ascariasis

Clinical Setting

  • Usually asymptomatic
    • Rare intestinal obstruction in children.
    • Rare biliary obstruction due to migrating adult worm.
    • Eosinophilic pulmonary infiltrates during initial migration of immature larvae
  • Oral or nasopharyngeal regurgitation of large adult roundworms in young children

Etiologies

  • Ascaris lumbricoides

Diagnosis

  • Diagnosis:  large eggs easily seen in unconcentrated stool specimens.
  • Imaging:  larges intestinal worms may be obvious on plain abdominal films or CT with or without oral contrast.
    • if biliary obstruction ERCP may be both diagnostic and therapeutic

Primary Regimens

  • Albendazole 400 mg po x 1 dose
    • Co-infection with hookworm and trichuris common; if co-infection known or possible use Albendazole 400 mg po x 3 days
    • Pediatrics:
      • WHO allows Albendazole use in children 1 year of age and older and this is acknowledged as safe in CDC materials.
      • CDC recommends children aged > 2 years: 400 mg po once only;  1-2 yrs of age 200 mg po once only
      • Do not use Albendazole in children <1 yr of age
    • Pregnancy:
      • WHO allows use of single-dose Albendazole in 2nd and 3rd trimesters.
      • Remains FDA pregnancy risk category C.
      • Large systematic analyses show no harm in 1st trimester Albendazole (Intl J Parasitol 2019 Jun;49(7):541)
  • Mebendazole 100 mg po bid x 3 days for all persons >1 yr of age
  • Mebendazole 500 mg po x 1 dose for all persons >1 yr of age (500 mg tablets not sold in the US)

Alternative Regimens

  • Ivermectin 150–200 μg/kg po x 1 dose
  • Pyrantel pamoate 11 mg/kg base (to maximum dose of 1 gm) po x1 dose (OTC in US). Ref for single-dose regimen: BMJ 2017;358:j4307.
    • Pregnancy: safe in pregnancy, highly effective but no effect against trichiuris or hookworm.

    Comments

    • Albendazole preferred over Mebendazole for hookworm; Mebendazole preferred over Albendazole for trichuris
    • Vermox brand Mebendazole 500 mg tablet is FDA-approved but for donation only and not sold in the U.S.
      • Recent data on efficacy of single-dose 500 mg chewable pediatric tablet as used in mass treatment campaigns against soil-transmitted helminths (Am J Trop Med Hyg 97:1851, 2017).
    • Albendazole 200 mg tablets  available from Expert Compounding Pharmacy (see Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources).  Not FDA- approved for ascariasis
    • Generic Albendazole (Albenza) marketed by Amneal is very costly and is difficult for retail pharmacies to access.  
    • Pyrantel over the counter so convenient and similar efficacy to others.
    • Nitazoxanide effective but less active than other options.
    • Efficacy of multi-dose Albendazole for hookworm especially: PLoS One 2011;6(9):e25003.; JAMA 299:1937, 2008.
