Endocarditis, Chlamydial

Clinical Setting

  • Specific therapy for culture-negative endocarditis with positive serologies for chlamydial infection and/or positive PCR at valve surgery.
  • Relatively rare compared to other etiologies.
  • History of bird exposure usually present.

Etiologies

  • Chlamydophila psittaci
  • Chlamydophila pneumoniae (J Clin Microbiol 40:718, 2002)

Primary Regimens

  • Medical and surgical therapies for this infection are not well defined; several reported cases have had valve surgery.
  • Chlamydia are susceptible to tetracyclines and macrolides and these agents have been used; rifampin has been added in some cases.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • In some serological assays, cross-reactivity may misidentify Bartonella as Chlamydia (Clin Microbiol Rev 14:177, 2001).
  • Prolonged course of therapy (months to years), which may be oral but exact duration not well-defined.
  • Reference: Infect Dis Clin North Am 24:7, 2010.
