Cytomegalovirus, Congenital

Clinical Setting

  • Congenital cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection.
  • Common congenital infection - most common cause of sensorineural hearing loss
  • On average 0.6% of newborns infected. Prevalence varies with population. About 40,000 per year in US.
  • About 90% of infected infants are asymptomatic at birth
  • Evaluation of children with PCR positive urine or saliva should include
    • Assessment of size for gestational age and head circumference
    • CNS imaging. MRI more sensitive than ultrasound
    • Eye exam by opthalmologist
    • Hearing screen and if abnormal, confirmatory testing
    • CBC and liver function tests
  • Clinical manifestations of symptomatic infants vary
    • Petechiae
    • Jaundice 
    • Hepatomegaly
    • Microcephaly
    • Sensorineural hearing loss
    • Lethargy/hypotonia
    • Poor suck
    • Chorioretinits
    • Seizures
    • Intracranial calcifications
    • Hemolytic anemia, neutropenia
    • Pneumonia
    • Less commonly enterocolitis, myocarditis, cardiomyopathy
  • Hearing loss may occur in up to 10-15% of asymptomatic infants; infants who fail newborn hearing screen should have confirmatory ABR and be tested for CMV

Diagnosis

  • Diagnosis by PCR or culture of urine or saliva. False positive saliva tests can occur because of breast milk.

Etiologies

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV)

Primary Regimens

  • Supportive measures as necessary including transfusion, fluid and nutritional support, oxygen and mechanical ventilation, seizure control
  • Valganciclovir 16 mg/kg/dose po q12h for 6 months for neonates with symptomatic congenital CMV (See Comments). Adjust for weight as child grows

Alternative Regimens

  • For severely ill infants and those unable to take po: Ganciclovir 6 mg/kg/dose IV q12h. Monitor CBC
  • Resistance can occur, but is rare. Alternative is Foscarnet

Comments

  • Based on randomized clinical trials (N Engl J Med. 2015 Mar 5;372(10):933-43), antiviral treatment for 6 months modestly improves hearing and neurodevelopmental outcomes for infants with symptomatic congenital CMV.
  • Cranial imaging, hearing evaluation, LFTs, CBC and renal function should be checked at baseline
  • Many monitor of serum CMV viral load at baseline, 2-3 months and at 6 months but role is unresolved. Monitor weekly initially in life threatening symptomatic disease to demonstrate response.
  • Study did not evaluate asymptomatic children with isolated hearing loss (one enrolled). Treatment with Valganciclovir as above can be considered after discussion of risks and unknown benefits with family
  • European consensus guidance recommends treatment of isolated sensorineural hearing loss (SNHL) but very limited data as of 2024 (Lancet Reg Health Eur1:40:100892 2024)
  • Long term followup for neurodevelopmental and hearing outcomes is important
  • Neutropenia, thrombocytopenia with valganciclovir and IV ganciclovir and line complications seen with IV ganciclovir. 
  • Less common with valganciclovir than IV ganciclovir but monitor CBC weekly at outset then monthly
