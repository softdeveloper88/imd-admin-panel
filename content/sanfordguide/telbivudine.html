Telbivudine
Tradename: Tyzeka,

Usage and Dosing

  • NOTE: THIS PRODUCT WAS DISCONTINUED IN 2016 AND IS NO LONGER AVAILABLE IN THE US.
  • Telbivudine is an oral nucleoside analog approved for treatment of Hepatitis B. It has better rates of response and superior viral suppression than Lamivudine monotherapy (N Engl J Med 357:2576, 2007).
  • Black Box warnings regarding lactic acidosis/hepatic steatosis with nucleosides and potential for severe exacerbation of Hepatitis B on dc. Generally well-tolerated with decreased mitochondrial toxicity vs other nucleosides and no dose limiting toxicity observed (Ann Pharmacother 40:472, 2006; Med Lett Drugs Ther 49:11, 2007).
  • Genotypic resistance rate was 4.4% by one year, ↑ to 21.5% by 2 years of therapy of eAg+ patients. Selects for YMDD mutation like Lamivudine. Combination with Lamivudine was inferior to monotherapy (Hepatology 45:507, 2007).

Adverse Effects

  • Generally well-tolerated.
  • Myalgias, myopathy and rhabdomyolysis reported. Peripheral neuropathy.
