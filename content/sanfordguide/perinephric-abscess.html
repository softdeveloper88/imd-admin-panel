Perinephric Abscess

Clinical Setting

  • Perinephric abscess associated with either hematogenous spread (bacteremia) or direct extension from ascending pyelonephritis

Etiologies

  • Staph. aureus
  • Enterobacteriaceae

Primary Regimens

  • Drainage of collection an antibacterial therapy should be directed against pathogen identified in blood, urine, and/or drainage of perinephric collection
  • Empiric therapy could be same as pyelonephritis or if S. aureus bacteremia depending on likely source

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Percutaneous catheter drainage preferred to surgical drainage; abscesses < 3 cm may respond to medical therapy alone.
