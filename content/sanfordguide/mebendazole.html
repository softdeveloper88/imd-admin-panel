Mebendazole
Tradename: Vermox

Usage and Dosing

  • Broad-spectrum against nematode helminths, primarily ascariasis and trichuriasis.
  • Mebendazole clearly superior for Trichuris (PLoS One. 6:e25003, 2011; N Engl J Med 370:610, 2014)
    • Use Albendazole if co-infection with hookworm has not been ruled out.
    • Albendazole much more effective for hookworm.  
  • Second line therapy for pinworm, angiostrongyliasis, toxocariasis, capillariasis, and trichinosis.

Adult Dose

  • In developing world: chewable tablet, 500 mg po once in public health campaigns.
  • 100 mg po twice/day for 3 days is optimal for most intestinal nematodes.
  • 100 mg single dose adequate for pinworm. 
  • Single dose therapy of either Albendazole or Mebendazole as used in public health programs has lower cure rates and is not acceptable for Trichuris.
  • FDA recommended single 500 mg dose for trichuriasis has 10-50% cure rate in some studies. Lancet Infect Dis 2015, 15:277.

Pediatric Dose

  • The safety of mebendazole in children is unclear. Limited data in children <2 years of age.

Adverse Effects

  • Rarely causes abdominal pain, nausea, diarrhea.

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Category C. Limited data in pregnancy. Evidence suggests no difference in congenital abnormalities in the children of women accidentally treated with mebendazole during MDA campaigns compared with those who were not. In MDA campaigns in countries where STH are common, WHO allows use in the 2nd and 3rd trimester. Balance the risk of treatment for the fetus with the risk of disease progression in the untreated woman.
Use during Lactation Minimally excreted in breast milk. WHO classifies mebendazole as safe during lactation.
