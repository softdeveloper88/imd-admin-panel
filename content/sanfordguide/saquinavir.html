Saquinavir
Tradename: Invirase

Usage and Dosing

  • Saquinavir (SQV) is indicated for use as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually combined with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents (e.g., TDF-FTC or ABC-3TC). Must be used with Ritonavir-boosting. Considered an alternative regimen for naive patients; used in treatment experienced patients when the resistance test indicates susceptibility to Saquinavir.
  • Saquinavir side effects include nausea, diarrhea, headache, ↑ AST/ALT.
  • Avoid Rifampin with saquinavir + ritonavir: ↑ hepatitis risk.
  • Black Box warning— Saquinavir to be used only with Ritonavir.
  • Possible QT prolongation. Use with caution in those with cardiac conduction abnormalities or when used with drugs with potential to prolong QTc interval.
  • Caution: Numerous drug interactions (see Major Drug Interactions chart below, not all-inclusive)

Adult Dose

Usual Dose

2 tabs Saquinavir (1000 mg) + 1 cap Ritonavir (100 mg) po bid with food OR
4 tabs Saquinavir (2000 mg) + 1 cap Ritonavir (100 mg) po qd with food (alternative regimen; use only in ARV-naive patients)

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Warning—Use Saquinavir only with Ritonavir.
    • Avoid garlic capsules (may reduce SQV levels) and use cautiously with proton-pump inhibitors (increased SQV levels significant; may lead to increased GI symptoms, triglycerides, DVT).
    • Preliminary data suggest use of Saquinavir/Ritonavir may increase QTc.
  • Most Common:
    • Diarrhea, abdominal discomfort, nausea, headache
  • Photosensitivity (one report)
