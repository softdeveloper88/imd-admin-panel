Anthrasil

Usage and Dosing

  • Anthrax Immune Globulin Intravenous
  • Indicated as part of combination therapy for treatment of inhalational anthrax in adults and children.
  • Anthrasil is human IgG containing polyclonal antibodies that bind the protective antigen of Bacillus anthracis toxin.
  • 1 vial has a minimum potency of 60 units
  • Contraindications
    • History of anaphylaxis or severe systemic reaction of human immune globulins
    • IgA deficiency with anti-IgA antibodies and a history of IgA hypersensitivity

Adult Dose

  • Usual dose: 420 units (7 vials) IV administered at a starting infusion rate of 0.5 ml/min for first 30 min, then 1 ml/min as tolerated for next 30 min up to a maximum rate of 2 ml/min

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days) Wt-based dosing, single-dose:
<10 kg: 60 units (1 vial)
10 to <18 kg: 120 units (2 vials)
18 to <25 kg: 180 units (3 vials)
25 to <35 kg: 240 units (4 vials)
35 to <50 kg: 300 units (5 vials)
50 to <60 kg: 360 units (6 vials)
≥60 kg: 420 units (7 vials)

Adverse Effects

  • Most common: Headache, infusion site pain or swelling, nausea, back pain
  • Hypersensitivity reactions
  • Interference with blood and urine glucose testing
  • Thrombosis
  • Acute renal dysfunction
  • Aseptic meningitis
  • Transfusion-related acute lung injury
  • Transmission of blood-borne pathogens

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • Toxin of Bacillus anthracis (no direct antibacterial activity)
