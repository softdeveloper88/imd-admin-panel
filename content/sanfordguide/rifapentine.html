Usage and Dosing

  • Rifapentine is a long-acting rifamycin anti-tuberculous agent that can be substituted for rifampin or rifabutin
  • Indications:
    • Initial 2 months of therapy for active tuberculosis, drug-susceptible strain: rifapentine 600 mg twice weekly directly observed therapy (DOT) in combination with daily Isoniazid (INH) 300 mg, Ethambutol 15 mg/kg, and Pyrazinamde 25 mg/kg (NOT studied in HIV-positive patients) THEN
      • Continuation phase of therapy, HIV-negative adult, noncavitary, drug-susceptible pulmonary tuberculosis
        • Smear-negative after 2 months initial therapy: once weekly DOT with rifapentine 600 mg plus INH 900 mg x 4 months
        • Smear-positive after 2 months initial therapy: once weekly DOT with rifapentine 600 mg plus INH 900 mg x 7 months
    • Aa a component of a four-month regimen of Rifapentine 1200 mg once daily + Moxifloxacin 400 mg once daily + Isoniazid 300 mg once daily + PZA 25 mg/kg/d for 8 weeks followed by Rifapentine + Moxifloxacin + Isoniazid for 9 weeks for treatment of pulmonary tuberculosis caused by drug-susceptible strains (N Engl J Med 2021; 384:1705-18).

    • Latent tuberculosis infection (LTBI): once weekly DOT with rifapentine 900 mg plus Isoniazid 900 mg for 3 months.

  • Caution: Numerous drug interactions (see Major Drug Interactions chart below, not all-inclusive)

Adult Dose

  • Usual dosing:
    • 600 mg once or twice weekly
    • 900 mg once weekly
    • 1200 mg once daily

Adverse Effects

  • Similar to other Rifamycins. (See Rifampin and Rifabutin). Hyperuricemia seen in 21%. Causes red-orange discoloration of body fluids.
  • Among 3893 patients who received weekly rifapentine plus Isoniazid for 3 months for latent tuberculosis, 138 had a significant systemic drug reaction; of which 87 patients had a flu-like syndrome (Clin Infect Dis 61:527, 2015)
    • Increased frequency of flu-like symptoms in volunteer study when Rifapentine plus Isoniazid was given concomitantly with dolutegravir (Clin Infect Dis 2018;67:193)

Comments
