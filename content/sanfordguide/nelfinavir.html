Nelfinavir
Tradename: Viracept

Usage and Dosing

  • Nelfinavir (NFV) is indicated for use as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually combined with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents (e.g., TDF-FTC or ABC-3TC). Ritonavir-boosting does not improve Nelfinavir levels significantly; therefore Ritonavir-boosting not recommended.
  • Nelfinavir diarrhea is common.
  • Coadministration of drugs with life-threatening toxicities & which are cleared by CYP3A4 is contraindicated.
  • Not recommended in initial regimens because of inferior efficacy; prior concerns about EMS now resolved.
  • Acceptable choice in pregnant women.
  • Pediatrics: The DHHS Pediatric ARV Guideline Panel does not recommend NFV-based regimens for use in children due to inferior potency compared to other regimens.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).

Adult Dose

Adult DoseTwo 625 mg tabs (1250 mg) po bid, with food

      Adverse Effects

      • Most Significant:
        • Potential for drug interactions. Powder contains phenylalanine.
      • Most Common:
        • Mild to moderate diarrhea 20%. Oat bran tabs, calcium, or oral anti-diarrheal agents (e.g., loperamide, diphenoxylate/atropine sulfate) can be used to manage diarrhea.

      Pregnancy Risk

      FDA Risk Category 1 B
