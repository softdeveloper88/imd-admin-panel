Ganciclovir
Tradename: Cytovene, Vitraset

Usage and Dosing

  • Ganciclovir is used for cytomegalovirus (CMV) infections. It also has activity against other herpes viruses, although it is rarely used for these infections.
  • Black Box warnings: cytopenias, carcinogenicity/teratogenicity and aspermia in animals.
  • Neutropenia may respond to granulocyte colony stimulating factor (G-CSF or GM-CSF). Severe myelosuppression may be increased with coadministration of Zidovudine or azathioprine. 32% discontinue/interrupt therapy, principally for neutropenia. Avoid extravasation.
  • Also, as Ganciclovir intraocular implant for CMV retinitis. Does not prevent CMV retinitis in good eye or visceral dissemination. Replacement every 6 months is recommended.

Adult Dose

Induction Dose

5 mg/kg IV q12h x14 days or
Vitreal implant in affected eye (replace every 6 months or so)

Maintenance Dose

5 mg/kg IV q24h or 6 mg/kg 5x/week or
1000 mg po tid

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days)Symptomatic congenital CMV: 12 mg/kg/day (divided q12h)

    Adverse Effects

    • Absolute neutrophil count dropped below 500 per mm3 in 15%, thrombocytopenia 21%, anemia 6%. Fever 48%. GI 50%: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain 19%, rash 10%. Retinal detachment 11% (likely due to underlying diseases). Confusion, headache, psychiatric disturbances and seizures.
    • Intraocular implant: Late retinal detachment (7/30 eyes).
