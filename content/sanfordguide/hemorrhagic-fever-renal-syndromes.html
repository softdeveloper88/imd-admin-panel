Hemorrhagic Fever, Renal Syndromes

Clinical Setting

  • Viral hemorrhagic fever with renal syndromes
    • Lassa, Venezuelan, Korean, Sabia, Argentinian, Bolivian, Junin, Machupo
  • >90% occur in China; seasonal variation (Clin Infect Dis 59:1040, 2014)
  • Review of diagnostic methods (Clin Infect Dis 62:214, 2016).
  • Lassa: Older age, Acute renal failure associated with higher mortality (Adjusted OR = 15) (Lancet Infect Dis 2018, 18:684)

Etiologies

  • Lassa
  • Venezuelan
  • Korean
  • Sabia
  • Argentinian
  • Bolivian
  • Junin
  • Machupo

Primary Regimens

  • Lassa fever (WHO recommendation)
    • Ribavirin 30 mg/kg po initial dose, then 15 mg/kg po q6h x 4 days, then 7.5 mg/kg/day x 6 days
  • Ribavirin is not effective against other etiologies above

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Controversy regarding the quality of data supporting Ribavirin use in Lassa fever (Emerg Inf Dis, Aug 2022)
  • Some contradiction in Treatment Guidelines for Viral Hemorrhagic Fever (Lancet ID 23(7):E240, 2023)
  • Toxicity low, hemolysis reported but recovery when treatment stopped.
  • No significant changes in WBC, platelets, hepatic or renal function.
  • See Clin Infect Dis 36:1254, 2003, for management of contacts.
  • Lassa Fever: Favipiravir effective in Macaques (Emerging Inf Dis 24:1696, 2018)
