Necrotizing Fasciitis, Overview

Overview

  • Necrotizing fasciitis is a rapidly progressive, life-threatening soft-tissue infection that is typically caused by Group A Streptococcus (GAS) or mixed aerobic/anaerobic bacteria.
    • Type I: Polymicrobial, e.g., Meleney's and Fournier's
    • Type 2: Monomicrobial, e.g., S. pyogenes, Clostridia sp., S. aureus (MRSA), Aeromonas sp., Vibrio vulnificus, Klebsiella sp.
  • Klebsiella type is especially prevalent in Taiwan (Clin Infect Dis 55:930, 2012).
  • IDSA Practice Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 2014;59:e10
  • Reviews: J Infect Dis 2017;215:269 ; Infect Dis Clinic North Am 2017;31:497; New Engl J Med 2017;377:2253; Curr Opin Infect Dis. 2018;31:113
  • Excellent historical review: Lancet Infect Dis 15:109, 2015.

Empiric Management

  • Prompt surgical consultation
  • Tetanus Toxoid as Tdap if more than 10 yrs since last tetanus toxoid
  • Wound gram stain and culture; Blood cultures
  • Broad initial empiric therapy. See specific topic, but several choices:
    • [Vancomycin or Linezolid or Daptomycin]  +  [Piperacillin-tazobactam or a carbapenem or (Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole)]
    • If suspect streptococcal toxic shock, add Clindamycin

Specific Topics

  • Clostridial
  • Staphylococcal
  • Streptococcal
  • Synergistic: Meleney's and Fournier's
  • See also Klebsiella pneumoniae and Vibrio vulnificus
