Truvada
Tradename: Emtricitabine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate

Usage and Dosing

  • Truvada is indicated for use as part of a dual nucleoside (fixed-dose combination) part of a 3-drug regimen. Often paired with either an NNRTI, PI, or an INSTI.
  • Black Box warning—Exacerbation of Hepatitis B after stopping FTC; but preferred therapy for those with Hepatitis B.
  • See individual components for drug-specific data:
    • Emtricitabine (FTC)
    • Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF)
  • Truvada is available in four tablet strengths: 200 mg/300 mg, 167 mg/250 mg, 133 mg/200 mg, and 100 mg/150 mg of FTC and TDF, respectively
  • For class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.
  • Descovy (FTC + TAF) is now generally preferred over Truvada owing to a lower toxicity profile, especially among those who are taking boosted PI regimens.

Adult Dose

  • Weight ≥35 kg: 1 tab po once daily with or without food
  • Tablet: FTC 200 mg + TDF 300 mg
