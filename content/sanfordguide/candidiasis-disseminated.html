Candidiasis, Chronic Disseminated

Clinical Setting

  • Chronic, disseminated infection of liver and spleen.
  • Also termed hepatosplenic candidiasis.
  • Febrile patient with hematological malignancy recovering from neutropenia.

Etiologies

  • Candida albicans
  • Other non-albicans Candida sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Echinocandin (Caspofungin 70 mg IV loading dose, then 50 mg IV qd OR Micafungin 100 mg IV qd OR Anidulafungin 200 mg IV loading dose then 100 mg IV qd) OR Lipid-based Amphotericin B 3–5 mg/kg/day for several week, followed by Fluconazole 400 mg (6 mg/kg)/day IV/po when stable (or other azole depending on species and susceptibility)

Alternative Regimens

  • Amphotericin B 0.5–0.7 mg/kg/day
  • Fluconazole 400 mg (6 mg/kg)/day IV/po when stable (or other azole depending on species and susceptibility)

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Treat until lesions have resolved or calcified (usually several months). Additional chemotherapy can proceed when needed, provided that antifungal therapy is continued.

Comments

  • Corticosteroids may reduce fever and abdominal pain but do not speed radiographic resolution of lesions (Clin Infect Dis 46:696, 2008).
  • IDSA treatment guidelines:  Clin Infect Dis 62:409, 2016.
