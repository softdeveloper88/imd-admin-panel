Pharyngitis, Tonsillitis: Recurrent

Clinical Setting

  • A small percentage of patients have recurrent culture-positive Group A streptococcus episodes with symptomatic tonsillo-pharyngitis.
  • At least 4 possibilities:
    • True new infection due to Group A Strep from family or community contact
    • Active viral infection in a carrier of Group A streptococci
    • Non-compliance with prescribed antibiotic treatment regimens
    • Treatment failure of primary regimen due to antibiotic resistance or other reasons. Rare explanation.

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus pyogenes (Group A)

Primary Regimens

  • Pediatric:
    • Clindamycin 20-30 mg/kg/day po divided q8h x 10 days
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 45 mg/kg/day divided q12h x 10 days
    • Tonsillectomy ?  See Comments below
  • Adult:
    • Clindamycin 450 mg po q6h x 10 days
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate (500/125) 1 tab po tid x 10 days
  • For tonsillectomy, see comments below

Alternative Regimens

  • Pediatric: Benzathine Penicillin G 25,000 units/kg IM (to maximum 1.2 million units) ± Rifampin 10 mg/kg q24h x 4 days
  • Adult: Benzathine Penicillin G 1.2 million units IM ± Rifampin 20 mg/kg/day x 4 days (to maximum 300 mg bid)

Comments

  • Tonsillectomy for documented recurrent tonsillitis ? See Clinical Guideline: Otolaryngology Head and Neck Surgery 2019; 160:187.
  • Recommend watchful waiting and no surgery if:
    • <7 episodes in last year
    • <5 episodes/year in past 2 years
    • < 3 episodes /year in past 3 years
    • An episode requires: Temp. over 38.5, tonsillar exudate, adenopathy, and documentation of presence of GpA beta-hemolytic streptococci
    • Modify  and consider surgery if:
      • Multiple allergies or intolerance to antibiotics
      • Recurrent episodes  of PFAPA syndrome:
        • Periodic fever
        • Aphthous stomatitis
        • Pharyngitis
        • Adenitis
