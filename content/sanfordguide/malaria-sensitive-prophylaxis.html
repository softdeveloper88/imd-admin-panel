Malaria, Chloroquine Sensitive, Prophylaxis

Clinical Setting

  • Prophylaxis for persons traveling to areas free of chloroquine-resistant Plasmodium falciparum.
  • Sensitive (all malaria) in
    • Central America west of the Panama canal
    • Caribbean
    • Korea
    • Most Middle Eastern countries
  • See CDC Malaria Prophylaxis coverage for Country-by-Country risk.
  • Prophylaxis includes anti-malarial drugs + personal protection: screens, nets, 30–35% DEET skin repellent (avoid products with >50% DEET), permethrin spray on clothing and mosquito nets.
  • Tafenoquine FDA approved in 2018. Arakoda (U.S) and Kodatef (Australia) are commercially available but availability in retail pharmacies may be variable.

Etiologies

  • Plasmodium falciparum (only refers to P. falciparum in Central America and Caribbean)
  • Plasmodium vivax (except Papua New Guinea and Indonesia)
  • Plasmodium ovale
  • Plasmodium malariae
  • Plasmodium knowlesi

Primary Regimens

  • Adult
    • Chloroquine phosphate 500 mg (300 mg base) po per week starting 1–2 weeks before travel, each week during travel and 4 weeks post-travel
    • Atovaquone-proguanil 1 adult tab per day with food  (1 day prior to, during and 7 days post-travel)
  • Pediatrics
    • Chloroquine phosphate 8.3 mg/kg (5 mg/kg of base) po 1x/wk up to 300 mg (base) max. dose
    • Atovaquone-proguanil by weight (tabs/day):

Alternative Regimens

  • Adult
    • Mefloquine 250 mg (228 mg base) 1 tab po once per week, 2-3  weeks before, during and for 4 weeks after travel change to plain text
      • If possible begin 3 weeks ahead to assess for intolerance
    • Hydroxychloroquine 400 mg po weekly taken on the same schedule as chloroquine.
      • Can be used if chloroquine is unavailable or in a situation where daily hydroxychloroquine is already being prescribed; a daily dose of at least 100 mg is adequate to match the weekly dose.
    • Tafenoquine (age ≥18 years only): 
      • Before travel to malaria-endemic area (loading regimen): 200 mg once daily x3 days (starting 3 days before travel)
      • While in endemic area (maintenance regimen): 200 mg once weekly (starting 7 days after last loading regimen dose)
      • After exiting malaria endemic area: 200 mg one-time dose (7 days after last maintenance dose)
    • Doxycycline 100 mg po qd. Take 1-2 days before, daily during and daily for 4 weeks after travel
    • For P. vivax only countries (very few): Primaquine phosphate 30 mg base po daily and for 7 days after travel in non-pregnant, G6PD-normal travelers (> 92% effective vs. P. vivax, Am J Trop Med Hyg 75:402, 2007; Travel Med Infect Dis 17:5, 2017.
  • Pediatrics
    • Age 8-12 years: Doxycycline 2.2 mg/kg once/day up to 100 mg/day. Take 1-2 days before, during and for 4 weeks after travel

Comments

  • Atovaquone-proguanil (Malarone) regimen may be more convenient with increased adherence for short trips but expensive for longer trips.
  • Weekly drugs are often dosed on Sunday for convenience.
  • Tafenoquine:
    •  Must ensure normal G6PD status before prescribing.  G6PD test results difficult to intrepret.  Review: J Travel Med. 26: pii: taz023, 2019.
    • Approved for administration for up to six months of continuous dosing
  • Chloroquine may exacerbate psoriasis.
  • Doxycycline adverse effects: photosensitivity (advise diligent use of sunblock), gastritis, Candida vaginitis.
