Pneumonia, Moraxella catarrhalis

Clinical Setting

  • Normal nasopharyngeal flora but also a cause of community-acquired pneumonia (CAP), frequently in association with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
  • Positive blood culture (uncommon) or positive sputum Gram stain and culture, although the latter could reflect colonization.
  • ~90% are β-lactamase positive.

Etiologies

  • Moraxella catarrhalis

Primary Regimens

  • Oral regimens
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po bid x 5-7 days
    • TMP-SMX 160/800 mg po bid x 5-7 days
    • Levofloxacin 750 mg po q24h x 5 days
  • Parenteral regimens
    • Ceftriaxone 1 gm IV q24h x 5-7 days
    • Levofloxacin 750 mg IV q24h x 5 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po/IV q12h x 5-7 days
  • Cefuroxime 500 mg po bid x 5-7 days
  • Azithromycin 500 mg po day 1 then 250 mg po for days 2-5

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • M. catarrhalis is a highly drug susceptible organism with the exception of resistance to amoxicillin and penicillin due to beta-lactamase production.
  • Duration of therapy: 5-7 days of antimicrobial therapy is sufficient in most cases.

Comments

  • Some continue treatment until patient is afebrile for 3-5 days.
