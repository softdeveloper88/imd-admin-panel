Tuberculosis, Latent, High-Risk

Clinical Setting

  • Positive test for latent tuberculosis, either positive tuberculin skin test (TST) or interferon-gamma release assay (IGRA) PLUS no evidence of active disease (see also Diagnostic Testing).
    • Positive TST is > 5 mm induration for the following high risk groups:
      • HIV-positive: risk of progression to active disease is 10-fold higher in advanced HIV compared to otherwise normal host.
      • Recent close contact to active case.
      • Radiographic evidence of old, healed tuberculosis no prior treatment.
      • Immunosuppression.
      • Chronic steroid therapy (prednisone >15 mg/day).
    • Positive TST is > 10 mm induration for those with other risk factors including foreign-born from high-prevalence region, injection drug use, chronic renal failure, illicit IV drug use, silicosis; diabetes mellitus, weight >10% below normal or chronic malnutrition, previous gastrectomy; smoker.
  • TNF-α inhibitor therapy significantly increases risk of progression to active tuberculosis and candidates should be pre-screened for latent tuberculosis and treated with latent TB if TST or IGRA is positive (Infect Dis Clin N Am 2020;34:413).
    • See Prevention: Persons starting Biologics for other agents associated with risk of TB.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis
