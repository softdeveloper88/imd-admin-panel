Coccidioidomycosis, Meningitis

Clinical Setting

  • Subacute or chronic meningitis presenting with headache, cranial nerve palsies, focal neurological deficits.
  • CSF findings: high protein, lymphocytic pleocytosis, and low glucose.
  • Diagnostics: presence of coccidioidal antigen in CSF is more sensitive than CSF antibodies (immunodiffusion or complement fixation) which is much more sensitive CSF culture (Clin Infect Dis 61:1521, 2015).
  • Negative serologies do not exclude infections; tests obtained early in the course of disease may be falsely negative and repeated testing may be required to establish the diagnosis.

Etiologies

  • Coccidioides immitis

Primary Regimens

  • Fluconazole 400–1,200 mg po once daily
  • See Comments regarding use of adjunctive corticosteroids

Alternative Regimens

  • Itraconazole 400-800 mg po divided 2 to 4 times daily
  • Voriconazole: 6 mg/kg po/IV q12h x 24h, then 4 mg/kg po q12h (Clin Infect Dis 36:1619, 2003; Antimicrob Agents Chemother 48:2341, 2004).
  • Amphotericin B intrathecal 0.1-1.5 mg/dose, daily to weekly to achieve therapeutic response (consider only in refractory cases)
    • For practical details of intrathecal administration, see Clin Infect Dis 64: 519, 2017
  • Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg IV daily (Mycopathologia 183:619, 2018)

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Lifelong suppressive therapy is recommended given very high relapse rate.

Comments

  • In very small retrospective controlled study, data suggest that adjunctive therapy  with corticosteroids may reduce the risk (p < 0.049) of a second CVA in patients with Coccidioidomycosis meningitis (Clin Infect Dis 2017;65:338).
  • Pregnant women should received intrathecal Amphotericin B in the first trimester and then can consider transitioning to fluconazole in the second and third trimesters.
  • In cases where decision is made for long-term intrathecal Amphotericin B, consider placement of Omaya reservoir. Intrathecal therapy can be associated with arachnoiditis.
  • Review articles on coccidioidal meningitis: Clin Infect Dis 42:103, 2006 and Medicine 89:251, 2010.
  • IDSA guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 63:e112, 2016
