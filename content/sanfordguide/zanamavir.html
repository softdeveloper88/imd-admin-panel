Zanamivir
Tradename: Relenza

Usage and Dosing

  • Zanamivir is indicated for:
    • Treatment of uncomplicated influenza A or B in patients age ≥7 years who have been symptomatic for ≤2 days.
    • Prophylaxis of influenza in patients age ≥5 years.
  • Active by inhalation against neuraminidase of both influenza A and B to inhibit release of virus from epithelial cells of respiratory tract. Approx. 4–17% of inhaled dose absorbed into plasma. Excreted by kidney but with low absorption, dose reduction not necessary in renal impairment.  
  • IV formulation approved in Europe for treatment of severe influenza pneumonia among hospitalized patients; US approved as an Emerging Investigational New Drug (EIND; see below)
  • Reports of activity vs oseltamivir-resistant influenza: Clin Infect Dis 50:1249, 2010; Clin Infect Dis 50:1493, 2010;  N Engl J Med 362:88, 2010.
  • Caution: do not reconstitute Zanamivir powder for use in nebulizers or mechanical ventilators (MedWatch report of death).
  • Zanamivir for IV administration is being explored in pediatric populations for severe disease. IV formulation is available for compassionate use through an FDA emergency investigative new drug (EIND) application.

Adult Dose

Treatment Dose
  • Oral inhalation of 2 blisters (10 mg) bid for 5 days. Powder is inhaled by specially designed inhalation device. Each blister contains 5 mg Zanamivir.
IV EIND
  • If failure to respond to Oseltamivir and/or patient unable to either use inhaled Zanamivir or oral Oseltamivir, IV Zanamivir is available through an emergency investigative new drug application (EIND);
    • Contact Glaxo SmithKline to obtain EIND forms: (+1) 919-315-5215 (24/7), then
    • Contact the FDA: (+1) 301-796-1500 (business day); (+1) 301-796-9900 (after hours).
Prophylaxis Dose
  • Oral inhalation of 2 blisters (10 mg) once daily for 10 days (household outbreak) or up to 28 days (community outbreak).

Hepatic Adjustment

  • None

Adverse Effects

  • <3% cough, sinusitis, diarrhea, nausea and vomiting. Reports of respiratory adverse events in patients with or without h/o airways disease, should be avoided in patients with underlying respiratory disease. Allergic reactions and neuropsychiatric events have been reported.
