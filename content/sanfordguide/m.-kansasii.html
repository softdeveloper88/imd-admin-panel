Mycobacterium kansasii

Clinical Setting

  • Pulmonary infection in immunocompetent host.
  • Disseminated infection in HIV/AIDS.
  • Prognosis related to level of immunosuppression.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium kansasii

Primary Regimens

  • ([Isoniazid 300 mg + Pyridoxine 50 mg OR Azithromycin 250-500 mg] + Rifampin 600 mg + Ethambutol 15 mg/kg) po q24h
    • Preference is shifting toward use of a macrolide rather than Isoniazid.
    • Treat 12 months 
  • Rifampin resistant organism
    • Include at least 3 active drugs in the regimen, for example Ethambutol 15 mg/kg + Moxifloxacin 400 mg + Azithromycin 250-500 mg all administered po once daily
    • Treat 12 months

Alternative Regimens

  • For nodular/bronchiectatic disease due to a rifampin-susceptible organism a 3x weekly regimen may be used as follows:
    • (Azithromycin 500 mg)  + Rifampin 600 mg + Ethambutol 30 mg/kg 
    • Treat until 12 months of culture-negative sputum
    • 3x weekly therapy not recommended with INH or for cavitary disease or in setting of HIV infection due to lack of data
  • Clarithromycin 500 mg bid may be substituted for Azithromycin
  • Rifabutin 300 mg once daily (150 mg if Clarithromycin is used and as indicated for other drug interactions) or 300 mg 3x weekly may be substituted for Rifampin

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Confirm susceptibility to macrolides and rifampin; CLSI does not recommend susceptibility testing for Isoniazid or Ethambutol as MIC values do not correlate with clinical response (J Clin Microbiol. 2019;57:e00834-19).
  • Amikacin or Streptomycin not recommended for routine use but may be an option if rifampin cannot be used in the regimen.
  • For rifampin resistant isolate or drug intolerance determine susceptibilities to Amikacin, Ciprofloxacin, Doxycycline, Linezolid, Minocycline, Moxifloxacin, and Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole.

Comments

  • All isolates are resistant to Pyrazinamide.
  • ATS/ERS/ESCMID/IDSA Clinical Practice Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 2020; 71:e1-e36.
