Trichinosis, Trichinellosis

Clinical Setting

  • Consumption of undercooked meat: pig/boar (pork), bear, cougar, horse, walrus, seal, fox, other carnivorous animals
  • Initial diarrhea followed by muscle invasion 1 week after infection causing myositis, myocarditis and rarely CNS or lung involvement.
  • Light infections may be asymptomatic. Severe myositis wanes as larvae encyst and become chronic.
  • Inadequate drying or smoking of game meat associated with outbreaks.

Diagnosis

  • Serologic diagnosis (ELISA using E-S antigens).  IgG may not be detectable during initial 14 days of symptoms. 
  • Muscle biopsy not necessary.
  • A cause of massive eosinophilia >5,000 mm3
    • Eosinophilia, increased IgE, increased CPK, ESR 0.

Etiologies

  • Trichinella spiralis (usual)
  • Other Trichinella sp.
    • T. pseudospiralis
    • T. nativa (Arctic bear)
    • T. nelsoni (African predators)
    • T. murelli
    • T. britovi

Primary Regimens

  • Albendazole 400 mg po bid x 8–14 days + Prednisone 40–60 mg po q24h.
    • Antiparasitic agents kill adults and stop production of new larvae but do not kill existing larvae in muscle.

Alternative Regimens

  • Mebendazole 200 to 400 mg po tid x 3 days, then 400 to 500 mg po tid x 10 days + Prednisone 40–60 mg po q24h

Comments

  • Albendazole/Mebendazole are Pregnancy Category C due to lack of data. Use if benefit outweighs theoretical risk. WHO allows use in 2nd and 3rd trimester; no evidence of harm if given in 1st trimester.
  • Preliminary data on post-exposure albendazole/mebendazole prophylaxis within 6 days of ingestion Clin Infect Dis. 60:e98, 2015.
  • Formulations:
    • Mebendazole 500 mg chewable tablet available for donation only, not commercially available in U.S.
    • Albendazole 200 mg tablets and Mebendazole 100 mg tablets (see Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources).
  • Review: Clin Microbiol Rev 22:127, 2009.
