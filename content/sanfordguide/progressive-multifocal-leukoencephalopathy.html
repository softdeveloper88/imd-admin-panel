Progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy, JC virus

Clinical Setting

  • Progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML) is a serious demyelinating disease caused by JC virus in immunocompromised patients e.g., HIV/AIDS or those on immunomodulating agents (see Comments).

Diagnosis

  • White matter disease on MRI. JC virus PCR of CSF

Etiologies

  • JC virus

Primary Regimens

  • No antiviral therapy is indicated
    • Decrease immunosuppressive therapy.
  • If HIV+:
    •  Antiretroviral therapy (ART) (Watch for IRIS) +
    • Cidofovir (may be effective)

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Failure of treatment with interferon alfa-2b, cytarabine and topotocan.
  • Risk factors for persons on biologics:
    •  Natalizumab is first line therapy for relapsing/remitting multiple sclerosis. PML has occured in approx. 0.2% of multiple sclerosis (MS) patients treated with natalizumab.  The risk increases proportionate to the duration of therapy; its use is restricted to certified providers
    • Case reports of PML after use of the janus kinase inhibitor drug Ruxolitinib and the MS drug fingolimod
    • Review: Infect Dis Clin N Am 2020; 34: 359
  • Mixed reports on Cidofovir. Most likely effective in persons on ART.
