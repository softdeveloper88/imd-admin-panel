Sinusitis, Rhinosinusitis, Atrophic

Clinical Setting

  • Two types of atrophic rhinosinusitis:
    • Primary: young people in developing world. Colonization of atrophic  mucosa by Klebsiella ozaenae and other bacteria. Associated foul-smelling nasal discharge.
    • Secondary: atrophic nasal mucosa at any age due to aging and/or post-trauma, post-injury/radiation/surgery or chronic granulomatous inflammation

Etiologies

  • Pathogenesis of atrophic mucosa poorly understood

Primary Regimens

  • Specific therapy based on clinical evidence of inflammation and  results of culture and susceptibility testing.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Difficult cases: suggest consultation with otolaryngologist
