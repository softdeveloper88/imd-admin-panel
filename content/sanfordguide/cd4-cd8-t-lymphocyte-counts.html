CD4 & CD8 T-Lymphocyte Counts

Definitions

  • There are 2 types of T-lymphocytes:
    • CD4: Helper T-lymphocytes
    • CD8: Cytotoxic T-lymphocytes
  • Unique surface antigens (cluster determinants) are detected by flow cytometry.
  • HIV infects and destroys CD4 cells; CD8 cells are cytotoxic for infected CD4 cells.
  • Note: Persistent elevation of CD8 counts during antiretroviral therapy (ART) forecasts treatment failure (J AIDS 57:396, 2011).

Clinical Use

  • CD4 counts are used clinically:
    • As a marker of the stage of HIV infection
    • To assess risk for opportunistic infections (OIs)
    •  As a benchmark for diagnosis of AIDS (<200 μL)
    • Previously used as  a benchmark for initiating antiretroviral therapy (ART)
    • As a benchmark for starting prophylaxis for OIs
    • As an indicator of response to antiretroviral therapy (ART) (repeat every 6-12 months)
  • Review of current uses: Curr Opin HIV AIDS 2017;12:123

Laboratory Methods

  • CD4 counts are obtained by flow cytometry. Testing should be performed within 18 hours of cell collection.
  • Lab method: Fluoresceinated CD4 antibody added; % CD4 cells counted. Total CD4 count = WBC x % lymphocytes x % CD4 cells.
  • CD4 counts reflect either an absolute cell count (cells/μL) or a percentage (%) of CD4 cells. Significant change (2 SD) between 2 tests: 30% change in absolute count or 3% change in % CD4 cells.
  • Now possible to do CD4 counts at point of care (see Alere Pima Analyser) which allows earlier initiation of appropriate therapy (PLOS One. doi:10.1371/journal.pone 0155256)

  • Note: Cirrhotics have low absolute CD4 counts but normal percentage of CD4 cells (Clin Infect Dis 54:1798, 2012; Clin Infect Dis 54:1806, 2012).
  • Factors that affect absolute CD4 counts:
    • Variability in test procedure (increase or decrease)
    • Time of day (increase or decrease)
    • Season of year (increase or decrease)
    • HTLV-1 infection (increases)
    • Splenectomy (increases)
    • Alpha interferon (decreases)
  • Factors that decrease CD4 counts:
    • Acute infection other than HIV
    • Acute corticosteroids
    • Progression of HIV
    • Idiopathic CD4 lymphocytopenia

Course of CD4 Counts

  • Mean count prior to seroconversion: 1000/μL (mean value)
  • One year after seroconversion: 670/μL
  • Thereafter, average annual decline: 50/μL/yr
  • Large variation between patients
