Anidulafungin
Tradename: Eraxis

Usage and Dosing

  • Anidulafungin is an echinocandin which inhibits synthesis of β-(1,3)-D-glucan.
  • Fungicidal against Candida, including those resistant to other antifungals (e.g., C. glabrata and C. krusei), and active against Aspergillus.
  • Inactive against Cryptococcus, Trichosporon, molds other than Aspergillus.
  • Candida parapsilosis and C. guilliermondii less susceptible to all echinocandins relative to other species, but clinical response generally satisfactory.
  • For treatment of esophageal candidiasis (EC), candidemia, and other complicated Candida infections.
  • Effective in clinical trials of esophageal candidiasis & in 1 trial was superior to Fluconazole in treatment of invasive candidiasis/candidemia in 245 patients (75.6% vs 60.2%).
  • Negligible drug concentrations in CSF or urine.

Adult Dose

Usual Dose
Candidemia: 200 mg IV on day 1 followed by 100 mg/day IV
Esophageal Candida: 100 mg IV on day 1, then 50 mg/day IV
  • The rate of infusion should not exceed 1.1 mg/min.

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days)1.5-3 mg/kg load, then 0.75-1.5 mg/kg/day (once daily)

      Adverse Effects

      • Like other echinocandins, remarkably non-toxic.
      • Most common side-effects: nausea, vomiting, decreased Mg, decreased K, and headache in 11–13% of patients.
      • Infusion-related adverse effects, possibly histamine-related (e.g. rash, urticaria, flushing, pruritus, bronchospasm, dyspnea, hypotension). Do not exceed infusion rate of 1.1 mg/min.
      • Risk of metabolic crisis in patients with hereditary fructose intolerance.
