Hepatitis A

Clinical Setting

  • Acute Hepatitis A infection.
  • Several outbreaks of acute Hepatitis A infection among homeless populations in many areas in the United States (https://emergency.cdc.gov/han/han00412.asp)
    • Aggressive Hepatitis A vaccination recommended among at risk populations

Etiologies

  • Hepatitis A virus (positive-strand RNA picornavirus)

Primary Regimens

  • No therapy recommended. Supportive measures.

Prevention

  • See Hepatitis A, Vaccines for indications, available products, dosing, and vaccine characteristics for pre-exposure prevention.
  • For Post-exposure prophylaxis in persons aged ≥ 12 months, HepA vaccine with 1 age appropriate dose is preferred over IGIM and should be given as soon as possible, ideally within 2 weeks of exposure.
    • Alternative:  Immune Globulin: 0.1 mL/kg given IM as sole agent in children <12 mos of age, and if HepA vaccine not feasible
    • Both agents may be given based on risk assessment for persons over 40, HIV+,  and immunocompromised persons.
    • IVIG might be preferred over Hepatitis A vaccination among seronegative individuals with significant underlying liver disease.

Comments

  • 40% of patients with chronic Hepatitis C who developed superinfection with Hepatitis A developed fulminant hepatic failure (N Engl J Med 338:286, 1998). Therefore, ALL patients with underlying Hepatitis B or C infection should receive Hepatitis A vaccination.
  • Up to 20% of patients need hospitalization (N Engl J Med 353:890, 2005).
