Pneumonia, Children, Age > 3 months

Clinical Setting

  • Community-acquired pneumonia in infants and children, age > 3 months.
  • Empiric therapy for presumed bacterial pneumonia. Viral etiology is more common that bacterial overall even among hospitalized children N Engl J Med 372:845 2014.
    • Consider influenza virus, RSV (respiratory syncytial virus), human metapneumovirus, parainfluenza virus, adenovirus, SARS-CoV-2.
    • Influenza testing recommended for all hospitalized children in winter season.
    • Multiplex viral testing may be useful if it will change infection control or treatment, including avoidance of antibiotics.
  • Obtain blood culture if hospitalized; Blood cultures have very low yield in non-hospitalized children.
  • Often of mild to moderate severity.

Etiologies

  • RSV
  • Human metapneumovirus
  • Influenza
  • Common "other" viruses: rhinovirus, parainfluenza, adenovirus
  • SARS-CoV-2
  • Strep. pneumoniae
  • H. influenzae
  • Mycoplasma
  • Group A Streptococcus
  • Staph. aureus (uncommon)
  • M. tuberculosis

Primary Regimens

  • Outpatient:
    •  Preferred: Amoxicillin 90 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses x 5 days (see Lancet 371:49, 2008) 
      • Antimicrobial therapy is not routinely required for preschool-aged children with community-acquired pneumonia (CAP) because viral pathogens cause most clinical disease.
    • If influenza is diagnosed, treatment with oseltamivir or baloxavir is recommended for all children < 5 and those with hight risk conditions
  • Inpatient:
    • If atypical infection suspected ADD Azithromycin 10 mg/kg (maximum 500 mg/day) IV day 1, then 5 mg/kg (maximum 250 mg)
    • If community-associated MRSA a concern, ADD  Vancomycin 60- 80 mg/kg IV divided 3-4 times a day to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 10-15 μg/mL but AUC targeting is preferred) or Clindamycin 40 mg/kg/day divided q6-8h.
      • Negative nasal PCR test for MRSA makes MRSA pneumonia highly unlikely
    • If influenza is diagnosed or strongly suspected: Oseltamivir
      • Wt < 15 kg: 30 mg in 2 doses x 5 days
      • Wt > 15 kg to 23 kg: 45 mg in 2 doses x 5 days
      • Wt 23 kg to 40 kg: 60 mg in 2 doses x 5 days
      • Wt > 40 kg: 75 mg in 2 doses x 5 days
    • Infants 2 wks-11 months: Oseltamivir 3 mg/kg in 2 doses x 5 days
    • Fully immunized and bacterial infection suspected: Ampicillin 150-200 mg/kg/day IV divided q6h
    • Not fully immunized and bacterial infection suspected OR life-threatening illness: Cefotaxime 150 mg/kg/day IV divided q8h (where available outside the US) or Ceftriaxone 75-100 mg/kg/day IV once daily

Alternative Regimens

  • Outpatient
    • Azithromycin 10 mg/kg po x 1 dose (maximum 500 mg), then 5 mg/kg (maximum 250 mg) po x 4 days (for true amoxicillin allergy or suspected or proven atypical pathogen)
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 90 mg/kg/day (amoxicillin component)
    • Clarithromycin 15 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses x 7-14 days
  • Inpatient, fully immunized
    • Cefotaxime 150 mg/kg/day IV divided q8h (where available)
    • Ceftriaxone 75-100 mg/kg/day IV once daily

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • May transition to oral regimen as early as 2-3 days depending on clinical response
  • Shorter duration (5-7 days) shown to be equally effective in adults and pediatric outpatients. JAMA Pediatr. 2022 Mar 1;176(3):253
  • Pediatric data on shorter course for hospitalized children limited. 2011 guidelines still recommend 10-14 days but 5-7 are likely adequate in absence of complicated pneumonia
  • MRSA therapy can be stopped if nasal PCR for MRSA is negative
  • Viral causes predominate in children age <5 years. Antibiotics may not be necessary especially if virus detected
  • Low procalcitonin (<0.25 ng/mL) has high negative predictive value for excluding bacterial disease. J Pediatric Infect Dis Soc. 2017 Feb 3. Antibiotics can usually be stopped.
  • Longer durations of therapy may be required for methicillin-resistant Staph. aureus (MRSA)
  • For appropriately drained empyema, treat for 7-10 days after resolution of fever or up to 4-6 weeks

Comments

  • IDSA treatment guidelines for treatment of community-acquired pneumonia in infants and children older than 3 months of age: Clin Infect Dis 53:617, 2011; Clin Infect Dis 53:e25, 2011.
  • Etiology among hospitalized children N Engl J Med 372:845 2014
  • Vancomycin: traditional dosing of 45-60 mg/kg/day frequently does not achieve target AUC in term infants and older children with normal renal function. Use of AUC24  closer to 400 μg/mL adequate for most non-CNS infections. Clin Infect Dis Jul 13 2020
