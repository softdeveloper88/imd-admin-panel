Histoplasmosis, Pulmonary

Clinical Setting

  • Pulmonary histoplasmosis is most often asymptomatic, but when symptomatic, frequently presents as a self-limited community-acquired pneumonia. Rarely patients can have diffuse, severe pulmonary disease leading to respiratory failure or a chronic nodular or cavitary process.

Diagnosis

  • Multiple modalities are available for diagnosing histoplasmosis and include histopathology of infected tissue, culture, serology, antigen testing of serum, urine, and bronchoalveolar lavage fluid, serum IgG/IgM antibody. Etiologies

  • Histoplasma capsulatum

Primary Regimens

  • Acute pulmonary infection
    • Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg IV daily x 1-2 weeks, followed by Itraconazole (dosing as below) + methylprednisolone 0.5-1 mg/kg/day x 1-2 weeks for severe cases
    • Moderately severe to severe disease:
    • Mild to moderate disease:
      • Immune-competent host, symptoms < 1 month: No treatment
      • Immunocompromised host or symptoms > 1 month: Itraconazole 200 mg po tid x 3 days, then bid x 6-12 weeks
  • Chronic pulmonary infection
    • Itraconazole 200 mg po tid x 3 days, then bid x 1-2 years

Alternative Regimens

  • Amphotericin B 0.7-1 mg/kg IV daily
  • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs)
    • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily
    • Suspension 200 mg qid, then 400 mg po bid after stabilization of disease
    • Posaconazole IV 300 mg IV over 90 minutes bid x 1 day, then 300 mg IV daily
  • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg po bid x 2 doses, then 4 mg/kg po bid
  • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily

Comments

  • Use of Itraconazole solution enables higher serum levels than capsules and is the preferred preparation. Serum levels of Itraconazole should be determined after 2 weeks to ensure adequate drug exposure (goal serum level >1.0 µg/mL).
  • Posaconazole and Voriconazole have been efficacious as salvage therapy (J Infect 54:319, 2007; Antimicrob Agents Chemother 53:1648, 2009).
  • Fluconazole is inferior to Itraconazole in the treatment of histoplasmosis and is not preferred therapy (Clin Infect Dis 23:996, 1996)
  • Antigen/antibody testing: Clin Infect Dis 62:896, 2016.
  • IDSA treatment guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 45:807, 2007.
