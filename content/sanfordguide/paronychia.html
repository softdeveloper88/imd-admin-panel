Paronychia

Clinical Setting

  • Cutaneous infection of the hand, cuticle or fingers occurring near or around the nail.
  • Often results from thumb sucking, nail biting, manicuring, other trauma.
  • Inflammation without purulent drainage or abscess can be treated with warm soaks 3-4 times a day.

Etiologies

  • Bacterial
    • Staph. aureus
    • Gram-negative rods
  • Fungal
    • Candida sp.
  • Viral
    • H. simplex (Whitlow)
    • Orf

Primary Regimens

  • Bacterial
    • Incision and drainage and culture of abscess, if present
    • TMP-SMX-DS 1-2 tab po bid (covers MRSA, preferred in high prevalence settings)
    • Cephalexin 500 mg tid while waiting for culture result
  • Fungal/Chronic Paronychia (see Comments)
    • Topical Amphotericin B, Clotrimazole, Econazole, Miconazole, or Nystatin tid/qid x 7–14 days
    • Ketoconazole 400 mg po qd or Itraconazole 200 mg po qd x 14 days
    • Ciclopirox olamine 1% cream/lotion; apply topically bid x 7–14 days
  • Viral
    • Acyclovir 400 mg po tid x 10 days
    • Famciclovir 250 mg po tid x 7-10 days or Valacyclovir 1000 mg po bid x 7-10 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Bacterial
    • MRSA or MSSA, anaerobes: Clindamycin 300-450 mg po tid
    • MSSA, Gram-negatives, anaerobes:  Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg po bid

Comments

  • Candida is a frequent colonizer, but may not be the primary pathogen in chronic paronychia, defined as inflammation of the nail fold for 6 weeks or longer.
    • One open label, randomized controlled trial found topical steroids were superior to antifungal agents: J Am Acad Dermatol. 2002;47:73.
    • Chronic paronychia is often due to repetitive mechanical and/or chemical injury resulting in inflammation and skin break down, identification and elimination of which results in resolution.
  • See also, Onychomycosis.
  • See J Am Acad Dermatol 70:120, 2014 for microbiology of antibiotic resistant cases, which are often herpetic.
  • Review articles:  Hand Clinic. 2020; 36:313 and Orthop Clin North Am. 2024; 55:265.
  • Review of differential diagnosis and treatment of common nail disorders: Ann Med. 2022; 54:694-712.
