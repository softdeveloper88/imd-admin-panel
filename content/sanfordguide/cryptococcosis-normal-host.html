Cryptococcosis, Pulmonary, Non-Meningeal

Clinical Setting

  • Cryptococcosis in HIV-negative patients: pulmonary or (rarely) disseminated disease in the absence of CNS disease
  • Positive culture or serum cryptococcal antigen.

Etiologies

  • Cryptococcus neoformans
  • Cryptococcus gattii

Primary Regimens

  • Immunocompetent host
    • Mild-to-moderate disease: Fluconazole 400 mg/day IV/po x 6-12 months
    • For more severe disease, treat as for cryptococcal meningitis
    • Pulmonary infection, Disseminated disease: treat as for cryptococcal meningitis
  • Immunocompromised host or transplant recipient
    • Mild-to-moderate pulmonary or non-CNS disease: Fluconazole 400 mg/day IV/po x 6-12 months
    • For more severe pulmonary disease or non-CNS disease (>1 noncontiguous foci of infection): treat as for cryptococcal meningitis.

Alternative Regimens

  • Oral alternatives to Fluconazole for mild-to-moderate pulmonary disease:
    • Itraconazole 200 mg capsules or oral solution bid x 6 to 12 months
    • Voriconazole 200 mg bid x 6-12 months
    • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs (Clin Infect Dis 45:562, 2007; Chest 132:952, 2007).
      • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily
      • Suspension 200 mg qid, then 400 mg po bid after stabilization of disease
      • Posaconazole IV 300 mg IV over 90 minutes bid x 1 day, then 300 mg IV daily
    • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily

Comments

  • Perform a lumbar puncture to rule out meningeal disease in most cases, particularly immunocompromised hosts.
  • If an azole other than Fluconazole is used, check levels to document adequate serum concentrations.
  • IDSA Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 50:291, 2010.
