Fexinidazole
Tradename: Fexinidazole Winthrop

Usage and Dosing

  • Nitroimidazole antiparasitic agent FDA, EMA, WHO approved for treatment of both first-stage (hemolymphatic) and second-stage (meningoencephalitic) human African trypanosomiasis.
    • Since 2024 drug of choice for both Trypanosoma brucei gambiense (g-HAT) and T. brucei rhodesiense (r-HAT) in adults and children (age ≥6 years and wt ≥20 kg).
  • Fexinidazole has revolutionized treatment as the first exclusively oral regimen for HAT.
  • Available only to government programs in affected areas (Lancet 391:144, 2018). 
    • Sanofi donates drug to WHO for use in endemic areas only.
    • 2024 WHO Guidelines
      • In the US, FDA-approved but must be obtained directly from Sanofi.  See Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources (international supply points outside US listed also).
      • Discussion of WHO Guidelines (Lancet Infect Dis. 2024 Oct 7:S1473-3099(24)00581-4)

  • Available as 600 mg tablets.
  • For patients aged <6 yrs or <20 kg OR ≥ 6 years and and g-HAT patients with body weight ≥ 20 kg presenting with < 100 WBC/µL in CSF. 
    • See Trypanosomiasis, East African and Trypanosomiasis, West African
  • NECT may have lower fatality and fewer relapses but oral fexinidazole much higher adherence (Cochrane Database Sys Rev 2021 Dec 9;12(12):CD015374)
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • Trials for T. cruzi disappointing so far due to toxicity of prolonged courses (Clin Infect Dis 2022 Aug 4)

Adult Dose

  • Wt ≥35 kg:
    • Loading dose 1800 mg (3 tablets) once daily x 4 days
    • Maintenance dose 1200 mg (2 tablets) once daily x 6 days
      • Patient must be able to swallow the 600 mg tablets after a substantial intake of food before each dose
      • Must complete the full 10-day treatment schedule. 
          • FDA suggests avoiding use if eGFR < 30 mL/min.
      • Avoid alcohol until >48 after course completion due to disulfiram-like reactions that occur with nitroimidazoles.
      • Do not re-dose after vomiting.
      • Take for a total of 10 daily doses, even if 1 day accidentally missed.
      • Tablets should not be broken or crushed.

Pediatric Dose

Dose Age ≥6 yr, wt ≥20 kg: use adult dose
Wt ≥20 kg and <35 kg:
  • Loading dose 1200 mg (2 tablets) once daily x 4 days
  • Maintenance dose 600 mg (1 tablet) once daily x 6 days

    Adverse Effects

    • Contraindications:
      • Cirrhosis
      • Jaundice
      • Patients at risk of QT interval prolongation (congenital QT prolongation, hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, bradycardia, CHF, other drugs that prolong the QT interval such as  those that block cardiac potassium channels, and/or those that induce bradycardia, or are inducers of hepatic CYP450). QTcF prolongation in the pivotal clinical study: 15-20 ms. Avoid use if QTcF  >470 ms.
    • Warnings:
      • CSF WBC >100 μL: use only if no alternative.
    • Most common AEs:
      • Nausea/vomiting
      • Headache
      • Insomnia
      • Asthenia
      • Dizziness
      • Tremor
    • Mood changes, psychiatric disorders: agitation, anxiety, abnormal behavior, depression, hallucinations, nightmares, personality changes.

    Pregnancy Risk

    FDA Risk Category 1 Evidence of toxicity in rats and rabbits at supranormal doses. May be used after the first trimester for g-HAT. 3, 6
