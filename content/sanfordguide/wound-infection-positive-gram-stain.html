Wound Infection, Post-Operative, Positive Gram Stain

Clinical Setting

  • Empiric therapy for post-operative wound infection with positive gram stain for gram-positive cocci, culture result pending.

Etiologies

  • Staphylococcus aureus (MSSA, MRSA)

Primary Regimens

  • Open and drain the wound
  • Oral:
    • TMP-SMX-DS 1-2 tabs po bid
    • Clindamycin 300–450 mg po tid
  • IV:
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative target is trough of 15-20 μg/mL
    • Daptomycin (4 mg/kg IV q24h or 6 mg/kg IV q24h)
    • Ceftaroline 600 mg IV q12h

Alternative Regimens

  • Open and drain the wound
  • Oral:
    • Minocycline 100 mg po q12h or Doxycycline 100 mg po bid
    • Linezolid 600 mg po q12h
  • IV: Telavancin 10 mg/kg IV q24h

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • In randomized trial of TMP-SMX and Clindamycin both regimens efficacious, but fewer recurrences with Clindamycin: Clin Infect Dis 62:1505, 2016
  • If S. aureus is Clindamycin-sensitive but Erythromycin-resistant, watch out for inducible Clindamycin resistance.

Comments

  • Based on a 2011 global survey of ~ 5,000 S. aureus isolates, for MSSA approx. 94% were susceptible to Tetracycline and >98% were susceptible to minocycline. For MRSA, approx. 85% were susceptible to Tetracycline, and 88.3% (by Eucast breakpoint) or 97.2% (by CLSI breakpoint) were susceptible to Minocycline (Diag Microbiol Infect Dis 75:402, 2013).
  • Avoid Telavancin in pregnancy; potential for fetal risk.
