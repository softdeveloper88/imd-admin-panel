Pilonidal Cyst, Pilonidal Abscess

Clinical Setting

  • Pilonidal "nest of hair" disease is an inflammatory process in the skin and subcutaneous tissue. The vast majority of cysts are at the cephalad end of the intergluteal cleft. The majority are in males.
  • Thought to result from a congenital failure to separate the ectoderm from the mesoderm with resultant trapping of "skin rests" under the surface of the skin.
  • Patients can present with asymptomatic painless cysts or sinus openings, acute abscesses, or chronic persistent drainage.

Etiologies

  • Anaerobes, mixed aerobes and anaerobes

Primary Regimens

  • Surgical excision or incision and drainage (Am Surg 2017 May 1;83(5):453-457).

Alternative Regimens

  • Antibacterial therapy is adjunctive and controversial:
    • Mild disease: Amoxicillin-clavulanate  875/125 mg  po bid
    • More severe disease:
      • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h
      • Metronidazole 500 mg IV po q6h + Ceftriaxone 1 gm IV
