Endocarditis, Viridans Group Streptococci

Clinical Setting

  • Endocarditis due to viridans group streptococcci and S. gallolyticus
  • Signs and symptoms of endocarditis; positive blood or valve culture
  • Susceptibility results are available: choice of therapy is based on MIC results.

Etiologies

  • Viridans streptococci
  • S. gallolyticus (formerly S. bovis)

Primary Regimens

  • Pen G MIC ≤ 0.12 μg/mL
    • Native valve
      • Penicillin G 12–18 million units/day IV, divided q4h x 4 weeks 
      • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h x 4 weeks
    • Prosthetic valve
      • One of the above administered 6 weeks
  • Pen G MIC > 0.12 to < 0.5 μg/mL
    • Native valve
      • For Ceftriaxone MIC < 0.5 μg/mL (EUCAST breakpoint, see Comments): Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h  x 4 weeks
      • Penicillin G 24 million units/day IV, divided q4h x 4 weeks + Gentamicin 3 mg/kg IV q24h for the first 2 weeks 
      • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h) x 4 weeks + Gentamicin 3 mg/kg IV q24h for the first 2 weeks
    • Prosthetic valve
      • Penicillin G 24 million units/day IV, divided q4h x 6 weeks + Gentamicin 3 mg/kg IV q24h for > 2 weeks 
      • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h) x 6 weeks + Gentamicin 3 mg/kg IV q24h for > 2 weeks
      • Note:  American Heart Association guidelines recommend 6 weeks of gentamcin, European Society of Cardiology guidelines recommend at least 2 weeks
  • Pen G MIC ≥ 0.5 μg/mL
    • Native valve:
      • Penicillin G 24 million units/24h IV divided q4h x 4 weeks + Gentamicin 3 mg/kg IV q24h x 2 weeks
      • For Ceftriaxone MIC ≤ 0.5 μg/mL (EUCAST breakpoint, see Comments): Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h x 4 weeks +  Gentamicin 3 mg/kg IV q24h x 2 weeks.
    • If prosthetic valve, one of above regimens administered for 6 weeks
      • Note:  American Heart Association guidelines recommend 6 weeks of Gentamcin, European Society of Cardiology guidelines recommend at least 2 weeks.

Alternative Regimen

  • Pen G MIC ≤ 0.12 μg/mL
    • For patients intolerant or allergic to beta-lactam:  Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h.
      • Dose to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL
      • Administer for 4 weeks for native valve, 6 weeks for prosthetic valve
    • Native valve only, uncomplicated disease (see Comments)
      • Penicillin G 12–18 million units/day IV, divided q4h x 2weeks + Gentamicin 3 mg/kg IV q24h x 2 weeks
      • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h) x 2 weeks + Gentamicin 3 mg/kg IV q24h x 2 weeks
  • Pen G MIC > 0.12 to < 0.5 μg/mL
    • For patients intolerant or allergic to beta-lactam, presence or risk of Gentamicin toxicity: Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h.
      • Dose to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL
      • Administer for 4 weeks for native valve, 6 weeks for prosthetic valve
  • Pen G MIC ≥ 0.5 μg/mL
    • For patients intolerant or allergic to beta-lactam, presence or risk of Gentamicin toxicity: Vancomycin 15 mg/kg IV q12h.
      • Dose to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL
      • Administer for 4 weeks for native valve, 6 weeks for prosthetic valve

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Infectious diseases consultation recommended
  • If the infected valve has been removed and the valve culture is negative, 2 weeks of antibiotic therapy post-op is sufficient (Clin Infect Dis 41:187, 2005).

Comments

  • CLSI breakpoint for Ceftriaxone susceptibility is ≤ 1.0 μg/mL; the EUCAST breakpoint of ≤ 0.5 μg/mL is more conservative and favored by the authors given paucity of data correlating MIC with efficacy of Ceftriaxone against strains of viridans streptococci or S. gallolyticus with penicillin MICs > 0.12 μg/mL.
  • 4-week regimens preferred for most patients with viridans streptococci or S. gallolyticus and Pen MIC ≤ 0.12 μg/mL; avoid 2-wk regimen for patients age > 65 years, those with cardiac or extracardiac abscess, creatinine clearance of <50 mL/min, impaired eighth cranial nerve function, or Abiotrophia, Granulicatella, or Gemella spp infection.
  • S. gallolyticus suggests occult bowel pathology:
    • Evaluation for gastrointestinal malignancy may be indicated (Clin Infect Dis 53:870, 2011).
    • Increased risk of occult colorectal cancer with bacteremia by other organisms as well: i.e., Bacteroides sp.,Clostridium species, and Fusobacterium sp. (Gastroenterology doi: 10.1053/j.gastro.2018.04.028)
  • Guidelines: American Heart Association (AHA): Circulation 132:1435, 2015; European Society of Cardiology: Eur Heart J. 2015; 36:3075 and Eur Heart J 2023; 44:3948.
