Transplants, Candida Prevention

Clinical Setting

  • Hematological malignancy
    • Acute leukemia undergoing intensive induction or re-induction during neutropenia.
    • Autologous hematopoietic cell transplant recipients with mucositis pre-engraftment.
    • Allogeneic hematopoietic cell transplant (HCT) recipients until engraftment when mold-active prophylaxis is not felt to be indicated.
    • Patients with acute myelogenous leukemia and myelodysplastic syndrome with neutropenia and allogeneic hematopoietic cell transplant recipients with graft versus host disease should be considered for antifungal prophylaxis that includes activity against aspergillus, see Transplants, Aspergillosis Prevention.
  • Select solid organ transplant recipients (Clin Transplant 33: e13623, 2019)
    • Liver transplant recipients with ≥2 of the following risk factors: prolonged or repeat operation; retransplantation; renal failure; high transfusion requirement; choledocojejunostomy; or perioperative Candida colonization.
    • Pancreas transplant recipients with enteric drainage, vascular thrombosis, or postperfusion pancreatitis.
    • Small-bowel recipients with graft rejection or dysfunction, enhanced immunosuppression,
      anastomotic disruption, abdominal reoperation or multivisceral transplantation.
    • Lung and heart/lung transplant recipients should be considered for antifungal prophylaxis that includes activity against aspergillus, see Transplants, Aspergillosis Prevention.
  • IDSA Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 62:409, 2016

Etiologies

  • Candida albicans most common
  • Non-albicans Candida sp. can cause disease

Primary Regimens

  • Hematological malignancy where goal is prevention of candida infection (not aspergillus)
    • Fluconazole 400 mg po/IV q24h until recovery of neutropenia
  • High-risk liver, small-bowel, and pancreas transplant recipients:
    • Fluconazole 400 mg po q24h, duration varies by risk factors and which organ transplanted

Alternative Regimens

  • Caspofungin 70 mg IV loading dose, then 50 mg/day IV or Micafungin 150 mg/day IV or Anidulafungin 200 mg IV loading dose, then 100 mg/day IV
  • In prospective randomized study in liver transplant patients, Micafungin once daily found non-inferior to Fluconazole, Caspofungin and Liposomal Amphotericin B (Clin Infect Dis 60:997, 2015)
  • Itraconazole oral solution 200 mg po bid
  • Posaconazole oral suspension (different than delayed release tablets: 200 mg (5 ml) po tid with full meal and/or acidic carbonated drink (e.g., ginger ale)
  • Voriconazole 200 mg po/IV bid
  • Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg/day

Comments

  • Recommendations on prophylaxis against candida infections in patients with hematological malignancy can be found through the IDSA Clinical Practice Guidelines for the Use of Antimicrobial Agents in Neutropenic Patients with Cancer (Clin Infect Dis 52:427, 2011) and National Comprehensive Cancer Network Guidelines on Prevention and Treatment of Cancer-Related Infections, NCCN. Recommendations on prophylaxis against candida infections in solid organ transplant recipients are available through the American Society of Transplantation Guidelines on Candida in Solid Organ Transplant Recipients (Clin Transplant 33: e13623, 2019)
