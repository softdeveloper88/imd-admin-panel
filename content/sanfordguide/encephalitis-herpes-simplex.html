Encephalitis, Herpes simplex

Clinical Setting

  • Herpes simplex virus (HSV) Type 1 is most common cause of sporadic encephalitis.
  • Risk factor: use of natalizumab for treatment of multiple sclerosis or Crohn's disease: Clin Infect Dis 57:849, 2013.
  • Survival and recovery from neurological sequelae are related to mental status at time of initiation of therapy.
  • Early diagnosis and treatment imperative (N Engl J Med 371:68, 2014).

Diagnosis

  • Brain Biopsy (Gold Standard)
  • CSF HSV PCR (Replaced brain biopsy; ~98 - 99 % sensitivity and specificity.  If first test neg, repeat in 3 - 7 days.  Do not delay Rx)

Etiologies

  • Herpes simplex virus (HSV) (Type 1)
  • HSV (Type II) causes occasional cases

Primary Regimens

  • Adult: Acyclovir IV 10- 12.5 mg/kg IV (infuse over 1 hr) q8h x 14–21 days. 
    • Dose calculation in obese patients uncertain.
    • To lessen risk of nephrotoxicity with larger doses, seems reasonable to infuse each dose over >1 hour.
  • Pediatric: Acyclovir 10-15 mg/kg q8h in children age <12 years; 20 mg/kg q8h in neonatal herpes CNS disease

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Higher doses (up to 15 mg/kg q8h) have been used in adults but with little additional benefit (Review in Current ID Reports 19:13, 2017).

Comments

  • Mortality rate reduced from >70% to 19% with acyclovir therapy. 
  • Autoimmune encephalitis following successful treatment of HSV encephalitis (via N-Methyl-D-Aspartate (NMDA) receptor antibodies).  Initially thought to be relapse of HSV disease 1 - 7 weeks following initiation of Rx, but later found to be autoimmune phenomena. Diagnosis via detection of neuronal antibodies in CSF (antigen tests are negative).  Treat with systemic corticosteroids (preferred), IVIG, or plasma exchange.( Neurol Clin 39 (2021) 197–207; Lancet Neurology 17, 760, 2018)
  • Series report (106 treated adults) in J Clin VIrol 60:112, 2014
  • Excellent reviews: Neurologic Clin  35: 695, 2017. Curr Inf Dis Rep 19: 13, 2017
