Trizivir
Tradename: Abacavir, Lamivudine, Zidovudine combination tab

Usage and Dosing

  • Used as "triple nucleoside" therapy. Inferior efficacy compared to 2 nucleoside agents + a third drug (NNRTI or PI or INSTI). Should be used in special circumstances where 2 nucleoside analogs + one of these third drugs cannot be used (e.g., drug interaction).
  • Combination formulation:
    • Abacavir (ABC)
    • Lamivudine (3TC)
    • Zidovudine (ZDV)
  • Black Box warnings: ABC hypersensitivity reaction, ZDV hematologic toxicity, ZDV myopathy, lactic acidosis and severe hepatomegaly with steatosis, HBV exacerbation. Should not be used in those with HIV-HBV co-infection (resistance to 3TC within 20 weeks when used as 'mono therapy' for HBV).
  • Limited data for viral load >100,000 copies/mL. Not recommended as initial therapy because of inferior virologic efficacy.
  • Screen for HLA-B*5701 allele prior to initiating therapy.
  • Available as a generic formulation.
  • 2024 IAS-USA recommendation: Patients with, or at high risk for, cardiovascular disease who are receiving an abacavir-containing regimen should switch to a non-abacavir-containing regimen if an active regimen is available (JAMA 2024 Dec 1. doi: 10.1001/jama.2024.24543 [online ahead of print].
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • See individual components for drug-specific data. For class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.

      Adverse Effects

      • Serious, sometimes fatal hypersensitivity reactions.
      • Hematologic toxicity, including neutropenia and anemia.
      • Symptomatic myopathy.
      • Lactic acidosis and severe hepatomegaly with steatosis.
      • Exacerbations of hepatitis B.
      • Hepatic decompensation in patients co-infected with HIV and HCV.
      • Immune reconstitution syndrome (IRIS).
      • Lipoatrophy.
      • See individual components for more information.
