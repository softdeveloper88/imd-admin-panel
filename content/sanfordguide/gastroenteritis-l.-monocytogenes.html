Listeria monocytogenes, Gastroenteritis

Clinical Setting

  • Listeria is a cause of food-associated febrile gastroenteritis.
    • Usually self-limited.
    • Not detected in standard stool cultures.
    • Often unrecognized.
  • Listeria also cause of neonatal sepsis, peripartum bacteremia, meningitis, endocarditis and systemic therapy is required.

Etiologies

  • Gram positive bacilli
    • Listeria monocytogenes

Primary Regimens

  • Value of oral antibiotics is unknown and infection is self-limited (Clin Infect Dis 40:1327, 2005)
  • Ampicillin (500 mg qid x 5 days) OR TMP-SMX (160-800 mg po bid x 5 days) might be reasonable in populations at increased risk of severe systemic disease: pregnant women, neonates, the elderly, and immunocompromised hosts (MMWR 57:1097, 2008).

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Risk of invasive disease not known.  
  • Reviews: StatPearls Publishing; 2024 Jan. and Br J Hosp Med (Lond). 2021;82:1.
