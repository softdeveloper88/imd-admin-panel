Oral Penicillin Desensitization

Overview

  • Oral Penicillin VK desensitization is preferred over parenteral desensitization. 1/3 of patients will develop a transient reaction, usually mild, during desensitization or treatment.
  • For parenteral method, see Parenteral Penicillin G Desensitization.
  • See also, Penicillin (Beta Lactam) Allergy Overview.
  • Perform in ICU setting. Discontinue all β-blockers. Have IV line, epinephrine, ECG, and spirometer available.
  • Prepare dilutions using Penicillin VK oral solution, 250 mg/5 mL. Administer each dose at 15 minute intervals in 30 mL water or flavored beverage.
  • After step 14 is completed, observe patient for 30 minutes and then administer full therapeutic dose by route of choice.
  • Desensitization works as long as patient is receiving drug; allergy returns shortly after drug is discontinued. A history of Stevens-Johnson syndrome, exfoliative dermatitis, or erythroderma are absolute contraindications to desensitization.
  • Note regarding Penicillin skin testing for evaluation of Penicillin allergy:
    • Testing with major determinant (Benzylpenicilloyl Polylysine) and minor determinants has negative predictive value of 97-99%. Risk of systemic reaction to skin testing <1%. See Ann Allergy, Asthma & Immunol 106:1, 2011.
  • References: Clin Infect Dis 35:26, 2002; Am J Med 121:572, 2008; Curr Clin Top Infect Dis 13:131, 1993.
