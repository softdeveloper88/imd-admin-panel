Tradename: Alinia

Usage and Dosing

  • Nitazoxanide is primarily used for its activity vs protozoan parasites: Cryptosporidium and Giardia.
    • Primary therapy in HIV is immune reconstitution.
  • Tablets and pediatric suspension available.
  • Active metabolites are tizoxanide and tizoxanide glucuronide; the parent compound is not detectable in plasma.
  • No convincing evidence of clinical efficacy against COVID-19.

Adult Dose

Cryptosporidium or Giardia500 mg po with food bid x 3 days
Crytposporidum with HIV

Nitazoxanide 500 to 1,000 mg po bid x 14 days plus optimized ART

No data to support use in immunocompromised subjects

Adverse Effects

  • Low incidence; similar to placebo.
  • Abdominal pain 7.8%, diarrhea 2.1%, discolored eyes and urine.
    • Review: Clin Infect Dis 40:1173, 2005; Expert Opin Pharmacother 7:953, 2006. Headaches; rarely yellow sclera (resolves after treatment).

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Category B. Usage data in pregnant women are limited, and embryo-fetal risk is unknown. Use if benefit outweighs risk.
Use during Lactation Breast milk excretion unknown. Use with caution.

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • Cryptosporidium parvum, hominis
  • Giardia duodenalis
  • Fasciola hepatica (refractory)
  • Many poorly controlled studies for other helminths and protozoa, none sufficient to recommend use.

Major Drug Interactions

  • None reported

Comments

  • Use in children with Cryptosporidium infections: Clin Infect Dis 40:1173, 2005.
  • Though FDA approved for Giardia, scant RCT proof of efficacy: Cochrane Database Syst Rev 12:CD007787, 2012.
  • Scant data on amebiasis: Trans R Soc Trop Med Hyg 101:1025, 2007.
  • Use suspension with caution in diabetics - contains 1.5 gm sucrose.
  • Non-controlled mostly scant studies showing some activity against many parasitic and viral pathogens.
  • Prolonged therapy in unreconstituted AIDS patients but antiretrovirals needed to stop diarrhea
