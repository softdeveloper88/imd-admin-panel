Epiglottitis, Supraglottitis

Clinical Setting

  • Inflammation of the epiglottis and surrounding structures.
  • Primarily due to infection but caustic ingestion and foreign body can cause epiglottitis
  • Can progress rapidly to airway obstruction
  • Have tracheostomy set “at bedside” due to risk of airway obstruction.
  • Young children may present with drooling, anxiety and sniffing posture (leaning forward, neck extended)
  • Older children and adults complain of severe sore throat, difficulty swallowing, drooling, hoarse voice
  • Presence of Stridor raises likelihood but may be absent

Etiologies

  • Immunocompetent children and adults:
    • Haemophilous influenzae type B (Once predominant cause, now rare in vaccinated populations)
    • Beta hemolytic streptococci (Groups A, B, C,F, G)
    • Staphylococcus. aureus (including MRSA)
    • Streptococcus pneumoniae
    • Other H. influenzae capsular types and non-typeable strains
    • Neisseria meningitidis
    • Pasteurella multocida
    • Moraxella catarrhalis
    • Rarely coliforms: e.g., Enterobacter sp., E. coli, Klebsiella sp.
    • Variety of viruses: Respiratory (influenza, parainfluenza), H. simplex, Varicella zoster, EBV, SARS-CoV-2
  • Immunocompromised children and adults:
    • Pseudomonas. aeruginosa
    • Candida sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Culture blood and airway secretions
  • Main objective is airway management. Involve ENT or anesthesia immediately if possible. Video laryngoscope should be used if attempting intubation. Tracheostomy or cricothyroidotomy may be needed if intubation fails
  • Pediatric: Ceftriaxone 75 mg/kg IV q24h or ( cefotaxime where available) + Vancomycin 60- 80 mg/kg IV divided 3-4 times a day to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 10-15 μg/mL; see Comments).
  • Adult: Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h) or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q4–8h (where available) + Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL)
  • Variable duration. Continue therapy until sufficient resolution of airway edema and endotracheal tube safely removed.

Alternative Regimens

  • For severe IgE-mediated Penicillin allergy
    • Pediatric: Levofloxacin (see Comment) 16-20 mg/kg/day (divided q12h, max 750 mg/day) + Clindamycin 7.5 mg/kg IV q6h (see Comment)
    • Adult: Levofloxacin 750 mg IV q24h + Clindamycin 600-900 mg IV q6-8h

Comments

  • Note traditional Vancomycin dosing of 45-60 mg/kg/day frequently does not achieve target AUC in term infants and older children with normal renal function. Use of AUC24  closer to 400 μg/mL adequate for most non-CNS infections. Clin Infect Dis Jul 13 2020
  • Reference:  Emerg Med Clin North Am 2021; 39:661
