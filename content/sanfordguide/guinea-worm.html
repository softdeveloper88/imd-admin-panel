Dracunculus medinensis, Guinea Worm

Clinical Setting

  • Dracunculus medinensis (Guinea worm) infection
  • Transmitted by minute copepods in contaminated drinking water
  • Subcutaneous roundworm with blisters that discharge larvae into environment on contact with water

Etiologies

  • Dracunculus medinensis

Primary Regimens

  • The emerging worm can be removed by manual traction: rolling it up on a stick or gauze a few centimeters each day. Complete removal requires approximately 4 weeks on average.
    • Topical antibiotics and gauze for emergence site daily
  • No effective antimicrobial therapy

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Oral analgesics, anti-inflammatory drugs, topical antiseptics/antibiotic ointments to alleviate symptoms and facilitate worm removal by gentle manual traction over several days.
  • Near eradication: 13 human cases worldwide in 2023.  Chad, South Sudan, Mali, Cameroon.
  • However, D. medinensis in domestic dogs has emerged
    • 713 global cases in 2023 and now outnumber human infections (Chad, Ethiopia, Mali, Cameroon).
