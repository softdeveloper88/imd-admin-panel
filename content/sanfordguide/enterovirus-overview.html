Enterovirus, Overview

Introduction

  • Enteroviruses are members of the picornavirus family. Enteroviruses include multiple clinically important subgroups
    • Enterovirus
      • Further differentiated into Enterovirus A-H. Examples include enterovirus D68 (EV-D68) associated with severe respiratory disease and enterovirus 71 associated with hand foot and mouth disease and outbreaks of severe neurologic disease.
    • Poliovirus: PV1, PV2, PV3
    • Coxsackie virus: A, B
    • Echovirus: 1-33
    • Rhinovirus: HRV-A, HRV-B, HRV-CRh
    • Human parechovirus (HPeV)

Clinical Syndromes

  • Broad range of syndromes. Some association of enterovirus types with disease types but great deal of overlap
    • Asymptomatic infection
    • Non-specific febrile illness
    • Sepsis in neonates
    • Conjunctivitis
    • Herpangina
    • Hand, foot and mouth disease
    • Pharyngitis
    • Pericarditis and myocarditis
    • Pleuritis (Bornholm's disease is epidemic pleurodynia)
    • Encephalitis
    • Meningitis
    • Myelitis
    • Polio

Treatment

  • No approved therapy currently available
  • IVIG has been used, but data are limited, for:
    • Neonatal sepsis
    • Myocarditis
    • Enterovirus D-68
    • Enterovirus 71
