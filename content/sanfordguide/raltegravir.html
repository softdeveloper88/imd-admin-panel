Raltegravir
Tradename: Isentress

Usage and Dosing

  • Raltegravir (RAL) is an integrase strand transfer inhibitor (INSTI) indicated for use as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually combined with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents (e.g., TDF-FTC or ABC-3TC). Considered a primary regimen option for naive patients; used in treatment experienced patients when not used previously or when the resistance test indicates susceptibility to Raltegravir in those patients who previously have taken the drug.
  • Nausea, diarrhea, headache, fever similar to placebo. CK increase & rhabdomyolysis reported, with unclear relationship to drug.
  • Watch for increased depression in those with a history of depression.
  • Low genetic barrier to resistance.
  • The various formulations of Raltegravir are not interchangeable. Do not substitute the chewable tablets or oral suspension for the film-coated tablets (see Pediatric dosing charts below).
  • To prepare and administer a chewable tablet as a crushed tablet: Place in a small cup. For each tablet, add 5 mL of liquid (water, juice, or breast milk). Within two minutes the tablet(s) will absorb the liquid and fall apart. Crush any remaining tablet pieces with a spoon, administer immediately.
  • Do not shake the oral suspension. Dose should be administered within 30 minutes of mixing, discard remainder.
  • Once-daily dosing should not be used in pregnancy until more data are available.

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Hypersensitivity can occur. Rash & Stevens-Johnson syndrome reported post-marketing. ↑CK and rhabdomyolysis reported (AIDS 22:1382, 2008). ↑ of preexisting depression reported in 4 patients; all could continue Raltegravir after adjustment of psychiatric meds (AIDS 22:1890, 2008).
  • Most Common:
    • Diarrhea, headache, nausea. LFT ↑ may be more common in patients co-infected with HBV or HCV.
  • Chewable tablets contain phenylalanine, which can be harmful to patients with phenylketonuria,
