Cimduo, Temixys
Tradename: Lamivudine + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate

Usage and Dosing

  • Cimduo and Temixys are a two-drug combination of lamivudine (3TC) and tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF) for use in combination with another antiretroviral agent (INSTI, PI, or NNRTI) for the treatment of HIV-1 infection.
  • Can be thought of as a generic version of Truvada (substituting 3TC for FTC). Not formally approved for use as PreEP, though would likely work.
  • Black box warning: Severe acute exacerbations of hepatitis B have been reported in patients co-infected with HBV and HIV-1 who have discontinued 3TC and TDF. Monitor liver function in these patients carefully. Anti-HBV therapy may be appropriate.

Adult Dose

  • Take one tablet daily, with or without food
  • Tablets: 3TC 300 mg + TDF 300 mg

      Adverse Effects

      • Lamivudine is generally well tolerated. Adverse effects possibly or probably associated:
        • Anaphylaxis, pruritus, urticaria
        • Anemia
        • Hepatic steatosis
        • Hyperglycemia
        • Lactic acidosis
        • Muscle weakness, CPK elevation, rhabdomyolysis
        • Redistribution/accumulation of body fat
      • Tenofovir is also well tolerated. Adverse effects possibly or probably associated:
        • Angioedema, dyspnea
        • GI (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)
        • Hepatic steatosis, hepatitis, increased LFTs
        • Hypokalemia, hypophosphatemia
        • Lactic acidosis
        • Osteomalacia
        • Pancreatitis, increased amylase, abdominal pain
        • Rash
        • Renal injury (renal failure, ATN, Fanconi syndrome, interstitial nephritis, nephrogenic diabetes insipidus, proteinuria, polyuria)
        • Rhabdomyolysis
