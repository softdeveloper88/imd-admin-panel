Salmonella, Overview

Overview

  • Aerobic gram negative bacilli.
  • Causes gastroenteritis, which is often self-limited (especially non-typhoidal Salmonella), and does not require treatment
  • Also associated with febrile systemic bacterial infections, including typhoid fever (Salmonella enterica Serotype Typhi and Serotype Paratyphi).
  • Usually transmitted by ingestion of contaminated water or food.
  • Invasive Salmonella disease often associated with immunocompromise (HIV, cancer), higher gastric pH, extremes of age (very young or old), sickle cell, liver disease.
  • Typhoid fever seen mostly in developing countries. In high prevalence, countries infection (and other enteric pathogens) is best avoided by:
    • drinking bottled, boiled or bottled carbonated water
    • avoid ice unless made from bottled or boiled water
    • no eating raw, unpeeled fruits and vegetables
    • eat cooked food that is still hot and steaming
    • take care in eating food from street vendors.
  • Increasing resistance to antibiotics throughout the world, so geographic patterns of susceptibility should guide treatment if needed.
  • Extensively resistant isolates described in South Asia, particularly India and Pakistan.
