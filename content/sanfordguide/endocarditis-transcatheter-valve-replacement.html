Endocarditis, Transcatheter Valve Replacement

Clinical Setting

  • Fever, positive blood cultures with organisms typical of prosthetic valve endocarditis or negative blood cultures otherwise meeting criteria for endocarditis.  See  Endocarditis Overview and Prosthetic Valve Endocarditis

Etiologies

  • Staphylococcus aureus
  • Enterococci
  • Coagulase-negative staphylococci
  • Streptococci 
  • Gram-negative bacilli (uncommon)
  • HACEK (rare)

Primary Regimens

  • Empirical therapy pending culture results: Same as Empirical Therapy for Prosthetic Valve Endocarditis
  • Links to recommendations for pathogen-directed therapy once culture and susceptibility results are available: Staphylococcus aureus, viridans group streptococci or S. gallolyticus, coagulase-negative staphylococci, enterococci

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Enterococcus faecalis is a leading cause of transcatheter aortic valve endocarditis (Clin Microbiol Rev. 2024; 37:e001682)
  • References: Clin Infect Dis 2024; 78:179, Curr Cardiol Rep. 2024; 26:767
