Cobicistat
Tradename: Tybost

Usage and Dosing

  • Cobicistat (Tybost) is an inhibitor of CYP3A, CYP2D6, and multiple transporters. Thus, it increases systemic exposure of many drugs.
  • Cobicistat is FDA-approved for co-administration with atazanavir (ATV) or darunavir (DRV) so as to increase serum levels as part of multidrug therapy of HIV. Not interchangeable with ritonavir.
  • Cobicistat is one of the constituents of Evotaz, Genvoya, Prezcobix, Stribild, and Symtuza, and hence should not be used alone in patients treated with one of these combination products. In Genvoya and Stribild, cobicistat enhances the systemic exposure of elvitegravir (EVG), a CYP3A4 substrate.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see Major Drug Interactions chart below, not all-inclusive).

Adult Dose

Usual Dose:
150 mg po q24h (with Atazanavir 300 mg po q24h OR Darunavir 800 mg po q24h)
Take with food (including all cobicistat-containing combinations)

        Adverse Effects

        • Cobicistat increases the serum creatinine up to 0.4 mg/dL by competing for tubular secretion. The glomerular filtration rate is not affected. However, the estimated Creatinine Clearance calculation, using the serum creatinine, will show a decrease. Consider this effect when cobicistat is used concomitantly with tenofovir; it is appropriate to monitor renal function closely in tenofovir-treated patients.
        • In controlled clinical trials, 5% or less of the patients exposed to cobicistat experienced rash, jaundice, or nausea.

        Pregnancy Risk

        FDA Risk Category 1 Use of COBI-boosted ATV, DRV, or EVG not recommended in pregnancy (HHS Jan 2024)
