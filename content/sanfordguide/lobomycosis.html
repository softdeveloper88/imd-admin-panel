Lobomycosis

Clinical Setting

  • Lobomycosis or keloidal blastomycosis.
  • Chronic nodular or keloidal skin lesions caused by a fungus endemic in the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America.
  • No systemic involvement.

Etiologies

  • Lacazia loboi

Primary Regimens

  • Surgical excision

Alternative Regimens

  • Anecdotal reports of successful therapy with Clofazimine 100 mg/day with or without Itraconazole 100 mg/day.
  • Posaconazole was effective in a single case report (Am J Trop Med Hyg 88:1217, 2013).

Comments

  • Reference: Actas Dermosifiliogr 100 (Suppl 1):92, 2009. Ther Clin Risk Manag 10:851, 2014.)
