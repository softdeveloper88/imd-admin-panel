Endophthalmitis, Post-traumatic

Clinical Setting

  • Endophthalmitis following penetrating trauma to the eye.

Etiologies

  • Bacillus sp.
  • Staphylococci
  • Gram-negative bacilli
  • Streptococci
  • Fungi

Primary Regimens

  • Ophthalmology consultation as soon as possible
  • Intravitreal Vancomycin 1 mg + (Ceftazidime 2.25 mg or Amikacin 0.4 mg) + systemic Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator), alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL  + [(Ceftazidime 1 gm IV q8h or Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV/po q12h].
  • Vitrectomy often necessary.

Alternative Regimens

  • For fungal endophthalmitis: Intravitreal Amphotericin B 5 μg OR Voriconazole 100 μg + systemic Fluconazole 400 mg IV/po once daily or Voriconazole 400 mg q12h x 2 doses then 200 mg po twice daily.
  • Topical antibiotics (Vancomycin 25 mg/mL + Ceftazidime 100 mg/mL hourly) or subconjunctival antibiotics (Vancomycin 25 mg + Ceftazidime 100 mg) often used in conjunction with intravitreal antibiotics post-operatively.

Comments

  • Consider prophylactic administration of systemic + intravitreal antibiotics in high risk injuries (open globe laceration, contaminated wound, > 24h delay in wound closure, intraocular foreign body).
