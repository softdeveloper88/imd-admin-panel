Myiasis

Clinical Setting

  • Maggot, furuncular myiasis. Larvae (botfly, tumbu fly, screw worm, warble fly) burrow into intact skin in travelers. Person often feels movement inside.
  • Usually cutaneous/subcutaneous nodule with central punctum
  • Caused by larvae of flies - ectoparasites

Etiologies

  • Botfly (Dermatobia hominis in Latin America) - usually single lesion
  • Tumbu fly (Cordylobia anthropophaga, rarely C. rhodhaini in Africa) -usually multiple lesions
    • Case series from Africa: PLoS Negl Trop Dis. 2024; 18(3):e0012027.  
  • Cochliomyia homnivorax, (New World screw worm) is very painful
  • Wohlfahrtia magnifica: BMC Microbiol. 2024; 24(1):3.
  • H. tarandi (the warble fly of reindeer and caribou) occurs in subarctic regions worldwide and may be migratory
  • Oestrus ovis causes ophthalmomyiasis, infection of the eye with fly larvae or rarely involves the nasopharyngeal cavity
  • Chrysomya bezziana infests human skin wounds and is highly invasive and painful (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 13: e0007391, 2019)
  • Lucilia sericata

Primary Regimens

  • Occlude punctum to prevent gas exchange: petrolatum, fingernail polish, makeup cream or an occlusive dressing. When larva migrates, manually remove.
  • Surgical removal if larvae doesn't emerge in 24 hours and for all invasive species

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Superinfection does not occur, removal sufficient.
  • Cochliomyia homnivorax  is endemic in Costa Rica, Cuba, Haiti, the Dominican Republic, and countries in South America.
  • Constant risk of introduction into the US. Although eradicated from humans in US. Infestation in a traveler is considered a a veterinary public health emergency due to livestock threat. 
    • Contact public health authorities immediately.
  • Extraction: Clin Infect Dis 35:336, 2002.
  • Comprehensive review: Clin Microbiol Rev 25:79, 2012.
