Fitzhugh-Curtis Syndrome

Clinical Setting

  • Infection of liver capsule and peritoneal surfaces around the liver (RUQ)
  • Perihepatitis, violin-string adhesions.
  • Sudden onset of severe RUQ pain, often with pleuritic component or referred to R shoulder
  • Transaminases are elevated in < 30% of cases
  • Associated with salpingitis

Etiologies

  • Chlamydia trachomatis
  • Neisseria gonorrhoeae

Primary Regimens

  • Treat as for Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

Alternative Regimens

  • None
