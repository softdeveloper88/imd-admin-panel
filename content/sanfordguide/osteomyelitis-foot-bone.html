Osteomyelitis, Foot Bone

Clinical Setting

  • Foot bone osteomyelitis caused by a puncture wound.
  • For diabetic patient also see Diabetic Foot.

Etiologies

  • P. aeruginosa (non-diabetics, children)
  • S. aureus and mixed infection (diabetics)

Primary Regimens

  • Empiric therapy not recommended: definitive therapy depends on results of bone culture.

Alternative Regimens

  • As above.

Comments

  • Case series of microbiology of puncture wound soft-tissue and bone infections of the foot found that Pseudomonas aeruginosa was the predominant pathogen in non-diabetics versus S. aureus in diabetics. Polymicrobial infections were common, 83% in diabetics and 53% in non-diabetics (J Am Podiatr Med Assoc. 2020 Nov 2;20-206)
  • Debridement may be needed to remove foreign body if infection is trauma or puncture-wound related.
  • Duration of therapy ill-defined but 6-8 weeks has been typically recommended.
    • Limited data suggest that outcomes in diabetic foot infection with 3 weeks of therapy following surgical debridement are comparable to the more traditional 6-week duration (Clin Infect Dis. 2021; 73:e1539-e1545).
  • Check patient's tetanus prophylaxis status.
  • See also: puncture wounds.
