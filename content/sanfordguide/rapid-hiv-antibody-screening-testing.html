Rapid HIV Antibody Screening, Testing

Rapid HIV Antibody Tests

  • High sensitivity and specificity for chronic HIV infections (see Table below)
    • For use when need for rapid results; prefer CDC endorsed diagnostic ALGORITHM
    • Definition of rapid tests: antibody test results are available within 30 minutes.
    • For general reference, see Clin Infect Dis 52:257, 2011; Clin Infect Dis 45:S221, 2007.
    • Positive rapid antibody test results must be confirmed using either an EIA or Western Blot.
  • Clinical uses:
    • Patient in labor, no prenatal HIV test
    • Patient who is source of needlestick injury to health care worker
    • Evaluation of acutely ill patient with possible PCP (Pneumocystis jiroveci pneumonia)
    • Patients who are unlikely to return for test results
  • Precautions
    • Rapid antibody tests may miss acute HIV infections
    • Reports suggest that rapid tests using saliva may be less sensitive than rapid tests using finger stick blood samples
