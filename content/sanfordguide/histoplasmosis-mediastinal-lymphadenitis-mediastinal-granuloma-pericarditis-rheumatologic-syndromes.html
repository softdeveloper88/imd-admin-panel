Histoplasmosis, Mediastinal

Clinical Setting

  • These complications of histoplasmosis infection are due to an exuberant immune response to the infection and not associated with a high burden of organisms. Patients are often culture and antigen negative.
    • Mediastinal lymphadenitis often presents as chest pain, dysphagia and/or cough due to bronchial or esophageal compression.
    • Mediastinal granulomas are often asymptomatic but may present with compressive symptoms.
    • Mediastinal fibrosis may present as occlusion of airways or vessels.

Etiologies

  • Histoplasma capsulatum

Primary Regimens

  • Mediastinal lymphadenitis:
    • Asymptomatic or mild symptoms < 1 month: no treatment
    • Mild symptoms > 1 month:
      • Itraconazole 200 mg tid for 3 days then bid for 6-12 weeks
    • Severe cases with obstruction or compression of vital structures:
      • Itraconazole (dosing and duration as above) + Prednisone 0.5-1 mg/kg/day tapered over 1-2 weeks
  • Mediastinal granuloma:
    • Asymptomatic: no treatment
    • Symptomatic: Itraconazole (dosing and duration as above)
  • Mediastinal fibrosis:
    • Many experts believe that neither antifungal therapy nor steroids are helpful
    • Consider stenting of affected structures (Medicine (Baltimore) 90:412, 2011)

Alternative Regimens

  • If itraconazole is not an option for the patient as above, may consider Posaconazole

Comments
