Diagnosis of Primary/Acute HIV Infection

Primary (Acute) HIV Infection

  • Clinical picture is non-specific. Usually described as an acute mononucleosis syndrome: i.e., fever, lymphadenopathy, sore throat, and rash. Think Primary HIV infection when mono spot tests are negative.
  • Can present as  aseptic meningitis.
  • Important to recognize as  patients have very high viral loads and pose significant risk of transmission of HIV to others.
  • Diagnosis: HIV RT-PCR

Diagnostic Tests

  • Very high level of circulating virus as measured by RT-PCR. Typically, >100,000 copies/mL; values of <2000 copies/mL suggest a false positive result
  • p24 antigen levels also very high
  • HIV antibody tests are negative
  • Subsequently, need to check for presence of drug-resistant genotypes
  • Guidelines: clinicalinfo.HIV.gov
