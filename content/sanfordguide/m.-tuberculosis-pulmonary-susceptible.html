Tuberculosis, Pulmonary, Susceptible

Clinical Setting

  • Active pulmonary M. tuberculosis: Isolate susceptible to Isoniazid, Rifampin, and first-line agents.
  • In patients with newly diagnosed HIV and pulmonary TB, therapy for both should be started as soon as feasible (N Eng J Med 362:697, 2010).
  • Treatment consists of
    • Initial phase (2 months)
    • Continuation phase of 4-7 months, longer duration for cavitary disease on initial  chest radiograph AND positive culture at end of the 2-month continuation phase).
      • Other factors to weigh when considering whether to prolong the continuation phase to 7 months are: either cavitation on initial chest radiograph OR positive culture at 2 months, smoker, weight less than 90% of ideal body weight, diabetes, HIV infection or other immunosuppression, or extensive disease on chest radiograph.
    • Directly observed therapy (DOT) regimens are preferable.
    • 2x weekly and 3x weekly regimens must be DOT.
  • Isolation is essential!
    • Isolation may be discontinued after 5 days of therapy in smear-negative cases.
    • Isolation may be discontinued after 3 consecutive negative smears after 2 weeks or more of therapy in smear-positive cases.
    • Hospitalized patients with suspected or documented active TB should be isolated in single rooms using airborne precautions until deemed non-infectious.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis

    • Children and adolescents who do not meet criteria for non-severe TB should receive the standard six-month treatment regimen

Alternative Regimens

  • Can substitute Rifabutin 5 mg/kg/d (max dose 300 mg) 5-7x weekly for rifampin but not recommended on twice-, or thrice-weekly dosing schedule
  • A four-month regimen of Isoniazid + Rifampin + Pyrazinamide + Ethambutol for 2 months followed by Isoniazid + Rifampin for 2 months may be used for treatment of adults with culture-negative pulmonary TB (i.e., compatible chest x-ray findings but at least 3 negative sputum cultures)
  • Four-month regimen of Rifapentine 1,200 mg once daily for 17 weeks + Isoniazid 300 mg once daily for 17 weeks + Moxifloxacin 400 mg once daily for 17 weeks +  Pyrazinamide (40 to < 55 kg: 1000 mg; > 55 kg to 75 kg: 1,500 mg; > 75 kg: 2000 mg) once daily for the first 8 weeks  (N Engl J Med 2021; 384:1705-18).
    • Dosed 5 of 7 days with DOT (directly observed therapy)
    • Age > 12 years old
    • TB susceptible to Isoniazid, rifamycins, and fluoroquinolones
    • HIV+ patients with CD4 >100 cells/mm3 were included.
    • Pregnancy was an exclusion.
    • Weight < 40 kg an exclusion

Prevention

  • See BCG, Vaccine for indications, available products, dosing, and vaccine characteristics for pre-exposure prevention.
    • BCG is a consideration in only very limited situations

Comments

  • Thrice weekly therapy for both the initial and continuation phase and twice weekly therapy in the continuation phase have higher rates of relapse (Incidence Rate Ratio = 1.8 to 2.0), microbiological failure (IRR = 3.0 to 3.7), and/or acquired drug resistance (IRR= 10 for thrice weekly therapy in both initial and continuation phase) compared to daily therapy (Clin Infect Dis 64:1211, 2017).
  • ATS, IDSA, CDC treatment guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 2016; 63:e147 and American Journal of Respiratory and Critical Care Medicine, Volume 211, Issue 1 (January 2025)
  • For suspected treatment failure:
    • Assure treatment adherence (e.g., establish DOT if not already done), lack of which is the most likely explanation;
    • Assay of serum drug concentrations (therapeutic drug monitoring, TDM) to assess adequacy of drug exposure when there is poor response to treatment despite treatment adherence, in presence of gastrointestinal disease that may impair drug absorption, to monitor drug-drug interactions, in setting of impaired renal clearance of drug.  Although not routinely recommended, TDM may be helpful if there is a delayed response to treatment in patients with HIV infection or diabetes, which may also be associated with lower than anticipated serum drug concentrations.
  • A randomized controlled trial of bedaquiline–linezolid plus isoniazid, ethambutol, and pyrazinamide for rifampin-susceptible pulmonary tuberculosis for which duration of therapy was determined based on clinical and microbiologic response and which averaged 85 days, found this regimen to be non-inferior to the standard 6-month regimen of isoniazid, rifampin, pyrazinamide, and ethambutol (N Engl J Med. 2023; 388:873).  The eventual role of this promising treatment strategy, which allows for significantly shorter durations of therapy awaits public heath guidance from CDC and WHO.
