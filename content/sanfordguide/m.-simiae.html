Mycobacterium simiae

Clinical Setting

  • Infrequent cause of pulmonary infection, even if isolated in culture, associated with pseudo-outbreaks.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium simiae complex, also includes:
    • Mycobacterium sherrisii
    • Mycobacterium lentiflavum
    • Mycobacterium triplex
    • Mycobacterium heidelbergense
    • Mycobacterium palustre

Primary Regimens

  • Not defined, choice should be guided by drug susceptibility test results. Consider including Amikacin in the regimen for cavitary or severe disease
  • Azithromycin 500 mg once daily + Moxifloxacin 400 mg once daily + one or two other drugs based on drug susceptibility testing (e.g., Rifampin 600 mg once daily, Ethambutol 15 mg/kg once daily, Clofazimine 50 mg po once daily, Amikacin 15 mg/kg IM/IV once daily, TMP-SMX 160/800 mg po bid) 
  • Treat for 12 months after conversion of cultures to negative.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • See Lancet Infect Dis 2022; 22: e178-90 and Chest. 2023; 163:281 for discussion of treatment options.
  • Isolates are often not clinically significant.
  • Most isolates resistant to all first-line anti-tuberculosis drugs and among most most resistant of all mycobacterial species.
  • Bedaquiline has good in vitro activity but role in treatment unknown (J Med Microbiol. 2019;68:1137-1139).
