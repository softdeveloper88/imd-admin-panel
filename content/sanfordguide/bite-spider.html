Bite: Spider

Clinical Setting

  • Bite wound from spiders.
  • Most necrotic ulcers attributed to spiders are probably due to another cause, e.g., cutaneous anthrax (Lancet 364:549, 2004) or Staph. aureus (MRSA) infection. Infection associated with spider bites is overdiagnosed.
  • Spider bite is painful; cutaneous anthrax infection is not painful. (Reference: Lancet 364:549, 2004).
  • Black widow bite may be confused with "acute abdomen".
  • Brown recluse spider habitat limited to the United States (South Central and desert Southwest states).
    • NOT RECLUSE Mnemonic to prevent over-diagnosis (JAMA Dermatol 153: 377, 2017).

Etiologies

  • Not infectious

Primary Regimens

  • Black widow (Latrodectus):
    • No antibiotic therapy indicated
    • Diazepam or calcium gluconate is helpful to control pain, muscle spasm.
    • Evaluate need for tetanus prophylaxis.
  • Brown recluse (Loxosceles):
    • Bite usually self-limited and self-healing.
    • No therapy of proven efficacy.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Brown recluse spider bite:
    • Dapsone 50 mg po q24h is often used despite marginal supportive data.
    • Dapsone can cause hemolysis (check for G6PD deficiency). Can cause hepatitis; baseline and weekly liver panels suggested.
  • Reference on brown recluse spiders and suspected necrotic arachnidism, N Engl J Med 352:700, 2005.
