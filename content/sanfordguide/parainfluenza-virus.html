Parainfluenza virus

Clinical Setting

  • URI, bronchiolitis, croup, viral pneumonia in children.
  • URI, bronchitis, viral pneumonia, exacerbation of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and cardiovascular disease (CVD) in older adults Clin Infect Dis 2021. Dec 6;73(11):e4433. For clinical characteristics in hospitalized adults, see Clin Infect Dis. 2019 Jan 7;68(2):298-305
  • Severe pneumonia in transplant patients Blood 119:2738, 2012.
  • More common in fall (parainfluenza 1) and spring (parainfluenza 3).

Etiologies

  • Parainfluenza virus type 1, 2, 3, and 4
  • Less known about parainfluenza 4 and only a few diagnostic panels detect it

Primary Regimens

  • Supportive therapy. See Bronchiolitis

Alternative Regimens

  • Intravenous immune globulin has been used for transplant patients with severe disease due to parainfluenza virus
  • Intravenous or inhaled Ribavirin (usually with IVIG) has been used for transplant patients with severe disease due to parainfluenza virus but only small uncontrolled series

Comments
