Syphilis, Secondary

Clinical Setting

  • Fever, papulosquamous rash often involving palms of hands and soles of feet, aseptic meningitis.
  • References: JAMA 312:1905, 2014; Clin Infect Dis 2015 61:S818.; Lancet 2017 389:1550 and N Engl J Med. 2020;382:845

Etiologies

  • Treponema pallidum

Primary Regimens

  • Benzathine Penicillin G (Bicillin L-A) 2.4 million units IM x 1 dose

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x 14 days or Tetracycline 500 mg po qid x 14 days
  • Ceftriaxone 1 gm IM/IV q24h x 10 -14 days though exact duration and dose unclear
  • European 2020 Syphilis Guidelines recommend Procaine Penicillin 600,000 units IM daily for 10–14 days, if benzathine Penicillin G not available. 
  • Azithromycin 2 gm po single dose no longer recommended due to increasing resistance.
  • Follow-up is mandatory.
  • Pregnancy: all should be treated with Penicillin; If penicillin-allergic, the patient should be desensitized. See Methods for Penicillin Desensitization.
  • In some countries, benzathine Penicillin G is not available and shortages are increasingly common.
    • Consider high dose Amoxicillin 3 gm/day with probenecid (750 mg/day) po given tid in divided doses x 2 weeks (early syphilis) x 4 weeks (late latent or unknown duration) based on Japanese experience in HIV-infected individuals (efficacy 95.5%, 273/286 patients; Clin Infect Dis 61:177, 2015).
    • Another alternative is Amoxicillin 1.5 g/day is better tolerated than above Amoxicillin/Probenicid combination with similar efficacy (94.9%) Sex Transm Infect 98:173 2022

Comments

  • Test all patients with syphilis for HIV and all HIV patients for syphilis.
  • Perform quantitative nontreptonemal test (VDRL or RPR) at 0, 3, 6, 12 and 24 months after treatment.
    • If primary or secondary syphilis, VDRL should decrease 2 tubes (i.e. 4 fold) at 6 months, 3 tubes at 12 months and 4 tubes in 24 months.
  • Current 2021 CDC recommendations no longer recommend to examine CSF if titers fail to fall 4-fold, rather to follow titers and neurological exam. 
  • Secondary syphilis as the great imitator: e.g., cervical lymphadenitis Clin Infect Dis 58: 1577, 2014.
  • Reviews: 2021 CDC STD guidelines MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021; European 2020 Syphilis Guidelines and N Engl J Med. 2020;382:845
  • Review of evidence for CDC guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 74:S127 2022
