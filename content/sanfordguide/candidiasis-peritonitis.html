Candidiasis, Peritonitis

Clinical Setting

  • Peritonitis with positive peritoneal fluid culture for Candida.
  • Associated with chronic ambulatory peritoneal dialysis (CAPD).
    • Cloudy peritoneal dialysis fluid with abdominal pain.
    • Dialysate fluid positive for Candida.

Etiologies

  • Candida albicans
  • Other non-albicans Candida sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Removal of the peritoneal catheter AND
  • Caspofungin 70 mg IV on day 1, then 50 mg IV q24h OR Micafungin 100 mg q24h (empiric therapy until speciation and/or MICs available or if fluconazole-resistant species)
  • Fluconazole 200 mg po/IV q24h (fluconazole-susceptible species)

Alternative Regimens

  • Amphotericin B 0.7-1 mg/kg/day

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy not well established but treat for at least 10 days after catheter removal.

Comments

  • Remove peritoneal dialysis catheter immediately.
  • References: IDSA treatment guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 62:409, 2016
