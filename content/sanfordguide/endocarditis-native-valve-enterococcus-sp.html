Endocarditis, Enterococcus sp.

Clinical Setting

  • Specific therapy for enterococcal endocarditis: culture positive for E. faecalis or E. faecium.
  • Gram positive cocci, normal flora of the gastrointestinal tract.
  • It is important to obtain speciation of enterococcl
  • If Streptomycin or Gentamicin combination therapy is used document that MICs of Streptomycin or Gentamicin in the range that is predictive of in vitro synergy and monitor serum concentrations.
  • Infectious disease consultation advised if available.
  • Review of dual beta-lactam therapy: Clin Infect Dis 2018;67: 303

Etiologies

  • E. faecalis
  • E. faecium

Primary Regimens

SettingRegimensComments
Penicillin susceptible and synergy with Gentamicin positive
  • Ampicillin 12 gm/day IV divided q4h + Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h) x 6 weeks (E. faecalis only, efficacy against E. faecium not established)
  • Ampicillin 12 gm/day IV, divided q4h + Gentamicin 3 mg/kg/d IV x 4-6 weeks (6-weeks for patients with > 3 months of symptoms or for prosthetic valve infection)

Optimal dosing and duration of Gentamicin not well defined. 2015 American Heart Association (AHA) guidelines (Circ. 132:1435, 2015) recommend Gentamicin 3 mg/kg/d in 2-3 divided doses for 4-6 weeks. 2023 European Society of Cardiology (ECS) guidelines (Eur Heart J. 2023;44:3948) recommend 3 mg/kg once daily and for the first two weeks only and treating for a total of 6 weeks. Limited data suggest 2 of Gentamicin weeks  is similar in efficacy to 4 to 6 weeks and is less toxic (Circ. 2013;127:1810).

AHA guidelines recommend Penicillin G 24 million units divided q4h as a alternative to Ampicillin in combination with Gentamicin

See Stewardship for monitoring of Gentamicin serum concentrations,

Penicillin-resistant (MIC > 16 μg/mL) or patient is penicillin allergic but synergy with aminoglycoside (gentamicin) is positive (MIC ≤ 500 µg/mL):
  • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h + Gentamicin 3 mg/kg/d  x 6 weeks

Significant toxicity with this regimen: consider using a Daptomycin combination regimen (see Penicillin, vancomycin, and aminoglycoside resistance below)

2015 AHA guidelines recommend administration of Gentamicin in 3 divided doses; 2023 guidelines recommend once daily dosing. 

See Stewardship for monitoring of Gentamicin serum concentrations,

High-level gentamicin resistance (MIC > 500 μg/mL), E. faecalis

  • Ampicillin 12 gm/day IV divided q4h + Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h) x 6 weeks (Ann Intern Med 146:574, 2007; Clin Infect Dis 56:1261, 2013)
  • Less desirable but another option if susceptible to Streptomycin (MIC ≤ 1500 µg/mL)
    • Ampicillin 12 gm/day IV divided q4h + Streptomycin 15 mg/kg IV/IM in two divided doses x 4-6 weeks ((6-weeks for patients with > 3 months of symptoms or for prosthetic valve infection) OR
    • Penicillin G 24 million units/day IV divided q4h x 4-6 weeks + Streptomycin 15 mg/kg IV/IM in two divided doses x 4-6 weeks (6-weeks for patients with > 3 months of symptoms or for prosthetic valve infection) 

Efficacy of Ampicillin + Ceftriaxone not established for E. faecium and this combinatoin should not be used.

See Stewardship for monitoring of Streptomycin  serum concentrations,

High-level gentamicin resistance (MIC > 500 μg/mL), E. faecium
  • If Ampicillin susceptible:
    • Ampicillin 2 gm IV q4h or Penicillin G 4 million units IV q4h x 8-12 weeks. Cure rate only 50%.
  • If Ampicillin susceptible and susceptible to Streptomycin (MIC ≤ 1500 µg/mL):
    • Ampicillin 12 gm/day IV divided q4h + Streptomycin 15 mg/kg IV/IM in two divided doses x 4-6 weeks ((6-weeks for patients with > 3 months of symptoms or for prosthetic valve infection)

Reports of single patients successfully treated with combination of Daptomycin and Ampicillin: Antimicrob Ag Chemother 56:6064, 2012; Eur J Clin Micro Infect Dis 30:807, 2011

See Stewardship for monitoring of Streptomycin  serum concentrations,

Penicillin, vancomycin, and aminoglycoside resistance
  • Daptomycin 10-12 mg/kg IV q24h + (Ampicillin 2 gm IV q4h or Ceftaroline 600 mg IV q8h) (Curr Inf Dis Rep 16:431, 2014).
  • If no other alternative:
    • Linezolid 600 mg IV/po q12h ≥ 8 weeks.
Linezolid is bacteriostatic with high relapse rate. Avoid unless no other choice. Recommend addition of another active agent if possible.

    Alternative Regimens

    • For either E. faecalis or E. faecium, if unable to use penicillin or vancomycin:
      • Daptomycin 10-12 mg/kg IV q24h
      • Best combination is with a beta-lactam. If severe allergy precludes use of a beta-lactam, can try combination therapy with either rifampin or gentamicin

    Antimicrobial Stewardship

    • If Gentamcin or Streptomycin is used confirm that the MIC is within the range (Gentamcin MIC < 500 μg/m; Streptomycin MIC ≤ 1500 µg/mL) that predicts in vitro synergistic activity when combined with penicillin or ampicillin (and presumably with vancomycin or daptomycin as well). 
    • Gentamicin dosing recommendations (monitor at least weekly).
      • If Gentamicin is administered every q8 to q12h then the dose should be adjusted to achieve a peak serum concentration (peak defined as 30-60 minutes after a dose) of 3-4 µg/mL and a trough concentration (trough defined as just before the next dose)  of < 1 µg/mL.
      • If Gentamicin is administered as a once daily dose then the dose should be adjusted to achieve a peak serum concentration 10-12 µg/mL and a trough concentration of < 1 µg/mL.
    • Streptomycin dose should be adjusted to obtain a serum peak concentration of 20 to 35 μg/mL and a trough concentration of <10 μg/mL; monitor at least weekly.

    Comments

    • See Clin Infect Dis 2023;76:281 for review of outcomes and risk of relapse for E. faecalis endocarditis with various treatment. Relapses after treatment are frequent, often asymptomatic, and may occur more than 6 months after the initial episode.
    • Review of treatment options for vancomycin-resistant enterococci: Infect Dis Clin North Am. 2020; 34:751.
    • In national retrospective study, daptomycin doses ≥ 10 mg/kg  associated improved survival and microbiologic cures without muscle toxicity (Clin Infect Dis 2017;64:605)
    • Consider valve replacement if the strain is resistant to penicillin, vancomcyin, and aminoglycoside.
    • See American Heart Association guidelines (Circulation 132:1435, 2015) and European Society of Cardiology guidelines (Eur Heart J. 2023;44:3948).
