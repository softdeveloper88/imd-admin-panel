Trifluridine
Tradename: Trifluorothymidine, Viroptic

Usage and Dosing

  •  Trifluridine (trifluorothymidine) is used for Herpes simplex (HSV) keratoconjunctivitis or recurrent epithelial keratitis.
  • Available as 1% ophthalmic solution.

Adult Dose

  •  1 drop q2h (max. 9 drops/day) until corneal re-epithelialization, then dose is reduced for 7 more days (1 drop q4h for at least 5 drops/day), not to exceed 21 days total therapy.

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days) 1 drop q2h until corneal re-epithelialization, then 1 drop q4h x7 days (min 5 drops/day) 9
Max/Day 9 drops

Administer eyedrops while awake

Adverse Effects

  • Mild burning (5%), palpebral edema (3%), punctate keratopathy, stromal edema.
