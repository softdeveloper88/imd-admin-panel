Hordeolum, Stye

Clinical Setting

  • External hordeolum: infection of the superficial sebaceous gland (ciliary gland)
  • Internal hordeolum: infection of the meibomian glands; also called acute meibomianitis.
  • A chalazion is a non-infectious process with acute or chronic inflammation of the eyelid due to obstruction of oil glands.

Etiologies

  • Staph. aureus (MSSA or MRSA)

Primary Regimens

  • Warm compresses for 15 minutes 4x daily, will drain spontaneously

Alternative Regimens

  • Role of topical or systemic antibiotics unclear but may be indicated for significant surrounding erythema, concern for preseptal celllulitis
    • MSSA:  Dicloxacillin 500 mg po q6h
    • MRSA or penicilllin allergy or empiric therapy: TMP-SMX 5-10 mg/kg/d of TMP component twice daily

Comments

  • Reference: StatPearls; 2024 Jan. 2023 Aug 8.
  • For larger lesions or those unresponsive to conservation management referral to an ophthalmologist for incision and drainage is recommended.
