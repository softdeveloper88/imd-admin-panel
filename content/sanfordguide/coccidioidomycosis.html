Coccidioidomycosis, Pulmonary

Clinical Setting

  • Pulmonary syndromes:
    • Mild disease: usually self-resolving. Treatment is recommended for select patients (see below) or those with persistent signs and symptoms of infection.
    • Acute pneumonia:
        • Immunosuppressed patients: HIV infection, post-transplantation, hematological malignancies or immunosuppressive therapies (steroids, TNF-α antagonists, etc.)
        • Filipino or African ancestry
        • Pregnancy or immediately post-partum
        • Diabetes
        • Pre-existing cardiopulmonary disease
        • Complement fixation antibody titer ≥1:16
      • Severe/diffuse disease: Bilateral reticulonodular or miliary infiltrates can be seen.
    • Chronic nodular and/or cavitary disease
    • Chronic progressive fibrocavitary pneumonia
    • Evidence of delayed hypersensitivity may help in assessment of chronic disease and/or if question of past exposure. FDA-approved skin test is now available. Trade name: Spherusol

Etiologies

  • Coccidioides immitis

Primary Regimens

  • Acute pneumonia:
    • No risk factors for persistence or progression (see above), treatment not indicated.
    • Mild disease:
      • ≥ 1 risk factors present (see above) or persistence/progression of disease:
        • Fluconazole 400 mg po once daily x 3–12 months
        • Itraconazole oral solution 200 mg po bid x 3-12 months
    • Severe/diffuse pneumonia:
      • Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg IV daily
      • Fluconazole 800-1200 mg po once daily
  • Chronic nodular and/or cavitary disease:
    • Asymptomatic, immunocompetent host: no therapy indicated; serial imaging
    • Asymptomatic, immunocompromised host:
      • Fluconazole 400 mg po once daily
      • Itraconazole oral solution 200 mg po bid
    • Symptomatic or chronic progressive fibrocavitary pneumonia:
      • Fluconazole 400 mg po once daily
      • Itraconazole oral solution 200 mg po bid
      • Consider surgical resection for cavitary disease since azoles may not be curative

Alternative Regimens

  • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg po bid x 24h, then 4 mg/kg po bid
  • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs)
    • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily
    • Suspension 200 mg qid, then 400 mg po bid after stabilization of disease
    • Posaconazole IV 300 mg IV over 90 minutes bid x 1 day, then 300 mg IV daily
  • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily
  • Amphotericin B 0.6-1 mg/kg IV daily

Comments

  • Monitor CF titers: CF titer ≤1:4 associated with a good prognosis, CF titer ≥1:16 associated with disseminated disease.
  • Secondary prophylaxis is recommended in HIV-positive patients until CD4 >250 and infection is controlled with Fluconazole 400 mg po once daily or Itraconazole 200 mg po bid (MMWR Recomm Rep. 58:1, 2009).
  • A small retrospective study found that of patients unresponsive to Fluconazole or Itraconazole, Voriconazole or Posaconazole was effective as salvage therapy in 67% and 75% of cases respectively  (Clin Infect Dis 53:1060, 2011).
  • Open-label, nonrandomized study revealed efficacy and safety of isavuconazole in treatment of wide range of fungi (Clin Infect Dis 63:356, 2016)
  • Treatment of pediatric cocci to include salvage therapy with Voriconazole + Caspofungin: Clin Infect Dis 56:1573, 2013; Clin Infect Dis 56:1579, 2013.
  • IDSA Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 63:e112, 2016
