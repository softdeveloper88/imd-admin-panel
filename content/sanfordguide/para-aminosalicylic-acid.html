Para-aminosalicylic acid
Tradename: PAS, Paser

Usage and Dosing

  • Para-aminosalicylic acid is a second-line anti-tuberculous agent used primarily to treat tuberculosis caused by multiple drug resistant (MDR) and extensively drug resistant (XDR) strains of M. tuberculosis (see Lancet Infect Dis 2015;15:1091).
  • Resistance
    • Rare, usually encountered in MDR or XDR strains (see Emerg Infect Dis 19:449, 2013).
    • No cross-resistance with other anti-tuberculous agents.

Adult Dose

  • Usual dose: 8-12 gm/day in 2-3 divided doses

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days) 200-300 mg/kg/day (divided q6-12h)

Adverse Effects

  • Most common:  nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
  • Less common: hepatitis, hypersensitivity reactions with fever, rash, vascilitis, eosinophilia, leukopenia, thrombocytopenia
