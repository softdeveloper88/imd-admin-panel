Filariasis, Overview

Overview

  • Filariasis describes a variety of extraintestinal infections caused by tissue/blood nematodes (roundworms).
  • Wolbachia bacteria are needed for filarial development (not Loa loa).
  • Treatment with Doxycycline 100–200 mg/day x 6-8 weeks reduces the number of Wolbachia and has been shown to kill both microfilaria and adult worms (Parasitology 2014,141:119).
  • For lymphatic filariasis mass triple-drug therapy with ivermectin, diethylcarbamazine, and albendazole is being introduced for control programs in many endemic countries.
  • Moxidectin was FDA-approved in 2018 for onchocerciasis but not available or commercially distributed in human formulation.
    • Trials underway for lymphatic filariasis and several related helminths
