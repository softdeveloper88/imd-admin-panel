Biktarvy
Tradename: Bictegravir + Emtricitabine + Tenofovir alafenamide

Usage and Dosing

  • Indicated for treatment of HIV-1 infection in adults, either as initial antiretroviral (ARV) therapy or as a replacement for existing or prior ARV therapy.
  • Single tablet three-drug regimen consisting of:
    • Nucleoside: Emtricitabine (FTC)
    • Nucleotide: Tenofovir alafenamide fumarate (TAF)
    • Integrase strand transfer inhibitor (INSTI): Bictegravir (BIC)
  • Two available tablet strengths:
    • BIC 50 mg, FTC 200 mg, TAF 25 mg
    • BIC 30 mg, FTC 120 mg, TAF 15 mg
  • Black-Box Warning: possible acute exacerbation of hepatitis B in co-infected patients who discontinue Biktarvy
  • For class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.

Adult Dose

  • 1 tablet (BIC 50 mg, FTC 200 mg, TAF 25 mg) po q24h
  • Take with or without food

      Adverse Effects

      • GI (nausea, diarrhea)
      • Headache
      • Immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome (IRIS)
      • Lactic acidosis
      • Hepatomegaly, steatosis

      Comments

      • Watch for IRIS (immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome) if treating an ARV naive patient with a lower CD4 count (< 150 cells /ul)
      • Expect serum creatinine to increase slightly (~ 0.1 - 0.15 mg/dL) when initiating bictegravir owing to inhibition of proximal tubular secretion of creatinine. Note well: This is not a reflection of any change in true GFR, although the estimated GFR (eGRF) will suggest a reduction.
