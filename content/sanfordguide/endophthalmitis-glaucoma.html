Endophthalmitis, Glaucoma

Clinical Setting

  • Bacterial infection of the ocular cavity post-glaucoma surgery.
  • Unintended and uncommon (1-2%) surgical complication from creation of filtering blebs used to treat glaucoma.
  • Recommendations are for empiric therapy; definitive therapy based on culture.
  • Consultation with and prompt referral to ophthalmologist strongly recommended.

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus sp. (pneumococci, viridans group, and others)
  • H. influenzae
  • Moraxella spp.
  • Gram-negative bacilli
  • Staphylococcus spp.

Primary Regimens

  • Intravitreal Vancomycin 1 mg + Ceftazidime 2.25 mg + a topical ophthalmic antimicrobial.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Poor outcome in many patients.
