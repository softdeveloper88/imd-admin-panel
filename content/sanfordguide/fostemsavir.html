Fostemsavir
Tradename: Rukobia

Usage and Dosing

  • Fostemsavir tromethamine (FTR) is a gp120-directed attachment inhibitor for use in combination therapy for treatment of HIV-1 infection.
  • Drug is recommended for use in Highly Treatment Experienced HIV patients as 'salvage' therapy.
  • FTR tromethamine is a prodrug of Temsavir, the active moiety. Fostemsavir is not detectable in plasma following oral administration.
  • Attaches to region of gp120 blocking binding of gp120 to CD4 receptor. Mutations in gp120 (at positions S375H/I/M/N/T, M426L/P, M434I/K, and M475I) can lead to reduced attachment (~100-fold) and some reduction in efficacy of the drug (occurs in ~12% of patients) but did not appear to effect 96 week efficacy (IAS 2020 Poster 0503).
  • No pre-treatment 'resistance' testing is recommended prior to use of fostemsavir.
  • Use with at least 2 other 'active' antiretroviral agents (if possible).
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all inclusive).

Adult Dose

  • One 600 mg extended-release tablet twice daily, with or without food.
  • Tablets should be swallowed whole. Do not chew, crush, or split tablets.
  • Tablets have slight odor similar to vinegar.

      Adverse Effects

      • Headache
      • Rash
      • Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
      • Fatigue, lack of energy
      • IRIS
      • Increased hepatic transaminases in patients with hepatitis B or C co-infection
      • QT-interval prolongation with higher than recommended dosages

      Pregnancy Risk

      FDA Risk Category 1 No human data, safe in animals
      Use during Lactation If on ART + suppressed VL throughout pregnancy, breastfeeding is a reasonable option after counseling about infant feeding options, risks and benefits
