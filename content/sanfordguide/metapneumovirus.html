Metapneumovirus

Clinical Setting

  • Human metapneumovirus (HMPV) is a paramyxovirus isolated from patients of all ages, with mild bronchiolitis/bronchospasm to pneumonia.
    • Discovered in 2001
    • Most common in young children 
    • Can cause lethal pneumonia in stem cell transplant patients (Ann Intern Med 144:344, 2006).
    • Clinical picture in the elderly (J Infect Dis 2018;218:868).
      • Often spread within Nursing Facilities

  • HMPV is most likely spread from an infected person to others through: 

    • Secretions from coughing and sneezing
    • Close personal contact, such as touching or shaking hands
    • Touching objects or surfaces that have the viruses on them then touching the mouth, nose, or eyes
  • Review: Semin Respir Crit Care Med 32:447, 2011.

Diagnosis

  • Nucleic acid amplification test (NAAT) approved to detect 12 respiratory viruses (xTAG Respiratory Viral Panel, Luminex Molecular Diagnostics).

Etiologies

  • Human metapneumovirus (HMPV)

Primary Regimens

  • No proven antiviral therapy

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Ribavirin IV used anecdotally with variable results. Aerosolized ribavirin NOT recommended.
  • Several experimental treatments under evaluation.  Reviewed in Clin Vaccine Rev 2015, 22:858.
  • Human metapneumovirus isolated from 6-21% of children with RTIs (N Engl J Med 350:443, 2004).
  • Dual infection with RSV associated with severe bronchiolitis (J Infect Dis 191:382, 2005).
