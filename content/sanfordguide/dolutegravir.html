Dolutegravir
Tradename: Tivicay, Tivicay PD

Usage and Dosing

  • Dolutegravir (DTG) is an integrase strand transfer inhibitor (INSTI) for use as part of a combination antiretroviral therapy (ART) drug regimen for both naive and treatment-experienced patients.
  • Dolutegravir co-administered most often with TDF/TAF-FTC or ABC-3TC in clinical trials of naive patients.
  • Outperformed most initial regimens in clinical studies (e.g, statistically better than EFV-TDF-FTC and Raltegravir-TDF-FTC).
  • Now designated a preferred antiretroviral for pregnant women, regardless of trimester (HHS guidelines, Dec 2019).
  • Tablets and dispersible tablets for oral suspension are NOT interchangeable (different bioavailability). See Pediatric Dosing below.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see Table below, not all-inclusive).
  • Review: Clin Infect Dis 59:265, 2014.

Adult Dose

Adult Dose
  • 50 mg orally once daily (for naive patients; and for those without pre-existing INSTI mutations)
  • 50 mg twice daily (for those with pre-existing INSTI mutations) or if co-administered with Efavirenz, Fosamprenavir-Ritonavir, Tipranavir-Ritonavir or Rifampin.

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Hypersensitivity reaction (uncommon)
    • Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome, IRIS (as with other potent ARV agents)
    • Elevation of liver enzymes among those with Hepatitis B or C co-infection (uncommon)
  • Most Common:
    • Insomnia (< 3%)
    • Headache (< 2%)
    • GI: nausea/vomiting (< 1 %)
    • Rash (< 1%)
    • Abnormal laboratory values: ALT/AST (< 3%); total bilirubin (< 2%); CPK (< 4%); glucose (< 5%); lipase (<5%); neutropenia (< 3%); mild increase in cholesterol or triglycerides
  • Other: 2 of 4 volunteers developed flu-like symptoms, elevated aminotransferases, markedly elevated gamma-interferon, and changes in DTG and INH concentrations after once-weekly isoniazid and rifapentine (for latent tuberculosis) was added to daily dolutegravir (Clin Infect Dis 67:193, 2018).
