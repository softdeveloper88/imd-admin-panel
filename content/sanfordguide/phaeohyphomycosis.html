Dematiaceous molds, Phaeohyphomycosis

Clinical Setting

  • Phaeohyphomycosis, black molds, pigmented molds, dematiaceous fungi
  • Opportunistic infection in an immunocompromised host (e.g., HIV/AIDS, transplants)
  • Most often presents with skin and soft tissue infection or mycetoma but can cause disease in bone and joint, brain abscess, endocarditis, and disseminated disease in immunocompromised hosts.

Etiologies

  • Most clinically relevant species are within the genera of Exophiala, Cladophialophora, Coniosporium, Cyphellophora, Fonsecaea, Phialophora, and Rhinocladiella

Primary Regimens

  • Surgical resection plus Itraconazole 200 mg po bid

Alternative Regimens

  • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg po bid x 1 day and then 4 mg/kg po bid
  • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs)
    • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily
    • Suspension 200 mg qid, then 400 mg po bid after stabilization of disease
    • Posaconazole IV 300 mg IV over 90 minutes bid x 1 day, then 300 mg IV daily
  • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily

Comments

  • Both Voriconazole and Posaconazole have demonstrated efficacy for treating severe infection in published cases (Med Mycol 48:769, 2010; Med Mycol 43:91, 2005) often in addition to surgical therapy.
  • Consider obtaining anti-fungal susceptibility testing to guide treatment choice.
  • Review article: Clin Microbiol Rev 27:527, 2014.
