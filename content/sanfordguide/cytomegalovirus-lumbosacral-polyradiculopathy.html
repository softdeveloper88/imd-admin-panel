Cytomegalovirus, Neurologic

Clinical Setting

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV) associated neurological disease is rare but can occur in highly immunosuppressed patients such as those with:
    • Late stage HIV/AIDS (CD4 count < 50/mm3)
    • Hematopoietic stem cell transplant (HCT) recipients
    • Organ transplant and solid organ transplant (SOT) recipients.
  • CMV associated neurological disease, particularly encephalitis is associated with a high mortality rate even with antiviral therapy (Clin Infect Dis 27:345, 1998; Bone Marrow Transplant 45:979, 2010).
  • Clinical manifestations of CMV infection in the CNS includes:
    • Encephalitis (altered mental status and focal neurologic changes)
    • Myelitis (lower extremity weakness and hyperreflexia)
    • Polyradiculopathy (lower extremity weakness and reduced sensation, hyporeflexia, loss of cremasteric reflex in men)

Diagnosis

  • Confirmed with a combination of consistent MRI findings and often high levels of CMV DNA detected by PCR in CSF.

Etiologies

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV)

Primary Regimens

  • Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV q12h

Alternative Regimens

  • Valganciclovir 900 mg po q12h
  • Foscarnet 90 mg/kg IV q12h
  • Cidofovir 5 mg/kg IV once weekly

Comments

  • Treatment duration should be individualized but will likely be prolonged. Continue treatment until CMV PCR has become undetectable in both CSF and serum and clinical evidence of disease has resolved. Once these markers are achieved, transition to secondary prophylaxis (maintenance therapy) with Valganciclovir 900 mg po q24h until there is some immune recovery (HCT recipients) or CD4 count remains >100/mm3 for 6 months (HIV).
  • Combination therapy with Ganciclovir and Foscarnet have been used in some cases (J Neurological Sci, 2018) as have the addition of IVIG or CMV Hyperimmune globulin (Cytogam) with unclear benefit (AIDS 14:517, 2000; Bone Marrow Transplant 45:979, 2010).
  • Compartmentalized ganciclovir-resistant CMV has been reported during the treatment of CMV encephalitis (Diagn Microbiol Infect Dis 74:307, 2012).
  • Early antiretroviral therapy is important in adults with HIV and CMV neurologic disease.
  • IRIS has been reported in patients started on ARVs during therapy for CMV neurologic disease (HIV Med 1:107, 2000).
