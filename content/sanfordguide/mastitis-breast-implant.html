Mastitis, Breast Implant

Clinical Setting

  • Mastitis occurs in approximately 2% of breast implant procedures. 
  • Empiric therapy.

Etiologies

  • Acute infection
    • Staph. aureus
    • S. pyogenes
    • Coagulase-negative staphylococci
  • Chronic infection
    • Rapidly growing mycobacteria
    • Mycobacterium avium

Primary Regimens

  • Outpatient:
    • Suspected or confirmed MSSA: Dicloxacillin 500 mg po qid or Cephalexin 500 mg po qid
    • Suspected or confirmed MRSA: TMP-SMX 160-800 mg 1-2 tabs po bid
  • Inpatient:
    • Empiric therapy, acute infection: Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL)
      • For suspected Gram-negative infection or Gram stain showing Gram-negative organisms add Cefepime 1 gm IV q8h (or Aztreonam 2 gm IV q6h or Levofloxacin 750 mg IV/po q24h for serious penicillin allergy)
    • MRSA confirmed: Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL)
    • MSSA: Nafcillin 2 gm IV q4-6h or Oxacillin 2 gm IV q4-6h OR Cefazolin 2 gm IV q8h
  • For chronic infection: wait for culture results and treat accordingly.

Alternative Regimens

  • Penicillin-allergic patient: TMP-SMX 160-800 mg 1-2 tabs po bid or Clindamycin 300 mg po tid or Linezolid 600 mg po bid

Comments

  • Antibiotics alone may not be successful and salvage reconstruction or explantation required (Plast Reconstr Surg 131:1223, 2013)
  • Outcomes worse in patients with MRSA infection or history of radiation therapy.
  • Implant colonized or infected with S.aureus can result in S.aureus toxic shock syndrome.
