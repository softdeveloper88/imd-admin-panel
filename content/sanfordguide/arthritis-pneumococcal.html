Arthritis, Septic, Pneumococcal

Clinical Setting

  • Septic arthritis with Strep. pneumoniae isolated from blood or joint fluid.
  • Pneumonia present in about half of cases.
  • More common in children.

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus pneumoniae

Primary Regimens

  • Drainage of infected synovial fluid by needle aspiration, arthroscopy, or arthrotomy repeated as needed to remove re-accumulated pus.
  • Penicillin susceptible organism (MIC ≤2 μg/mL):
    • Penicillin G 2 mU IV q4h
    • Ceftriaxone 2g IV q24h OR Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q8h (where available)
  • Penicillin-resistant or intermediate organism (MIC >2 μg/mL):
    • Ceftriaxone 2g IV q24h or Cefotaxime 2 gm IV q8h (where available)
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg q8-12h (may be preferred if penicillin MIC >4 μg/mL)

Alternative Regimens

  • Levofloxacin 750 mg q24h po or IV 
  • Linezolid 600 mg q12h po/IV (oral alternative for severely penicillin-allergic patient or multidrug-resistant organism; monitor for bone marrow suppression for duration of therapy > 2 weeks)(not an approved indication).

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy:
    • Optimal duration of therapy unclear: 7-10 days of parenteral therapy with step down to an active oral agent for an additional 2-3 weeks based on clinical response is reasonable.  
    • Consider 6 weeks for prosthetic joint infection; prosthetic removal not always necessary, although late post-surgical infection (> 3 months) and rheumatoid arthritis associated with failure (Clin Infect Dis 2017;64:1742).
