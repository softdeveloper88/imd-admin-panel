Trichosporon sp.

Clinical Setting

  • Trichosporon species are found in soil but can also colonize skin and be found as part of the normal skin flora
  • When Trichosporon species cause disease, they predominantly cause superficial skin infection (white piedra) but they can cause invasive disease in immunocompromised hosts

Classification

  • Trichosporon species are a heterogenous group of organisms

Primary Regimens

  • Superficial infection (white piedra)
    • 2% ketaconazole shampoo daily until resolution
  • Invasive infection (MIC testing recommended)
    • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg IV/po q12h x 1 day and then 4 mg/kg IV/po q12h

Alternative Regimens

  • Superficial infection (white piedra)
    • 2% ketaconazole shampoo daily until resolution
  • Invasive infection (MIC testing recommended)
    • Lipid-based Amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg IV qd
    • Amphotericin B 0.7 mg/kg IV qd
    • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs)
      • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily
      • Suspension 200 mg qid, then 400 mg po bid after stabilization of disease
      • Posaconazole IV 300 mg IV over 90 minutes bid x 1 day, then 300 mg IV daily
    • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily

Comments

  • MIC testing for invasive infection is recommended because it can be resistant to many antifungal treatments
  • When possible, consider reducing immune suppression
  • Trichosporon species are resistant to echinocandins
