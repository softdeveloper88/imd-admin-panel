Surgical Prophylaxis, Colorectal

Clinical Setting

  • Antimicrobial prophylaxis for colorectal surgical procedures, both elective and emergency. See also, Prevention of Surgical Infections,Overview.
  • For optimal efficacy intravenous antibiotics should be administered 30-60 minutes prior to incision.
  • References:
    • Clinical practice guideline: Dis colon rectum 2019; 62:3
    • In depth reference, oral antibiotic bowel prep: Ann Surg 2015;261:1034

Etiologies

  • Colorectal normal flora as well as skin bacterial flora

Primary Regimens

  • If elective procedure, recommend a combination of:
    • Mechanical bowel prep
    • Oral antibiotics
    • IV antimicrobials
  • If emergency colorectal surgery, likely that oral antibiotics and mechanical prep are not possible
  • Oral (for elective surgery):
    • Neomycin + Erythromycin on pre-op day:
      • 10 am, 4L polyethylene glycol electrolyte solution (Colyte, GoLYTELY) po over 2 hours
      • Clear liquid diet only
      • 1 pm, 2 pm and 11 pm, Neomycin 1 gm + Erythromycin base 1 gm po
      • Nothing taken orally after midnight
  • Parenteral (emergency or elective surgery):
    • Cefazolin 1-2 gm IV + Metronidazole 0.5 gm IV.
      • Some experts routinely use cefazolin 2 gm with an increase to 3 gm if patient is >120 kg. Need to repeat the cefazolin dose 4 hrs. after the initial pre-op dose.
      • NOTE: cefazolin and metronidazole can be mixed together in same IV bag (sometimes referred to as "cefanidazole").
    • Cefoxitin 2 gm IV or Cefotetan 2 gm IV (if available).
      • NOTE: Increasing resistance of pertinent anaerobes to both cefoxitin and cefotetan. Further, the short half-life of cefoxitin requires frequent re-dosing.
    • Ampicillin-Sulbactam 3 gm IV.
      • Many centers experiencing increasing resistance of aerobic gram-negatives to Ampicillin-Sulbactam. No longer recommended for empiric therapy of peritonitis.
    • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV + Metronidazole 500 mg IV.
      • NOTE: This regimen may be  useful in centers with high rates of resistance to first and second generation cephalosporins.
    • Ertapenem 1 gm IV

Alternative Regimens

  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate (1000 / 200 mg or 2000 / 400 mg) IV.
  • Alternative oral regimens have been less well studied: GoLYTELY 1–6 pm, then Neomycin 2 gm po + Metronidazole 2 gm po at 7 pm and 11 pm.
  • If allergic to beta-lactams: Clindamycin 900 mg IV + (Gentamicin 5 mg/kg IV x 1 dose or Aztreonam 2 gm IV or Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV)
