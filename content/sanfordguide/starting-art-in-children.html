Starting ART in Children

When to Start ART in Children

  • Rapid initiation of antiretroviral therapy (ART) is now recommended for all children.. Choosing regimens that will allow for a life time of successful treatment is challenging. Data specific to outcomes in children are more limited than in adults, & clinical trial data on when to start equivalent to START trial in adults do not exist for children. Natural history studies in children & extrapolation from adult studies are used to derive guidelines. The weight of evidence supports early treatment in children:
    • Potential benefits include improved survival and health benefits, neurodevelopmental benefits, immune benefits, and viral reservoirs and viral suppression
    • 25–35% of HIV-infected children will be rapid progressors.
    • Viral load & CD4 are associated with rapid progression but cannot accurately identify all rapid progressors in first year of life.
    • The CHER study in South Africa demonstrated improved survival in asymptomatic infected infants when therapy was started at <12 weeks compared to waiting for symptoms (4th AIDS Conference on HIV Pathogenesis, Treatment and Prevention 2007 Sydney Abstract LB WES103).
    • Immune control of virus limited in first year of life.
    • Trials in adults have shown clear benefits to early treatment (N Engl J Med 373:795 2015, AIDS 31:953, 2017).
    • Trial data demonstrate decreased mortality with early vs late initiation of ART in children <2 (N Engl J Med 359:2233, 2008, Lancet Infect Dis 12:933, 2012) and improved growth, better immune parameters, fewer opportunistic infections. Clin Infect Dis 62:1586, 2016;  Int J Epidemiol. 2017 Apr 1;46(2):453
    • Viral reservoir may be limited when treatment started as infant Clin Infect Dis. 2021 Aug 2;73(3):427 

    • Therefore, ART is now recommended for all children regardless of disease stage.

  • There may be some psychosocial or clinical considerations that might require waiting to begin therapy

Guidelines for Beginning Treatment

  • Three sets of guidelines have been developed. 
  • The two most recently updated guidelines recommend treatment for all HIV infected children if feasible regardless of disease stage. The WHO guidelines await updating
  • All emphasize the need for education to ensure adherence, & routine monitoring for efficacy & safety.
