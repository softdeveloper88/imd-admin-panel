Darunavir
Tradename: Prezista

Usage and Dosing

  • Darunavir (DRV) is indicated for use as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually combined with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents (e.g., TDF-FTC or ABC-3TC). Must be used with Ritonavir-boosting. Darunavir once daily dosing regimen used mostly in 1st-line therapy. A preferred boosted-PI by HHS Guidelines.
  • Use with caution in patients with hepatic dysfunction, especially early in the course of treatment. Monitor carefully, especially first several months and with pre-existing liver disease.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • May cause hormonal contraception failure.

Adult Dose

Usual Dose[600 mg Darunavir + 100 mg Ritonavir] po bid, with food
or
[800 mg Darunavir (two 400 mg tabs) + 100 mg Ritonavir] po once daily
with food (Preferred regimen in ART naive patients)

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Hepatitis in 0.5%, some with fatal outcome. Use caution in patients with HBV or HCV co-infections or other hepatic dysfunction. Monitor for clinical symptoms and LFTs.
    • Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis, erythema multiforme.
    • Contains sulfa moiety. Potential for major drug interactions. May cause failure of hormonal contraceptives.
  • Most Common:
    • With background regimens, headache 15%, nausea 18%, diarrhea 20%, ↑ amylase 17%.
    • Rash in 17% of treated; 0.3% discontinuation.
