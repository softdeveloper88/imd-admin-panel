DDx: Neuropathy, HIV

Clinical Syndrome

  • Neuropathies can be central (myelopathy) or peripheral. Peripheral can manifest as sensory neuropathy (painful or non-painful) or mixed sensory and motor deficits.
  • Increased risk in patients with low CD4 counts and high viral loads.
  • Several clinical patterns of nerve damage:
    • Distal sensory symmetrical polyneuropathy
    • Mononeuropathy or mononeuropathy complex
    • Lumbosacral polyradiculopathy with lower extremity sensory and motor deficits.

Differential Diagnosis

  • Distal sensory symmetrical polyneuropathy
    • Tingling and numbness in feet that spreads proximally
    • Often painful
    • Risk factors: Advanced HIV (low CD4 count), diabetes mellitus, vitamin deficiency, exposure to neurotoxic medications (Didanosine, Stavudine, Nevirapine, Dapsone, Isoniazid, Ethambutol)
    • Pathogenesis is not completely understood
    • Diagnosis
      • Physical exam and nerve conduction studies. CSF exam not usually necessary.
    • Treatment
      • Discontinue offending drugs
      • Antiretroviral therapy
      • For pain relief: gabapentin, tricyclic antidepressants, topical capsacin (difficult to administer), opiods (for acute management only; do not use opioids for chronic pain)
  • Mononeuropathy
    • Cranial or peripheral nerves
    • In acute HIV, usually presents as unilateral or, rarely, bilateral Bell's Palsy due to varicella zoster or meningeal lymphomatosis.
    • In late stage HIV, can present as Bell's Palsy due to varicella zoster or meningeal lymphomatosis.
  • Mononeuropathy multiplex
    • Asymmetric painful sensorimotor polyneuropathy in a non-symmetric pattern.
    • If early in the course of HIV, think antigen-antibody immune complex deposition in blood vessels.
    • In late stage HIV, think complication of chronic Hepatitis B, CMV or varicella zoster
    • Diagnosis
      • Nerve conduction studies
      • Hepatitis B surface antigen, Hepatitis C antibody, serum cryoglobulins
      • If CD4 < 200, CSF exam for CMV PCR
      • May need sural nerve biopsy
  • Demyelinating polyradiculopathy or lumbar polyradiculopathy
    • Lower extremity symmetrical proximal and distal muscle weakness and araflexia.
    • Can be encountered either early in HIV infection as Guillian-Barre syndrome or in advanced HIV (low CD4 count) as a chronic inflammatory demyelinating process.
    • Diagnosis
      • CSF shows elevated protein (> 100 mg/dL) with mild lymphocyte predominance.
      • In advanced HIV, do CMV PCR on CSF.
      • Exclude syphilis with treponemal antibody.
    • Treatment
      • Antiretroviral therapy (ART) in those with advanced disease.
