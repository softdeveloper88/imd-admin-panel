Blastomycosis, Meningitis

Clinical Setting

  • Chronic fungal meningitis

Etiologies

  • Blastomyces dermatitidis

Primary Regimens

  • Liposomal Amphotericin B 5 mg/kg/day x 4-6 weeks, followed by
    • Fluconazole 800 mg/day OR
    • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg q12h x 2 doses then 4 mg/kg q12h
  • Treat for at least 12 months and until CSF has normalized

Alternative Regimens

  • Itraconazole oral solution 2.5 mg/kg po q12h or 200 mg capsules po q12h can be used in place of Fluconazole or Voriconazole

Comments

  • Fluconazole and Voriconazole have excellent CNS penetration, maybe counterbalancing slightly reduced activity compared to Itraconazole.
  • Document serum Itraconazole levels to assure adequate drug concentrations.
  • Chronic suppressive therapy with an azole may be indicated in patients whose immunosuppression cannot be reversed and in those who have experienced relapse.
  • See IDSA treatment guidelines for more information: Clin Infect Dis 46:1801, 2008.
