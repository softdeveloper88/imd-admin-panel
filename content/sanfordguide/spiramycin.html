Spiramycin
Tradename: Rovamycin

Usage and Dosing

  • Macrolide antiparasitic and antibacterial drug.
  • For suspected or confirmed T. gondii infection acquired during the first 18 weeks of pregnancy.
  • Contact U.S. FDA +1 301-796-1600 for IND application after appropriately positive serology result at Remington Laboratory for Specialty Diagnostics (previously the Toxoplasma Serology Lab): +1 650-853-4828.  Email: RemingtonLab@sutterhealth.org. Website: www.sutterhealth.org/RemingtonLab
  • Drug then supplied free by Sanofi-Aventis.
  • 1 gm = 3 million units.

Adult Dose

  • 1 gm po q8h

Adverse Effects

  • Gastrointestinal (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, C. difficile colitis)
  • Liver function test abnormalities
  • Hypersensitivity reactions (rash, urticaria, pruritus, angioedema, anaphylaxis)
  • Transient paresthesias
  • Acute hemolysis
  • QT interval prolongation
