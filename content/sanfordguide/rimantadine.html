Rimantadine
Tradename: Flumadine

Usage and Dosing

  • Rimantadine is used for treatment of influenza A, but not recommended because of high levels of resistance of influenza A to adamantanes.
  • No activity against influenza B.

Adult Dose

Age <65 years100 mg po q12h
Age ≥65 years100 mg po q24h
  • Dose is for treatment or prophylaxis.

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days)Age 1 to <10 years: 5 mg/kg (max 150 mg) po q24h
Age ≥10 years: use adult dose

Hepatic Adjustment

Mild impairment (Child-Pugh Class A) No dosage adjustment, use caution
Moderate impairment (Child-Pugh Class B) No dosage adjustment, use caution
Severe impairment (Child-Pugh Class C) Reduce dosage by 50%

Adverse Effects

  • CNS (nervousness, insomnia, dizziness, difficulty concentrating, headache)
    • In a six-week prophylaxis study, CNS adverse effects occurred in 6.9% of patients on rimantadine vs. 14.7% on amantadine.
    • Usually decrease after the first week and disappear when drug is discontinued.
  • GI (nausea, anorexia)
  • Some serious side effects, such delirium, hallucinations, and seizures, are associated with high plasma drug levels resulting from renal insufficiency, especially in older patients, patients with prior seizure disorders, and patients with psychiatric disorders.
