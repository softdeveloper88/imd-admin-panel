Drug Interactions, Overview

Overview

  • The most important drug-drug interactions occur with the following drugs or classes of drugs:
    • Macrolides, in particular clarithromycin and erythromycin
    • Fluoroquinolones, e.g., ciprofloxacin
    • Rifamycins, particularly rifampin
    • Azole antifungals, e.g., fluconazole
    • Antiretrovirals (ARVs)
    • Hepatitis C direct acting agents (DAAs)
  • An inset table summarizing major drug-drug interactions (effect and suggested management) is found on each drug page under the heading Major Drug Interactions.
  • Unstudied drug interactions may be predicted using the enzyme- & transporter-mediated interactions table (link below). This table may also be used to understand the mechanism of known interactions.
