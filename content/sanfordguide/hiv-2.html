HIV-2

Clinical Setting

  • HIV-2 is an etiology of the acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS).
  • Originally identified in West Africa; currently HIV-2 has spread throughout Africa, to India, Europe, and the United States.
  • Patients may be co-infected with HIV-1.
  • Diagnosis:
    • Many, but not all, HIV-1 antibody tests detect HIV-2 antibody.
    • New testing platforms and new HIV-testing Guidelines distinguish HIV-1 from HIV-2.
    • Western blot is no longer required in HIV-1 or HIV-2 testing algorithms.
    • New recommendations available at:  http://stacks.cdc.gov/view/cdc/23447

    • Specimens with a reactive antigen/antibody combination immunoassay result should be
      tested with an FDA-approved antibody immunoassay that differentiates HIV-1 antibodies from HIV-2 antibodies.
    • Quantitative HIV-2 RNA viral load testing available from:
      • In US: University of Washington (800-713-5198) or commserv@u.washington.edu or New York State Department of Health (https://www.wadsworth.org/programs/id/bloodborne-viruses/clinical-testing/hiv-2-nucleic-acid)
      • In France: http://etudes.isped.u-bordeaux2.fr
      • Risk factors and transmission same as HIV-1.
      • HIV-2 has a longer asymptomatic phase and slower progression to AIDS than HIV-1 infection.

    Etiologies

    • HIV-2
    • Mixed HIV-1 and HIV-2 infections

    Primary Regimens

    • No controlled studies of what therapy to start for HIV-2 infected individuals. Following suggestions are based on limited in vitro data and limited clinical experience.
    • Standard doses of:
      • Darunavir/r + (Descovy or Truvada)
      • Raltegravir + (Descovy or Truvada)
      • Dolutegravir + (Descovy or Truvada)
      • Bictegravir (co-formulated with TAF / FTC)

    Alternative Regimens

    • Darunavir/r + Combivir (Zidovudine + Lamivudine)

    Comments

    • Avoid NNRTI regimens, enfuvirtide, or other PIs ; they are selective for HIV-1 with limited activity vs HIV-2 (rilpivirine may have activity; no clinical trials)
    • References: Clin Infect Dis 52:780, 2011, MMWR 60:985, 2011.
