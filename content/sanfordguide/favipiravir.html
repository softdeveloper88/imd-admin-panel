Favipiravir
Tradename: Avigan

Usage and Dosing

  • Favipiravir is a prodrug that is ribosylated and phosphorylated intracellularly to form the active triphosphate metabolite. This metabolite competes with purine nuclosides for incorporation into viral RNA, thus inhibiting RNA-dependent RNA polymerase.
  • Teratogenic: See contraindications and warnings.
  • Approved in Japan for the treatment of influenza when there is an outbreak of novel or re-emerging virus.

Adult Dose

  • 1600 mg po bid x 1 day, then 600 mg po bid x 4 days

Adverse Effects

  • Contraindications
    • Teratogenic: do not use in pregnancy (known or suspected)
    • If known hypersensitivity reaction
  • Warnings
    • Women of child-bearing age: confirmed negative pregnancy test before use
    • Men: use effective contraception during and for 7 days post-treatment (drug is distributed in sperm)
  • Adverse effects:
    • Diarrhea
    • Increased blood uric acid level
    • Increased AST/ALT
    • Decreased neutrophil count
