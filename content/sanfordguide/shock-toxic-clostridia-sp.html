Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS), Clostridial

Clinical Setting

  • Pathogenomic syndrome caused by Clostridium sordellii (now Paeniclostridium sordellii) toxins.
    • Differs from toxic shock syndrome caused by Staph. aureus and Streptococcal species.
  • Patient presents with shock plus:
    • Hemoconcentration manifest as high hemoglobin/hematocrit
    • Profound capillary leak requiring massive amounts of parenteral fluid
    • Leukemoid reaction with WBC >50,000 cells/μl
    • Absence of fever
  • Best reviews: Clin Infect Dis 43:1436, 2006; Clin Infect Dis 43:1447, 2006.
  • NOTE: unlike Clostridium perfringens, there is no gas production.
  • Patients may have a profoundly low serum calcium concentration; mechanism is unknown.
  • Requires underlying process that creates anaerobic environment: e.g.,
    • Tissue damage/necrotizing fasciitis from injection drug use (skin popping black tar heroin: Clin Infect Dis 2004;38:e87)
    • Post-partum endometritis, medically-induced abortion with mifepristone + misoprostol, intrauterine device (IUD), and other clinical scenarios.
    • Occult GI malignancy with mucosal disruption
  • Tissue damage due to lethal and hemorrhagic organism toxins that target endothelium of capillaries

Etiologies

  • Clostridium sordellii ( now Paeniclostridium sordellii)

Primary Regimens

  • Fluids + aqueous Penicillin G 18–20 million units/day divided q4–6h + Clindamycin 900 mg IV q8h to hopefully turn off toxin production
  • Major challenge is early recognition followed by rapid removal of necrotic tissue that is serving as the sanctuary for the toxin-producing organism
  • No commercially available antitoxin

Alternative Regimens

  • Organism sensitive to a large variety of antibacterials: cephalosporins, penicillins (not aztreonam), carbapenems, tetracycline, and chloramphenicol.

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Resistant to aminoglycosides and sulfonamides.

Comments

  • Several deaths reported after use to abortifacient regimen of mifepristone (RU486) and misoprostol.
    • 2001-2006 standard regimen following medical abortion was oral mifepristone, then vaginal misoprostol. Since 2006, switch to buccal misoprostol + routine antibiotics has resulted in a dramatic decrease in incidence of post-abortion Clostridial  toxic shock syndrome (TSS), N Engl J Med 361:145, 2009.
  • If leukemoid reaction exceeds 50,000 cells/μL, the mortality is nearly 100%.
