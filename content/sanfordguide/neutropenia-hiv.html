DDx: Neutropenia, HIV

Clinical Syndrome

  • Neutropenic patient: immunosuppressed patient with an absolute neutrophil count (ANC) of < 1000 neutrophils/μL. Increased infection risk with ANC < 500 neutrophils/μL.
  • May be febrile or afebrile: post chemotherapy, post bone marrow transplant, HIV/AIDS.
  • IDSA Practice Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 52:427, 2011.

Differential Diagnosis

  • Differential diagnosis of neutropenia in HIV/AIDS:
    • Antineutrophil antibodies in nearly 1/3 of HIV patients, but no correlation with ANC
    • Ineffective granulopoiesis: marrow dysfunction due to infiltration by, or influence of, systemic infection or malignancy
    • Drug induced (80% of patients). The following are commonly involved:
      • 5-flucytosine
      • Amphotericin B
      • Ganciclovir
      • Pentamidine
      • Pyrimethamine
      • Ribavirin
      • TMP-SMX
      • Valganciclovir
      • Zidovudine
