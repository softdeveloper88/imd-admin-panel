Diagnosis of HIV-2 Infection

HIV-2

  • Important to distinguish HIV-2 from HIV-1 because HIV-2 is resistant to NNRTIs and fusion inhibitors. References: PLoS ONE 11:e0157690, 2016; J Clin Micro 54:1282, 2016.
  • No longer limited to West Africa. Dual infection with HIV-1 can occur.
  • Most, but not all, enzyme immunoassay (EIA) test kits have HIV-2 antigens. An FDA-approved HIV-2 EIA test has been used to screen blood donors since 1992
  • Reference: J Clin Micro 2023; 61: e0061923.

Diagnosis

  • HIV screening tests detect antibodies to both HIV-1 and HIV-2 plus p24 antigen:
    • Architect HIV Ag/Ab Combo
    • Elecsys HIV Duo
  • Two FDA-approved screening tests detect and differentiate HIV-1 and HIV-2:
    • Multispot HIV-1/HIV-2  Rapid Test
    • Geenius HIV1/2 Supplemental Assay (Bio-Rad Labs)
    • VItros  approved for use in Europe
  • Confirm positive test for HIV-2  with either a Western Blot or a NAAT (RT-PCR) viral "load" quantitation:
    • Qualitative testing from Focus and Quest Diagnostics
    • For Quantitative testing contact:
      • In U.S.: University of Washington (1-800-713-5198) or commserv@u.washington.edu
      • In Europe: http://etudes.isped.u-bordeaux2.fr
