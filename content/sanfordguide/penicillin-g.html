Usage and Dosing

  • Penicillin G is a primary drug for infections due to Strep. pyogenes, the rare non-penicillinase-producing Staph. aureus, selected anaerobic bacteria, Treponema pallidum and a variety of less common pathogens.
  • Penicillin G is rendered inactive by penicillinase which can be produced by S. aureus and a variety of other bacteria.
  • Benzathine penicillin G is used to treat syphilis and for rheumatic fever prophylaxis.
  • Allergic reactions are a major issue. 10% of all hospital admissions give history of penicillin allergy; but only 10% have allergic reaction if given penicillin. Why? Possible reasons: inaccurate history, waning immunity with age, aberrant response during viral illness. Bicillin C-R (Procaine penicillin + Benzathine penicillin) IM could be reaction to the procaine.
  • Penicillin allergy:
    • See Beta-lactams, Drug Allergy Overview and Penicillin Desensitization: Oral Method (preferred) and Parenteral Method.
    • Note regarding penicillin skin testing for evaluation of penicillin allergy:
      • Testing with major determinant (benzyl penicillin polylysine) and minor determinants has negative predictive value of 97-99%. Risk of systemic reaction to skin testing <1%. See Ann Allergy, Asthma & Immunol 106:1, 2011.
    • Review of Penicillin allergy: JAMA 2019; 321:188.
  • 1 unit penicillin G = 0.6 μg (roughly).

Adverse Effects

  • Most serious reaction is immediate IgE-mediated anaphylaxis; incidence only 0.05% but 5-10% fatal. Other IgE-mediated reactions: urticaria, angioedema, laryngeal edema, bronchospasm. Morbilliform rash after 72 hours is not IgE-mediated and not serious.
  • Serious late allergic reactions: Coombs-positive hemolytic anemia, neutropenia, thrombocytopenia (Drug-induced immune thrombocytopenia (J Thrombosis & Haemostasis 11:169, 2012), serum sickness, interstitial nephritis, hepatitis, eosinophilia, drug fever.
  • Allergy references: Am J Med 121:572, 2008; N Engl J Med 354:601, 2006.
  • High CSF concentrations cause seizures. To avoid high concentrations, reduce dose with renal impairment.
