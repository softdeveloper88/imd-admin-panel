Trichostrongylus sp.

Clinical Setting

  • Transmitted by skin penetration similar to hookworm
  • Livestock nematode with worldwide distribution sometimes infects humans.
  • Human infection usually light and asymptomatic; heavy infections with non-specific gastrointestinal symptoms
    • 80% with some symptoms in Iran (J Helminthol 93;149, 2019)

Etiologies

  • Trichostrongylus colubriformis
  • Trichostrongylus orientalis
  • Trichostrongylus axei
  • Trichostrongylus vitrinus

Primary Regimens

  • Pyrantel pamoate 11 mg/kg (maximum 1 gm) po x 1 dose
  • Albendazole 400 mg po daily x 10 doses
  • Mebendazole 500 mg po qd x 10 days
  • Ivermectin 200 μg/kg po x 1 day

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Eggs often mistaken for hookworm.
  • Insufficient human data for any drug; Albendazole/Mebendazole often ineffective in livestock.
  • Formulations
    • Mebendazole 500 mg chewable tablet available for donation only, not commercially available in U.S.
    • Albendazole 200 mg tablets and Mebendazole 100 mg tablets (see Antiparasitic Drugs Sources).
  • Review of biology (J Helminthol 2023,97:e26).
