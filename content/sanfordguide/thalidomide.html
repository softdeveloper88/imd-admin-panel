Thalidomide
Tradename: Thalomid

Usage and Dosing

  • TNF alpha inhibitor agent used primarily in treatment of erythema nodosum leprosum, type reaction during treatment of leprosy
    • Other uses: HIV-wasting syndrome, aphthous ulcers, and myeloma.
  • Contraindicated in pregnancy: if used in women of child-bearing age, patient and must use 2 forms of birth control (rifampin interaction with birth control pills may render these ineffective and 2 barrier methods should be used) and undergo monthly pregnancy tests.
  • Available only through pharmacists participating in System for Thalidomide Education and Prescribing Safety.
  • Known to cause severe birth defects. Pregnancy category X, see Adverse Effects.

Adverse Effects

  • Contraindicated in pregnancy. Causes severe life-threatening birth defects. (Pregnancy Category X).
  • Frequently causes drowsiness or somnolence.
  • May cause peripheral neuropathy (Am J Med 108:487, 2000).
