Kaletra
Tradename: Lopinavir + Ritonavir

Usage and Dosing

  • Indicated for use as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually combined with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents (e.g., TDF-FTC or ABC-3TC). Ritonavir dosing included in the fixed dose combination.  Considered an alternative first-line therapy for naive patients owing to side effect profile and lipid elevations.  For use in treatment experienced patients whose resistance profile indicates activity of Lopinavir.
  • Lopinavir + Ritonavir (LPV/r) combination formulation.
  • Nausea/vomiting/diarrhea (worse when administered with Zidovudine), increased AST/ALT, pancreatitis.
  • Oral solution 42% alcohol (bad taste).
  • Lopinavir + Ritonavir can be taken as a single daily dose of 4 tabs (total 800 mg Lopinavir + 200 mg Ritonavir), except in treatment-experienced patients or those taking concomitant Efavirenz or Nevirapine.
  • Possible PR and QT prolongation. Use with caution in those with cardiac conduction abnormalities or when used with drugs with similar effects.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive)

Adult Dose

  • 2 tablets po bid
  • Tablet: Lopinavir 200 mg + Ritonavir 50 mg
  • Higher dose may be needed in non-ARV-naïve patients when used with Efavirenz or Nevirapine: 4 tabs po qd (alternative regimen for naive patients; do not use this dosing regimen in experienced patients or in combination with a NNRTI)

      Adverse Effects

      • Most Significant:
        • Lipid abnormalities in up to 20–40%. Possible increased risk of MI with cumulative exposure (J Infect Dis 201:318, 2010).
        • ↑ PR interval, 2º or 3º heart block described. Post-marketing reports of ↑ QTc and torsades: avoid use in congenital QTc prolongation or in other circumstances that prolong QTc or increase susceptibility to torsades.
        • Hepatitis, with hepatic decompensation; caution especially in those with pre-existing liver disease.
        • Pancreatitis, including fatalities. Inflammatory edema of legs (AIDS 16:673, 2002). Stevens-Johnson syndrome & erythema multiforme reported.
        • Note high concentration in oral solution.
      • Most Common:
        • GI: diarrhea 14–24%, nausea 2–16%. More diarrhea with q24h dosing.
