Pneumonia, Neutropenic Patient

Clinical Setting

  • Empiric therapy for suspected bacterial pneumonia in neutropenic patients, i.e., patient with <500 neutrophils/mm3.
  • Classically, a setting in which Pseudomonas aeruginosa is concern.

Etiologies

  • Pseudomonas aeruginosa
  • Any of the bacterial or viral pathogens listed for community-acquired or hospital-acquired pneumonia
  • Molds: Aspergillus sp., Mucor sp.

Primary Regimens

  • See regimens for hospital-acquired pneumonia.
  • Include an agent with anti-pseudomonal activity in the regimen.

Alternative Regimens

  • See regimens for hospital-acquired pneumonia.
  • Include an agent with anti-pseudomonal activity in the regimen.

Comments

  • Inappropriate therapy for pseudomonas pneumonia is associated with increased mortality (Clin Infect Dis 2020; 70:1068)
  • Vancomycin is not included in initial therapy unless high suspicion of infected IV access, severe sepsis, hypotension, methicillin-resistant S. aureus (MRSA).
  • Antifungal coverage should not be used routinely; consider adding for a high clinical likelihood of fungal infection.
  • German Society of Hematology and Medical Oncology Guidelines: Ann Oncol 26:21, 2015.
