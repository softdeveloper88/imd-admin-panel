Bursitis, Septic

Clinical Setting

  • Swelling, tenderness, and erythema (over superficial bursae) of a bursal sac.
  • Bursal fluid aspirate with 1000 to 20,000 polys/mm3.
  • Positive culture.

Etiologies

  • Staphylococcus aureus (> 80%)
  • Streptococci
  • Rarely mycobacteria, fungi, other organisms

Primary Regimens

  • If MSSA:
    • (Nafcillin or Oxacillin) 2 gm IV q4h or Cefazolin 2 gm IV q8h
  • If MRSA:
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) or Linezolid 600 mg po bid
  • Incision and drainage or daily aspiration until fluid stops accumulating

Alternative Regimens

  • If MSSA: Dicloxacillin 500 mg po qid as oral step-down
  • If MRSA: Daptomycin 6 mg/kg IV q24h or Linezolid 600 mg po bid

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy. Typically treat for 2 weeks, combined IV and oral, although duration of therapy seems not to be a risk factor for recurrence (Arch Orthop Trauma Surg. 2022;142:2445 and J Antimicrob Chemother 65:1008, 2010)
  • Non-immunocompromised patients undergoing bursectomy may be cured with as little as a week of therapy. 

Comments

  • Surgical excision of bursa reserved for more refractory cases.  
  • Review article: StatPearls Publishing; 2022 Jul 8.
  • French guidelines: Joint Bone Spine. 2024; 91:105664.
