Sexual Exposure, Antibacterial Prophylaxis

Clinical Setting

  • Post-exposure prophylaxis (antibacterial) for sexual assault victim or following sexual contact.
  • Sexual assault
    • Obtain expert advice re: forensic exam and specimens, pregnancy, physical trauma, psychological support.
    • If decision is to proceed with specimen collection at initial exam: Test for gonococci & chlamydia with NAAT at site of penetration, wet mount for Trichomonas vaginalis (and culture vaginal swab). Serologic evaluation for syphilis, Hepatitis B, HIV, others as appropriate.
    • Initiate post-exposure protocols for HIV post-exposure prophylaxis and Hepatitis B exposure as appropriate.
    • Follow-up exam for STD at 1–2 weeks. Retest for syphilis, HBV, HCV, and HIV serology at 6, 12, 24 weeks if negative earlier.
  • Sexual contact
    • Be sure to check for syphilis since all regimens may not eradicate incubating syphilis. Consider also Trichomonas vaginalis.
    • Identify and treat contacts as appropriate to suspected STD (see CDC 2021 STI guidelines MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021 for other etiologies and treatment options)
    • Evaluate for HIV/Hepatitis B risk.
    • Presumptive therapy for syphilis exposure within 3 months, as tests may be negative. Make effort to diagnose syphilis.

Etiologies

  • Neisseria gonorrhoeae
  • Chlamydia trachomatis
  • Treponema pallidum
  • Trichomonas vaginalis
  • Also consider implications for HIV and Hepatitis B exposure

Primary Regimens

  • Sexual assault
    • Anatomic female (Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM)  + (Metronidazole 500 mg po bid x 7 days) + [(Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x 7 days)]. See Comments.
    • Anatomic male (Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM) or (Cefixime 400 mg po single dose)] + (Metronidazole 2 gm po single dose) + [ (Doxycycline 100 mg po bid for 7 days)].
    • Give 1 g IM Ceftrixone if >150kg
    • HIV and/or Hepatitis B post-exposure prophylaxis as appropriate
  • Sexual contact, to cover gonococci and chlamydia:
    • (Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM once) for Gonococci + (Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x 7 days) for Chlamydia
    • Give 1 g Ceftrixone if >150kg
  • Syphilis, post-exposure prophylaxis: Open label randomized study in men that have sex with men (Lancet Infect Dis 2018;18:308).
    • Doxycycline 200 mg po x 1 dose within 72 hrs of exposure. 73% decrease in development of syphilis

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Hepatitis C is not readily transmitted from usual sexual activity, but consider test in high risk circumstances, e.g., HIV-infected MSM (MMWR 60:945, 2011).
  • CDC 2021 Guidelines for sexually-transmitted infections: MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021
  • For review of overall care of adult sexual assault survivor: N Engl J Med 365:834, 2011.
