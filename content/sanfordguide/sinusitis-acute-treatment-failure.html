Sinusitis, Acute, Failure of Empiric Therapy

Clinical Setting

  • Acute sinusitis, clinical failure after 3 days of empiric antibacterial treatment:
    • Persistent sinus pain, nasal congestion, purulent nasal discharge and, perhaps, fever despite recommended therapy.
  • Could result from:
    • Infection due to an antibiotic resistant organism
    • Obstruction of the ostium of one or more sinuses
    • Rarely, deep fungal infection (especially if immunocompromised patient or insulin-dependent diabetic
    • Sinusitis due to a non-infectious disease; e.g., Granulomatosis with polyangiitis (Formerly Wegener's granulomatosis)
  • Optional diagnostic method: collection of sinus exudate for culture by either sinus puncture or direct sinus endoscopy.
  • For initial empiric therapy of acute sinusitis, see Sinusitis, Acute,  Empiric Therapy.

Etiologies

  • Strep. pneumoniae (33%)
  • H. influenzae (32%)
  • M. catarrhalis (9%)
  • Streptococcus sp. (Group A) (2%)
  • Anaerobes (6%)
  • Viral (15%)
  • Staph. aureus
    • See Clin Infect Dis 54:826, 2012; Clin Infect Dis 45:e121, 2007: Data analysis suggests S. aureus should NOT be considered a potential etiology of acute uncomplicated sinusitis; incidence of positive cultures same as the incidence in asymptomatic  controls. Hence, positive culture may represent only colonization.

Primary Regimens

Empiric Therapy Failure Adjustment

  • Child: Empiric second line recommendations in the absence of cultures:
    • For children failing Amoxicillin: Amoxicillin-clavulanate (extra strength) suspension 90 mg/kg/day (Amox component) po divided q12h for 10-14 days.
    • For children failing Amoxicillin-clavulanate: Clindamycin 30-40 mg/kg/day divided q8h + (Cefuroxime axetil 30 mg/kg/day po divided q12h, Cefdinir 14 mg/kg/day divided q12h or q24h, or Cefpodoxime 10 mg/kg/day po divided q12h). Note: Cefuroxime susp not available in the US, may be available in other markets.
  • Adult: If failure to respond, empiric therapy pending culture results;
    • If mild (outpatient): Amoxicillin-clavulanate 1000mg/62.5 mg tabs; Tabs 2 po bid or Levofloxacin 750 mg po once daily
    • If severe (inpatient): Ceftriaxone 1-2 gm IV q24 h or Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 gm IV q6h or Levofloxacin 750 mg IV/po q24h
    • If no response within 24 hrs, CT scan of sinuses and surgical consultation

Alternative Regimens

Directed/Specific Therapy

  • Base choice of agent on results of  culture and susceptibility testing
