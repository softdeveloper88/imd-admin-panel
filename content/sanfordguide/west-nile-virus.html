West Nile Virus

Clinical Setting

  • A flavivirus transmitted by mosquitoes (Culex sp.), blood transfusions, transplanted organs and breastfeeding. Birds (>200 species) are the main host with humans and horses as incidental hosts.
  • Usually presents as a nonspecific febrile disease but 1/150 cases develops meningoencephalitis, aseptic meningitis or polio-like paralysis (Ann Intern Med 140:545, 2004; J Clin Invest 113:1102, 2004).
  • Long-term sequelae (neuromuscular weakness and psychiatric) are common (Clin Infect Dis 43:723, 2006). Evidence of long-term, persistent infection in some (J Infect Dis 201:2, 2010)
  • Diagnosis by elevated IgM in serum and CSF or CSF PCR (contact State Health Dept/CDC).
  • Blood supply is tested in U.S. due to increased serum lipase in 11/17 cases (N Engl J Med 352:420, 2005).

Etiologies

  • West Nile Virus

Primary Regimens

  • No proven antiviral therapy to date.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Interferon alfa-N3 (Clin Infect Dis 40:764, 2005) , ribavirin, and IVIG are not effective.
  • Summary of therapeutic targets and drugs in development (Expert Opinion Drug Discovery, 2020)
  • Several vaccine candidates are being evaluated
