Cellulitis, Buccal

Clinical Setting

  • Woody induration of the soft tissues of the cheek or face in children less than 5 years old
    • Usually due to H. influenza; marked decrease in incidence due to effectiveness of the H. influenza immunization.
    • Can occur in infants prior to immunization.
  • Marked cheek swelling with minimal trismus and systemic symptoms, e.g., fever.

Etiologies

  • Haemophilus influenzae, type B
  • Streptococcus pneumoniae (rare)

Primary Regimens

  • Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IV q24h

Alternative Regimens

  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 45-90 mg/kg po divided bid
  • TMP-SMX 8-12 mg/kg (TMP component) po/IV divided bid

Comments

  • Immunization has decreased rates of invasive haemophilus and pneumococcal infections. See immunize.org/askexperts.
