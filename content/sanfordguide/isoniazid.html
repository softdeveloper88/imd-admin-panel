Isoniazid
Tradename: Nydrazid, Laniazid, Teebaconin
Drug Shortage Information
Prep Started At Notes
100 mg, 300 mg tablets 2022-09-01

Usage and Dosing

  • First line anti-tuberculosis drug.
  • Resistance rates
    • U.S. born: ~5%
    • Foreign born: ~10%
    • Rates are twice as high in those with previous history of TB
  • Directly observed therapy (DOT) advised for treatment of active TB
  • Isoniazid monitoring:
    • Baseline liver enzymes not routinely recommended in latent TB except for patients with HIV, liver disease, immediate post-partum < 3 months, or regular alcohol use
    • Baseline liver enzymes recommended in treatment of active TB; repeat liver function tests monthly if there were abnormalities at baseline, symptoms consistent with hepatotoxicity develop, or for patients who chronically consume alcohol, take other potentially hepatotoxic medications, or have viral hepatitis or history of liver disease, human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) infection, or prior drug-induced liver injury.
  • Add pyridoxine to Isoniazid.
  • Avoid crushing tablets. Mean AUC reduced by 42% in 20 subjects treated for MDR-TB (Int J Tuberc Lung Dis 2019;23:1068).
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see Major Drug Interactions chart below, not all-inclusive).

      Adverse Effects

      • Overall approximately 1%
      • Liver:
        • Hepatitis (children 10% mild increase AST, normalizes with continued therapy, age <20 years rare, 20–34 years 1.2%, ≥50 years 2.3%)
        • May be fatal.
        • With prodromal symptoms, dark urine do LFTs; discontinue if AST > 3–5x normal.
      • Peripheral neuropathy:
        • 17% on 6 mg/kg/day, less on 300 mg, incidence increases in slow acetylators.
        • Pyridoxine 10-50 mg q24h will decrease incidence.
      • Others:
        • Convulsions, optic neuritis, toxic encephalopathy, psychosis, muscle twitching, dizziness, coma (all rare).
        • Allergic skin rashes, fever, minor disulfiram-like reaction, flushing after Swiss cheese.
        • Photosensitivity (confirmed by rechallenge)
        • Blood dyscrasias (rare); positive antinuclear (20%).
