Miltefosine
Tradename: Impavido

Usage and Dosing

  • Miltefosine, an alkylphosphorylcholine compound, was initially developed as an anticancer agent but found to have activity against Leishmania parasites.
  • Mechanism of action vs Leishmania: Primarily via disruption of parasite lipid metabolism and interference with activation of host cell lipid signaling pathways, inducing apoptosis in infected cells. May also affect calcium homeostasis in Leishmania (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 2025;19:e0012735).
  • Miltefosine 50 mg tabs FDA approved for cutaneous, mucocutaneous, and visceral disease.  
    • Must be ordered directly from Profounda, Inc. 407-270-7790. www.impavido.com. (patient assistance program available).  
  • Also licensed in Germany, India, Central and South America but not widely available. BMJ Glob Health 3:e000709, 2018.
    • Miltefosine in combination with other anti-leishmanial agents for various leishmania species and various clinical scenarios; see Leishmaniasis
    • Data on shorter courses for new world cutaneous leishmaniasis when combined with thermotherapy (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 2022; 16(3):e0010238).
    • Data on miltefosine X 14d combined L-AMB for visceral leishmaniasis with HIV infection (Lancet Infect Dis 2022 Aug;22(8):1124).
    • Data on miltefosine plus paromomycin for refractory VL in East Africa (Clin Infect Dis 2022 Sep 27;ciac643).
    • Black box warning: Contraindicated in pregnancy. Based on animal data, may cause fetal harm. Verify pregnancy status prior to initiation of therapy. Females of reproductive potential should use effective contraception during treatment and for five months after the last dose.
    • Contraindicated in patients with Sjogren-Larsson syndrome.

        Adverse Effects

        • Side-effects vary: kala-azar patients, vomiting in up to 40%, diarrhea in 17%, “motion sickness." headache, increased serum creatinine.
        • Daily dose >150 mg can cause severe GI side effects (Lancet 352:1821, 1998).
        • Increased ALT, AST, and bilirubin.
        • Reports of thrombocytopenia.
        • Reports of Stevens-Johnson syndrome. Discontinue miltefosine if an exfoliative or bullous rash occurs during therapy.
        • Reports of decreased ejaculation in males during therapy.

        Pregnancy Risk

        FDA Risk Category 1 Contraindicated. May cause fetal harm, based on animal data.
        Use during Lactation Safety not established, avoid use
