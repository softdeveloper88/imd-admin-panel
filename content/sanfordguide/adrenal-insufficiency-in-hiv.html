DDx: Adrenal Insufficiency, HIV Patient

Clinical Syndrome

  • Adrenal insufficiency in HIV patients presents as orthostatic hypotension with or without  nausea and vomiting. Patient may be febrile. Classic laboratory abnormalities: Hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, low plasma AM cortisol levels with a poor response in a cortrosyn stimulation test.

Differential Diagnosis

  • Infection of adrenal gland by CMV, mycobacteria, cryptococci, histoplasmosis or perhaps HIV itself
  • Destruction by neoplastic disease: Kaposi's sarcoma or lymphoma
  • Drug-induced inhibition of cortisol synthesis: e.g., Ketoconazole
  • Pituitary insufficiency (rare)

Treatment

  • For activities of daily living: hydrocortisone 15-20 mg po upon awakening and 5-10 mg po in the mid afternoon.
  • Alternatives: either dexamethasone 0.5 mg po or prednisone 5 mg po at bedtime with 5-10 mg po of hydrocortisone in the early afternoon.
  • Add fludrocortisone 0.1 mg po daily
  • If an adrenal crisis, need stress doses:
    • Dexamethasone 4 mg IV over 1-5 minutes and then repeat q12h. Dexamethasone does not interfere with measurements of plasma cortisol.
    • Alternative if dexamethasone is not available: Hydrocortisone 100 mg IV and repeat every 6 hrs.
