Cabotegravir
Tradename: Vocabria, Apretude

Usage and Dosing

  • Oral cabotegravir (CAB) is an integrase strand transfer inhibitor (INSTI) indicated for use (in combination with oral rilpivirine or, in the case of PrEP use, CAB alone) as oral lead-in therapy to assess tolerability prior to administration of the injectable forms, and as oral therapy for patients who will miss planned injection dosing.
  • Patients should take oral cabotegravir and rilpivirine or CAB alone at approximately the same time each day, with food.
  • Cabotegravir extended-release injectable suspension is indicated for pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) to reduce the risk of sexually acquired HIV-1 infection in at-risk adults and adolescents weighing ≥35 kg. 
  • For injectable two-drug combination of cabotegravir + rilpivirine, see Cabenuva.

Adult Dose

  • Oral lead-in dosing: cabotegravir 30 mg (+ rilpivirine 25 mg for treatment dosing; without rilpivirine for PrEP use) once daily for one month. The last oral dose should be taken on the same day that injections are commenced.
  • If a patient plans to miss a scheduled injection by more than seven days, daily oral therapy with cabotegravir 30 mg + rilpivirine 25 mg is taken to replace up to two consecutive monthly injections. The first dose of oral therapy should be taken approximately one month after the last injections and continued until the day injection dosing is resumed.
  • For PrEP:
    • Regimen consists of two initiation injections followed by continuation injections.
    • Initiation injections: Cabotegravir 600 mg/3 mL IM, repeat in one month.
    • Continuation injections: Cabotegravir 600 mg/3 mL IM every two months, beginning two months after the second initiation injection.
    • Lead-in with oral cabotegravir (30 mg po once daily x28 days) may be administered before administering the first initiation injection (to assess tolerability). If oral lead-in is used, administer the first initiation injection on the last day of oral lead-in or within three days thereafter.
    • The second initiation injection, or any continuation injection, may be administered up to seven days before or after the scheduled administration date.
    • The ventrogluteal site is recommended for injection. A dorsogluteal approach (upper outer quadrant) is also acceptable, if preferred by the healthcare professional. Do not administer by any other route or anatomical site.

Adverse Effects

  • Most common adverse reactions during lead-in (all <1%): headache, nausea, abnormal dreams, anxiety, insomnia.
  • Hypersensitivity (signs and symptoms include rash, fever, malaise, fatigue, myalgia, arthralgia, blisters, mucosal involvement, conjunctivitis, facial edema, hepatitis, eosinophilia, angioedema, dyspnea).
  • Hepatotoxicity.
  • Depressive disorders.

Comments

  • Rifabutin can be coadministered with oral cabotegravir, but is contraindicated with injectable cabotegravir/rilpivirine.
  • CAB for PrEP: When rifabutin is started before or with the first initiation injection, the recommended CAB dose is 600 mg/3 mL IM followed 2 weeks later by 600 mg/3 mL IM and monthly thereafter (while on rifabutin). When rifabutin is started at the time of the second initiation injection or later, the recommended CAB dose is 600 mg/3 mL monthly (while on rifabutin). After stopping rifabutin, the recommended CAB dose is 600 mg/3 mL IM every 2 months.
