HIV Genotype Resistance Mutations: PI

Genotype Resistance Mutations: PI

  • Mutations in the protease gene associated with resistance to Protease Inhibitors (PIs).
  • In general, multiple mutations are needed for high-level resistance.
  • Cross-resistance is common, e.g., mutations at codons 82, 84 & 90.
  • Exceptions: no cross-resistance for D30N nelfinavir & I50L atazanavir mutations.

Mutations

  • Major mutations are BOLD.
  • Table Key: Header = letter code for amino acid encoded in wild type virus; Number = mutated codon; Letter = amino acid encoded in mutated virus.
  • ATV = Atazanavir, DRV = Darunavir, FOS = Fosamprenavir, IDV = Indinavir, LPV = Lopinavir, NFV = Nelfinavir, SQV = Saquinavir, TPV = Tipranavir, /r = ritonavir-boosted
  • For the most current genotype resistance mutations tables go to IAS-USA or http://hivdb.stanford.edu
