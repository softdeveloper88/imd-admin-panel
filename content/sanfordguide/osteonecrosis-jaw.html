Osteonecrosis, Jaw

Clinical Setting

  • Osteonecrosis of the jaw, medication related, typically seen in oncology patients.
  • Infection is secondary to bone necrosis and loss of overlying mucosa.

Etiologies

  • Adverse reaction to bisphosphonates
  • Denosumab
  • Angiogenesis inhibitors (e.g., bevacizumab)

Primary Regimens

  • Minimal surgical debridement.
  • Chlorohexidine rinses, optimize oral hygiene.
  • Role of antimicrobial therapy and optimal regimens not well established (Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2016 Feb 26;2:CD008455).
  • Numerous agents have been used but antibiotics should cover oral aerobic and anaerobic organisms (see Oral Surg Oral Med Oral Pathol Oral Radiol 2017;123(4):436) (e.g., Piperacillin-tazobactam, Ampicillin-sulbactam, Moxifloxacin, Amoxicillin-clavulanate, Doxycycline).

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy not well established, highly variable (see J Oral Maxillofac Surg 69:362, 2011).
  • 3-4 weeks of pre-operative antibiotics followed by 3-4 weeks of post-operative antibiotics is a typical regimen

Comments

  • References: Cureus. 2023; 15:e51183;  Int J Oral Sci. 2020;12:30; J Oral Maxillofac Surg. 2022;80:920-943.
