Acute Rheumatic Fever, Empiric Therapy

Clinical Setting

  • Non-suppurative immunologic sequelae 2-4 weeks after group A streptococcal pharyngitis.
  • Five major manifestations:
    • Carditis
    • Migratory large joint polyarthritis
    • Chorea
    • Subcutaneous nodules
    • Erythema marginatum
  • Evidence of prior group A streptococcal infection
    • Rising ASO or DNAase titer or positive culture or rapid streptococcal antigen test
  • See Rheumatic Fever, Prophylaxis for recommendations for prevention of recurrence
  • See also:  Post-streptococcal reactive arthritis

Etiologies

  • Strep. pyogenes (Group A streptococcus)

Primary Regimens

  • Relief of symptoms (fever, arthritis, arthralgia):
    • Aspirin 80-100 mg/kg/day (pediatric), 4-8 gm/day (adult)
    • May add Prednisone 2 mg/kg po q24h (see Comments)
  • Eradication of group A streptococcus
    • Child: Penicillin VK 250 mg po tid x 10 days
    • Adult: Penicillin VK 500 mg po tid x 10 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Eradication of group A streptococcus
    • Child:
      • Benzathine penicillin G
        • Wt > 27 kg: 1,200,000 units IM x 1 dose
        • Wt< 27 kg: 600,000 units IM x1
      • Cephalexin 25-50 mg/kg/day po in 2-4 divided doses x 10 days
      • Azithromycin 10 mg/kg (max dose 500 mg) po day 1, then 5 mg/kg (max dose 250 mg) po q24h for days 2-5
    • Adult:
      • Benzathine penicillin G 1.2 million units IM x 1 dose
      • Cephalexin 500 mg po bid x 10 days
      • Azithromycin 500 mg po day 1, then 250 mg q24h days 2-5

Comments

  • Cochrane review found no evidence of benefit of corticosteroids or IVIG compared to aspirin (Cochrane Database Syst Rev May 28;5:CD003176, 2015)  
  • Reference: Pediatr Rev. 2021 May;42(5):221-232.
