Asplenia, Bacteremia, Prevention

Clinical Setting

  • Prevention of infection in anatomic or functional asplenia e.g., bacteremia:
    • Sickle cell disease with functional splenic insufficiency
    • Pre- or post surgical splenectomy
    • Post- dog bite Capnocytophaga species bacteremia/shock (See Shock-Post Splenectomy)
  • In addition, asplenic children due to sickle cell anemia, thalassemia, and perhaps others, daily antimicrobial prophylaxis until at least age 5 yrs : N Engl J Med 2017;376:1561.
  • Sepsis due to susceptible organisms may occur despite daily prophylaxis.
  • Reviews of care of the asplenic patient: N Engl J Med 371:349, 2014; Chest 2016;150:1394; N Engl J Med  2017;376:1561. 

Etiologies

  • Likely agents:
    • Streptococcus pneumoniae (90%)
    • Meningococci
    • Haemophilus influenzae, type b
  • Others:
    • Enterobacteriaceae
    • Staph. aureus
    • Capnocytophaga canimorsus (post-dog bite)
    • Bordetella holmesii (Lancet Infect Dis 2014;14:510)
    • P. aeruginosa (rare).
  • Also at increased risk for fatal malaria, severe babesiosis.

Primary Regimens

  • Antibiotic Prophylaxis:
    • Child age < 5 years:
      • Amoxicillin 20 mg/kg/day or Penicillin VK 125 mg bid
    • Age > 5 years:
      • Penicillin VK 250 mg bid x at least 1 year post-splenectomy
      • Some recommend prophylaxis for a minimum of 3 years or until at least age 18 years.
    • All ages:
      • Maintain immunizations PLUS
      • If not taking daily prophylaxis: self-administer Amoxicillin-clavulanate with any febrile illness while seeking physician assistance.
  • Sickle cell disease:
    • Child age < 5 years: Penicillin VK 125 mg po bid
    • Age > 5 years: Penicillin VK 250 mg po bid or Amoxicillin 20 mg/kg/day

Alternative Regimens

  • Alternative for self-administered therapy at onset of fever:
    • Cefuroxime axetil can be used in the penicillin-allergic patient who is not allergic to cephalosporins.
    • Alternatively, respiratory Fluoroquinolone can be considered in beta-lactam-allergic patient in appropriate populations.
    • If Penicillin allergy: TMP-SMX or Clarithromycin are options, but resistance in Strep. pneumoniae may be significant in some areas, particularly among penicillin-resistant isolates.
  • Post dog bite (C. canimorsus): See Shock-Post Splenectomy

Prevention

  • Ensure administration of pneumococcal vaccine (PCV20; Prevnar 20 or PCV21; Capvaxive), H. influenzae (type b) (one dose only), and both quadrivalent meningococcal vaccine (2 doses) and serogroup B meningococcal vaccines at recommended intervals for asplenic patients.  
    • See Pneumococcal, Vaccine (Adult), Meningococcal ACWY, Vaccines, Meningococcal B, Vaccines for indications, available products, dosing, and vaccine characteristics for pre-exposure prevention.
    • See Recommended Immunizations Indicated for Adults Based on Medical and Other Conditions for a consolidated ACIP Table
    • Ideally administer first doses in advance of elective surgical splenectomy otherwise as soon as possible thereafter. See Clin Infect Dis 73:e1489, 2021.
  • See above for prophylactic antibiotic considerations.

Comments

  • Sickle cell disease: Start prophylaxis by 2 months. Continue until at least age 5. When to discontinue must be individualized. Ensure age-appropriate vaccines, including pneumococcal, Hib, influenza, meningococcal: Clin Infect Dis 58:309, 2014.
  • Treating infections, consider possibility of penicillin non-susceptible pneumococci.
  • Asplenic state recognized as risk for thrombosis and pulmonary hypertension in addition to risk of severe infection: Chest 2016;150:1394.
