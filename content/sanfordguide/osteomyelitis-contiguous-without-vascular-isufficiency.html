Osteomyelitis, Contiguous Focus

Clinical Setting

  • Osteomyelitis with a contiguous focus of infection and without vascular insufficiency.
  • Involves long bone or post internal fixation of fracture.
  • Empiric therapy indicated in septic patient, otherwise await culture results.

Etiologies

  • Staph. aureus
  • Coagulase-negative staphylococci
  • Gram-negative bacilli
  • Streptococcal species
  • P. aeruginosa

Primary Regimens

  • Empiric therapy (for acutely ill patient, otherwise wait for culture and susceptibility data):
    • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) + (Ceftazidime 2 gm IV q8h OR Cefepime 2 gm IV q12h)
  • Specific therapy based on culture results:
    • If susceptible gram-negative bacillus:
      • Ciprofloxacin 750 mg IV or po bid OR Levofloxacin 750 mg IV or po q24h
    • If methicillin-sensitive staphylococci:
      • Nafcillin 2 gm IV q4h OR Oxacillin 2 gm IV q4h OR Cefazolin 2 gm IV q8h
    • If methicillin-resistant staphylococci:
      • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL

Alternative Regimens

  • Empiric therapy for acutely ill patient
    • Linezolid 600 mg po/IV bid or Daptomycin 10 mg/kg IV q24h + Meropenem 1 gm IV q8h
  • Oral regimens for methicillin-susceptible or methicillin-resistant staphylococci once susceptibility is confirmed:
    • Ciprofloxacin 750 mg po bid + Rifampin 300-450 mg po bid OR
    • Levofloxacin 750 mg po once daily + Rifampin 600 mg po once daily 
    • Linezolid 600 mg po bid (potential toxicity if used for longer than 2-3 weeks)

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • If possible withhold empirical therapy until appropriate cultures have been obtained.
  • De-escalate based on culture results.
  • See comments below for duration of therapy

Comments

  • Duration of therapy not well defined but 6-8 weeks is generally recommended if no hardware.
  • For hardware infections
    • Ideally, remove hardware
    • Early hardware infection (symptoms < 4 weeks)
      • After  hardware is removed treat for 6 weeks
      • If hardware is retained because of bony non-union, treat until bony fusion; once the hardware if removed, treat for an additional 6 weeks.
    • Late infection
      • Remove hardware and then treat for 6 weeks
      • If retained hardware, treat for 3-6 months (risk of treatment failure, relapse if hardware is retained)
        • Once the hardware is removed, treat for another 6 weeks. 
    • Treatment recommendations are modeled after those for periprosthetic joint infections (see Clin Infect Dis. 2023; 77:e34)
  • For persistent bone non-union, may be necessary to remove hardware and use external fixation.
