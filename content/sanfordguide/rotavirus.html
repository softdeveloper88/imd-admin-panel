Rotavirus

Clinical Setting

  • Leading recognized cause of diarrhea-related illness among infants and children worldwide. Vaccine has greatly reduced burden where used in both developed and resource-poor countries.
  • Kills one-half million children annually.

Etiologies

  • Rotavirus

Primary Regimens

  • No antiviral therapy available.
  • Fluid replacement is life-saving

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Prevention

  • Two live-attenuated vaccines highly effective (85 and 98%) and safe in preventing rotavirus diarrhea and hospitalization (N Engl J Med 354:11, 2006 & N Engl J Med 354:23, 2006).
    ACIP (Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices) recommends either of the two vaccines, RV1 or RV5, for infants (MMWR 58(RR-2):1, 2009).  See CDC vaccine page for children
  • Vaccine has been rolled out in over 120 countries worldwide.  Summary in Vaccines 10:346, 2022.
  • Widespread implementation of Rotavirus Vaccination in infants / young children has reduced morbidity and mortality by over half in Sub-Saharan Africa in meta-analysis (PlosOne April, 2020)

Comments

  • Earlier schedule of RV5 vaccine (starting at 2 -5 weeks of age) appears safe and effective in a pilot study (J Ped Infect Dis Soc 7: 82, 2018)
