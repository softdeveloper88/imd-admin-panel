Smallpox

Clinical Setting

  • Post-exposure prophylaxis for exposure to smallpox virus.
  • Contact vaccinia resulting from smallpox vaccination.
  • Smallpox disease presents as either:
    • Variola major, a serious illness with a mortality rate of 30% to 50% in the unvaccinated persons
    • Variola minor, a milder infection with a mortality of < 1%.

Etiologies

  • Variola virus

Primary Regimens

  • Post-exposure prophylaxis:
    • Administer Smallpox vaccine (if within 4 days of exposure) + Tecovirimat (Tpoxx) 600 mg (three 200 mg capsules) po bid x 14 days OR
    • Administer Smallpox vaccine (if within 4 days of exposure) + Cidofovir (dose is uncertain, but likely similar to cytomegalovirus (CMV) dose (5 mg/kg IV once weekly for 2 weeks followed by once weekly dosing)
      • Must be used with hydration and Probenecid
    • Contact the CDC: +1 770-488-7100
    • See Comments regarding Tecovirimat
  • Contact vaccinia (post vaccination):
    • Immune globulin may be of benefit. To obtain immune globulin, contact CDC: +1 770-488-7100. (Clin Infect Dis 39:759, 2004; Clin Infect Dis 39:776, 2004; Clin Infect Dis 39:819, 2004)

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Prevention

  • See Smallpox, Vaccine for indications, available products, dosing, and vaccine characteristics for pre- and post-exposure prevention.

Comments

  • Tecovirimat is indicated in adults and pediatric patients weighing at least 13 kg.
    •  Tecovirimat is approved by the FDA for use in this setting, however, No clinical trials demonstrating efficacy have been performed.
    •  Approval is based on in vitro activity of the drug and the absence of alternative regimens (other than cidofovir, which also has no clinical data supporting its use).
    • It's unknown whether (and how much) co-administration of an antiviral (e.g., tecovirimat or cidofovir) will interfere with the effectiveness of Smallpox vaccine immune responses
    • Reference: New Engl J Med 2018;379:44.
    • Review of tecovirimat:  Expert Rev Anti-Inf Therapy 19 (3): 331, 2020
  • Preparedness and response to a smallpox emergency, see: https://www.cdc.gov/vaccinesafety/ensuringsafety/monitoring/emergencypreparedness/index.html.
