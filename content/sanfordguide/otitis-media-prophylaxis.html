Otitis Media, Prophylaxis

Clinical Setting

  • Antibiotic prophylaxis to prevent recurrent acute otitis media (AOM) in children
    • Selects for antibiotic-resistant Strep. pneumoniae
    • Is not recommended in current AAP guidelines (Pediatrics 131:e964, 2013)
    • Reduces the number of episodes by about half but risks may outweigh benefits
  • Pneumococcal protein conjugate vaccine decreases frequency of acute otitis media infections.
  • Evidence supporting benefits of adenoidectomy are inconclusive (Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2010:CD008282).

Etiologies

  • Strep. pneumoniae
  • H. influenzae
  • M. catarrhalis
  • Staph. aureus
  • Streptococcus sp. (Group A)

Primary Regimens

  • Prophylactic antibiotics are not recommended
  • Recurrent AOM: Antibiotics may be prescribed to reduce the frequency of episodes of AOM in children

Alternative Regimens

  • Tympanostomy tubes may be offered for recurrent AOM (3 episodes in 6 months or 4 episodes in 1 year with 1 episode in the preceding 6 months) (see Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2018 May 9;5:CD012017).
    • Avoid concomitant fluoroquinolone ear drops increased risk of TM perforation requiring tympanoplasty (Clin Inf Dis 2017;64: 1052)

Comments

  • Reference: Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2006:CD004401
