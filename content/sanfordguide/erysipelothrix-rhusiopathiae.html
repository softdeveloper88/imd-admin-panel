Erysipelothrix rhusiopathiae

Clinical Setting

  • Positive lab result: recovery of the organism in culture.
  • Causes localized (without systemic symptoms) or diffuse (systemic symptoms present) skin lesions, bacteremia, or endocarditis.
  • Zoonotic infection of fishermen, fish handlers, butchers acquired by direct animal contact, most commonly with fish, but also with other animals (e.g., swine).

Classification

  • Gram-positive, pleomorphic rod non-sporulating

Primary Regimens

  • Localized skin infection. Usual treatment is for 7 days.
    • Penicillin VK 500 mg po qid or Amoxicillin 500 mg po tid
    • Cephalexin 500mg po q6h
    • Ciprofloxacin 250 mg po bid or Levofloxacin 750 mg po once daily
    • Clindamycin 300 mg po q8h
  • Widespread skin infection or bacteremia. Often requires 4 weeks or more of therapy.
    • Penicillin G 2-4 million units IV q4h
    • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h
    • Imipenem-cilastatin 500 mg IV q6h (other carbapenems also active)
    • Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q12h or Levofloxacin 750 mg IV q24h

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Resistant to Vancomycin, antipseudomonal aminoglycosides, TMP-SMX.
  • Not consistently active vs macrolides, chloramphenicol and tetracyclines.
