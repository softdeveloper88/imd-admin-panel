Angiostrongylus cantonensis

Clinical Setting

  • Eosinophilic meningitis; angiostrongyliasis.
    • Severe headache, cranial nerve (6 or 7) palsy, migratory trunk and limb pain, dysethesias, urinary hesitancy.
  • Rat lungworm; eggs excreted to environment. Ingestion of larvae in snails, slugs or salad/salad contaminated with slugs.
  • Southeast Asia, Pacific islands including current increased transmission in Hawaii (Am. J. Trop. Med. Hyg., 101; 608, 2019), expanding area of the south and gulf coast of the US (MMWR Morb Mortal Wkly Rep 2018,67:825; Emerg Infect Dis. 21:1234, 2015).
    • Invasive giant semi-slug Parmarion cf. martensi  implicated as host in Hawaii
  • MRI with leptomeningeal thickening and other distinctive signs; CT scan unhelpful.

Diagnosis

  • PCR of CSF available, serology unreliable and converts late.

Etiologies

  • Angiostrongylus cantonensis

Primary Regimens

  • Symptomatic therapy with analgesics and, if necessary, serial LPs as often as needed. 
    • Prednisone 60 mg/day (2 mg/kg for children up to 60 mg/day) x 14 days reduces duration, headache and need for LPs (Trans R Soc Trop Med Hyg 102:990, 2008) but not indicated for mild disease.  Slow taper of prednisone for those with severe disease.

Alternative Regimens

  • Adding Albendazole 15 mg/kg/day x 14 days or Mebendazole 10 mg/kg/day x 14 days to corticosteroid treatment may minimally decrease median duration of headache and need for repeat LP based on several small studies.  
    • Pros and cons of albendazole/mebendazole (Parasitology 2021 Feb;148(2):227).

Comments

  • Do not use Albendazole without steroid cover as may worsen disease. Albendazole benefit above steroid alone is marginal but not harmful.
  • Albendazole theoretically may be more effective early in infection with smaller larvae but diagnosis is often delayed
  • Other parasitic causes of eosinophilic meningitis include Gnathostoma spinigerum and Baylisascaris procyonis.
  • Vermox brand mebendazole 500 mg tablets is FDA-approved but for donation only and not sold in the U.S.
  • Albendazole 200 mg tablets available from Expert Compounding Pharmacy (see Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources). Generic albendazole (Albenza) marketed by Amneal are very costly and is difficult for retail pharmacies to access.
