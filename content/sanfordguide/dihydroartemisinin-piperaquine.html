Dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine
Tradename: Eurartesim

Usage and Dosing

  • Anti-malarial drug approved in Europe for treatment of uncomplicated P. falciparum and as part of national programs in southeast Asia.
    • SE Asian programs no longer using due to resistance development
  • Artemisinin resistance is present in the Greater Mekong subregion of Asia Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2021; AAC0112121 and in Eritrea, Rwanda and Uganda.
    • Artemisinin resistance alone rarely leads to treatment failure but resistance to both the artemisinin derivative and the partner ACT drug (Mekong region only) does.
  • Good review of drug-drug interactions with artemisinin-based combination therapies: J Clin Pharmacol 2022;62:1197. Also see Major Interactions below.
  • Caution: Numerous drug interactions (see Major Drug Interactions chart below, not all-inclusive).

        Adverse Effects

        • Contraindications:
          • Severe malaria
          • QTc interval prolongation
            • DHA-PPQ should be taken more than 3 h after food and patients should not eat for 3 h after doses to prevent excessive peak piperaquine levels. DHA-PPQ should not be used in patients with previous arrhythmias, cardiac conditions that predispose to arrhythmia, or those taking drugs that prolong the QT interval. ECGs should be monitored. 
          • Arrythmias, bradycardia
          • Hypokalaemia, hypocalcaemia or hypomagnesaemia
        • Adverse effects (common)
          • Anemia, headache, QTc-prolonged tachycardia, asthenia, pyrexia
        • Adverse effects (uncommon)
          • Respiratory infection, influenza, anorexia, convulsion, dizziness, arrythmias, bradycardia, cough, vomiting, diarrhea, nausea, abdominal pain, hepatitis, hepatomegaly, abnormal liver function tests, pruitis, arthalgia, myalgia
