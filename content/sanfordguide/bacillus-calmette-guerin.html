Bacillus Calmette-Guerin

Clinical Setting

  • Iatrogenic infection caused by administration of Bacillus Calmette-Guerin (BCG), a vaccine strain derived from Mycobacterium bovis:
    • For immunization
    • As as complication of intravesicular administration for immunotherapy of bladder cancer
  • Resistant to Pyrazinamide.
  • Treatment depends on presentation:
    • Febrile illness only (>38.5°C x 12-24 hours)
    • Systemic illness or sepsis
    • Hepatitis
    • Pneumonitis
    • Arthritis

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium bovis/BCG strain

Primary Regimens

  • Moderate cystitis persistent fevers beyond 48 hours
    • Optimal therapy unknown. 
    • Consider symptomatic treatment for less severe symptoms.  For more severe disease, loner duration of symptoms treat with the regimen below.
      • Duration of therapy unclear and based on severity of symptoms and clinical response.
  • Severe cystitis with persistent fevers, disseminated disease outside the bladder, systemic illness, sepsis.
    • Discontinue BCG instillations
    • (Isoniazid 300 mg + Rifampin 600 mg + Ethambutol 1200 mg) q24h x 2 months then Isoniazid + Rifampin for 4-7 months depending on disease severity and clinical response with a longer duration for those with extensive disease
  • Disseminated infection post-BCG vaccination:
    • Adult dose: (Isoniazid 300 mg + Rifampin 600 mg + Ethambutol 1200 mg) q24h x 2 months then Isoniazid + Rifampin for 4-7 months, depending on disease severity and clinical response with a longer duration for those with extensive disease and/or immunodeficiency
    • Pediatric dosing for above: See First-Line TB Drug Dosing

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Also susceptible to Rifabutin, Moxifloxacin, Levofloxacin
  • See BCG, Vaccine
    • BCG vaccination may cause regional adenitis, pulmonary disease, or disseminated infection in HIV-infected children.
  • See also, Mycobacterium bovis.
