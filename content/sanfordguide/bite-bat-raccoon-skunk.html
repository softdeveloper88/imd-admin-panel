Bite: Bat, Raccoon, Skunk

Clinical Setting

  • Bite wound from bat, raccoon or skunk.
  • Such animals are considered potentially rabid unless testing of euthanized animal proves negative.

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus sp.
  • Staphylococcus sp.
  • Rabies (See Comments)

Primary Regimens

  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate (875/125 mg po bid or 500/125 mg po tid)

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid

Comments

  • Administer tetanus toxoid or Tdap (preferred) if > 10 years since last dose
  • Raccoons, skunks, foxes, and most other carnivores as well as bats, presume rabid unless definitely proved negative by lab testing.
  • See Rabies, Vaccines and PEP for detailed evaluation procedure and recommendations.
