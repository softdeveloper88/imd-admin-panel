Triumeq
Tradename: Triumeq: Abacavir + Dolutegravir + Lamivudine

Usage and Dosing

  • Triumeq is a once-daily, fixed-dose triple combination regimen for patients with HIV infection.
  • Must be HLA-B*5701 negative.
  • Available as tablets (ABC 600 mg, DTG 50 mg, 3TC 300 mg) for adults, and PD tablets for oral suspension (ABC 60 mg, DTG 5 mg, 3TC 30 mg) for pediatric patients weighing 10 to <25 kg. The two dosage forms must not be interchanged on a mg-for-mg basis due to differing PK profiles of the DTG component. The PD tablets are not recommended for use in patients weighing ≥25 kg.
  • See individual components for drug-specific data:
    • Abacavir (ABC)
    • Dolutegravir (DTG)
    • Lamivudine (3TC)
  • For class-wide adverse effects, see ARV Drugs, Overview.
  • Designated a preferred antiretroviral for pregnant women, regardless of trimester.
  • Black Box Warning: abacavir hypersensitivity reactions, exacerbations of hepatitis B in co-infected patients who discontinue lamivudine.
  • 2024 IAS-USA recommendation: Patients with, or at high risk for, cardiovascular disease who are receiving an abacavir-containing regimen should switch to a non-abacavir-containing regimen if an active regimen is available (JAMA 2024 Dec 1. doi: 10.1001/jama.2024.24543 [online ahead of print].
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).

      Adverse Effects

      • Abacavir or dolutegravir hypersensitivity reactions.
      • Exacerbation of hepatitis B after discontinuation of lamivudine (in patients co-infected with HIV and HBV).
      • Hepatotoxicity.
      • Lactic acidosis and severe hepatomegaly with steatosis.
      • Immune reconstitution syndrome (IRIS).
      • For further information, see individual components.
