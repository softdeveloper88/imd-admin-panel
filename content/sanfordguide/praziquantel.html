Praziquantel
Tradename: Biltricide

Usage and Dosing

  • Praziquantel is active against all Trematodes (Flukes) except Fasciola hepatica - dose, frequency and duration vary depending on the individual fluke. Also active against cestodes.
    • See Cestodes (Tapeworms) for dosing
      • Used in larval cestode disease including cysticercosis, hydatid disease, and sparganosis.
      • Note: Do not use if ocular cysticercosis present.
  • Only drug for therapy of Schistosomiasis.
  • Mechanism of action is unclear; drug disrupts outer layer of susceptible parasites and may enhance immune destruction.
  • No adequate studies in pregnant women, no difference in adverse birth outcomes during mass treatment campaigns.  
  • WHO encourages the use of praziquantel in any stage of pregnancy if risk-benefit favorable. 
  • For control programs WHO recommends for all persons >2 yrs old, including pregnant women after the first trimester and lactating women.

    • Low concentrations in human milk.  WHO guidelines encourage use in individual cases during lactation if favorable risk-benefit.

Adverse Effects

  • Well-tolerated, AEs mild & transient.
  • Mild: dizziness/drowsiness, nausea/vomiting, rash, fever. Only contraindication is ocular cysticercosis. Carefully observe patients with neurocysticercosis due to potential for exacerbation of disease with Praziquantel therapy.

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Category B. Available data suggest no difference in adverse birth outcomes in the children of women who were accidentally treated with PZQ during MDA campaigns compared with those who were not. In MDA campaigns for which the WHO has determined that the benefit of treatment outweighs the risk, WHO encourages the use of PZQ in any stage of pregnancy.
Use during Lactation PZQ is excreted in low concentrations in breast milk. According to WHO guidelines for MDA campaigns, the use of PZQ during lactation is encouraged. In other clinical settings, balance the risk of treatment in lactating women with the risk of disease progression in the absence of treatment.

Major Drug Interactions

  • Praziquantel serum levels are reduced in patients taking glucocorticoids, chloroquine, carbamazepine, or phenytoin.
  • Praziquantel increases serum levels of albendazole benefitting combination therapy for echinococcosis and neurocysticercosis.

Comments

  • Prolonged therapy such as for cysticercosis (sometimes used in combination with albendazole) extremely costly.
  • Obtain from Expert Compounding pharmacy (see Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources)
