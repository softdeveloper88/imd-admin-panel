Balantioides coli (formerly Balantidium Coli)

Clinical Setting

  • Ubiquitous porcine intestinal protozoa that uncommonly infects humans via fecal contamination.
  • Usually asymptomatic but invasive disease in a few due to unknown reasons.
  • If untreated, watery diarrhea progresses to fulminating dysentery with multiple intestinal perforations.

Etiologies

  • Balantioides coli (formerly Balantidium coli)

Primary Regimens

  • Adult: Tetracycline 500 mg po qid x 10 days
  • Pediatrics (>8 years of age): Tetracycline 40 mg/kg/day (max. 2 gm) in 4 doses x 10 days
    • AAP now permits tetracyclines <8 yrs of age for short courses (<21 days)

Alternative Regimens

  • Adult: Metronidazole 750 mg po tid x 5 days
  • Pediatrics: Metronidazole 35-50 mg/kg/day in 3 doses x 5 days; maximum 2 gm/day
  • Another alternative: Iodoquinol 650 mg po tid x 20 days
