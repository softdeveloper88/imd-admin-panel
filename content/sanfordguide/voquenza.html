Voquezna
Tradename: Voquezna

Usage and Dosing

  • Vonoprazan/Amoxicillin/Clarithromycin (Voquezna Triple Pak) and Vonoprazan/Amoxicillin (Voquezna Dual Pak) are indicated for the treatment of Helicobacter pylori infection in adults.
  • Co-packaged products consisting of vonoprazan + amoxicillin, with or without clarithromycin.
  • Vonoprazan is an oral potassium-competitive acid blocker (PCAB).
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see Major Drug Interactions chart below, not all-inclusive)

Adult Dose

  • Triple Pak (with or without food):
    • Vonoprazan 20 mg po bid x14 days
    • Amoxicillin 1000 mg po bid x14 days
    • Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid x14 days
  • Dual Pak (with or without food):
    • Vonoprazan 20 mg po bid x14 days
    • Amoxicillin 1000 mg po tid x14 days

Adverse Effects

  • Gastrointestinal (diarrhea, abdominal pain)
  • Hypersensitivity reactions
  • Severe cutaneous adverse reactions
  • C. difficile-associated diarrhea
  • Vulvovaginal candidiasis
  • Vonoprazan reduces intragastric acidity, which increases serum chromogranin A levels and may cause false positive results in diagnostic investigations for neuroendocrine tumors.
  • Dysgeusia (due to clarithromycin)
  • Hepatotoxicity (due to clarithromycin)
  • QT prolongation (due to clarithromycin)
  • High urine concentrations of amoxicillin may cause false-positive results when using glucose tests based on the Benedict’s copper reduction reaction that determines the amount of reducing substances like glucose in the urine.
