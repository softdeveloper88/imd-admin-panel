Candidiasis, CNS Infection

Clinical Setting

  • Candida meningitis
  • CSF or CNS biopsy culture positive for Candida sp.
  • Usually seen post-neurosurgical procedure, intraventricular drainage device, or with disseminated candidiasis.

Etiologies

  • Candida albicans
  • Other non-albicans Candida sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Liposomal Amphotericin B 3–5 mg/kg/day IV ± Flucytosine 25 mg/kg po qid

Alternative Regimens

  • Fluconazole 400–800 mg (6-12 mg/kg) IV/po (oral step-down therapy or in patients intolerant of Amphotericin B)
  • Voriconazole 400 mg (6 mg/kg) bid x 2 doses then 200 mg (4 mg/kg) q12h (oral step-down therapy or in patients intolerant of Amphotericin B when the Candida isolate is fluconazole resistant)

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Treat until resolution of CSF, radiographic and clinical abnormalities.

Comments

  • Removal of intraventricular devices recommended.
  • Echinocandins not recommended due to their poor CSF penetration.
  • For IDSA treatment guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 62:409, 2016.
