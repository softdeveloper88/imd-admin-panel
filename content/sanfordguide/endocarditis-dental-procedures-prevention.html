Endocarditis Prevention, Dental Procedures

Clinical Setting

  • Predisposing cardiac conditions and/or type of dental procedure guide prophylaxis recommendation.
  • Antibiotic prophylaxis is recommended for patients with the following predisposing cardiac conditions:
    • Prosthetic cardiac valve or prosthetic material used for cardiac valve repair.
    • Previous infective endocarditis (IE).
    • Congenital heart disease (CHD) (unrepaired cyanotic CHD, including palliative shunts and conduits; completely repaired congenital heart defect with prosthetic material or device within the first 6 months after the procedure; repaired CHD with residual defects at the site or adjacent to the site of a prosthetic patch or prosthetic device).
    • Cardiac transplantation recipients who develop cardiac valvulopathy.
  • Prophylaxis is recommended for the following dental procedures:
    • Any manipulation of gingival tissue, dental periapical regions, or perforation of the oral mucosa.
  • Prophylaxis is NOT RECOMMENDED for the following:
    • Cardiac conditions other than those listed above (e.g., mitral valve prolapse, gastrointestinal or genitourinary procedure).
    • Anesthetic injections (unless through infected area), dental x-rays, shedding of primary teeth, adjustment of orthodontic appliances or placement of orthodontic brackets or removable appliances, bleeding from trauma to the lips or oral mucosa.

Etiologies

  • Oral streptococci

Primary Regimens

  • Usual oral prophylaxis
    • Adult: Amoxicillin 2 gm po 30-60 minutes before procedure
    • Pediatric: Amoxicillin 50 mg/kg po 30-60 minutes before procedure

Alternative Regimens

  • Patient unable to take oral medications
    • Adult: Ampicillin 2 gm IV/IM  or Cefazolin 1 gm IV/IM or Ceftriaxone 1 gm IV/IM 30-60 minutes before procedure
    • Pediatric: Ampicillin 50 mg/kg IV/IM or Cefazolin 50 mg/kg IV/IM or Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IV/IM 30-60 minutes before procedure
  • Penicillin allergy, oral regimen (NOTE: Cephalosporins should not be administered to an individual with history of an immediate type hypersensitivity reaction to penicillins)
    • Adult:
      • Cephalexin 2 gm 30-60 minutes before procedure
      • Azithromycin or Clarithromyin 500 mg po 30-60 minutes before procedure
      • Doxycycline 100 mg 30-60 minutes before procedure
    • Pediatric:
      • Cephalexin 50 mg/kg po 30-60 mintues before procedure
      • Azithromycin or Clarithromyin 500 mg po 30-60 minutes before procedure
      • Doxycycline
        • < 45 kg, 2.2 mg/kg 30-60 minutes before procedure
        • > 45 kg, 100 mg 30-60 minutes before procedure
  • Both unable to take oral medications and penicillin allergy (NOTE: Cephalosporins should not be administered to an individual with history of an immediate type hypersensitivity reaction to penicillins)
    • Adult: Cefazolin 1 gm IV/IM or Ceftriaxone 1 gm IV/IM 30-60 minutes before procedure
    • Pediatric: Cefazolin 50 mg/kg IV/IM or Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IV/IM 30-60 minutes before procedure

Comments

  • Reference for most recent and updated American Heart Association (AHA) guidelines: Circulation. 2021;143:e963-e978.
  • Pediatric dose should not exceed the adult dose.
  • AHA lists other 1st or 2nd generation cephalosporins in equivalent doses as potential alternatives to Cephalexin.
