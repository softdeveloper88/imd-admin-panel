Candidiasis, Oropharyngeal

Clinical Setting

  • Thrush: creamy white patches overlying inflamed mucosa of buccal and pharyngeal mucosa, palate, and tonsils.
  • Erythematous variant with flat erythematous lesions.
  • Angular cheilitis with erythematous fissures in the corners of the mouth.

Etiologies

  • Candida albicans
  • Other non-albicans Candida sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Mild disease:
    • Clotrimazole troches 10 mg 5x/day x 7-14 days OR
    • Nystatin suspension 500,000 units (5 mL) swish & swallow qid x 7-14 days
  • Moderate-severe disease and/or HIV-positive patient:
    • Fluconazole 100-200 mg/day x 7-14 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Itraconazole solution 200 mg po daily x 7-14 days
  • Voriconazole 200 mg q12h x 7-14 days
  • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily
  • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs)
    • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily
    • Suspension 200 mg qid, then 400 mg po bid after stabilization of disease
    • Posaconazole IV 300 mg IV over 90 minutes bid x 1 day, then 300 mg IV daily
  • Caspofungin 70 mg loading dose, then 50 mg/day IV or Micafungin 100 mg/day IV or Anidulafungin 200 mg IV loading dose, then 100 mg/day IV x 7-14 days
  • Amphotericin B 0.3 mg/kg/day x 7-14 days

Comments

  • HIV-positive patients: Antiretroviral therapy (ART) is recommended to prevent recurrent disease.
  • Suppressive therapy in select immunocompromised patients with recurrent disease: Fluconazole 100 mg po 3x/week.
  • Dysphagia or odynophagia is predictive of esophageal candidiasis.
  • IDSA treatment guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 62:409, 2016
