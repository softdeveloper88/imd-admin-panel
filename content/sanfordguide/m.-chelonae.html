Mycobacterium chelonae

Clinical Setting

  • Non-tuberculous mycobacteria. Rapid grower.
  • Cause of wound, skin and soft tissue infections, disseminated and pulmonary infections, particularly in immunocomprosed hosts.
  • Infections associated with cosmetic surgery (Int J Infect Dis 2017; 63:1), tattooing (MMWR 61:653, 2012).
  • Expert consultation strongly recommended.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium chelonae (speciation mandatory to differentiate from M. abscessus)

Primary Regimens

  • Treatment based on results of susceptibility testing (see Comments for potentially active drugs).
  • Skin infection: Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid + one other active agent from the list below x 4-6 months (see Comments)
  • Serious skin and soft tissue infection, bone infection, disseminated disease, infection in immunocompromised host: two-drug combination therapy (consider three drugs for more severe infections) for first 2-8 weeks or more with: 
    • Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid + Tobramycin 5 mg/kg IV once daily + one additional agent from among those listed below.
    • THEN Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid plus one additional oral agent to which the isolate is susceptible if possible to complete at least 6 months of therapy; up to 12 months for more severe skin and soft tissues infections, bone infection, disseminated disease; and 12 months after cultures become negative for pulmonary infection.

Alternative Regimens

  • Azithromycin 500 mg once daily can substitute for Clarithromycin, although Clarithromycin has better activity
  • Amikacin 10-15 mg/kg IV once daily can substitute for Tobramycin

Comments

  • Reference: Lancet Infect Dis 2022; 22: e178
  • Agents potentially active against M. chelonae and their doses (approximate percentage of strains with MICs in the susceptible or intermediate range)
    • Amikacin 10-15 mg/kg IV once daily (58-100%)
    • Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid (92-100%)
    • Clofazimine 100-200 mg once daily
    • Doxycycline 100 mg po bid  (25%)
    • Imipenem 1 gm IV bid (8-50%)
    • Levofloxacin 500 mg IV/po once daily (0-50%)
    • Linezolid 300-600 mg IV/po once daily (50-100%)
    • Moxifloxacin 400 mg po once daily (0-50%)
    • Omadacycline 100 mg IV once daily or 300 mg po once daily (no established breakpoint, MIC90 = 0.25 µg/mL) (see Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2019; 63:pii: e02522-18). 
    • Tedizolid 200 mg po once daily (no established breakpoint, MIC90 = 2 µg/mL)
    • Tigecycline 50 mg IV bid (no established breakpoint, MIC90 = 0.125 µg/mL)(J Antimicrob Chemother 69:1945, 2014).
  • References: J Infect Chemother 2019; 25:117;  J Clin Microbiol 2017; 55:1747; BMC Infect Dis 2017; 17:197; Int J Antimicrob Agents 2003; 21:585-8; Antimicrob Agents Chemother 1992; 36:180. 
  • Agents NOT active against M. chelonae
    • Cefoxitin
    • Meropenem
    • TMP-SMX
  • Removal of foreign-bodies and surgical drainage for abscesses or, if possible, for extensive disease.
