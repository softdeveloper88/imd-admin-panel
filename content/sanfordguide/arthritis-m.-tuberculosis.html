Arthritis, M. tuberculosis

Clinical Setting

  • Subacute or chronic inflammatory arthritis, usually monoarticular, with soft tissue swelling, pain, slowly progressive stiffness and loss of function.
  • Synovial fluid cell counts 10-20,000 cells/mm3, but routine cultures are negative.
  • Synovial fluid smears AFB positive ~20% of time; sensitivities of synovial fluid culture and synovial tissue biopsy are ~ 80% and >90%, respectively.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis

Primary Regimens

  • Choice regimen is based on isolate susceptibility: see Susceptible, Mono-resistant, MDR-TB or XDR-TB.
  • Some experts favor a total duration of therapy of 9 months instead of 6 months.

Alternative Regimens

  • Not applicable

Comments
