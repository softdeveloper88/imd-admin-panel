Clofazimine
Tradename: Lamprene

Usage and Dosing

  • Activity against:
    • M. leprae
    • M. tuberculosis
    • Non-tuberculous mycobacterial species
  • Not available through retail pharmacies
  • Anti-leprosy agent.
    • For leprosy: free-drug from National Hansens Disease program 
  • Component drug in WHO recommended 9-11 month and 18 month bedaqiline-containing regimens for treatment of tuberculosis caused by  MDR or XDR strains (WHO Drug-Resistant Tuberculosis Treatment Guidelines).
  • Increasingly used in treatment of non-tuberculous mycobacterial infections.
    • U.S. FDA expanded access programs click here.

Adult Dose

  • 50 mg po q24h + 300 mg 1x/month

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days) Age 10-14 yrs: 150 mg once monthly + 50 mg on alternate days
Age <10 yrs or wt <40 kg: 100 mg once monthly + 50 mg twice weekly

Adverse Effects

  • Skin: pigmentation (pink-brownish black) 75–100%, dryness 20%, pruritus 5%.
  • GI: abdominal pain 50% (rarely severe leading to exploratory laparoscopy), splenic infarction (VR), bowel obstruction (VR), GI bleeding (VR).
  • Eye: conjunctival irritation, retinal crystal deposits, corneal pigmentation.
  • Cardiac: cases of torsade de Pointes with QT prolongation has been reported in patients receiving dosage regimens containing higher than 100 mg daily dose of clofazimine or clofazimine in combination with QT prolonging medications.
