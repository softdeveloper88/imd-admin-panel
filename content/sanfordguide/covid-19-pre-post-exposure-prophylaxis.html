COVID-19, Pre- & Post-exposure Prophylaxis

Pre-exposure

  • Primary COVID-19 prevention / pre-exposure prophylaxis for at risk individuals.

  • The U.S. Food and Drug Administration issued an Emergency Use Authorization (EUA) for Pemivibart (Pemgarda), an investigational monoclonal antibody that has activity against SARS-CoV-2, including the circulating Omicron variants, for use as primary prophylaxis against SARS-CoV-2 for those at high risk for complications of COVID.  Prior monoclonal Evusheld (tixagevimab co-packaged with cilgavimab) had its EUA withdrawn owing to its lack of efficacy against Omicron Variants of SARS-CoV-2.   

    • Pemivibart (Pemgarda), is indicated in adults and adolescents (12 years of age and older weighing > 40 kg, or 88 pounds) who are not currently infected with SARS-CoV-2 and who have not had a known recent exposure to an individual with active SARS-CoV-2 infection and who have moderate to severe immune compromise due to a medical condition or due to taking immunosuppressive medications or treatments that make them unlikely to mount and adequate immune response to COVID-19 vaccination.
    • Pemivibart is administered via IV infusion over 60 minutes.  Readministration is recommended every 3 months.  
    • Pemivibart is reecommended as PRE-exposure prophylaxis only
    • Pemivibart is NOT approved for post-exposure prophylaxis nor for treatment of active, current SARS-CoV-2 infection

Post-exposure

  • Not recommended: bamlanivimab plus etesevimab and casirivimab plus imdevimab (REGEN-COV) for post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP), as the Omicron variant and its subvariants, which are not susceptible to these agents, are currently the dominant SARS-CoV-2 variants circulating in the United States.
