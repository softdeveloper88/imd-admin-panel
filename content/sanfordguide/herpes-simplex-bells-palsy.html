Herpes simplex, Bell's Palsy

Clinical Setting

  • Bell's Palsy: facial muscle weakness (VII cranial nerve, peripheral)
  • Guidelines on evaluation and management (Otolaryngol Head Neck Surg 149:656, 2013)

Etiologies

  • Herpes simplex (HSV) (Types 1, 2) (most common)
  • Others: Varicella zoster (VZV), HHV-6, Lyme disease

Primary Regimens

  • As soon as possible after onset of palsy: Prednisone 1 mg/kg po divided bid x 5 days then taper to 5 mg bid over the next 5 days (total of 10 days prednisone) plus either:
    • Acyclovir 1600-2400 mg po daily (divided q4h) x 10 days
    • Valacyclovir 1000-1500 mg po daily (divided bid or tid ) x 7 days
    • Famciclovir 750 mg po daily x 7 days  

Alternative Regimens

  • Corticosteroids alone (not as effective as combination regimens above)

Comments

  • Important to rule out Lyme disease in endemic areas; treatment is different (see Lyme disease).
  • Systemic review, meta-analysis demonstrated clear benefit of combination steroids plus antiviral therapy (Laryngoscope 2021)
