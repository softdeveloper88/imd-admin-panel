Temixys
Tradename: Temixys

Usage and Dosing

  • NOTE: Please see the Cimduo, Temixys page for the most complete and updated information.
  • Temixys is a two-drug fixed-dose NRTI combination product containing 300 mg of lamivudine (3TC) and 300 mg of tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF).
  • The product is a generic formulation that has similar efficacy as brand-name Truvada (Tenofovir + FTC); however, Temixys is not formally approved for use as PrEP (though it likely would work)
  • Black Box warning: hepatitis exacerbations reported in patients coinfected with HBV and HIV after stopping 3TC or TDF. Monitor liver function carefully; anti-HBV treatment may be required.

Adult Dose

  • One tablet po once daily, with or without food.
    • Tablet: 3TC 300 mg + TDF 300 mg

Pediatric Dose

  • Weight ≥35 kg: one tablet po once daily, with or without food.

    Adverse Effects

    • Lamivudine is generally well tolerated. Adverse effects possibly or probably associated:
      • Anaphylaxis, pruritus, urticaria
      • Anemia
      • Hepatic steatosis
      • Hyperglycemia
      • Lactic acidosis
      • Muscle weakness, CPK elevation, rhabdomyolysis
      • Redistribution/accumulation of body fat
    • Tenofovir is also well tolerated. Adverse effects possibly or probably associated:
      • Angioedema, dyspnea
      • GI (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea)
      • Hepatic steatosis, hepatitis, increased LFTs
      • Hypokalemia, hypophosphatemia
      • Lactic acidosis
      • Osteomalacia
      • Pancreatitis, increased amylase, abdominal pain
      • Rash
      • Renal injury (renal failure, ATN, Fanconi syndrome, interstitial nephritis, nephrogenic diabetes insipidus, proteinuria, polyuria)
      • Rhabdomyolysis

    Pregnancy Risk

    FDA Risk Category 1 Humans: 3TC safe, TDF safe
    Animals: 3TC embryotoxic, TDF safe
    Use during Lactation If on ART + suppressed VL throughout pregnancy, breastfeeding is a reasonable option after counseling about infant feeding options, risks and benefits
