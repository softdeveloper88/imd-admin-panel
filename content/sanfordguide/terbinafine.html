Terbinafine
Tradename: Lamisil

Usage and Dosing

  • Terbinafine is used for treatment of onychomycosis and Tinea infections.
  • Possible alternative agent for chromoblastomycosis, Scedosporium infections.
  • Rare hepatotoxicity, including fatal hepatic failure.
  • Terbinafine granules are no longer available. Mixing a crushed tablet in 30 mL of chocolate syrup or corn syrup is an effective alternative for patients unable to swallow a pill (Cutis 2023;111:137).
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions. Terbinafine is a CYP2D6 inhibitor. See Major Drug Interactions below, not all-inclusive.

Adult Dose

  • Usual dose: 250 mg po once daily

    Hepatic Adjustment

    Terbinafine is contraindicated in chronic or active liver disease

      Mild impairment (Child-Pugh Class A)Use not recommended
      Moderate impairment (Child-Pugh Class B)Use not recommended
      Severe impairment (Child-Pugh Class C)Use not recommended

      Adverse Effects

      • Elevated transaminases, hepatotoxicity, hepatic failure
      • Neutropenia
      • Headache
      • Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
      • Taste disturbance, smell disturbance
      • Rash
      • Possible photosensitivity: See Photosensitivity Reaction page

      Pregnancy Risk

      FDA Risk Category 1 No evidence of toxicity in animal or limited human studies
      Use during Lactation Milk concentrations low, adverse effects not expected. Monitor infant for liver toxicity.
