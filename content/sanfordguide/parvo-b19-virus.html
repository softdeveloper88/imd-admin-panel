Parvo B19 Virus

Clinical Setting

  • Parvo B19 virus (also Erythrovirus B19) has multiple manifestations, symptomatic infections.
  • Usually "Fifth's Disease" in children.
  • Can present as papular-purpuric gloves and socks syndrome, which typically presents as rapidly progressive, painful, pruritic, and symmetric swelling and erythema of the distal hands and feet, usually in the spring and summer. May be confused with acute meningococcemia. (Clin Infect Dis 35:1558, 2002).

Diagnosis

  • IgM and IgG antibody titers. Perhaps better: blood parvovirus PCR.

Etiologies

  • Parvo B19 Virus
  • Erythrovirus B19

Primary Regimens

  • Erythema infectiosum: Symptomatic treatment only
  • Arthritis/arthalgia: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAID)
  • Transient aplastic crisis: Transfusions and oxygen
  • Fetal hydrops: Intrauterine blood transfusion
  • Chronic infection with anemia: Intravenous immunoglobulin (IVIG) and transfusion
  • Chronic infection without anemia: IVIG (perhaps)
  • Papular-purpuric gloves and socks syndrome: Symptomatic treatment

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Dose of IVIG not standardized; suggest 1-1.5 gm/kg IV of IVIG for 3 days (Clin Infect Dis 56:968, 2013). Alternative dose: 400 mg/kg IV of IVIG for 5 or 10 days.
  • Most dramatic anemias present in patients with pre-existing hemolytic anemia.
  • Bone marrow shows erythrocyte maturation arrest with giant pronormoblasts. Blood disorders associated with Parvovirus B19 reviewed in (Rev in Med Virology, 2015, 25: 224–240)
  • Reviewed in Aspects of Mol Med 2023
