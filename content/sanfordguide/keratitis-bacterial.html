Keratitis, Bacterial

Clinical Setting

  • Bacterial keratitis can be a vision-threatening disease: prompt consultation with an ophthalmologist is essential.
  • Bacterial keratitis presentation:
    • Contact lens associated (most common risk factor)
    • Systemic diseases: e.g., diabetes, connective tissue disease, immunosuppression
    • Trauma, ocular surgery, other injury to the ocular surface
  • All treatment regimens are topical.
  • Microbioloigcal diagnosis
    • Most community-acquired cases can be managed without cultures (see regimens below)
    • Smears and cultures (alginate swab) or corneal biopsy indicated for :
      • Large ulcers, significant stromal involvement
      • Chronic infection or infection unresponsive to antimicrobial therapy
      • History of corneal surgery
      • Atypical clinical features concerning for fungal, amoebic, nocardial, or mycobacterial infection
      • Multiple corneal infiltrates
      • Sight threatening infection

Etiologies

  • Staphylococcus aureus
  • Coagulase-negative staphylococci 
  • Streptoccus pyogenes
  • Pseudomonas aeruginosa
  • Enterobacteriaceae

Primary Regimens

  • Refer to an ophthalmologist
  • Initial, empiric therapy for uncomplicated, community-acquired infection
    • (Ciprofloxacin 3 mg/mL optic solution or Levofloxacin 15 mg/mL optic solution) 1 gtt hourly x 24–72 hrs then taper based on clinical response

Alternative Regimens

  • Tobramycin or Gentamicin 3 mg/mL optic solution 1 gtt hourly x 24–72 hrs, then taper based on clinical response
  • Suspected drug resistance: Vancomycin 50 mg/mL + Amikacin 20 mg/mL optic solution 1 gtt x 24-72 hrs, then taper based on clinical response

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Specific therapy is guided by the results of alginate swab culture and sensitivity.
  • Prior use of fluoroquinolones associated with increased MICs (JAMA Ophthalmol 131:310, 2013); high MICs associated with poorer outcome (Clin Infect Dis 54:1381, 2012).
  • MRSA strains are often fluoroquinolone resistant and may respond poorly to topical therapy with these agents and Vancomycin 50 mg/ml optic solution preferred.
