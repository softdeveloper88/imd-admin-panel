Endocarditis, Cardiac Implantable Electronic Device (CIED)

Clinical Setting

  • Positive blood cultures in the patient with a cardiac implantable cardiac device (CIED) (see Circulation. 2024;149:e201 and Circulation. 2010;121:458).

    • Two or more positive blood cultures for Staphylococcus aureus or the same strain of coagulase-negative staphylococci with no alternative source PLUS a transesophageal echocardiogram (TEE) with findings of echodensity on the device lead or valvular vegetations OR 18F-FDG PET/CT with findings of abnormal heterogeneous activity along leads, or native or prosthetic heart valves.

    • Persistent (>72 h) bacteremia due to non-staphylococcal organism despite adequate pathogen-directed therapy, with no alternative source of infection with imaging findings as above or equivocal findings OR septic pulmonary emboli is supportive of CIED infection.

  • If negative blood cultures, fever, systemic inflammatory response syndrome, no defined source of infection and 8F-FDG PET/CT with findings of abnormal heterogeneous activity along leads, or native or prosthetic heart valves is suggestive of CIED infection

Etiologies

  • Most common (60% or more of cases)
    • Staphylococcus aureus
    • Staphylococcus epidermidis
  • Less common: streptococci, enterococci, Gram-negative bacilli

Primary Regimens

  • Complete device removal is recommended to achieve cure.
  • Empirical therapy pending culture results: Same as Empirical Therapy for Prosthetic Valve Endocarditis
  • Links to recommendations for pathogen-directed therapy once culture and susceptibility results are available: Staphylococcus aureus, viridans group streptococci or S. gallolyticus, coagulase-negative staphylococci, enterococci

Alternative Regimens

  • For patients in whom device removal is not feasible, e.g., those with major co-morbid illness, high risk for poor outcome from the extraction procedure, limited life expectancy, complete a course of therapy as for prosthetic valve endocarditis followed by pathogen-directed, life-long oral suppressive antibiotic therapy.

Comments

  • Optimal timing of new device replacement is undefined, but implantation after 14 days from the first negative blood culture is reasonable.
