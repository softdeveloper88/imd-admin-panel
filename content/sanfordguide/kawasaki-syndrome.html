Kawasaki Disease (Kawasaki Syndrome)

Clinical Setting

  • Kawaski's disease (syndrome) is an acute self limited febrile vasculitis of children (most often under age 5 years). Usually affects medium sized arteries with the coronary artery aneurysms developing in 25% of untreated children.
    • Peaks at about age 1 year; 85% of cases are in children age <5 years
    • Reference: Circulation. 2024 Dec 3;150(23):e481; Circulation 2017; 135: e927; Expert Review Clinical Immunology 2019;15:1089
  • COVID-19, SARS-CoV-2: related multisystem inflammatory syndrome (MIS-C) can mimic Kawasaki disease. For specifics see: Arch Cardiovasc Dis 2021; 114:426.

Etiologies

  • Unknown

Diagnosis 

  • Classic KD is diagnosed in the presence of fever for at least 5 d together with at least 4 of the 5 following principal clinical features. Circulation. 2024 Dec 3;150(23):e481
    • Oral mucosal changes: Erythema and cracking of lips, strawberry tongue, and/or erythema of oral and pharyngeal mucosa
    • Bilateral non-puruluent bulbar conjunctival injection. Limbal sparing may be noted
    • Rash
    • Swelling of hands and/or feet in acute phase. Periungual desquamation later
    • Cervical lymphadenopathy (≥1.5 cm diameter)
  • Experienced clinician can make diagnosis of complete KD in presence of ≥4 principal clinical features with 4 days of fever
  • Incomplete KD is more complex to diagnose
    • Consider with fever for at least 5 days, 2 or 3 clinical criteria, systemic inflammation (CPR > 3 or ESR >40 )and positive echocardiogram or 3 or more lab abnormalities
      • Sterile pyuria
      • Thrombocytosis >450,000
      • Anemia for age
      • WBC >15,000
      • Elevated ALT
  • High risk features include age < 6 months, LAD or RCA z-score ≥ 2.5 on baseline echo

Primary Regimens

  • Antimicrobial therapy is NOT indicated.
  • IVIG + aspirin reduces number of coronary artery aneurysms and is cost-effective; mechanism of benefit is unknown.
  • IVIG 2 gm/kg over 12 hours + aspirin 20-25 mg/kg qid, then aspirin 3–5 mg/kg per day po q24h x 6–8 weeks
    • Infuse IVIG over 8-12 hrs
    • If patient is still febrile after 1st dose of IVIG, some give a 2nd dose.
  • In a multicenter, prospective, randomized open-label blinded-endpoints trial in Japan, there were fewer coronary artery abnormalities in patients treated with IVIG + prednisolone 2 mg/kg per day over 15 days as compared to IVIG + aspirin (Lancet 379:1613, 2012). American Heart Association recommends agains routine use of single dose of steroids.
  • Intensification of initial therapy (dual therapy with infliximab or longer course corticosterioids) may benefit high-risk patients with KD (baseline CAA Z score ≥2.5, infants <6 months, or in high risk category using the Son risk score 

  • Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • IV gamma globulin (2 gm/kg over 10 hours) before the 10th day of illness reduced coronary artery lesions.
  • Differential diagnosis in children with possible KD includes: measles, adenovirus infection, streptococcal scarlet fever, juvenile idiopathic arthritis, Covid-associated MIS-C
  • Biomarkers may help differentiate KD from MIS-C Pediatr Cardiol. 2025 Jan;46(1):116. Troponin and BNP were higher in MIS-C
  • In children, wait 11 months after IVIG before giving live virus vaccines.
