Surgical Prophylaxis, Gastroduodenal, Biliary

Clinical Setting

  • Antimicrobial prophylaxis for gastroduodenal and biliary surgical procedures to include PEG tubes and pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple procedure):
    • Gastroduodenal
      • When lumen is entered surgically (e.g., gastrostomy, pancreaticoduodenectomy).
      • When lumen is not entered (e.g., vagotomy, reflux treatment procedures)
      • High-risk: morbid obesity, obstruction, decreased motility, decreased gastric acid, bleeding, cancer or other debilitation
    • Biliary
      • Open procedures (e.g., cholecystectomy) or those at risk for conversion of a laparoscopic to an open procedure
      • Laparoscopic procedures (e.g., cholecystectomy)
      • High-risk: age >70 years, acute cholecystitis, non-functioning gallbladder, obstructive jaundice, common duct stones, diabetes, pregnancy, immunosuppression and other factors. With cholangitis, treat as infection, not prophylaxis.
    • Clinical practice guidelines: Am J Health Syst Pharm 70:195, 2013.
    • ERCP (endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography)
  • See also, Prevention of Surgical Infections, Overview.

Etiologies

  • E. coli
  • Klebsiella species
  • Enterococci
  • Less commonly: other aerobic gram-negative bacteria, other streptococci and staphylococci
  • Occasional report of Clostridium species

Primary Regimens

  • Gastroduodenal
    • Lumen entered or any high-risk: Cefazolin 2 gm IV as a single dose (3 gm IV if weight ≥120 kg)
  • Biliary
    • Open procedure or high-risk laparoscopic: [Cefazolin 2 gm IV as a single dose (3 gm IV if weight ≥120 kg)] OR [Cefoxitin 2 gm IV as a single dose] OR [Cefotetan 2 gm IV as a single dose] OR [Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV as a single dose, only for those likely to need treatment for established infection]
    • Low-risk elective laparoscopic: value of prophylaxis unproven
  • Endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography (ERCP)
    • No antimicrobial needed if no obstruction. If obstruction: Ciprofloxacin 500–750 mg po or 400 mg IV 2 hours prior to procedure (if resistance profile permits) or Pip-Tazo 4.5 gm IV 1 hour prior to procedure.

Alternative Regimens

  • For gastroduodenal or biliary procedures:
    • [(Vancomycin 15 mg/kg (maximum 2 gm) IV as a single dose) OR (Clindamycin 900 mg IV as a single dose)] + [(Aztreonam 2 gm IV as a single dose) OR (Gentamicin 5 mg/kg (dosing weight) IV as a single dose) OR (a fluoroquinolone) IV as a single dose]
    • See Obesity dosing adjustments for Gentamicin "dosing weight"

Comments

  • 2013 Clinical Practice Guidelines: Am J Health Syst Pharm 70:195, 2013.
  • In ERCP:
    • Most studies show that achieving adequate drainage will prevent postprocedural cholangitis or sepsis and no further benefit from prophylactic antibiotics; greatest benefit likely when complete drainage cannot be achieved. Meta-analysis suggested antibiotics may decrease bacteremia, but not sepsis/cholangitis (Endoscopy 31:718, 1999).
    • See Gastroint Endosc 81: 81, 2015 for American Society of Gastrointestinal Endoscopy recommendations for ERCP: treat existing cholangitis; use prophylaxis if biliary drainage is (or anticipated to be) not complete.
  • Up to 40% of E. coli resistant to ampicillin-sulbactam and fluoroquinolones: Surg Infect 10:99, 2009.
