Candidiasis, Cutaneous

Clinical Setting

  • Superficial Candida infection of the epidermis, including folliculitis.
  • Risk factors include infants (diaper rash), obesity, diabetes, immunosuppression.
  • Presents as erythematous plaques and erosion which may have satellite papulopustular lesions in warm, moist skin folds.

Etiologies

  • Candida albicans

Primary Regimens

  • Miconazole 2% solution, lotion, cream or powder applied twice daily OR
  • Clotrimazole 1% solution, lotion, or cream applied twice daily OR
  • Nystatin 100,000 units/gm lotion, cream, or powder applied twice daily

Alternative Regimens

  • Fluconazole 100 mg po once daily in refractory cases

Comments

  • IDSA Candida Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 62:409, 2016
