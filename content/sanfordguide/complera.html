Complera, Eviplera
Tradename: Emtricitabine + Tenofovir + Rilpivirine

Usage and Dosing

  • Complera (US), Eviplera (EU) is a single tablet co-formulation of Emtricitabine (FTC) + Tenofovir (TDF) + Rilpivirine (RPV).
  • Preferred use is in patients with HIV RNA levels < 100,000 copies/mL.
  • Should not be used with proton pump inhibitor (PPI) agents.
  • Resistance testing is recommended prior to selection of this medication to assess likely effectiveness; expert consultation is recommended if resistance mutations to RPV, TDF, or FTC is observed.
  • Lower exposure of RPV observed during pregnancy. Monitor viral load closely.
  • Black Box warning: severe acute HBV exacerbations have occurred in patients coinfected with HIV-1 and HBV who have discontinued products containing FTC or TDF.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • See also, ARV Drugs, Overview for class-wide adverse effects.

      Adverse Effects

      • Skin and hypersensitivity reactions
      • Hepatotoxicity
      • Depressive disorders
      • New onset or worsening renal impairment
      • Bone loss and mineralization defects
      • Lactic acidosis and severe hepatomegaly with steatosis
      • Immune reconstitution syndrome (IRIS)
      • QT prolongation (rilpivirine)
      • See also individual components
