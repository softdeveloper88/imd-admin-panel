Quinine dihydrochloride

Usage and Dosing

  • Active against all Plasmodium spp.
  • Outside the US, IV quinine dihydrochloride is sometimes still the parenteral anti-malarial agent of choice for severe malaria in areas where IV artesunate or artemether are not available.
  • IV quinidine sulfate no longer produced or available in the US.
  • Always use in combination with a second drug, usually doxycycline or tetracycline (clindamycin in pregnant women)
  • When clinically stable and parasitemia <1% switch to a complete course of oral quinine (3-7 days) or an oral artemisinin combination therapy.

Adult Dose

  • 20 mg (of salt)/kg IV loading dose then 10 mg (of salt)/kg IV q8h
  • Infuse all doses over four hours to avoid hypotension

Adverse Effects

  • Hypotension and death If intravenous infusion too rapid, never give as a bolus
  • Hypoglycemia
  • Cinchonism, blindness

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Use only if other effective antimalarials are unavailable
Use during Lactation Probably safe, but avoid if infant G6PD-deficient
