Capreomycin sulfate
Tradename: Capreomycin sulfate, Capastat sulfate

Usage and Dosing

  • Capreomycin is a second-line injectable anti-tuberculous agent used in the treatment of tuberculosis caused by multi-drug resistant (MDR) and extensively-drug resistant (XDR) strains.
  • For dosing, use adjusted weight as follows: Ideal body weight + 40% of excess weight
    • Ideal body weight (men): 50 kg plus 2.3 kg/inch over 5 ft
    • Ideal body weight (women): 45 kg plus 2.3 kg/inch over 5 ft
    • Monitor serum concentrations
  • Resistance 0.1% (mean), 0-0.9% (range).
    • Kanamycin and Amikacin resistant strains may be cross resistant to Capreomycin: confirm in vitro susceptibility
    • Capreomycin resistance associated with 16S ribosomal RNA gene (rrs) mutations, e.g.,  A1401G, C1402T, and G1158T (PloS One 7:e33275, 2012)

Adult Dose

Usual Dose1 gm IM/IV q24h (15 mg/kg/day) as 1 dose, or 25 mg/kg IM/IV three times weekly

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days)15-30 mg/kg/day (divided q12-24h)

Adverse Effects

  • Nephrotoxicity (36%)
  • Ototoxicity (auditory 11%)
  • Eosinophilia, leukopenia
  • Skin rash
  • Fever
  • Hypokalemia
  • Neuromuscular blockade
