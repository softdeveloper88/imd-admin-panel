Toxoplasmosis, HIV/AIDS

Clinical Setting

  • Cerebral toxoplasmosis in HIV-positive or AIDS patients, usually seen as ring-enhancing lesions on CT or MRI
  • Toxoplasmosis can also present as disseminated infection, though much less common.
  • Most individuals (>95%) will have IgG evidence of preexisting latent infection, but acute new infection also reported.
  • Antibody titers often very low, borderline positive.
  • Regimens for:
    • Treatment
    • Primary prophylaxis in AIDS patients who are IgG toxoplasma antibody positive and who have CD4 count <100/mL
    • Chronic suppression after treatment
  • See guidelines: clinicalinfo.HIV.gov

Etiologies

  • Toxoplasma gondii

Primary Regimens

  • Treatment:
    • Treat for minimum of 6 weeks after resolution of signs/symptoms, followed by suppressive therapy (see below)
    • [Pyrimethamine 200 mg x 1 po, then (75 mg po daily for those weighing ≥60 kg) or (50 mg po daily for those weighing <60 kg)]+ [Sulfadiazine (1.5 gm if ≥60 kg or 1 gm if <60 kg) po q6h] + [Folinic acid 10–25 mg po daily]
  • Primary Prophylaxis:
    • Can discontinue if CD4 count >200 for 3 months
    • TMP-SMX-DS, 1 tab po q24h
  • Chronic Suppression (secondary prophylaxis):
    • (Sulfadiazine 2-4 gm po divided in 2-4 doses/day) + (Pyrimethamine 25–50 mg po q24h) + (Folinic acid 10–25 mg po q24h) OR
    • TMP-SMX-DS, 1 tab po BID (preferred) or q24h --less clinical experience but may be used to reduce pills or if pyrimethamine not available.
    • Can discontinue suppression if CD4 count >200 for 6 months

Alternative Regimens

  • Treatment:
    • Treat 4–6 weeks after resolution of signs/symptoms, then suppression.
    • Pyrimethamine 75 mg po daily (Wt ≥60 kg) or 50 mg po daily (Wt <60 kg) + Folinic acid 10–25 mg/day po + Clindamycin 600 mg po or IV q6h OR
    • TMP-SMX 5/25 mg/kg q12h po or IV OR
    • Pyrimethamine 75 mg po daily (Wt ≥60 kg) or 50 mg po daily (Wt <60 kg) + Folinic acid 10–25 mg/day po + Atovaquone 1,500 mg po bid with food OR
    • Atovaquone 1,500 mg po bid with food + Sulfadiazine 1.5 gm (Wt ≥60 kg) or 1 gm (Wt <60 kg q6h OR
    • Atovaquone 1,500 mg po bid with food OR
    • Pyrimethamine 75 mg po daily (Wt ≥60 kg) or 50 mg po daily (Wt <60 kg) + Folinic acid 10–25 mg/day po + Azithromycin 900-1,200 mg po once daily
  • Primary Prophylaxis:
    • TMP-SMX-DS 1 tab po 3x/week, OR
    • TMP-SMX-SS 1 tab po daily, OR
    • Dapsone 50 mg po q24h + Pyrimethamine 50 mg po once weekly) + Folinic acid 25 mg po once weekly OR
    • Atovaquone 1500 mg po q24h ± (Pyrimethamine 25 mg + folinic acid 10 mg) po q24h OR
    • (Dapsone 200 mg + Pyrimethamine 75 mg + Folinic acid 25 mg) po once weekly
  • Chronic Suppression:
    • Clindamycin 600 mg po q8h + Pyrimethamine 25–50 mg po q24h + Folinic acid 10–25 mg po q24h OR
    • Atovaquone 750 mg po q6–12h ± (Pyrimethamine 25 mg po q24h + Folinic acid 10 mg po q24h) or (Sulfadiazine 2 to 4 gm po daily)

Comments

  • Treatment:
    • Use alternative (non-sulfa) regimen for patients with severe sulfa allergy.
    • If multiple ring-enhancing brain lesions (CT or MRI), >85% of patients respond to 7–10 days of empiric therapy; if no response in 14 days or clinical worsening in first week, suggest brain biopsy.
    • Pyrimethamine penetrates brain even if no inflammation
    • Folinic acid prevents pyrimethamine hematologic toxicity
    • Most experts prefer and guidelines recommend pyrimethamine regimens, but now VERY expensive in the US. Meta analysis suggests TMP-SMX is a reasonable alternative HIV Med 2016
  • Primary Prophylaxis:
    • Prophylaxis for pneumocystis pneumonia (PJP)(Pneumocystis jiroveci) also effective against toxoplasmosis. See guidelines: clinicalinfo.HIV.gov.
  • Chronic Suppression:
    • (Pyrimethamine + sulfadiazine) prevents PCP and toxoplasmosis.
    • (Clindamycin + Pyrimethamine) prevents toxoplasmosis only.
    • If PJP is present or a concern, drugs must cover both organisms
