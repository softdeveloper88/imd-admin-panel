Surgical Prophylaxis, Breast, Herniorrhaphy

Clinical Setting

  • Antimicrobial prophylaxis for breast surgery, herniorrhaphy and hernioplasty.
  • See 2013 Clinical Practice Guidelines: Am J Health-Syst Pharm 70:195, 2013
  • See Prevention of Surgical Infections, Overview.

Etiologies

  • Staph. aureus
  • Streptococcus sp.
  • If moist/macerated skin, aerobic gram-negative bacilli

Primary Regimens

  • Cefazolin 2 gm IV as a single dose for weight < 120kg; 3gm IV for weight >= 120kg; redose after 4 hours
  • If patient known to be colonized with MRSA, add Vancomycin (as below) to Cefazolin.

Alternative Regimens

  • Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 gm IV as a single dose, OR
  • Clindamycin 900 mg IV as a single dose, OR
  • Vancomycin 1 gm IV as a single dose; use 1.5 gm IV as a single dose if weight >90 kg

Comments

  • Benefits of prophylaxis for clean surgical procedures without foreign material in healthy individuals are not clear (Treat Guidel Med Lett 10:73, 2012).
  • Antibiotics appear especially useful for breast cancer surgery or insertion of implants.
  • For hernia repair, benefits of prophylaxis are particularly important when mesh is used, but ASHP guidelines use in both herniorrhaphy and hernioplasty.
