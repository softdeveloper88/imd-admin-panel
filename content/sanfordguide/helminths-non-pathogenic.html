Helminths, Zoonotic, No Drug Therapy

Introduction

  • The following organisms are zoonotic helminths (worms) of various animals that very rarely are accidently acquired by humans.
  • Immature larvae cannot reach the adult stage or sexual maturity in humans (not natural hosts) but may cause damage or inflammation during migration or final resting place.
  • Drug therapy not required (ineffective) but surgical removal may be indicated.
  • CDC DPDx website links are provided below for further information.
