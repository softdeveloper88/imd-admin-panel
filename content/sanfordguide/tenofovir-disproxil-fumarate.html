Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate
Tradename: Viread

Usage and Dosing

  • Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (Tenofovir or TDF) is indicated for use as a nucleoside as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually paired with another nucleoside (e.g., FTC or 3TC) plus an NNRTI, a boosted PI, or an integrase inhibitor. Do not use with Abacavir (decreased antiviral activity).
  • Tenofovir common side effects include headache, nausea and vomiting. Cases of renal dysfunction reported: check renal function before using (dose reductions necessary if CrCl <50 mL/min); avoid concomitant nephrotoxic agents (See adverse effects below). One study found ↑ renal dysfunction at 48-weeks in patients receiving TDF with a PI (mostly Lopinavir/Ritonavir) than with a NNRTI (J Infect Dis 197:102, 2008).
  • Black Box warning—exacerbations of Hepatitis B reported after stopping Tenofovir. Monitor several months after stopping TDF in Hepatitis B patients; some may need anti-HBV Rx.
  • See ARV Drugs, Overview for class-wide adverse effects.
  • See also, Tenofovir alafenamide fumarate (TAF)
  • Combination formulations:
    • Truvada (Emtricitabine + Tenofovir DF)
    • Atripla (Efavirenz + Emtricitabine + Tenofovir DF)
    • Complera (Emtricitabine + Rilpivirine + Tenofovir DF)
    • Stribild (Elvitegravir + Cobicistat + Emtricitabine + Tenofovir DF)
    • Cimduo, Temixys (Lamivudine + Tenofovir DF)
    • Delstrigo (Doravirine + Lamivudine + Tenofovir DF)
    • Symfi, Symfi Lo (Efavirenz + Lamivudine + Tenofovir DF)
  • Tenofovir DF containing regimens used during pregnancy, but label recommends not using tenofovir while breast feeding. Not well studied. Fetal exposure in utero much greater than infant exposure in breast milk. Hence, use during breast feeding seems reasonable (Clin Infect DIs 60: 275, 2015; Clin Infect Dis 60:279, 2015).

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Black Box Warning—Severe exacerbation of hepatitis B reported in patients who stop Tenofovir. Monitor carefully if drug is stopped; anti-HBV treatment may be warranted if TDF stopped.
    • Potential for osteomalacia due to tubular wasting of phosphorus (N Engl J Med 370:959, 2014). Check for phosphaturia and consider monitoring bone density of patients at risk.
    • Reports of Fanconi syndrome & renal injury induced by Tenofovir (Clin Infect Dis 56:567, 2013; Top Antivir Med 22:655, 2014). Fanconi syndrome is consequence of damage to renal proximal tubular cells resulting in wasting of phosphate, glucose, potassium, amino acids and uric acid. Patients can present with non-gap acidosis.
      • Monitor serum electrolyte and phosphate levels. Clinical diagnosis. Early injury is reversible; late chronic injury irreversible and associated with falling GFR (CrCl).
      • Check for drug-drug interactions that increase Tenofovir serum levels.
  • Most Common:
    • Diarrhea 11%, nausea 8%, vomiting 5%, flatulence 4% (generally well tolerated).
  • See ARV Drugs, Overview for class-wide adverse effects.
