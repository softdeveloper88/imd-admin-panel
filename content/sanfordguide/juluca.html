Juluca
Tradename: Dolutegravir + Rilpivirine

Usage and Dosing

  • Juluca is a two-drug INSTI/NNRTI combination formulation of Dolutegravir (DTG) 50 mg + Rilpivirine (RPV) 25 mg.
  • Complete two-drug substitute regimen for the treatment of HIV-1 infection in adult patients who:
    • Are virologically suppressed (HIV-1 RNA <50 copies/mL) on a stable antiretroviral regimen for at least 6 months.
    • Have no baseline pre-therapy resistance mutations.
    • Have no history of virologic failure.
  • Pregnancy risk and lactation safety:
    • Two-drug ARV regimens are not recommended for use in pregnancy.
    • Manufacturer recommends pregnancy test before use in individuals of childbearing potential.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
    • See specific dosage recommendation if co-administered with rifabutin.

Adult Dose

  • One tab once daily with food

    Adverse Effects

    • Contraindications:
    • Prior hypersensitivity reaction to DTG or RPV
    • Do not co-administer with dofetilide or any drug that significantly decreases RPV plasma concentrations (See Major Interactions).
    • Warnings:
      • Severe skin and hypersensitivity reactions (rash) and sometimes liver injury have been reported.
    • Common adverse effects: diarrhea and headache
    • Depressive disorders
    • QTc prolongation (due to rilpivirine)
