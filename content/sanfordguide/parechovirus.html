Parechovirus

Clinical Setting

  • Introduction
    • Human parechovirus (HPeV) are members of the family Picornaviridae. 19 genotypes: HPeV-1(formerly echovirus 22) is most prevalent; HPeV-3 associated with severe disease in young infants.
    • Seasonality: year round but HPeV-3 more common in summer months.
  • Clinical features
    • Common cause of illness in infants and young children. Clinical illness is uncommon in older children and adults.
    • Gastroenteritis most common manifestation, but may be detected in stool of asymptomatic infants.
    • Respiratory tract infections
    • Severe infections in young infants (< 3 months)
      • Most often with HPeV-3
      • Fever, rash, irritability most common
      • Diarrhea, abdominal distension/tenderness, respiratory illness may be present
      • Can present as sepsis-like syndrome
    • CNS infections
      • Viral meningitis
      • Encephalitis (2nd most commonly identified organism in children < 2 months in one study (J Clin Microbiol. 2016, 54:2251)
      • Seizures
  • Diagnosis by PCR of blood or CSF. Included in some multiplex meningitis panels

Etiology

  • Parechovirus

Primary Regimens

  • No approved therapy
  • IVIG has been used for severe disease

Alternative Regimens

  • None
