Postpartum Endometritis, Endomyometritis

Clinical Setting

  • Onset of fever (>38 degrees C) on 2 or more of the first 10 post-partum days.
  • Infection of the uterine endometrium by vaginal bacteria. Infection can spread by contiguity to the myometrium, parametrium or other pelvic structures (e.g., pelvic abscess, suppurative phlebitis of pelvic veins).
  • Risk factors: Cesarean section, manual removal of placenta, prolonged rupture of membranes, precedent bacterial vaginosis, colonization by group B streptococci.
  • Clinical picture: Fever, lower midline abdominal pain, tender uterine fundus, purulent lochia.

Diagnosis

  • Blood cultures.
  • Culture of Cesarean incision if latter is inflamed.
  • Culture of vaginal secretions if needed to determine presence , or absence, of group B streptococci.

Etiologies

  • Polymicrobic mixture of aerobic and anaerobic bacteria
    • Aerobes: 
      • Group B streptococci
      • Gram-negative bacilli (E.coli)
      • Group A streptococci (rare)
      • S. aureus (rare)
    • Anaerobic bacteria:
      • Bacteroides sp.
      • Peptostreptococcus sp. (now Peptoniphilus sp.)
      • Clostridium sp. (C. perfringens, C. sordellii [now Paeniclostridium sordellii])
  • If HIV/AIDS: also consider H. simplex and CMV

Primary Regimens

  • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h (see drug page for comment on loading dose)
  • If critically ill: Meropenem 1-2 gm IV loading dose, then 0.5-1 gm IV q8h
  • If S. aureus or Streptococcal toxic shock syndrome (TSS):
    • Streptococcal: Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h + Clindamycin 900 mg IV q8h
    • Staph. aureus (rare): add empiric Vancomycin for potential MRSA
  • If Clindamycin resistant Bacteroides sp.: Ceftriaxone + Metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h
  • May need concomitant Doxycycline if positive screen for C. trachomatis

Alternative Regimens

  • Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 gm IV q6h (Caution as reports of in vitro resistance of E.coli in up to 50% of isolates)
  • Amoxicillin-clavulanate 1000 /200 mg IV q8h (where available)
  • If mild illness and/or cannot treat with IV drugs:
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate  875/125 mg tab 1 po bid
    • Amoxicillin 500 mg po tid + Metronidazole  500 mg po tid

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • No firm consensus on duration of therapy; usual suggestion is 10 days. Alternatively could trend serum procalcitonin levels (if available).
  • Increasing resistance of Bacteroides sp. to Clindamycin
  • Over 45% of group B streptococci are resistant to Clindamycin
  • Try to avoid aminoglycosides due to risk of nephrotoxicity and ototoxicity

Comments

  • Some references suggest Cefotetan or Cefoxitin as alternative therapy, but due to increasing resistance of anaerobic gram negative bacilli to cefoxitin and cefotetan, susceptibility should be determined before considering use.
  • If fever persists or relapses, differential diagnosis includes:
    • Presence of antibiotic resistant bacteria: e.g., Enterococci, multidrug resistant gram negative bacilli
    • Infected pelvic hematoma; evolving pelvic abscess
    • Development of  suppurative phlebitis of pelvic veins
    • May have infected retained products of conception; if so, indication for gentle D & C
