Sulfadiazine

Usage and Dosing

  • Sulfadiazine is an oral sulfonamide antimicrobial in clinical use since the early 1940s (JAMA 1984;251:1467).
  • No longer widely used due to the availability of safer, more effective antimicrobials. Widespread resistance is an issue.
  • However, sulfadiazine (in combination with pyrimethamine and folinic acid) continues to be a key drug in the management of toxoplasmosis.
  • Silver sulfadiazine was synthesized in 1947. The 1% cream is used topically in the management of burn wounds.

Adult Dose

  • Usual dose: 1-1.5 gm po q6h.
  • Details of treatment and secondary prophylaxis of toxoplasmosis: see Toxoplasma gondii, Toxoplasmosis, Overview. Choose the appropriate topic link on the page.

      Adverse Effects

      • Contraindications
        • Sulfonamide hypersensitivity.
        • In infants <2 months of age, except as adjunctive therapy with pyrimethamine for congenital toxoplasmosis.
        • In pregnancy at term and during the nursing period (risk of kernicterus).
      • Warnings and Precautions
        • Not for the treatment of an established GABS infection.
        • Fatalities have occurred from hypersensitivity reactions and blood dyscrasias.
        • Use with caution in patients with impaired renal or hepatic function, and those with severe allergy or bronchial asthma.
        • Dose-related hemolysis may occur in G6PD deficiency.
        • Maintain adequate fluid intake to prevent crystalluria and stone formation.
      • Adverse effects
        • Hematologic (thrombocytopenia, leukopenia, hemolytic anemia, aplastic anemia, methemoglobinemia).
        • Hypersensitivity reactions (erythema multiforme, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis, rash, urticaria, serum sickness, pruritus, exfoliative dermatitis, anaphylactoid reactions, periorbital edema, conjunctival and scleral injection, photosensitization, arthralgia, allergic myocarditis, fever, chills).
        • GI (nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea, anorexia, hepatitis, pancreatitis, stomatitis).
        • CNS (headache, peripheral neuritis, depression, seizure, ataxia, hallucinations, tinnitus, vertigo, insomnia).
        • Renal (crystalluria, stone formation, toxic nephrosis with oliguria and anuria).
