HBV Chronic, Treatment

Clinical Setting

  • Most individuals with Chronic Hepatitis B infection should be treated with nucleoside therapy (regimens listed below).  The exceptions to treatment initiation are individuals who have all of the following:  
    • No evidence of liver fibrosis (< F2) and
    • HBV DNA level < 2000 IU / ml and
    • No confections (e.g., HIV, Hep D infection, or Hep C infection)
    • No family history of liver cancer or cirrhosis
    • No chronic immuneuppression (e.g., long-term steroid Rx, solid oran or stem cell transplant, or biologics with immunosuppression)
    • No comorbidities (e.g., Diabetes mellitus, metabolic syndrome, liver steatosis)
    • No extra hepatic manifestations of HBV infection (e.g., glomerulonephritis, or vasculitis
  • All of these conditions should be met to exclude an individual from chronic treatment of HBV infection.
  • Duration of therapy: Because cure rates are low, most patients will require therapy indefinitely (JAMA 2018;319:1802); see Antimicrobial Stewardship for details.

Etiologies

  • Hepatitis B virus (HBV)

Primary Regimens

  • Patients not co-infected with HIV:
    • Tenofovir disporoxil fumarate 300 mg po daily
    • Tenofovir alafenamide 25 mg po daily
    • Entecavir 0.5 mg po daily (1 mg once daily for those with decompensated cirrhosis; these individuals should be managed by a Liver Specialist / Hepatologist) 

          Choice of drug considerations and duration of treatment, see Comments.

    • Patients co-infected with HIV:
      • Truvada (Tenofovir disproxil fumarate 300 mg + Emtricitabine  200 mg) po once daily + either (Stand transfer Integrase Inhibitor, HIV protease inhibitor, or NNRTI)
      • Descovy (Tenofovir alafenamide fumarate 25 mg + Emtricitabine 200 mg) po once daily + either (Stand transfer Integrase Inhibitor, HIV protease inhibitor, or NNRTI)

    Alternative Regimens

    • Patients not co-infected with HIV:
      • Pegylated Interferon (PegIFN) 2a 180 ug sq qweek  (For those with documented cirrhosis, interferon therapy is contraindicated)

    Antimicrobial Stewardship

    • Treatment duration:  For the most part, treatment with nucleoside agents (e.g., Tenofovir DF (or AF) or Entecavir) should continue indefinitely (WHO Guidelines 2024).  The conditions under which treatment might be discontinued includes all of the following:  
      • APRI score is < 2  and
      • Loss of (or absence of) HBeAg with seroconversion to anti-HBe and 
      • There is persistently normal ALT levels and
      • Persistently undetectable HBV DNA levels and
      • The patient can be followed long term for reactivation.
    • For all patients with cirrhosis, treatment should not be discontinued under any circumstances.

    Prevention

    • See Hepatitis B, Vaccine for indications, available products, dosing, and vaccine characteristics for pre-exposure prevention.

    Comments

    • Tenofovir/Entecavir:
      • Choose Entecavir or Tenofovir AF over Tenofovir DF in those with renal insufficiency; choose tenofovir (DF or AF) over entecavir in those with normal renal function and those who had failed prior lamivudine therapy.
