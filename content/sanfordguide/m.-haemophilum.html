Mycobacterium haemophilum

Clinical Setting

  • Rare cause of ulcerating skin lesions, tenosynovitis, dactylitis, osteomyelitis, pulmonary infections, disseminated disease typically in immunocompromised hosts.
  • Cervicofacial infections and lymphadenitis in immunocompetent children and adults.

Diagnosis

  • Diagnosis requires media supplemented with iron-containing compounds such as ferric ammonium citrate, hemin, or hemoglobin to isolate the organism which grows best at 28o to 30oC.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium haemophilum

Primary Regimens

  • Ciprofloxacin 750 mg po bid + Rifabutin 300 mg po once daily + Clarithromycin 500 mg po bid for 6-12 months

Alternative Regimens

  • May substitute Rifampin 600 mg once daily to Rifabutin as above
  • Two drug regimen of a macrolide (Clarithromycin, as above, or Azithromycin 250-500 mg once daily) + a fluoroquinolone (Ciprofloxacin as above or Levofloxacin 750 mg once daily) may be considered for treatment of limited cutaneous infection

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Longer duration of therapy (e.g., 12 months) recommended for more severe or extensive or disseminated disease; 3-6 months may be sufficient for limited cutaneous infection.

Comments

  • Surgical debridement may be necessary and excision may be curative in immunocompetent host with localized disease, e.g., cervicofacial lymphadenitis.
  • Typically resistant to Isoniazid, Ethambutol, Pyrazinamide
  • Reference: Emerg Infect Dis. 2019; 25:1648-1652.
