Bartonella sp.

Clinical Setting

  • Slow growing fastidious gram-negative bacteria that cause a variety of human infections.
  • Hold cultures for 21 days. See links to specific clinical syndromes:
Bartonella speciesTransmitted byClinical manifestations*
Bartonella henselae (Cat scratch disease)Fleas (cat)Regional lymphadenitis, ocular disease, encephalitis, rarely endocarditis
Bartonella quintana (Trench Fever)Body liceFever, bacteremia, culture negative endocarditis
Bartonella bacilliformis (Carrion's disease)Nocturnal sand flies (Ecuador and Peru)Two phases: Early - bacteremia and hemolysis. Late - Vascular skin papules/ nodules

  • *Manifestations vary with immune status of the host

Classification

  • Gram negative bacilli
  • Three most important Bartonella species are:
    • Bartonella bacilliformis
    • Bartonella henselae
    • Bartonella quintana

Primary Regimens

  • Varies with clinical syndrome (see specific topic)

Alternative Regimens

  • None
