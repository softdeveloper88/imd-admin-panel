ARV Regimens Not Recommended, Adult

Overview

  • Certain ART regimens and/or ARV drugs are NOT recommended for use under current guidelines.
  • This page discusses the logic for not recommending these regimens and/or drugs and exceptions, if any.
  • For recommended adult regimens, see ART in Treatment Naive Adults.  Note, several drugs are no longer recommended for initial therapy but can be used as second-line Rx or beyond (e.g., maraviroc, fosamprenavir)

Specific ARV Agents & Regimens

Regimens

  • Monotherapy with Any Antiretroviral Drug
    • Logic: Development of resistance & inferior antiviral activity.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Dual NRTI combinations
    • Logic: Resistance and inferior antiretroviral activity compared with recommended drug combinations.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Triple NRTI combinations
    • Logic: Triple-NRTI regimens have shown inferior virologic efficacy in clinical trials: (tenofovir + lamivudine + abacavir) & (didanosine + lamivudine + tenofovir) and others.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Double boosted PIs
    • Logic: Using 2 or more PI agents boosted with ritonavir adds nothing in terms of anti-HIV activity but can add extra toxicity.
    • Exceptions: None
  • 2 NNRTI agents
    • Logic: Increased rate of side effects. Drug-drug interaction between etravirene and nevirapine and etravirene and efavirenz.
    • Exceptions: None

Specific Agents, Combinations

  • Saquinavir hard gel cap or tab (Invirase), darunavir, or tipranavir as single (unboosted) PI
    • Logic: Bioavailability only 4%; inferior antiretroviral activity
    • Exceptions: None
  • Stavudine + didanosine
    • Logic: High frequency of toxicity: peripheral neuropathy, pancreatitis & mitochondrial toxicity (lactic acidosis). In pregnancy: lactic acid acidosis, hepatic steatosis, ± pancreatitis.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Stavudine + zidovudine
    • Logic: Antagonistic.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Atazanavir + indinavir
    • Logic: Additive risk of hyperbilirubinemia.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Emtricitabine + lamivudine
    • Logic: Same target and resistance profile.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Abacavir + tenofovir
    • Logic: Rapid development of K65R mutation; loss of effect.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Tenofovir + didanosine
    • Logic: Reduced CD4 cell count increase; concern of K65R development.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Abacavir + didanosine
    • Logic: Insufficient data in naive patients.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Tenofovir alafenamide fumarate (TAF) + Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF)
    • Logic: There are no data supporting any potential additive efficacy or toxicity if TAF and TDF are used in combination; there is no advantage to using both formulations of tenofovir together. (Note: This combination may be prescribed inadvertently during transition from one formulation to another)
    • Exceptions: None
  • Cobicistat + Ritonavir as Pharmacokinetic Enhancers
    • Logic:  These drugs, independently, are inhibitors of many CYP isoenzymes, most notably 3A4.  Use of both drugs together could enhance the inhibition of CYP3A4 leading to untoward adverse events owing to very high drug levels
    • Exceptions: None
  • Etravirine + Unboosted Protease Inhibitor
    • Logic:  Etravirene reduces drug levels of unboosted PI agents, resulting in reduced efficacy.
    • Exceptions: None
  • Etravirine + Tipranavir/Ritonavir
    • Logic:  Tipranavir/ritonavir (TPV/r) significantly reduces ETR concentrations
    • Exceptions: None
  • Nevirapine Initiated in ARV-Naive Women with CD4 Counts >250 cells/mm3 or in ARV-Naive Men with CD4 Counts >400 cells/mm3
    • Logic:  Initiating NVP in antiretroviral-naive individuals with CD4 counts above these thresholds increases the risk of symptomatic, potentially life-threatening, hepatic events.
    • Exceptions:  ARV-experienced patients can safely switch to nevirapine if they have CD4 counts above these thresholds as a result of receiving effective ART)
