Superior Saggital Sinus Suppurative (Septic) Thrombophlebitis

Clinical Setting

  • Superior saggital sinus suppurative (septic) thrombophlebitis is a complication of bacterial meningitis or bacterial frontal sinusitis.
  • Prognosis is bad. Can cause cortical vein thrombosis, hemorrhagic infarcts and brain stem herniation.
  • Anticoagulants not recommended

Diagnosis

  • MRI venogram

Etiologies

  • Streptococcus pneumoniae
  • Neisseria meningitidis
  • Haemophilus influenzae (rare)
  • Staphylococcus aureus (very rare)

Primary Regimens

  • As for meningitis (empiric therapy): Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h + Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) + Dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg IV q6h for 4 days. Give first dose 15-20 minutes before, or concomitant with, first dose of antibiotics

Alternative Regimens

  • As for meningitis: Meropenem 1-2 gm IV q8h +  Vancomycin as above PLUS Dexamethasone

Comments

  • Total sinus occlusion is rare presumably due to the large size of the sinus.
  • A complete occlusion of the sinus is universally fatal.
