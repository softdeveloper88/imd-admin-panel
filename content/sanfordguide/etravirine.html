Etravirine
Tradename: Intelence

Usage and Dosing

  • Etravirine (ETR) is used for patients with HIV-1 resistant to NNRTIs & other ARV drugs. Active in vitro against most such isolates. Usually paired with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents as part of a 3-drug regimen. If used with a boosted PI, Darunavir is often preferred owing to drug interactions with most other PI drugs.
  • Rash common, but rarely can be severe.
  • Because of interactions, do not use with boosted Atazanavir, boosted Tipranavir, unboosted PIs, or other NNRTIs.
  • Generally, multiple mutations are required for high-level resistance. See Etravirine Resistance Mutations.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive)

Adult Dose

Adult Dose200 mg bid after a meal (tablet may be dissolved in water and administered as a 'slurry')
Can also be taken as 400 mg once daily

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Severe rash (erythema multiforme, toxic epidermal necrolysis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome) has been reported.
    • Hypersensitivity reactions can occur with rash, constitutional symptoms and organ dysfunction, including hepatic failure (see FDA label). Monitor liver enzymes.
    • Potential for CYP450-mediated drug interactions.
  • Most Common:
    • Rash 9%, generally mild to moderate and spontaneously resolving; 2% discontinued clinical trials for rash. More common in women. Nausea 5%
