Benznidazole
Tradename: Abarax, Benznidazol

Usage and Dosing

  • Primary therapy for Chagas disease caused by Trypanosoma cruzi: not all clinical forms should be treated, see American Trypanosomiasis (Chagas disease)
  • Chagas disease, American trypanosomiasis. Reviews:  Lancet 2024;403:203-218  Ann Intern Med 2023.176:ITC17-ITC32. Am J Trop Med Hyg 2023;109:1006-1011.
  • See JAMA 2024 Jun 18;331(23):2037 for further details and need for ongoing follow-up of chronic infection.
    • Benefit in chronic indeterminant infection without clinical manifestations remains unclear (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 2022 May 16;16(5):e0010386) and

Adult Dose

  • Trypanosoma cruzi: 
    • Standard regimen:  Adult (age ≥12 years): 5-7 mg/kg/day po in 2 doses (divided q12h) x 60 days.
    • Due to adverse effects experienced clinicians treat adults with 300 mg per day for 60 days, regardless of body weight give 300 mg per day but prolong treatment to complete the total dose corresponding to 5 mg/kg per day for 60 days.
    • Preliminary trials on shorter duration regimens encouraging.
      • Phase 3 trials of 2 or 4 weeks of 100 mg po tid are underway.

Adverse Effects

  • Allergic dermatitis is common and responds to topical corticosteroids (discontinue therapy, if severe, exfoliative, or purpuric; Stevens Johnson syndrome has been reported)
  • Angioedema
  • Gastrointestinal: abdominal pain, nausea/vomiting, anorexia/weight loss
  • Neurologic: peripheral neuropathy (frequent; late in course), headache, insomnia.  
  • Hematologic (discontinue therapy, if leukopenia or thrombocytopenia develops)
  • US case series (N=30) found neuropathy in 47%, debilitating neuropathy in 17%, angioedema in 20%, overall discontinuation rate of 30% (Clin Infect Dis 60:1237, 2015) but used treatment doses at higher end.

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Animal studies suggest risk of fetal harm. Pregnancy testing recommended for females of reproductive potential.
Use during Lactation Milk concentrations low, adverse effects not expected. Use in nursing mothers is acceptable.

Major Drug Interactions

  • Alcohol: disulfiram-like reaction (abdominal cramps, nausea, vomiting, headache, flushing) may occur as a result of interference of Benznidazole with the oxidation of alcohol, resulting in accumulation of acetaldehyde.
