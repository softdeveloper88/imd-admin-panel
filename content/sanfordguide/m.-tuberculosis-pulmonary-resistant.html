Tuberculosis, Pulmonary, Mono-resistant

Clinical Setting

  • Mono-resistance to Isoniazid (INH) or Rifampin but not both and susceptible to other first-line drugs.
  • Isolated Rifampin resistance is uncommon and a marker for multi-drug resistance so important to confirm susceptibility to other first-line drugs.
  • Mono-resistance to Pyrazinamde is rare and a marker for Mycobacterium bovis infection.
  • For multi-resistant and extensively-resistant strains, see multi-drug resistant (MDR-TB) or extensively drug resistant (XDR-TB) M. tuberculosis.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis

Primary Regimens

  • For first-line drug dosing see First-Line TB Drug Dosing Table.
  • For Isoniazid-resistant strains:
    • Rifampin + Pyrazinamide + Ethambutol + (Levofloxacin 750 mg po q24h or Moxifloxacin 400 mg po q24h) 
      • Addition of Levofloxacin or Moxifloxacin strengthens the regimen and is recommended by ATS/CDC/ERS/IDSA Guidelines
        • In patients with low disease burden or poorly tolerant of Pyrazinamide, the duration of treatment with this agent may be shortened with the addition of a fluoroquinolone
        • For patients poorly tolerate of Levofloxacin or Moxifloxacin in the 4-drug regimen, Rifampin + Pyrazinamide + Ethambutol can be used
      • Treat for 6 months (extend to 9 months for cavitary disease or positive cultures at 2 months)
  • For Rifampin-resistant strains (Mono-rifampin resistance is rare and RIF resistance usually indicates MDR (resistance to both INH and RIF)
    • See regimens for multi-drug resistant TB (MDR-TB) and Curry Center Guidelines
  • For mono-resistance or poly resistance to other drugs see Curry Center Guidelines

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • No benefit of continuing Isoniazid when treating Isoniazid resistant disease.
