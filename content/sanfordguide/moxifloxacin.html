Usage and Dosing

  • Moxifloxacin, approved in 1999, is a fluoroquinolone (FQ) with a broad spectrum of activity that includes respiratory and enteric pathogens.
  • Moxifloxacin is the most active of current FQs against anaerobic enteric pathogens. High level of activity vs. 87% of anaerobic pathogens isolated from patients with peritonitis (Clin Infect Dis 2011;53:1074).
  • Used as a second-line agent for M. tuberculosis, but the indication is not FDA-approved.
  • Oral moxifloxacin should be taken at least 4 hours before or 8 hours after antacids containing magnesium or aluminum, sucralfate, iron, and multivitamin preparations with zinc. In contrast, the effect of calcium on moxifloxacin PK is considered to be clinically insignificant (Clin Pharmacokinet 2001;40 Suppl 1:27).
  • See Fluoroquinolones, Overview for additional class-wide information.

Adult Dose

  • Usual dose: 400 mg po or IV q24h
  • Bacterial conjunctivitis: 1 drop in affected eye q8h x7 days

      Pregnancy Risk

      FDA Risk Category 1 Avoid in pregnancy due to possible renal, cardiac, and CNS toxicity in the fetus. Consider use if no alternatives exist, and if benefit outweighs risk.
      Use during Lactation Short-term use safe, monitor infant for GI toxicity; avoid if possible.
