Ivermectin
Tradename: Stromectol, Mectizan, Ivextrim

Usage and Dosing

  • Activates chloride channels leading to parasite muscle paralysis.
    • Affinity for drug binding sites 100 times greater in nematodes than human tissues.
  • Used in mass treatment campaigns filariasis and onchocerciasis for over 20 years.
  • Do not use use in patients with COVID-19 (SARS-CoV-2); stated in all authoritative guidelines
  • Moxidectin provides longer suppression of microfilaria in onchocerciasis.

Adult Dose

  • See individual parasites for more details.
    • Filariasis, Lymphatic: generally used as part of combination therapy only in public health programs
Strongyloidiasis200 μg/kg/day po x 1-2 days
If hyperinfection, with larvae in stool and sputum, repeat every 15 days while stools are positive and then 1 more treatment cycle. If unable to take po can give veterinary Ivermectin s.c. or per rectum.
  • For hyperinfection with sepsis or meningitis in severely immunocompromised hosts: Ivermectin 200 µg/kg per day orally until stool and/or sputum exams are negative for 2 weeks.
Onchocerciasis (River blindness)Active vs microfilaria; not active vs adult. Give 150 μg/kg po x 1 dose. Repeat q 3-6 mos. Check for concomitant Loa loa; if Loa loa present, treat the onchocerciasis first.
Scabies200 μg/kg po x 1 dose. If AIDS pt with Norwegian scabies, may need 2 or more doses separated by 14 days.
Head licePatients >6 months of age, a single 10 minute topical application of 0.5% Ivermectin lotion yielded 74% louse free on day 15 (N Engl J Med 367:1687, 2012). 250 μg/kg po at 7 day intervals x 3 also used.
  • Ivermectin also used in the treatment of ascariasis,  Mansonella ozzardi, Mansonella streptocerca, gnathostomiasis, and as an alternative for head lice. See individual parasites for details.

Pediatric Dose

  • Note: According to WHO guidelines for mass prevention campaigns, children who are ≥90 cm tall can be safely treated with ivermectin. WHO growth standard curves show that 50% of boys reach this height by age 28 months and 50% of girls by age 30 months. Some MDA campaigns use ivermectin to treat younger children who are able to swallow tablets safely, albeit at a reduced dose.

        Adverse Effects

        • Mild side effects: fever, pruritus, rash. In treatment of onchocerciasis, can see tender lymphadenopathy, headache, bone/joint pain.
        • Host may experience inflammatory reaction to death of adult worms: fever, urticaria, asthma, GI upset (Mazzotti reaction).
        • Increased LFTs, possible hepatotoxicity.
        • Concomitant use of alcohol anecdotally reported in increasing frequency/severity of adverse effects.

        Pregnancy Risk

        FDA Risk Category 1 Category C. The available evidence (limited) suggests no difference in congenital abnormalities in the children of women who were accidentally treated during mass prevention campaigns with ivermectin compared with those who were not. The WHO excludes pregnant women from mass prevention campaigns that use ivermectin. Prescribe ivermectin only if the potential benefit to the woman justify the potential risk to the fetus.
        Use during Lactation Excreted in low concentrations in breast milk. Use only when the risk to the infant is outweighed by the risk of disease progression in the untreated mother.
