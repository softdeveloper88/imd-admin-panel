HBV Drugs in Development

New Drugs for Hepatitis B

  • Most of the focus of newer drugs for HBV infection is to achieve cure.  The barrier to cure is the elimination of the covalently closed-circular (ccc) DNA component of HBV. The drugs listed in the table are categorized by their mechanism of action.  A nice summary of emerging drug targets is  reviewed here.

1 Interferes with the viral DNA protein shield

2 Binds to the viral mRNA to prevent it from turning into viral protein

3  These products are based on programmable RNA-Guided DNA endonucleases.  The Spy Cas9/sgRNA (Strept pyogenes CRISPR-associated protein 9 /single guide RNA) are combinations of endonucleases (Spy-Cas9) associated with sgRNA that are transducted into a lentivirus vector for delivery into the cell. The combination sgRNA product has been shown to cleave HBV DNA (~1000-fold), as well as cleave cccDNA substantially (~10-fold) (J Virology 476:196-205,2015)

4  Short-interfering RNA (siRNA) inhibit gene expression at the mRNA level via interaction with the cell’s natural RNA inhibitory mechanisms creating a ‘silencing’ effect of the gene product.  A key barrier is assurance that the siRNA is delivered to the proper cell.  The creation of platforms for the delivery of siRNA products into the cell have been developed.  One such platform is the DPC approach, which mimics how viruses deliver their nucleic acids into target host cells. (Methods in Molec Biology 1218:163, 2015)
