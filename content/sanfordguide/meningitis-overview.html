Meningitis, Overview

Overview

  • Empiric and specific therapies for bacterial meningitis in both immunocompetent and immunocompromised patients, e.g., HIV/AIDS, as well as specific therapy for fungal, viral and parasitic etiologies.
  • Hallmark features: fever, headache, altered level of consciousness, signs of meningeal irritation
  • For suspected bacterial meningitis, microbiologic diagnosis is essential; blood culture, CSF, or both positive; molecular tests may be useful for diagnosis of culture-negative case.
    • Early recognition and prompt initiation of antibiotics are key.
    • Blood cultures and lumbar puncture (LP) should be obtained promptly.
    • LP without CT for patients with altered level of consciousness and non-focal neurological exam associated with earlier treatment and improved outcome (Clin Infect Dis 60:1162, 2015; Clin Infect Dis & 66:321, 2018).
    • If any delay in LP is anticipated (e.g., need to obtain CT before LP because of focal neurologic deficit, immunodeficiency), obtain blood cultures and give empiric therapy prior to head CT.
  • Clinical review:  JAMA 2022; 328:2147
