ART Treatment Failure, Adult

Treatment Failure

  • Definitions:
    • Therapeutic Failure:  Progression of clinical disease, virologic failure, or  development of toxicity.
    • Virologic Failure: Persistent (confirmed) HIV RNA (VL) > 200 copies/mL 6 months after starting ART.
  • General approach to management:
    • Evaluate adherence, tolerability, drug-drug interactions, and psychosocial issues.
    • If tolerability/toxicity issue, identify the most likely offending agent and substitute another agent that is likely to have antiviral activity and not have overlapping toxicities or drug-drug interactions with remaining agents.
    • Obtain/review antiretroviral drug history (including tolerability to prior agents used).
  • Reference:  IAS-USA Treatment Guidelines (JAMA 329: 63- 84, 2022)

Virologic Failure

  • Approaches to management of virologic failure:
    • For documented HIV RNA > 200 but < 500 c/mL
      • Carefully review adherence/barriers to medication access
      • Improve PK if possible (boosting, drug-drug interactions). Look specifically for divalent cations in multivitamins (interfere with InSTI PK)
    • For confirmed HIV RNA > 500 – 1000 c/mL
      • Change regimen as soon as possible (prevent emergence of further resistance)
      • Obtain resistance test while on the failing regimen (genotype preferable in general; phenotype most helpful for complex treatment history)
    • If no resistance mutations noted
      • Reassess adherence/drug-drug interactions/absorption issues/ possible drug-drug interactions
    • If resistance mutations observed
      • Change regimen based on findings (see below) and ART history
      • Expert discussion advised if unfamiliar with mutations or findings

Resistance Test Mutations

  • General rules for use of resistance test mutations
    • Use 2 (and preferably 3) fully active agents; can be active drugs from prior regimen(s). However beware of archived resistant virus.
    • Unclear how to ‘count’ partially active drugs. 3TC and FTC usually have residual partial activity and are often included in the new regimen (though not considered ‘fully active’ agents when M184V or I mutation is present).  With newer InSTI agents, presence of M184V alone has no detrimental effect on efficacy of next regimen.  OK to use either 3TC or FTC in that setting.  
    • Avoid ‘double boosted’ PIs (i.e., two PI agents + ritonavir). (See ARV Regimens NOT Recommended)
  • If many options are available, choose agent(s):
    • That are better tolerated
    • More convenient or simpler (for improved compliance)
    • With the least drug-drug interactions
  • If only a limited number of active drugs are identified:
    • If 2 fully active drugs not identified or available, generally should defer changing therapy until newer agents are available. If clinical deterioration impending, switch to best available alternative. Clinical judgment is necessary.
    • Treatment interruption should be avoided; even partially active regimens (~ 0.5 - 1.0 log reduction) are better than no therapy.
    • Note:  Prevalence of Highly Treatment Experienced (HTxE) patients has dropped dramatically over the last decade, from 7 -8% to < 0.9% (Bajema, et al, AIDS 34: 2051, 2020 )

Therapeutic Drug Monitoring

Rationale, Limitations

  • The rationale for therapeutic drug monitoring (TDM) is that drug levels can be highly variable, especially PIs.
  • TDM should be considered for PIs, NNRTIs and Maraviroc in patients presenting the following:
    • Drug-drug interactions suspected
    • HIV agents with partial activity (desire to get optimal drug levels)
    • Pregnancy (selected cases)
    • Concentration-dependent toxicities
    • Unexplained absence of virologic response (e.g., wild type virus in pt with virologic failure)
  • Limitations on the use or benefit of TDM:
    • Few prospective studies demonstrating benefit
    • Incomplete knowledge of therapeutic ranges
    • Considerable inter-individual variation of levels
    • Little or no role in NRTI medications owing to role of intracellular concentrations
    • Only a few qualified labs
