Enfuvirtide
Tradename: Fuzeon

Usage and Dosing

  • Enfuvirtide (ENF, T20) is a fusion inhibitor indicated for use as part of a 3- or 4-drug combination regimen in ART-experienced patients; the other drugs in the combination regimen should be selected based on results of resistance test results. Available only as an injectable; no oral formulation.
  • Enfuvirtide produces local reaction site reactions in 98%, 4% discontinue; erythema/induration ~80–90%, nodules/cysts ~80%.
  • Hypersensitivity reactions reported (fever, rash, chills, N/V, ↓ BP, &/or ↑ AST/ALT)—do not restart if occur.
  • Including background regimens, peripheral neuropathy 8.9%, insomnia 11.3%, ↓ appetite 6.3%, myalgia 5%, lymphadenopathy 2.3%, eosinophilia ~10%.
  • Increased incidence of bacterial pneumonias.
  • Alone offers little benefit by simply adding Enfuvirtide to a failing regimen (N Engl J Med 348:2249, 2003).

Adult Dose

Usual Dose90 mg (1 mL) subcutaneously q12h
Rotate injection sites, avoiding those currently inflamed.

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • ↑ Rate of bacterial pneumonia (6.7 pneumonia events/100 pt yrs), hypersensitivity reactions ≤1% (rash, fever, nausea & vomiting, chills, rigors, hypotension, & ↑ serum liver transaminases); can occur with reexposure.
  • Most Common:
    • Local injection site reactions (98% at least 1 local ISR, 4% dc because of ISR) (pain & discomfort, induration, erythema, nodules & cysts, pruritus, & ecchymosis).
    • Diarrhea 32%, nausea 23%, fatigue 20%.
