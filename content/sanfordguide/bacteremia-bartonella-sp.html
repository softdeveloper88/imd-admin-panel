Bacteremia, Asymptomatic, Bartonella sp.

Clinical Setting

  • Asymptomatic infection, usually not recognized.
  • Lice borne; often seen in homeless alcoholics.
  • Occurs in HIV/AIDS patients as well, but rarely asymptomatic. See Bacillary angiomatosis/Bacillary peliosis.
  • Can lead to endocarditis and/or trench fever.

Etiologies

  • Bartonella henselae
  • Bartonella quintana

Primary Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po/IV bid x 4 weeks + Gentamicin 1 mg/kg IV tid x 14 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po/IV bid x 4 weeks + Rifampin  300 mg po bid x 14 days

Comments
