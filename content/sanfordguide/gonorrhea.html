Gonorrhea, Overview

Overview

  • Causative agent: Neisseria gonorrhoeae, a gram negative diplococcus.
  • Development of antimicrobial resistance has been inexorable.
    • Due to a high prevalence of resistance, Fluoroquinolones are not recommended for treatment of gonococcal infections (MMWR 56:332, 2007).
    • Due to rising in vitro resistance and clinical failures, Oral cephalosporins are also no longer recommended. For Cephalosporin resistance in North America, see JAMA 2013. 309:1989.
    • Previously the CDC recommended the combination of Ceftriaxone + Azithromycin to ensure cure and to prevent further development of resistance, but as of 2020 the CDC recommends targeted treatment with Ceftriaxone alone.
    • See the CDC report on 2018 resistance patterns
    • There are many case reports of isolates with multiple drug resistance. Treatment was successful in one MDR case in the UK with resistance to Ceftriaxone and high-level resistance to Azithromycin with Ertapenem Euro Surveill 2018 Jul;23(27).
  • See 2020 CDC guidelines MMWR 69:1911external icon 2020; or 2021 CDC STD guidelines MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021
    • The 2020 revised recommendation is Ceftriaxone 500 mg IM x one dose
    • If chlamydial infection has not been excluded with negative NAAT or if concomitant C. trachomatis infection add Doxycycline 100 mg po bid x 7 days
  • See 2020 European guidelines Int J STD & AIDS, 2020 
    • Revised recommendation is Ceftriaxone 1 gm IM x one dose + Azithromycin  2 gm po x 1 dose
  • Review of evidence for CDC guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 74:S95 2022 (Gonorrhea) and Clin Infect Dis 74:S112 2022 (Chlamydia)
  • Review of the history of gonorrhea treatment and diagnostics: Clin Infect Dis 2018 doi: 10.1093/cid/ciy271.
