Usage and Dosing

  • Cidofovir is used for treatment of cytomegalovirus (CMV). FDA indication is only for CMV retinitis in HIV patients. However, it has been used successfully for JC virus disease (PML) and in the treatment of other herpesvirus infections (e.g., Acyclovir resistant HSV infections and refractory VZV infections). Cidofovir also the drug of choice for the treatment of smallpox (although no clinical data exist for this indication).
  • Properly timed IV prehydration with normal saline and Probenecid must be used with each Cidofovir infusion: 2 gm po 3 hours before each dose and further 1 gm doses 2 and 8 hours after completion of the Cidofovir infusion. Probenecid (a potent inhibitor of OAT) reduces uptake of Cidofovir by proximal tubular cells, lessening nephrotoxicity.
  • Renal function (serum creatinine and urine protein) must be monitored prior to each dose (see package insert for complete details).
  • Contraindicated if creatinine >1.5 mg/dL, CrCl ≤55 mL/min or urine protein ≥100 mg/dL.
  • Black Box warning. Renal impairment can occur after ≤2 doses.
  • For off label use of topical cidofovir see comments

Adverse Effects

  • Nephrotoxicity; dose-dependent proximal tubular injury (Fanconi-like syndrome): proteinuria, glycosuria, bicarbonaturia, phosphaturia, polyuria (nephrogenic diabetic insipidus, Lancet 350:413, 1997), acidosis, increased creatinine.
  • Concomitant saline prehydration, probenecid, extended dosing intervals allow use but still highly nephrotoxic.
  • Other toxicities: nausea 69%, fever 58%, alopecia 27%, myalgia 16%, probenecid hypersensitivity 16%, neutropenia 29%. Iritis and uveitis reported; also decreased intraocular pressure.
  • Black Box warning. Renal impairment can occur after ≤2 doses. Contraindicated in patients receiving concomitant nephrotoxic agents. Monitor for decreased WBC.
  • In animals, carcinogenic, teratogenic, causes decreased sperm and reduced fertility.

Antimicrobial Spectrum

Major Drug Interactions

  • Increases nephrotoxicity of Amphotericin B

Comments

  • Recommended dosage, frequency or infusion rate must not be exceeded.
  • Dose must be reduced or discontinued if changes in renal function occur during therapy. For increase of 0.3–0.4 mg/dL in serum creatinine, Cidofovir dose must be reduced from 5 to 3 mg/kg; discontinue Cidofovir if increase of 0.5 mg/dL above baseline or 3+ proteinuria develops (for 2+ proteinuria, observe patients carefully and consider discontinuation).
  • Topical cidofovir (Int J Pharm Compd 2006;10:324)
    • Numerous case reports describe success in treatment of a variety of viral skin infections due to Herpes simplex virus, molluscum contagiosum, and human papillomavirus (HPV)
    • For severe plantar warts see New Engl J Med 2017; 377:267
    • Not FDA approved. Need compounding pharmacy
