Sepsis, Child, Age > 1 mo

Clinical Setting

  • Sepsis in children, age > 1 month with signs of sepsis. Not to be confused with evaluation of the febrile infant 30-90 days where only ~10% have serious bacterial infection, most commonly UTI. These recommendations are for patients without obvious source of infection. For specific source, see pneumonia, peritonitis, meningitis.
  • Patient is not neutropenic.
  • Major concerns are Strep. pneumoniae, Group A Strep and Staph aureus (including community-associated MRSA). Less commonly gram negative bacteria

Etiologies

  • Strep. pneumoniae
  • Strep. pyogenes (Group A Strep)
  • N. meningitidis
  • Staph. aureus (both MSSA and MRSA)
  • Enterococcus and enteric gram negative rods in setting of UTI or hospitalized patienbt
  • H. influenzae (less common)

Primary Regimens

  • (Cefotaxime 150-200 mg/kg/day IV div q8h (where available) or Ceftriaxone 100 mg/kg IV q24h) +  Vancomycin 60- 80 mg/kg/day IV divided 3-4 times a day to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative is trough of 10-15 μg/mL but AUC targeting is preferred).

Alternative Regimens

  • Cefepime 150 mg/kg/day IV div q8h + Vancomycin as above (especially if concern for ESBL E. coli or Pseudomonas)
  • Piperacillin-tazobactam 300 mg/kg/day IV (divided q6h) + Vancomycin as above or Linezolid 10 mg/kg IV q8h (up to age 12 years -only if vancomycin cannot be used) (especially if abdominal source is suspected)

Comments

  • Vancomycin: traditional dosing of 45-60 mg/kg/day frequently does not achieve target AUC in term infants and older children with normal renal function. Use of AUC24  closer to 400 μg/mL adequate for most non-CNS infections. Clin Infect Dis Jul 13 2020
  • Coverage for Gram-negative bacilli is included but H. influenzae infection is now rare. 
  • Meningococcemia mortality remains high, Lancet 356:961, 2000.
  • See section on evaluation of febrile infant for children 30-90 days with fever but no signs of sepsis
  • Concomitant use of piperacillin-tazobactam with vancomycin may be associated with increased acute kidney injury
  • Surviving sepsis guidelines and the executive summary of the guidelines address fluids, pressors and management. 
  • Pediatric sepsis scores and criteria have been developed JAMA. 2024 Feb 27;331(8):675 and validated JAMA. 2024 Feb 27;331(8):665
