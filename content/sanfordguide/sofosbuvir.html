Sofosbuvir
Tradename: Solvadi

Usage and Dosing

  • NS5B RNA polymerase inhibitor for treatment of Genotype 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6 Hepatitis C infection in combination with another NS5A inhibitor or an NS3/4a (protease) inhibitor. Fixed dose combination forms:
    • Ledipasvir + Sofosbuvir (Harvoni)
    • Velpatasvir + Sofosbuvir (Epclusa)
    • Voxilaprevir + Velpatasvir + Sofosbuvir (Vosevi)
  • Efficacy established in patients awaiting liver transplant and in patients with HIV-1/HCV co-infection.
  • Test for evidence of current or prior Hepatitis B (HBV) infection: measure HBsAG and anti-HBC before initiating HCV treatment. If HBV co-infection, monitor patient for HBV reactivation during and post-HCV therapy.
  • Product availability: tablets (200, 400 mg) and oral pellets (150, 200 mg).

Adult Dose

  • Usual Dose: one 400 mg tablet po once daily administered with food, typically for 8 to 12 weeks (depending on genotype, state of liver fibrosis, and HCV RNA level) in combination with another DAA (direct acting agent). See HCV Treatment for specific regimens and duration of therapy

Adverse Effects

  • Most common (in combination with interferon and ribavirin): fatigue, headache, nausea, insomnia, anemia.
  • NOTE: FDA warning of risk of serious bradycardia when sofosbuvir is taken with amiodarone.
  • Black Box Warning: Cases of HBV reactivation, occasionally fulminant, during or after DAA therapy have been reported in HBV/HCV coninfected patients who were not already on HBV suppressive therapy.
