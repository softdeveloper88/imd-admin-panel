Lyme Disease, Overview

Overview

  • Lyme Disease: tick borne spirochetal disease caused most commonly by Borrelia burgdorferi.
    • In the U.S., rare cases are caused by Borrelia mayonii (Int J Syst Evol Microbiol 2016; 66:4878).
  • Important to avoid laboratory pitfalls in laboratory diagnosis. Diagnostic testing & serologic guidelines and emerging methods (Emerging Infect Dis 22:1169, 2016).
  • Lyme Disease references
    • 2020 Clinical practice guidelines for the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of Lyme disease, from the Infectious Diseases Society of America, American Academy of Neurology, and American Academy of Rheumatology (Neurology 96:262, 2021)
    • Lyme Disease Review Panel: Clin Infect Dis 73:1-48, 2021.
    • CDC surveillance and statistical data
  • Tick-borne Illness, Overview (differential diagnosis and images)
  • Potential co-infections include:
    • Babesia microti
    • Anaplasma phagocytophilum
    • Deer tick virus
    • Borrelia miyamotoi
    • Ehrlichia species Wisconsin
