Mycobacterium xenopi

Clinical Setting

  • Usually a colonizer (Epidemiol Infect. 2017; 145:515)
  • May cause pulmonary or disseminated infection in compromised hosts and may be hospital acquired.
  • Slow growing thermophile, growing best at 45ºC; may contaminate hospital hot water supplies and natural water sources (and hence may be associated with pseudoinfection).

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium xenopi

Primary Regimens

  • Azithromycin  250-500 mg + Rifampin 600 mg once daily + Ethambutol 15 mg/kg/day
  • Cavitary or advanced/severe bronchiectatic disease
    • Consider adding Amikacin 15-25 mg/kg IV or IM 3x weekly (adjust dose to obtain a peak serum concentration of 65-80 μg/mL in a sample drawn 30 min after a 30 min infusion and a trough of < 5 μg/mL) to the above regimen. Duration not well defined but toxicity becomes treatment limiting after 2-3 months.
  • Optimal duration of therapy not defined; recommendation is to treat for 12 months after conversion to culture-negative

Alternative Regimens

  • Clarithromycin 500 mg bid may be substituted for Azithromycin.
  • Moxifloxacin 400 mg once daily may be substituted for a macrolide.
  • Rifabutin 300 mg (150 mg if Clarithromycin is used) may be substituted for Rifampin.

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Mortality remains high; optimal regimen and duration of therapy not well defined (Chest. 2017; 152:120-142)
  • Expert consultation recommended.

Comments
