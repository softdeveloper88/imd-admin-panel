Brain Abscess, Nocardia

Clinical Setting

  • Usually occurs in immunocompromised hosts, often with concomitant pulmonary infection or other evidence of disseminated disease.
  • May occur with meningitis and/or ventriculitis.

Etiologies

  • Over 40 species of Nocardia have been identified in human infections (Clin Microbiol Rev. 2006; 19:259; Clin Microbiol Rev. 2022; 35:e0002721). The more common are listed below.
    • Nocardia nova complex
    • Nocardia farcinica
    • Nocardia cyriacigeorgica
    • Nocardia brasiliensis
    • Nocardia abscessus

Primary Regimens

  • Consider surgical drainage for larger absceses
  • [TMP-SMX (15 mg/kg/day TMP), (75 mg/kg/day SMX) IV/po divided in 2-4 doses] + Imipenem-cilastatin 500 mg IV q6h
  • If multiorgan involvement some add Amikacin 7.5 mg/kg q12h.
  • See Antimicrobial Stewardship for duration of therapy and oral step-down.

Alternative Regimens

  • Linezolid 600 mg IV or po q12h + Meropenem 2 gm IV q8h
  • See Comments for duration of therapy and oral stepdown.

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of treatment:
    • After 3-6 weeks of IV therapy (prefer 6 weeks for immuncompromised patients, those with extensive disease), switch to po therapy.
    • Immunocompetent patients: TMP-SMX, Minocycline or Amoxicillin-clavulanate x 3 or more months (longer for meningitis, extensive disease).
    • Immunocompromised patients: Treat with 2 drugs for at least one year.

Comments

  • Susceptibility tested should be performed to guide therapy.
  • TMP-SMX remains a drug of choice for CNS nocardia infection.
    • If organism is sulfonamide-resistant or patient is sulfa-allergic, use Amikacin plus one of Imipenem, Meropenem, Ceftriaxone.
  • N. farcinica is resistant to Ceftriaxone and it should not be used for treatment of infection caused by this organism.
