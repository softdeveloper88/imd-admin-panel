Transplants, CMV Prevention, Solid Organ

Clinical Setting

  • Prevention of cytomegalovirus (CMV) as an opportunistic infection in solid organ transplant (SOT) recipients.
  • Risk: highest among CMV IgG negative recipients (R-) of organs from CMV IgG+ donors (D+) and those who have received lymphocyte depleting antibody therapy (thymoglobulin, ATG, OKT-3, alemtuzumab).
  • Solid Organ Treatment Guidelines (Transplantation 102:900-31, 2018)
  • Prevention strategies: prophylaxis or preemptive therapy, prophylaxis is used more commonly: Clin Infect Dis 58:785, 2014.
  • See Transplant Infection Prevention, Overview for additional guidelines. See also prevention of CMV infection in stem cell transplant recipients.

Etiologies

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV)

Primary Regimens

  • Prophylaxis:
    • Valganciclovir 900 mg po daily
    • Letermovir 480 mg IV/po daily (approved for kidney transplant)
    • Duration of CMV Primary Prophylaxis by Organ and Donor/Recipient CMV Serostatus (Recommendations based on Clin Transplant 2019)

Alternative Regimens

  • Prophylaxis
    • Letermovir 480 mg IV/po daily; 240 mg per day if taking cyclosporine)
    • Ganciclovir 1000 mg po 3 x/day (see comment)
    • Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV 1 x/day
    • Valacyclovir 2 gm po 4 x/day (kidney only, see comment)
    • CMV IVIg or IVIg (see comments below)
  • Preemptive Therapy:
    • Monitor weekly for CMV viremia by PCR for 3-6 months post transplant.
    • If viremia detected, start Valganciclovir 900 mg po bid or Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV q12h until clearance of viremia, but for not less than 2 weeks. At that point, either switch to secondary prophylaxis or resume preemptive approach.

Comments

  • Consider restarting prophylaxis (or preemptive strategy) for patients who have received lymphocyte depleting antibodies for the treatment of rejection.
  • Valganciclovir not FDA-approved for CMV prophylaxis in liver transplant recipients due to a higher rate of tissue invasive disease when compared to Ganciclovir po for prophylaxis (Am J Transplant 4:611, 2004) but widely used and recommended as first-line by many experts.
  • CMV D-/R- organ transplant recipients do not require CMV prophylaxis but should receive CMV negative or leukocyte reduced blood products. Consider prophylaxis against HSV/VZV during the first at least one month post-transplant (Clin Transplant 33:e13526, 2019).
  • Ganciclovir 1000 mg po 3 x/day is as effective as Valganciclovir 900 mg once daily at reducing CMV disease in high risk patients but associated with higher rate of viremia while on prophylaxis (Am J Transplant 4:611, 2004).
  • CMV immune globulin can be considered as an adjunct to prophylaxis in high-risk lung, heart/lung, heart, or pancreas organ transplant recipients. Dosing: 150 mg/kg within 72 hrs of transplant and at 2, 4, 6 and 8 weeks; then 100 mg/kg at weeks 12 and 16.
  • Letermovir was FDA approved for the prevention of CMV infection following stem cell transplant. It is not active against HSV or VZV so addition of another agent for HSV/VZV prophylaxis is required (N Engl J Med 2017,377:2433)
  • Lower dose Valganciclovir (450 mg po q24h): concern for clinical failure and emerging resistance on low-dose prophylaxis and NOT recommended in D+/R- patients (Pharm Ther 35:676, 2010; Clin Infect Dis 52:313, 2011; Clin Infect Dis 52:322, 2011).
  • With anti-viral prophylaxis, onset of CMV disease appearing later; maintain vigilance for possible late-onset CMV disease and treat accordingly.
  • Resistance demonstrated in 5% of transplant recipients receiving primary prophylaxis (J Antimicrob Chemother 65:2628, 2010).
  • Review of Anti-CMV drugs (Curr Opin Organ Trans 24:469,2019)
