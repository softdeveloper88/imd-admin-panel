Acyclovir
Tradename: Zovirax

Usage and Dosing

  • Acyclovir is used to treat herpes virus infections (e.g., HSV and VZV), but not cytomegalovirus (CMV) infections.
  • Acyclovir also used for prophylaxis in transplantation protocols, for recurrent genital Herpes simplex, and in patients with recurrent keratitis due to Herpes simplex. Report of Acyclovir resistance in 35% of immunocompetent patients receiving standard prophylactic doses for recurrent stromal keratitis: J Infect Dis 208:1353, 2013; J Infect Dis 208:1359, 2013.
  • Allergy: see Acyclovir Desensitization.

Adult Dose

Genital herpes simplex (HSV), first episode400 mg po q8h
Varicella zoster (VZV)800 mg po 5x/day
Serious HSV/VZV infections (e.g., CNS infections)5-12.5 mg/kg IV q8h

    Adverse Effects

    • PO or IV: Neurotoxicity: manifest as hallucinations, death delusions, and involuntary movements. Result of high serum levels as result of overdosage in patients with renal insufficiency. Reversible: Am J Med. 128:692, 2015.
    • PO formulations: Generally well-tolerated with occasional diarrhea, vertigo, arthralgia. Less frequent rash, fatigue, insomnia, fever, menstrual abnormalities, acne, sore throat, muscle cramps, lymphadenopathy.
    • IV: Phlebitis, caustic with vesicular lesions with IV infiltration, CNS (1%): lethargy, tremors, confusion, hallucinations, delirium, seizures, coma—all reversible. Renal (5%): ↑ creatinine, hematuria. With high doses may crystallize in renal tubules → obstructive uropathy (rapid infusion, dehydration, renal insufficiency and ↑ dose ↑ risk). Adequate pre-hydration may prevent such nephrotoxicity. Hepatic: ↑ ALT, AST. Uncommon: neutropenia, rash, diaphoresis, hypotension, headache, nausea.
