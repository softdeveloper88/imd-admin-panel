Fluconazole
Tradename: Diflucan
Drug Shortage Information

Injection 2024-08-09

Usage and Dosing

  • Triazole antifungal agent. Candida krusei and Candida glabrata are resistant to Fluconazole.
  • Patients with candidal infection and prior triazole therapy have higher likelihood of Fluconazole (and other triazole) resistance.
  • Numerous drug-drug interactions. See below.
  • Fluconazole IV dose = oral dose because of excellent bioavailability.

    Adverse Effects

    • Fluconazole adverse effects overall: 16% [more common in HIV+ patients (21%)].
    • GI: Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, diarrhea.
    • Headache
    • Rash
    • Mild transaminase elevations
    • Possible QTc interval prolongation
    • Alopecia (scalp, pubic crest) in 12–20% patients on ≥400 mg po q24h after median of 3 months (reversible in ≈6 months).
    • Reversible adrenal insufficiency
    • Rare: severe hepatotoxicity (Clin Infect Dis 41:301, 2005), exfoliative dermatitis.
    • Miscarriage risk under evaluation (JAMA 315:58, 2016)

    Pregnancy Risk

    FDA Risk Category 1 Avoid if possible, especially high doses (6-12 mg/kg/day) for a prolonged period during first trimester.
    The risk of miscarriage or congenital abnormalities with low doses (e.g., 150 mg x1) is unclear; CDC recommends topical azoles for VVC.
    Use during Lactation Safe with monitoring
