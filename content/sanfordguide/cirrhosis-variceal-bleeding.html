Cirrhosis/Variceal Bleeding

Clinical Setting

  • Prophylaxis for spontaneous bacterial peritonitis (SBP) in the setting of active variceal bleeding, cirrhosis, with or without ascites.

Etiologies

  • Gastrointestinal flora

Primary Regimens

  • Ceftriaxone 1 gm IV q24h for maximum 7 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q12h or 500 mg po bid for 5-7 days
  • Norfloxacin 400 mg po bid for 5-7 days

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Long-term prophylaxis not indicated unless spontaneous bacterial peritonitis develops.

Comments

  • 2021 AASLD Guideline (Hepatology 2021;74:1014) 
  • Short-term prophylactic antibiotics in cirrhotics with gastrointestinal (GI) hemorrhage, with or without ascites, decreases the rate of bacterial infection and increases survival (Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2016; 14:1629).
  • Child-Pugh class B and C at greater risk of infection and death than class A (Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 13:1189, 2015).
  • Prior antibiotics and prophylaxis, are associated with infection caused by multidrug resistant gram-negatives (Hepatology 2016; 63:2019).
  • Long-term therapy with proton pump inhibitors and prophylaxis for SBP are risk factors for subsequent infections (Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 13:753, 2015).
