Streptococcus pneumoniae

Clinical Setting

  • Isolation of Streptococcus pneumoniae; may display in vitro resistance to penicillin, macrolides, and/or TMP-SMX.
  • Criteria for resistance varies depending on whether the in vivo target is effective concentrations in the CSF or non-CSF sites of infection.

Classification

  • Gram positive cocci in pairs, chains 

Primary Regimens

  • Non-meningeal infections
    • Penicillin G Susceptible (MIC < 2 μg/mL):
      • Penicillin G 2 million units IV q4h
      • Ceftriaxone 1-2 gm IV q24h
      • Clindamycin 600 mg IV q8h
      • Moxifloxacin 400 mg IV q24h
      • Levofloxacin 750 mg IV q24h
    • Intermediate (MIC = 4 μg/mL)
      • Few data, penicillin, ampicillin, or amoxicillin may be effective for less serious infections, but for more serious infections prefer the drugs that are listed below for penicillin-resistant infections
    • Resistant to Penicillin G (MIC ≥ 8.0 μg/mL):
      • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h
      • Moxifloxacin 400 mg IV q24h
      • Levofloxacin 750 mg IV q24h
      • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve trough concentration of 15-20 μg/mL
      • Linezolid 600 mg IV q12h
      • Ceftaroline 600 mg IV q12h
  • Meningeal infection
    • See Meningitis, Streptococcus pneumoniae

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Prevention

  • See Pneumococcal, Vaccines, Adult for indications, available products, dosing, and vaccine characteristics for pre-exposure prevention.
    • Single dose Prevnar 20 (PCV20) has de facto replaced all previous vaccines (PCV13, PPSV23). Less cumbersome than sequential dosing with PCV15/PPSV23 spaced by 1 year.

Comments

  • Resistance to Penicillin G (MIC > 4.0 μg/mL):
    • Meningeal isolates: < 0.1= Susceptible; 0.1-1.0 = Intermediate; ≥ 2.0 = Resistant.
    • Non-meningeal isolates: ≤ 2.0 = Susceptible; 4.0 = Intermediate; ≥ 8.0 = Resistant.
  • Resistance to Ceftriaxone
    • Meningeal isolates: < 0.5 = Susceptible; > 0.5 and < 1.0 = Intermediate; > 1.0 = Resistant.
    • Non-meningeal isolates: ≤ 1.0 = Susceptible; > 1 and < 2.0 = Intermediate; > 2.0 = Resistant.
