Meningitis, Post-Trauma or Post-Neurosurgery

Clinical Setting

  • Post head trauma; post-neurosurgery, e.g., post-ventriculostomy, post-lumbar drain, ventriculoperitoneal shunts, Ommaya reservoir. Fever and reduced level of consciousness.
  • See IDSA Guideline on Healthcare-Associated Ventriculitis and Meningitis: Clin Infect Dis. 2017; 64:e34–e65.
  • For shunt or CNS catheter infections see Meningitis: Catheter, Shunt

Etiologies

  • Staph. epidermidis
  • Staph. aureus
  • Enterobacteriaceae
  • C. acnes
  • Facultative and aerobic Gram-negative bacilli, to include:
    • P. aeruginosa: may be multi-drug resistant
    • Acinetobacter: may be multi-drug resistant
  • Post-trauma recurrent meningitis: S. pneumoniae, H. influenzae, N. meningitidis

Primary Regimens

  • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) + (Cefepime 2 gm IV q8h or Ceftazidime 2 gm IV q8h)
    • Discontinue Vancomycin when MRSA is ruled out and modify primary regimen based on culture results.
    • If resistant gram-negative rod and not responding to IV beta-lactam, may need to add intraventricular therapy, see Comments.
  • If recurrent meningitis: Vancomycin as above + (Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q12h or Cefotaxime  2 gm IV q4h (where available outside the US)) + dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg IV q6h x 2-4 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Meropenem 2 gm IV (infused over 4 hrs) repeat q8h + Vancomycin as above
    • Population PK dosing regimen in patients with/without drug loss via CSF drainage. With CSF drainage of less than 150 ml/day, above regimen has best probability of attainment (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 60:6619, 2016)
  • If severe penicillin/cephalosporin allergy, for possible infection due to gram-negatives, substitute either Aztreonam 2 gm IV q6-8h or Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q8-12h

Comments

  • Vancomycin alone is not optimal for Strep. pneumoniae. Once susceptible Strep. pneumoniae is identified, switch to Ceftriaxone or Cefotaxime.
  • For meningitis due to drug-resistant enteric organisms or P. aeruginosa, consider adding adjunctive intraventicular or intrathecal therapy.
    • Use only preservative-free drug for intraventricular dosing; clamp/close catheter for 1 hour after 1st dose.
    • Intraventricular adult daily dose (mg) for instillation into lateral ventricle (J Microbiol Immunol Infect 47:204, 2014):
      • Vancomycin 10-20 mg
      • Amikacin 30 mg
      • Gentamicin 4-8 mg
      • Tobramycin 5-20 mg
      • Colistin (colistimethate sodium) 10 mg daily or 5 mg q12h
      • Polymyxin B 5 mg
  • Treatment of Acinetobacter sp. meningitis: Lancet Infect Dis 9:245, 2009; Antimicrob Ag Chemother 57:1938, 2013; Antimicrob Ag Chemother 56:4416, 2012.
  • Prevention note: Chlorhexidine applied to skin exit site of ventriculostomy drains decreased rate of meningoventriculitis (Clin Infect Dis 62:404, 2016).
