Otitis Media, Treatment Failure

Clinical Setting

  • Otitis media, treatment for clinical failure after 3 days of therapy.
  • Definition of failure: no change in ear pain, fever, bulging TM or otorrhea.

Etiologies

  • Drug-resistant Streptococcus pneumoniae (Int J Pediatr Otorhinolaryngol 79:2129, 2015)  

Primary Regimens

  • Pediatric
    • Amoxicillin-clavulanate high dose 90 mg/kg/day po of Amoxicillin component x 10 days
    • Cefdinir 7 mg/kg po q12h or 14 mg/kg po q24h x 10 days
    • Cefpodoxime proxetil 10 mg/kg/day po as single daily dose x 10 days
    • Cefprozil 15 mg/kg po q12h x 10 days
    • Cefuroxime axetil 30 mg/kg/day po divided q12h x 10 days
      • Note: Cefuroxime susp not available in the US, may be available in other markets.
    • Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IM x 3 days
    • Patient has had no antibiotics in the prior month other than in last 3 days:
    • Patient has had antibiotics in the prior month in addition to the last 3 days:
      • Ceftriaxone 50 mg/kg IM x 3 days
      • Levofloxacin: for patients in whom Ceftriaxone is contraindicated or refractory infection
        • Age < 5 years: 10 mg/kg po q12h x 10 days
        • Age > 5 years 10 mg/g po once daily for 10 days (max daily dose 750 mg)
      • Tympanocentesis
    • Duration: see regimens above; unknown if these are optimal for persistent disease.
  • Adult
    • Cefdinir 600 mg po once daily x 10 days
    • Cefpodoxime proxetil 200 mg po bid for 10 days
    • Cefprozil 500 mg po bid x 10 days
    • Cefuroxime axetil 500 mg po bid x 10 days
    • Ceftriaxone 1-2 gm IV/IM x 3 days
    • Levofloxacin 750 mg po once daily x 10 days

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Tympanocentesis allows for etiologic diagnosis, susceptibility testing, and pathogen-directed therapy
