Endocarditis: HACEK, HABCEK

Clinical Setting

  • Specific/directed therapy for endocarditis caused by slow-growing, fastidious Gram-negative bacilli
  • HACEK group of organisms (HABCEK if Bartonella included).  For Bartonella, see Endocarditis, Bartonella.

Etiologies

  • Haemophilus parainfluenzae
  • Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans (formerly Actinobacillus)
  • Aggregatibacter aphrophilus (formerly Haemophilus)
  • Cardiobacterium hominis
  • Eikenella corrodens
  • Kingella species
  • Also, Bartonella sp.
    • NOTE: Suggested treatment regimens are not predictably active vs Bartonella sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h x 4 weeks OR Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q12h x 4 weeks
  • Treat for 6 weeks if prosthetic valve infection.

Alternative Regimens

  • Ampicillin-sulbactam 3 gm IV q6h x 4 weeks
  • Ampicillin 2 gm IV q4h (if growth is sufficient to permit susceptibility testing)
  • Levofloxacin 750 mg po/IV q24h x 4 weeks OR Moxifloxacin 400 mg po/IV q24h x 4 weeks
  • Ciprofloxacin 750 mg po q12h (option for oral step-down in selected patients who have responded to IV therapy and are clinically stable, but clinical experience is limited).

Comments

  • Avoid combination therapy with gentamicin due to nephrotoxicity
  • Guideline references for HACEK/HABCEK endocarditis:
    • American Heart Assn (Circulation 132:1435, 2015).
    • British Society for Antimicrobial Therapy (J Antimicrob Chemother 67:269, 2012)
    • European Heart Journal 25: 267, 2004
