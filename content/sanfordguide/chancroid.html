Chancroid

Clinical Setting

  • Sexually transmitted painful genital ulcers.
  • Prevalence declining world-wide.
  • A cause of chronic skin ulceration in children from developing countries. (Emerg Infect Dis 22:1, 2016)
  • Genital ulcers:
    • Painful: HSV, Chancroid (Curr Opin Infect Dis 29:52, 2016) or Mpox (monkeypox)
    • Painless: syphilis, LGV (though painful lymphadenopathy), and Granuloma inguinale (Klebsiella granulomatis or Donovanosis)
  • All patients with genital ulcers should be evaluated for syphilis (Darkfield; serology), HSV (culture or PCR) and culture for H. ducreyi (PCR not FDA approved but available in some labs). Granuloma inguinale needs biopsy.
    • On occasion, more than one pathogen is present.
  • Evaluate after 7 days, ulcer should objectively improve.

Diagnosis

  • Haemophilus ducreyi culture though need special media, not widely available
  • NAAT, more sensitive than culture, but not widely available.
  • Probable diagnosis if all of below:
    • 1) One or more painful genital ulcers
    • 2) Typical clinical presentation with genital ulcers +/- regional lymphadenopathy
    • 3) no evidence of T. pallidum infection (darkfield examination or NAAT or by serology) 7–14 days after onset of ulcers
    • 4) HSV-1 or HSV-2 NAAT or HSV culture on the ulcer exudate or fluid negative.

Etiologies

  • Haemophilus ducreyi

Primary Regimens

  • Ceftriaxone 250 mg IM single dose
  • Azithromycin 1 gm po single dose

Alternative Regimens

  • Ciprofloxacin 500 mg bid po x 3 days
  • Erythromycin base 500 mg tid po x 7 days

Comments

  • As with all STDs, all patients diagnosed with chancroid should be tested for HIV.
  • In HIV+ patients, failures reported with single dose Azithromycin (Clin Infect Dis 21:409, 1995).
  • People with HIV or uncircumcised males may respond less well to treatment.
  • Reports of Ciprofloxacin and Erythromycin resistance but few reliable reports of antimicrobial susceptibilities. 
  • Relatively little high quality data as to most efficacious treatment (Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2017 12:CD012492)
  • All sex partners of patients with chancroid should be examined and treated if they have evidence of disease or have had sex with index patient within the last 60 days.
  • See 2021 CDC STD Guidelines:  MMWR Recomm Rep 70 (RR-4):1 2021
  • Review non syphilis non HSV genital ulcers: Infect Dis Clin North Am 37:369 2023.
