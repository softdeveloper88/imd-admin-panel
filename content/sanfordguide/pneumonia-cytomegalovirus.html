Pneumonia, Cytomegalovirus

Clinical Setting

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV) pneumonia is a rare but life-threatening complication of clinical CMV disease. It is most commonly seen in solid organ transplant (SOT) and hematopoietic stem cell transplant (HCT) recipients but can occur in other immunocompromised populations.
  • Chest CT scan often reveals ground-glass opacities but can also have scattered nodules or consolidations.
  • Among adults with HIV, CMV pneumonia is rare.  BAL can be positive for CMV, especially in the setting of another opportunistic lung infection (e.g., PJP); however treatment of CMV typically does not improve the clinical outcome.

Diagnosis

  • Combination of classic imaging findings + high serum CMV PCR or antigenemia in an at-risk host.
    • Gold standard: evidence of CMV on histology specimen from lung biopsy.
    • CMV culture from bronchoscopy: non-specific and positive results can be seen in immunocompromised patients in the absence of pneumonitis.

Etiologies

  • Cytomegalovirus (CMV)

Primary Regimens

  • Ganciclovir 5 mg/kg IV q12h with transition to Valganciclovir 900 mg po q12h following clinical improvement (Rx duration individualized)

Alternative Regimens

  • Foscarnet 90 mg/kg IV q12h
  • Cidofovir 5 mg/kg IV once weekly
  • IVIG or CMV immune globulin (as adjunctive therapy, see Comments)

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Treatment duration should be individualized. Continue treatment until: (1) CMV PCR or antigenemia has become undetectable, (2) clinical evidence of disease has resolved, and (3) at least 3 weeks of treatment (often 3-6 weeks).

Comments

  • Treatment option review: J Infect Dis 2020; 221(S1): 532
  • CMV immune globulin can be considered as adjunctive therapy in cases of life-threatening pneumonitis. There is retrospective data in HCT recipients that suggests benefit of both IVIg (Ann Intern Med 109:777, 1988) and CMV hyperimmune globulin (Ann Intern Med 109:783, 1988) and many experts recommend adding this therapy particularly in HCT recipients (Blood 113:5711, 2009). In contrast, retrospective review of  421 cases over 25 years found  no effect on overall or attributable mortality (Clin Infect Dis 61:31, 2015).
  • Guidelines for CMV therapy: Transplantation 96:333, 2013;  Am J Transplant 13:93, 2013.
  • Letermovir is used a prophylaxis agent in adult CMV-seropositive recipients of an allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplant (HSCT)
