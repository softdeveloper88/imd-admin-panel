Mycobacterium chimaera

Clinical Setting

  • Infection with Mycobacterium chimaera, a member of the Mycobacterium avium complex (MAC), has been associated with heater-cooler units (HCU) used in cardiopulmonary bypass procedures (see Curr Opin Infect Dis. 2017; 30:388  and Infect Control Hosp Epidemiol. 2022;43:1333 for review).
    • The organism is transmitted by contaminated aersols generated by heater-cooler units, LivaNova 3T HCUs in particular.
  • Most infections are recognized within 2 years of the procedure with a latency of up to 5 years.
  • Types of infection include chorioretinitis, endocarditis, sternal, graft, and disseminated infections. Three patients with disseminated encephalitis: suggested steps in patient evaluation and management (Clin Infect Dis 2020; 70: 692)

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium chimaera

Primary Regimens

  • For severe disease, including those associated with HCUs (see Clin Infect Dis. 2019; 68:1244).
    • Four or five-drug regimen similar to that for treatment of Mycobacterium avium-intracellulare (MAC) selected from the following (see Comments):
      • Azithromycin 500 IV or po once daily OR Clarithromycin 500 mg po twice daily
      • Ethambutol 15 mg/kg po once daily
      • Rifampin 600 mg IV or po one daily OR Rifabutin 300 mg po once daily
      • Amikacin 15 mg/kg IV once daily or three times weekly (include in initial regimen, then as tolerated)
      • Moxifloxacin 400 mg IV or po once daily
      • Clofazimine 100 mg po once daily
      • Linezolid 600 mg po or IV once or twice daily
      • Bedaquiline 400 mg po daily for 2 weeks as a load then 200 mg po three times weekly (use beyond 24 weeks has not been studied)

Alternative Regimens

  • For less severe diseases, not HCU-associated, not disseminated disease.
    • See recommendations for Mycobacterium avium-intracellulare.

Comments

  • Results of In vitro susceptibility of 87 clinical isolates (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2019;63: e00755-19):
    • All isolates susceptible to clarithromycin (azithro not tested)
    • 98% suscept amikacin and streptomicin
    • 91% suscept to ethambutol
    • 82% suscept to rifampin
    • 52% were resistant to moxifloxacin
    • 39% were resistant to linezolid
  • Drug-related adverse events are common.  Monitor as follows:
    • Monthly complete blood count, metabolic panel, inflammatory markers
    • While administering Amikacin: serum creatinine weekly, monthly audiography, assess for vestibular signs and symptoms monthly
    • Ethambutol: color vision testing at baseline then every 3-6 months
    • Bedaquiline: Electrocardiogram (ECG) to monitor QTc at baseline and at 2, 12, and 24 months and weekly if used in combination with Moxifloxacin; discontinue for QTc > 500 ms on repeat ECG.
    • Linezolid: monitor for hematologic toxicity and symptoms of neuropathy (optic neuropathy in particular)
  • Optimal duration of therapy unknown: 12-24 months or longer, consider lifelong suppression for those with retained prosthetic material (Diagn Microbiol Infect Dis, 2016;86:382-384; Clin Infect Dis. 2019; 68:1244).
  • Mortality is high, up to 50%, particularly with HCU-associated disease and the infection may not be curable.
