Severe Fever Thrombocytopenia Syndrome Virus, SFTSV

Clinical Setting

  • Severe Fever with Thrombocytopenia Syndrome Virus (SFTSV).
    • Clinical Symptoms:  Fever, weakness, myalgias, GI Symptoms
    • Lab:  thrombocytopenia and leukopenia; Elevated LDH (> 1200) and CPK (> 800) associated with higher mortality rates
  • Infection reported in northwest Missouri. Initially thought to be an anaplasma  infection, but serology showed a new virus.
  • Possibly transmitted by Haemaphysalis longicomis or Amblyomma americanum (lone star) tick.
  • See New Eng J Med 364:1523, 2011; Review Viruses 16(1): 128, 2024
  • Epidemiology update  (Clin Exp Vaccine Res 7: 43- 50, 2018)
    • Cases reported worldwide
    • Most cases in Asia (China, Japan, Korea); also reported in Mediterranean countries and the US
    • Sequence homology indicates animals may be a reservoir (goats / cattle / dogs /cats)
    • Those working in rural areas at risk; also hospital workers exposed to SFTS patients

Etiologies

  • New virus in the Phlebovirus / Bunyaviridae virus family described in China.
  • Six clades identified (A - F; based on 159 SFTSV strains)

Primary Regimens

  • Antiviral therapy has not been shown to be effective.

Alternative Regimens

  • Ribavirin use did not effect case fatality rate among 311 patients followed in large case series (Clin Infect Dis 57:1292, 2013).

Comments

  • Vaccine development underway
  • Review of emerging therapeutics (Front Microbiol,  2020)
  • Among strains:  A, D and F (China); Clade B (Korea / Japan).  Recombinant viruses common
  • Wide distribution of virus believed to be result of transmission by migratory birds
  • Favipiravir treatment studies underway  (PLoS Neglected Diseases 2021)
