Gongylonemiasis

Clinical Setting

  • Rare infection of humans with adult worms localizing in oral mucosa and sometimes pharynx and esophagus
  • Mucosal tracks seen in mouth
  • Acquired through accidental ingestion of an infected insect, such as a beetle or cockroach (or cockroach parts in foodstuffs)

Etiologies

  • Gongylonema pulchrum

Primary Regimens

  • Surgical removal

Alternative Regimens

  • Albendazole 400 mg/day po x 3 days (but no data). Case report suggesting longer albendazole course is needed (Am J Trop Med Hyg 96:873, 2017)

Comments

  • References: Clin Infect Dis 32:1378, 2001; J Helminthol 80:425, 2006.
