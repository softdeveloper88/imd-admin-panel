Nevirapine
Tradename: Viramune

Usage and Dosing

  • Nevirapine (NVP) is indicated for use as an NNRTI as part of a 3-drug combination regimen, usually paired with 2 nucleos(t)ide agents (e.g., FTC + TDF or ZDV + 3TC).
  • Nevirapine Black Box warning—fatal hepatotoxicity:  Women (including pregnant women) with CD4 >250/μl, or men with CD4 >400/μl. Avoid in these groups unless benefits clearly > risks. If used, intensive monitoring required.
  • 2 week dose escalation period reduces skin reactions and other adverse effects (induces its own metabolism), except possibly when switching from Efavirenz to Nevirapine, see Clin Infect Dis 51:365, 2010.
  • Rash severe enough to stop drug in 7%, severe or life-threatening skin reactions in 2%. Do not restart if any suspicion of such reactions.
  • As with Efavirenz, because of long T½, consider continuing companion agents for several days if Nevirapine is discontinued.
  • Generic formulations available: 200 mg immediate-release tablets, 400 mg extended-release tablets. Generic oral suspension not available in the US.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive). Also see ARV Drug-Drug Interactions.

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Black Box warning—Severe life-threatening skin reactions reported: Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis, & hypersensitivity reaction or drug rash with eosinophilia & systemic symptoms (DRESS) (Arch Intern Med 161:2501, 2001).
    • For severe rashes, stop drug immediately & do not re-challenge. In a clinical trial, the use of prednisone ↑ the risk of rash.
    • Black Box warning—Life-threatening hepatotoxicity reported, 2/3 during the first 12 weeks of therapy. Overall 1% develop hepatitis. Patients with pre-existing ↑ in ALT or AST &/or history of chronic Hep B or C ↑ susceptible (Hepatol 35:182, 2002). Women with CD4 >250, including pregnant women, at ↑ risk. Avoid in this group unless no other option. Men with CD4 >400 also at ↑ risk. Monitor patients intensively (clinical & LFTs), especially during the first 12 weeks of treatment. If clinical hepatotoxicity, severe skin or hypersensitivity reactions occur, discontinue drug & NEVER rechallenge.
  • Most Common:
    • Rash 37%: usually occurs during 1st 6 weeks of therapy. Follow recommendations for 14-day lead-in period to ↓ risk of rash. Women experience 7-fold ↑ in risk of severe rash (Clin Infect Dis 32:124, 2001). 50% resolve within 2 weeks of dc drug & 80% by 1 month. 6.7% discontinuation rate.
