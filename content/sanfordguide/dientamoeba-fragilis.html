Dientamoeba fragilis

Clinical Setting

  • Diarrhea, abdominal pain, anorexia, and fatigue; symptoms may be chronic.
  • Role as a pathogen in chronic cases accepted by most but not all studies, life cycle not fully defined.
  • Celiac disease and other pathogens should be ruled out before treating a positive PCR result.
  • Fecal oral transmission, not an ameba but an intestinal flagellate
  • References: Am J Trop Med Hyg 82:614, 2010; J Clin Microbiol 54:2243, 2016; Clin Microbiol Rev 29:553, 2017.

Diagnosis

  • Increased diagnosis recently due to presence in commercial stool multiplex PCR panels.
    • 18.7% of returned travelers with chronic abdominal symptoms had D. fragilis by PCR (J Trav Med 2022 Jul 14;29(4):taac011)
    • 68% prevalence in European children (Trop Med Infect Dis 2021 Sep 2;6(3):160)

Etiologies

  • Dientamoeba fragilis

Primary Regimens

  • Iodoquinol 650 mg po tid x 20 days
  • Metronidazole 750 mg po tid x 10 days (20-40% clinical failure rate in most studies)
  • Paromomycin 25-35 mg/kg/day po in 3 divided doses x 7 days
  • D. fragilis should be treated when the organism is found as a sole potential pathogen in stools from patients with abdominal pain or diarrhea lasting for more than one week.

Alternative Regimens

  • For treatment failure:
    • A second course of Iodoquinol
    • Iodoquinol and Paromomycin in combination; doses as above.
    • (Tetracycline 500 mg po qid  + Iodoquinol 650 mg po tid) x 10 days

Comments

  • Stool PCR indicates that therapy often fails to eradicate organism.
  • Non-axenic in vitro culture data indicates tinidazole and metronidazole to be most active compounds (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 56:487, 2012).
  • Some authors have linked D. fragilis to irritable bowel syndrome.
  • There are no properly controlled studies that compare the efficacies of the various treatment options.
    • Most clinicians treat symptomatic persons.
  • Metronidazole vs placebo in children showed no difference in symptom resolution even with 62% initial parasite clearance rate Clin Infect Dis 58:1692, 2014.
  • The combination therapies provided have been listed in anecdotal reports.
  •  Iodoquinol and Paromomycin availability, see Antiparasitic Drugs, Sources.
