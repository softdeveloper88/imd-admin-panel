Meningitis, Herpes simplex

Clinical Setting

  • Headache, photophobia, fever, meningismus, and cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) lymphocytic pleocytosis, accompanied by mildly elevated protein and normal glucose.
  • Herpes simplex virus 2 (HSV-2) is a leading cause of viral meningitis.
    • Benign clinical course in most cases
    • May be associated with primary genital infection, but vesicles often not present.
  • Mollaret’s recurrent “aseptic” meningitis is an uncommon form of benign, recurrent meningitis.
    • Patients may also have other neurological manifestations such as seizure, cranial neuropathies (See Lancet Infect Dis 14:1022, 2014).
  • Positive PCR for HSV in CSF confirms diagnosis.

Etiologies

  • Herpes simplex virus (HSV) (type 2>>type 1)

Primary Regimens

  • Acyclovir 10 mg/kg IV q8h until clinical improvement, then Valacyclovir 1000 mg po tid to complete a 10-14 day course

Alternative Regimens

  • See Comments

Comment

  • Evidence lacking for effectiveness of antivirals and optimal treatment duration: oral valacyclovir may be an option for those with minimal symptoms, mild disease caused by HSV-2.
  • Recent reviews: Medicine. 2021;100:e27856; Clin Infect Dis. 2022;75:753; Curr Opin Infect Dis. 2023; 36:177.
