Osteomyelitis, Sternal

Clinical Setting

  • Osteomyelitis of the sternum, post-operative.

Etiologies

  • Staphylococcus aureus
  • Staphylococcus epidermidis
  • Less commonly, Gram-negative bacteria

Primary Regimens

  • Sternal debridement is indicated for bone culture and for removal of necrotic bone.
  • Empiric therapy: Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) + Cefepime 1-2 gm IV q8h
  • Confirmed methicillin-resistant staphylococcal strain:  Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL)
  • Confirmed methicillin-susceptible staphylococcal strain:
    • Nafcillin 2 gm IV q4h

Alternative Regimens

  • LinezolidNAI 600 mg IV/po bid or Daptomycin 10 mg/kg IV q24h for confirmed methicillin-resistant staphylococcal strain (See Comments)
  • Cefazolin 2 gm IV q8h for confirmed methicillin-susceptible staphylococcal stain

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy not well defined, but a prolonged course of therapy, e.g., 6-8 weeks, is recommended.

Comments

  • For gram negative infections, select an agent based on local factors: species prevalence in surgical infections and susceptibility profiles (e.g., expanded-spectrum cephalosporins, Piperacillin-tazobactam, carbapenems)
  • Linezolid less attractive for prolonged therapy (> 2 weeks) because of potential toxicity.
  • A fluoroquinolone is an alternative for gram-negatives but use may be limited if there is high prevalence of resistance among Enterobacteriaceae and Pseudomonas aeruginosa.
