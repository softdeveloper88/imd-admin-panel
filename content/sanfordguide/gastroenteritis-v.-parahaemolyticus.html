Gastroenteritis, Vibrio sp.

Clinical Setting

  • Gastroenteritis typically with watery diarrhea, commonly associated with consumption of raw shellfish.
  • Vibrio parahemolyticus is the most common cause of Vibrio gastroenteritis. Other Vibrio species causing gastroenteritis include V. mimicus, V. fluvialis and non-toxigenic V. cholerae (non 01 and non 0139)(Epidemiol Infect 124: 489, 2000).
  • Review comparing different Vibrio infections (Nat Rev Dis Primers 4:8 2018).
  • See also Gastroenteritis, Vibrio cholerae.

Etiologies

  • Vibrio parahaemolyticus
  • Vibrio fluvialis
  • Vibrio mimicus
  • Non-toxigenic V. cholerae

Primary Regimens

  • Mild disease: hydration is the primary treatment (See Antimicrobial Stewardship)
  • Severe disease, immunocompromised host:
    • Ciprofloxacin 500 mg po twice daily, 1-3 days
    • Levofloxacin 500 mg once daily, 1-3 days
    • Ceftriaxone 1 gm IV q24h, 1-3 days

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 100 mg po twice daily (See Antimicrobial Stewardship)
  • Azithromycin 1 gm po single dose

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Mild disease or moderate disease: Antimicrobial therapy does not shorten course and is not recommended.
  • Duration of therapy not well established

Comments

  • Resistance patterns vary widely geographically (for rates in various countries see Food Microbiol 2016 Aug;57:128-34).
