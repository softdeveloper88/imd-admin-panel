Microsporidiosis

Clinical Setting

  • Intracellular spore forming organisms.
  • Formerly considered protozoa; they are protists related to fungi, at least 6 genera.
  • At risk: immunocompromised patients, travelers, children, and elderly.
  • Can cause a variety of infections: intestinal, ocular, muscular, pulmonary, and renal.
  • In HIV patients, most microsporidia infections occur when CD4 count <100 cells/mL.

Diagnosis

  • Most labs use modified trichrome stain. Need electron micrographs for species identification.
  • FA and PCR methods in development.

Etiologies

  • Ocular (Keratoconjunctivitis)
    • Encephalitozoon hellum or cuniculi
    • Vittaforma (Nosema) corneae
    • Nosema (Vittaforma) ocularum
  • Intestinal: non-bloody watery diarrhea; also cholangitis or acalculous cholecystitis
    • Enterocytozoon bieneusi most common species in patients with HIV; also reported in transplant patients (Am J Transplant 10:1925 2010)
    • Encephalitozoon (Septata) intestinalis
  • Disseminated
    • E. hellum, cuniculi or intestinalis
    • Pleistophora sp.
    • Trachipleistophora sp.
    • Anncallia species: e.g., A. vesicularum and A. algerae
  • Skeletal muscle; paranasal sinuses

Primary Regimens

  • For HIV patients with low CD4 counts, effective antiretroviral therapy is of highest import.
  • For keratoconjunctivitis:
    • Topical fumagillin bicylohexylammonium (Fumidil B) 3 mg/mL in saline (fumagillin 70 µg/mL) eye drops: two drops every 2 hours for 4 days, then two drops four times daily 
      • Sources unclear as currently not available from compounding pharmacies
    • If associated disseminated microsporidiosis, use Albendazole 400 mg po bid x 2-4 weeks (exact duration unclear, if HIV associated, continue treatment until symptoms resolve and if CD4 responsive to ART, continue 6 months after CD4 >200).
  • For intestinal or disseminated disease:
    • Albendazole
      • Adults: 400 mg po bid x 2-4 weeks. Exact duration is unclear. For HIV associated, latest guidelines suggest treating for 6 months after CD4 >200.
        • Children: 15 mg/kg/day po divided bid.
      • Itraconazole 400 mg orally daily plus albendazole 400 mg orally twice daily may have activity for Trachipleistophora or Anncaliia.
      • Not effective for E. bieneusi and Vittaforma corneae,
    • Success for E. bieneusi reported with oral Fumagillin, 20 mg po tid (AIDS 14:1341, 2000; N Engl J Med 346:1963 2002; Am J Transplant 10-1925 2010) and related compound TNP-470, but neither available in the U.S (caused neutropenia and thrombocytopenia). Fumagillin no longer made by Sanofi.

Alternative Regimens

  • At present, there are no effective alternative regimens.
  • For E. bieneusi can consider nitazoxanide 500 mg po bid at least 14 days based upon isolated case reports. 

Comments
