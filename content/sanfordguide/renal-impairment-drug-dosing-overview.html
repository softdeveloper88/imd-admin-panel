Renal Impairment Dosing, Calculations Overview

Overview

  • This page provides the formulas for estimating creatinine clearance (CrCl) and glomerular filtration rate (GFR).
  • To make the calculations, use the interactive dosing calculators found under Tables and Tools.
  • Note that estimations of creatinine clearance are less accurate in patients with fluctuating renal function.

Creatinine Clearance-based Calculations

Ideal Body Weight

  • Calculations for ideal body weight (IBW) in kg:
    • Male: 50 kg + 2.3 kg/inch over 60 inches height.
    • Female: 45.5 kg + 2.3 kg/inch over 60 inches height.
  • Reference: Drug Intell Clin Pharm 1974;8:650.

Adjusted Body Weight

  • Calculation for adjusted body weight (adjusted BW):
    • Adjusted BW = IBW + 0.4 (Actual body weight minus IBW)
  • All weights are in kg.

Estimation of CrCl

  • The Sanford Guide uses the Cockcroft-Gault equation (Nephron 1976;16:31) for estimation of CrCl in mL/min (age in years, Scr=serum creatinine in mg/dL):
    • Male: (140 minus age in years) x weight in kg / (72 x Scr)
    • Female: [(140 minus age in years) x weight in kg / (72 x Scr)] x 0.85
  • The body weight (kg) used in the Cockcroft-Gault equation depends on the patient's BMI, based on a study designed to evaluate the impact of various body weights on the bias and accuracy of the equation compared with measured 24-hour creatinine clearance (Pharmacotherapy 2012;32:604):
    • Underweight patients (BMI <18.5 kg/m2): use actual body weight
    • Normal weight patients (BMI 18.5-24.9 kg/m2): use IBW
    • Overweight patients (BMI 25-29.9 kg/m2): use adjusted BW
    • Obese patients, all three classes of obesity (BMI ≥30 kg/m2): use adjusted BW

Glomerular Filtration Rate-based Calculations

GFR (Pediatric): Bedside Schwartz Equation

  • Equation for use in pediatrics (age 1-18 yrs): estimated GFR (mL/min/1.73 m2 = (0.413) x (height/SCr)
    • Height in centimeters
    • SCr is serum creatinine in mg/dL. For our users who use serum creatinine in µmol/L, the conversion factor is 88.4. In other words, to convert mg/dL to µmol/L, multiply the mg/dL value by 88.4.
  • This equation (known as the Bedside Schwartz equation) is currently the best formula for estimating glomerular filtration rate (GFR) from serum creatinine in children. It is intended for use with creatinine methods with calibration traceable to IDMS (isotope dilution mass spectrometry). All laboratories should be using creatinine methods calibrated to be IDMS traceable.
  • Ref: J Am Soc Nephrol 2009;20:629

GFR (Adult): 2021 CKD-EPI Creatinine Equation

  • Chronic Kidney Disease Epidemiology Collaboration (CKD-EPI) equation for estimation of GFR in adults using serum creatinine, age, and sex. Recommended by the National Kidney Foundation (NKF) for estimation of GFR.
  • Originally described in 2009 (Ann Intern Med 2009;150:604-12). Updated by the NKF and the American Society of Nephrology (ASN) in 2021 to estimate GFR without use of race.
  • Equation: eGFR = 142 x min(Scr/κ, 1)α x max(Scr/κ, 1)-1.200 x 0.9938Age x 1.012 [if female]
    • Scr = standardized serum creatinine in mg/dL
    • κ = 0.7 (females) or 0.9 (males)
    • α = -0.241 (female) or -0.302 (male)
    • min(Scr/κ, 1) is the minimum of Scr/κ or 1.0
    • max(Scr/κ, 1) is the maximum of Scr/κ or 1.0
    • Age is in years

GFR (Adult): 2021 CKD-EPI Creatinine-Cystatin C Equation

  • Chronic Kidney Disease Epidemiology Collaboration (CKD-EPI) equation for estimation of GFR in adults using serum creatinine, cystatin C, age, and sex.
  • Cystatin C is produced by all nucleated cells. It is a basic protein freely filtered by the glomerulus and reabsorbed and metabolized by tubular cells, with only small amounts excreted in the urine. Generated at a relatively constant rate, regardless of muscle mass.
  • Equation: eGFR = 135 x min(Scr/κ, 1)α x max(Scr/κ, 1)-0.544 x min(Scys/0.8, 1)-0.323 x max(Scys/0.8, 1)-0.778 x 0.9961Age x 0.963 [if female]
    • Scr = standardized serum creatinine in mg/dL
    • Scys = standardized cystatin C in mg/L
    • κ = 0.7 (females) or 0.9 (males)
    • α = -0.219 (female) or -0.144 (male)
    • min(Scr/κ, 1) is the minimum of Scr/κ or 1.0
    • max(Scr/κ, 1) is the maximum of Scr/κ or 1.0
    • min(Scys/0.8, 1) is the minimum of Scys/0.8 or 1.0
    • max(Scys/0.8, 1) is the maximum of Scys/0.8 or 1.0
    • Age is in years
