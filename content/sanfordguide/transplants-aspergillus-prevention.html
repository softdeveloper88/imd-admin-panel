Transplants, Aspergillus Prevention

Clinical Setting

  • Acute myelogenous leukemia (AML) and myelodysplastic syndrome (MDS) with neutropenia (N Engl J Med 356:348, 2007; N Engl J Med 356:335, 2007).
  • Allogeneic hematopoietic cell transplant (HCT) recipient with graft versus host disease (GVHD).
  • Lung or heart/lung transplant recipient.

Etiologies

  • Aspergillus fumigatus most common, other species can cause disease

Primary Regimens

  • AML and MDS with neutropenia or Allogeneic HCT wit GVHD  
    • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs)
      • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily
      • Suspension 200 mg qid, then 400 mg po bid after stabilization of disease
      • Posaconazole IV 300 mg IV over 90 minutes bid x 1 day, then 300 mg IV daily
  • Lung or heart/lung transplant
    • Aerosolized Amphotericin B 6 mg q8 (or 25 mg/day)
    • Aerosolized liposomal Amphotericin B 25 mg/day
    • Voriconazole 200 mg po bid
    • Posaconazole (see dosing above)
    • Itraconazole 200 mg po bid
    • Inhaled Amphotericin B product and/or a mold active oral azole are commonly used, but optimal regimen not defined (Clin Transplant, 33:e13544, 2019)

Alternative Regimens

  • Patients with AML and MDS with neutropenia and recipients of allogeneic HCT with GVHD
    • Voriconazole 200 mg bid
    • Itraconazole 200 mg bid
    • Inhaled Amphotericin B 12.5 mg on 2 consecutive days/week
    • Caspofungin 70mg IV x 1 followed by 50 mg IV daily (Micafungin and Anidulafungin likely similarly effective) is more  effective than fluconazole

Comments

  • Patients with AML and MDS with neutropenia and recipients of allogeneic HCT with GVHD
    • Voriconazole showed trend to reduced invasive fungal infection vs. Fluconazole in patients undergoing HCT in the pre-engraftment period (Blood 116:5111, 2010).
    • Itraconazole was more effective than Fluconazole to prevent invasive fungal infection in HCT (Ann Intern Med 138:705, 2003) but less effective than Posaconazole (N Engl J Med 356:335, 2007).
    • Inhaled Amphotericin B reduces the rates of invasive aspergillosis in HCT recipients (Clin Infect Dis 46:1401, 2008).  
    • Caspofungin vs. Fluconazole prophylaxis was associated with a significant reduction in invasive fungal infections in neutropenic HCT recipients (JAMA 322:1673, 2019). Micafungin and Anidulafungin likely have similar activity.
    • A detailed discussion on prevention of fungal infections in cancer patients can be found in the National Comprehensive Cancer Network Guidelines on Prevention and Treatment of Cancer-Related Infections, 2018
  • Recipients of a lung or heart/lung transplant
    • Universal prophylaxis with Voriconazole 200 mg bid more effective than targeted prophylaxis with Itraconazole 200 mg bid plus inhaled Amphotericin B (Am J Transplant 6:3008, 2006) at preventing aspergillus infection.
    • Rate of invasive fungal infections with Itraconazole 200 mg bid x 3 months slightly higher than with Voriconazole 200 mg bid x 3 months/inhaled Amphotericin B 10 mg bid x 2 wks (4/32 vs. 1/35) (Am J Transplant 9:2085, 2009).
    • Most centers (58.6%) employ universal prophylaxis for 6 months in lung transplant recipients with 97.1% targeting Aspergillus sp: Voriconazole alone or in combination with inhaled Amphotericin B preferred first-line agents (Am J Transplant 11:361, 2011).
      • Evolving appreciation of various risks of prolonged voriconazole use to include squamous cell carcinoma (Am J Transplant 18:5, 2018)
    • Reference for antifungal prophylaxis in lung transplant recipients: Ann Pharmacother 44:546, 2010.
  • In any of these groups, consider secondary prophylaxis during periods of increased risk (repeat episodes of neutropenia, intensified immune suppression).
  • IDSA Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 63:433, 2016.
