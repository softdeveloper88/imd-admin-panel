Pyronaridine-artesunate
Tradename: Pyramax

Usage and Dosing

  • Pyronaridine-artesunate (Pyramax) is a WHO-recommended fixed-dose artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT) for treatment of uncomplicated P. falciparum and P. vivax malaria. One tablet contains 180 mg pyronaridine tetraphosphate and 60 mg artesunate.
  • Pyronaridine is a benzonaphthyridine derivative.
  • Artesunate is a artemisinin that is metabolized to its active metabolite dihydroartemisinin (DHA).
  • Pyronaridine tetraphosphate 180 = 100 mg free base. Dosage is expressed as the salt form.
  • Pyronaridine monotherapy is available in China as Malaridine (enteric-coated tablets containing 100 mg free base, 80 mg free base/2 mL for IM injection).
  • Large differences in PK between healthy subjects and malaria-infected patients, suggesting substantial disease-associated differences.
  • Review: J Antimicrob Chemother 2023;78:2406-2418.

Adult Dose

  • Tablet dosing (with or without food):
    • Wt 20 to <24 kg: 1 tablet daily x3 days
    • Wt 24 to <45 kg: 2 tablets daily x3 days
    • Wt 45 to <65 kg: 3 tablets daily x3 days
    • Wt ≥65 kg: 4 tablets daily x3 days

Adverse Effects

  • Headache
  • Hematologic (neutropenia, eosinophilia, anemia)
  • Abdominal pain, vomiting
  • Bradycardia
  • Increased transaminases
  • Hypoglycemia
