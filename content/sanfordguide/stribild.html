Stribild
Tradename: Stribild: Elvitegravir + Cobicistat + Emtricitabine + Tenofovir disproxil fumarate

Usage and Dosing

  • Strand transfer, integrase inhibitor (INSTI) combination.
  • Stribild is a once-daily 4-drug co-formulation of Elvitegravir (EVG), Cobicistat (Cobi), Emtricitabine (FTC) and Tenofovir disoproxil fumarate (TDF)
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive),
  • Not recommended for use in pregnancy due to substantially lowered exposures of elvitegravir and cobicistat. Treatment should not be initiated in pregnant women, and an alternative regimen should be used in women who become pregnant on Stribild therapy; continuation with frequent viral load monitoring can be considered.

Adult Dose

  • 1 tab once daily with food
  • Tablet: EVG 150 mg + Cobi 150 mg + FTC 200 mg + TDF 300 mg

      Adverse Effects

      • In clinical trials: overall, 3.7% of patients discontinued therapy due to an adverse reaction.
      • Adverse reactions reported in ≥ 5% of clinical trial participants (all severities):
        • Nausea 16%
        • Diarrhea 12%
        • Headache 7%
        • Abnormal dreams 9%
        • Rash 3%

      FDA Risk Category 1 Not recommended (↓EVG/c exposure)

      Comments

      • Do not use if impaired renal function.
      • Cobicistat inhibits proximal tubule enzyme (MATE 1) that actively secretes creatinine into urine.  Use of cobicistat is associated with a 0.1 to 0.15 mg/dl increase in serum creatinine values without any change in actual GFR; however estimated GFR (as determined by Cockcroft Gault estimation, for example) will artificially indicate a reduction in GFR.
