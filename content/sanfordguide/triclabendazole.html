Triclabendazole
Tradename: Egaten

Usage and Dosing

  • FDA approved in early 2019 (Trans R Soc Trop Med Hyg. 12:797, 2019)
    • Available and shipped for free from Novartis (+1 888-669-6682) to any pharmacy having a valid prescription on hand.  Novartis will request patient name, DOB, and pharmacy address/phone.  
    • Also available from Victoria Pharmacy, Zurich, Switzerland: (+41) 43-344-6060.
  • Drug failures in F. hepatica in Peru with multiple doses (Emerg Infect Dis 2021 Jul;27(7):1850)
    • Repeated course may be of benefit.

Adult Dose

  • If dosage cannot be adjusted exactly, round dose upwards.
  • Take with food. Tablets can be swallowed whole, or divided in half and taken with water or crushed and administered with applesauce.
  • A crushed tablet mixed with applesauce is stable for up to 4 hours.

Adverse Effects

  • Headache and dizziness are common complaints.
  • Epigastric pain, biliary colic, nausea and vomiting. Believed due to expulsion of dead and dying worms from the biliary tree.
  • QT prolongation observed in dogs. Prudent to monitor ECG in patients with a history of QT prolongation or who are taking other drugs known to prolong the QT interval.
  • PLoS Negl Trop Dis 6(8):e1720, 2012

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • Benzimidazole antihelmintic for preferred therapy of Fasciola hepatica and Fasciola gigantica
  • Alternative therapy for Paragonimus species lung fluke infections.
