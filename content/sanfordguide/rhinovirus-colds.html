Rhinovirus, Colds

Clinical Setting

  • Common cold
  • Found in 1/2 of children with community-acquired pneumonia; role in pathogenesis unclear (Clin Infect Dis 39:681, 2004).
  • High rate of rhinovirus identified in children with significant lower respiratory tract infections (Pediatr Infect Dis J 28:337, 2009).
  • See Lancet 361:51, 2003.

Etiologies

  • Rhinoviruses

Primary Regimens

  • No antiviral therapy indicated (Pediatr Ann 34:53, 2005).
  • Symptomatic treatment:
    • Ipratropium bromide nasal spray (2 sprays per nostril tid)
    • Clemastine 1.34 mg 1–2 tab po bid–tid (OTC)
    • Intranasal zinc preparations should not be used owing to the association with permanent anosmia 
    • Is there evidence of clinically relevant treatment effects for pharmacologic or non-pharmacologic therapies in reducing the duration/severity of cough associated with the common cold ?  See systematic review of randomized controlled trials: Chest 2017;152:1021. Short answer, no strong evidence for any of commonly employed therapies.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Symptomatic relief: ipratropium nasal spray reduces rhinorrhea and sneezing vs placebo (Ann Intern Med 125:89, 1996).
  • Clemastine (an antihistamine) reduces sneezing, rhinorrhea but associated with dry nose, mouth and throat in 6–19% (Clin Infect Dis 22:656, 1996).
  • Public health advisory advising that three over-the-counter cold remedy products containing zinc (e.g., Zicam) should not be used because of multiple reports of permanent anosmia.
  • Echinacea does not work (Clin Infect Dis 38:1367, 2004 & Clin Infect Dis 40:807, 2005).
  • Routine use of masks during COVID-19 epidemic led to marked reduction in incidence of influenza, RSV and the Common Cold
