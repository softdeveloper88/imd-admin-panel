Bone, M. tuberculosis

Clinical Setting

  • Tuberculosis of the spine (i.e., Pott's disease, tuberculous spondylitis) is the most common type of skeletal tuberculosis, lower thoracic vertebra more common than lumbar.
  • Other locations include sternum, symphysis pubis, sacroiliac joint, and much less frequently other bones.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis

Primary Regimens

  • Same as for tuberculous arthritis.
  • Duration of therapy not well defined but 9-12 months is generally recommended, particularly for advanced disease.

Alternative Regimens

  • Not applicable.

Comments

  • Most patients respond to medical therapy; consider surgery for relief of cord compression, persistent or worsening neurological deficits, for treatment of spine instability, drainage of large abscesses, extensive bony destruction with devitalized bone.
  • Reference:  Infect Dis Clin North Am 2017; 31:369.
