Aspergilloma

Clinical Setting

  • Aspergilloma (or fungus ball): presence of a solid rounded mass, within a cavity.
  • Local pleural thickening is often present.
  • Positive aspergillus antibodies present in serum.
  • Hemoptysis.

Etiologies

  • Aspergillus sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Surgical resection (optimal candidates are patients with single aspergilloma)
  • No therapy if asymptomatic, stable, single lesion, but monitor for progression

Comments

  • If there is a concern for invasive disease please see section on Invasive Aspergillosis
  • Transthoracic, intracavitary instillation of Amphotericin B has been used in poor surgical candidates (Radiology 188:821, 1993)
  • Goal of therapy is prevention or treatment of life-threatening hemoptysis; bronchial artery embolization an option for patients with hemoptysis and who are poor surgical candidates (Thorac Surg Clin 22:345, 2012).
  • Role of medical therapy is uncertain.
  • See IDSA guidelines for further information: (Clin Infect Dis 63:433, 2016)
