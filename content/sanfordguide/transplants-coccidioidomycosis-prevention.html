Coccidioidomycosis, Prophylaxis

Clinical Setting

  • Prevention of progressive coccidioidomycosis in previously infected solid organ transplant recipients. Most often identified by serology pre-transplant.
  • Prevention of progressive coccidioidomycosis in recipients of organs from infected organ donors.
  • Prevention of primary coccidioidomycosis in recipients living in endemic regions

Etiologies

  • Coccidioides immitis

Primary Regimens

  • Recipient with positive serology, no active infection at time of transplant:
    • Fluconazole 400 mg once daily x 1 year; then 200 mg once daily indefinitely
  • Recipient of organ from donor with positive serology, no active infection:
    • Lung transplant recipients: Fluconazole 400 mg once daily, indefinitely
    • Other organ recipients: Fluconazole 400 mg once daily x 1 year; then 200 mg once daily indefinitely
  • Recipient residing in an endemic area who underwent an organ transplant (primary prevention)
    • Fluconazole 200 mg once daily x 6-12 months post transplantation (Clin Infect Dis 63:e112, 2016)

Alternative Regimens

  • Itraconazole 200 mg po bid

Comments

  • Azoles (Itraconazole > Fluconazole) are CYP450 inhibitors and interact with tacrolimus and cyclosporine.
  • Recommendations of the American Society of Transplantation (AST) Guidelines on Endemic Fungal Infections in Solid Organ Transplant Recipients: Clin Transplant 33:e13353, 2019
  • IDSA Guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 63:e112, 2016
  • For additional Guidelines, see Transplant Infection Prevention, Overview.
