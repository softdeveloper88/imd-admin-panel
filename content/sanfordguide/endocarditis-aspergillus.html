Endocarditis, Aspergillus

Clinical Setting

  • Culture-negative endocarditis, aortic or mitral valve typically involved.
  • Large vegetations on echocardiogram, systemic embolization predominant feature.
  • Prior cardiac surgery, immunosuppression are risk factors.

Diagnosis

  • Blood cultures usually negative, galactomannan and other serological tests insensitive.
  • Diagnosis usually established by culture of embolus, valve tissue, or by PCR.

Etiologies

  • Aspergillus sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Valve replacement surgery critical
  • Voriconazole 6 mg/kg IV/po q12h on day 1; then 4 mg/kg IV/po q12h,

Alternative Regimens

  • Liposomal amphotercin B 3-5 mg/kg/day
  • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Consider suppressive lifelong antifungal therapy following surgery

Comments

  • High mortality (~70%) even with surgery, approaching 100% without surgery.
  • IDSA treatment guidelines: Clin Infect Dis 63:433, 2016.
