Endocarditis, Bartonella sp.

Clinical Setting

  • Bartonella is the second most common cause of culture-negative endocarditis
    • Bartonella quintana endocarditis is associated with alcoholism, homelessness and body lice.
    • Bartonella henselae is associated with cats and valvular disease.

Diagnosis

  • Methods (see J Clin Micro 2021.59: e02217-20 for advantages and limitations of these methods):
    • Positive serology
      • Recommend EIA
      • Titer of ≥  1:800 good correlation with infective endocarditis
        • NOTE: low titer cross-reactivity with Coxiella burnetii,  Brucella spp, and rarely Enterococcus faecalis
    • Bartonella-specific RT-PCR of resected heart valve
    • Valve tissue histopathology
    • Culture for Bartonella sp.(Rarely positive)

Primary Regimens

  • Due to risk of relapse, treatment of bartonella endocarditis is prolonged. Suggest repeat antibody titers every 6-8 weeks as guide to duration of therapy.
  • Specific therapy:
    • Doxycycline 100 mg IV/po bid x 6 weeks + Gentamicin 1 mg/kg IV q8h x first 2 weeks
      • Gentamicin target serum levels: peak 3-4 ug/mL and trough <1 ug/mL
      • Duration of Doxycycline therapy depends on valve resection surgery:
        • If infected valve is resected, continue Doxycycline for another 6 wks
        • If no valve surgery, continue Doxycycline for another 3 months
      • If cannot use Doxycycline, substitute, Azithromycin 500 mg IV/po once daily and combine with Gentamicin for 14 days
        • If infected valve is surgically resected, continue Azithromycin for another 3 months
        • If infected valve is not resected, continue Azithromycin for another 6 months

Alternative Regimens

  • If Gentamicin toxicity precludes its use, consultation is suggested:
    • Doxycycline as above + Rifampin 300 mg IV/po bid for first 14 days

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Resistance of Bartonella to Doxycyline has not been documented; hence treatment failures likely indicate relapse, not resistance.
  • TMP-SMX, fluoroquinolones, 1st generation cephalosporins and penicillins are not predictably active against Bartonella quintana and should not be used.
