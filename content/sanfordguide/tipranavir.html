Tipranavir
Tradename: Aptivus

Usage and Dosing

  • Tipranavir (TPV) is indicated for use as part of a 3-drug combination regimen; other agents selected based on results of resistance drug testing. Must be used with Ritonavir-boosting.  Not used in treatment naive patients; used in treatment experienced patients when the resistance test indicates susceptibility to Tipranavir.
  • Rarely used; Consult with HIV resistance expert prior to selection of this agent 
  • Tipranavir contains sulfa moiety.
  • Black Box warning—reports of fatal/nonfatal intracranial hemorrhage, hepatitis, fatal hepatic failure. Use cautiously in liver disease, especially hep B, hep C; contraindicated in Child-Pugh class B-C. Monitor LFTs.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see chart below, not all-inclusive).
  • Tipranavir oral solution contains 116 IU vitamin E per mL. Patients taking the oral solution should avoid taking any form of supplemental vitamin E that contains more vitamin E than found in a standard multivitamin.
  • For highly ART-experienced patients or for multiple-PI resistant virus.
  • Do not use Tipranavir and Etravirine together owing to 76% reduction in Etravirine levels.

Adult Dose

Usual Dose500 mg (two 250 mg caps) + Ritonavir 200 mg po bid with food.

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days) Age <2 yrs: Not approved for use
Age ≥2 to 18 yrs (not recommended if tx-naive):
By BSA: 375 mg/m² (+ RTV 150 mg/m²) q12h
By wt: 14 mg/kg (+ RTV 6 mg/kg) q12h
Max/Day 500 mg (+ RTV 200 mg) q12h

Mild impairment (Child-Pugh Class A) No dosage adjustment

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Black Box Warning—associated with hepatitis & fatal hepatic failure. Risk of hepatotoxicity increased in hep B or hep C co-infection.
    • Associated with fatal/nonfatal intracranial hemorrhage (can inhibit platelet aggregation). Caution in those with bleeding risks.
    • Potential for major drug interactions.
    • Contains sulfa moiety and vitamin E.
  • Most Common:
    • Nausea & vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal pain.
    • Rash in 8-14%, more common in women, & 33% in women taking ethinyl estradiol.
    • Major lipid effects.

Antimicrobial Spectrum

  • HIV
