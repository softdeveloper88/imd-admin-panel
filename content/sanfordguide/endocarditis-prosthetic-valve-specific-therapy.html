Endocarditis, Coagulase-Negative Staphylococci

Clinical Setting

  • Specific therapy for coagulase-negative Staphylococcal endocarditis.
  • Prosthetic valve.
  • Signs and symptoms of endocarditis. Positive blood cultures for Staph. epidermidis, other coagulase-negative species of staphylococci.

Etiologies

  • Staphylococcus epidermidis
  • Coagulase-negative staphylococci

Primary Regimens

  • MRSE: Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL) + Rifampin 600-900 mg/day po/IV (see Antimicrobial Stewardship) in 2-3 divided doses x 6 weeks + Gentamicin 1 mg/kg IV q8h x 2 weeks
  • MSSE: Nafcillin or Oxacillin 2 gm IV q4h can be substituted for Vancomycin

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Wait 3-5 days after starting vancomycin/gentamicin before starting rifampin, to decrease bacterial density and thus minimize risk of selecting for rifampin resistance. 

Comments

  • Monitor renal function carefully on Vancomycin/Gentamicin-containing regimens.
  • AHA endocarditis treatment guidelines: Circulation 132: 1435, 2015.
