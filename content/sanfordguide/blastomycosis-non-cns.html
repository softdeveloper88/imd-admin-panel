Blastomycosis, Non-CNS

Clinical Setting

  • Blastomycosis: cutaneous, pulmonary or extrapulmonary disease
  • For CNS disease, see Blastomycosis, Meningitis.

Diagnosis

  • Definitive diagnosis via positive culture
  • GMS staining of tissue can detect characteristic broad-based yeast
  • Beware of false positive histoplasma antigen test. The histoplasma antigen cross-reacts with antigens of blastomycosis, coccidioides ,Penicillium, and sporothrix

Etiologies

  • Blastomyces dermatitidis

Primary Regimens

  • Moderately severe/severe:
    • Itraconazole oral solution 2.5 mg/kg po q8h or 200 mg capsules po q8h x 3 days, then once or twice/day x 6-12 months
    • Liposomal Amphotericin B 3-5 mg/kg/day (preferred) or Amphotericin B 0.7 -1 mg/kg/day x 1-2 weeks or until clinical response, then
  • Mild/moderate disease:
    • Itraconazole oral solution 2.5 mg/kg po q8h or 200 mg capsules po q8h x 3 days, then once or twice/day x 6-12 months

Alternative Regimens

  • Posaconazole (NOTE: different dosing delayed release tabs/IV vs. suspension, better levels achieved with delayed-release tabs)
    • Delayed-release tabs 300 mg po bid x 2 doses and then 300 mg po daily
    • Suspension 200 mg qid, then 400 mg po bid after stabilization of disease
    • Posaconazole IV 300 mg IV over 90 minutes bid x 1 day, then 300 mg IV daily
  • Voriconazole 4 mg/kg po bid
  • Isavuconazonium sulfate (prodrug of Isavuconazole) 372 mg po/IV q8h x 6 doses and then 372 mg po/IV daily
  • Fluconazole 400-800 mg/day po

Comments

  • Serum levels of Itraconazole should be determined after 2 weeks to ensure adequate drug exposure (goal serum level >1.0 µg/mL).
  • Fluconazole less effective than Itraconazole.
  • Voriconazole and Posaconazole are active in vitro, clinical experience limited.
  • Chronic suppressive therapy may be indicated with Itraconazole 200 mg/day in patients whose immunosuppression cannot be reversed and in those who have experienced relapse.
  • See IDSA treatment guidelines for more information: Clin Infect Dis 46:1801, 2008.
  • Cutaneous disease (New Engl J Med 2022 May 5;386(18):e49)
