Daclatasvir
Tradename: Daklinza

Usage and Dosing

  • NOTE: THIS PRODUCT WAS DISCONTINUED IN 2019 AND IS NO LONGER AVAILABLE.
  • Daclatasvir is a NS5A inhibitor used in combination with Sofosbuvir for treatment of Hepatitis C virus (HCV), genotype 3 infection.
  • Sustained virologic response (SVR) is reduced in patients with cirrhosis
  • Test for evidence of current or prior Hepatitis B (HBV) infection: measure HBsAG and anti-HBC before initiating HCV treatment. If HBV co-infection, monitor patient for HBV reactivation during and post-HCV therapy.
  • Caution: numerous drug interactions (see below)

Adult Dose

  • 60 mg po once daily with or without food x 12 weeks + Sofosbuvir
  • Dose adjustments:
    •  Strong CYP3A inhibitors: 30 mg po once daily
    • Moderate CYP3A inducers: 90 mg po once daily

Adverse Effects

  • Most common: headache and fatigue
  • Black Box Warning: Cases of HBV reactivation, occasionally fulminant, during or after DAA therapy have been reported in HBV/HCV coninfected patients who were not already on HBV suppressive therapy
