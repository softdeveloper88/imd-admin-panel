DDx: Pneumonia, HIV

Clinical Syndrome

  • HIV positive patient with CD4 count less than 200, but greater than 100 cells/mm3 presenting with pneumonia.

Differential Diagnosis

  • There are many possibilities, but 3 etiologies stand out.

Pneumocystis Pneumonia (PJP)

  • Clinical Presentation
    • Gradual onset of dypsnea and hypoxemia
    • Elevated LDH
  • Diagnosis
    • Sputum PCR is best
  • Therapy
    • Treatment options for PCP pneumonia
  • Prevention
    • Post-treatment suppression with daily TMP-SMX + anti-retroviral therapy ( ART)

Mycobacterium Tuberculosis

  • Clinical Presentation
    • Weeks of fever, cough, night sweats
    • Weight loss
  • Diagnosis
    • Sputum AFB smear and culture (x 3)
  • Therapy
    • Treatment options for Mycobacterium tuberculosis
  • Prevention
    • Antiretroviral therapy

Bacterial (Pneumococcal) Pneumonia

  • Clinical Presentation
    • Acute onset of fever with cough productive of purulent sputum
    • Lobar infiltrate(s)
  • Diagnosis
    • Sputum and urine Ag; Where available: PCR on airway secretions or nasopharyngeal swab
  • Therapy
    • Treatment options for bacterial pneumonia
  • Prevention
    • ART
    • Pneumococcal vaccine
