Ritonavir
Tradename: Norvir

Usage and Dosing

  • Ritonavir (RTV) full dose is not recommended. With rare exceptions, Ritonavir is used exclusively to boost or enhance the pharmacokinetics of other protease inhibitors, using lower Ritonavir doses. Used to increase bioavailability of nirmatrelvir as part of Paxlovid therapy for COVID-19 (Note:  No dose adjustment is needed for those on ritonavir-based or cobicistat-based antiretroviral therapy)
  • Nausea/vomiting/diarrhea, extremity & circumoral paresthesias, hepatitis, pancreatitis, taste perversion, ↑ CPK & uric acid.
  • Black Box warning— potentially fatal drug interactions. See ARV Drug-Drug Interactions.
  • Available as tablets, an oral solution (tastes terrible), and oral powder (usable only in 100 mg increments).
  • The oral powder is preferred over the oral solution in children (who cannot swallow tablets) because the oral solution contains 43% (v/v) ethanol and about 27% (w/v) propylene glycol.
  • See Lopinavir/ritonavir, Saquinavir.

Adverse Effects

  • Most Significant:
    • Hepatic failure (Ann Intern Med 129:670, 1998). Black Box warning relates to many important drug-drug interactions—inhibits P450 CYP3A & CYP2D6 system—may be life-threatening.
    • Rarely Stevens-Johnson syndrome, anaphylaxis. Primary A-V block (and higher) and pancreatitis have been reported.
  • Most Common:
    • GI: bitter aftertaste ↓ by taking with chocolate milk, Ensure, or Advera; nausea 23%, ↓ by initial dose esc (titration) regimen; vomiting 13%; diarrhea 15%.
    • Circumoral paresthesias 5–6%. ↑ dose >100 mg bid assoc. with ↑ GI side effects & ↑ in lipid abnormalities.
