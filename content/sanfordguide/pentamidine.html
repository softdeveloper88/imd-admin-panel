Pentamidine
Tradename: NebuPent, Pentam 300

Usage and Dosing

  • Pentamidine is available as an isethionate salt that can be administered IV or IM or reconstituted and given as an aerosol (NebuPent).
  • Alternative therapy to Fexinidazole for early West or East African trypanosomiasis.
  • An alternative drug for the prophylaxis and treatment of Pneumocystis pnemonia (PJP). 
  • Part of combination therapy of meningoencephalitis due to Acanthamoeba (FEMS Immunol Med Micro 50:1, 2007) and Balamuthia (Clin Infect Dis 51:e7, 2010).

Adult Dose

T. b. gambiense, T.b. rhodesiense4 mg/kg IV or IM once daily x 7-10 days
For PJP treatment4 mg/kg IV once daily x 21 days
For PJP prophylaxis300 mg in 6 mL sterile water by aerosol q4 weeks

 

Pediatric Dose

Dose (Age >28 Days)

PJP treatment: 4 mg/kg/day IM/IV (div q24h)
PJP prophylaxis: 300 mg by aerosol q-month

T. b. gambiense, T.b. rhodesiense: 4 mg/kg IV or IM once daily x 7-10 days

Adverse Effects

  • Electrolyte abnormalities: ↓ Na+, ↑ K+, ↓ Mg++, and ↓ Ca++ concentrations.
  • Aerosolized Pentamidine causes coughing/bronchospasm in 30-40% of patients (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 60:2502, 2016)
  • IM injections can cause sterile abscesses.
  • Hypotension with rapid IV administration.
  • Neutropenia (15%), thrombocytopenia.
  • Nephrotoxicity in up to 1/3 of patients.
  • Others: nausea/vomiting, ↑ liver tests, rash.
  • Potential to prolong QTc, see concomitant drugs with potential to prolong QTc.
  • Toxic to pancreatic islet-beta cells → increased insulin release → hypoglycemia (15-25%).
  • Severe pancreatitis can occur.
  • Accumulates in liver, spleen, adrenals and kidneys.

Pregnancy Risk

FDA Risk Category 1 Limited data. Use only if the potential benefit justifies the potential risk to the fetus. WHO: May be administered to humans after the first trimester. 3
Use during Lactation Breast milk excretion unknown. WHO: Continue breastfeeding during treatment for HAT. 3

  • Acanthamoeba species
  • Balamuthia mandrillaris
