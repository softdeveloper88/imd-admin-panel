Inmazeb
Tradename: Inmazeb

Usage and Dosing

  • Inmazeb is a combination of three monoclonal antibodies (atoltivimab, maftivimab, and odesivimab-ebgn in a 1:1:1 ratio), approved by the US FDA for the treatment of infection caused by Zaire ebolavirus in adult and pediatric patients, including neonates born to a mother who is RT-PCR positive for Zaire ebolavirus infection.

Adult Dose

  • Dose: 50 mg/kg atoltivimab, 50 mg/kg maftivimab, and 50 mg/kg odesivimab IV as a single infusion.
  • Two different strength presentations: single-dose vials containing 16.67 mg or 33.33 mg of each antibody per mL.
  • Compatible with NS, D5W, or Lactated Ringers (LR); infuse through an IV line with a 0.2-micron filter.
  • Stability:
    • NS: 8 hours at 25ºC, 24 hours at 2-8ºC
    • D5W, LR: 4 hours at 25ºC, 4 hours at 2-8ºC
  • Infusion volume and time according to body weight:

      Adverse Effects

      • Hypersensitivity reactions, including infusion-related adverse events (e.g. hypotension, chills, fever).

      FDA Risk Category 1 Humans, animals: no safety data
