Otitis Media, Post-Intubation

Clinical Setting

  • Prevalence of true infection poorly defined.
  • Otitis media that develops after > 48 hours of nasotracheal intubation.

Etiologies

  • Pseudomonas sp.
  • Klebsiella sp.
  • Enterobacter sp.

Primary Regimens

  • Ceftazidime 2 gm IV q8h or Cefepime 2 gm q8h
  • Imipenem 0.5 gm IV q6h or Meropenem 1 gm IV q8h
  • Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q8h
  • Ciprofloxacin 400 mg IV q12h

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Duration of therapy not well defined.

Comments

  • With intact tympanic membrane, pathogen may not be available for susceptibility testing; however, if a suspected pathogen is recovered, determine susceptibility to help inform therapy.
  • Middle ear effusions are common in ICU patients and related to intubation and nasogastric tube placement.
