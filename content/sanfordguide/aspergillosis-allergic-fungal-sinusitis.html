Aspergillosis, Allergic Fungal Sinusitis

Clinical Setting

  • Relapsing chronic sinusitis; nasal polyps without bony invasion; asthma, eczema or allergic rhinitis.
  • Increased IgE levels and isolation of Aspergillus sp. or other dematiaceous sp. (Alternaria, Cladosporium, etc.).
  • Treatment is controversial.

Etiologies

  • Aspergillus fumigatus
  • Aspergillus flavus & others

Primary Regimens

  • No antifungal therapy OR
  • Itraconazole oral solution 2.5 mg/kg po q12h or 200 mg capsules po q12h

Alternative Regimens

  • Fluconazole nasal spray

Comments

  • Itraconazole may be steroid sparing.
  • Endoscopic surgical sinus drainage for sinus obstruction.
  • For more information see: Curr Infect Dis Rep 12:217, 2010 and IDSA Guidelines (Clin Infect Dis 63:433, 2016)
