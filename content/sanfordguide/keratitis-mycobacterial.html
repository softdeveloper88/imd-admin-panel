Keratitis, Mycobacterial, Non-tuberculous

Clinical Setting

  • Non-tuberculous mycobacterial infection of the cornea
  • Complication of Lasik surgery and may occur after trauma.

Etiologies

  • Mycobacterium chelonae
  • Mycobacterium abscessus
  • Mycobacterium massiliense

Primary Regimens

  • Optimal regimen and duration of therapy not well defined.
  • Ophthalmologic consultation strongly recommended.
  • Gatifloxacin or Moxifloxacin eye drops: 1 gtt qid in conjunction with two other active antimicrobial eyedrops, Amikacin 50 mg/L and Clarithromycin 10 mg/L.
  • Consider use of systemic therapy in recalcitrant cases: Doxycycline 100 mg bid + Clarithromycin 500 mg bid or other agents based on species and susceptibility (For details see Mycobacterium chelonae, M. abscessus).

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • Prolonged course of therapy, weeks to months.
  • Surgical intervention often required.
  • References: Clin Microbiol Infect 19:221, 2013 and PLoS One 10:e0116236, 2015.
