HCV Epidemiology

Caseload

  • 58 million cases of Hepatitis C virus (HCV) worldwide (Lancet GI and Hep online, Feb 2023).
    • 1.5 million new cases per year
    • The highest burden of disease:
      • Eastern Mediterranean Region and European Region  (12 million people chronically infected in each region).
      • South-East Asia Region and the Western Pacific Region (10 million people  chronically infected in each region)
      • African Region (9 million people are chronically infected)
      • Region of the Americas (5 million chronically infected)
    • 290,000 die each year from complications of HCV
  • 4.1 million cases in the US (1.6%). Prior to the recent opioid epidemic in the US, most patients infected prior to 1990 (Hepatology 49:1335, 2009; Hepatology 57:1333, 2013). However, since 2010 increasing prevalence of HCV among 20 - 30 year olds, especially caucasians (Ann Int Med 166:775, 2017) 

    The steady increase of new infections in younger people is attributed to the opioid epidemic and associated injection drug use.(Am J Pub Health 108:175, 2018)

  • Approximately 20,000 new infections/year in the US.
  • Most HCV infected persons do not know their status.

Transmission Risk

  • Transmission risk profile depends on time of infection (remote vs. recent).

  • (N Engl J Med 341:556, 1999; MMWR 47:1, 1998; as above)
  • Intravenous drug users (IVDU) > Blood products recipients >> homosexual > heterosexual.
  • Sexual transmission.
    • Number of sexual partners (2.2-2.9 relative risk (RR)).
    • For persons with pre-existing sexually transmitted infection:
      • Herpes simplex virus (HSV) (3.85 RR)
      • Trichomonas (3.3 RR)
      • HIV (1.9-4.4 RR)
    • For men who have sex with men (MSM)
      • Significant increased risk for HCV transmission with specific activities (AIDS 21:983, 2007):
        • Receptive unprotected anal intercourse (UAI)
        • Insertive UAI
        • 'Rimming'
        • Insertive fisting
        • Receptive fisting
        • Use of sex toys
        • Group sex participation
  • Healthcare workers (see HCV Exposure Management for more specific details).
    • Primarily needle stick injury
    • Hepatitis B* (HBV) ≥ HCV >>> HIV.  * (If not vaccinated for HBV)
  • Vertical transmission.
