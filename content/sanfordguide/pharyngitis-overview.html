Pharyngeal Infections, Overview

Overview

  • Pharyngeal and peri-pharyngeal infections
  • Infections in the mouth, teeth, throat, tonsils, pharynx
  • Note: there are many associated syndromes and potential etiologies. The topics list is illustrative and serves as a guide, but it is not necessarily inclusive.
  • For a full listing of pharyngitis-associated pathogens see:  J of Infectious Diseases.2024;230 (S3):S173

Specific Topics

  • Adenovirus: Diffuse erythema of pharynx with conjunctivitis
  • Arcanobacterium haemolyticum: Diffuse erythema of pharynx with rash
  • Chlamydia trachomatis: Pharyngitis
  • Chlamydia pneumoniae: Associated atypical pneumonia
  • Epstein-Barr virus: Infectious mononucleosis, diffuse adenopathy)
  • Fusobacterium necrophorum: Lemierre's Syndrome, jugular vein phlebitis, septic, peri-tonsillar abscess and/or suppurative emboli to lungs
  • Herpes group of viruses:  CMV, EBV, and Herpes simplex
  • HIV: Acute or primary
  • Influenza and other respiratory viruses to include SARS-CoV -2
  • Odontogenic infection: Ludwig's Angina, Vincent's Angina
  • Parapharyngeal Space infection: Mouth
  • Pharyngitis, Diphtheria
  • Pharyngitis, Recurrent
  • Pharyngitis/Tonsillitis, Exudative or Diffuse Erythema
  • Pharyngitis, Vesicular or Viral
  • Pharyngitis with purulent cervical lymphadenopathy: Cat scratch disease, Tularemia
  • SARS-CoV-2, COVID-19
  • Sexually-transmitted pathogens: Chlamydia trachomatis, Neisseria gonorrhoeae, Treponema pallidum
  • Streptococcus pyogenes: Groups A, C, and G
