Protozoa, Intestinal, Non-pathogenic

Overview

  • The following organisms are non-pathogenic intestinal protozoa that should not treated but may be markers of exposure to fecal contamination and presence of other pathogenic organisms:
    • Entamoeba dispar
    • Entamoeba moshkovskii (emerging evidence of possible pathogenicity: PLoS Negl Trop Dis. 2023 May 11;17(5):e0011287)
    • Entamoeba coli
    • Entamoeba gingivalis (oral cavity only)
    • Entamoeba hartmanni
    • Entamoeba polecki (contact with pig or monkey feces)
    • Entamoeba bangladeshi
    • Iodamoeba buetschlii
    • Endolimax nana
    • Chilomastix mesnili (can be confused with Giardia sp.)
    • Enteromonas hominis
    • Retortomonas intestinalis
    • Pentatrichomonas hominis

Diagnostics

  • Commercial multiplex stool PCR may be needed to distinguish E. histolytica from non-pathogenic entamoebas.
  • E. dispar, E. bangladeshi, and E. moshkovskii cannot be morphologically distinguished from one another or from E. histolytica; stool antigen testing or PCR needed to distinguish
