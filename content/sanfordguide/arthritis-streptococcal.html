Arthritis, Septic, Streptococcal

Clinical Setting

  • Definitive therapy of septic arthritis caused by hemolytic and non-hemolytic streptococci
  • Signs and symptoms of septic arthritis.
  • Synovial fluid WBC >25,000.
  • Gram strain positive for Gram-positive cocci in chains and/or positive synovial fluid or blood culture for streptococci.

Etiologies

  • Group A, B, C streptococci
  • Streptococcus pneumoniae
  • Viridans group streptococci
  • Other streptococcal species

Primary Regimens

  • In addition to antimicrobial therapy, drainage or debridement should always be performed by needle aspiration (repeated as needed), arthroscopy, or arthrotomy.
  • Antimicrobial therapy:
    • Penicillin G 2 mU IV q4h x 2-3 weeks
    • Ceftriaxone 2 gm IV q24h x 2-3 weeks

Alternative Regimens

  • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 µg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough level of 15-20 µg/mL) x 2-3 weeks
  • Linezolid 600 mg bid x 2-3 weeks

Comments

  • Large series of streptococcal prosthetic joint infection managed with debridement, implant retention, and antibiotics found a failure rate of 42% (Clin Infect Dis 2017;64:1742).
    • Predictors of failure were rheumatoid arthritis, late post-surgical infection (> 3 months), bacteremia, infection with S. pyogenes.
    • Predictors of success were exchange of removable components, use of rifampin within the first 30 days,  a prolonged course (>21 days) of a beta-lactam antibiotic with or without Rifampin.
  • Role of oral step-down therapy is unclear; potentially effective agents include Linezolid 600 mg po bid, Clindamycin 600 mg po tid, or Amoxicillin 1 gm po tid.
