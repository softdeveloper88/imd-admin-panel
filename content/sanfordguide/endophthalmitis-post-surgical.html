Endophthalmitis, Post-surgical or Post-injection

Clinical Setting

  • Post-cataract surgery infection.
  • Haziness of vitreous humor is key to diagnosis.
  • Prior to therapy, culture needle aspirate of both vitreous and aqueous humor.
  • Presents as:
    • Early, acute onset
    • Low grade, chronic

Etiologies

  • Early, acute:
    • Staphylococcus epidermidis (60%)
    • Staphylococcus aureus (5-10%)
    • Streptococcus sp. (5-10%)
    • Enterococcus sp. (5-10%)
    • Gram-negative bacilli (6%)
    • Candida albicans
  • Low grade, chronic:
    • Cutibacterium acnes (formerly Propionibacterium acnes)
    • Staphylococcus epidermidis
    • Staphylococcus aureus (rare)
    • Fungal

Primary Regimens

  • Early, acute:
    • Immediate ophthalmologic consult is needed.
    • Intravitreal Vancomycin 1 mg + intravitreal Ceftazidime 2.25 mg or Amikacin 0.4 mg: may need to repeat intravitreal antibiotics in 2–3 days.
    • Can usually leave lens in. No clear data on intravitreal steroid.
    • If only light perception or worse, perform immediate vitrectomy
  • Low grade, chronic:
    • May require removal of lens material.
    • Intravitreal Vancomycin 1 mg ± vitrectomy.
    • Fungal
      • Candida: see Candida endophthalmitis.
      • Molds: Intravitreal Amphotericin B 5 mcg in 0.1 mL sterile water or intravitreal Voriconazole is 100 mcg in 0.1 mL sterile water; vitrectomy; benefit of systemic antifungal therapy unclear but Voriconazole is often used.

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments
