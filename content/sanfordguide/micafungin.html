Micafungin
Tradename: Mycamine

Usage and Dosing

  • Micafungin is an echinocandin which inhibits synthesis of β-(1,3)-D-glucan.
  • Approved for treatment of candidemia, acute disseminated candidiasis, Candida peritonitis, Candida abscess, esophageal candidiasis, and prophylaxis against Candida infections in stem cell transplant recipients.
  • Fungicidal against Candida, including those resistant to other antifungals (e.g., C. glabrata and C. krusei), and active against Aspergillus.
  • Micafungin is inactive against Cryptococcus, Trichosporon, molds other than Aspergillus.
  • Candida parapsilosis and C. guilliermondii less susceptible to all echinocandins relative to other species, but clinical response generally satisfactory.
  • No antagonism seen when combined with other antifungal drugs.
  • Under study: extended dose intervals, lock solutions and more (Clin Infect Dis 61(suppl 6):S604, 2015)

Adult Dose

Candida esophagitis:150 mg IV q24h
Candidemia:100 mg IV q24h
Prophylaxis, post transplant:50 mg IV q24h

      • Obesity: See Obesity Dosing Adjustments table.
      • ECMO
        • Adult: See ECMO Drug Dosing Adjustment.
        • Infant: 5 mg/kg q24h for invasive candidiasis and 2.5 mg/kg q24h for prophylaxis is recommended (Pediatr Infect Dis J 2016;35:1204).

      Adverse Effects

      • Well tolerated. Common adverse events include nausea 2.8%, vomiting 2.4%, & headache 2.4%.
      • Transient increase in LFTs, BUN, creatinine reported; rare cases of significant hepatitis and renal insufficiency. See Clin Infect Dis 42:1171, 2006.
      • No drug in CSF or urine.
