HCV Treatment Setting

Treatment Setting

  • Indications for treatment of HCV must take into account both relative and absolute contraindications, although with the Direct Acting Agents (DAAs) available now there are few contraindications, though most individuals with detectable HCV RNA should be treated
  • Response to therapy reflects total clearance of infection as determined by Sustained Virologic Response (SVR),  a negative HCV RNA value obtained > 3 - 4 months after completion of anti-HCV treatment.
  • Duration of therapy is based on genotype, stage of disease / fibrosis (e.g., non-cirrhotic, compensated cirrhosis, decompensated cirrhosis), regimen, and history of prior treatment with direct acting agents (DAAs)
  • See HCV Treatment for drug regimens, duration of therapy and recommended follow-up during and post-treatment.

Indications for Treatment

  • Treatment is indicated for any patient with Chronic Hepatitis C, regardless of liver fibrosis stage and genotype, except:
    • Those patients who have advanced liver disease and are awaiting transplant (discuss with Hepatology/Transplant team to determine if eradication of HCV will effect ability to receive a liver sooner) or
    • Those who have a limited survival owing to other conditions (e.g., cancer, advanced CV disease), where life expectancy is <1 - 2 years.
  • Use DAA-based regimens that are effective with genotype of the patient.  Pan-genotypic DAAs are now widely available, assuring that most patients who are naive to therapy have at least one option for all oral therapy, usually with an 8 to 12 week duration of therapy.  For those with with prior DAA therapy failure, treatment should be guided by resistance test results.  If unfamiliar with interpretation, refer to HCV Guidelines or seek expert consultation.
  • Pegylated Interferon (Peg-IFN) + Ribavirin (RBV) is not recommended for use in any clinical setting  
  • For further information on indications for treatment, see hcvguidelines.org

Predictors of Successful Response to Therapy

  • Presence of these factors suggest best response to DAA therapy, although most patients treated achieve SVR (cure):
    • Recent infection with HCV (acute HCV infection)
    • Patients with genotypes 1b or 2
    • Less severe liver disease on biopsy (Higher metavir score /presence of cirrhosis (esp decompensated cirrhosis), less likely to achieve SVR)
    • HCV Viral Load < 800,000 c/ml
    • Naive to DAA therapy
