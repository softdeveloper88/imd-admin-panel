Chloroquine phosphate
Tradename: Aralen

Usage and Dosing

  • 4-aminoquinoline antimalarial agent for Chloroquine-sensitive strains of all species of Plasmodium.
  • Do not use parenteral preparations available in some endemic countries.
  • For prophylaxis: 
    • Hydroxychloroquine (Plaquenil and generics) may be used if chloroquine is unavailable.
      • If the traveler is not taking routine hydroxychloroquine, the dose is 400 mg weekly taken on the same schedule as chloroquine.
      • If already taking hydroxychloroquine for a medical condition, a daily dose of at least 100 mg is adequate.

Adult Dose

  • Prophylaxis
    • Chloroquine phosphate 500 mg (300 mg base) po per week starting 1–2 weeks before travel, each week during travel and each week for 4 weeks post-travel
  • Treatment of chloroquine-sensitive Plasmodium
    • Chloroquine phosphate 1 gm (600 mg base) po, then 0.5 gm in 6 hrs, then 0.5 gm at 24 and 48 hrs.
      • Total: 2500 mg (Entire course is 5 x 500 mg tablets) 
      • Add Primaquine or Tafenoquine for relapse prevention in P. vivax and P. ovale infections

Adverse Effects

  • Very low therapeutic index. Keep tablets away from children and use care in dosing children. Two tablets may be fatal in an infant and 20 mg/kg single dose may be fatal in an adult.
  • Use with caution in patients with epilepsy.
  • May exacerbate psoriasis
  • Major: protracted treatment in rheumatoid arthritis can lead to retinopathy.
  • Minor: anorexia/nausea/vomiting, headache, dizziness, blurred vision, pruritus in dark-skinned patients.
  • QT prolongation, take care in using with other QT prolonging drugs such as Azithromycin and quinolones, see QTc Interval Prolongation.
