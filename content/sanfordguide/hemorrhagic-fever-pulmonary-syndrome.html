Hemorrhagic Fever, Pulmonary, Hantavirus

Clinical Setting

  • Two major hyperinflammatory syndromes worldwide:  
    • Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS), 'sin nombre virus' and Andes virus, mainly in the US / Americas
    • Hemorrhagic Fever with Renal Syndrome (HFRS) associated with Hantaan, Seoul, Dobrava, and Puumala (PUUV) viruses, mainly in Eurasia.
  • Humans acquire infection through the inhalation of aerosolized excreta from infected rodents. Risk factors for infection include involvement in outdoor activities, such as rural- and forest-related activities, peridomestic rodent presence, exposure to potentially infected dust, and outdoor military training. (Crit Rev Microbiol 40:261, 2014).
  • HPS:
    • 35% fatality rate
    • Presents with acute onset of fever, headache, myalgias, non-productive cough, thrombocytopenia, hemoconcentration (elevated hemoglobin), elevated prothrombin time and non-cardiogenic pulmonary edema with respiratory insufficiency following exposure to rodents.
  • HFRS:
    • 10% fatality rate
    • Presents with flu-like illness and ultimately hypotension, vascular leak, and renal failure

Etiologies

  • Hantavirus (Bunyavirales order; Hantaviridae, genus Orthohantavirus)
  • Puumala virus (PUUV; variant of Hantavirus in Europe/ Asia)

Primary Regimens

  • No treatment has established efficacy. Ribavirin use is controversial; many believe it can reduce mortality when given early in the course of infection.
  • Early recognition of disease and supportive care (usually in the ICU) is key to successful outcome.

Alternative Regimens

  • Case reports of successful corticosteroid use to resolve renal injury from Dobrava hantavirus (Ther Apher Dial 17:402, 2013).

Comments

  • Review of potential antiviral and immunotherapies (Frontiers in Microbiology 2023)
  • Owing to the hyperimmune nature of these infections, immunochemotherapy that controls excessive immune activation has been proposed; no specific regimen or approach his been rigorously tested (JIM 285: 510-23, 2019)
  • Review of therapeutics (Current Treatment Options ID 12: 410, 2020)
