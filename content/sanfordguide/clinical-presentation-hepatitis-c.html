HCV Clinical Presentation and Staging

Introduction

  • For full summary, see HCVguidelines.org. 

Symptoms

  • Usually asymptomatic
  • When symptomatic, common complaints include:
    • Fatigue
    • Nausea
    • Anorexia
    • Myalgias
    • Arthralgias
    • Asthenia
    • Weight loss (except where ascites)
  • Note: there is poor correlation between symptoms and disease stage or transaminase elevations.

Signs: Depends on Stage of Disease

  • Skin disorders:
    • Spider angiomas
    • Porphyria cutanea tarda (PCT)
  • Stigmata of cirrhosis (Compensated)
    • Mild jaundice
    • Hepatomegaly
    • Splenomegaly
    • Peripheral edema
    • Hemorrhoids
  • End Stage Liver Disease (Decompensated Cirrhosis)
    • Ascites
    • Encephalopathy
    • Coagulopathy (PT > 3 seconds; INR > 1.3)
    • Varices (bleeding or non-bleeding)
    • For predictors of mortality at first decompensation, see Hepatology 45:797, 2007.

Laboratory Abnormalities

  • Transaminase elevation:
    • 1/3 with normal transaminase levels.
    • Only 25% have transaminases > 2x ULN.
    • Poor correlation between transaminase elevation and liver histology.
    • New increase in transaminases following a previous cure (SVR) suggests reinfection (re-check HCV RNA)
  • Otherwise, no specific lab abnormalities except when cirrhotic or hepatic decompensation:
    • Low albumin
    • Elevated bilirubin
    • AST: ALT ratio > 1
    • Low platelet count
    • Cryoglobulinemia

Extrahepatic Manifestations

  • Lymphoma
  • Mixed cryoglobulinemia
  • Glomerulonephritis (membranoproliferative)
  • Auto-antibody disorders, e.g., thyroiditis
  • PCT and Lichen planus
  • Diabetes mellitus

Non-Invasive Tests

  • Most Commonly Used Liver Staging Tests:
    • Fib 4 = Age (yrs) x AST (U/L) / (Plt count (109/L) x √ (ALT (U/L))
      • Fib 4 value < 1.45 ( fibrosis unlikely); > 3.25 (fibrosis likely)
    • APRI = AST (IU/L) / AST (Upper limit of Normal) (IU/L) /Platelet Count (109/L)
      • APRI value < 0.7 (fibrosis unlikely); > 1.0 (fibrosis likely)
    • Fibroscan: Optimal cut-offs: F0 < 4.5 kPa; F2 ~ 7.1 kPa; F4: > 12.5kPa

MELD Score

  • Model for End-Stage Liver Disease (MELD) score
  • MELD score used to determine End-Stage Liver Disease:
    • If MELD score > 10 (or Child-Pugh score = C), refer to hepatologist for transplantation.
    • MELD Score calculator
