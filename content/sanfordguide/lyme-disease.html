Lyme Disease, Post-Exposure Prophylaxis

Clinical Setting

  • Bite by infected Ixodes tick (deer tick) in an endemic area (Image: nymphal stage).
  • In endemic areas, patient with a partially engorged Ixodes (deer) tick. CDC statistics by State.
  • "High Risk" tick bite = Ixodes vector + bite occurred in highly endemic area + tick attached for > 36 hours (all 3 criteria must be present)
  • Think about concomitant tick-borne diseases: babesiosis, ehrlichiosis, anaplasmosis as well as Lyme. For additional images, see Tick Gallery.

Etiologies

  • Borrelia burgdorferi

Primary Regimens

  • Only use If "High Risk" tick bite (see definition above) and administered within 72 hours of tick removal:
    • Adult: Doxycycline 200 mg po x 1 dose with food for prophylaxis
    • Child of any age: Doxycycline 4 mg /kg not to exceed 200 mg po x one dose

Alternative Regimens

  • None

Comments

  • 2020 Clinical practice guidelines (Neurology 96:262, 2021).
  • Efficacy of prophylaxis, N Engl J Med 345:79, 2001; N Engl J Med 345:133, 2001.
  • Recommendation only for patient groups in whom Doxycycline can be used safely.
  • Prevention of tick bites and prompt removal of attached ticks is of primary importance.
  • Experience of Doxy-PEP for Lyme from 2010- 2020 suggests it is underutilized for children (OFID 11(10) 2024)
