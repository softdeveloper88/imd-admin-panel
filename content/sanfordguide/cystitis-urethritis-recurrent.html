Prevention of Recurrent UTI, Female

Clinical Setting

  • Recurrent urinary tract infection (UTI) in women: cystitis, urethritis.
  • Young person: ≥ 3 culture-positive episodes/year.
  • Post-menopausal person: ≥3 culture + symptomatic UTIs in 1 year or 2 UTIs in 6 months.

Etiologies

  • E. coli
  • Enterobacterales
  • Enterococcus sp.
  • Staph. saprophyticus

Primary Regimens

  • Pre-menopausal woman: 
    • Avoid spermicide
    • Increase fluid intake (additional 1.5L/day) (JAMA Intern Med 2018; 178:1509)
    • Methenamine hippurate 1gm po bid (Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2012; BMJ 2022;376: e068229)
    • Post-coitol antibiotics: Single dose immediately after intercourse (JAMA 1993; 264: 703):
      • Nitrofurantoin 100 mg
      • TMP-SMX 80mg/400 mg
      • Trimethoprim 100 mg
      • Cephalexin 250 mg
  • Post-menopausal woman:
    • Intravaginal estrogen: 0.5 mg Estriol cream intravaginal daily x 2 weeks and then twice weekly as maintenance (N Engl J Med 1993; 329: 753)
    • Increase fluid intake (additional 1.5L/day) (JAMA Intern Med 2018; 178:1509)
    • Methenamine hippurate 1gm po bid (Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2012)
    • Post-coitol antibiotics: Single dose immediately after intercourse (JAMA 1993; 264: 703):
      • Nitrofurantoin 100 mg
      • TMP-SMX 80mg/400 mg
      • Trimethoprim 100 mg
      • Cephalexin 250 mg

Alternative Regimens

  • In persons with recurrent UTIs who have failed strategies above and have significant reduced quality of life and/or repeat hospitalizations, consider a limited time trial of antibiotic prophylaxis with an antibiotic active against the predominant infecting pathogen. (Lancet Infect Dis 2018; 18:957).  Providers need to balance risk of developing antibiotic resistant pathogens with benefit.  Regimens could include one of the following:
    • Daily regimens:
      • Nitrofurantoin 50-100 mg once daily 
      • Trimethoprim 100 mg daily
      • TMP-SMX 40mg/200mg once daily or 3x weekly
      • Cephalexin 250 mg daily
    • Weekly regimen:
      • Fosfomycin 3gm once weekly + (Cefixime or Amoxicillin-clavulanate or TMP-SMX) (Clin Infect Dis 71;3128: 2021)

Comments

  • Strategies which have NOT demonstrated efficacy to prevent UTIs:
    • Cranberry products  (JAMA 2016;316:1873; JAMA 2016;316:1879) 
    • Oral probiotics (Arch Int Med 2012; 172:204)
  • Intravaginal probiotics may be useful: a randomized placebo controlled trial of a once weekly Lactobacillus crispatus probiotic vaginal suppository for 10 weeks showed a 50% reduction in recurrences with the probiotic (Clin Infect Dis 52:1212, 2011) and another study (Clin Infect Dis 2024) demonstrated reduced recurrence of UTIs at 4 months in women who received regimens including intravaginal lactobacillus when compared with those who received placebo or oral lactobacillus alone.
  • Consider urologic evaluation/imaging if hematuria without dysuria, elevated serum creatinine, recurrent Proteus infections, or incontinence/retention
  • References:  New Engl J Med 366:1028, 2012; Clin Infect Dis 52: e103-120, 2011 (IDSA-ESCMID Guidelines).
