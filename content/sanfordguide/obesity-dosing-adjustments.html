Obesity Dosing Adjustments

Introduction

  • The number of obese patients is increasing. Intuitively, the standard doses of some drugs may not achieve effective serum concentrations. Pertinent data on anti-infective dosing in the obese patient are gradually emerging. Even though some of the data need further validation, the following table reflects what is currently known.
  • Obesity is defined as weight ≥20% over ideal body weight (Ideal BW) or body mass index (BMI) >30. 
  • Click for calculator: Ideal body weight (Ideal BW); body mass index (BMI); lean body weight (LBW)

  • The pharmacokinetics of antibacterials in obese patients is emerging, but only some drugs have been evaluated. Those drugs for which data justifies a dose adjustment are summarized under Obesity Dosing Adjustment (table below).

Obesity Dosing Adjustments

  • Click drug for full information.
  • Dose = suggested body weight (Actual, Ideal, or Adjusted body weight) for dose calculation in an obese patient, or specific dose if applicable. Adjusted BW = Ideal BW + 0.4 x (Actual BW – Ideal BW).
DrugRecommendationComments
AcyclovirUse Adjusted BW PK data suggest that adjusted BW in obesity approximates drug exposure seen in non-obesity using actual BW (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2016;60:1830). Use of adjusted BW in obesity also supported by a retrospective observational study (J Clin Neurosci 2025;136:111230).
Aminoglycosides
Use Adjusted BW
Ref: Pharmacother 2007;27:1081. Follow levels so as to modify dose, if necessary, once hemodynamics stabilize.
Amphotericin B (Liposomal)
Use Adjusted BW, but consider actual BW if critically ill
Recommendation based on a retrospective analysis evaluating clinical outcomes in 238 patients (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2021;65:e02366-20). Earlier data from a small PK study support a fixed dose in patients ≥100 kg (e.g., 500 mg for a target dose of 5 mg/kg), which similarly suggests that using actual BW in all obese patients is not the best strategy (Clin Infect Dis 2020;70:2213).
Anidulafungin
Insufficient data
Accumulating data suggest reduced echinocandin exposure in obesity, but evidence for the ideal dose adjustment strategy is limited (Am J Health Syst Pharm 2023;80:503). PK modeling data suggest a 25% increase in loading and maintenance dose in patients >140 kg results in exposure comparable to non-obese patients receiving usual dosing (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2018;62:e00063-18). A retrospective cohort study (n=173) showed no difference in outcomes across BMI category in patients receiving standard dosing (J Pharm Pract 2022;35:20).
Artemether-lumefantrine
No dose adjustment appears necessary 
Data from a PK study in healthy volunteers (6 normal weight, 7 overweight [mean BMI 28.2], 3 obese [mean BMI 33.9]). Only one with BMI >35. Small numbers limit conclusions (Int J Antimicrob Agents 2022;59:106482).
Caspofungin
Insufficient data
Accumulating data suggest reduced echinocandin exposure in obesity, but evidence for the ideal dose adjustment strategy is limited (Am J Health Syst Pharm 2023;80:503). A weight-based regimen (2 mg/kg load, 1.25 mg/kg q24h) has been suggested (AAC 2020;64:e00905-20).
Cefazolin (surgical prophylaxis in adults)
3 gm x1 if ≥120 kg
Joint guideline recommendation is 3 gm x1 if ≥120 kg, 2 gm x1 if <120 kg (Am J Health Syst Pharm 2013;70:195). However, in a recent systematic review, 3/3 outcome studies and 9/15 PK studies found the 3 gm dose in patients ≥120 kg to be unnecessary (Obes Surg 2022;32:3138). Similar finding (i.e., 2 gm adequate in obesity) in a recent model-based analysis (Br J Anaesth 2025;134:1041). 
Cefazolin (treatment of active infection)
Insufficient data
PK considerably altered in obesity. Consider higher dose and/or continuous infusion in patients ≥120 kg with less susceptible gram-negative infection, critically ill, or immunocompromised (IJAA 2023;61:107651).
CefepimeModest dose increase:
2 gm IV q8h instead of the usual q12h		Data from 10 patients (mean BMI 48) undergoing bariatric surgery; regimen yields free T > MIC of 60% for MIC of 8 μg/mL. Ref: Obes Surg 22:465, 2012.
CefotetanNo dose adjustment appears necessaryIn a small retrospective study, there was no difference in surgical site infection rates between 2 gm and 3 gm prophylaxis doses in patients weighing ≥120 kg with median BMI 42 kg/m2 (Surg infect 2018;19:504).
Cefoxitin (surgical prophylaxis)Larger dose possibly requiredInsufficient data for a firm recommendation.. Single 40 mg/kg perioperative dose in obese patients (based on actual BW; range 4-7.5 gm) resulted in suboptimal achievement of pharmacodynamic targets in both serum and tissue, although performance was better than a simulated 2 gm dose. Ref: Antimicrob Agents Chemother 60:5885, 2016. A single 4 gm dose administered preop to 200 pts (mean BMI 45.8 kg/m2) failed to reach predefined PK/PD targets for most common pathogens (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2019;63:e01613-19).
CeftarolineNo dose adjustment appears necessaryBased on PK simulations. Consider q8h dosing if targeting 50% fT>MIC for MRSA (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
CeftazidimeLarger dose possibly requiredInsufficient data for a firm recommendation. Retrospective cohort study suggests the need for higher doses in obese ICU patients (J Antimicrob Chemother 2023;78:2968).
Ceftazidime-avibactamNo dose adjustment appears necessaryBased on PK/PD data (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
Ceftolozane-tazobactamNo dose adjustment appears necessaryBased on limited PK/PD and clinical data (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
CeftriaxoneLarger dose possibly requiredInsufficient data for a firm recommendation. Retrospective cohort study suggests clinical failure more likely in obese patients treated with standard dosing; higher quality data are required before a recommendation is appropriate (Diseases 2020;8:E27).
CiprofloxacinNo dose adjustment appears necessaryNo influence of obesity on ciprofloxacin plasma PK in a prospective clinical study (n=20, median BMI 45 kg/m2). Increased dosing may be required if target tissue penetration is known to be impaired in obesity, e.g., in skin and soft tissue infection (Clin Pharmacokinet 2022;61:1167).
ClindamycinConsider Actual BWCombined PK modeling data from three prospective trials in children suggests actual BW to be most appropriate; no clinical outcome data are available. Ref: Antimicro Agents Chemother 2017;61:e02014-16.
ColistinUse Ideal BWMaximum colistin base activity 360 mg/day to limit risk of nephrotoxicity. Clinical data in obese patients limited and difficult to interpret (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
DalbavancinNo dose adjustment appears necessaryRef: Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923.
DaptomycinAdjusted BW suggestedData from a retrospective study suggest similar outcomes using actual BW and adjusted BW (Ther Adv Infectious Dis 2019;6:1). Other data suggest similar outcomes with ideal BW and actual BW (Antimicrob Ag Chemother 58:88, 2014). Some recommend fixed, non-weight-based dosing in morbid obesity based on a recent PK study (Pharmacotherapy 2018;38:981).
DelafloxacinNo dose adjustment appears necessaryData limited to skin infections (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923).
DoripenemNo dose adjustment appears necessaryConsider optimized dosing regimens (e.g., extended infusion) for pathogens with high MICs or critically ill patients with augmented renal clearance. TDM, if available, may be useful (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
EravacyclineNo dose adjustment appears necessaryStandard dosing uses actual BW. A post hoc analysis of two RCTs demonstrates safety and efficacy in patients with BMI ≥30 kg/m² (OFID 2020;7:ofaa548).
Ertapenem
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Consider optimized dosing regimens (e.g., extended infusion) for pathogens with high MICs or critically ill patients with augmented renal clearance. TDM, if available, may be useful (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
Ethambutol
Use Ideal BW
Ref: Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923.
FluconazoleLarger dose possibly required
Insufficient data for a firm recommendation. Case reports suggest larger dose required in critically ill obese patients. Recent paper suggests that weight based dosing (12 mg/kg load, then 6 mg/kg/day using total BW) reaches desired PK/PD targets more reliably than fixed dosing; clinical validation required (Pharmacotherapy 17:1023, 1997; Antimicrob Agents Chemother 60:6550, 2016).
FlucytosineUse Ideal BWData from one obese patient with cryptococcal disease. Ref: Pharmacother 15:251, 1995.
Fosfomycin (IV)
Insufficient data
Limited data suggest ↓exposure in obesity. If MIC ≤16 µg/mL, 8 gm IV q8h appears sufficient if CrCl ≤130 mL/min, regardless of obesity status or PK/PD index employed (AAC 2022;66:e0230221; JAC 2019;74:2335).
Ganciclovir
Similar outcomes using actual BW and adjusted BW
No differences in safety or efficacy outcomes observed in a single-center retrospective cohort study. Larger, prospective studies focusing on specific patient subgroups are needed (Transplant Infect Dis 2023;25:e14134).
Imipenem-cilastatin
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Consider optimized dosing regimens (e.g., extended infusion) for pathogens with high MICs or critically ill patients with augmented renal clearance. TDM, if available, may be useful (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
Isavuconazonium sulfate
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Similar AUC in obese (BMI >30 kg/m2) and non-obese patients. Data published in abstract form. Ref: Open Forum Infect Dis 2016;3. Available at: http://dx.doi.org/10.1093/ofid/ofw172.1498.
Isoniazid
Use Ideal BW
Ref: Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923.
Levofloxacin
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Data from 13 obese patients; variability in study findings renders conclusion uncertain. Refs: Antimicrob Ag Chemother 55:3240, 2011; J Antimicrob Chemother 66:1653, 2011. A PK study (requiring clinical validation) in patients with BMI ≥40 kg/m2 suggests that higher doses may be necessary to achieve adequate drug exposure (Clin Pharmacokinet 53:753, 2014).
Linezolid
Standard dosing may be inadequate
In a PK/PD modeling study in 15 obese patients with MRSA pneumonia (MIC 1-4 μg/mL), standard dosing led to low probability of target attainment in patients age <65 years (J Antimicrob Chemother 2019;74:667). Insufficient concentrations from standard dosing also suggested by PK studies in intraabdominal surgery patients (J Clin Med 2020;9:1067) and critically ill patients with skin and soft tissue infections (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2021;65:e01619-20).
Meropenem
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Consider optimized dosing regimens (e.g., extended infusion) for pathogens with high MICs or critically ill patients with augmented renal clearance. TDM, if available, may be useful (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
Meropenem-vaborbactam
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Ref: Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923.
Metronidazole
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Insufficient evidence to support the use of higher doses in obesity (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923). Use actual BW for weight-based dosing, max 4 gm/day.
Micafungin
Insufficient data
Accumulating data suggest reduced echinocandin exposure in obesity, but evidence for the ideal dose adjustment strategy is limited (Am J Health Syst Pharm 2023;80:503). A PK/PD study suggests 150 mg q24h adequate for C. albicans in pts ≤115 kg, 200 mg q24h in pts >115 kg. 200 mg q24h adequate for C. glabrata in pts ≤115 kg (Crit Care 2018 Apr 15;22(1):94). A more recent PK/PD study suggests 200 mg q24h in patients >125 kg with a Candida species that has an MIC of 0.016 µg/mL, and 300 mg q24h if the MIC is 0.032 µg/mL. A loading dose of 2x the maintenance dose is also suggested (J Antimicrob Chemother 2019;74:978).
Moxifloxacin
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Based on limited PK data (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923).
Omadacycline
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Based on two post hoc analyses of clinical trial data (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
Oritavancin
Insufficient data
No available data (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923).
Oseltamivir
No dose adjustment appears necessary

Data from 10 obese volunteers, unclear if applicable to patients >250 kg (OK to give 150 mg po q12h). Ref: J Antimicrob Chemother 66:2083, 2011.  Newer data, consistent: Antimicrob Ag Chemother 58:1616, 2014.

Piperacillin-tazobactam4.5 gm IV (over 4 hr) q6-8hSuggested dosing in obesity particularly for critically ill patients, if MICs ≥8 µg/mL, or CrCl >100 mL/min (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
Polymyxin BUse Adjusted BW Maximum daily dose of 200-249 mg suggested to limit risk of nephrotoxicity (not clinically validated); may be insufficient if MIC >0.5 µg/mL (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
Posaconazole (IV)
Consider increased treatment dose (not prophylaxis) in patients >140 kg
In a PK study, total body weight best predicted changes in drug clearance and Vd. For sufficient exposure, modeling suggests increasing the dose used for treatment to 400 mg IV q24h in patients weighing >140 kg. For prophylaxis, 300 mg IV q24h provides sufficient exposure in patients up to 190 kg. Ref: J Antimicrob Chemother 2020;75:1006.
Pyrazinamide
Use Ideal BW
Ref: Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923.
Rezafungin
Insufficient data
Accumulating data suggest reduced echinocandin exposure in obesity, but evidence for the ideal dose adjustment strategy is limited (Am J Health Syst Pharm 2023;80:503). A sub-analysis of phase 2 trial data (abstract only) suggests dose adjustment in obesity unnecessary (OFID 2020;7(suppl 1):S378-9.
Rifampin
Insufficient data
Current recommendation is for no dose adjustment (i.e., flat dosing), but limited data suggest a higher dose (with TDM) might be more appropriate (Clin Pharmacokinet 2025;64:193; Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923).
Tecovirimat
600 mg po q8h x14 days
300 mg IV q12h x14 days
Manufacturer PI recommendations for pediatric and adult patients weighing ≥120 kg. Infuse IV doses over 6 hours.
Tedizolid phosphate
No dose adjustment appears necessary
Based on limited data (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
Teicoplanin
Use Actual BW
Ref: Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923.
Telavancin750 mg IV q24h probably adequate for most patientsData suggest this dose achieves efficacy targets and minimizes risk of nephrotoxicity. If higher systemic exposure is desired, consider 10 mg/kg q24h based on adjusted BW, not to exceed 1000 mg q24h (Pharmacotherapy 2023;43:226).
TigecyclineNo dose adjustment appears necessaryStandard dosing recommended in obesity. However, for resistant Gram-negative bacteria, consider higher dose (200 mg, then 100 mg IV q12h) regardless of patient weight (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923).
TMP-SMXUse Adjusted BWBased on extremely limited evidence (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2024;68:e0171923). Adjusted BW = Ideal BW + 0.4 x (Actual BW – Ideal BW).
Vancomycin
Use Actual BW
Current guidelines: loading dose 20-25 mg/kg (max 3 gm), maintenance dose 15-20 mg/kg (max 2 gm) q8-12h. Daily doses >4.5 gm should rarely be required. Early and frequent AUC24 monitoring is recommended (Am J Health Syst Pharm 2020;77:835). Decreased AKI risk with AUC24 monitoring (compared to trough monitoring) in patients with BMI >30 (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 2022;66:e0088621). Controversy continues: recent review suggests loading dose based on actual BW and maintenance dose based on adjusted BW (Expert Opin Drug Metab Toxicol 2022;18:323).
Voriconazole
Use Ideal BW
Dosing using actual body weight may result in supratherapeutic concentrations, but published data are insufficient to recommend adjusted BW vs. ideal BW. Best approach for now is to use ideal BW and check serum concentrations to avoid underdosing (Antimicrob Agents Chemother 55:2601, 2011; Clin Infect Dis 53:745, 2011; Clin Infect Dis 63:286, 2016; Antimicrobial Agents Chemother 2021;65:e02460-20).
