Clostridium bifermentans

Clinical Setting

  • Anaerobic infection caused by Clostridium bifermentans

Classification

  • Anaerobic spore-forming Gram-positive bacillus

Primary Regimens

  • Optimal regimen not defined, case reports only: in vitro susceptibilities similar to other Clostridium spp. (see Comments)

Alternative Regimens

  • Not defined

Comments

  • Case report of isolate susceptible to Amoxicillin-clavulanate, Cefoxitin, Clindamycin, Meropenem, Metronidiazole, Penicillin (Can J Infect Dis Med Microbiol. 2015; 26: 105–107).
