Drugs in Development (ARV)

HIV Drugs in Development

  • The following antiretroviral (ARV) agents are in various stages of development.
AgentClassStatus / Comments
Apricitabine (ATC)NRTI
  • Nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor
  • 600 mg bid dosing (being finalized in current studies)
  • 3TC-like agent with activity against virus harboring M184V mutation.
  • Antagonistic against 3TC and FTC.
AlbuvirtideFusion Inhibitor
  • Like T-20 except with very long half life
  • Long acting
  • Maleimide-modified peptide from gp41
  • Plasma t1/2  = 11 days  (IV)
  • Blocks binding to CD4 receptor
  • ~1 log10 decrease in HIV RNA (320 mg dose)
  • No ISR or antibodies detected (short term)
bNABBroadly Neutralizing Antibody
  • Targeted against envelop glycoproteins
  • Administer together with Long-acting small molecules (e.g., cabotegravir)
CX05168 / 05045 / 14442LEDGINs (new class)
  • First in class allosteric integration inhibitors
  • Bind to the LEDGF/p75 binding site on integrase gene product
  • Active against a wide range of INSTI resistant mutants and active against a broad range of HIV-1 subtypes
  • Synergy with Integrase Strand-transfer Inhibitors (e.g., Raltegravir, Elvitegravir)
Islatravir (MK-8591)NRTTI
  • Nucleoside reverse transcriptase translocation inhibitor
  • Long acting
  • 10 mg oral daily dose OR parenteral extended release (dosed q3 months)
  • Active against most NNRTI resistance strains (e.g., K103N, Y181C, G190A, E101K, E138K and mixtures thereof)
MK-8507NNRTI
  • High potency against K103, Y181, G190 variants
  • Long acting
  • PK supports weekly administration (half life ~ 70 hrs)
