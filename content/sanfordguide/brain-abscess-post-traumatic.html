Brain Abscess, Post-traumatic or Post-surgical

Clinical Setting

  • Fever, change in mental status after head trauma or in post-neurosurgical patient.
  • Penetrating head injury.

Etiologies

  • Staph. aureus, MRSA and MSSA
  • Coagulase-negative staphylococci
  • Enterobacteriaceae
  • Pseudomonas aeruginosa
  • Streptococci
  • Anaerobes

Primary Regimens

  • Empiric therapy pending culture results:
  • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg q8-12h + Cefepime 2 gm IV q8h + Metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h
  • Vancomycin 15-20 mg/kg q8-12h + Meropenem 2 gm IV q8h
    • Vancomycin dosing, see Comments

Alternative Regimens

  • Linezolid 600 mg q12h po/IV instead of vancomycin as above

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Modify regimen based on results of abscess culture and susceptibility testing.
  • Duration of therapy not well defined but 6-8 weeks is usual.

Comments

  • Adjust Vancomycin dosing to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator); alternative is trough of 15-20 μg/mL
  • Aspiration and/or surgical drainage of the abscess is recommended for diagnosis and definitive therapy.
  • Review articles: N Engl J Med 2014; 371:447; Clin Microbiol Infect 2017; 23:614; Semin Neurol 2019; 39: 334, Curr Opin Infect Dis. 2024; 37:211.
