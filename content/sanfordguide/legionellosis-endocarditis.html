Endocarditis, Legionella

Clinical Setting

  • Specific therapy for Legionella endocarditis in patient with prosthetic valve.
  • Culture-negative prosthetic valve endocarditis; often nosocomial.
  • Diagnosis by isolation of the organism from resected valve tissue but with special handling may also be isolated from blood culture.

Etiologies

  • Legionella spp.

Primary Regimens

  • Levofloxacin 750 mg once daily po/IV
  • Azithromycin 500 mg once daily po/IV

Alternative Regimens

  • Doxycycline 200 mg bid po/IV
  • Erythromycin 500 mg po/IV q6h + Rifampin 600-1200 mg po in 2 or more divided doses
  • Ciprofloxacin at 400 mg IV q12h or 500 mg po bid
  • Combination of erythromycin and ciprofloxacin has also been used (Emerg Infect Dis 18:95, 2012).

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Advise microbiology laboratory when considering the diagnosis of Legionella spp. endocarditis as it is possible to isolate the organism in blood culture media with special handling.
  • Duration of therapy unknown, most patients have been treated for at least 5 months.
  • Infectious Diseases consultation recommended

Comments

  • Several Legionella spp have been reported as causes of endocarditis.
  • Most patients reported in the literature have undergone valve replacement in addition to medical therapy.
  • Treatment recommendations based on anecdotal case reports.
  • References: J Infect 51:e256, 2005,  Clin Micro Rev 14:177, 2001, Circulation. 2015;132:1435-1486.
