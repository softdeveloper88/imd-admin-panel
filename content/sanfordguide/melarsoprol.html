Melarsoprol
Tradename: Mel B, Arsobal

Usage and Dosing

  • Melarsoprol is a toxic arsenical used for treatment of stage 2 (CNS) trypanosomiasis as rescue therapy when fexinidzole is not available.
    • For stage 2 T. gambiense nifurtimox/eflornithine combination therapy (NECT) is a preferable alternate to Fexinidazole.
    • For stage 2 T. rhodesiense Melarsoprol is the main alternate to Fexinidazole.
  • Available from CDC Drug Service. Manufactured by Aventis (Arsobal) as 180 mg/5 mL of propylene glycol (IV ampule).
  • Given as slow IV injection.

Adult Dose

  • Stage 2 r-HAT: Melarsoprol 2.2 mg/kg/day IV x 10 days; non-inferior to long-standing standard regimen (PLoS Negl Trop Dis 2012, 6:e1695).
    • Prednisone pre-treatment may prevent encephalopathy.
    • Pre-treatment with Suramin as above is used by some clinicians to clear blood stages to minimize reactions to melarsoprol.

Adverse Effects

  • Encephalopathic reaction (5–10% of patients), with up to ~50% mortality rate; corticosteroid therapy may prevent/attenuate encephalopathy. Other: elevated blood pressure, albuminuria, abdominal pain, nausea/vomiting, phlebitis, peripheral neuropathy, Herxheimer-like reaction, pruritus.
  • Severe hemolytic reactions in patients with G6PD deficiency.
