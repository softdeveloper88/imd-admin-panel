Vancomycin Desensitization

Overview

  • 5-step rapid vancomycin desensitization protocol.
  • Can be completed in four hours if no steps need to be repeated.
  • References: Ann Pharmacother 2001;35:1458; Ann Intern Med 1984;100:157.
  • If rapid desensitization is unsuccessful, consider slow desensitization: Arch Intern Med 1990;150:2197.

Method

  • Prepare five vancomycin infusions:
    • Standard dilution: 500 mg in 250 mL D5W or NS (2 mg/mL); this will be infusion #5.
    • Infusion #4: 10 mL of infusion #5 added to 100 mL (approx 0.2 mg/mL).
    • Infusion #3: 10 mL of infusion #4 added to 100 mL (approx 0.02 mg/mL).
    • Infusion #2: 10 mL of infusion #3 added to 100 mL (approx 0.002 mg/mL).
    • Infusion #1: 10 mL of infusion #2 added to 100 mL (approx 0.0002 mg/mL).
  • IV premeds: diphenhydramine 50 mg, hydrocortisone 50 mg (repeat q6h).
  • Initiate infusion #1 at 0.5 mL/min. Increase by 0.5 mL/min q5 minutes (as tolerated) to maximum rate of 5 mL/min. Proceed to next infusion after each infusion is successfully completed.
  • If pruritus, hypotension, rash, or difficulty breathing occurs during an infusion, stop infusion and reinfuse previously tolerated infusion at the highest tolerated rate. Repeat up to three times for any given concentration.
  • Following completion of infusion #5, immediately administer desired full dose of vancomycin over two hours. Consider diphenhydramine 50 mg IV prior to each dose.
