Vincent's Angina, Necrotizing Gingivitis, Trench mouth

Clinical Setting

  • Person with poor dental hygiene develops inflamed, ulcerated and painful gingiva with associated halitosis.
  • Can mimic scurvy
  • Most severe form is called Noma or cancrum oris (Lancet 368:147, 2006).

Etiologies

  • Mixture of usual oral anaerobic bacteria and fusospirochetal organisms
  • Associated with vitamin deficiencies (vitamins A-D)

Primary Regimens

  • Severe infection: Penicillin G 4 million units IV q4h + Metronidazole 500 mg IV q8h
  • Less severe and able to take po meds: Penicillin VK 500-1000 mg po qid + Metronidazole 500 mg po tid
  • Replete vitamins
  • Dental care

Alternative Regimens

  • If severe, Clindamycin 600 mg IV q8h
  • Less severe and can take oral medications: Clindamycin 300-450 mg po qid
  • Replete vitamins
