Endocarditis, Overview

Overview

  • Endocarditis diagnostic criteria include (see 2023 Duke-International Society for Cardiovascular Infectious Diseases Criteria for complete list):
    • Echocardiographic evidence of valvular vegetation, valve/leaflet perforation, fistula, aneurysm, or abscess; significant, new valvular regurgitation
      • TEE (trans-esophageal) more sensitive than TTE (trans-thoracic)
      • Positive cardiac CT or PET-CT now included as a major criterion imaging modality
    • Two or more separate positive blood cultures for organisms that commonly cause endocarditis
      • Positive polymerase chain reaction or other nucleic acid-based technique for Coxiella burnetii, Bartonella species, or Tropheryma whipplei, postive serology for Bartonella henselae or Bartonella quintana with immunoglobulin G (IgG) titer ≥1:800 now major microbiologic criteria
    • Definite emboli; microembolic/immunologic phenomena
    • Updated modified Duke criteria: Clin Infect Dis 2023; 77:518
  • Review articles: N Engl J Med. 2020;383:567; JAMA 320:72, 2018.
  • Refs.: European Heart J 30:2369, 2009 (European Society Guidelines); Circulation 132:1435, 2015 (AHA Treatment Guidelines)
  • For surgical management of endocarditis, see Ann Thorac Surg 9:2012, 2011; N Engl J Med 368:1425, 2013; and Clin Infect Dis 60:741, 2015.
  • One randomized controlled trial found that early surgery significantly decreases risk of systemic embolization in patients with left-sided infective endocarditis and vegetations >10 mm in size (N Engl J Med 366:2466, 2012).
