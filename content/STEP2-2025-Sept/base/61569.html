Transverse myelitis

Guillain-Barré syndrome

Motor
  • Early flaccid, late spastic paralysis
  • If quadriplegia, weakness in LE = UE
  • Ascending paralysis
  • Weakness in LE > UE in early disease

Sensory
  • Clearly identifiable sensory level
  • Mild sensory loss
  • No spinal cord level

Autonomic
  • Bowel & bladder dysfunction
  • Cardiovascular instability

Cranial nerves
  • None
  • Oculomotor, glossopharyngeal, or facial paralysis

Electromyography/nerve
conduction velocity
  • Mostly normal
  • Peripheral motor &/or sensory NCV reduced

MRI
  • Focal enhanced area of T2 signal
  • Normal
  • Enhancement of anterior nerve roots or cauda equina

Cerebrospinal fluid
  • Pleocytosis
  • ± Increased IgG index
  • Absence of pleocytosis
  • Elevated protein

LE = lower extremity; NCV = nerve conduction velocity; UE = upper extremity.
