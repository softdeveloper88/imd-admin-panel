Empyema, Neonatal

Clinical Setting

  • Neonatal pleural empyema (Age 0-1 month).

Etiologies

  • Staph. aureus (MSSA and MRSA)
  • Occasionally Group B strep
  • Rarely Streptococcus pneumoniae, Group A Strep
  • Consider iatrogenic chylous effusions in patients with heart surgery or mediastinitis in patients with tracheo-esophageal fistula (TEF)

Primary Regimens

  • Perform diagnostic thoracentesis; catheter or chest tube drainage for empyema.
  • Gestational age ≤34 weeks
    • Age 0-7 days
      • MSSA: Nafcillin (or Oxacillin) 25 mg/kg IV q12h
      • MRSA:  Vancomycin 60- 80 mg/kg IV divided 3-4 times a day to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 10-15 μg/mL but AUC targeting is preferred).
    • Age 7-28 days
      • MSSA: Nafcillin (or Oxacillin) 25 mg/kg q8h
      • MRSA: Vancomycin 60- 80 mg/kg IV divided 3-4 times a day to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 10-15 μg/mL but AUC targeting is preferred).
  • Gestational age >34 weeks
    • Age 0-7 days
      • MSSA: Nafcillin (or Oxacillin) 25 mg/kg IV q8h
      • MRSA: Vancomycin 60- 80 mg/kg IV divided 3-4 times a day to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 10-15 μg/mL but AUC targeting is preferred).
    • Age 7-28 days
      • MSSA: Nafcillin (or Oxacillin) 37 mg/kg q6h
      • MRSA: Vancomycin 60- 80 mg/kg IV divided 3-4 times a day to achieve preferred target AUC24 400-600 μg/mL x hr (see vancomycin AUC dosing calculator; alternative is trough of 10-15 μg/mL but AUC targeting is preferred).
  • Note regarding Vancomycin dosing: Traditional dosing of 45-60 mg/kg/day frequently does not achieve target AUC in term infants and older children with normal renal function. Use of AUC24  closer to 400 μg/mL adequate for most non-CNS infections. Clin Infect Dis Jul 13 2020

Alternative Regimens

  • Gestational age <32 wks
    • Age 0-14 days
      • MSSA: Cefazolin 50 mg/kg IV q12h
    • Age 14-28 days
      • MSSA: Cefazolin 50 mg/kg q8h
  • Gestational age ≥32 weeks
    • Age 0-7 days
      • MSSA: Cefazolin 50 mg/kg IV q12h
    • Age 7-28 days
      • MSSA: Cefazolin 50 mg/kg q8h

Antimicrobial Stewardship

  • Cefazolin probably equivalent to nafcillin with fewer adverse events if CSF is normal
  • Narrow to Ampicillin +/- Gentamicin if Group B strep; Ampicillin if S. pneumoniae
  • Chlyothorax and transudates should not be mistaken for empyema. Cell counts, protein and culture can differentiate.
